title: Feast Your Eyes On This Smooth Subway Map Design
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="41214map1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507075154im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/byakas/41214map1.jpg" width="640" height="447"> <br> <i> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507075154/http://www.theatlanticcities.com/design/2014/04/architect-has-painstakingly-standardized-worlds-subway-maps/8843/">via Atlantic</a></i></div> <p></p>

<p>Comparing and contrasting various subway map designs is a NYC pastime, from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507075154/http://gothamist.com/2010/12/08/map_nerds_debate_relative_merits_of.php#photo-3">Massimo Vignelli&apos;s classic 1972 design</a> to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507075154/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/25/fun_map_nyc_subway_map_as_a_circle.php">concentric circle versions</a> to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507075154/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/02/super_mario_subway_map.php">Super Mario-enhanced maps.</a> Now, Serbia-born, Paris-based architect Jug Cerovic has developed his own unique brand of subway maps for transit cartography nerds to salivate over.</p>

<p>As <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507075154/http://www.theatlanticcities.com/design/2014/04/architect-has-painstakingly-standardized-worlds-subway-maps/8843/">Atlantic Cities reports</a>, Cerovic has made new subway maps for a dozen major cities around the world, including Paris, Barcelona, Tokyo, Berlin and of course, NYC. He purposefully kept a certain uniformity for all the maps, &quot;so if you go to another place, you&apos;ll seem to recognize the meaning of the signs.&quot;</p>

<p>His guidelines include enlarging city centers (to space out all the lines and stations), using uniform set of colors, symbols and labels, and most importantly, keeping &quot;all the lines vertical, horizontal, or 45 degrees inclined, and limited most of them to no more than five bends on their entire lengths.&quot; Check it out below.</p>
