</div></div><p><br>
The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130515185418/http://gothamist.com/tags/frankenstorm">FRANKENSTORM</a> hasn&apos;t touched down in the tri-state area yet, but it&apos;s already caused a wave of destruction at our local grocery stores. The first wave <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130515185418/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/28/park_slope_food_co-op_already_in_st.php">hit the Park Slope Food Coop early this morning</a>, leaving in its wake a Kafkaesque nightmare with 78% cacao chocolate bars. And now, that chaos has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130515185418/http://brooklynheightsblog.com/archives/49944">wiped out Key Foods</a>, decimated the wine shelves at Trader Joe&apos;s, and spread to all the Whole Foods and other supermarkets across the city. &quot;Whole Foods on Bowery is like a hipster-yuppie Thunderdome right now,&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130515185418/https://twitter.com/jessicacoen/status/262578302387093504">tweeted Jessica Coen</a>. Click through to check out some tortured tweets from the ground, and send your scenes of grocery store panic and despair to tips@gothamist.com</p>
