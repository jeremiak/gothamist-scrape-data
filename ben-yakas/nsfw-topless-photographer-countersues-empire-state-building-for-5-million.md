</div></div>					<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231317/http://gothamist.com/tags/allenhenson">Allen Henson</a> has built up quite a following for himself with his &apos;boobs-about-town&apos; project&#x2014;he&apos;s had photoshoots <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231317/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/11/nsfw_photos_get_ready_for_more_topl.php#photo-1">at restaurants</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231317/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/14/nypd_boobs_naked_diner_poses_with_c.php">with policemen</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231317/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/12/nsfw_woman_goes_topless_at_rooftop.php#photo-1">on rooftop bars</a>, and at the top <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231317/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/11/nsfw_photos_topless_woman_atop_empi.php#photo-1">of the Empire State Building</a>. That last stunt led to him being <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231317/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/14/nsfw_topless_photos_elicit_11_milli.php#photo-1">sued for $1.1 million by the ESB.</a> Now, a defiant Henson has countersued ESB for $5 million. &quot;I&apos;m hoping for a very fair judge,&quot; Henson told us. &quot;I&apos;m extremely confident in my attorney.&quot;</p>

<p>According to the original lawsuit (you can see both in full at the bottom of the page), <br>
&quot;[Henson] intentionally violated rules of the Observatory, intentionally engaged in unauthorized, objectionable, and inappropriate conduct in full view of ESB&apos;s customers, tenants, visitors, including families with children, and employees, and caused ESB to suffer economic losses and damage to its reputation.&quot; </p>

<p>Henson counters that ESB&apos;s reputation was already damaged way before he brought a semi-nude woman there: &quot;[Henson] avers that the approximately 30 suicide attempts from the Observation Deck...are the cause of any damage to ESB&apos;s business and reputation as a &quot;safe and secure family friendly tourist attraction.&quot;</p>

<p>The lawsuit has done nothing to snuff out Henson&apos;s love for topless photography&#x2014;he was back at it at Grand Central last month. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231317/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/23/nsfw_topless_photographer_takes_to.php">As he told us then</a>: &quot;Since the lawsuit happened, I&apos;m significantly less worried. I&apos;m a lot more informed as to what&apos;s legal and not, what&apos;s private and not. I&apos;m way more confident about doing this now. What&apos;s the worst that can happen? Be sued for $2 million?&quot;</p>

<p>As for his next project: &quot;I will be doing Austin, Texas this weekend to test some more conservative mindsets. We&apos;ll see how that goes.&quot;</p>
