<p>The Boy Scouts have a new, strangely mesmerizing <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130224023331/http://www.buzzfeed.com/jpmoore/the-new-boy-scouts-ads-are-ron-swanson-approved">ad campaign</a>&#x2014;with the emphasis decidedly on <em>strange.</em> The manly new campaign features young boys with long scraggily beards and the inviting motto, &quot;Be One With The Wild.&quot; We definiely think <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130224023331/http://www.bestweekever.tv/2011-09-23/ron-swanson-beard-photos-parks-and-recreation/">Ron Swanson</a> would approve. </p>

<p>The organization, which recruits 11 to 17-year-old boys, writes <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130224023331/http://www.scouting.org/">on their website that</a> members will dive &quot;into the rugged world of outdoor adventure, relying on teamwork and character to accomplish what everyone else thinks is impossible.&quot; So beards obviously...are rugged, too. Not sure if they really want to be associating with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130224023331/http://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=beardo">&quot;beardos&quot;</a>, however.</p>

<p>Recently, the Girl Scouts stirred up a bit of controversy when a seven-year-old Colorado boy who &quot;likes girl stuff&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130224023331/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/27/transgender_boy_tries_to_join_girl.php">was denied a chance to join.</a> But who could resist the lure of the Girl Scouts when they have puppy parties:</p>
