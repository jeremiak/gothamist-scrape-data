                            <p><br>
NYC and the De Blasio administration successfully navigated its way through the first major snowstorm of 2014, thanks to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/03/de_blasio_winter_storm_update_serio.php">mayoral stripteases</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/03/cozy_subterranean_bars_to_wait_out.php">cozy bars</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/03/photos_cats_dogs_enjoy.php#photo-1">wet animals</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/03/breaking_dante_de_blasio_shoveled_t.php">Dante trolling</a>, and of course, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/03/best_blizzard_photo_area_man_relaxe.php">hot tub guy.</a> Friday evening may have been the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/03/coldest_night_in_ten_years_expected.php">coldest night in a decade</a>, but winter has no interest in leaving us alone now: much of the US, including NYC, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://hosted.ap.org/dynamic/stories/U/US_DEEP_FREEZE?SITE=AP&amp;SECTION=HOME&amp;TEMPLATE=DEFAULT">is expected to feel</a> the wrath of a &quot;polar vortex&quot; this week, which will send the temperatures back down into the single digits and below.</p>

<p>Besides being a great name for a band, a polar vortex is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/01/03/cold-us-temperatures_n_4538827.html">a counterclockwise-rotating pool of cold, dense air</a>. &quot;It&apos;s just a large area of very cold air that comes down, forms over the North Pole or polar regions ... usually stays in Canada, but this time it&apos;s going to come all the way into the eastern United States,&quot; explained National Weather Service meteorologist Phillip Schumacher. &quot;It&apos;s the mother lode of cold air,&quot; said Weather Channel coordinating meteorologist Tom Moore. &quot;On the heels of what will be the coldest air of the season, will be dangerous, life-threatening winds.&quot;</p>

<p>The midwest will get the worst of it, but temperatures <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://www.weather.com/weather/wxdetail3/10025">are expected to plummet</a> in NYC between Monday evening and Tuesday. We could be looking at temperatures in the single digits on Tuesday&#x2014;and with wind chill included, it could be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140208081103/http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2533458/Flood-Hercules-grip-Northeast-Canada-records-temperatures-cold-MARS.html">even colder than last night.</a> </p>
