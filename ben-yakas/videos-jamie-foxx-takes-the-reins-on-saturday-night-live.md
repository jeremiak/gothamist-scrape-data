Jamie Foxx, no stranger to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430034917/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In_Living_Color">live sketch comedy</a>, hosted <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430034917/http://gothamist.com/tags/saturdaynightlive"><em>Saturday Night Live</em></a> for the second time last night. Unlike with some recent guest hosts (ahem, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430034917/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/07/videos_snl_skewers_the_presidential.php#photo-1">Daniel Craig</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430034917/http://gothamist.com/2012/11/18/videos_snl_mocks_the_petraeus_scand.php#photo-1">Jeremy Renner</a>), Foxx was a dominant presence throughout the night. There were a lot of dead-in-the-water sketches during the first half of the show, but things picked up very well in the second half, with hilarious, weird sketches including a game show in which nobody can tell Dylan McDermott from Dermot Mulroney, and a Maine courtroom show where everyone thinks they&apos;re in Louisiana.</p>

<p>Click through to check out those sketches as well as the cold open about Republicans beating up John Boehner, a Japanese version of Christmas, Tyler Perry&apos;s next Medea role, the power of Swarvoski crystals, a visit from Mrs. Claus on Weekend Update, and Ne-Yo performing two songs.<br>
