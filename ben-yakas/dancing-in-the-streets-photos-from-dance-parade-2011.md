</div></div><p><br>
No one may have ascended into the heavens yesterday, but a lot of folks got down in Manhattan yesterday for the 5th annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110522223555/http://gothamist.com/2011/05/20/celebrate_judgment_day_with_a_dance.php">Dance Parade</a>. Thousands of folks <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110522223555/http://www.dnainfo.com/20110521/lower-east-side-east-village/thousands-of-dancers-get-limber-east-village">turned out</a> for the intermittently sunny day, as more than 160 dance troupes danced down Broadway. The event, which calls itself the &quot;most culturally diverse festival in the world,&quot; featured over 74 different styles of dances with more than 10,000 dancers showing their moves with everything from salsa, to pole-dancing, to stilts to disco. Take a look at some pictures above, and check out a few videos of the event below taken <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110522223555/http://www.flickr.com/photos/christinyca/">by christiNYCa</a>.</p>
