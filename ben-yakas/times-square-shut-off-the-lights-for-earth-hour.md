<p>In case you were too busy preparing for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507104814/http://www.holidayinsights.com/moreholidays/March/incontrolday.htm">&quot;I Am In Control&quot; Day</a> to leave your apartment on Saturday, Earth Hour was last night. Major landmarks across the world turned off all non-essential power for an hour between 8:30 and 9:30 p.m. local time to honor the Earth, although perhaps telling the Earth you appreciate it a little more often would have been just as good as a global <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507104814/http://www.philstar.com/news-feature/2014/03/31/1307121/spidey-leads-everyday-heroes-earth-hour-campaign"><em>Amazing Spider-Man 2</em> promo</a>. In NYC, those landmarks included Times Square, Rockefeller Center, the World Trade Center, and the Empire State Building. As you can see from the pictures, it&apos;s both a little eerie and a bit of a relief to see all those LCD screens turned off.</p>
