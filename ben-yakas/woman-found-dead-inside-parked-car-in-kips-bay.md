<p>A woman was found dead in a parked car in Kips Bay in Manhattan today. According to FDNY, the unidentified 58-year-old woman was found in the driver&apos;s seat of a 1993 Volvo wagon on Second Avenue between E. 31st and 32nd Streets just after 9:30 a.m. <br>
Sources <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205210206/http://nypost.com/2013/12/19/woman-found-dead-inside-parked-car/">told the Post</a> that the woman had been living out of the car, which has Virginia license plates.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205210206/http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20131219/kips-bay/woman-found-dead-volvo-kips-bay">Locals told DNAInfo</a> that the car had been parked on the corner for a few months now, and the woman would often put messages in her window: &quot;She took out the signs from the back window saying, &apos;My husband left me and I&apos;m trapped in the car,&apos;&quot; said Deborah Little, who works at a nearby Staples. &quot;And I saw the police talking to her on Monday. I thought they were going to give her a ticket but they didn&apos;t.&quot;</p>

<p>Police say the investigation is still ongoing. </p>
