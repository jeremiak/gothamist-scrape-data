<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170909184214/http://gothamist.com/tags/santacon">SantaCon</a>&#x2014;the annual tradition that allows overgrown frat dudes and the women who love/tolerate them to relive their halcyon undergraduate bar-crawling days&#x2014;left the usual trail of spilled beer, discarded santa hats, and urine across lower Manhattan. Compared to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170909184214/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/12/flashback_video_the_first_ever_sant.php">early nihilistic years</a> of SantaCon, this year&apos;s festivities were relatively tame and tolerable. Sure, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170909184214/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/11/santacon_2016_photos.php#photo-1">over 100 summonses</a> for disorderly conduct were given out, and some children were undoubtedly scarred for life after seeing Santa dry-hump a fire hydrant on 3rd Avenue, but that&apos;s the price we pay as a society in order to coddle a bunch of jolly douchebags who &quot;like getting drunk and wearing a hat.&quot; </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170909184214if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/LuQ34-OKIXM" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>My favorite thing about the video above is that the cameraperson keeps cutting everyone off just before they finish trying to weave together a few positive things to say about SantaCon that don&apos;t have anything to do with drinking. Which is because, charitable contributions aside, there is no culture or purpose to SantaCon other than drinking. The first step to making SantaCon tolerable to the rest of New York is admitting that, letting go of the defensiveness, and then maybe erecting an electric fence around Murray Hill and letting participants roam free without worrying about anyone shouting &quot;MAKE AMERICA SANTA AGAIN&quot; at innocent passersby.</p>

<p>This video is beautiful because of the juxtaposition of the wimpy Christmas song soundtrack and the shrill voices and boisterous hollering that emerges from the people on camera:</p>
