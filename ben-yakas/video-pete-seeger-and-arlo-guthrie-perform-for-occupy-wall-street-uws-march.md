<p>Folk legend Pete Seeger and &apos;60s folk singer Arlo Guthrie joined in with Occupy Wall Street protesters last night for a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111031060309/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/21/tonight_late_night_occupy_wall_stre.php">march on the Upper West Side</a>. Approximately a thousand people <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111031060309/http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-500188_162-20124142/seeger-guthrie-join-occupy-wall-street-rally/">took part in the march</a> down Broadway from Symphony Space to Columbus Circle, mixing popular OWS chants (&quot;We are the 99 percent&quot;) with protests songs (including &quot;We Shall Overcome&quot;) all along the way. The 92-year-old Seeger was accompanied by musician-grandson Tao Rodriguez Seeger, composer David Amram and bluesman Guy Davis. Below, check out several videos of Seeger, Guthrie and all the other marchers singing in the streets:</p>
