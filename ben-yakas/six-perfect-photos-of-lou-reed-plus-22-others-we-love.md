Former Velvet Underground singer, rock and roll icon, lifelong New Yorker, and cantankerous genius <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429153528/http://gothamist.com/tags/loureed">Lou Reed</a> died on Sunday at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429153528/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/27/lou_reed_is_dead_at_71.php">the age of 71.</a> His doctor has since confirmed that the cause of death was his liver disease, which he had undergone <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429153528/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/01/lou_reed_recovering_after_liver_tra.php">liver surgery for in May.</a> Rock Paper Photo, a fine art photography archive site, has now released an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429153528/http://www.rockpaperphoto.com/featured-collections/lou-reed-a-photographic-tribute.html">online gallery dedicated to Reed</a>&#x2014;you can see five of those incredible photos above, along with the last photo ever taken of him (#2) and 22 classic and recent shots that capture his spirit.</p>

<p>And below, you can check out more tributes, remembrances, and other Reed ephemera from the last couple days.</p>

The screening takes place on Saturday, November 2, 7:30 p.m., in the Museum&#x2019;s Redstone Theater.
