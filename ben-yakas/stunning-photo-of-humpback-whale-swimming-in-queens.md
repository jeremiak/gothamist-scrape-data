<p>Yesterday morning, flyfishing guide <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928032830/https://twitter.com/UrbanFlyGuide">Brendan McCarthy</a> was out on the water near the Rockaways <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928032830/http://gothamist.com/2013/05/02/videos_whale_spotted_waving_at_flyf.php">when he spotted a humpback whale</a> playing in the water. &quot;I am a flyfishing charter boat captain and have spent a lot of time out here for the past 20 years,&quot; he told us. &quot;Never see them this close here.&quot; Robert DiGiovanni of The Riverhead Foundation told us that whales are sometimes spotted offshore during this time of year in that area of Queens&#x2014;and we have gotten <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928032830/http://gothamist.com/2011/08/22/video_photos_humpback_whale_spotted.php#photo-1">some</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928032830/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/19/photos_dolphin_pod_more_whales_spot.php#photo-1">reports</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928032830/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/17/photos_hipster_humpback_whale_waves.php#photo-1">of them</a> over the last couple years, but rarely have we seen as gorgeous a photo as the one above. Click below for some more videos and pictures of the whale waving at the Rockaways.</p>
