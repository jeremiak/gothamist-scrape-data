Yesterday marked the colorful, joyous and somewhat messy Hindu celebration Phagwah (also known as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507103843/http://gothamist.com/tags/holi">Holi</a>). And there&apos;s a damn good reason why it&apos;s known as a &apos;festival of colors,&apos; as you can see in the gorgeous portraits of many of the revelers in Richmond Hill, Queens. Check out the photos taken by Tom Giebel above, then look at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507103843/http://www.flickr.com/photos/atomische/sets/72157633125133245/">more portraits here.</a></p>

<p>The celebration occurs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507103843/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/12/phagwah_photos_the_dye_is_cast_duri.php#photo-1">every Spring</a> on the Sunday after the first full moon of the Hindu calendar&#x2014;kids and families are encouraged to &apos;color&apos; one another with dye (abrac) and powder to signify the end of winter. You can check out a few videos from yesterday&apos;s festivities below.</p>
