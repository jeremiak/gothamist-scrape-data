We come here to bury <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150322055717/http://gothamist.com/tags/santacon">SantaCon 2013</a>, not to praise it. The evil that men do lives after them; the good is oft interred with their bones&#x2014;or it&apos;s embedded in super defensive, thou-doth-protest-too-much quotes <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150322055717/http://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/15/nyregion/weather-and-criticism-are-no-damper-on-santacon.html?partner=rss&amp;emc=rss&amp;_r=0">in NY Times articles</a>. &quot;This is real street theater and I&apos;m a fan of that,&quot; Sidney Oolongo told them. &quot;We&apos;re supporting local businesses,&quot; argued Viking Santa. &#x201C;For us, it&#x2019;s a tradition, and nothing is going to stop us,&quot; said Amber Tyson, who travelled from Philly for the occasion. &quot;I have on a shirt, actually,&quot; added Angelique Blake.</p>

<p>All this talk of t-shirts and Viking Santas makes us wonder: have we judged SantaCon too harshly? Oh sure, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150322055717/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/15/watch_santacon_videos_to_remind_you.php">if you watch the videos</a>, it makes everyone participating look like thuggish fratboys who have reached the &apos;anger&apos; stage of a three-day bender. And if you read the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150322055717/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/26/elected_officials_to_santacon_youre.php">neighborhood complaints</a>, you&apos;d think the streets are flooded with regurgitated candy canes and the shell-shocked husks of revelers after the festivities.</p>

<p>But there are also those who think the day is about &quot;togetherness.&quot; So this has inspired us to take a long hard look at the human faces of SantaCon. The drunken, rosy-cheeked, makeup-smeared, vomit-flecked, sour-breathed faces of SantaCon. See you all next year, unfortunately.</p>
