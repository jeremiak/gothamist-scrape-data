While you were busy last night gorging on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141022173810/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/22/in_defense_of_pizza_bagels.php">pizza bagels</a> or <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141022173810/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/23/video_snow_lateness_dantes_afro_de.php#photo-1">hobnobbing with dorks</a>, East Williamsburg was host to BangOn! NYC, which threw its first warehouse party of 2014, titled &apos;Frequency.&apos; Attendees had to climb a pedestrian overpass over some railroad tracks to get in, so you know this was some serious shit.</p>

<p>The festival and immersive 3D party, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141022173810/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/17/photos_hey_girls_heres_how_a_real_b.php#photo-1">which took Bushwick by storm</a> last summer, had a couple fun additions this time: there were gogo girls dancing in a suspended cage as well as multiple aerial acts alternating between elaborate costumes and being nearly nude. There was fire breathing on top of a party bus, a zipline across the whole space, and the Calexico taco truck. Check out some pics courtesy of photographer Tod Seelie above, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141022173810/http://bangon-nyc.com/party">check back here</a> so you don&apos;t miss out next time.</p>
