</div></div>					<p>Animals have not been immune to the devastation that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/http://gothamist.com/tags/hurricanesandy">Hurricane Sandy</a> brought upon the city this week. And of course, everything that could go wrong had gone wrong this week to cause a sudden surge in homeless animals: Animal Care &amp; Control web servers have been down since the storm, local shelters have lost power, and there has been a lack of support from local chapters of National Animal Groups. So there are a lot of homeless kitties and lost doggies who desperately need you to adopt or foster them, or else they&apos;ll start being killed next week.</p>

<p>You can see some of the adorable animals above. But there are plenty more (<strong>over 200!</strong>) that need saving quick: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/https://www.facebook.com/media/set/?set=a.172404072772480.42595.152876678058553&amp;type=3">here are the &quot;super urgent&quot;</a> dogs, who are either high risk, injured or have previously appeared on the &quot;To Be Destroyed&quot; list and survived. Here are the dogs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/https://www.facebook.com/media/set/?set=a.161896980489856.39457.152876678058553&amp;type=3">in Brooklyn and Staten Island.</a> Here are the dogs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/https://www.facebook.com/media/set/?set=a.161896683823219.39456.152876678058553&amp;type=3http://on.fb.me/sdSs4P">in Manhattan.</a> And here are the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/https://www.facebook.com/media/set/?set=a.255779241100582.77234.155925874419253&amp;type=3">super urgent kitties</a> that need adopting; here are the kittens <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/https://www.facebook.com/media/set/?set=a.266242446720928.79111.155925874419253&amp;type=3">in Brooklyn and Staten Island</a>; and here are the kitehs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/https://www.facebook.com/media/set/?set=a.267273579951148.79236.155925874419253&amp;type=3">in Manhattan.</a></p>

<p>On the plus side, <a href="WWW.ADOPTNY.ORG">Adopt NY</a> has been working tirelessly to get people to sign up, and expedite the process to get animals into people&apos;s hands as soon as possible. If you&apos;re interested in fostering an animal, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/http://www.doghabitat.org/volunteer/foster/">fill out the foster application here.</a>  In the application, please specify what type of animal you&apos;re prepared to foster (Dog/Puppy, Cat/Kitten or a full litter of bottle babies). They aren&apos;t accepting applications from people outside a 25 mile radius of Manhattan&#x2014;you must be prepared to get to the shelters to pick up the animal. If you&apos;re not able to foster an animal, you can still contribute <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160422132451/http://www.doghabitat.org/donate/">with a donation to ADOPT NY</a> that will go directly to this mission. </p>
