<p>If you haven&apos;t already contracted the flu during <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538/http://gothamist.com/tags/flu">our current epidemic</a>, then you probably made a deal with the devil a la Jimi Hendrix&#x2014;and we all know how that turned out (there&apos;s a reason there are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538/http://consequenceofsound.net/2013/01/listen-to-jimi-hendrixs-new-song-somewhere/">magically unearthed &quot;new&quot; Hendrix recordings</a> being premiered on <em>Hawaii Five-0</em> in 2012). But for the rest of us fragile creatures, getting the flu is a miserable reality. But you are not alone! Humanity has struggled with the aches and sniffles of influenza <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538/http://www.flu.gov/pandemic/history/index.html">throughout the 20th century</a>. And likewise, songwriters have been grappling with it in song; below, we&apos;ve compiled a 14-song flu playlist to help take some attention away from the sound of your clogged up nose. </p>

<p>Oh, you always thought Gordon Matthew Thomas Sumner was singing about an illicit teacher/student relationship? How wrong you were:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/KNIZofPB8ZM" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Essie Jenkins&apos; song is all about the influenza pandemic of 1918-1919. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538/http://www.history.navy.mil/library/online/influenza%20pan.htm">Learn your history</a> and listen. </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/dCuqQmukOcE" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>There are a lot of ailments the Stones could have been suffering from, but the pain where there once was a heart sure sounds like a rough illness.</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/WQ7cooiw-K4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>One of the all-time great American folk songs, &quot;St. James Infirmary Blues&quot; has been covered by everyone from the White Stripes to Louis Armstrong.</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/QzcpUdBw7gs" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Why, pray tell, do you think Declan MacManus was having such a hard time staying upright?</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/SEZrBw6mcSM" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The great blues master Skip James sang about his sickbed in this late-period classic, taken from his phenomenal <em>Blues From the Delta</em> album.</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/OgcpuhvjNuw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The Ramones skip straight to the point, as only the Ramones can do: &quot;You sound like you&apos;re sick / You&#xFEFF; look like you&apos;re sick too.&quot;</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/HyYUOr0dSp4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The Beastie Boys actually sound pumped about spending time indoors clutching the tissues and the TV remote:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/5KRYwYnC5cg" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Mudhoney are asking for it with this one, those sickos:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/_nGsT_qFMBs" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151008012538if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/CQExEuMDM8U" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Johnny Rivers sounds positively joyous about contracting pneumonia:</p>

<p>Warren Zevon offers this sobering, utterly brilliant song about being ill and having a doctor who tells it to you straight: &quot;The shit that used to work / won&apos;t work now.&quot;</p>
