Earlier this week, photos of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430035100/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/08/photo_niagara_falls_has_frozen_over.php">a completely-frozen over Niagara Falls</a> spread around the internet making all other arctic photos look pathetic and puny&#x2014;as majestic as those photos were though, it turned out they were actually from 2011. But the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430035100/http://gothamist.com/tags/polarvortex">polar vortex</a> that enveloped most of the country did end up turning Niagara into an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430035100/http://www.washingtonpost.com/national/cold-snap-drapes-niagara-falls-in-icy-beauty/2014/01/09/31f1e8ce-7994-11e3-b1c5-739e63e9c9a7_gallery.html#photo=1">icy spectacle</a>, freezing a section of the American Falls waterfall. As you can see in the pictures above and videos below, there&apos;s a damn good reason why tourists have been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430035100/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/northamerica/usa/10565409/Frozen-by-the-polar-vortex-tourists-flock-to-Niagara-Falls.html">flocking there</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430035100/http://www.buffalonews.com/city-region/niagara-falls/spectacle-of-snow-and-ice-20140109">all week</a>.</p>
