After last week&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429074243/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/20/videos_saturday_night_live_returns.php#photo-1">stinker</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429074243/http://gothamist.com/tags/saturdaynightlive"><em>Saturday Night Live</em></a> bounced back with last night&apos;s episode. Maroon 5 singer Adam Levine was the sometimes-shirtless host&#x2014;and despite some obvious line reading troubles, he did a pretty decent job mocking himself, especially in the star-studded monologue (featuring Jerry Seinfeld, Cameron Diaz, and Andy Samberg), and the Maroon 5/Train/Jason Mraz/John Mayer dance-off sketch (which, sadly, isn&apos;t online).</p>

<p>The return of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429074243/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/27/video_digital_short_returns_to_snl.php">Samberg and Lonely Island for &quot;Yolo&quot;</a> was probably the biggest highlight of the evening, but our favorite sketch was definitely the killer &quot;The Sopranos Diaries.&quot; Fred Armisen did a PERFECT Paulie Walnuts impression. Click through for those sketches, plus the real use of Rosetta Stone, Obama visited by the ghost of MLK, Biden Bash, mocking &quot;Catfish,&quot; Arianna Huffington and Ray Lewis on Weekend Update, and performances by Kendrick Lamar.</p>
