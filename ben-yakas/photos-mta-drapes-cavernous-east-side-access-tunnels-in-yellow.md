Last we checked in with the ongoing work on the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150424125203/http://gothamist.com/tags/eastsideaccess">East Side Access</a> project, which will connect commuter trains from Long Island Rail Road to Grand Central, the long-delayed endeavor was being delayed even longer, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150424125203/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/24/east_side_access_pushed_back_to_202.php">likely until  the next decade</a>. But at least the MTA is trying to put a sunny face on the whole endeavor&#x2014;as Gwyneth Paltrow&apos;s husband once predicted, everything is all yellow in the tunnels under Manhattan&apos;s East Side. </p>

<p>Or maybe they&apos;ve been watching <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150424125203/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/17/true_detective_review.php">a lot of <em>True Detective</em></a> and have been driven mad by <em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150424125203/http://io9.com/the-one-literary-reference-you-must-know-to-appreciate-1523076497">The King in Yellow</a></em>. Either way, it&apos;s kind of hypnotizing watching the space come together after so many years&#x2014;remember when it <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150424125203/http://gothamist.com/2013/02/16/photos_east_side_access.php">looked like the set of some &apos;70s sci-fi/James Bond-esque film?</a> So click through the photos above and feast your eyes on all its mustard-stained glory.</p>
