Now get your damn feet off the seat, kid: a subway conductor <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150402081131/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/queens/conductor_hits_teen_on_subway_cops_DQicrwppNdPCLDxQOdz39I">has been arrested and charged for</a> allegedly assaulting a bratty teen who wouldn&apos;t remove his feet from the seat early this morning. The conductor should just be glad <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150402081131/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/01/ellen_barkin_pissed_off_at_nypd_for.php">Ellen Barkin wasn&apos;t there.</a><br>
 <br>
According <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150402081131/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/queens/conductor_hits_teen_on_subway_cops_DQicrwppNdPCLDxQOdz39I">to the Post</a>, conductor Thomas Christopher, 35, allegedly got into a verbal argument with the 18-year-old boy aboard the southbound R train as it traveled near the 36th Street station in Astoria around 3:50 a.m. today (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150402081131/http://www.dnainfo.com/20120101/midtown/mta-worker-arrested-for-assault-after-fight-with-r-train-rider-cops-say">DNAInfo claims the incident occurred</a> around 11 p.m. last night). The conductor lost his cool and allegedly punched the teen. Christopher, an MTA employee since March 2007, was arrested on misdemeanor assault charges. <p></p>

<p>Over the past few years, police have become <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150402081131/http://gothamist.com/2010/02/16/still_not_safe_to_put_feet_on_seat.php">increasingly aggressive</a> about <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150402081131/http://gothamist.com/2010/03/21/cops_really_are_cracking_down_on_su.php">cracking down on</a> seat hogs who put their bag or their feet on another seat. </p>
