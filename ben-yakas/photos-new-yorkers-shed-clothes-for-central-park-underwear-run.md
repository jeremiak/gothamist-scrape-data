Normally, New Yorkers have to wait until the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150722170017/http://gothamist.com/tags/nopantssubwayride">No Pants Subway Ride</a> or the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150722170017/http://gothamist.com/tags/worldnakedbikeride">World Naked Bike Ride</a> to see fellow NYers in their skivvies&#x2014;it&apos;s not like we live in a city where one can just walk outside and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150722170017/http://gothamist.com/2014/07/14/underwear_trend_alert.php">be greeted by people in underoos</a>, doncha know. But twas so Friday evening in Central Park, as hundreds of New Yorkers stripped to their pantaloons for the annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150722170017/http://gothamist.com/tags/UnderwearRun">Underwear Run.</a></p>

<p>The Naked Cowboy, Robert Burck, was the enthusiastic grand marshal as around 500 triathletes participated in the 1.7-mile run, which kicked off New York City Triathlon weekend. More than 4,000 runners are expected to take part in the real Triathalon, which takes place this Sunday. </p>

<p>Whether or not they wear their superhero-emblazoned underwear is up to them&#x2014;but they certainly came out in droves yesterday, as you can see in the photos above. Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150722170017/http://saimokhtari.com/">Sai Mokhtari</a> said the short race was a reminder of everything good about NYC: &quot;I kept finding myself thinking, I LOVE THIS FUCKING CITY!!! The fun, the irreverence, acceptance, confidence and diversity of the crowd..! NO other city gives it to you like this.&quot;</p>
