We live in a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160302111427/http://gothamist.com/tags/banksy">post-Banksy society</a> now, but there&apos;s still plenty of incredible street art going on. At the top of our list right now is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160302111427/https://www.facebook.com/pages/RAE-BK/465960303465806">RAE&apos;s</a> incredibly detailed &quot;Word Of Mouth&quot; piece in the East Village, in which he transforms a normal old school bodega into a wondrous art landscape. </p>

<p>The shelves are stocked with classic bodega fare (EBT cards, bread, cans of beans, loosies, lotto tickets), but the walls and margins of all the surfaces are filled with a staggering array of RAE&apos;s drawings, paintings, stickers, and sculptures. Photographer Scott Lynch was impressed: &quot;The attention to detail is awesome&#x2014;the products, the security cameras, the pay phone, all of it&#x2014;and people kept stopping in and saying, &apos;Wait, is this a store?&apos; I loved it, it&apos;s got street art and vanishing NYC angles.&quot;</p>

<p>You can also buy stuff there if you want (the gallery worker also functions as a cashier). The exhibit will run through November 16th on the corner of Avenue C and East 12th Street. It is open Thursday through Saturday from 2 to 7 p.m.</p>
