title: Video: Jon Stewart And Al Sharpton Criticize Occupy Wall Street "Theatrics"
---

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="102011stewart.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20111021102437im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/byakas/102011stewart.jpg" width="640" height="413" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>The newly-slimmed down (and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111021102437/http://gothamist.com/2011/08/24/confirmed_rev_al_sharpton_gets_his.php">cable-ready</a>) Rev. Al Sharpton visited the Daily Show last night, and sat down with Jon Stewart to discuss the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111021102437/http://gothamist.com/tags/occupywallstreet">Occupy Wall Street</a> protesters who have been nestled in Zuccotti Park for the last month. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111021102437/http://gawker.com/5851590/jon-stewart-are-we-expecting-too-much-from-occupy-wall-street">Among other things</a>, they covered the media bias in reporting on OWS, and Sharpton&apos;s full embrace of OWS, including the time he <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111021102437/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/10/listen_al_sharpton_broadcasts_live.php">broadcast his radio show live</a> from the park. But the most interesting parts of the interview came when the two criticized the &quot;theatrics&quot; around OWS. Watch below.</p>

<div style="text-align: center;"><object width="512" height="288"><param name="movie" value="http://www.hulu.com/embed/oyGjwcK47_vSjoLueMUNtA"><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true"><embed src="https://web.archive.org/web/20111021102437oe_/http://www.hulu.com/embed/oyGjwcK47_vSjoLueMUNtA" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="512" height="288" allowfullscreen="true"></object></div>

<p>&quot;For me, this is my own problem, my own inhibitions, the theatrical aspects of all that and some of the elements that are attracted down there, I&apos;m not usually down with, but I am very much interested in the message that&apos;s being articulated and the feeling that is,&quot; Stewart said, asking Sharpton whether the theatrics are a necessary evil to attract the media attention. &quot;What they&apos;ve done there is so unusual that it can&apos;t be ignored,&quot; Stewart added, but now that they <em>have</em> the world&apos;s attention, people are left wondering, &quot;&apos;<strong>When are you going to change the world for us?&apos; Maybe we are expecting too much.&quot;</strong></p>
