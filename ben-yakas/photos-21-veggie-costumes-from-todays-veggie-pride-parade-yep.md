If you happened to come across a giant chicken or killer tomato in lower Manhattan earlier, don&apos;t be alarmed&#x2014;today was the 6th annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508123216/http://gothamist.com/tags/veggieprideparade">Veggie Pride Parade</a>, with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508123216/http://gothamist.com/2013/03/20/veggie_pride_parade.php">participants marching</a> from the Meatpacking District to Union Square. We didn&apos;t spot any giant pink <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508123216/http://gothamist.com/2008/05/19/veggie_pride_pa.php#photo-1">human colon costumes</a>, but there were plenty of other interesting ones that you can click through in the photos above. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508123216/http://www.veggieprideparade.org/">The parade</a> was started by author Pamela Rice (&#x201C;101 Reasons Why I&#x2019;m a Vegetarian&#x201D;) and is a celebration of all things vegan and vegetarian. It seems especially appropriate that this year&apos;s parade took place during the same week that one of the patron saints of vegetarians, Morrissey, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508123216/http://gothamist.com/2013/03/23/still_ill_morrissey_pooh-poohs_doct.php">vowed to continue singing &quot;Meat Is Murder&quot; as long as he humanely can.</a> Below, check out a video from last year&apos;s parade.</p>
