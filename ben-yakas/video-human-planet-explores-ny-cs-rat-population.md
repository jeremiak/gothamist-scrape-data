										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-left" style=" width:200px; "> <img alt="43011rats.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430052512im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/byakas/43011rats.jpg" width="200" height="251"> <br> <i><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430052512/http://www.flickr.com/photos/dannydaly/5662028347/">dannydailypic&apos;s flickr</a></i></div> By now, we think we&apos;re all quite comfortable admitting that NYC is really the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430052512/http://gothamist.com/tags/rats">rats&apos;</a> fiefdom&#x2014;we&apos;re just renting their property, riding their subways, and stuffing <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430052512/http://gothamist.com/2011/03/09/video_sad_rat_man_makes_out_with_ra.php">our mouths</a> full with them in a desperate bid for some semblance of control. But according to BBC/Discovery Channels excellent <em>Human Planet</em>, there&#x2019;s <strong>at least</strong> one rat for every person in NYC. The show will explore the Big Apple&apos;s rat population in this Sunday&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430052512/http://dsc.discovery.com/tv/human-planet/episodes.html">episode</a>. Below, you can check out a clip from it, in which pest control firm M&amp;M; Environmental explores the basement of one unnamed East Village restaurant. It makes you think extra hard about where you&apos;re ordering out from, to say the least.<p></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430052512/http://www.boweryboogie.com/2011/04/lower-east-side-rats-coming-to-discoverys-human-planet/">Bowery Boogie has</a> some more factoids from the episode: In May of 2010, the city health department laid off 75 percent of its pest control staff; In 2010, there were 10,500 citywide complains of rodents received by 311- up more than 5 percent since 2009; <strong>and so far in 2011, rodent complaints are already up by 9 percent since 2010&#x2019;s numbers</strong>.</p>
