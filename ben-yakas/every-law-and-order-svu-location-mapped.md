<p>In Gothamist&apos;s never-ending quest to document all good things <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428161833/http://gothamist.com/tags/lawandorder"><em>Law And Order</em></a>-related&#x2014;from Sam Waterston&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428161833/http://gothamist.com/2012/05/23/best_subway_commute_ever_spotting_s.php">subway exploits</a> to Jerry Orbach&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428161833/http://gothamist.com/2007/01/09/jerry_orbach_ey.php">eternally blue eyes</a>&#x2014;we&apos;ve come across some cool maps, including one <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428161833/http://gothamist.com/2012/02/06/mapping_law_orders_first_season.php">documenting the first season</a> of the original <em>L&amp;O;</em>. And now, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428161833/http://culture.wnyc.org/articles/features/2012/jun/18/law-and-order-special-victims-unit-mapped/">WNYC has mapped out</a> every location from 13 seasons of <em>Law And Order: Special Victim&apos;s Unit</em>.</p>

<p><iframe src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428161833if_/http://project.wnyc.org/law-and-order-map" height="650" width="100%" scrolling="no" frameborder="0"></iframe></p>

<p>By the looks of it, the Upper West Side and Meatpacking District are the two most dangerous neighborhoods in NYC for schizophrenic rape victims and nightclub-frequenting teenage girls living secret lives. Check it out in full above, and keep it open while there&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428161833/http://allthingslawandorder.blogspot.com/2012/06/usa-network-hosts-special-law-and-order.html">a marathon of fan favorite episodes</a> from seasons 6, 8, 9 and 10 on USA on Sunday. It may not fill the Christopher Meloni-sized hole in your heart, but it&apos;s a start.</p>
