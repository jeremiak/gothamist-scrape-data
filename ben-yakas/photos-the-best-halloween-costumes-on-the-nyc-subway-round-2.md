					<p>Yesterday, we published <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110085843/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/31/best_halloween_subway_costume.php#photo-1">one gazillion amazing photos</a> showing New Yorkers traversing our mass transit system during the Halloween weekend while dressed up as Khaleesi&apos;s dragons pre-special effects, a mashup of David S. Pumpkins and Jason Voorhees, and a troupe of Guys Fieri. But last night was the big show, so we sent photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110085843/https://www.instagram.com/the_sai_life/">Sai Mokhtari</a> back down to the subways for even more creative and whimsical costume pics. </p>

<p>While it was a little bit quieter than <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110085843/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/02/subway_halloween_costumes.php#photo-1">previous</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110085843/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/01/photos_nyc_subway_best_halloween_costumes_part1.php#photo-1">Halloweens</a>, those who got dressed up for the parade or the bar made up for it with lots of elaborate homemade costumes. A Monday Halloween, it seems, is for the professionals. Highlights of last night&apos;s batch include Barb from <em>Stranger Things</em>, a ridiculously accurate and awesome Cher outfit from the 1985 Academy Awards, a homemade scorpion costume, a giant National Enquirer cover come to life, some Trump-hating <em>Scream</em> monsters, Jackie Kennedy Onassis after JFK was shot, <em>Labyrinth</em>-era Bowie, a giant Pizza Dude, and a man with dozens of stuffed animals attached to his body.</p>
