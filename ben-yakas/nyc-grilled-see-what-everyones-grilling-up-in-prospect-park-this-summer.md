					<p>It&apos;s the end of June and all the typical signs of summer in NYC have now emerged: ice cream trunk jingles (and the cries of people <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015112458/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/28/ice_cream_jingle_madness.php">complaining about them to 311</a>) fill the air; air conditioner fluid (a.k.a. City Juice) drips from the sky; and fire hydrants have been magically unburdened of their clapper valves. And if you walk through almost any major park in the city, you&apos;ll smell the sweetest aroma: charcoal, lighter fluid, and unprocessed meat.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="grilling1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015112458im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/byakas/grilling1.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> Sai Mokhtari/Gothamist</i></div> <p></p>

<p>We sent photographer Sai Mokhtari out to Prospect Park last weekend to check out what New Yorkers are grilling up this summer, and she ended up gorging on a lot of BBQ. </p>

<p>&quot;The first dude I saw had a couple burgers and a hot dog on there and I was thinking, &apos;Please don&apos;t let today be all about shriveled up hot dogs and burgers,&apos;&quot; she said. &quot;Thankfully, the people at Prospect Park were a lot more creative than me and they were grilling up some things that I never would have thought to bring to the park.&quot; Like these people below who offered her some fish balls:</p>
