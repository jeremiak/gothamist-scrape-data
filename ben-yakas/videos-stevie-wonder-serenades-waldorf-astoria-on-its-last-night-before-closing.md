<p>New York-Presbyterian Hospital celebrated the 20th anniversary of their merger with a big fundraiser gala last night at the historic <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122911/http://gothamist.com/tags/waldorfastoria">Waldorf-Astoria</a> in Midtown. It also happened to be the final event for several years at the 86-year-old luxury hotel and event space, which was purchased by the Anbang Insurance Group a few years ago, and has now closed its doors for a major renovation project that will reportedly see many rooms <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122911/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/15/waldorf_astoria_luxury_condos.php">turned into apartments.</a> You can check out some last glimpses of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122911/http://ny.curbed.com/2017/3/1/14780192/waldorf-astoria-hotel-midtown-closed">iconic space here.</a></p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122911if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/Sqf_0c3G9Jk" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Anbang <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122911/http://ny.curbed.com/2016/10/28/13462356/waldorf-astoria-hotel-interior-landmarks-nyc">has promised</a> to preserve and restore the hotel&apos;s more accessible interiors, but who knows what it will look like once everything has been converted in three or four years. At least they went out with the best goddamn soundtrack imaginable, courtesy of the one-and-only <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122911/http://gothamist.com/tags/steviewonder">Stevie Wonder.</a></p>
