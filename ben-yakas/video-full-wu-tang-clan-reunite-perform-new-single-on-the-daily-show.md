										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="8714wutang.jpeg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503153012im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/byakas/8714wutang.jpeg" width="640" height="400" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503153012/http://gothamist.com/tags/wutangclan">Wu-Tang Clan</a> have been mired in inter-personal beefs (as well as some <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503153012/http://pitchfork.com/news/54627-rza-says-wu-tang-clan-offered-5-million-for-new-album-thats-only-available-as-one-copy/">artsy shenanigans</a>) which have delayed the release of their upcoming album, <em>A Better Tomorrow.</em> But it appears the beefs have been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503153012/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6084816/rza-raekwon-end-beef-over-wu-tang-clans-a-better-tomorrow-album">squashed</a>, and now it&apos;s time to bring the ruckus: all nine members of the reunited Wu (roll call: RZA, GZA, Ghostface Killah, Raekwon, Inspectah Deck, Cappadonna, Method Man, U-God, and Masta Killa) appeared on The Daily Show last night to plug the new album (it&apos;s coming out in November) and premiere the first single, &quot;Ron O&apos; Neal.&quot; Check it out below.</p>

<p>Raekwon explained the complexities of getting the whole gang back together: &quot;I think it&apos;s important, as an athlete of music, to make sure you&apos;ve got your business together.&quot; Also, watch out for prospective new member Jordan Zlepper.</p>
