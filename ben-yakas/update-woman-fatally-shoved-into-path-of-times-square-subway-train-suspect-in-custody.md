<p><strong>[Update Below]</strong> A unidentified woman has been taken into custody after she allegedly shoved a person to her death in front of a train at Times Square today.</p>

<p>Police say the incident happened at 1:30 p.m. Monday at the southbound 1 train track at Times Square. The victim was waiting for the train when the woman, whom <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110184526/http://nypost.com/2016/11/07/man-shoved-to-his-death-in-front-of-times-square-train/">the Post says</a> was wearing a pink shirt, shoved her in front of the oncoming train.</p>

<p>The suspect was stopped by police and taken into custody at the scene. 1/2/3 trains are currently running with delays, and 2 train is running along the 5 line from the 149th Street-Grand Concourse station to the Nevins Street station due to the police investigation.</p>

<p><strong>Update</strong>: Police now confirm that the victim was a 49-year-old woman. The suspect, a 30-year-old woman whose name hasn&apos;t been released, had been reportedly arguing with the victim on the platform before she pushed her into the oncoming train. &quot;Twenty police officers escorted her out&#x2026;Her head was up, kinda proud of what she did,&quot; an MTA worker <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110184526/http://nypost.com/2016/11/07/woman-shoved-to-her-death-in-front-of-times-square-train/&gt;told the Post. &lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
