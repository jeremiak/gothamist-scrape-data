</div></div>					<p>While Alec Baldwin and Kate McKinnon <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214003/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/16/video_alec_baldwins_trump_turns_int.php">grabbed the morning headlines</a> by reenacting scenes from <em>Jaws</em> at the second presidential debate in the cold open, there were plenty more politically-minded sketches on last night&apos;s episode of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214003/http://gothamist.com/tags/snl"><em>Saturday Night Live</em></a>. Host Emily Blunt was front and center for the other standout pre-taped sketch of the night, <strong>Melanianade</strong>, in which the ladies of SNL performed a wonderfully faithful parody of Beyonc&#xE9;&apos;s &quot;Sorry&quot; with Melania Trump, Omarosa, Kellyanne Conway, and Ivanka and Tiffany Trump singing about being done with Donald.</p>

<p>Overall, this was the weakest of the first three episodes of SNL this season&#x2014;Blunt was game, especially excited about showing off her singing bonafides in the <strong>Monologue</strong> in anticipation of her starring role next year in <em>Mary Poppins</em>, but the energy levels felt off all episode. Whether it was a matter of the cast and writers being a little burnt out on the furious last-minute Trump-induced rewrites of the past month, it was especially felt in the first post-Monologue sketch, <strong>Escorts</strong>, which was filled with awkward timing (though Blunt did her best to save it with an especially great pratfall toward the end).</p>

<p>The show has really been embracing short, artsy, somewhat surreal pre-taped segments this season, with the recurring <strong>Melania Moments</strong> making its third appearance this week. There was also <strong>The Sink</strong>, in which Blunt voiced a sink having an existential crisis; and best of all, <strong>Chonk</strong>, which is already one of my favorite sketches of this year.</p>

Other highlights: Emily Blunt&apos;s pronunciation of &quot;quesadilla&quot; and nearly-successful attempts to make McKinnon break in the otherwise forgettable <strong>Honda Robotics</strong>; Bobby Moynihan and Kenan Thompson tapping into their inner weirdos for the poorly-paced, but pretty funny <strong>Drive-Thru Window</strong>; Vanessa Bayer calling out NBC&apos;s Billy Bush payout as Laura Parsons on <strong>Weekend Update</strong>; and McKinnon, perfect as ever, as Olya Povlotasky also on <strong>Weekend Update</strong>; and the 10-to-1 re-staging of <em>Who&apos;s Afraid Of Virginia Wolf?</em>...but with <strong>Hamsters.</strong></p>
