Back <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405012757/http://gothamist.com/2014/04/09/photos_swoon_brooklyn.php#photo-1">in April</a>, street artist <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405012757/http://gothamist.com/tags/swoon">Swoon</a> moved inside for her <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405012757/http://www.brooklynmuseum.org/exhibitions/swoon/">Submerged Motherlands exhibit</a> at the Brooklyn Museum. And tomorrow is your last chance to see it in person, so don&apos;t delay!</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20150405012757if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/oRyvMpeWuMA" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Inspired by contemporary and historical climate change events, Swoon and her team created a site-specific installation in the rotunda gallery. According to the museum, they transformed it into &quot;a fantastic landscape centering on a monumental sculptural tree with a constructed environment at its base, including sculpted boats and rafts, figurative prints and drawings, and cut paper foliage.&quot; </p>

<p>For more art fun, check out this Museum discussion about street art with Managing Curator of Exhibitions Sharon Matt Atkins and guests Keith Schweitzer, Luna Park and Swoon. </p>
