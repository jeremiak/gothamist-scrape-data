<p>Back in November, Emma Watson <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170718070533/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/09/emma_watson_will_be_leaving_books_a.php">snuck around the city</a> stashing Maya Angelou&apos;s book <em>Mom and Me and Mom</em> inside various subway stations as part of the feminist book project, Our Shared Shelf (video documenting this was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170718070533/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/07/emma_watson_hiding_books_again.php">only released this week</a>). It seems that the self-proclaimed &quot;ninja book-fairy&quot; returned to the city on Wednesday to perform a few more random acts of book-giving, heading to statues of various famous women to drop off books and handwritten notes in honor of International Women&apos;s Day. </p>

<p>Watson teamed up with the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170718070533/http://www.thebookfairies.org/">Book Fairies</a>, a non-profit that promotes literacy through a shared book program, for the latest project, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170718070533/http://www.bbc.com/news/uk-england-london-39204071">leaving over 1,000 books</a> various places worldwide (in addition to Angelou&apos;s book, she left others including Alice Walker&apos;s <em>The Color Purple</em> and Caitlin Moran&apos;s <em>How To Be A Woman</em>). Watson, decked out in a red beret, documented her busy day on Instagram, posting videos from the Harriet Tubman Memorial at St. Nicholas Avenue and Frederick Douglass Boulevard in Harlem; the Joan of Arc Memorial in Riverside Park; the Gertrude Stein Monument in Bryant Park; and The Eleanor Roosevelt Monument near W. 72nd Street also in Riverside Park.</p>
