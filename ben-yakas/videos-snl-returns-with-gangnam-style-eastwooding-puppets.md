<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141016201738/http://gothamist.com/tags/snl"><em>Saturday Night Live</em></a> returned last night for its 38th season, with host Seth MacFarlane and musical guest Frank Ocean&#x2014;and the emphasis was on political sketches, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141016201738/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/15/snls_back_sans_wiig_and_samberg_but.php">as expected.</a> Jay Pharaoh made his debut as President Obama in the cold open (Fred Armisen introduced him), which was followed by an anti-Romney commercial, and an ad for Eastwood and Chair on Broadway. As you can imagine with MacFarlane, there were plenty of strange voices and dead-on impressions (of George Takei and Marty McFly, among others, in his monologue song)&#x2014;but the musical highlight may have been the appearance of Psy for a Gangnam Style sketch.</p>

<p>Click through for those sketches, as well as Frank Ocean performing two songs (one with John Mayer), Ryan Lochte&apos;s fall TV preview, Honey Boo Boo on Weekend Update, a very damaged war vet trying to get into puppets, and lots more!</p>
