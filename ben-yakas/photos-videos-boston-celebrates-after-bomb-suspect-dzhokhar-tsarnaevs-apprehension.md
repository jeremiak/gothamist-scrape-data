	<p><br>
After <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130525021454/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/19/one_boston_bomb_suspect_is_dead_as.php#photo-1">a harrowing confrontation</a> with authorities late Thursday evening kicked off an all-day manhunt, Boston marathon bombing suspect Dzhokhar Tsarnaev <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130525021454/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/20/after_exchanging_gunfire_boston_bom.php#photo-1">was finally arrested</a> by police Friday night after yet another standoff. A very palpable sense of relief spread across Boston, as cops blocked traffic to let <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130525021454/http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/early-lead/wp/2013/04/20/arrest-sparks-widespread-jubilation-2/">hundreds march down Commonwealth Avenue</a> chanting &#x201C;USA&#x201D; and singing Neil Diamond&apos;s &#x201C;Sweet Caroline.&#x201D; People <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130525021454/http://www.slate.com/blogs/crime/2013/04/19/dzhokhar_tsarnaev_captured_the_celebration_in_watertown.html">cheered and high-fived</a> cops in Watertown, and the jubilation <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130525021454/http://deadspin.com/baseball-fans-react-to-the-capture-of-the-second-bombin-476575215?utm_campaign=socialflow_deadspin_twitter&amp;utm_source=deadspin_twitter&amp;utm_medium=socialflow">even spread to Citi Field</a>, where Mets fans informed by jumbotron of the arrest also broke out singing &quot;Sweet Caroline.&quot;</p>

<p>&#x201C;This was like our first opportunity to really be outside without feeling like there imminent danger,&#x201D; Boston University juniors Brendan Hathaway <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130525021454/http://news.yahoo.com/boston-beyond-thanks-jubilation-080556734.html">told AP</a>. &#x201C;It was close to home for me.&#x201D; Fellow Boston University student Aaron Wengertsman said, &quot;I&apos;m glad they caught him alive. I thought people might be more excited, but it&apos;s humbling to see all these people paying their respects.&quot; You can check out videos from Boston below:</p>

<p>&#x201C;Sometimes college students have a somewhat contentious relationship with police, and you see none of that tonight,&#x201D; Zach Tucker, a 20-year-old Emerson College student, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130525021454/http://www.bostonglobe.com/metro/2013/04/20/boston-watertown-erupt-with-cheers-relief-after-capture-terror-suspect/O3MjKHppLKUH6fqOioha3M/story.html">told Boston Globe</a>. &#x201C;In a way it feels like we&#x2019;ve been in this siege mentality, and it just feels like it&#x2019;s been lifted,&#x201D; added Back Bay resident Ricky Pickering.</p>
