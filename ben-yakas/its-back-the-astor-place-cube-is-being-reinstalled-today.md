					<p>It&apos;s been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/03/photos_olek_bombs_the_alamo_aka_the.php#photo-1">yarnbombed</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/14/caltech_prank_blankets_the_astor_pl.php">damaged</a>, forced into <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2007/10/30/astor_place_cub.php">marriage</a>, and turned into <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/01/photos_astor_place_cube_man_is_the_best.php">a Halloween costume</a>, but something even better has happened today to The Alamo, a.k.a. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/tags/astorplacecube">the Astor Place Cube</a>&#x2014;it&apos;s finally been put back where it belongs.</p>

<p>After two years in storage for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2014/11/25/bye_for_now_astor_cube.php">much-needed</a> repairs, The Cube&#x2014;which we consider one of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/20/alamo_returns_2016.php">more fascinating pieces of public art in the city</a>&#x2014; is being reinstalled at Astor Place today. It was removed for construction and cleaning in the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2014/10/15/astor_place_cube_poof.php">fall of 2014</a>; then it was supposed to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/19/the_astor_place_cube_will_be_back_o.php">come back in June</a>...and then was delayed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/26/fake-out_astor_place_cubes_return_w.php">until August</a>...and then nothing. </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170828030607if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/XOAgaKj6eak" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>But our urban public art nightmare is finally at an end. Daniel Leibel, a spokesperson for the Department of Design and Construction, chalked up the long-awaited return to &quot;logistics&quot; that went beyond the cube. &quot;We&apos;re not just doing the cube, there was an expansion of four major plazas: The Alamo plaza, the subway plaza, Peter Cooper triangle and the village plaza,&quot; he explained. &quot;And of course in collaboration in construction, balancing everything and making sure we have all the permits and we let the community know [what&apos;s going on], it takes time.&quot; </p>

<p>He added that the DDC was only responsible for when the Alamo returned, &quot;We just got the call this morning that it was coming back.&quot;</p>

<p>We&apos;ll update the post once the Cube is fully installed, but the real joy will come when a whole new generation of hammered NYU students gets to discover its unique charms at 3 a.m. on a restless fall evening. </p>
