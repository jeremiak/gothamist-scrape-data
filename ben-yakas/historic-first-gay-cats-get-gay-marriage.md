<p>Gov. Andrew Cuomo is hoping to bring <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130523111429/http://gothamist.com/2011/01/17/cuomo_cabinet_addition_brings_marri.php">gay marriage</a> to a vote by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130523111429/http://gothamist.com/2011/01/18/gay_marriage_heading_back_to_the_st.php">the summer</a>, but until then, maybe we can learn something from the cats. Bruno and Solomon, two male felines who reside at Ollie&apos;s Place, a no-kill cat shelter on E 26th Street, became the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130523111429/http://blogs.discovery.com/cat-scratchings/2011/01/new-york-city-shelter-announces-first-same-sex-cat-marriage.html">first same-sex married cat couple</a> this week!</p>

<p>Really, Ollie&apos;s Place wanted to ensure that the two cats didn&apos;t get separated by adoption, and to raise awareness for the cat shelter. Which doesn&apos;t necessarily explain why they needed their own <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130523111429/http://www.myregistry.com/visitors/giftlist.aspx?sid=fb880b64-b9d1-47e7-8a8e-1274589eacc0">gift registry</a>, but you know what? We&apos;ll go with it. All those gay animals who&apos;ve had to be content with domestic partnerships now have had their day. Hopefully Bruno and Solomon&apos;s love will last longer than <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130523111429/http://gothamist.com/2006/11/17/central_parks_g.php">Silo and Roy&apos;s.</a> Mike Birbiglia has his own idea as to how they knew the cats were gay:</p>
