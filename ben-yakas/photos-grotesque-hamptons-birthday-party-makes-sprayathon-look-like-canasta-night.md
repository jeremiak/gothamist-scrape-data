Between the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/27/rose_shortage.php"> Ros&#xE9; shortages</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://nymag.com/thecut/2016/08/botox-doctor-in-the-hamptons-gives-injections-in-his-gym.html">the botox crises</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/09/hamptons_may_descend_into_chaos_ove.php">helicopter bans</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://gothamist.com/2015/08/05/montauk_brodown_showdown.php">fornicating yupster Bros</a> and the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/07/sprayathon_ends_badly.php">condom-flinging #Sprayathon2016 hordes</a>, the sterling reputation of the Hamptons has really taken a hit in recent years. It makes sense that the few remaining hard-poloing, hard-partying millionaires trying to keep the dream of the Hamptons alive would resort to more and more extreme means to keep the riff raff out.</p>

<p>So it went for Sir Ivan Wilzig, a banker-turned-musician and philanthropist <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/confidential/hamptons-bash-drone-eye-party-guests-article-1.2762762">whom the News reports</a> celebrated his 60th birthday with a summertime &quot;Garden of Eden themed party&quot; at his 10-acre Hamptons estate. To really hammer home the seriousness of the soiree, Wilzig had drone security guards monitoring the 700+ guests he invited to his home, which has gained the reputation as being the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://www.businessinsider.com/ivan-wilzig-hamptons-photos-2011-7">&quot;Playboy Mansion of the Hamptons.&quot;</a> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/https://www.thrillist.com/vice/party-essentials-i-learned-at-sir-ivan-s-sex-dungeon">Thrillist once called it</a> &quot;a rich dude&apos;s sex dungeon mansion in the Hamptons.&quot;)</p>

<p>As Wilzig <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-3756759/Millionaire-philanthropist-s-security-team-used-DRONE-eye-guests-wild-60th-birthday-bash-castle-dubbed-Playboy-Mansion-Hamptons.html">comfortingly wrote</a> on Facebook, &quot;According to the Bible, God sees everything... But just in case he misses someone, I&apos;ve got my drones.&quot; The guests were apparently encouraged to frolic in their birthday suits in honor of the occasion (which was taking eight months after Wilzig&apos;s actual birthday, for those keeping track of such things), which to me would send up a lot of red flags about how much those drones were recording, but hey, my invitation was apparently lost in the mail, so what do I know? </p>

<p>Mostly, it sounds like a very fine way to spend a Saturday evening if you&apos;re into hairy-chested dudes who like to make &quot;pop-dance remakes of 1960s and early 1970s peace songs&quot; in their sweaty sex dungeons with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161230165244/http://www.vice.com/read/we-went-to-sir-ivans-hamptons-castle">&quot;bathrooms [dusted] with glitter and cocaine residue.&quot;</a> Don&apos;t believe me? Just check out Wikipedia&apos;s very unbiased (and very unattributed) writeup of the occasion:</p>

<blockquote>Sir Ivan is an unrepentant party animal. On August 20, 2016, he gave a lavish come-au-naturel party in Water Mill, NY. It was a birthday bash (his, naturally), and many of the 700-plus guests cavorted in their birthday suits, some enjoying their host&#x2019;s opulent house, others finding intimacy in the more bucolic setting of the 10-acre woods that surround it. The verdict? &#x201C;A Good Time Was Had by All.&#x201D;</blockquote>
