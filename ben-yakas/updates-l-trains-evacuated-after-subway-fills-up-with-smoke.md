<p><strong>Updates Below</strong> L train service between Brooklyn and 8th Avenue in Manhattan was suspended during this morning&apos;s rush hour after the tunnel under the East River&#x2014;and some train cars&#x2014;filled up with smoke. But of course, even an entire station filled with smoke isn&apos;t enough to scare off some New Yorkers who insisted on waiting <em>just in case</em> the train came.</p>

<p>The FDNY says they were called about the smoke condition around 9:20 a.m. Thursday morning, and a spokesperson confirmed they are on the scene investigating reports of a fire, originating somewhere around 1st Avenue and 14th Street. As a result, there are no L trains currently running between Lorimer Street and 8th Avenue.</p>

<p>No injuries have been reported at this time.</p>

<p>One 31-year-old Williamsburg resident who was on his way to work aboard one of the smoke-filled trains that was evacuated, and told us what it was like down there: &quot;I was in the last car of the train,&quot; he said. &quot;We were getting close to the 1st Avenue stop and the car started to smell of burning and then filled with smoke. It was right at the level where everyone was very nervous but no one was visibly freaking out.&quot;</p>

<p>&quot;We pulled into the station and someone yelled, &apos;Everyone get off this train,&apos;&quot; he continued. &quot;But since I&apos;m in the last car, there was an endless mass of people and distance between us and the exit, and there&apos;s a lot of smoke in the station. It was very claustrophobic...People started yelling at the crowd to walk faster.&quot;</p>
