<p>A 23-year-old Florida man was fatally struck by a train on the Upper West Side early this morning. According to police, 23-year-old Bobby Legrand Guin was struck by a northbound local A train at the Cathedral Parkway-110th Street station around 5:15 a.m. </p>

<p>Guin was taken to St. Luke&apos;s Hospital where he was pronounced dead. Police couldn&apos;t say how or why Guin, who was from Mary Esther, Florida, ended up on the tracks. The investigation is still ongoing, and we&apos;ll update as more information becomes available.</p>

<p><strong>Update</strong>: Although police couldn&apos;t confirm these details with us, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130510145827/http://online.wsj.com/article/AP80b21b3f44b443f181f736db4a7ff506.html">Wall Street Journal</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130510145827/http://www.nbcnews.com/id/51433453/ns/local_news-new_york_ny/#.UV3q-qvuVg8">NBC</a> claim that Guin had jumped down onto the subway tracks to retrieve some $20 bills that were lying there. Police did add that no criminality is suspected in the incident. </p>
