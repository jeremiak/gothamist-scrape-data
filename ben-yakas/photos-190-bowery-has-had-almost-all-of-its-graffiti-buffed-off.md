					<p>Last year, the landmarked graffiti-covered mansion at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031055921/http://gothamist.com/tags/190bowery">190 Bowery</a>, aka the historic Germania Bank Building, was sold <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031055921/http://gothamist.com/2015/02/07/landmarked_bowery_mansion_sold_for.php">for $55 million</a> (photographer and longtime owner Jay Maisel had purchased it for a mere $102,000 in 1966). With plans in place to turn it into high-end office space, the new owners <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031055921/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/10/photos_bowery_officially_stripped_o.php#photo-1">began the process</a> of buffing the iconic street art from the building (which just suffered from a minor fire <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031055921/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/08/190_bowery_fire_2016.php">earlier this month</a>). And now, that process has almost been completed, with 80-90% of the street art buffed.</p>

<p>You can still see some of the art up close, but it has become very faded, and will only continue to fade into artistic oblivion&#x2014;currently the new white-ish sheen is an odd sight after decades covered in dirt, and makes it look like it&apos;s seasick. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031055921/http://ny.curbed.com/2016/8/25/12639362/190-bowery-graffiti-clean-aby-rosen">Curbed points out</a> that the one major piece still standing is a tag from NEKST, the late street artist Sean Griffin, whose name has adorned the building since 2007. So it seems that new owner Aby Rosen, who <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031055921/http://massappeal.com/all-the-graffiti-on-190-bowery-getting-buffed-except-for-nekst-rip/">had previously promised</a> to at least keep the NEKST tag, is making good on that. For now, crews appear to be taking a break from cleaning the building to deal with construction on the first floor.</p>

<p>Click through to check out photos of the newly-scrubbed building, along with some flashback shots of 190 Bowery earlier this summer, before it had its soul scrubbed. And below, you can watch a video detailing the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031055921/http://gothamist.com/2014/12/01/190_bowery_uugghh.php">moment in 2014</a> when artist Eric Rieger, aka HOTTEA, put up a piece reading &quot;UUGGHH&quot; around the building in response to the then-imminent sale.</p>

<p>As for getting a peak inside before it is totally converted, security is still pretty tight, and keeping an eye out for photographers. In other words, as with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031055921/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/17/scenes_from_190_bowery.php#photo-1">previous false starts</a>, don&apos;t hold your breath about seeing the inner workings for yourself.</p>
