					<p>Whether they are judging <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170219223130/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/12/judgmental_manhattan_subway_map.php">Manhattan neighborhoods</a>, exploring <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170219223130/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/12/subway_desert_map.php">subway deserts</a>, offering a glimpse into the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170219223130/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/15/map_of_1926_manhattan.php">city&apos;s past</a>, or turning the five boroughs into <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170219223130/http://gothamist.com/2011/08/31/map_the_five_boroughs_meet_mariolan.php">Super Mario Land</a>, we can&apos;t get enough of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170219223130/http://gothamist.com/tags/map">maps</a> reinterpreting how we look at the city. A new book out this week has assembled an impressive gallery of hundreds of them to give readers a strange and wondrous view of NYC&apos;s past, present and imagined future.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170219223130/https://www.facebook.com/mapasart/">Katharine Harmon</a>&apos;s <em>You Are Here: NYC: Mapping the Soul of the City</em> collects 200 maps created between 1600 and 2015 that show the myriad number of ways one could begin to approach &quot;mapping the soul&quot; of NYC. Peruse a number of highlights from the book above, including Stanley Donwood&apos;s &quot;Metallic Manhattan,&quot; which was inspired by roadside advertising and was used by Radiohead for their album <em>Hail To The Thief.</em> There&apos;s also the first sketch of Maira Kalman and Rick Meyerowitz&apos;s now-classic &quot;Newyorkistan,&quot; which eventually appeared on the cover the <em>The New Yorker</em>.</p>

<p>There&apos;s more whimsical fare like Liz Hickok&apos;s jelly-based moldings of lower Manhattan, Katarina Jerinic&apos;s &quot;Brooklyn Constellations&quot; (Jerinic used the Yellow Pages to create an astronomy of the borough with places like Star Sporting Goods, Planet Kidz Fulton and other such star/planet establishments) and Inaki Aliste Lizarralde&apos;s deep dive into the &quot;Apartments of Chandler + Joey and Monica + Rachel.&quot; Then on the historical side, there&apos;s everything from John Cage&apos;s waltz map commemorating <em>Rolling Stone&apos;s</em> move from San Francisco to New York, to Henry Wellge&apos;s &quot;Greatest New York&quot; lithograph from 1911.</p>

<p>The maps in the gallery above are just skimming the surface of Harmon&apos;s book&#x2014;if you want to check out Andy Warhol&apos;s Central Park map, Christopher Mason&apos;s subway bacteria map, and the marvelous Manhattan Pizza Delivery Routes map, <em>You Are Here: NYC, Mapping The Soul Of The City</em> is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170219223130/http://www.mcnallyjackson.com/book/9781616895266">available now</a>.</p>
