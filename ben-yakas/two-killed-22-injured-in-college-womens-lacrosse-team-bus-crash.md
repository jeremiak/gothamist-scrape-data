title: Two Killed, 22 Injured In College Women's Lacrosse Team Bus Crash
---

<p>Two people were killed and 22 others were injured after a bus carrying the Seton Hill University women&apos;s lacrosse team <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130605041949/http://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation/2013/03/16/tour-bus-crashes-in-pa-serious-injuries-reported/1992577/">crashed on a highway in central Pennsylvania today</a>. The bus, which was carrying 23 Seton Hill lacrosse players and three coaches, crashed into a tree after careening off the Pennsylvania Turnpike around 9 a.m. this morning. The bus driver and one passenger were killed in the crash, according to Pennsylvania Turnpike spokeswoman Renee Colborn. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130605041949/http://cumberlink.com/news/local/tour-bus-carrying-seton-hall-lacrosse-team-crashes-on-turnpike/article_8dca4356-8e41-11e2-920b-0019bb2963f4.html?comment_form=true">One of the people killed</a> was 30-year-old coach Kristina J. Quigley, who was six-months pregnant. </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130605041949if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/U0qHEUayKig" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The other person killed was 61-year-old driver Anthony M. Gueaetta. At least three people were taken by helicopter to the hospital (including Quigley), according to Penn State Hershey Medical Center spokeswoman Danielle Ran. Police are still investigating what may have led to the crash. The bus operator, Mlaker Charter &amp; Tours, of Davidsville, Pa., has also sent investigators to the scene. Seton Hill, a Catholic university located near Pittsburgh, was set to play at Millersville University, outside Lancaster, PA, today.</p>
