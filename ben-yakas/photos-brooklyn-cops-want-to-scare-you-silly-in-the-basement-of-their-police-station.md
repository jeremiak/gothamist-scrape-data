					<p>Last year, the officers at Brooklyn&apos;s 78th Precinct stationhouse in Prospect Heights took a cue from one of the Halloween episodes of <em>Brooklyn Nine-Nine</em> and turned their precinct basement into a house of (non criminal) horrors. But they were just getting warmed up: as you can see in the photos above and video below, they&apos;ve upped the ante this year with an even spookier, more impressive array of scares awaiting all who dare descend their steps. </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102013309if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/ihpzAB7KPq4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Last year <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102013309/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/29/brooklyn_cop_haunted_house.php#photo-1">when we took a tour</a> of the basement, several officers explained why the precinct jumped at the chance to host a haunted house for adults and children: &quot;The reason we&apos;re doing it is 90 percent of people come into a police precinct for bad things, they don&apos;t come in for good things,&quot; said Captain Frank DiGiacomo, the Precinct&apos;s commanding officer. &quot;So we want to get the kids and teenagers&#x2014;come meet the police, come into the station house for something good like a haunted house and meet the police officers...who&apos;ve given their own time to scare them and introduce and meet them.&quot;</p>
