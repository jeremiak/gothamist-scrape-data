					<p>Looking past the onslaught of nausea brought about by the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170124151320/http://dcist.com/2017/01/donald_trump_inauguration_live.php#photo-1">inauguration of Donald Trump</a> this weekend, you may be looking for some sort of artistic counter-programming. It is especially important in light of the fact that Trump and his fascistic cronies/Russian handlers have already insinuated that they want to eliminate <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170124151320/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/19/shrink_and_drown_in_bathtub.php">various major arts endowments</a>. If you&apos;re looking for an exhibit that is counter to everything Trump stands for, we heartily recommend checking out Marilyn Minter&apos;s &apos;Pretty/Dirty&apos; retrospective, now at the Brooklyn Museum.</p>

<p>According <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170124151320/https://www.brooklynmuseum.org/exhibitions/marilyn_minter_pretty_dirty?gclid=Cj0KEQiA5IHEBRCLr_PZvq2_6qcBEiQAL4cQ001cxFtpZLg87cU0EY2SYmRlWcxcgDL6LFyvDsl5Z_IaAmdL8P8HAQ">to the museum</a>, Minter&#x2019;s sensual paintings, photographs, and videos &quot;vividly explore complex and contradictory emotions around beauty and the feminine body in American culture. She trains a critical eye on the power of desire, questioning the fashion industry&#x2019;s commercialization of sex and the body.&quot; The retrospective includes pieces from over 40 years of work, including the intersection of food and sex, a photo series on her drug-addicted mother, and work that confronts the manipulative nature of the fashion industry.</p>

<p>You can watch a short film on the exhibit below.</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170124151320if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/qMNGNn6CdwY" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Minter talked with Madonna about the exhibit, art, growing up in NYC, feminism, and the current state of the country at the Brooklyn Museum <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170124151320/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/13/madonna_marilyn_minter_brooklyn_museum.php">on Thursday night</a>. Unsurprisingly, there was much talk <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170124151320/http://pagesix.com/2017/01/20/madonna-on-trump-we-have-gone-as-low-as-we-can-go/?_ga=1.257022984.20992601.1472994863">about Trump</a>: &#x201C;This is the most frightened I&#x2019;ve ever been,&#x201D; Minter said. &quot;The most qualified candidate who ever ran was defeated by the most unqualified candidate who ever ran, and it&#x2019;s all because of misogyny.&#x201D;</p>

<p>Madonna tried to find a silver lining: &quot;He&#x2019;s actually doing us a great service, because we have gone as low as we can go,&#x201D; she said. &#x201C;We can only go up from here, so what are we going to do? We have two choices, destruction and creation. I chose creation.&#x201D; You can watch their whole talk below.</p>
