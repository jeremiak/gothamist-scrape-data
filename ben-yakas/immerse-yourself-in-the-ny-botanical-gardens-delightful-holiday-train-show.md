					<p>The New York Botanical Garden&apos;s marvelous annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161205153253/http://gothamist.com/tags/NYBG">Holiday Train Show</a> has come around the bend and pulled into the NYBG station for another year. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="111516train1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161205153253im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/byakas/111516train1.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> Sai Mokhtari/Gothamist</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Our favorite model train exhibition features recreations of New York landmarks (including Brooklyn Bridge, City Hall, Rockefeller Center and the Guggenheim Museum) made out of twigs, bark, fruit, seeds, pinecones and more, designed by Paul Busse&apos;s team at Applied Imagination. </p>

<p>This year&apos;s show includes the exhibition&apos;s first roller coaster, the Coney Island Cyclone, as well as Coney Island&apos;s Wonder Wheel and Elephantine Colossus; a new replica of the Ed Koch Queensboro Bridge; and a new book, &quot;The Holiday Train Show: A City Within a City.&quot;</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="111516train2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161205153253im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/byakas/111516train2.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> Sai Mokhtari/Gothamist</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The show opens to the public on November 19th and will be at NYBG until January 16th. Check out a video below of some of the new sights.</p>
