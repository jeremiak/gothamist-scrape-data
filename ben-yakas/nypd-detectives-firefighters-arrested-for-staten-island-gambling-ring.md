<p>Federal investigators <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110606124919/http://www.ny1.com/content/top_stories/140220/city-cops--firefighters-among-15-charged-in-gambling-roundup">have charged</a> 14 people in connection with a Staten Island gambling ring, including at least one active NYPD detective and two city firefighters. In addition to the three active duty public officers, a retired NYPD cop and firefighter were also arrested.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110606124919/http://www.nydailynews.com/news/ny_crime/2011/06/02/2011-06-02_nypd_cop_2_fdny_firefighters_busted_in_illegal_gambling_ring_on_staten_island_.html">Among those</a> arrested were detective Richard Palase, who is assigned to the Criminal Justice Bureau in Brooklyn, and firefighter Michael Bergen&#x2014;they were charged with conspiracy to operate an illegal card game at on Arthur Kill Road. Firefighter Gerald parsons was charged with working as a dealer at card games held at a parlor on Victory Boulevard.  There were at least four gambling parlors scattered around the borough.</p>

<p>Also arrested were retired NYPD cop Ralph Mastrantonio and Bergen&apos;s father, James, who is a retired firefighter. Authorities said they didn&apos;t believe the ring was connected to organized crime; they also wouldn&apos;t say how much money was being earned by the operation, but one source <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110606124919/http://www.silive.com/news/index.ssf/2011/06/15_arrested_in_staten_island_g.html">told SILive</a>: &quot;My sense is that it was a lot of money. Some of the places were operating almost every night.&quot; </p>

Is the Academy giving lessons on how to shoot your foot?  I ask because We&apos;re pretty fvcking good at it.&quot;</p>
