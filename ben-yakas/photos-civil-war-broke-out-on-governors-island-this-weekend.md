<p>This weekend, Governor&apos;s Island was host to the annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150110122041/http://gothamist.com/tags/civilwarweekend">Civil War Weekend</a>&#x2014;historians portraying various units from the Civil War (including Confederate prisoners, music boys, laundresses, and officer&apos;s wives) gave participants a glimpse of what life was like on the island during the bloodiest conflict in America&apos;s history. From 1783 through 1966, the island <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150110122041/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governors_Island#18th_and_19th_centuries">was used as an army post</a>, and during the Civil War it held Confederate prisoners. Click through to check out some photos from Saturday.</p>
