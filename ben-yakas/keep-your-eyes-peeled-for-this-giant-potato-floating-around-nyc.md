<p><br>
&quot;Potato barge&quot; may sound like the name of an obscure B-52s song, but it&apos;s also something that is actually (sort of) floating around NYC today. Multiple people have spotted what appears to be a six-ton spud that has been hoisted upon a tractor trailer on a barge and is being pulled by a tugboat in the waterways around New York.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105107/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Giant-Potato-Hudson-River-New-York-City--391173392.html?_osource=SocialFlowTwt_NYBrand">According to NBC</a>, the &quot;potato&quot; has been making cross-country trips since 2012, when the Idaho Potato Commission saw the potatotality of ways to celebrate the agency&apos;s 75th anniversary. (Although our hearts want to believe this really is a giant potato that would make 30,325 servings of mashed potatoes, our eyes are not fooled&#x2014;this potato is made of steel, plywood, foams and concrete. You can learn about <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105107/https://idahopotato.com/dr-potato/dr-potato-2015-is-it-real-what-does-the-great-big-idaho-potato-truck-spud-really-look-like-without-its-clothes">the process of how they made it here</a>)</p>

<p>They&apos;ve sent the faux potato, which is the equivalent of 32,346 medium-sized Idaho potatoes (and 1,102 times heavier than the largest potato ever grown), across the country every year since 2012&#x2014;we imagine it took a lot of hashing it out to be able to get it to NYC, but thankfully, a latke hard work was put in to get it here. No second tots about it.</p>
