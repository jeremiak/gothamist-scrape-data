<p>Obscure Wings <em>bassist</em> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117214738/http://gothamist.com/tags/paulmccartney">Sir Paul McCartney</a> played the first of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117214738/http://gothamist.com/2011/06/08/paul_mccartney_to_play_yankee_stadi.php">two massive shows</a> at Yankees Stadium last night. McCartney serenaded the crowd with a 35-song set&#x2014;you can see the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117214738/http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2011/07/paul_mccartney_20.html">full setlist here</a>. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117214738/http://www.nytimes.com/2011/07/17/arts/music/paul-mccartney-yankee-stadium-concert-review.html">NY Times called</a> the show &quot;a gentle reminder of his survival and vitality.&quot; They also noted that he got in a few relevant baseball jokes: &#x201C;Who is this Derek Jeter guy?&#x201D; Mr. McCartney joshed. &#x201C;Somebody said he&#x2019;s got more hits than me.&#x201D;</p>

<p>Last night was McCartney&apos;s debut at the stadium&#x2014;previously, he had played at CitiField in 2009, and Shea Stadium in 1965 at a legendary concert (which you can <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117214738/http://www.veoh.com/watch/v6413373fzZAYjdK?h1=The+Beatles+Concert+at+Shea+Stadium+1965+(entire+show+%2B+bonus)">watch here</a>). Below you can see/hear a couple highlights of the show, including a fireworks display during &quot;Live And Let Die,&quot; the John Lennon tribute &quot;Here Today,&quot; the premiere of neglected Beatles tune &quot;The Night Before,&quot; and a rocking &quot;Band On The Run.&quot;</p>
