<p>Today was the fifth annual national <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231358/http://gothamist.com/tags/toplessday">Go Topless Day</a>, a celebration of women&apos;s legal right to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231358/http://gothamist.com/tags/topless">bare their breasts in public</a> in NYC. Lots of topless women and men hung out around <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231358/http://gothamist.com/2012/08/26/nsfw_topless_women_invading_times_s.php">Bryant Park and Times Square</a> all afternoon&#x2014;as one participant told us, &quot;Rockin&apos; your titties in New York City is a 24 hour job.&quot; Unfortunately, the rally attracted a lot of ogling onlookers as well, who seemed to go against the spirit of the whole event. </p>

<p>According to photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503231358/http://colormekatie.blogspot.com/">Katie Sokoler</a>, who took the lovely photos above, there were less than 20 women participating in the event at Bryant Park&#x2014;but there were 10 times as many men hanging out, taking photos, and generally being disrespectful. &quot;They would just swarm a girl and get right up in her boobs to take close ups,&quot; she said. &quot;Three girls got so angry, they screamed, &apos;They&apos;re just fucking boobs!&apos; and left.&quot;</p>

<p>Although there were shirtless men there laying in the grass, they were left alone for the most part. &quot;People wouldnt even ask to take a picture of the women,&quot; Sokoler continued. &quot;They would just be surrounded by 50 dudes taking pictures of their boobs. And that&apos;s why although it&apos;s legal to be topless, it&apos;s not equal.&quot; </p>
