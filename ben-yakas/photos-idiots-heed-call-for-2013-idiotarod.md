</div></div>					<p><br>
Last year&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180553/http://gothamist.com/tags/idiotarod">Idiotarod</a> was by all accounts the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180553/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/29/2012_idiotarod_needed_more_idiots.php#photo-1">&quot;worst Idiotarod ever,&quot;</a> an event so hampered by &quot;wack ass&quot; routes and low-interest that it almost brought the whole enterprise to an end. But after being reorganized <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180553/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/19/idiotarod_returns_next_week_under_n.php">under new management</a> and with a renewed sense of purpose (Hurricane Sandy relief), we can report that this year&apos;s Idiotarod bounced back just fine from last year&apos;s nadir.</p>

<p>Despite the cold weather, a large amount of people turned out for this year&apos;s event. A lot of awards were given out too (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180553/https://www.facebook.com/events/543034852373383">see them all here</a>), including Best Costumes to &quot;Game of Thongs,&quot; Best Cultural Reference to &quot;Pac-Man,&quot; Lifetime Achievement Award to &quot;Fake Work,&quot; and Best In Show to &quot;Disasterpiece Knight Shift.&quot;</p>

<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180553/http://www.todseelie.com/">Tod Seelie</a> noted that the team Skulleaters took second place with their impressive &quot;four-cart creation with a working wood stove in it.&quot; He also gave this report on the event: &quot;I think less than 15 teams showed up. Most people blame last year&apos;s fiasco for driving people away. But this year&apos;s organizers made it a friendlier race for that very reason. The route started at the Brooklyn Bridge Park and wound its way through Carroll Gardens, Red Hook and Gowanus.&quot;</p>

<p>Click through to see all those costumes and more; and later, we&apos;ll have a separate post with photos and videos from the firey post-race party. </p>
