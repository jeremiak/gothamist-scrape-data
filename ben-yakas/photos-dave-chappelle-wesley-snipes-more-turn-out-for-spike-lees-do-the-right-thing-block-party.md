Dave Chappelle, Wesley Snipes, Yasiin Bey (Mos Def), Chuck D, Erykah Badu, and more stopped by Bed-Stuy on Saturday to join Spike Lee in celebrating the twenty-fifth anniversary of <em>Do The Right Thing</em> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150425175255/http://gothamist.com/2014/06/24/on_the_steps_behind_brooklyn.php">at a giant summer block party</a>. Thousands of people came out to the area around Stuyvesant and Lexington Avenues, the same Brooklyn neighborhood where it was filmed: &quot;Oh my God I&#x2019;m gonna be on YouTube tonight,&quot; Chappelle told the crowd. &#x201C;I haven&#x2019;t seen this many black people in nine years, ladies and gentleman.&quot; Check out some videos below.</p>

<p>&quot;It&#x2019;s good to see,&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150425175255/http://theurbandaily.com/2014/06/28/dave-chappelle-spike-lee-do-the-right-thing/">Chappelle added</a>. &quot;My God, Brooklyn has changed. Just a few short years ago I was in this very neighborhood...horrified! And now I just love. God bless you all and thank you for welcoming me.&quot;</p>

<p> According to photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150425175255/http://ellenmoynihanphoto.com/home.html">Ellen Moynihan</a>, who took the photos above, it was hard to get around the area: &quot;My lord, it was packed. Like sardines. It could take five minutes to get through the crowds to cross the block.&quot; She added that there were lots of families around, and Lee was roaming the crowd, signing books, t-shirts and other items for fans.</p>
