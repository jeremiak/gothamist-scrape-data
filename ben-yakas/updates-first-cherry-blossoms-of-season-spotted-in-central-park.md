<p>[Updates Below] It has been a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140006/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=frt_f0eP_Hs&amp;feature=kp">brutal winter</a>, one which truly pushed Gothamist writers to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140006/http://gothamist.com/tags/winter">the brink of insanity</a> (guzzling icy blood from a snowman&apos;s skull will do that to you). Thankfully, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140006/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/20/spring_is_supposedly_here.php">thongs are out</a> now, and the temperatures will remain exceedingly pleasant this week, in the mid 50s to low 60s. Another sign of spring this week: the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140006/http://gothamist.com/tags/cherryblossoms">cherry blossoms</a> are coming!</p>

<p>Brooklyn Botanic Garden currently estimates that the first cherry blossoms will bloom sometime this week. If you&apos;re like us, then you&apos;ll be checking their handy <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140006/http://www.bbg.org/discover/cherries">CherryWatch Blossom Status Map</a> to keep apprised of any bloom. Once the blooming has started, the season lasts for about five weeks. </p>
