</div></div>					<p>In case you couldn&apos;t tell from the first <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150131042114/http://gothamist.com/2011/01/28/photos_2011_high_line_snow_sculpt-o.php">two</a> or <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150131042114/http://gothamist.com/2011/01/27/photos_venus_de_snowmilo_and_friend.php">three</a> times we mentioned it, we love snowmen. Maybe we saw the Rankin/Bass &quot;Frosty the Snowman&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150131042114/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frosty_the_Snowman_%28TV_program%29">TV special</a> one too many times when we were kids, or maybe we&apos;re just trying to purge ourselves of our memories of Michael Keaton&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150131042114/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Frost_%281998_film%29">portrayal</a> of the white wizard. Either way, who could resist the charms of Tyrannosnowus Rex in Union Square Park, the massive cone-headed snowman at Essex St., or the 15-foot snowdude Nanook, who was constructed by 18 random strangers near the Heckscher Playground near Columbus Circle at the southwest corner of Central Park. </p>
