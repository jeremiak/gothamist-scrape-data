<p>Getting Bar/Bat Mitzvah&apos;d occurs when a young Jewish person turns 13&#x2014;but thankfully for us, trend pieces about Bark Mitzvahs happen every six years or less! A <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119105827/http://judaism.about.com/od/americanjewry/a/bark_mitzvah.htm">Bark Mitzvah</a>, of course, is the &quot;pseudo-traditional observance and celebration of a dog&apos;s coming of age.&quot; The NY Times drank from the Manischewitz bowl <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119105827/http://gothamist.com/2004/12/20/the_chosen_dog.php">for a feature piece in 2004</a>, Dr. Ruth <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119105827/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9fKGljjmC7k">helped Elvis Best</a> have his, and now the AP has video of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119105827/http://www.theawl.com/2012/01/dogs-think-theyre-jewish-people">one NJ family embracing it.</a> So below, enjoy dogs in yarmulkes, and just imagine what <em>Party Down</em> could have done <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119105827/http://www.catersource.com/resources/library/caterer-profiles/catering-by-max-goes-to-the-dogs">if they had been given a third season.</a></p>
