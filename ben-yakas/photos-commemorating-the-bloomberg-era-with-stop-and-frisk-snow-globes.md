</div></div>					<p>Old school blogger <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150422150354/https://twitter.com/newyorkshitty">Miss Heather</a> has been running <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150422150354/http://www.newyorkshitty.com/">New York Shitty</a> for over seven years, documenting life in Greenpoint during the Bloomberg era. Now that he&apos;s getting ready to go to the big Bermuda in the sky, Miss Heather is commemorating the time period with a series of NYC-centric snow globes that depict Bloombergian staples such as stop-and-frisk, urban displacement, SantaCon, topless women, mass arrests, construction debris, and more.</p>

<p>The result is Urban Renewal, a line of hand-crafted snow globes from places people do not want to go to. She explained <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150422150354/http://www.flickr.com/photos/missheather/sets/72157637747975843">the project so</a>: </p>

<blockquote>In essence, my plan was to explore dystopias in small(ish) hand held form. I hit a brick wall and tabled this project.

<p>Recently I had a breakthrough. Here it is very simplistically stated:</p>

<p>1. in just over a month the Bloomberg Administration will be over.<br>
2. Mayor Mike&apos;s New York City is *quite* dystopic.<br>
3. I need only leave my apartment, parse through the photos I have taken over the years or simply surf the Internet to find inspiration/material.</p>

<p>Consider these to be souvenirs of /a testament to what 12 years of Bloomturd have done to my community. NOTE: this could not have happened without enablers. These snow globes are dedicated to them.</p></blockquote><p></p>

<p>Miss Heather told us the stop-and-frisk one is her favorite, and the one she is most looking forward to completing is the SantaCon one &quot;for obvious reasons.&quot;</p>
