<p>Coldplay&apos;s Chris Martin turned Mercury Lounge into his own private elevator last night when he performed a surprise solo show for Manhattan homeless shelter <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161225004525/https://www.bowery.org/">the Bowery Mission,</a> and it happened to be the most mind-blowingly <em>basic</em> music experience in NYC history. </p>

<p>Before we go any further, we must establish a few things: 1. Performing for charity is always a good and honorable thing, and Martin is nothing if not <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161225004525/https://www.looktothestars.org/celebrity/chris-martin">a charitable musician</a> who does a lot of good for people; 2. At some point in your life, you heard a Coldplay song softly strummed in the background of a commercial or a hotel lobby and you didn&apos;t run away, you actually lingered there a moment longer, because even the most vehement Coldplay-hater has one Coldplay song they secretly enjoy (FULL DISCLOSURE: mine is probably &quot;God Put A Smile On Your Face&quot; or &quot;The Scientist,&quot; because nobody said it was easy to be alive); 3. Chris Martin <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161225004525/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/22/watch_coldplays_full_game_of_throne.php#photo-1">loves <em>Game Of Thrones</em></a>, doesn&apos;t trash talk his exes, and generally seems to be a likable and well-adjusted human being and father. </p>

<p>HAVING SAID THAT. Let&apos;s say you are musician Chris Martin, the guy with a voice like fuzzy slippers who vacations <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161225004525/http://mix949.com/jay-z-and-chris-martin-to-vacation-together-with-their-families-this-summer/">with Jay Z</a>. You are rich, you are successful, you wear a lot of bracelets, you are musically-talented (enough)&#x2014;you can play any song you want for charity from any band you love (including your own). What do you choose?</p>

<p>Motherfucking &quot;Yesterday.&quot; </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161225004525if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/qEM8nYDs6X8" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>You want to deliver a tribute to Leonard Cohen, awesome! Just don&apos;t play &quot;Hallelujah.&quot; No one wants to hear <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161225004525/http://www.newsweek.com/60-versions-leonard-cohens-hallelujah-ranked-303580">yet another version</a> of &quot;Hallelujah.&quot; Pick literally any other Cohen song, it would be amazing. We know you can play <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161225004525/http://ew.com/article/2016/11/12/chris-martin-leonard-cohen-cover-coldplay/">other Cohen songs</a>. Don&apos;t do it Chris. Don&apos;t do it.</p>

<p>God damnit.</p>
