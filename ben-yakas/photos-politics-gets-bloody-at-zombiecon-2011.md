<p>It&apos;s been a busy weekend filled with lots of exciting events, with the Occupy Wall Street <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111017171003/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/15/live_video_stream_occupy_wall_stree_1.php#photo-1">march on Times Square</a>, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111017171003/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/16/photos_creators_project_takes_over.php">Creators Project in DUMBO</a>, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111017171003/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/16/photos_the_best_costumes_from_the_2.php#photo-1">Comic Con 2011</a>. But there was also room for one of our favorite events yesterday: the seventh annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111017171003/http://gothamist.com/tags/zombiecon">Zombiecon</a>! Though <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111017171003/http://www.zombiecon.com/">this year&apos;s theme</a> was &quot;A Game of Brains&quot; (influenced by <em>Game Of Thrones</em> ostensibly), the bloody participants struck a decidedly political tone&#x2014;and ultimately, many of the zombies made their way to Times Square to join up with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111017171003/http://gothamist.com/tags/occupywallstreet">OWS</a> protesters.
