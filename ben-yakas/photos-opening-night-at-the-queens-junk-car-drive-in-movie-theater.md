                            <p>This weekend, our dreams of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205202207/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/17/why_dont_we_have_outdoor_movies_in.php">watching outdoor movies</a> at a drive-in movie theater in the five boroughs was realized when the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205202207/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/19/junk_car_drive-in_coming_flushing_m.php">Empire Drive-In kicked off</a>. Empire Drive-In is a large-scale temporary drive-in theater installation built from junked cars and salvaged materials. Above, check out some photos by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205202207/http://blog.suckapants.com/">Tod Seelie</a> of the opening night of the event.</p>

<p>In an evening programmed by programmed by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205202207/http://01sj.org/2010/artists/chandler/">Todd Chandler</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205202207/http://jeffstark.org/portfolio/home.html">Jeff Stark</a>, Deerhoof drummer Greg Saunier and 30 pianists (playing old Casio keyboards) performed pieces for a three silent films and Jim Jarmusch&#x2019;s <em>Night on Earth</em> on Friday. The events (held outside the New York Hall of Science in Flushing Meadows Corona Park) will run through October 20th (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205202207/http://empiredrivein.com/empire-drive-in-queens/">see the full lineup here</a>), and cost $15 a ticket.</p>
