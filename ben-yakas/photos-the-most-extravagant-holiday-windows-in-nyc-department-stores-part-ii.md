					<p>Yesterday, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101224154/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/30/holiday_window_displays.php#photo-1">we took a close look</a> at this year&apos;s ambitious and artistic holiday windows at Bloomingdales, Bergdorf Goodman and Barneys. Up above, you can feast your eyes on what three more of the big department stores&#x2014;Macy&apos;s, Saks Fifth Avenue and Lord &amp; Taylor&#x2014;created to send you into the most pleasant consumerist fugue state imaginable.  </p>

<p>The comforting days of seeing <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101224154/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/24/photos_holiday_windows_are_here_to.php#photo-44">Snoopy, Charlie Brown and all their friends</a> in the Macy&apos;s windows have unfortunately passed into history, and this year&apos;s display seemed a bit less focused than previous years, with some general Christmas tropes but no clear overall cohesion. Lord &amp; Taylor&apos;s had the cutest display, with lots of cartoon animals. (They even added squirrels to the lights outside to complement the ones inside some of the windows.)</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101224154if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/eMTnkLv1HvA" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Saks Fifth Avenue had the biggest production of all the stores, with a mix of &quot;holiday&quot; windows under the theme &quot;Land of 1000 Delights&quot; featuring lots of intricate moving parts, as well as &quot;fashion&quot; windows featuring mannequins displaying their latest wares (they are still a store, after all). It is, overall, more refreshing and fun than last year&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101224154/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/24/photos_holiday_windows_are_here_to.php#photo-44">dark &quot;Winter Palace&quot; theme.</a><br>

<iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101224154if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/44dTX2XdUZs" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>And as a bonus: if you are wondering what the single weirdest holiday display window in NYC is, look no further than the Christmas In New York store on Fifth Avenue, which seems to depict a strange sex act between a young boy and his teddy bear. </p>
