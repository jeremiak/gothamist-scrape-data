The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150425231556/http://gothamist.com/tags/mets">Mets</a> iconic theme song clearly states that you are &quot;guaranteed to have a heck of a day&quot; when you go to the stadium to watch them play, which is tantamount to a legally binding contract in our eyes. And the team made good on that promise last night (even though Matt Harvey didn&apos;t pitch!) as it invited sleepy fans to Citi Field for the first ever <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150425231556/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/25/spend_the_night_at_citi_field.php">giant slumber party</a> hosted by Mr. and Mrs. Met. </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20150425231556if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/UsZTvLaAphE" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>There were <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150425231556/https://twitter.com/Jay_HorwitzPR/status/366278760783294465">togas</a>, a dinner buffet, free tickets to a future game (the September 28th home game against the Brewers), and reportedly, Edgardo Alfonzo&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150425231556/https://twitter.com/Jay_HorwitzPR/status/366217660939239426">blue and orange Mets pajamas.</a> After they watched the Mets <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150425231556/http://scores.espn.go.com/mlb/recap?gameId=330810129">beat the Arizona Diamondbacks 4-1</a> (in Arizona), fans got to see <em>Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs</em> on the big screen in center field. </p>

<p>About 400 people attended overall according to the Mets, and it seems like fans had a good time&#x2014;and hopefully this guarantees that at least 400 people will attend that late season game against the Brewers!</p>
