<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150420111943/http://gothamist.com/tags/occupywallstreet">Occupy Wall Street</a> is well into its third week now, and the protesters finally have a meme worthy of their cause: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150420111943/http://twitter.com/#!/search?q=%23OccupySesameStreet">Occupy Sesame Street</a>. The hashtag <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150420111943/http://twitter.com/#!/nonstopdemo/status/119855033675292672/photo/1">was first started</a> on Twitter last week during the whole <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150420111943/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/02/alleged_radiohead_imposter_hoax_was.php">Radiohead hoax saga</a>, but has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150420111943/http://www.buzzfeed.com/jpmoore/the-13-best-tweets-from-occupysesamestreet">really taken off</a> this week with everyone from comedian Patton Oswalt to the Daily Caller&apos;s CJ Ciaramella getting in on tweet action. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150420111943/http://motherjones.com/mixed-media/2011/10/best-occupysesamestreet">Mother Jones has a breakdown</a> of how the meme spread; below, you can check out some of our favorite tweets about counting vampires, Cookie distribution inequality, and wages at Mr. Hooper&apos;s store.</p>
