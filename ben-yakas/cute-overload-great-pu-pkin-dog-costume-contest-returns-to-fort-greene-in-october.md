					<p>The Tompkins Square Dog Halloween Parade isn&apos;t the only very important must-attend animal costume contest in town <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171023025615/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/29/tompkins_square_dog_costumes.php#photo-1">this fall</a>. There&apos;s also The 18th Annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171023025615/http://www.fortgreenepups.org/site_2009/php/fgp.php?page=news&amp;item=upcoming">Great PUPkin Dog Costume Contest</a> in Fort Greene Park, which is happening Saturday, October 29th this year. They were kind enough to schedule it a week after Tompkins, so you and your pet will have the time to get your doggy piano tuned.</p>

<p><iframe frameborder="0" width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20171023025615if_/http://www.dailymotion.com/embed/video/k7HwgBM3SVBVAWdEeOt" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171023025615/http://www.fortgreenepups.org/site_2009/php/fgp.php?page=news&amp;item=upcoming">Registration for the event</a>, which is put on by Fort Greene Pups (you can read <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171023025615/http://gothamist.com/2010/10/29/kath_hansen_founder_of_fort_greene.php#photo-1">an interview with founder Kath Hansen here</a>), will open at 10:30 a.m. on the monument plaza. The event formally begins at 11:30 a.m., and there is a suggested donation of $5 per dog. </p>

<p>There&apos;s also a big change for potential participants from previous years: for the first time, organizers are limiting the number of entrants to 120 (they had nearly 170 last year and it was &quot;too much&quot;). They add that they are hoping that limiting the entries boosts the creativity level, and they don&apos;t just get 100 dogs dressed as Donald Trump (although that would still be pretty cute, see above!).</p>

<p>There will also be a &quot;Dogtoberfest&quot; afterparty at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171023025615/http://dsk-brooklyn.com/">DSK Biergarten</a> in the neighborhood from 2 p.m. to 6 p.m. after the contest. While you mull that over, you can revisit previous years&apos; photo galleries <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171023025615/http://gothamist.com/2014/10/27/pupkin_dog_costume_photos.php#photo-1">here</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171023025615/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/28/photos_sharknado_falcor_edward_scis.php#photo-1">here</a>.</p>

<p>And don&apos;t forget to come up with some creative, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171023025615/https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/business/wp/2016/09/29/is-your-dogs-halloween-costume-sexist/?postshare=5791475170267307&amp;tid=ss_tw">non-sexist</a> ideas for your dogs&#x2014;sometimes the impersonated are paying attention!</p>
