title: Chick-Fil-A Closes After Health Department Inspection

										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="010316chickfila.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170429124158im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/010316chickfila.jpg" width="640" height="426"> <br> <i> (Scott Heins/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>New York City&apos;s first <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170429124158/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/01/inside_chickfila_nyc.php#photo-4">towering Chick-Fil-A</a> has hit a stumbling block in its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170429124158/http://gothamist.com/2014/04/08/chick-fil-a_opening_multiple_nyc_re.php">plans for domination</a> of the city&apos;s fast food fried chicken landscape. A Christmas Eve inspection by the Department of Health uncovered a host of critical violations, resulting in 59 violation points, squarely in C territory. On December 30th, the restaurant closed its doors, indicating it would reopen for breakfast on January 4th.</p>

<p>The five critical violations included food held at improper temperatures and food not cooled by an approved method. Filth flies, food not protected from possible contamination and soiled wiping cloths were also cited. A previously ungraded inspection earlier in the month returned with 39 violation points, indicating things got worse&#x2014;not better&#x2014;between the two inspections.</p>
