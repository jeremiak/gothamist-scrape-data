<p>There are now six outlets to stuff your body full of cereal milk soft serve, compost cookies and crack pie with the new Chelsea location of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111311/http://milkbarstore.com/">Milk Bar</a>. Christina Tosi&apos;s sugar empire has settled on 8th Avenue between 21st and 22nd Streets&#x2014;the same block as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111311/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/08/david_chang_momofuku_nishi.php">David Chang&apos;s Momofuku Nishi</a>&#x2014;for its newest shop, which they designed with the LOT-EK studio. The brand&apos;s signature pink scroll adorns the indoor counter walls, with another counter with a window for street service. </p>

<p>The confetti cookies have been baked up for opening celebrations and Tosi <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111311/http://ny.eater.com/2016/4/29/11535294/milk-bar-chelsea-christina-tosi">told Eater</a> this location will offer $1 cups of her cereal milk soft serve for opening day. The James Beard Award-winning chef said she was &quot;taking a nod from the McDonald&apos;s playbook.&quot; Except with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111311/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/12/this_is_what_a_mcrib_actually_looks.php">way better food</a>.</p>
