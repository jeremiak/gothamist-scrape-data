					<p>La Superior owner Felipe Mendez expands on his Cerveceria Havemeyer restaurant in Williamsburg next week, opening up a new space devoted to Mexican pantry items, mezcal and music. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170302110653/https://www.facebook.com/Mezcaleria-La-Milagrosa-149926895499065/">La Milagrosa Agave Bar and Listening Room</a> (opening Wednesday) packs a lot of concepts into one space. From the outside, it looks and functions like a &quot;Mexican deli,&quot; as the press release puts it, selling ingredients like dried chilies, chorizo and prickly pear, alongside bottled sauces made by Mendez that he uses at the restaurant next door. This&apos;ll also be home to a coffee and juice bar.</p>

<p>In the back, however, Mendez created a &quot;secret,&quot; exclusive bar that&apos;s accessible through a freezer door located inside the deli. For access, you&apos;ll have to call to make a reservation for the intimate space, which features a curved wood ceiling that&apos;s supposed to maximize the acoustics of the room. </p>

<p>Mendez is a bit of a music freak, which explains the specifically-constructed room. He&apos;s a musician and DJ with regular appearances at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170302110653/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/08/the_lot_radio_williamsburg.php">The Lot radio</a> and reportedly owns about 15,000 vinyl records. He&apos;ll be booking both DJs and live performers to soundtrack the bar.</p>

<p>Agave-based spirits are the focus of the bar program, with ceviches and other small dishes to eat.</p>

<p><em>149 Havemeyer Street, Williamsburg, (718) 599-1499</em></p>
