					<p>&quot;We can have a cocktail bar and not have it be uptight,&quot; muses Michael Lombardozzi of his newly opened tiki spot <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170712185949/https://www.facebook.com/Dromedary-Bar-1086609718030694/">Dromedary Bar</a> on Bushwick&apos;s Irving Avenue. &quot;Not that I&apos;m saying all cocktail bars are uptight.&quot; Whether or not you agree with him, there&apos;s no mistaking the Hawaiian-shirt clad bar staff&#x2014;who turn out festive drinks inside mugs shaped like marlins&apos; heads&#x2014;for their mustachioed, vest-wearing kin. </p>

<p>Lombardozzi&apos;s a 20-year industry vet who did a decade at One If By Land, Two If By Sea before a one-night training stint at Maison Premiere inspired him to open his own place. He&apos;s been working on the Bushwick space since July, transforming a once gaudy yet dim catering hall&#x2014;there were mirrors along the walls but no windows&#x2014;into the casually Hawaiian-inspired bar it is today. The self-taught mixologist created a collection of tiki-esque drinks, plus collaborated on a menu of pan-Asian eats with chef Brian Baskoff, who worked at St. Anselm in Williamsburg and consulted on the menu for Empire Biscuit.</p>

<p>In creating the bar program, Lombardozzi renounced the frozen sugar drinks commonly associated with appropriated tiki spots, instead wanting to see if there was a way to recreate them with more finesse. Ice is an important element in attaining the look he wants, as the crushed variety he uses &quot;works like glass to show the colors inside.&quot; That&apos;s particularly evident in the Mt. Kilauea Colada ($11), which is supposed to depict a volcanic mountain rising out of the sea. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="050316drom1.jpeg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170712185949im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/050316drom1.jpeg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> Salmon Poke Tacos (Scott Heins/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Visits to Hawaii got Lombardozzi thinking about how to incorporate the islands&apos; beloved Spam, which is presented here as Spam Musubi Arancini ($6). The Korean Drumsticks (three for $8, five for $12)&#x2014;double-fried chicken legs in an oyster sauce-base with Korean chili flakes&#x2014;have been an early hit and also show up in the Pupu Platter ($22/$42) with Caribbean Ribs, Ginger Grapefruit Prawns and Mushroom and Brussels Sprouts Wontons. There&apos;s also Hawaiian Mac Salad ($3/$6), naturally.</p>

<p>This Friday, to celebrate the opening, the bar&apos;s got a tattoo artist coming in around 6 p.m. to ink out free tattoos. Then there&apos;ll be regular burlesque and other live events, plus a Monday night &quot;Dinner &amp; A Movie&quot; series that&apos;ll include drink and sandwich pairings. Brunch begins this weekend with a &quot;Big Breakfast&quot; of fried egg, sausage, spam, rice and mac salad. </p>
