<p>It&apos;s not the ideal weekend for traipsing around outdoors, but the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://www.licflea.com/">LIC Flea&apos;s</a> hosting a cheese festival that might be worth getting wet for. On Saturday and Sunday from 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. they&apos;re putting on <strong>The Big Cheese Event</strong> in partnership with Queens food blog <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://chopsticksandmarrow.com/">Chopsticks and Marrow</a>. Expect &quot;The Cheese Guy, Whats the Dillaz unique (i.e. reuben) Quesadillas, Rib in a Cups&apos; rich waffle mac &amp; cheese, Cugini Paninis&apos; cheese loaded grilled sandwiches, [and] cheesy laden pierogi from Taste of the Ukraine&quot; per organizers. </p>

<p> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/https://www.oysterbarny.com/">Grand Central Oyster Bar&apos;s</a> 14th annual <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://oysterbarny.com/event/14th-annual-2016-oyster-frenzy/">Oyster Frenzy</a></strong> unfolds this Saturday at the historic restaurant. Beginning at noon, stop by for chef&apos;s demos, shucking competitions, and sixteen varieties of oysters, half from the East coast and half from the West. The public can compete in &quot;Slurp Off&quot; competitive eating events, as well as &quot;Beer Shucking,&quot; where you try to open all the bottles in a case of beer faster than your competitors. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="093016roast.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/093016roast.jpg" width="640" height="480"> <br> <i> Sunday Roast at The Shakespeare (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/https://www.facebook.com/theshakespearenyc/photos/a.448902598549093.1073741827.439419422830744/924896247616390/?type=3&amp;theater">Facebook</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Whole roasted animals and...karaoke...are on tap at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://www.sycamorebrooklyn.com/">Sycamore Flowershop &amp; Bar</a> on Saturday. Korean BBQ eatery <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://www.insabrooklyn.com/">Insa Brooklyn</a> is doing a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/2595849"><strong>Pig &amp; Goat Roast</strong></a> at the Ditmas Park space as a collaboration with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://www.fleishers.com/">Fleishers Craft Butchery</a> that includes spit-roasted, pasture-raised animals, sauces and sides, and drinks. They have three entry times (1, 3 or 5 p.m.) and a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/2595849">$30 ticket</a> includes food, a pint of beer and two raffle tickets to win stuff like karaoke time at Insa, knives, flower crowns and other prizes.</p>

<p>Chilly weather and rainy skies call for holing up in a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/03/cozy_subterranean_bars_to_wait_out.php">cozy subterranean bar</a> with a mug of ale and some hearty roasted foods to fill your belly. Satisfy these cravings Sundays at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041313/http://www.theshakespearenyc.com/">The Shakespeare</a>, which is kicking off <strong>traditional, British family-style roasts</strong> beginning at 11:30 a.m. and running &apos;til 7 p.m. The restaurant offers a choice of three different meats (or a vegetarian nut roast) accompanied by veggies, roasted potatoes, Yorkshire pudding and &quot;customary condiments&quot; for $19 per person. For reservations, email reservations@theshakespearenyc.com or call 646.837.6779.</p>
