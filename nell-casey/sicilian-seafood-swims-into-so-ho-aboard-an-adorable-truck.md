					<p>The opening of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015125047/http://www.piccolacucinagroup.com/piccola-cucina-estiatorio-soho-nyc/">Piccola Cucina Estiatorio</a> in SoHo on March 8th completes a trio of Sicilian-inspired restaurants from chef and restaurateur Philip Guardione, who&apos;s mining his childhood home&apos;s seafood bounty for the new restaurant on Thompson Street. </p>

<p>Siciliy&apos;s island status means inundation from seas on all sides, which is where much of the restaurant&apos;s ingredients will be sourced. A stationary <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015125047/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piaggio_Ape">Piaggio Ape</a>&#x2014;which visitors to Italy will recognize as the mini delivery trucks bearing down on them on the country&apos;s tinier streets&#x2014;displays the fresh catch of the day and guests can opt for their selection grilled, fried or broiled. </p>

<p>Raw fish (Italy&apos;s now ubiquitous &quot;crudos&quot;) include a passion fruit-marinated salmon tartare and a carpaccio made with yellowtail topped with orange and fennel. Simply prepared fish fritto like calamari and shrimp, plus grilled octopus, sardines and fish skewers, complete the seafood appetizers. Don&apos;t sleep on the arancino catanese (riceballs); Sicilians do these very well.</p>

<p>Pasta alla Norma&#x2014;a very traditional Sicilain staple&#x2014;gets transformed into a ravioli of eggplant with ricotta cheese and the kitchen&apos;s using plenty of other seafoods in the pastas, like a Spaghetti ai ricci di mari (sea urchins) and a Scialatielli vongole (clams) with cured fish roe and cherry tomatoes.</p>

<p><em>75 Thompson Street; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015125047/http://www.piccolacucinagroup.com/">piccolacucinagroup.com</a></em></p>
