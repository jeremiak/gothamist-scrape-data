					<p>Whenever visitors from out of town ask where to eat in Midtown during their stay, my first response is usually: don&apos;t. But if they persist, my go-to suggestion will always be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501124745/http://www.molyvos.com/">Molyvos</a>, a homey yet elegant Greek restaurant that&apos;s destination-worthy even if you&apos;re not confined to the prison of Seventh Avenue. Now, the owners are opening their third Manhattan eatery, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501124745/http://www.ousianyc.com/">Ousia</a>, this evening, and there&apos;s a strong chance it&apos;ll make the wilds of 12th Avenue worth the trek, too. </p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501124745/http://www.livanosrestaurantgroup.com/">Livianos Restaurant Group</a>&#x2014;which also runs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501124745/http://www.oceanarestaurant.com/">Oceana</a> in Midtown and two Westchester restaurants&#x2014;once again mines its Greek roots for their new 57th Street eatery. Executive Chef Carlos Carreto is in charge here, whipping up spreads like Melitzanosalata (smoked eggplant, walnuts and feta, $10), Greek Salads with caperberries and hunks of briny feta ($14), and casual soups and sandwiches like a Cretan Flat Bread with spicy tomato sauce, Greek sausage, Louza (Mykonian prosciutto, essentially), mozzarella and oregano.</p>

<p>Beyond snacking foods, the extensive menu covers a lot of Mediterranean ground, employing seafood including a Whole Grilled Lavraki (a type of seabass, $32), prepared simply with a lemon and olive oil emulsion, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501124745/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/13/greek_food_nyc.php">as it should be</a>. The oft forgotten (outside of RenFairs) Turkey Leg is cooked confit here and served with roasted butternut squash and spiced honey ($22). An order of Greek Fries with a citrus spice and oregano ($8) is all but mandatory. </p>

<p>I&apos;m devastated they&apos;re not <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501124745/https://www.yelp.com/biz_photos/molyvos-new-york?select=6Z0L8pAnL1LRAuB2ZWXLYg">lighting haloumi cheese on fire</a> here, but I&apos;ll survive.</p>

<p>As you can see, Ousia&apos;s well-appointed and cheery, much more colorful than its sister to the east. The bright blue of Greece&apos;s flag appears all over the dining area, accented by pops of yellow and gold. If you can make it almost all the way to the Hudson, it looks like a good spot for a bit of Mediterranean escapism. </p>

<p><em>629 West 57th Street, 212-333-2000; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501124745/http://www.ousianyc.com/">ousianyc.com</a></em></p>
