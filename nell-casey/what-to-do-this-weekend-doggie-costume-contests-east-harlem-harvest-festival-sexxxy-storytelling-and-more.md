<p>Now that you&apos;ve picked out your perfect Halloween ensemble for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/18/halloween_2012_events.php">this weekend&apos;s festivities</a>, it&apos;s time to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/22/photos_more_adorable_dogs_dressed_i.php#photo-1">torment your pets</a>! There are two <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/16/its_that_magical_time_of_year_when.php">doggie dress-up contests</a> happening this weekend, starting with the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.dog-e-dog.org/2012/10/howl-ween.html">DOG-E-DOG Howl-A-Ween</a> dog costume party and contest in the McCarren Park dog run on Saturday, from noon until 3 p.m. Costumed mutts will compete for human prizes from Lucky Dog and Tommy Guns Salon, among others; there&apos;s also a special category for doggie tricks. And even if you don&apos;t have a furry friend, swing by to ogle the contestants, buy raffle tickets for some sweet swag or donate a toy or blanket to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.barcshelter.org/">BARC Shelter</a> for a pooch in need.</p>

<p>Over in Fort Greene,  the 14th Annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.fortgreenepups.org/site_2009/php/fgp.php?page=news&amp;item=upcoming">Great PUPkin Dog Costume Contest</a> wags its way into Fort Greene Park Saturday in conjunction with the Conservancy&apos;s annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://fortgreenepark.org/upcoming.html">Halloween Festival</a>. Pups register starting at 11 a.m. and the judging starts at noon with a $5 suggested donation. </p>

<p><em>Also this weekend:</em> </p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.eastharlemharvestfestival.com/">East Harlem Harvest Festival</a> unfolds over three days, beginning with the Friday night Gala, where a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://eastharlemharvestfestival.bpt.me/">$25 ticket</a> gets you beer and wine from the Harlem Brewing Company and Manhattan Beer Distributors plus hors d&apos;oeuvres from local restaurants like <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.yelp.com/biz/the-kiosk-new-york">Kiosk</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.aguafrescarestaurant.com/#!home/mainPage">Agua Fresca</a>. Then on Saturday, more local restaurants and business take over three blocks of Park Avenue to hand out samples and sell small bites from Lloyd&apos;s Carrot Cake, new candy shop Coco Le Vu, baked good from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://gothamist.com/2012/07/23/spanish_harlem_non-profit_bakery_op.php">Hot Bread Kitchen</a> and lots more. Beer flights, fruit hookah, cooking demos and all kinds of activities are scheduled from noon to 6 p.m. from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.nycedc.com/project/la-marqueta">La Marqueta</a> up to the Urban Garden Center. On Sunday from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m., the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.urbangardennyc.com/">Urban Garden Center</a> will give away 100 free trees courtesy of Bette Midler&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.nyrp.org/">NYRP</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.milliontreesnyc.org/html/home/home.shtml">MillionTreesNYC</a>.  </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="102612newsletter2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/102612newsletter2.jpg" width="300" height="256" class="image-right"> </span>Longtime Jim Henson collaborator and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://muppet.wikia.com/wiki/Jerry_Nelson">Muppeteer Jerry Nelson</a> passed away this summer, and to honor his memory, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.movingimage.us/">Museum of the Moving Image</a> has arranged a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/19/the_late_jerry_count_von_count_nels.php#photo-1">special tribute to his life and career</a> at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.movingimage.us/visit/calendar/2012/10/27/detail/a-tribute-to-jerry-nelson">2 p.m. on Saturday</a>. Nelson&#x2014;best known for his work as Count von Count on Sesame Street and Kermit&apos;s nephew Robin on <em>The Muppet Show</em>, among many, many other characters&#x2014;will be remembered by his Muppet colleagues, including Bill Barretta, Fran Brill (Prairie Dawn, Zoe) and Dave Goelz (Gonzo, others). The program will also include clips from Nelson&apos;s long puppeteering career and some &quot;special surprises,&quot; per the museum. The event is free with museum admission and on a first-come, first-served basis. </p>

<p>If you&apos;re feeling frisky this weekend&#x2014;or need something to get you in the mood&#x2014;head over to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.galapagosartspace.com/">Galapagos Art Space</a> for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://bawdystorytelling.com/events/no-holds-barred-nyc/">&apos;No Holds Barred&apos; Bawdy Storytelling</a> on Friday night. Think of it as &quot;The Moth for Pervs&quot;: storytellers have 10 minutes to titillate you with an honest sexploit from their past with &quot;no scripts, no nets and no filters.&quot; This go-around&apos;s storytellers include monologist <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://gothamist.com/tags/mikedaisey">Mike Daisey</a>, sex educator Ducky Doolittle, Late Night With Jimmy Fallon comedian Seth Herzog, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://bawdystorytelling.com/events/no-holds-barred-nyc/">three others</a>. If that&apos;s not enough to get your motor running, Vixen Creations and Aneros will both be giving away free sex toys. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406171322/http://www.ticketweb.com/t3/sale/SaleEventDetail?dispatch=loadSelectionData&amp;eventId=3151044">Tickets for this sexxxy event</a> run $15 and doors open at 7 p.m. </p>
