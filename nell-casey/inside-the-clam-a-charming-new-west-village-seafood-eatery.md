</div></div>					<p>There are other sea creatures on the menu at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151031123843/http://www.theclamnyc.com/">The Clam</a>, a charming new neighborhood eatery in the West Village, but it&apos;s the tiny jewels inside the shells that are the star of the show. Helmed by the guys behind <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151031123843/http://gothamist.com/2006/07/14/camera_in_the_k_32.php">Little Owl</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151031123843/http://gothamist.com/2008/02/01/camera_in_the_k_95.php">Market Table</a>, the new eatery shares the similar casual elegance as its sisters, with 50 seats tucked beneath arched mother-of-pearl ceilings and a few seats overlooking the raw bar. </p>

<p>There are raw Littlenecks on ice, naturally, as well as oysters, king crab and a refreshing shrimp campechana with avocado and tortilla chips. Chef and owner Mike Price offers clams in multiple preparations, including over spaghetti with a spicy red sauce, fried in sandwiches with a lobster remoulade and simply steamed with juicy bread. If you&apos;re not down with molluscs, try out the spicy jumbo lump crab cakes or a half Bell &amp; Evans chicken roasted with mushrooms and radishes.</p>

<p>The cocktail list aims to complement the briny bivalves, including a tequila-based Dark &amp; Stormy and a winter Old Fashioned with muddled kumquats. They&apos;ve got some wines on tap as well, with a special section devoted to vintages from Islands like Sardinia and the Canaries.</p>

<p><em>420 Hudson Street, (212) 242-7420; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151031123843/http://www.theclamnyc.com/">theclamnyc.com</a></em></p>
