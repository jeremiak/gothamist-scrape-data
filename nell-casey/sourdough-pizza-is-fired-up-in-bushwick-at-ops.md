					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170112050803/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/20/emmy_squared_detroit_pizza_heaven.php#photo-1">Detroit-style</a>, Neapolitan, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170112050803/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/26/robo_pizza.php">robot</a>, your classic slice&#x2014;New York likes its pizza anyway it can take it (except Chicago &quot;pizza,&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170112050803/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/14/jon_stewart_pizza_chicago.php">GTFO with that stuff</a>). NYC&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170112050803/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/26/new_pizza_roundup_2016.php">newest pizzeria</a>  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170112050803/http://www.opsbk.com/">Ops</a>, officially opening Saturday in Bushwick, is making wood-fired pies with a sourdough starter, which points to a lively, flavorful crust you&apos;re not likely to find at your corner pizzeria. </p>

<p>&quot;It is rare to make pizza with wild yeast, especially in NYC,&quot; says Mike Fadem, who along with Marie Tribouilloy and Gavin Compton, is running the restaurant. &quot;I&apos;m not sure why, maybe because it&apos;s more volatile and less consistent, but I believe the extra work pays off in flavor and texture.&quot; Ops will join spots like <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170112050803/http://wheatedbrooklyn.com/">Wheated in Flatbush</a> using the special yeast. The trio have an impressive collected resume that includes Estela, Marlow &amp; Songs, Buvette, Achilles Heel and Blue Collar. </p>

<p>Beyond the interesting pies, you&apos;ll find antipasti served in the &quot;peasant tradition,&quot; natural wines by the glass and a cocktail program built around European spirits like amari and...bottles of Budweiser. </p>

<p><em>346 Himrod Street; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170112050803/http://www.opsbk.com/">opsbk.com</a>. Open for dinner Wednesday - Sunday from 5 p.m. to midnight, with extended hours coming soon.</em></p>
