					<p>For his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170426001633/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/12/iron_chef_garces_to_open_first_nyc.php">second NYC restaurant</a>, Jose Garces&#x2014;a James Beard-award winning chef known for both his status as a prolific restaurateur in Philadelphia (and elsewhere) and a stint as a &quot;Next Iron Chef&quot;&#x2014;mines the rich tapestry of Spanish cuisine yet again at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170426001633/http://ortzirestaurant.com/">Ortzi</a>, which debuted near Bryant Park this week. The Basque Country, the cuisine of which Garces says &quot;helped shape the chef [he] is today,&quot; informs the dishes coming from Ortzi&apos;s kitchen, which will be led by Chef de Cuisine Michael Han.</p>

<p>Garces <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170426001633/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/26/jose_garces_amada.php">mined Andalusian fare</a> for the NYC offshoot of Amada, and though Ortzi&apos;s an original, the chef has previously <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170426001633/http://philadelphia.tintorestaurant.com/">explored Basque cuisine</a> at other restaurants. Hearty stews cooked in a terracotta cazuela, raw fish crudos, and conservas&#x2014;preserved foods typically served from cans or jars in France and Spain&#x2014;are the focal points of the menu.</p>

<p>Since it&apos;s located in a hotel, the restaurant will also serve breakfast dishes including a Bocadillo egg sandwich with mancheg and bacon on brioche with potato hash, and lunch dishes including an Ortzi Burger made with dry aged beef, idiaz&#xE1;bal (a sheep&apos;s milk cheese) and onion jam.</p>
