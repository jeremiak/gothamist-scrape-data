<p>While Bryant Park <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150205141535/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/24/taste_of_france.php">makes way for French culture</a>, a one night festival highlighting the shared histories of Celts from France and Ireland takes over <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150205141535/http://www.connollyspubandrestaurant.com/">Connolly&apos;s Pub</a>. The spectacularly fun to say <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150205141535/http://www.bzh-ny.org/">Interceltic Fest Noz</a> brings music, dancing and lots of drinking to the midtown bar from 7 p.m. to 2 a.m. tonight, &quot;celebrating the common heritage, traditions, culture, and story of immigration of Celtic people of NYC.&quot;</p>

<p>The Fest Noz (Breton for &quot;night festival&quot;) promises performances by both Irish and French groups, including <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150205141535/https://www.facebook.com/pages/The-Mickey-Finns/134374153099?directed_target_id=0">The Mickey Fins</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150205141535/https://www.facebook.com/armel.anhejer?directed_target_id=0">Armel An H&#xE9;jer</a> and some ceili, traditional Irish dancing, on the bar&apos;s proper stage. The bar also boasts a decent beer selection, including Kilkenny Irish Cream Ale, Stronbow Cider and Innis &amp; Gunn plus classic Irish dishes like Fish &amp; Chips, Beef Stew and Irish Smoked Salmon. </p>

<p>Entrance for the Interceltic Fest Noz is $10 for non-members.
