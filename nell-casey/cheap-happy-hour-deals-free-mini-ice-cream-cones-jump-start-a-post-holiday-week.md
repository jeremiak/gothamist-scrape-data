<p>Tuesday begins the Dragon Boat Festival, the annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/http://www.scmp.com/news/hong-kong/health-environment/article/2096096/hong-kong-set-heat-and-rain-over-dragon-boat">boat racing festival</a> that will be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/http://time.com/4797421/google-doodle-dragon-boat-china/">celebrated with boating and eating</a> across Asia and other places. Take part in festivities in NYC Tuesday night at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/http://nomwah.com/2017/05/evening-dragons/"><strong>An Evening With Dragons</strong></a>, a collaborative dinner event between Nom Wah Nolita, The Tang, Ho Foods, Win Son, and GN Chan taking place at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/http://openhouse.me/168-bowery/">The Old Bowery Station</a> in Nolita. Dine on shrimp buns, garlic cucumbers, chicken dumplings and breakfast congee with a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Century_egg">century egg</a>, among other dishes. There&apos;ll be plenty of Tsingtao beer and a cocktail made with a century egg, too. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/http://nomwah.com/2017/05/evening-dragons/">Tickets $60</a>. </p>

<p>A wallet-friendly happy hour has launched at &apos;70s-themed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/http://www.ethylsnyc.com/">Ethyls Alcohol and Food</a> on the Upper East Side. Monday through Friday from 5 p.m. to 7 p.m. take advantage of the <strong>Fi-Dolla Happy Hour</strong> deal, daily specials that include $5 drinks and $5 plates of food. Mondays it&apos;s Mexican Meatballs and a Jameson Black Barrel cocktail (&quot;The Gent&quot;), Tuesdays it&apos;s fish tacos and a Watermelon Felon cocktail, and Thursdays it&apos;s Disco Curds and the Bump &amp; Grind cocktail made with rye and ginger. Check out the full lineup <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/http://www.ethylsnyc.com/#events-section">here</a>.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="053017altesi.jpeg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/053017altesi.jpeg" width="640" height="350"> <br> <i> Altesi Downtown (Marili Forastieri)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Elsewhere in happy hour news, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/https://www.altesinyc.com/">Altesi Downtown</a> in SoHo is offering a new <strong>aperitivo</strong> program Monday - Saturday from 5 p.m. - 7 p.m. at the bar. $15 buys you a cocktail like an Aperol Spritz or half a bottle of prosecco, both of which come with a plate of food to nibble on. Bites include cheese, Italian crudit&#xE9;, bruschetta, ricotta with honey or anchovies with butter. </p>

<p>On Thursday and Friday get a sneak peak at a new ice cream offering at both locations of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/http://www.creamlinenyc.com/#local">Creamline</a> in Chelsea Market and Chelsea. The shops will be serving <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615203723/https://www.instagram.com/p/BUuF_9AFB3p/?taken-by=creamlinenyc">free mini cones</a></strong> in chocolate or vanilla Ronnybrook soft serve. After Friday, the cute ice cream cones can be purchased for $1. </p>
