					<p>NYC&apos;s collection of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170310171722/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/20/best_niche_museums_nyc.php">niche museums</a> grew by one this week with the opening of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170310171722/http://www.mrchocolate.com/museum/">Museum of Chocolate</a>, spearheaded locally by French chocolatier Jacques Torres. The museum&#x2014;fully titled &quot;Choco-Story New York: The Chocolate Museum and Experience with Jacques Torres&quot;&#x2014;operates like a natural history exhibition, examining the origins of chocolate from Mayan and Aztec civilizations and looking into the process of transforming the cacao bean into chocolate. </p>

<p>On display are pottery and tools related to the cultivation of cacao alongside artifacts that show the bean&apos;s importance in many cultures across what&apos;s now Central America and later in Europe. Another section follows the cacao pod from an Amazonian rainforest through fermentation and into its final, edible state. </p>

<p>Also included in the tour are chocolate tastings, Mayan hot chocolate, and chocolate truffles; along with samples of dark, milk and white chocolates, both blends and single-origin. Classes in chocolate-making will also be offered. </p>

<p><em>Choco-Story New York is located at 350 Hudson Street. Open Wednesday - Sunday 10 a.m. - 5 p.m.</em></p>
