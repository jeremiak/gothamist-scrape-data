					<p>Newly-opened <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170427032147/https://www.rawmkt.com/">RAW MKT</a> on East 8th Street ticks off quite a few boxes in current NYC dining trends: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170427032147/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/20/made_nice_eleven_madison_park.php#photo-1">chef-driven &quot;fancy&quot; fast casual</a>, restaurants <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170427032147/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/15/orchard_grocer_vegan.php#photo-1">masquerading as grocery stores</a>, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170427032147/http://gothamist.com/tags/poke">Hawaiian food</a>. Cafe-type dining spots have proliferated in the NYU-adjacent stretch&#x2014;chains like Cosi, Maoz, Subway and Chipotle have found great success there&#x2014;so are freshmen ready for some fresh fish?</p>

<p>Chef Bryan Narciso, who was a sous chef at Greenpoint&apos;s line-drawing brunch spot Five Leaves, trades pancakes for poke in its many forms. Bowls ($11.99/$13.99) can come with a base of rice or zucchini noodles with Shoyu Tuna with scallions and onions in a soy-sesame glaze or Salmon Avocado ($.99 more) with edamame and macadamia nuts in a shoyu glaze. The same proteins can be transformed into hand rolls, one for $4.99 or three for $11.99.</p>

<p>Nearby dorm rooms can also be stocked up with popular Hawaiian and Japanese sauces and products including sriracha, shrimp chips, Pocky, and wasabi peas.</p>

<p><em>61 East 8th Street; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170427032147/https://www.rawmkt.com/">rawmkt.com</a></em></p>
