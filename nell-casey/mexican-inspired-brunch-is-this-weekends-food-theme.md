<p>There are three new <strong>Mexican-inspired brunches</strong> to investigate this weekend, beginning at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/01/alta_calidad_prospect_heights.php#photo-1">newly opened</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/https://www.altacalidadbk.com/">Alta Calidad</a> in Prospect Heights, where you can get Huevos Divorciados ($13, pictured above): two tostadas with sunny side up eggs, guajillo and tomatillo salsas, avocado, chorizo, and beans or a Breakfast Quesadilla ($14) stuffed with scrambled eggs, pork and pico de gallo. In the East Village, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://www.guacnyc.com/">Guac Tacos + Tequila</a> offers a prix fixe meal ($15) with an entree, guacamole and a coffee, with the option to add unlimited margaritas and other brunch cocktails for $14.50. And finally on the Lower East Side, for $45 at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://www.tijuana-picnic.com/">Tijuana Picnic</a> you can score a three-course Mexican brunch plus unlimited bloodies and mimosas for an hour and a half.</p>

<p>Elsewhere in overindulgence, <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/2871754">Winter&apos;s Grind at Arrogant Swine: Part III</a></strong> offers an opportunity for all-you-can-eat sausages and all-you-can-drink beers on Saturday afternoon from 1 p.m. to 4 p.m. Stop by the Bushwick BBQ joint and go to town on Tyson Ho and pals&apos;  jerk chicken sausage, chicken satay, Ecuadorian morcilla, veal sausage with Pakistani fire seasoning and Sicilian lamb stigghiola plus options for vegetarians. On tap: home brews from 10 city brewers, plus &quot;March-friendly beers&quot; from 15 NYC breweries. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/2871754">Tickets $45</a>.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="031717yooeating.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/031717yooeating.jpg" width="640" height="481"> <br> <i> (courtesy Yooeating)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Saturday evening, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://www.yooeating.com/">Yooeating</a> materializes at Williamsburg&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://www.reclamationbarbrooklyn.com/">Reclamation Bar</a> for the second <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/https://www.facebook.com/events/245302375940433/">Yooeating Coreano Cubano Food Pop-Up</a></strong>, offering Korean-Cuban mashups like Bulgogi Papas Rellenas (bulgogi stuffed fried mashed potato balls, pictured above); Coreano Cubanos with coffee carnitas, fried spam, My Friend&apos;s Mustard and cheese on Cuban bread; and Ropa Vieja Lamb Jorim (soy and tomato braised lamb). The food service begins at 6 p.m. and runs until 10 p.m. or when food runs out.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="031717hotchix.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/031717hotchix.jpg" width="640" height="388"> <br> <i> (courtesy #BlazingFeathers)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>This Sunday begins the first in a series of pop-ups hosted by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://vicsnewyork.com/">Vic&apos;s</a> on Great Jones Street by the chefs of The Bowery Restaurant Group, including Vic&apos;s, Hundred Acres, Rosie&apos;s and Cookshop. #BlazingFeathers kicks off the series with Hot Chicken (choice of heat level) with sides including pimento mac and cheese, black eyed peas, slaw and a dessert of banana-nilla wafer. Grab the fiery bird for $15 a plate, with sides and sweets an additional $5. Looking ahead, subsequent Sundays will see pop-ups at Vic&apos;s including &quot;old school New York delis, Parisian falafel stands, Jersey bakeries and more.&quot; First come, first served. </p>

<p>Finally, on Sunday, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/https://www.duncansburgers.com/">Duncan&apos;s Burgers</a> takes up permanent residence inside <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/https://www.facebook.com/dromedarybar/">Dromedary Bar</a>, Bushwick&apos;s primary source for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170319172230/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/03/dromedary_tiki_bar_bushwick.php#photo-1">marlin&apos;s head cocktails</a>, and there will be a burger boatload&apos;s worth of free food to celebrate. Beginning at 1 p.m. until the locusts have decimated the supply (up to 500), they&apos;ll be giving away their dry aged beef burgers for free. If you miss the giveaway, take heart: burgers are only $3.49 after that.</p>
