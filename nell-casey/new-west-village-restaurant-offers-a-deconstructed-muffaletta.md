					<p>In keeping with the long tradition of naming your restaurant after your street address&#x2014;there are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419143536/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/15/burlap_sacked.php">worse ways</a> to go about this process&#x2014;<a href="www.33greenwich.nyc">33 Greenwich</a> makes its debut Wednesday night in the West Village. For his second restaurant, Danny Volk (The Upsider) is trying &quot;Southern hospitality&quot; on for size, bringing aboard chef Anne Thornton&#x2014;who co-founded Moby&apos;s LA vegan restaurant <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419143536/http://laist.com/tags/littlepine">Little Pine</a>&#x2014;who&apos;ll execute a menu of coastal Southern and New Orleans-inspired foods. Don&apos;t worry: there&apos;s meat.</p>

<p>In fact, it&apos;s extremely meaty. The menu&apos;s anchored by &quot;Suppers For 2&quot; like a Veal Chop ($65) or Fried Chicken ($55), both of which come with a choice of two sides like saut&#xE9;ed broccoli or sweet potato puree. Not a single Entree For 1 doesn&apos;t contain animal flesh of some kind, though if you&apos;re pescatarian you can slide by with Shrimp &amp; Grits ($27) or Seared Arctic Char ($32) with artichokes and fennel in buerre blanc, a.k.a. butter sauce. Vegetarians can satisfy their buttered and fried yens with Biscuits ($5) served with peppered honey butter or Fried Green Tomatoes ($14) with herbed buttermilk.</p>

<p>While there&apos;s always room for more fried chicken, will we see some backlash from the people of New Orleans over the &quot;Muffaletta Board&quot;? The dish is described as a &quot;deconstructed version of the beloved New Orleans sandwich with charcuterie, provolone, olives and a fennel and celery salad.&quot; Is a muffaletta really a muffaletta <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419143536/http://gothamist.com/2011/11/08/muffalettas.php">without the muffaletta loaf</a>? <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419143536/http://www.grubstreet.com/2008/02/the_deconstructed_muffuletta.html">Apparently</a>.</p>

<p><em>33 Greenwich Avenue, 646-609-3615; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419143536/http://www.33greenwich.nyc/">33greenwich.nyc</a>. Closed Sunday.</em></p>
