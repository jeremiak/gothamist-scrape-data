					<p>Not a trace of Metallica remains at 594 Union Avenue, the former site of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607193112/http://bedfordandbowery.com/2016/11/williamsburg-bar-venue-over-the-eight-is-closing-at-the-end-of-the-year/">divey Over The Eight bar</a> and its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607193112/http://gothamist.com/2015/04/27/secret_hot_dogs_williamsburg.php">Santa Salsa food counter</a>. The space has been reborn as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607193112/https://www.casapublicabk.com/">Casa P&#xFA;blica</a>, a fancy Mexican eatery that&apos;s as bright and cheerful as OT8 was dark and grungy. At least you can still get a Tecate here&#x2014;but it&apos;s $7 and there&apos;s no accompanying shot of cheap tequila.</p>

<p>The new restaurant is run by chef Bob Truitt, beverage director/general manager Meaghan Montagano and managing partner Daniel Ortiz de Montellano, who have transformed the former bar/venue into a sunny spot for mezcal cocktails, ceviches and tacos. Look closely and you&apos;ll see pages from vintage Mexican newspapers wrapped around certain surfaces; there&apos;s also a mosaic mural behind the bar depicting the hand-ground corn process in making tortillas.</p>

<p>The decor and the dishes are both meant to evoke the past and present of Mexico City. In this interpretation, that means heirloom corn tortillas for carnitas ($8) and squash blossom ($13) tacos; tostadas topped with crab, sea urchin and avocado in a spicy peanut sauce ($20); and Pollo Abobada ($24), roasted chicken in guajillo adobo with fingerling potatoes and a pico de gallo made with nopales (cactus). </p>

<p>Micheladas&#x2014;including that Tecate number, which is encrusted with salt and served with a douse of sangrita with lime&#x2014;anchor the beer program, while cocktails skew towards mezcals/tequilas, including larger &quot;Grande Cocteles&quot; like the 51-ounce, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607193112/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1VyaXkK0I7s">Patrick Swayze-approved</a> &quot;I Carried A Watermelon,&quot; ($48) with Olmeca Altos Blanco Tequila, Carpano Blanco Vermouth, watermelon, cucumber and citrus. </p>

<p>While my beloved <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607193112/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/30/best_nyc_grub_2015.php">Venezuelan hot dogs</a> may be gone&#x2014;a pared down Santa Salsa operation has been running out of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607193112/http://www.ionabrooklyn.com/">Iona</a> recently&#x2014;the team here is dangling a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607193112/http://www.grubstreet.com/2017/05/casa-pblica-opens-in-nyc.html">late night Mexican hot dog</a> to debut sometime soon.</p>
