<p>Michelin&apos;s list of New York City&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161119182138/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/01/2016_michelin_winners.php">best restaurants</a> will be unleashed next Thursday, but the French tire company announced their selections for the Bib Gourmand distinction today. Contenders for the list must offer two courses and a glass of wine or dessert for $40 or less before tax and gratuity, with 132 restaurants making the cut this year.</p>

<p>The big news around the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161119182138/http://ny.eater.com/2016/11/11/13590260/nyc-michelin-bib-gourmands-2017">food</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161119182138/http://www.grubstreet.com/2016/11/michelins-2017-bib-gourmand-for-nyc.html">sites</a> is about April Bloomfield&apos;s The Spotted Pig, which has been stripped of its single star rating and winds up on this year&apos;s Bib list. There&apos;s also speculation about some notable &quot;omissions&quot; to the Bib list from last year&#x2014;Danny Meyer&apos;s Untitled, for example, along with well-received newcomers like Faro and Oiji&#x2014;and whether we&apos;ll be seeing them adorned with stars this time next week.</p>
