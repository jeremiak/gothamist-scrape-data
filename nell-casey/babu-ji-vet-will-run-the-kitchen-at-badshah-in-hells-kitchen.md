					<p>New York City&apos;s outpost of Babu Ji is currently <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015112449/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/02/babu_ji_wage_theft_lawsuits.php">shuttered</a>, but a vet of the restaurant, Charles Mani, has landed a new gig helming the kitchen at Badshah, which opens Thursday in Hell&apos;s Kitchen. Restaurateur Abishek Sharma (Surya, Swagat) is behind the new restaurant, which will incorporate Indian flavors into traditional dishes from across Asia, including dishes from various parts of India.</p>

<p>A la carte dishes include a cross-over appetizer of General Tso Cauliflower ($12), hunks of cauliflower in a tomato-chili sauce with sesame and onion seeds, and Gol Goppa ($8), a snack of semolina balls in chutney, a version of which was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015112449/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/22/babu_ji_ave_b_review.php#photo-1">very popular at Babu Ji</a>. The restaurant will also serve clove-smoked lamb chops and jumbo prawns from the tandoor oven, along with pots of Butter Chicken ($20) in fenugreek gravy and Cheese Kofta ($17) in a cardamom, fennel, cumin and cashew nut curry.</p>

<p>Mani will also offer a chef&apos;s tasting menu for $45 per person, which includes appetizers, curries, naan, rice and dessert.</p>

<p><em>788 Ninth Avenue, 646-649-2407; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015112449/http://www.badshahny.com/">badshahny.com</a></em></p>
