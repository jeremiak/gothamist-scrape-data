					<p>This city contains multitudes, including the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170318030332/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/20/saddest_mcdonalds_ever.php">saddest McDonald&apos;s in the world</a>...and the &quot;nicest,&quot; which includes a concierge and fancy ass French pastry. I honestly don&apos;t know which one I&apos;d like to spend less time in. </p>

<p>A Chelsea franchise (809 6th Avenue) of Mickey D&apos;s&#x2014;part of a family dynasty that spans three (!!!) generations&#x2014;has reopened as a next generation McDonald&apos;s, complete with the popular new <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170318030332/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/18/eatsa_quinoa_robots.php">computer ordering system</a>, a &quot;sleek interior...with curated kitchen views,&quot; and an arrangement that dispatches servers to <em>bring your nuggets to your table</em>. If this is Trump&apos;s America then...nope, nope, will never be on board for that.</p>

<p>Besides the new system and the concierge, the baked options seem to be the larger part of what they&apos;re trying to accomplish. Their signature microwaved apple pie has been upgraded with a latticed crust, and they&apos;ll also serve things like chocolate and plain croissant.</p>

<p>McDonald&apos;s locations in Europe are already offering fresh pastries and the like. I made what the kids are calling a &quot;Snapchat&quot; about one on a recent trip to Spain but, as I am Old, I do not know how to revive it for the internet, so you&apos;ll have to trust me. </p>

<p>This store is the first of its kind in the United States and McDonald&apos;s says they&apos;re looking to &quot;elevate the customer experience in the New York Metro Region.&quot; With Millennials (allegedly) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170318030332/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/25/millennials_mcdonalds_lol.php">abandoning the brand</a> and companies like Starbucks <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170318030332/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/04/starbucks_phase_two.php">nipping at their heels</a>, McDonald&apos;s must find new ways to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170318030332/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/18/mcdonalds_not_expanding.php">stay competitive</a>. </p>

<p>McMuffins can <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170318030332/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/25/breakfast_saved_mcdonalds.php">take you only so far</a>.</p>
