					<p>DUMBO&apos;s not exactly famous for its great dining options, but the neighborhood is starting to redeem itself among sandwich lovers, who can enjoy <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307055250/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/18/fancy_grilled_cheese_no_frills_at_d.php#photo-1">fancy grilled cheese</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307055250/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/20/bread_spread_best_dumbo_sandwich.php#photo-1">made-to-order goodness</a>. Now comes specialty purveyor <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307055250/http://www.untamedsandwiches.com/">Untamed Sandwiches</a> in &quot;Dumbo Heights&quot;&#x2014;the name of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307055250/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/14/trump_watchtower_classy.php">Kushner development</a> (yes, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307055250/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/30/jared_kushner_not_good.php">that Kushner</a>) in the old Jehovah&apos;s Witness buildings.</p>

<p>The Midtown-born sandwich shop opens today at 117 Adams Street, a big (for DUMBO) space with seating for 35 people who aren&apos;t compelled to eat lunch at their desks. The eatery&apos;s schtick is approachable sustainability. &quot;Extra high-quality proteins in an exceedingly simple, sandwich format,&quot; co-founder Andy Jacobi told us yesterday. &quot;We&apos;re very dogmatic about where we source ingredients from. 100% grass-fed beef and lamb, grass-raised pork, free range chicken. We use local produce whenever we can.&quot;</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="110316goat.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307055250im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/110316goat.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> The Chupacabra (Scott Heins/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The menu&apos;s similar to the offerings in Bryant Park, including the popular &quot;The Butt,&quot; with cider-braised pork butt, broccoli rabe, pepper jelly, dijon mustard, and sharp cheddar. The Brooklyn shop will also see the return of the shop&apos;s original The Chupacabra, made with red wine-braised goat, roast chermoula carrots, kohlrabi slaw, red onion, and whipped goat cheese.</p>

<p>Vegetarians can look for the Sheemaker&apos;s Bounty, a charred broccoli sandwich with fried almond butter and pickled raisin jelly.</p>

<p>Bread comes courtesy of Grandaisy Bakery, who supply the ciabatta for all of the sandwiches (there&apos;s a gluten-free option, too). Those eschewing bread entirely can opt for any of the braised meats served as mixed green salads with a lemon vinaigrette dressing, and they&apos;ll have chili and housemade cookies on offer, too.</p>

<p><em>117 Adams Street; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307055250/http://www.untamedsandwiches.com/">untamedsandwiches.com</a></em></p>
