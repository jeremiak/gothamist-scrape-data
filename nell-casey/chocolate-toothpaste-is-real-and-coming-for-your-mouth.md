<p>Crest&apos;s latest toothpaste innovation seems tailor made for use the morning after a depraved Doritos Locos Tacos binge. The company recently debuted their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140307045513/http://news.pg.com/press-release/pg-corporate-announcements/crest-introduces-new-line-most-unexpected-and-fun-toothpast">new series of toothpastes</a>, called &quot;Be,&quot; in such tempting flavors as Vanilla Mint Spark, Lime Spearmint Zest and Mint Chocolate Trek. Can&apos;t you just taste the rising blood sugar levels?</p>

<p>With the new flavors, the company&apos;s team of in-house flavorists want consumers to &quot;explore outside their boundaries&quot; and &quot;arouse their senses.&quot; Is Crest trying to sell toothpaste or engage us in an illicit oral act? &quot;Crest Be&#xAE; Mint Chocolate Trek opens with a rich, creamy cocoa flavor that provides an indulgent and decadent experience,&quot; reads the press release. &quot;The cocoa flavor evolves into a minty cool sensation and ends with a lingering fresh feeling.&quot; </p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140307045513/https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=757712340923890&amp;set=pb.119473508081113.-2207520000.1390933846.&amp;type=3&amp;theater">totally not marketed to kids</a> toothpastes debut the first week of February for $4.99 a tube. Think of it as a thoughtful, inexpensive Valentine&apos;s Day gift. </p>
