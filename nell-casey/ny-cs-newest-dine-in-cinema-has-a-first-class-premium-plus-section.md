					<p>Modern movie theaters resemble <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170127131946/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/08/nitehawk_cinema_park_slope.php">restaurants</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170127131946/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/01/all_nyc_amc_theatres_will_offer_res.php">living rooms</a> more and more these days, and the newest one to drop in NYC will even give you a blanket for your movie viewing comfort. Nope, not a convenient vehicle <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170127131946/http://gothamist.com/2010/08/18/amc_empire_25_movie_theater_removes.php">for bed bugs</a>, seriously promise! Tonight, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170127131946/https://www.ipictheaters.com/">iPic Theaters</a> debuts their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170127131946/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/23/swanky_dine-in_movie_theater_coming.php">first NYC location</a> down at the Seaport, offering different &quot;classes&quot; of seating plus food and booze inside the theater. They even show movies, too!</p>

<p>The schlubs stuck in &quot;Premium&quot; get either a non-reclining boring movie chair or a chaise longue-style seat&#x2014;only sold in pairs&#x2014;and must fetch their own food and beverage at the &quot;iPic Express Counter&quot; before the show starts. These seats still cost $20.</p>

<p>The high rollers in &quot;Premium Plus&quot; get the full-service treatment with reclining, padded chairs, a pillow and that blanket. They also have their (pre-ordered) food and drink delivered to their table by a server plus as much popcorn as they care to eat. These seats cost $20 <em>plus</em> an additional VIP Fee. That&apos;s $32...for a movie. (Nitehawk Cinema&apos;s tickets are $12, for comparison.)</p>

<p>The Post says that the cinema will only serve <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170127131946/http://nypost.com/2016/10/03/this-luxe-dine-in-movie-theater-serves-only-quiet-food/">&quot;quiet food&quot;</a>, which seems to indicate lots of finger foods, according to press materials. Pizzas, sliders, fried appetizers and charcuterie, things of that nature, plus a full bar. </p>

<p>iPic bills itself as &quot;the ultimate night out.&quot; Stay tuned for our review next week. </p>
