					<p>Houston-born <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021121413/http://www.pondichericafe.com/">Pondicheri</a>, an all-day Indian eatery led by Chef and Restaurateur Anita Jaisinghani, has set down some roots up north with its first NYC restaurant on West 27th Street. The expansive menu&#x2014;which just covers breakfast and lunch until dinner launches in a few weeks&#x2014;transfers some dishes from the Lone Star to the Empire State and will act as a home to Bake Lab, an experimental sweet and savory bakery component led by Pastry Chef Ashley Dickson.</p>

<p>The restaurant will accommodate all types of diets, including vegetarian and gluten-free. Breakfast items include their version of the morning sandwich, a Breakfast Frankie roti wrap supped with masala eggs and cilantro chuntey and a gluten-free Jaggery Coconut Pancake dish made with rice and almonds scented with cardamom and topped with bananas and a jaggery syrup.</p>

<p>The lunch offerings are anchored by sandwiches like the Frankies, made with roti, and Bun Kebabs, a type of roadside sandwich. The Mushroom Frankie comes stuffed with pan-sauteed mushrooms, caramelized onions, sweet potatoes and black chloe and the Lamb Mint Bun Kebab with skewered lamb with onions in a kalonji and mint masala on a brioche bun.</p>

<p>In deference to the vegetarian culture of India, the restaurant will be completely meat-free on Mondays. Paneer, an Indian cheese, abounds, as do tons of roasted vegetables, mushrooms and naan breads for sopping up curries and soups. </p>
