<p>Delightful <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/03/cozy_subterranean_bars_to_wait_out.php">underground bar</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://jimmysno43.com/">Jimmy&apos;s No. 43</a> hosts a weekend full of savory stew with their annual celebration of cassoulet. This southern French dish contains hearty ingredients like meats, sausages and beans, which are slow-cooked in a a flavorful broth. First up, the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://jimmysno43.com/events/beer-and-cassoulet/">Beer &amp; Cassoulet</a></strong> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://beerandcassoulet2014.brownpapertickets.com/">$45</a>) meal on Saturday from 1 p.m. - 4 p.m., which includes three different versions of the dish (classic, goat and lamb, Kielbasa and chicken) paired with 10 beers and ciders. Then on Sunday the bar hosts its annual <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://jimmysno43.com/events/sixth-annual-cassoulet-cookoff/">Cassoulet Cookoff</a></strong> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://2014cassouletcookoff.brownpapertickets.com/">$30</a>), also from 1 p.m. to 4 p.m., featuring over 10 different professional and amateurs versions for judgement. Sample them all, pick a favorite and enjoy a gratis glass of wine, beer or cider. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-right"> <img alt="010914IPA.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/010914IPA.jpg" width="250" height="188"> <br> <i style=" width:250px; ;display:block"> <span class="photo_caption">(<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://www.flickr.com/photos/19471735@N00/5283110963/in/photolist-93RjQ4-7yRicT-8SppVP-8ahtTz">Idle Type</a>/Flickr)</span></i></div> Fans of spicy, strong beer should head to Williamsburg on Saturday for Mugs Alehouse&apos;s <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/534656">Mystery IPA Contest</a></strong>. Starting at noon, the bar will be pouring brews from 12 American IPAs in a blind taste test for imbibers to cast their impartial vote for favorite. The winning brew gets a place on the tap list for six months; last year that honor went to Greenport Harbor Brewing Company&apos;s &quot;Other Side IPA.&quot; Attendees can sample as much of the suds as they want until 3 p.m. Tickets also include bar snacks and a pint glass to take home; tickets are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/534656">$30</a>.<p></p>

<p>If you haven&apos;t yet visited Brooklyn&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/17/photos_gowanus_whole_foods.php#photo-1">most important supermarket</a> this Saturday would be a philanthropic time to do so. The Gowanus Whole Foods holds its first Community Giving Day, where they&apos;ll be donating 5% of the day&apos;s net profits to the Brooklyn Botanic Garden&#x2019;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://www.bbg.org/learn/children">children&#x2019;s education program</a>. The BBG&apos;s program offers classes to kids on subjects like trees and seeds, as well as family events like nature walks. With this money, the organization hopes to offer more free programing to kids in need. Now&apos;s as good a time as any to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140214033656/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/17/photos_gowanus_whole_foods.php#photo-23">stock up on artisanal cheese</a>!</p>
