					<p>The multi-purpose restaurant&#x2014;where you can stock up on pantry items or sit down for a full meal (or both)&#x2014;have become a popular concept. Think of April Bloomfield&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203153800/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/08/april_bloomfields_white_gold_new_an.php#photo-1">butcher shop and restaurant</a> or the grandfather of them all: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203153800/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/02/eataly_wtc_carbs_bread.php#photo-1">Eataly</a>. Well back at the beginning of November, another such outlet, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203153800/http://www.sundayinbrooklyn.com/">Sunday in Brooklyn</a>, debuted in Williamsburg, offering several different dining and eating experiences in addition to a market selling things like house-made pastries and herbed aiolis, opening soon.</p>

<p>Partners in the business are industry vets and best buds Jaime Young, former Chef de Cuisine at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203153800/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/16/michelin_guide_nyc_2017.php">Michelin darling</a> Atera, and Adam Landsman and Todd Enany, both of the Major Food Group&apos;s spendy empire. But don&apos;t expect the formality (or price tag) of anyone&apos;s former employment to be a factor here. The rustic dining space, divided into three distinct components, serves up dishes mostly under $30.</p>

<p>The ground floor houses the bar and cafe area, with the soon-to-open marketplace setup as well. Upstairs, a quaint and romantic dining room, where you&apos;re greeted with a welcome Campari cocktail and perhaps seated next to the antique-looking, wood-burning stove. The dining room itself&apos;s replete with festive ferns and other potted plants, but just a few steps up and you&apos;re in a seasonal rooftop garden with strings of lights overhead and glimpses of trees and rooftops surrounding the restaurant.</p>

<p>Peruse the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203153800/http://www.sundayinbrooklyn.com/menus/">current dinner menu</a>, which offers dishes like Black Cod Pastrami, Rye Sour Cream, Garden Pickles ($19), Honeynut Squash, Cultured Cheese, Crushed Nuts and Seeds ($10) and Aged Pork Chop, Hazelnut Dijon, Half Sour Mustard Greens ($27).</p>

<p>So far as the name goes, the restaurant&apos;s open seven days a week. Sunday just happened to be the day the guys typically had off from work, when they&apos;d head over to the space and get some work done on their labor of love.</p>

<p><em>348 Wythe Avenue; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203153800/http://www.sundayinbrooklyn.com/">sundayinbrooklyn</a></em></p>
