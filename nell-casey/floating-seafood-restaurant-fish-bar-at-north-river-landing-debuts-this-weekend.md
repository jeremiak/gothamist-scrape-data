title: Floating Seafood Restaurant Fish Bar At North River Landing Debuts This Weekend 
---

<p>Like its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025142546/http://gothamist.com/2014/05/13/lobster_boat_pier_81.php#photo-1">lobster-focused older sibling</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025142546/http://northriverfishbar.com/">Fish Bar at North River Landing</a>&#x2014;which debuts this Sunday, May 1st&#x2014;is a hybrid concept: part restaurant and bar, part mini-cruise ship. A 10,000-square-foot yacht houses a large, open air top deck, a bi-level interior restaurant space and multiple bars, all of which goes for mini jaunts into the Hudson throughout the day. </p>

<p>Unlike the more casual North River Lobster Company, which is more for hop-on, hop-off experiences, Fish Bar is a sit-down affair and offers <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025142546/http://northriverfishbar.com/reservations/">reservations</a>. On the menu, seafood-centric dishes including Plymouth Rock Oysters with a cornmeal crust and sriracha lime butter, Red Mullets with micro greens and garlic jus and a crudo version of Dover Sole. At the bars, 20 different craft beers, 26 types of champagne and &quot;farm-to-cocktail&quot; drinks featuring herbs grown on the roof of the vessel.</p>

<p>Cheers to a summer spent <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025142546/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/25/boat_dining_drinking_2016.php">floating and drinking</a>. </p>
