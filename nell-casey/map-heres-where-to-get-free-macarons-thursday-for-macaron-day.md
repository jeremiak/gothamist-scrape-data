<p>It&apos;s time once more to celebrate soft, sweet cookies of the French variety at Thursday&apos;s 5th annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://macarondaynyc.com/what_is-mac.html">Macaron Day</a> in NYC! Francois Payard&#x2014;who brought the annual celebration over from his native country&#x2014;along with other patisseries in Manhattan, Brooklyn and beyond will be offering free macarons to customers with the special password (&quot;Macaron Day NYC&quot;). It also happens to be the first day of spring. Hoo-RAY!</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://macarondaynyc.com/participants.html">Fifteen participating outlets</a> will be doling out one pastel cookie to each customer who asks, with a portion of all further macaron sales benefitting <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/https://www.cityharvest.org/">City Harvest</a>. Stalwarts <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://www.epicerieboulud.com/">Epicerie Boulud</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://bouchonbakery.com/macarons/">Bouchon Bakery</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://www.millefeuille-nyc.com/">Mille-Feuille</a> are all on the so-called &quot;Macaron Crawl,&quot; in addition to newcomer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://www.sugarandplumm.com/">Sugar &amp; Plumm</a>, dessert bar <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://www.spotdessertbar.com/">Spot</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://www.chantillybronxville.com/">Chantilly</a> in Bronxville. Francois Payard will also be debuting their special springtime macaron: Orange Blossom. </p>

<p>Think you can hit &apos;em all in one day? Use the map below to help plan your route. May the weather be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211645/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/19/a_precious_few_days_of_warmer_weath.php">ever in your favor</a>. </p>
