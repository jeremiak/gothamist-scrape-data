					<p>The newest sandwich to debut in Manhattan looks like it&apos;ll take about 350 napkins to get through. Meet Hank&apos;s Juicy Beef, a new Tribeca sandwich shop slapping together Italian beef sandwiches popular with denizens of the Windy City. We&apos;ll allow it, as long as there are no further moves to push <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110083743/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/14/jon_stewart_pizza_chicago.php">Chicago &quot;pizza&quot;</a> anywhere outside of Illinois.</p>

<p>The juicy sandwiches are a bit like <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110083743/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/15/french_dip_nyc_ultimate_guide.php">the French Dip</a>. Thinly sliced beef piled onto a hoagie roll&#x2014;or sub rolls or Italian rolls, whatever they call soft, torpedo-shaped bread&#x2014;with maybe some provolone cheese but definitely some giardineira, pickled vegetables that can be supplemented with hot peppers. The sandwiches are dipped in a flavorful jus of drippings, hence the mess.</p>

<p>Hank&apos;s has some other sandwiches available, including a vegetarian Eggplant Parm ($10.75), a Hot Italian Sausage ($9.75) and a combo of the beef and sausage ($12.75). They&apos;ve even got a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110083743/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/10/how_to_power_bowl_in_nyc.php">trendy power bowl</a> ($10.75) if you&apos;re watching your carbs or you&apos;ve given up on life.</p>

<p><em>84 Chambers Street, (646) 681-2641</em></p>
