We regret to inform you that reports of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404015740/http://gothamist.com/2014/12/10/5_food_trends_happening_in_2015_sup.php">death of avocado toast</a> have been greatly exaggerated. A new &quot;avocado bar&quot; opening in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404015740/https://industrycity.com/">Industry City</a> threatens our Instagram feeds with avocado <em>everything</em>, which, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404015740/https://www.instagram.com/avocaderia/">by the look of their Instagram</a>, promises to be an onslaught of alligator pear-topped bread the likes of which the city has never seen.
<br><br>
&quot;They&apos;re tasty and healthy, and I ate them every day,&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404015740/https://www.avocaderia.com/">Avocaderia</a> owner Francesco Brachetti <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404015740/https://www.nytimes.com/2017/04/03/dining/avocado-bar-avocaderia-brooklyn.html?_r=0">told the Times</a> of his beloved fruit. &quot;We didn&apos;t have them in Italy.&quot;

Brachetti, along with two other pals from Italy, will serve &quot;Toasts, Salads, Bowls and Smoothies,&quot; all made from avocado or featuring avocado in some kind of starring role. &quot;Avocados make just about anything better, from salads to veggie-packed salsas to smoothies,&quot; the website declares. &quot;We have developed tasty recipes just for you: we smear &apos;em onto toast, mash &apos;em into guacamole, or top your morning eggs with a couple slices.&quot;
<br><br>
Those closely following <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404015740/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/26/avocado_panic_mexico.php">the avocado crisis</a> might be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404015740/http://gothamist.com/2015/04/22/grandpa_what_were_avocados.php">rightfully concerned</a> about yet another venture that could further decimate the world&apos;s supply. But the trio claim they <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404015740/https://equalexchange.coop/avocados">source their avocados</a> from &quot;a consortium of local farmers&quot; in Mexico who treat both their workers and the environment with respect.
