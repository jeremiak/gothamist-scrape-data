					<p>Even if they&apos;re <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110091732/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/27/best_rooftop_bars_nyc.php">one of the good ones</a>, rooftop destination bars&#x2014;usually the ones attached to hotels&#x2014;can be sceney nightmares with overpriced drinks and an <em>American Psycho</em> crowd most of us would rather avoid. It&apos;s too soon to tell what kind of clientele <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110091732/http://www.socialdrinkandfood.com/">Social Drink &amp; Food</a>, the new outdoor drinking spot attached to the YOTEL hotel in Hell&apos;s Kitchen will attract (it opens tonight), but at least the prices are reasonable. Paying $7 for a glass of wine or pint of beer wouldn&apos;t seem out of place at some of the city&apos;s nicer watering holes, and $12 for a cocktail is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110091732/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/15/best_cocktail_bars_nyc.php">astonishingly reasonable</a> on a roof these days. </p>

<p>The views from the fourth floor space won&apos;t be as sweeping and dramatic as other aeries, but it&apos;s a quieter retreat from the jostle of 10th Avenue. Fitting for today&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110091732/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/28/rainbow_latte_video_whoa.php">rainbow-obsessed</a> landscape, there&apos;s crocheted yarn art by London Kaye, who did some <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110091732/http://gothamist.com/tags/olek">Olek-style</a> work on part of the space, and other furnishings are similarly vibrant to match. </p>

<p>A Cobb Salad ($13) with grilled romaine, sliced Steak Frites ($19) with chimichurri and DIY Chicken Shashlik ($14) chicken skewers and pita sound like fine enough drinking accompaniments. The Hula Hula pizza ($12) is the most interesting of the bunch, with a red sauce base topped with mozzarella, ham, pineapple and fresh jalapeno slices. The standout cocktail is most certainly the Tropical Triple ($23), a mix of rum, pine and coconut syrups, and a &quot;house tiki tincture,&quot; shaken up and served inside a pineapple. </p>

<p>London-based <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110091732/http://rooftopcinemaclub.com/">Rooftop Cinema Club</a> is also doing a series of outdoor films in the space this summer. It looks like <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110091732/http://rooftopcinemaclub.com/newyork/tickets/yotel/">the May selections</a> are already sold out, but the program runs through September with $30 tickets that include the screening, personal wireless headphones and a food and drink pairing that&apos;s matched to the film. </p>

<p><em>570 Tenth Avenue on the 4th floor of YOTEL, 646-449-7790; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110091732/http://www.socialdrinkandfood.com/">socialdrinkandfood.com</a></em></p>
