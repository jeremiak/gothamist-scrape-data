</div></div>					<p>It&apos;s been a long journey for Red Hook&apos;s newcomer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429161629/http://www.grindhausbrooklyn.com/">Grindhaus</a>, which opened last Thursday after five years of planning, building and re-building. Proprietess Erin Norris broke ground on her restaurant back in 2008, following an eight month wait for approval from DOB, only to discover rotten, load-bearing support beams; then Hurricane Sandy came along. Suffice to say, it&apos;s been a rough road, but the restaurant has moved forward and even secured their liquor license&#x2014;the night before they were scheduled to open.</p>

<p>Norris tapped Aaron Taber, a Pines alum, and Leon Douglas to design and flush out the restaurant&apos;s menu, which currently offers dishes like beef tartare with bone marrow and trout roe ($15) and Merguez sausage with chickpea, Harissa and artichoke ($22). They&apos;re also focusing on vegetarian options, including spiagarello (a variety of broccoli) with apple, lovage and fresh cheese ($9) and mushrooms en papillote ($19) with brown butter, farro verde and egg yolk. </p>

<p>The restaurant makes an effort to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429161629/http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20131130/red-hook/after-five-year-wait-red-hooks-grindhaus-open-dec-5">accommodate allergies and dietary restrictions</a>, including making vegan and gluten-free dishes. They aren&apos;t eschewing the bread altogether, however; they have plans to build a backyard wood oven to back up fresh loaves. For now, they&apos;re <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429161629/http://instagram.com/p/cR0846EjxJ/">turning out homemade breads</a> in a tiny convection oven in the kitchen.</p>
