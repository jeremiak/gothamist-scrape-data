<p>With balmy temperatures headed our way this weekend, indulge in a Mediterranean brunch at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/24/ousia_greek_hells_kitchen.php#photo-1">newly minted restaurant</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647/http://www.ousianyc.com/">Ousia</a>, which begins midday weekend meal service this weekend. Stay virtuous with Greek Yogurt ($10) with almond granola or embrace hedonism with the Donut Pikilia ($10), a sampler of their homemade donuts. They&apos;re doing a riff on the Greek Diner ($14) classics with two eggs, smashed potatoes and choice of meats. They&apos;re offering a full lunch-type menu, too, plus brunch cocktails like Bloody Marys ($12) and a Build Your Own Mimosa ($12). </p>

<p>Channel the sea (as in sea<em>food</em>) at Williamsburg&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647/http://www.allhands.nyc/">All Hands</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/21/all_hands_williamsburg.php#photo-1">take in the bridge views</a> while dining on their new brunch menu, which debuts on Sunday. Fish options include a Tuna Reuben ($16) with pickle cabbage, provolone cheese and Russian dressing. Brunch staples are updated, like Poached Egg Benedict ($16) with salmon rillettes on potato pancakes and Steak and Eggs ($17) can be upgraded with Maryland crabmeat for $9. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="120915insa2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/120915insa2.jpg" width="640" height="479"> <br> <i> (Craig LaCourt)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Tiki bar, Korean BBQ hotspot and karaoke destination <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647/http://www.insabrooklyn.com/">Insa</a> is throwing a giant fundraiser for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647/https://www.rescue.org/">International Rescue Committee</a> Sunday evening. Beginning at 11:30 p.m., the restaurant will offer free entry and free karaoke all night and will be selling $9 plates of Asian nachos prepared by chef Sohui Kim. 100% of the proceeds from the evening will be donated to the Committee, which assists refugees coming from conflict and disaster struck regions. </p>

<p>Indulge in many varieties of dolma&#x2014;a stuffed vegetable dish common in the Middle East&#x2014;this Sunday at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647/https://www.facebook.com/events/1249837428442767/">4th Annual DOLMA Festival</a> in Douglaston, Queens. Hosted by the ARENI Choir of New York, organizers promise &quot;delicious homemade meat and vegetarian dolmas ranging from traditional grape leaf dolma to modern invented dolmas&quot; prepared by the choir family. In addition to the dolma, snack on mezze and desserts and dance to tunes from the on-site DJ. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015142647/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/dolma-festival-areni-festival-tickets-31188781485?aff=es2">Tickets $50</a>.</p>
