title: Tickets Still Available For This Year's Village Voice Choice Eats
---


	
	
	
	<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="031412choice.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/031412choice.jpg" width="640" height="480" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>Time to invest in some elastic waist pants: this year&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://choiceeats.villagevoice.com/2013/">Village Voice Choice Eats</a> foodie feeding frenzy is coming up next month on Tuesday, March 19th. The annual food festival, now in its 6th year, brings together more than 50 of the city&apos;s most far-flung restaurants, plus plenty of complimentary booze&#x2014;Alphabet City Brewing Company, Bulldog Gin, Barefoot Wine &amp; Bubbly and a dozen others&#x2014;to wash it all down. As usual, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/14/six_scrumptious_offerings_from_last.php#photo-1">last year&apos;s event</a> got crowded as the night went on but <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2011/09/28/six_tips_for_actually_enjoying_food.php">follow our helpful advice</a> and you&apos;ll be able to stuff yourself headache-free&#x2014;at least until your hangover Wednesday morning.</p>

<p>Village Voice food critics <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2008/03/11/robert_sietsema.php">Robert Sietsema</a> and Tejal Rao curated <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://choiceeats.villagevoice.com/2013/restaurants.php">this year&apos;s restaurant lineup</a>, which includes <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/17/shake_the_winter_doldrums_with_bobw.php">Bobwhite Lunch and Supper Counter</a>&apos;s killer friend chicken, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/17/shake_the_winter_doldrums_with_bobw.php">Brooklyn Kolache</a>, veggie delights from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2008/11/01/openings_roundup_vero_dirt_candy.php">Dirt Candy</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/27/littleneck_brooklyns_new_new_englan.php">Littleneck</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2006/04/28/surfs_up_ditch.php">Ditch Plains</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://gothamist.com/2011/11/16/joedough_brings_the_brisket_to_the.php#photo-1">JoeDough</a> and tons more eateries from across the five boroughs. There&apos;s also an entire section devoted to sweets from dessert luminaries at Ample Hills Creamery, Fay Da Bakery and more. </p>

<p>VIP tickets to the event are sold out, but General Admission tickets <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://www.ticketfly.com/purchase/eventV2/203039?utm_medium=bks&amp;wrKey=84286C13F7B2A17373B8C9E611F56A0E">are still available</a> for now. Your $50 ticket&#x2014;proceeds from which will once again benefit <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130219210527/http://www.slowfoodnyc.org/">Slow Food NYC</a>&#x2014;buys you three hours of face-stuffing, gut-busting glory. If you go, plan on getting there before the doors open to get in early and make the most of it. <br>
</p>
