<p>The fun and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/03/five_reasons_new_taste_of_the_uws_i.php">belly-filling</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://tasteuws.com/">Taste of the UWS</a> returns for its 10th year this weekend. On Friday, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://tasteuws.com/summerevents/">Summer in the City: Surf &amp; Turf</a> ($105) even offers fare from 40 local restaurants (Maison Pickle, Kirsh Bakery, Fat Monk and others) for a walk-around tasting event with unlimited food and alcohol. Saturday it&apos;s the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://tasteuws.com/best-of-the-west/">Best of the West</a> ($135), where chef/restaurateurs April Bloomfield and Ken Friedman will be honored amidst more local restaurant fare from high-end spots including Jean-Georges, Boulud Sud, Dovetail and Rosa Mexicano. </p>

<p>Future food: Snap a picture of the barricade around the site of the forthcoming <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://andpizza.com/nyc/">&amp;pizza;</a> location at 15 W 28th Street between now and May 31st and score a free pizza! Anyone who takes a photo and posts it to social media with the hashtag #NYCandpizza will get a credit for a free pie when the restaurant opens this summer.</p>

<p>Stuff your face with chili on Saturday at the 2017 edition of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://www.ticketfly.com/purchase/event/1445590?utm_source=MEL&amp;utm_medium=219313">Texas Chili Cook-Off</a>, held this year at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.solar1.org/events/">Solar One</a> in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://www.google.com/maps/place/24-20+Fdr+Drive+Service+Rd+E,+New+York,+NY+10010/@40.7345981,-73.9742149,15z/data=!4m2!3m1!1s0x89c2590cd6fb3a65:0xcdf74755ff0157e9?hl=en-US">Stuyvesant Cove</a>. Beginning at noon, dozens of teams will compete for the best chili in the batch, offering samples to attendees, who also get <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://www.facebook.com/events/1858867067702286/">unlimited Shiner beer</a> and some live musical entertainment. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://www.ticketfly.com/purchase/event/1445590?utm_source=MEL&amp;utm_medium=219313">Individual tickets $65</a>.  </p>

<p>Slap on a bib and tuck into some mudbugs on Saturday at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://8thannualcrawfishboil.brownpapertickets.com/">8th Annual Crawfish Boil</a> ($30) at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.sycamorebrooklyn.com/">Sycamore Bar</a> in Flatbush in partnership <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.gaddylane.com/">Gaddy Lane</a>. The bar is offering three seatings (1, 3 and 5 p.m.) to devour a tray of crawfish with the fixings, a side (red beans and rice, southern slaw and corn bread pudding), and a beverage. Your ticket also buys you two raffle tickets to be entered to win gift certificates to Sycamore, food from Gaddy Lane and other prizes. </p>

<p>It may seem a little odd to some, but the apple trees growing in Brooklyn&apos;s beautiful <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.green-wood.com/">Green-Wood Cemetery</a> have been used to make hard apple cider and this Sunday, visitors will have a chance to drink it. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.green-wood.com/event/pouring-green-wood/">Pouring Green-Wood</a> event ($30) offers a unique opportunity to drink cider made from &quot;Baldwin, Granny Smith, various crab apples, and a yet-to-be-identified apple variety&quot; that Joy Doumis and Jeremy Hammond used to make their special cider, called Proper Cider. Learn how to make your own at the event and drink a bottle of the cider if you attend. </p>

<p>Sunday also brings the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://www.ticketfly.com/purchase/event/1445590?utm_source=MEL&amp;utm_medium=219313">Speed Rack Finals</a>, the all-female speed cocktail event that benefits breast cancer charities. Competitors from across the country&#x2014;including local finalists Anna Wingfield (Mother&#x2019;s Ruin, Ba&#x2019;sik) and Kristine Serrano (Forest Hills Station House)&#x2014;will vie for the Ms. Speed Rack 2017 title by mixing up cocktails with more speed and finesse than their fellow bartenders. To witness this awesome spectacle, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/speed-rack-season-6-national-finals-tickets-32963040341?aff=efbeventtix">purchase $25 tickets here</a>. </p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.thenyic.org/">New York Immigration Coalition</a> (NYIC) leads a dumpling tour on Sunday that highlights immigrant-owned businesses in recognition of the Coalition&apos;s 30th birthday. &quot;In light of the current political climate, The Dumpling Tour group decided to focus its effort to help the immigrant community in NYC,&quot; said a Coalition spokesperson. &quot;Its founder, Tom Nguyen, comes from a family of Vietnam War refugees, so the issue hits close to home.&quot; The tour begins with free coffee at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://uniongaragenyc.com/">Union Garage</a> at 11:00 a.m., followed by stops at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.mimichengs.com/">Mimi Cheng&apos;s</a> (1 p.m.), <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.beardpapa.com/">Beard Papa&apos;s</a> (2:30 p.m.), <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://empanadascafe.com/">Empanadas Cafe</a> (4:30 p.m.) and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.mashaandthebear.nyc/">Masha &amp; The Bear</a> (6 p.m.). Join the tour wherever (times are approximate); <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616011352/http://www.dumplingtournyc.com/products">merch sales</a> benefit NYIC.</p>
