					<p>It takes a daring individual to open any restaurant serving pizza in the hyper-critical pizza landscape of New York City and a particular kind of madman to open a vegan pizzeria at that. Yet that&apos;s just what restaurateur Matthew Kenney has done as of today, opening up <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021020437/http://matthewkenneycuisine.com/hospitality/00-co/">00+Co</a>, where chef Scott Winegard will be turning out vegan pies from a wood-fired oven. Winegard trained in Naples and is employing the lauded 00 flour to make his crusts; &quot;we&apos;re keeping with tradition to some extent,&quot; Winegard says. &quot;So we don&apos;t upset the purists.&quot;</p>

<p>Tradition, insofar as pizza-making history goes, usually means a dairy-based cheese, which obviously won&apos;t be the case here. The restaurant is making its own nut-based cheeses from almonds and cashews, resulting in what Winegard describes as similar to farmer&apos;s cheese or ricotta. &quot;It&apos;s not a cheese where you&apos;re going to be like, &apos;Oh my god, this is just like mozzarella,&apos;&quot; Winegard explains. &quot;But it&apos;s definitely going to have a lot of flavor and you&apos;re definitely going to be reminded of what it&apos;s supposed to be.&quot; </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="021116co_pizza.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021020437im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/021116co_pizza.jpg" width="640" height="510"> <br> <i> (courtesy 00 + Co.)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Winegard says his pies are similar to a Neapolitan style, but they certainly have their own unique character outside of their animal-free status. The pies are rustically-shaped but artistically topped, playing on the natural beauty of the ingredients. &quot;Vegetables honestly just taste better, the flavors last longer,&quot; Winegard professes. &quot;They&apos;re brighter, they&apos;re crisper, they&apos;re more vibrant, there&apos;s so much more color. There&apos;s so much vibrancy! I think it&apos;s more exciting.&quot;</p>

<p>A classic margarita-style pie with tomato, basil and cashew mozzarella ($15) will be on offer, as will a pie topped with a farro-fennel &quot;sausage&quot; ($18). None of the pies employ any faux-meat, but the restaurant is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021020437/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/11/by_chloe_vegan_bacon_magic_recipe.php">getting creative with mushrooms</a>, marinating shiitakes in umeboshi vinegar, tamari and olive oil to imbue them with essence of &quot;anchovy.&quot; They&apos;ll join potatoes, capers and baby kale in a pie bound together with almond cream ($16). The kitchen will turn out a selection of vegan small plates, too, and cheese plates of their house-made vegan cheese.</p>

<p>&quot;I think that the people that predominantly eat plant-based are gonna be really excited about us and they&#x2019;re gonna be our biggest champions to talk to other people that might not eat this way all the time,&quot; Winegard says, musing on where 00 + Co. will fit in amongst other pizzerias. &quot;There&#x2019;s going to be a real genuine interest in what we&#x2019;re doing and how we do it and as long as we make a delicious product I think that&#x2019;s really what we&#x2019;re gonna stand on, just being delicious.&quot;</p>
