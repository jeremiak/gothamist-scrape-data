					<p>One of fall&apos;s most <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170325025437/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/25/fall_restaurant_preview_2016.php">anticipated new restaurants</a> debuts today in the East Village with the opening of Drunken Dumpling, a mother-son collaboration that&apos;s poised to explode the city&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170325025437/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/31/best_soup_dumplings_nyc.php">soup dumpling scene</a>. They&apos;ll be offering something they&apos;re calling the XL Xiao Long Bao, an enormous soup dumpling &quot;the size of a bowl&quot; that comes with a straw for sucking up all the savory broth. Throw <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170325025437/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/31/how_to_eat_a_xiao_long_bao.php">all that training</a> out the window.</p>

<p>Qihui Guan&#x2014;a mathematics professor turned dumpling-maker at soup dumpling hype machine Joe&apos;s Shanghai&#x2014;and her son Yuan Lee will be steaming up all manner of potstickers, like their enormous signature XLB ($12.75) stuffed with a meatball of crab, scallop and shrimp suspended in an 8-hour broth made from chicken and vegetables. More reasonably-sized soup dumplings are on offer, too, including a Stony Mountain Ranch pork version ($11.75) and one made with crab ($12.75).</p>

<p>Beyond the soup variety, chicken, shrimp and vegetable dumplings; steamed pork buns; and a dessert bao and Red Bean Buns with honey and red bean paste. They&apos;ll also serve sake, wine and beer, from local spots like Sixpoint in addition to varieties from Asia including Qingdao Beer. </p>

<p><em>137 1st Avenue, 212-982-8882</em></p>
