					<p>The pigs have been replaced by marlins and the barbecue with fish, but many of the bones of the old Brother Jimmy&apos;s space on 2nd Avenue remain in its new incarnation as Ziggy&apos;s, a mezcal and mariscos bar opening next week. Corrugated metal and wooden barrels fit the California (as opposed to Carolina) vibe equally, it seems, but the uniquely-shaped bar and the Skee ball have been nixed in favor of a more open floor plan. If it sounds like I know a lot, I do: I worked for the company for five years before decamping to the blogosphere.</p>

<p>Noah Arenstein, proprietor of excellent new Mexican eatery El Atoradero in Prospect Heights, teamed up with Brother Jimmy himself, Jimmy Goldman, to open Ziggy&apos;s. Here, Arenstein&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161218173821/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/01/mezcal_101_nyc.php">knowledge of mezcal</a> will be put to good use. Cocktails skew &quot;retro California,&quot; according to Arenstein, which means tiki offerings like Pain Killers ($13) and Mai Tais ($14) made with mezcal. Brother Jimmy&apos;s notorious Swamp Water fishbowls (mercifully) aren&apos;t returning, but a Scorpion Bowl ($30) has moved in to take its place. </p>

<p>Though the food offerings include a wide range of Mexican and Mexican-inspired dishes, seafood&apos;s the main attraction. &quot;It&apos;s really not a thing that they have in New York City,&quot; Arenstein says of mariscos. &quot;Big seafood platters, some of it bathed in butter or cooked in Mexican beer. It&apos;s Pacific coast of Mexico seafood bowls.&quot;</p>

<p>Platters serve between one and six people and come filled with oysters, clams, crab claws, scallops and mussels with a side of Ziggy&apos;s Sauce, their take on the typical guajillo chili sauce served with the seafood. Raw preparations includine oysters ($1 for happy hour and on Mondays) and the Pacific Seafood Cocktail ($15), shrimp calamari and crab served in a tomato sauce with tortilla chips.</p>

<p>Ziggy&apos;s is slated to open Monday.</p>
