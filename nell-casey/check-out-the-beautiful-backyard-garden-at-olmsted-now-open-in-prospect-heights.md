					<p>There&apos;s farm to table and then there&apos;s backyard to table, which could be the third wave locavore movement as modeled by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630095256/http://olmstednyc.com/">Olmsted</a>, a new restaurant that opened last night in Prospect Heights. Chef Greg Baxtrom, whose resume reads like the world&apos;s most qualified chef&#x2014;Per Se and Blue Hill at Stone Barns are among his many experiences&#x2014;and Ian Rothman, the &quot;former horticulturist at Atera,&quot; are hoeing their own produce, herbs and spices in their gorgeous backyard garden, which helps populate the plates of the restaurant itself. </p>

<p>The green stalks in the Asparagus Salad ($11) with wheatgrass aioli? Those are from out back, as are the lemons for the potato dauphinoise that accompany the NY Grassfed Steak ($23). Fragrant flowering plants like lavender pop up in desserts like the frozen yogurt with lavender and honey, as well as in cocktails like the Lavender ($12), made with bourbon, herbs de provence, sherry and black walnut. Check out both full menus below.</p>

<p>If the name seems familiar, you need only look a few blocks south to Prospect Park, designed by one Frederick Law Olmsted, who&apos;s also responsible for Central Park. The pastoral theme continues with repurposed wood from the Bastrom family&apos;s barn at their home outside Chicago. But the backyard... that&apos;s where it&apos;s at. </p>

<p><em>659 Vanderbilt Avenue at Park Place, 718-552-2610, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630095256/http://olmstednyc.com/">olmstednyc.com</a></em></p>
