The chain just announced the re-release of the burger-scented candles, which are currently &quot;back by popular demand!&quot; and available <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231233633/http://whitecastle.csepromo.com/">on their website</a> until supplies run out.<p></p>

<p>White Castle first debuted the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231233633/http://newyork.grubstreet.com/2010/05/there_is_a_white_castle_candle.html">nausea-inducing</a> candles <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231233633/http://gothamist.com/2010/05/05/white_castle_now_in_scented_candle.php">two years ago</a> and have sold <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231233633/http://www.thedailymeal.com/white-castles-slider-scented-candle-returns">more than 10,000 eau-de-slider candles</a> to date. Proceeds from each sale benefit <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231233633/http://www.autismspeaks.org/">Austism Speaks</a>, so we won&apos;t get too judgmental about thousands of people&apos;s questionable taste in aromatherapy. You can <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231233633/http://whitecastle.csepromo.com/">purchase your own wax monstrosity for $15</a>, which also comes with a ceramic replica of a White Castle slider box. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231233633/http://www.whitecastle.com/food/menu">Chicken Ring</a>-scented air freshener not included. </p>
