<p>Cool Gowanus brewpub <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/http://www.threesbrewing.com/">Threes Brewing</a> has opened its large backyard for the season and also a series of new weekly specials with their food partner <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/http://the-meathook.com/">The Meat Hook</a>. Mondays it&apos;s pizza night, with meat-based pies like braised lamb neck, tomato sauce, basil. Also on Mondays: porchetta. And fancy porchetta to boot, served by a tie-clad waiter with radicchio salad for $18. Finally, Tuesdays are a &quot;themed&quot; steak night, like &quot;Gonzo New Orleans&#x201D; (with lamb bacon hushpuppies and spicy creole slaw) for $25. </p>

<p>East Williamsburg Taiwanese restaurant <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/http://www.winsonbrooklyn.com/#/?_k=wn75hx">Win Son</a> hosts <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/smoke-show-win-son-authentic-north-carolina-bbq-tickets-33231739026">Smoke Show</a></strong>, a whole hog/North Carolina-type bbq feast Monday evening. Chef Jon Botta trained for a decade under famed pitmaster Ed Mitchell, then moved to NYC to work at Ducks Eatery and open Harry and Ida&apos;s. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/smoke-show-win-son-authentic-north-carolina-bbq-tickets-33231739026">menu for the special dinner</a> includes cold smoked oysters, whole smoked hog, smoked sausage, cast iron corn bread and tableside quick pickles. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/smoke-show-win-son-authentic-north-carolina-bbq-tickets-33231739026">Tickets are $75</a> and inlcude one free drink. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="041017banhmi.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/041017banhmi.jpg" width="640" height="428"> <br> <i> Red Eye Beef and High Five Pork Belly (BONMi)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>A second location of Vietnamese fast-casual spot <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/http://eatbonmi.com/">BONMi</a> opened last week at 119 23rd Street between Park and Lex and they&apos;re celebrating with 2-for-1 entrees Monday and Tuesday from 11:30 a.m. to 2 p.m. Swing by for dishes including the Red Eye Banh Mi Sandwich with red curry beef or the High Five Rice &amp; Quinoa Bowl with five spice pulled pork. </p>

<p>A &quot;plant-powered&quot; five course dinner called <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/spring-fever-5-course-plant-powered-dinner-tickets-32970436463">Spring Fever</a></strong> is popping up at Williamsburg vegan spot <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/https://www.littlechoc.nyc/">Little Choc Apothecary</a> on Wednesday. Chef Leo will be whipping up dishes including stuffed crimini with babaganouch in marinara sauce, beet seitan with carrot noodles and buckwheat brownie sundaes with blueberry chia pudding. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170419022147/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/spring-fever-5-course-plant-powered-dinner-tickets-32970436463">Tickets $69</a>. </p>
