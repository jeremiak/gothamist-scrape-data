					<p>In LA, sushi chef Kazunori Nozawa has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170714020049/http://laist.com/2012/01/26/sayonara_sushi_nazi_sushi_nozawa_cl.php">gained a reputation</a> for his fastidious, uncompromising view of sushi, namely that it should be as fresh and well-sourced as possible, but also that the only person who really knows what&apos;s up is the chef. To that end, the menu&apos;s focused around a series of omakase experiences known as the &quot;Trust Me&quot; options, which the chef brought to NYC Friday with the opening of his first location outside of California. </p>

<p>Debuting for lunch and dinner in the Flatiron, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170714020049/http://sugarfishsushi.com/nyc-flatiron/">Sugarfish</a> aims to bridge the gap between <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170714020049/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/03/akashi_japanese_omakase.php#photo-1">formal omakase meals</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170714020049/http://gothamist.com/2014/04/02/the_best_sushi_in_nyc.php">casual neighborhood joints</a>, fusing the two ideas and making curated sushi experiences approachable and affordable for a wider audience. The menu helpfully lays out a few ground rules&#x2014;eat hand rolls immediately and don&apos;t dip sauced dishes into soy&#x2014;and politely lets guests know there won&apos;t be any special requests honored, like asking for additional sauces.</p>

<p>&quot;Almost everyone orders one of the three Trust Me&apos;s,&quot; the menu heralds, almost cautioning anyone who chooses to select pieces a la carte. There&apos;s the standard Trust Me ($39, gratuity included) that offers organic edamame, tuna sashimi, 8 pieces of sushi and two hand rolls, and a &quot;Lite&quot; option ($27) that cuts both the sushi and hand roll courses in half. &quot;The Nozawa&quot; deluxe option ($51) mirrors much of the original omakase, but with four additional pieces of sushi, including a &quot;Daily Special.&quot; </p>

<p>And before you ask, yes, the rice is supposed to be warm, so it &quot;melts in the mouth.&quot;</p>

<p><em>33 East 20th Street, (347) 705-8100; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170714020049/http://sugarfishsushi.com/nyc-flatiron/">sugarfishsushi.com</a></em></p>
