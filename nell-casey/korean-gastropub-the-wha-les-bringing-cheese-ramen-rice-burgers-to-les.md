					<p>A new restaurant extolling the virtues of eating while drinking&#x2014;or is it the other way around?&#x2014;is serving up piping hot bowls of cheese ramen, poke salad, and a boat load of wings to Clinton Street. WhaLES, which opened in early June, advertises itself as a Korean gastropub, a casual spot to sip cocktails while munching on Korean fried chicken. In Korea, you&apos;ll call someone a &quot;whale&quot; if they&apos;re dropping tons of cash&#x2014;but the menu here is very heavy drinking, comfort food-eating friendly as far as prices go.</p>

<p>They&apos;re specializing in the kinds of things that soak up alcohol well, like a simple but hearty Cheese Ramen ($7) with onions, bean sprouts and egg, a popular DIY soup one might make at home after hitting the bar. Korean Fried Chicken gets served up as wings, drumsticks or boneless Karage ($12), an excellent pairing with a cold beer or AGWA (a coca leaf liquor), especially during happy hour where drinks are half off.</p>

<p>Whether intentional for the gluten-free movement or just an appreciation of the versatility of rice, the grain is employed as a base for the Rice Pizza ($10) topped with a tuna, onion, paprika, corn and mozzarella cheese in a spicy sauce. It&apos;s also part of the bun holding together the Rice Burger ($7), made with chicken or beef and topped with lettuce, tomato, onion, fried egg and a spicy mayo, all wrapped in edible soy paper.</p>

<p>There&apos;s been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113153634/http://www.boweryboogie.com/2016/05/cursed-71-clinton-street-space-reboots-whales-week/">a lot of turnover</a> in the 71 Clinton Space in recent years, but so far the restaurant has been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113153634/http://www.yelp.com/biz/whales-new-york">well received</a> in an area saturated with options. </p>

<p><em>71 Clinton Street, 646-882-1305</em></p>
