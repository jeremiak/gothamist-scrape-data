					<p>When they ran the tiny kitchen at dinnertable in the East Village, husband-and-wife Chefs Scott Tacinelli and Angie Rito made a big splash with their unique and delicious dishes, in particular, their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021005833/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/29/dinnertable_reverse_speakeasy.php#photo-1">incredible lasagna for two</a>. Given the lasagna&apos;s popularity, it&apos;s not a surprise that the duo would make pasta the focus of their new endeavor, having left dinnertable. Their next venture: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021005833/http://www.spaghetti-and-meatballs.com/pasta-omakase">Pasta Omakase by Angie &amp; Scott</a>, a limited tasting menu that&apos;s all about pasta.</p>

<p>On Sunday evenings from November 6th through December 18th, they&apos;ll be setting up an encampment in the kitchen at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021005833/http://www.newyorksushiko.com/">New York Sushi Ko</a>&#x2014;the couple happen to live upstairs&#x2014;to turn out 10 carb-centric courses of pastas inspired by both Italy and Japan. Courses include three small plates to start, followed by six pastas and concluding with dessert. </p>

<p>Possible pastas include Buffalo Milk Caramelle with black sesame and persimmon; Kobe Carne Cruda with  Calabrian chili, anchovy and sesame; Tortelletti In Brodo with stracchino, pistachio, mortadella &quot;tonkotsu&quot;; and Pici di Grano Arso with duck sausage and preserved plum.</p>

<p>The Sunday seatings begin at 7 p.m. with room for just 10 diners at each. Tickets are $150 and include all the food, a sake-based cocktail, unlimited wine, tax and tip. </p>

<p><em>Pasta Omakase will pop-up at New York Sushi Ko at 91 Clinton Street</em></p>
