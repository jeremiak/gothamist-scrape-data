					<p>Ramen continues to be the most <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031045901/http://gothamist.com/tags/ramen">pervasive</a> Japanese noodle export in NYC so far, but udon&apos;s about to get some love, thanks to a Japanese chain opening up its first restaurant in the USA. On Monday, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031045901/http://www.tsurutontan.com/">TsuruTonTan</a> makes its debut on East 16th Street, where Danny Meyer&apos;s celebrated Union Square Cafe <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031045901/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/22/union_square_cafe_saved.php">once lived</a>. The restaurant&apos;s main drive is different types of udon, the thick, versatile noodles that are one of the national dishes of Japan.</p>

<p>Udon dishes come in many forms, but most of us have tried the doughy noodles in some kind of clear, savory broth, perhaps topped with shrimp tempura, or a richer curry sauce. Tsurutontan offers both, plus milky &quot;creme&quot; varieties and others that come without any broth at all, like their Mentai Caviar Udon, a signature dish that employs cod roe. </p>

<p>Beyond the dozens of different udons, this incarnation will also offer sashimi, Tatsuta Fried Chicken topped with a thick Shibazuke Tartar sauce, and other non-noodle-based dishes.</p>

<p><em>21 E 16th Street, (212) 989-1000; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031045901/http://www.tsurutontan.com/">website</a></em></p>
