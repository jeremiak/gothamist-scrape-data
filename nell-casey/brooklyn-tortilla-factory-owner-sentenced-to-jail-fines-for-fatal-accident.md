<p>The owner of a tortilla factory in East Williamsburg has been sentenced to 90 days in prison and ordered to pay $450,000 in restitution following a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160512152740/http://gothamist.com/2011/01/24/worker_dies_in_tortilla_mixer_at_br.php">deadly accident at his facility</a> in 2011. According to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160512152740/http://www.ag.ny.gov/press-release/ag-schneiderman-announces-sentencing-brooklyn-tortilla-factory-owner-labor-law-and">Attorney General Eric Schneiderman</a>, Erasmo Ponce, owner of Tortilleria Chinantla, pleaded guilty to violating worker&apos;s compensation and wage laws after investigators found his company&apos;s policies weren&apos;t up to date at the time of the accident. </p>

<p>In January of 2011, 22-year-old Juan Baten was pulled into a dough mixing machine and his neck was broken, killing him almost instantly. Because Ponce failed to have worker&apos;s compensation insurance&#x2014;a felony in New York State&#x2014;Baten&apos;s baby daughter and girlfriend were <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160512152740/http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20130708/east-williamsburg/owner-of-tortilla-factory-where-worker-died-sentenced-90-days-prison">left with nothing</a>. Ponce was eventually <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160512152740/http://blogs.villagevoice.com/runninscared/2013/07/brooklyn_tortil.php">arrested in March of 2012</a> and charged with 26 felony counts and 23 misdemeanors and the factory was fined over $62,000 for failing to install a safety guard on the machine that killed Baten. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160512152740/http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20130708/east-williamsburg/owner-of-tortilla-factory-where-worker-died-sentenced-90-days-prison">DNAinfo reports</a> that Ponce will serve his jail sentence at Rikers on the weekends&#x2014;yes, that&apos;s a thing&#x2014;and the factory will remain operational. </p>
