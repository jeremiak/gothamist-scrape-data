					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161028104847/http://www.21greenpoint.com/">21 Greenpoint</a>, the restaurant made famous before it even opened thanks to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161028104847/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/17/bill_murray_bartender.php#photo-1">celebrity bartender Bill Murray</a>, officially opened Wednesday evening inside a reboot of the former River Styx space adjacent to WNYC Transmitter Park. It&apos;s run by Sydney Silver and Homer Murray&#x2014;Bill&apos;s son, hence the guest bartending stint&#x2014;who&apos;ve tapped former Extra Fancy chef Sean Telo to run the kitchen, with its wood-burning oven that&apos;ll turn out pizzas and other roasted items and whatever else Telo&apos;s inspired by that day.</p>

<p>At Extra Fancy, Telo expertly displayed his finesse with seafood, and he&apos;s using that skill here, too. Darling canape-esque bites of salmon pastrami are curled around a creamy sauce gribiche and topped with salmon roe on rye bread. Plump porgy, served raw, takes a bath with plum, jalapeno and grapefruit. Firmly not in precious fish territory: the sinful-sounding steak tartare, which is served inside a roasted marrow bone with lamb fat bread.</p>

<p>The second Sean in the team is Sean McClure, the bar director, who was shaking up some serious cocktails at the preview parties as a foil to Murray&apos;s shots of tequila. Saddle up to the curvy bar for drinks like the Smokin&apos; Peaches with both tequila and mezcal plus peach and a habanero shrub. The 6&apos;7&apos;8&apos; Bunch&#x2014;of Banana Song fame&#x2014;is a tropical punch of banana rum, pineapple, coconut, lime, orange and grapefruit. </p>
