	<p>Now celebrating its 20th year, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130220131426/http://canstruction.org/">Canstruction</a> has returned to lower Manhattan in all its can-tastic glory. The annual design competition and charity event was originally scheduled to take place last November but because of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130220131426/http://gothamist.com/tags/hurricanesandy">Hurricane Sandy</a> organizers had to postpone the event until this month. </p>

<p>Twenty-six New York City architecture and design firms participated this year, using over 100,000 cans of food to build their works of art. A Jeff Koons dog, a beehive, a classic NYC coffee cup and a model of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130220131426/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/03/plans_for_giant_staten_island_ferri.php">Staten Island ferris wheel</a> are just a few of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130220131426/http://imgur.com/a/H8dwK">imaginative offerings</a> from this year&apos;s competition. </p>

<p>The winning can sculpture will be chosen at a private event this evening and you can see all of the designs in person at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130220131426/https://www.worldfinancialcenter.com/canstruction">World Financial Center</a> until 5 p.m. on February 11th. After that, the sculptures will be disassembled and the cans donated to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130220131426/http://www.cityharvest.org/">City Harvest</a>.</p>

<p>For a peek at how the sculptures come together, check out this timelapse of LERA CANstruction 2012 - &quot;Topping Hunger&quot; being assembled. </p>
