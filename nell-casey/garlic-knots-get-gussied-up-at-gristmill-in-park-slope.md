					<p>We&apos;d say don&apos;t mess with something as perfectly simple as the garlic knot, but then again the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025131237/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/20/photos_heres_what_gothamist_ate_thi.php?gallery0Pic=4#photo-1">pretzel croissant</a> is mind-blowing, so sometimes the classics benefit from some tinkering. At <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025131237/http://www.gristmillbk.com/">Gristmill</a>, which opened in Park Slope this week, the pizzeria staples get tossed in &quot;everything&quot; seasonings, black pepper and herb varieties. The knots are made from two different types of flour, too: a spelt variety from Four Star Farms and a high extraction from Farmer Brown. Leftover pizza dough this is not.</p>

<p>In a time when fitness heads speak breathlessly about the value of a paleo lifestyle, chef Jake Novick-Finder&apos;s running in the opposite direction, embracing the grains that have been so unfairly vilified. Grains are front and center in house-made pastas and pizza doughs lovingly crafted from a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025131237/https://www.instagram.com/p/BG3TMe3w7qV/?taken-by=gristmill_bk">starter named Daisy</a> that began her life in Boston before defecting to Brooklyn. The restaurant&apos;s going to source the bulk of its produce, grain and meats from local purveyors, including from Novick-Finder&apos;s mom&apos;s farm in Rhinebeck. D&apos;aww. </p>

<p>Sample the fruits of her and her son&apos;s labors in dishes like a steak tartare ($17) served on wheat toast with Prairie Breeze cheddar and mustard; kale salad ($14) with creme fraiche and egg yolk; and a summer squash soup ($12) with radish greens and basil. Pizzas are topped with things like mushroom, mozzarella and lemon thyme ($19) and fennel, bacon, dandelion, bleu cheese and balsamic ($17). </p>

<p>Ice cream&apos;s the theme for dessert, whether it&apos;s the a la mode component to a slice of pie (flavors change daily, $6 or $10 with ice cream) or served two scoops to a bowl ($7). Flavors include lemon thyme, strawberry, coffee and a fennel olive oil, which can be decorated with toppings like pie crust, sprinkles or a &quot;magic shell&quot; of either dark chocolate or caramelized white chocolate in a sundae ($10). </p>
