					<p>Last year, a &quot;legal technicality&quot; shuttered Harold Moore&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203181437/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/01/rip_commerce_west_village.php">beloved Commerce restaurant</a>, but the chef is back with a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203181437/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/25/fall_restaurant_preview_2016.php">new restaurant</a> inside the sleek <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203181437/http://www.arlohotels.com/">Arlo Hotel Hudson Square</a>, channeling the Southern tradition of the &quot;meat-and-three.&quot; Appropriately dubbed Harold&apos;s Meat + Three, the restaurant serves a version of the Nashville-based tradition where customers select a protein and then choose three sides to accompany it. Unsurprisingly, Moore&apos;s version takes the humble, cafeteria-style offering and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203181437/http://ny.eater.com/2016/9/22/13016746/harold-moore-meat-three">turns it all fancy-like</a>. </p>

<p>Fancy as in &quot;Lobster in Citrus Crust&quot; and &quot;Filet Au Poivre&quot; fancy, both of which appear in the $39 section alongside a Prime Rib ($16 supplement) and a Grilled Lamb Chop ($20 supplement), a far cry from the meatloafs of Tennessee. Probably unusual to find Thai-Inspired Pork Ribs and Whole Branzino with eggplant (both $29) down South either, but the Fried Chicken ($19) is a staple of the format, and the Plat du Jour menu ($29) also boasts a Ham Steak (Thursdays) and Smothered Pork Chops (Tuesdays) that wouldn&apos;t be out of place in their city of origin either.</p>

<p>Macaroni and cheese, grits, french fries and potato salad share the &quot;and three&quot; space with quinoa, elote corn, an herb salad and Japanese sweet potatoes, another mingling of tradition and gourmetification. Moore&apos;s also offering a rare-outside-the-deli salad bar, a la carte ($25) or as a supplement to a meal ($17). </p>

<p><em>Inside the Arlo Hudson Square at 2 Renwick Street, 212-374-2632</em></p>
