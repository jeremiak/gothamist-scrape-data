					<p>Take 2016&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630033711/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/03/pasta_trend_nyc.php">biggest dining trend</a>, add one part <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630033711/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/07/eatsa_automat_midtown.php#photo-1">burgeoning food technology integration</a>, and shake together with a giant scoop of <em>Harry Potter</em>&#x2014;conveniently celebrating 20 years since the first book&apos;s release&#x2014;and you&apos;ll get an idea of what to expect at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630033711/http://pastawiz.com/">Pasta Wiz</a>. Or maybe you won&apos;t, because this place looks pretty bonkers.</p>

<p>&quot;We decided Pasta Wiz, it&apos;s like a wizard magical place, so we&apos;ve made it <em>Harry Potter</em>-style. We decorated in a <em>Harry Potter</em>-style,&quot; owner Alex Dimitrov told Gothamist. &quot;The magic is making the pasta so fast, and so good.&quot; With a wizard-themed pasta place following a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630033711/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/09/this_is_the_end_my_friend.php#photo-1">unicorn latte</a>, you&apos;d have to think a dragon-themed fondue restaurant can&apos;t be far off. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630033711/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/21/ridiculous_staten_island_restaurant.php#photo-1">Oh, wait</a>.  </p>

<p>Dimitrov&apos;s been trying to open a restaurant in this space for nearly six years, facing <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630033711/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/09/williamsburg_condo_community_unhapp.php">stiff community opposition</a> from neighbors who feared he&apos;d replicate his &quot;immigrant embassy Balkan disco dive bar extravaganza&quot; Mehanata on the Lower East Side on North 1st Street. </p>

<p>&quot;They thought I was building a nightclub. In the end, though, it&apos;s this,&quot; Dimitrov told us. &quot;Every time I went for a liquor license, people were opposing me. I went back in September for a beer and wine license and said I was making a pasta place&#x2014;they didn&apos;t believe me.&quot;</p>

<p>Now he&apos;s finally succeeded in bringing spaghetti and Sorting Hats to the neighborhood he calls &quot;North Brooklyn.&quot;</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="011017hat.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630033711im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/011017hat.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> (Scott Heins/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The Hogwarts-esque chandeliers, baby grand piano and candles aren&apos;t the only magical elements up Dimitrov&apos;s sleep. In the time it takes to wave a magic wand, Dimitrov says a plate of piping hot pasta will be ready for inhalation. The restaurant promises that dishes will arrive from the kitchen between three to five minutes after ordering. </p>

<p>As with many fast-casual restaurants of its ilk&#x2014;order off the tablet and pick up at the counter, there&apos;s no table service&#x2014;the restaurant employs a mix-and-match method for DIY pastas, in addition to combinations they&apos;ve tested and approved. There are classics including &quot;Magic Meatballs&quot; ($12.95) over organic spaghetti and meat/dairy-free options like the &quot;Vegan Mediterranean&quot; ($13.95) with olive oil, garlic, scallions, olives, sundried tomatoes, zucchini, basil, mushrooms and vegan parmesan cheese over organic spaghetti. </p>

<p>If Pasta Wiz takes off, Dimitrov plans to replicate his wizarding world elsewhere in NYC on a smaller scale, like so many sequels and spinoffs. </p>
