					<p>Longtime Williamsburg haunt Cafe Moto has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170507232655/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/08/rip_cafe_moto_williamsburg.php">been reborn</a> as a cycling enthusiast&apos;s temple to two-wheeled transportation called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170507232655/http://www.barvelobrooklyn.com/">Bar Velo</a>, opening Monday on Broadway. John McCormick shut down his 14-year restaurant a few months back to retool the design a bit and update the menu to be completely vegetarian. And in the spring, they&apos;ll launch Velo-To-Go, offering grocery-type things like bread, fruit and flowers plus...cycling gear. </p>

<p>The cycling theme continues with items from McCormick&apos;s personal collection of bike gear, including jerseys and lots of bike fixtures (a bicycle has always hung outside the restaurant&apos;s front door). Like Moto, Velo will also double as a casual live music venue, stuffing musicians into the tiny space on certain days. </p>

<p>Though one shouldn&apos;t pair too much boozing with biking (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170507232655/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/31/bike_kill_photos_2016.php#photo-1">outside of Bike Kill</a>, of course), the cocktail options sound refreshing and simple. Faust Coppi takes mezcal for a spin with green chartreuse, maraschino and fresh lime, while whiskey rides tandem with lemon, roma tomato and basil in the Belleville. </p>

<p>Food&apos;s of the share plate variety and, again, all vegetarian. Lentil Walnut Pate comes with grilled baguette with miso and cornichons; fettuccini with slow roasted tomato, basil oil, olives and breadcrumbs; and the burger&apos;s a black bean offering with grilled portobello, avocado and garlic aioli. </p>

<p><em>394 Broadway; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170507232655/http://www.barvelobrooklyn.com/">barvelobrooklyn.com</a></em></p>
