					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615192227/http://www.tikkaindiangrill.com/">Tikka Indian Grill</a> was an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615192227/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/20/tikka_indian_grill_williamsburg.php#photo-1">excellent addition to the neighborhood</a> when it opened in Williamsburg last year, which hopefully means the owner&apos;s new spot in upper Manhattan is destined to be the same. Salim Rahman, who currently co-owns Tikka, and Amin Ruhel, previously of Tripti and Dipali, will run <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615192227/http://www.mumbaimasalanyc.com/">Mumbai Masala Indian Grill</a>, which officially opens Thursday evening on Amsterdam between 147th and 148th. Once again, a Bangladeshi chef will be running the kitchen.</p>

<p>The dishes emerging from Chef Fatema Khatum&apos;s kitchen include a wide range of offerings from across the many regions of India, with plenty of options for vegetarian and vegan diners. There&apos;s soft and savory Malai Kofta ($10.95), fiery vindaloos in chicken ($12.95) and lamb ($13.95) varieties, and Jhinga Moilee ($15.95), shrimp in a turmeric-infused coconut milk sauce. Check out the full menu below. </p>

<p>Like its Brooklyn sister, Mumbai Masala keeps the decor simple with similar street scenes photography. For now, the restaurant is BYOB but a license is currently in the works.  </p>

<p><em>1768 Amsterdam Avenue, 212-368-0100; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615192227/http://www.mumbaimasalanyc.com/">mumbaimasalanyc.com</a></em> </p>
