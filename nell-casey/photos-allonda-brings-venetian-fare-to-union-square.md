</div></div>					<p>We&apos;ve been anticipating Chef Chris Jaeckle and Chris Cannon&apos;s homage to Northern Italian cuisine <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150618092701/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/05/fall_restaurant_preview.php">for some time now</a> and two weeks ago the restaurant finally debuted in its home just south of Union Square. Chef Jaeckle, who&apos;s spent time in some of the city&apos;s most venerable kitchens like Eleven Madison Park and Ai Fiori, offers a menu rooted in Venetian sensibility with flecks of international flair, a nod to the port city&apos;s multicultural influences. Meanwhile Cannon, a former business partner of Michael White, assembled a diverse list of Italian wines drawn from Italy&apos;s many different regional specialties. </p>

<p>It&apos;s no surprise that seafood reigns on the menu, with flavors of the ocean infused into many of the dishes. Sea urchin is a recurring theme, surfacing most notably in the tender Arancini ($9) with squid ink and a Bucatini pasta ($19), where the creamy creatures are smoked and tossed with spicy bread crumbs. The restaurant&apos;s name stands for a kind of soupy risotto particular to the region and they offer a Risottoe Nero ($17), flavored with sepia, bitter radicchio and bottarga. </p>

<p>If seafood isn&apos;t your game, opt for the Pappardelle ($18) enveloped in aged duck ragu, treviso and chocolate or the meaty Strip Loin ($29) with parmesan potatoes, mustard greens and fonduta. If you eschew meat entirely, try the Tortellini ($15) with parmesan dashi, tomato oil and porcini mushrooms and the Jerusalem Artichokes ($7) tossed in brown butter, soy and a briny bite from capers.  </p>

<p>The bi-level space has been totally transformed from its previous life as a neighborhood tavern while still retaining some of the casual charm. The bar and a communal table occupy the ground floor, which has been decorated in greyscale tones with whitewashed brick walls and a geometric tiled floor. The main dining area upstairs boasts floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking 13th Street plus a large photograph depicting the watery canals of Venice. </p>

<p><em>22 East 13th Street, (212) 231-2366; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150618092701/http://allondanyc.com/">allondanyc.com</a></em></p>
