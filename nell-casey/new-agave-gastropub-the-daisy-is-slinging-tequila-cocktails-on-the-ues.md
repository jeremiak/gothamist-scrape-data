					<p>It&apos;ll probably never be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160720215609/http://gothamist.com/2014/05/02/jefftown_bushwick_guide.php">hip like Jefftown</a> or as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160720215609/http://gothamist.com/2012/12/09/rambo_everyone_wants_a_piece_of_god.php">exclusive as RAMBO</a>, but the Upper East Side still stoically marches forward in its effort to shake off the preppy douche cliche that&apos;s been lingering for decades. Will a so-called &quot;agave gastropub&quot; be part of this new wave of upscale establishments bucking the Irish bar trend? <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160720215609/http://thedaisynyc.com/">The Daisy</a>, now slinging tequila cocktails and Mexican-inspired plates, seems to be gesturing in that direction.</p>

<p>The restaurant took over for Molly Pitcher&apos;s, a fine if generic barstaurant on Second Avenue and 85th that&apos;d been slinging beers since the &apos;90s. In place of burgers and fish and chips...different takes on the dishes, including a &quot;BRGR&quot; ($18) with pickled pork belly BBQ and cloth-bound cheddar and Fish and Chips ($12) battered in chapulin (grasshoppers) with a bacon-cranberry remoulade. </p>

<p>Executive chef, Raul Sanchez&#x2014;who&apos;s also in the kitchen at sister restaurant Agave in the West Village&#x2014;designed the Mexican-American menu, which is divided by designations like &quot;Bold &amp; Warm&quot; (a pork belly entry, a mac &apos;n cheese) and &quot;Enticing &amp; Piquant&quot; (beef croquettes with chocolate-habanero salsa and guacamole with homemade chips). <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160720215609/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/03/brooklyn_menu_generator.php">No comment</a>.</p>

<p>If you&apos;ve come for the tequila, there&apos;s plenty of that to be found behind the handsome bar. The signature Daisy cocktail is kind of a paired down margarita ($13)&#x2014;the term margarita translates to &quot;daisy&quot; in English&#x2014;with Tromba Blanco Tequila infused with black pepper, St. Germaine and a lime marmalade. If that&apos;s not enough spice, there&apos;s another cocktail made with jalapenos (The Risk Taker, $14) and another with pomegranate-habanero syrup (The Scorcher, $13). Mexican beers by the can and bottle are available, too, plus craft drafts from Brooklyn, Southern Tier and Bell&apos;s.</p>

<p><em>1641 2nd Avenue, (646) 964-5756; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160720215609/http://thedaisynyc.com/">website</a></em></p>
