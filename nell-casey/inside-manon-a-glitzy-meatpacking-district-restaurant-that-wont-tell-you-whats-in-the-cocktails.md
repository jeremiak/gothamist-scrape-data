</div></div>					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408151308/http://www.cafe-pushkin-usa.com/#!/manon">Manon</a>, a new restaurant and lounge that debuted last Friday, dresses the part of swanky club but with a kitchen that dishes out unexpectedly subtle and refined dishes inside the opulent setting. Named for the Jules Massenet opera&apos;s title character, the enormous, three-level space embraces the dramatic, with sparkling chandeliers, mirrored wall decorations and plush velvet chairs and couches. Mixed in are more industrial elements, like a wonderfully distressed wood floor, weathered woven carpeting and exposed brick walls that make sure the space doesn&apos;t feel completely over the top. </p>

<p>Skeptics might write off the restaurant due to its location and lavish design, but we were pleasantly surprised by what we tried at a press preview last night. Executive Chef Tae Strain&#x2014;who trained under PUBLIC chef <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408151308/http://gothamist.com/2007/03/21/brad_farmerie_c.php">Brad Farmerie</a>&#x2014;doesn&apos;t limit himself to one style of cuisine, instead using flavors and ingredients found across the globe. The raw Steelhead Salmon ($12) echoes deconstructed maki, with crunchy pickled daikon providing tang to the savory miso and wakame. Creamy Grilled Japanese Eggplant ($11) comes buried under a hearty portion of red quinoa, pinenuts and creamy sheep&apos;s milk feta, while perfectly tender Viking Village Scallops ($19) rest on top of an umami rich lobster and sea urchin broth, with delicate strips of fennel and grapefruit on top. The Atlantic Cod ($27) has a delightfully crispy exterior that reveals buttery, expertly cooked fish in a savory bath of wilted leeks with a white &quot;harissa&quot; sauce that, while delicious, has very little in common with traditional harissa other than a great peppery flavor. </p>

<p>While the food has its feet on the ground, the cocktail program&#x2014;designed by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408151308/http://gothamist.com/2011/04/07/new_restaurant_and_bar_radar_44.php#photo-3">Amor y Amargo</a> vet Aaron Polsky&#x2014;reads like an SNL parody of bespoke cocktail culture. Instead of listing the type of booze in his creations, Polsky offers &quot;tasting notes&quot; to guide imbibes in their cocktail selection. The &quot;Do What thou Wilt&quot; promised &quot;a dark and energizing drink [with] orange, clove, wood, leather, tobacco and sassafras,&quot; but tasted like watered down Jaegermeister with a hint of Christmas tree. &quot;In the Shade&quot; at least got it right with its description of &quot;perfumy&#x2026;orchids on the nose&#x2026;floral touch;&quot; just imagine drinking your <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408151308/http://www.jenarichards.com/images/Sachet%20red%20rose%20petals%20favor_lg.jpg">grandmother&apos;s drawer sachet</a>. While you may be tempted to experience &quot;strawberries, sea air, straw hats and sunshine&quot; (served up!), you&apos;re better off forgoing the mysterious $14 cocktails and ordering a martini or glass of bourbon.</p>
