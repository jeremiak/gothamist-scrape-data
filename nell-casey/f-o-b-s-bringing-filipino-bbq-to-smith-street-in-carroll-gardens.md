					<p>NYC&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161121234745/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/19/filipino_food_nyc.php">strong contingent</a> of Filipino eateries welcomes another in its ranks with the opening of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161121234745/http://www.fobbrooklyn.com/">f.o.b.</a> this evening in Carroll Gardens. Filipino bbq&apos;s going to be the focus here, like pork skewers in Filipino bbq sauce and grilled spare ribs in a banana bbq sauce. </p>

<p>There&apos;s Chicken Adobo on the menu&#x2014;&quot;always better the next day&quot; according to Chef Armando Litiatco&apos;s dad&#x2014;along with Bola-Bola Dumplings with beef meatballs and Duck Insal, a grilled duck breast, its richness offset by calamansi lemon. Vegetarians are at a slight disadvantage here&#x2014;even the noodles are fortified with fish sauce&#x2014;but pescatarians eat well with peel and eat shrimp, whole grilled fish, and mussels coddled in garlic cream and melted cheese.</p>

<p>There&apos;s no booze on the menu just yet, so for liquid satisfaction turn to one of the restaurant&apos;s shakes, like ube&#x2014;the ubiquitous purple yam&#x2014;with coconut tapioca pearls or another made with avocado and tapioca. Or use those extra calories up on dessert with a Leche Flan made with duck eggs.</p>

<p><em>271 Smith Street, (718) 852-8994; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161121234745/http://www.fobbrooklyn.com/">fobbrooklyn.com</a></em></p>
