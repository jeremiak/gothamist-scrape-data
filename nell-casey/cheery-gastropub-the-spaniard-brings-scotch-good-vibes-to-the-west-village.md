					<p>The gastropub specialists behind neighborhood gems like Sweet Afton, The Bonnie and The Penrose debut the next in their fleet of charming yet sophisticated cocktail bars and restaurants with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170708101143/http://www.thespaniardnyc.com/">The Spaniard</a>, opening Monday in the West Village. The team already operates a spot near and dear to me (I&apos;ll let you guess why) called Wilfie &amp; Nell up West 4th Street, and now they&apos;re taking over the former Boxers/Oliver&apos;s City Tavern space just a few blocks away. Deep green leather banquettes look like the ideal spot to camp out on a lazy Saturday, while the cheery barstools beckon for a quick pint before heading home.</p>

<p>Craft cocktails have been the focus of the group&apos;s ventures since opening <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170708101143/http://www.buabar.com/">Bua</a> in 2004. While the name of this new pub might lead you to think riojas and vermouths, the main thrust of the spirits program is scotch&#x2014;with some Irish whiskey and bourbon, too&#x2014;though cocktails branch out into other territories. To wit, the Second Serve ($13) employs fino sherry, Montenegro and lime. </p>

<p>True to the gastropub format, the food offerings innovate on humble dishes like Buffalo Wings (made with duck here, $14) while sticking close to tried-and-true classics that go very well with drinking. Plump Oysters Rockefeller ($18) eat like a meal with spinach and bacon, Lobster Roast ($14) riffs on the roll with creme fraiche on filone bread, and they&apos;re also doing a Patty Melt ($16) with dry aged beef, cheddar and McClure&apos;s pickles.</p>

<p><em>190 West 4th Street; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170708101143/http://www.thespaniardnyc.com/">thespaniardnyc.com</a></em></p>
