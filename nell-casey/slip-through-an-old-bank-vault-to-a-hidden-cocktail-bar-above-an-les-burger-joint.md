					<p>Would it be odd to describe the new combination burger joint with adjoining cocktail lounge the mullet of Lower East Side restaurants...but it in a good way? It&apos;s swanky business up top, casual feeding party below, a dichotomy of styles all contained within a single space. Meet The Burgery and upstairs cocktail den Garfunkels, both now fully operational on Clinton Street a block north of Delancey. </p>

<p>The second floor &quot;secret&quot; cocktail bar opened first a few months back, though walking past you&apos;d have no idea it was up there save for a well-dressed door attendant who might engage if you appear bewildered while staring at the (then) under-construction storefront. Before its more boisterous sibling opened, Garfunkels lived in relative obscurity, serving drinks with names like Fancy Free, At Midnight and Queen&apos;s Park Swizzle in something akin to a Victorian drawing room, replete with stacks of books, mirrors and cozy couches to melt into after a few rounds of bourbon cocktails.</p>

<p>Both establishments are run by Tim Gashi and Valentino Gjekaj, lifelong friends who&apos;ve been involved in the restaurant business through their fathers&apos; restaurants and their own, which include Rocky Slims and Arte. For their collaborative effort, the duo are combining two of NYC&apos;s most enduring new trends: fast casual eats and mixology. &quot;This neighborhood needs something straightforward and simple,&quot; Gashi explains of The Burgery, though the process for getting upstairs isn&apos;t quite as easy (more on that below).</p>

<p>Downstairs, diners can plop down on bar stools and some booths to munch on build-your-own burgers with a proprietary Pat LaFrieda beef blend or one of their &quot;Burgar&quot; and sandwich combos including the Lower Eastsider ($14) with pastrami, gruyere cheese, Pickle Guys sour pickles and horseradish mustard on a toasted rye roll. Sides like tots and fries are $4 extra&#x2014;$10 if they&apos;re the Tater Tatchos topped with cheese sauce, pico, jalapenos and beans&#x2014;and there are Il Laboratorio Del Gelato shakes in Rootbeer Float and Toasted S&apos;mores flavors to sip.</p>

<p>Beers and cocktails can be procured downstairs, too, but upstairs is where the sipping takes priority. Reservations aren&apos;t strictly necessary, but those who have them are given priority in the seating-room-only upstairs space. Those with pre-booked seats are given an access code (which changes nightly) to gain entry via old bank vault doors in the back of the burger joint, a nod to the space&apos;s former home as the corrupt bank firm Garfunkel &amp; Tauster in the 1920s.</p>

<p><em>67 Clinton Street, 212-529-6999, The Burgery is on the ground floor, Garfunkel&apos;s on the second floor</em></p>
