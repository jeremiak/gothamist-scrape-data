					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035832/http://www.tapestryrestaurant.com/">Tapestery</a>, as its name implies, weaves together colorful strands of global foods to create one harmonious dish, or dishes, as the case may be. It&apos;s chef Suvir Saran at the loom, who uses the globalization of food as a lens through which to view the cuisines of his Indian heritage. Saran refuses to be limited by preconceptions about what is or is not Indian food, but just <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035832/http://ny.eater.com/2016/5/11/11658516/tapestry-west-village-opening">don&apos;t call it fusion</a>. </p>

<p>Rich chicken wings glazed in tangy tamarind come with a basil-mint yogurt sauce, while a classic French Salmon en Papilotte comes bathed in tomato chutney with an Indian kachumbar salad of tomatoes, onions and cucumbers. Saran achieves great success when he&apos;s riffing on classic comfort foods, like the Masala Fried Chicken ($26), with a thick batter of crunch. Think a three piece breast, leg and thigh meal at Popeye&apos;s, with peanut slaw and aloo bharta instead of mayonnaise-y cabbage and mashed potatoes. </p>

<p>The kitchen makes excellent use of cauliflower, dousing it in a sweet, sour and spicy Hakka sauce with flavors native to Taiwan but popular in India. Or in a more Italian-inspired preparation of wide ribbons of pappardelle noodles dotted with chile and sage with crunchy breadcrumbs and bits of fried garlic for texture. Less successful is a Rabbit Terrine, which lacked the expected lusciousness and yearned for a heavier hand with the salt shaker at a recent press preview.</p>

<p>But salt shows up to great effect in the cocktails ($14), which is perfect for someone like myself who avoids sweets and dessert-type things. A vodka, cucumber, dill and peppercorn offering (Kashmir Valley) drank like a Bloody Mary Lite, while another with tequila, saffron, turmeric, black pepper, lime and cayenne (Sela) might make you reach for the spice drawer next time you&apos;re whipping up adult beverages at home. </p>

<p><em>60 Greenwich Avenue, (212) 373-8900; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035832/http://www.tapestryrestaurant.com/">website</a></em></p>
