					<p>The space that housed St. Marks staple Yaffa Cafe, which <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043403/http://gothamist.com/2014/10/02/st_marks_staple_yaffa_cafe_closes.php">closed in 2014</a>, has reopened as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043403/https://www.taberna97.com/">Taberna 97</a>, a &quot;traditional Portuguese restaurant&quot; from the owners of Irish pub St. Dymphna&apos;s down the block. As was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043403/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/17/yaffa_cafe.php">prophesied</a>, this means a selection of petiscos, the Portuguese version of tapas. </p>

<p>Flaming crocks of sausages, cod fritters and clams in garlic and wine are a few of the snack options, ranging in price from $6 to $21. Owners had previously <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043403/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/17/yaffa_cafe.php">talked up</a> the caldo verde, a typical soup made with potatoes and collard greens, and it&apos;s indeed on the menu here. Entrees include a seafood rice, grilled sardines with roasted peppers, and piri piri-marinated chicken.</p>

<p>As you can see, the kitschy decorations and eye-grabbing wallpaper have made way for a pretty if plain dining room, with exposed brick and rafters, tile floors and a muted color scheme. Not a speck of zebra print in the place. The contested back yard area&#x2014;which was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043403/http://ny.eater.com/2014/10/6/6921873/yaffa-cafe-closed-due-to-complaining-neighbor-unwavering-dob-and-nine">mostly to blame</a> for Yaffa&apos;s shutter&#x2014;will be purely decorative, meant for &quot;[enjoyment] from inside our space,&quot; owners <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043403/http://evgrieve.com/2016/12/taberna-97-is-now-open-on-st-marks-place.html">told EV Grieve</a>. </p>

<p><em>97 St. Marks Place, 212-477-5600; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043403/https://www.taberna97.com/">taberna97</a></em></p>
