<p>The successful <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015122321/https://harlemeatup.com/">Harlem EatUp!</a> celebrates its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015122321/http://gothamist.com/2015/02/11/harlem_eatup_lineup.php">third year</a> showcasing Harlem&apos;s diverse and exciting dining scene, with four days of eating and drinking and celebrating coming up in May. </p>

<p>The bulk of the EatUp!&apos;s schedule centers around &quot;Dine-Ins,&quot; where Harlem eateries welcome chefs from other restaurants around the country to create special, one-night-only dinners. Participating restaurants for the 2017 edition include Red Rooster&#x2014;Marcus Samuelsson is a co-founder&#x2014;Melba&apos;s, Minton&apos;s, Chaiwali and Vinateria, among others. </p>

<p>The centerpiece of the festival is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015122321/https://harlemeatup.com/festivalevents/the-harlem-stroll-ultimate-grand-tasting">the Harlem Stroll</a>, a walk around tasting event that takes place in Morningside Park. From 12:30 to 5:30 p.m. on Saturday, May 20th, chefs from eateries including Harlem Tavern, Harlem Shake, Mountain Bird, Streetbird Rotisserie and more will offer up unlimited samples of bite-sized fare from their respective restaurants, paired with samples of beer, wine and cocktails. </p>

<p>Check out the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015122321/https://harlemeatup.com/festivalevents/">full schedule</a> of EatUp! events and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015122321/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/third-annual-harlem-eatup-festival-tickets-32560071049">purchase tickets</a> before dinners sell out.</p>

<p><em>Harlem EatUp! takes place between May 18th and May 21st; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015122321/https://harlemeatup.com/">harlemeatup.com</a></em></p>
