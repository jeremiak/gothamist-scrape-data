<p>Long Island&apos;s oldest craft brewery has joined the ranks of bro-approved Buds and Nattys. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410034741/http://bluepointbrewing.com/">Blue Point Brewing Company</a> was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410034741/http://www.newsday.com/business/blue-point-brewing-sold-to-anheuser-busch-1.6959447">sold to beer behemoth Anheuser-Busch</a>, reports <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410034741/http://bluepointbrewing.com/">Newsday</a>. The companies announced the deal today, with Anheuser-Busch chief executive Luis Edmond saying they hoped to &quot;accelerate the growth of the Blue Point portfolio and expand to new markets, while preserving the heritage and innovation of the brands.&quot;</p>

<p>The is the second major American craft brewery to be bought by AB InBev. The company <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410034741/http://gothamist.com/2011/03/28/goose_island_is_now_siblings_with_b.php">bought Chicago&apos;s Goose Island</a> in 2011 and also owns international brews Stella, Leffe and Hoegaarden, among others. </p>
