<p>Fresh off an experiment <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170428222048/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/17/doughnut_project_restaurant_series.php#photo-1">making doughnuts out of entrees</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170428222048/http://www.thedoughnutproject.com/#welcome=">The Doughnut Project</a> in the West Village now turns its attention to cocktails in a new series that launched last Friday. The team has sought inspiration from local bars and their signature cocktails, and beginning this Friday it&apos;s The Bennett Doughnut inspired by the &quot;Everlasting Arms&quot; cocktail which includes: Cachasa, pineapple, banana liquor, falernum and gardenia mix. Check out the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170428222048/http://www.thedoughnutproject.com/cocktail-series">full schedule here</a>. </p>

<p>Williamsburg&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170428222048/http://humboldtandjackson.com/">Humboldt &amp; Jackson</a> hosts (barely) teen chef Josh Reisner on Sunday for a <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170428222048/http://humboldtandjackson.com/hj-event/kitchen-takeover-by-josh-reisner-master-chef-junior/">Kitchen Takeover</a></strong> from the 13-year-old and MasterChef Junior finalist. Reisner will serve a menu bolstered by two ramens&#x2014;Special Shio with chicken and pork and a Short Rib Mazemen with sous vide short rib&#x2014;along with smaller dishes like pickled vegetables, blistered shishitos and scallop crudo. The takeover begins at 6 p.m.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="042817toast.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170428222048im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/042817toast.jpg" width="640" height="481"> <br> <i> Avocado Toast (Katie Burton)</i></div> <p></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170428222048/http://divyaskitchen.com/">Divya&apos;s Kitchen</a> in the East Village is your new spot for vegetarian brunch. Beginning last weekend, the restaurant started offering dishes like Avocado Toast on housemade einkorn-barley bread, sweet or savory Red Lentil Waffles, and scones made from sorghum and amaranth flours and scented with rose petal jam. The restaurant promises &quot;balanced meals that benefit the body and mind,&quot; if that&apos;s how you like to kick off your weekend. </p>

<p>Elsewhere in Brunchville, Bed-Stuy&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170428222048/http://www.hartsbrooklyn.com/">Hart&apos;s</a> has also begun serving midday weekend meals beneath the C train. Stop by for unique brunch dishes included Spanish tortilla with button mushrooms and aioli ($14), bone-in fried chicken with harissa and za&apos;atar ($19), and poached eggs with butter beans and bottarga ($15). They&apos;re making their bloodies with tequila, their own bloody mix, and horseradish for $12, with the option to add a beer back for $2. </p>
