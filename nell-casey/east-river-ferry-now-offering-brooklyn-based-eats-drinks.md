<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150331023150/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/26/push_to_improve_ferry_transportatio.php">very popular East River Ferry</a> announced today an expanded food and beverage menu that will be available seven days a week to commuters and tourists. They&apos;ve already been pouring <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150331023150/http://gothamist.com/2011/08/08/dumbo_buzzing_from_newly_opened_bro.php">Brooklyn Roasting Company</a> coffee on morning boats and the additional concessions will come predominantly from&#x2014;where else?&#x2014;local Brooklyn establishments. Nosh on a cupcake from Butter Lane and wash it down with &quot;retro-style&quot; chocolate and vanilla egg creams using U-bet syrups. These offerings come through a collaboration with celebrity chef Marc Murphy, whose events company will also supply additional as-yet-to-be-named snacks. </p>

<p>There is still no indication that you&apos;ll be able to buy alcohol on board anytime in the near future, so for now you&apos;ll just have to get your booze on the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150331023150/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/25/bloombergs_idea_for_a_perfect_first.php">Bloomberg-First-Date-Approved Staten Island Ferry</a>. </p>
