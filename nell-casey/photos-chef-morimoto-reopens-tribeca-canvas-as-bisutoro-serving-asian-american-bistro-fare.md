</div></div>					<p>Chef Masaharu Morimoto called a mulligan on his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429191827/http://gothamist.com/2012/11/29/chef_morimoto_enters_comfort_food_g.php#photo-1">ornate comfort food eatery</a> Tribeca Canvas, closing the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429191827/http://ny.eater.com/archives/2013/02/the_worst_lines_of_the_tribeca_canvas_reviews_with_cats.php">brutally reviewed</a> restaurant this past August less than a year after he opened it. Not to be undone by the failure, the Iron Chef returns to the scene of the culinary crime to open <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429191827/http://www.bisutoronyc.com/">Bisutoro</a>, an American-Asian fusion restaurant with a cocktail program heavy on the Japanese whisky. Chef Morimoto brings along Patrick Fahey and Tommy Hill of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429191827/http://gothamist.com/2008/11/30/macao_trading_co.php#photo-1">Macao Trading Company</a> and Los Americanos, respectively, to bring his new vision to life. </p>

<p>The mac &amp; cheese and corn dogs are gone, replaced with Uni Carbonara ($17/22) with poached quail egg, green peas and hari nori and Pork Tonkatsu ($26), fried crisp and served with kimchee, potato puree and katsu sauce. The chef&apos;s delicate version of soup dumplings ($13) come with molten mushroom and onion with kabocha squash puree, topped with panko fried maitake mushroom; another vegetarian option includes a Tofu Steak ($18) with zucchini fettuccine in lemon butter. </p>

<p>A large, square bar offers seating for 24 imbibers to knock back a few beers, wines, cocktails and a selection of Japanese whiskys. Sip on a &quot;Lost In Translation&quot; ($16) with Hakushu 12 Year, Carpano Antica, Cherry Heering and Absinthe or a large format Morimoto Soba Ale ($15) and take in street artist Charlie Doves&apos; beautiful cityscape murals that adorn the walls. A small bar menu, which includes fiery Shishito peppers ($8) with goat cheese and dashi soy and a Steak Frites ($36) with togarashi butter and beef jus, is available daily from 4 p.m. until one hour after the dining room closes for the evening. </p>

<p><em>Bisutoro is located at 313 Church St at Lispenard Street in Tribeca, (212) 727-0644; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429191827/http://www.bisutoronyc.com/">bisutoronyc.com</a>.
