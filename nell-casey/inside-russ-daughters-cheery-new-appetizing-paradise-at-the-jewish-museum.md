					<p>The lox lovers at Russ &amp; Daughters have substantially expanded their NYC footprint in recent years, first opening a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160904043213/http://gothamist.com/2014/05/08/photos_the_long-awaited_russ_daught.php#photo-1">new sit-down cafe</a> on the Lower East Side and then expanding their baking operation to a refurbished spot <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160904043213/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/01/russ_daughters_brooklyn_navy_yard.php">in the Brooklyn Navy Yard</a>. Now, their indelible mark can be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160904043213/http://gothamist.com/2014/09/09/russ_daughters_opening_cafe_on_uppe.php">found uptown</a> with the debut of their newest location inside <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160904043213/http://thejewishmuseum.org/">The Jewish Museum</a> on Fifth Avenue at 92nd Street. </p>

<p>The new location combines its downtown brethren into one space, operating as both a 60-seat restaurant and a take-out appetizing counter, both on the museum&apos;s lower level and accessible to those who aren&apos;t also taking in the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160904043213/http://thejewishmuseum.org/exhibitions">excellent exhibitions</a> celebrating Jewish artists and innovators (ideally, you should be doing both). Inside, they&apos;ll be offering the same dishes that made them famous downtown, like their smoked fish platters, bagel sandwiches, babkas and egg creams. </p>

<p>To further connect the cafe to the museum, The Jewish Museum Director Claudia Gould tapped artist Maira Kalman to create a mural to adorn the walls of the restaurant. The piece depicts &quot;120 vignettes and small scenes&quot; of Kalman&apos;s experiences with food, community and Russ &amp; Daughter&apos;s important cultural connection to New York City. Another wall captures a scene from the shop&apos;s historic space on Houston Street. </p>

<p><em>1109 Fifth Avenue; Open Sunday - Wednesday 11 a.m. - 5:45 p.m., Thursday 11 a.m - 8 p.m., Friday 11 a.m. - 4 p.m. with plans to extend hours in the future.</em></p>
