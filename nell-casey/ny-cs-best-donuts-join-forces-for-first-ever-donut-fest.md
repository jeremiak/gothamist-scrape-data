<p>Many of the city&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/06/the_best_doughnuts_in_nyc.php">finest donut purveyors</a> will be under one roof in a matter of weeks at NYC&apos;s first <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://donutfest.com/">Donut Fest</a>, which comes to us <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://chicagoist.com/tags/donutfest">by way of Chicago</a>. On Saturday, January 23rd, Williamsburg&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://www.verbotennewyork.com/">Verboten</a> transforms into a haven for all Homers seeking deep fried cake-and-yeast treats. Good clean fun for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/05/gold_donut_williamsburg.php">the everyman</a>. </p>

<p>Newcomers <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/27/doughnut_project_west_village.php#photo-2">The Doughnut Project</a> are on the docket, hopefully bringing their addictive, bacon-topped version of the West Coast maple bar. Also on hand: Mike&apos;s Donuts, Carpe Donuts (of the apple cider donut fame), vegan offerings from Dun-Well Doughnuts and a handful more. Coffee, the natural ally of the donut, will be poured from the likes of Brooklyn Roasting Company, D&apos;Amico Coffee Roasters, Cafe Grumpy and more. (No <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/05/gold_donut_williamsburg.php">gold donuts</a>, unfortunately.)</p>

<p>There are three sessions on the day, including a special <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://donutfest.com/tickets/">VIP entry</a> ($50) from 9:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. that awards attendees tastes of all the donuts, a Donut Fest mug and tickets for tree cups of coffee, which are needed at that hour on a weekend. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://donutfest.com/tickets/">Regular entry sessions</a> ($35) are 11:30 a.m. to 2 p.m. and 2:30 p.m. to 5 p.m. and include tastes of all the donuts plus three coffee tickets. All the entry fees will be donated to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708075107/http://www.foodbanknyc.org/">Food Bank for New York City</a>, a fitting karmic balance for a caloric excesses. </p>
