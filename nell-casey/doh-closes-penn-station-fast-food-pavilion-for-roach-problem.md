<p>Looks like desperate commuters weren&apos;t the only creatures filling up on Crunchwrap Supremes before boarding Amtrak. In an inspection on Friday, the Health Department discovered a lively roach population inside the Pizza Hut and Taco Bell kiosks in the station&apos;s main terminal and decided to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150121054416/http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20131111/midtown/penn-station-pizza-hut-taco-bell-shut-down-by-health-dept">shut down the eateries</a>, DNAinfo reported. </p>

<p>According to the DOH website, the eateries&#x2014;which also contain Tim Horton&apos;s, Nathan&apos;s and Colobo&apos;s Yogurt&#x2014;were cited a whopping 50 points for multiple violations including live roaches, improper food refrigeration and &quot;filth flies.&quot; Shudder.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="111213HD_TB.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150121054416im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/111213HD_TB.jpg" width="640" height="272" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>A sign on the restaurant yesterday indicated they were undergoing repairs, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150121054416/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/05/doh_closes_mission_chinese_again.php">common line</a> for DOH-shuttered establishments. In the meantime, hungry travelers can visit the nearby 8th avenue location for their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150121054416/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/12/taco_bell_and_cinnabon_collaborate.php">Cinnabon Delights fix</a>.</p>
