					<p>A few members of the crew from Tilda All Day, which <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170130204419/http://ny.eater.com/2016/9/22/13018838/tilda-all-day-nyc-close-evening">closed in September</a> after ownership squabbles, have reopened the space as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170130204419/http://www.otwaynyc.com/">Otway</a>. Samantha Safer and Claire Welle have tapped an all-female team to run the kitchen&#x2014;someday this will just be normal and awesome, but until that day, it&apos;s noteworthy. </p>

<p>That team will be responsible for breaking down and creating everything that&apos;s served, from in-house butchery to fermentation. &quot;There will be offal and there will <em>not</em> be signature dishes,&quot; they promise.</p>

<p>With that in mind, the current menu offers dishes including Pea Croquette with Lardo ($12), Fried Tripe ($10), Pan Roasted Perch with potatoes and buerre blanc ($25) and the alluring &quot;Cheese with seasonal things&quot; ($12). </p>

<p><em>930 Fulton Street, Clinton Hill, 917-909-1889; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170130204419/http://www.otwaynyc.com/">otwaynyc.com</a></em></p>
