					<p>Since last year, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134738/http://www.hailmarybk.com/">Hail Mary&apos;s</a> made a mission of offering comfort food classics that whisk us back to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134738/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/24/hail_mary_greenpoint.php#photo-1">Funfetti birthday parties</a> and after-school meals <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134738/http://mentalfloss.com/article/68222/14-cheesy-facts-about-velveeta">straight from a bright yellow container</a>&#x2014;only made from scratch and not from a box. And then they went ahead and blew the lid off mozzarella sticks, debuting <em>Deep Fried Burrata</em> about a month after they opened. </p>

<p>To the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134738/http://www.thekitchn.com/whats-the-difference-between-mozzarella-and-burrata-word-of-mouth-219642">uninitiated</a> (HOW?), burrata is mozzarella&apos;s creamier, sexier cousin. Instead of just a solid hunk of fresh buffalo milk cheese, burrata is like two cheeses in one: an outer case of stretchy mozzarella enveloping <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134738/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stracciatella_di_bufala">stracciatella</a>, a &quot;soft, stringy curd and cream.&quot; Now just imagine all of that...but breaded, fried, and served with a spicy tomato sauce. Sure, dim the lights.</p>

<p>&quot;We wanted to take a spin on a super traditional diner mozzarella stick but make it a little more interesting,&quot; Hail Mary owner Ham El-Waylly says of his creation. &quot;Everyone seems to love burrata these days. It just seemed like a perfect fit.&quot;</p>

<p>The burrata&apos;s flown in once a week from Puglia (yes, in Italy) from a purveyor whose only creation is this cheese. &quot;He doesn&apos;t know we fry it, [but I think] he&apos;d be fine with it,&quot; El-Waylly laughs. &quot;He&apos;s super proud of his burrata, so I didn&apos;t have the heart to tell him that we fry it.&quot;</p>

<p>The team experimented with a few methods of battering, ultimately settling on flour and buttermilk baths before a dip into Progresso breadcrumbs. &quot;At the beginning of this experiment, we knew that the one thing we wanted to keep from the original mozzarella stick were the Progresso breadcrumbs,&quot; El-Waylly explains. &quot;That&apos;s what gives you that nostalgic red sauce or diner feel. If you&apos;ve ever had mozzarella sticks anywhere, that&apos;s the brand of breadcrumbs that they use, I guarantee it.&quot;</p>

<p>The cheese comes to rest on a sauce made of hand-crushed, canned San Marzano tomatoes; toasted garlic, &quot;cut <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134738/http://www.thekitchn.com/the-celluloid-p-5-6591"><em>Goodfellas</em></a>-style&quot;; some Calabrian chili; and a handful of chopped parsley to finish. It&apos;s topped off with a final shaving of Pecorino cheese and a drizzle of Spanish olive oil, inspired by El-Waylly and his wife Sohla&apos;s favorite pizzeria, DiFara&apos;s.</p>

<p>So how popular has the burrata been on the menu? Along with their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134738/https://www.yelp.com/biz_photos/hail-mary-brooklyn?select=-pwIDH1KdEYuU5TsB7CStg">claw-on fried chicken</a>, it&apos;s the most ordered dish on the menu.</p>

<p>&quot;We had a three top <em>[Ed. Note: An industry term for a table of three]</em> come in for brunch and then they got one burrata to start. And then they got three burratas&#x2014;one for each of them&#x2014;right after,&quot; El-Waylly recalls. &quot;I think that&apos;s probably our most ridiculous burrata story.&quot; </p>
