					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161018192912/http://www.mofad.org/">Museum of Food and Drink</a> doesn&apos;t even have a permanent home yet, but they&apos;re already putting on their first public exhibit tomorrow at the final <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161018192912/http://gothamist.com/tags/summerstreets">Summer Streets</a>! Swing by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161018192912/http://www.nycgovparks.org/parks/thomaspainepark/history">Foley Square</a> between 7 a.m. and 1 p.m. to check out <em>BOOM! The Puffing Gun and the Rise of Breakfast Cereal</em>, the museum&apos;s inaugural exhibit, which chronicles the rise of the cereal industry and reveals the science behind making your cherished Cheerios.</p>

<p>Central to the exhibit is the museum&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161018192912/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/27/video_test-firing_mofads_explosive.php">explosive cereal puffing gun</a>, which transforms grains in their raw form and turns them into puffed bits. They&apos;ll be firing their puffing gun at regular intervals throughout their setup at Foley Square and serving up seasoned puffs from previous fires. </p>

<p>For a taste of what&apos;s in store, check out photos of the cannon being fired at MOFAD&apos;S Puff Party, which celebrated the museum&apos;s successful Kickstarter campaign. Below, Food Network host Ted Allen takes a swing and explodes four pounds of corn. The result? <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161018192912/https://twitter.com/nellcasey/status/367432116507664385">Roasted popcorn</a>.</p>
