					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015140715/https://sushisamba.com/">SushiSamba</a> flexes its Japanese, Brazilian, and Peruvian muscles starting today for a two month-long ramen special with four new bowls (well, one plate, technically) of noodles at their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015140715/https://sushisamba.com/location/new-york/nyc-7">West Village restaurant</a>. </p>

<p>Designed by the chain&apos;s corporate chef Koji Kagawa, the &quot;Samba Ramen by Koji&quot; looks to capitalize on ramen&apos;s versatility by employing Brazilian and Peruvian ingredients. Most of the ingredients look pretty typical to us, especially in NYC&apos;s rich ramen culture, but guess that&apos;s where the Peruvian corn and manchego come in.</p>

<p>Even if we&apos;ve <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015140715/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/18/best_ramen_nyc.php">seen some of these combos before</a>, the dishes sound pretty good and even include a vegetarian option, a welcome oasis in this tonkotsu-soaked town. The Yasai Yasai ramen ($14) employs kale, beet root, baby bok choy, honshimeji mushroom, Peruvian corn, snow pea shoot, spicy garlic, yuzu &quot;skin,&quot; which we take to mean the peel of the citrus fruit. </p>

<p>The Shotoribu ($17) puts us back on the meat beat with a bone marrow-based broth&#x2014;and more marrow served on top&#x2014;with short ribs, more of the Peruvian corn, baby bok choy, mushrooms and an egg. The Bekon, Uni and Chizu ($16) will be familiar to fans of Yuji Ramen&apos;s Bacon &amp; Egg Mazeman. The version of the brothless ramen here uses applewood smoked bacon, manchego cheese, onion, cream, with lobes of uni adding even more richness.</p>

<p>Finally, the less common dessert ramen. The Tori No Su ($13) crisps up the noodles and dresses them in a cinnamon and sugar coating. The nest of noodles gets a scoop of toasted ramen ice cream, hazelnut cream, dollops of milk chocolate sauce, caramelized bananas and a passion fruit-mango gelee. Ramen for breakfast, lunch, dinner and dessert. </p>

<p><em>SushiSamba is located at 87 7th Avenue South, (212) 691-7885; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015140715/https://sushisamba.com/location/new-york/nyc-7">sushisamba.com</a></em></p>
