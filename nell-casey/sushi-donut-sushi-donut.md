<p>Remember the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015120125/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/15/sushi_burrito_fine_whatever.php">Sushi Burrito</a><a>? That was sooooo 2015. In 2017, we must continue the trend of making everything </a><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015120125/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/30/spaghetti_donuts_smorgasburg.php#photo-1">into a donut</a>. This is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015120125/http://gothamist.com/2013/05/28/fine_lets_talk_about_nycs_out-of-co.php">literally all your fault</a>, Dominique Ansel.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015120125/http://www.pokeenyc.com/">POKEE</a> (emphasis theirs) opened late last year at the tail end of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015120125/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/28/poke_explainer_guide_nyc.php">wave of poke joints</a> that sprung up faster than you could say cold pressed organic green juice...wait, getting my trends confused. The restaurant debuted with a sushi donut that was ultimately removed from the menu. But like an indestructible humanoid cyborg sent from the future, they&apos;re back, available as a &quot;secret menu item&quot; at the West 3rd Street restaurant. </p>

<p>The ring-shaped raw fish vehicles are just spicy salmon-filled rice rings, with layers of cucumber, daikon radish, sesame seeds, nori seaweed and bubu arare (baked rice crackers) with either yellow fin tuna, salmon, or yellowtail. Mashable <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015120125/https://www.facebook.com/mashable/videos/10155072320434705/">did a deep dive</a> into how the unusually-shaped sushi is made, if you&apos;re hungry for more.</p>
