					<p>Situated in what&apos;s by and large a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031011802/http://gothamist.com/2015/08/25/murray_hill_parachute_journalism.php#photo-1">culinary wasteland</a>, The Cannibal has been a reliable Murray Hill staple whenever obligation demands a visit to the neighborhood. Christian Pappanicholas, Cory Lane and the rest of the crew have since expanded the Cannibal family into the adjacent space that formerly housed Resto, opening <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031011802/http://thecanniballiquorhouse.com/">The Cannibal Liquor House</a> more as a cocktail bar to its beer-focused sibling.</p>

<p>Chefs Francis Derby and Danny Hobby like to play with their food, it seems, offering things like Cacio Pepe Chips&#x2014;a riff on the Roman pasta&#x2014;with parmesan and cream; Rueben Spring Rolls with an IPA mustard; and Pigs Head In A Blanket with concord mustard and anise seed. There&apos;s an Every Burger (American cheese, lettuce, tomato, pickle and special sauce), but also Mama&apos;s Burger, with a patty made from dry aged beef, topped with provolone cheese, shishito peppers, onions and harissa mayo.</p>

<p>The Brandy Alexander cocktail employs Morgenstern&apos;s vanilla ice cream punched up with brandy, cardamaro (a wine-based aperitif) and Oreos. Other traditional cocktails that don&apos;t require the deployment of Lactaid include a Rhum Punch, a Harvey Wallbanger and the Blueberry Fix, made with bourbon, cassis, sherry cherry and blackberries.</p>

<p><em>111 East 29th Street; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031011802/http://thecanniballiquorhouse.com/">thecanniballiquorhouse.com</a></em></p>
