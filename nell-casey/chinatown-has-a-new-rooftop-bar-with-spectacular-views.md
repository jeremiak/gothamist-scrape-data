					<p>Fresh off opening a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615172732/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/04/the_heights_rooftop_nomad.php#photo-1">rooftop bar in NoMad</a>, chef Dale Talde and his Three Kings Restaurant Group debuted another aerie this week atop the Hotel 50 Bowery, a schmancy new Chinatown hotel. It&apos;s been a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615172732/http://gothamist.com/2017/02/21/talde_sports_bar_atlantic_social.php#photo-1">very busy year</a> so far for Talde, and like the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615172732/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/17/massoni_dale_talde_nomad.php#photo-1">multiple concepts</a> he opened at the Arlo hotel, he&apos;s got a slew of new bars and restaurants opening at this Chinatown spot&#x2014;starting with the rooftop.</p>

<p>Given Chinatown&apos;s low skyline, the views afforded from the 21st floor are pretty spectacular. Two large decks on different sides of the hotel offer dramatically different vantage points for award-winning Instagrams. One deck faces southernly, with views of the top of the Brooklyn Bridge, East River, and Financial District; the other faces north to Midtown and west toward the Hudson for splendid views of the sunset.</p>

<p>An interior lounge connecting the two decks serves house cocktails&#x2014;Black Dragon, with Elyx vodka, oolong tea, green tea, spiced calamansi, for example&#x2014;a seasonal &quot;Slush&quot; cocktail, beginning with a Lemon Lime Rickey; plus beer, wine, sake, soju and non-alcoholic mocktails. </p>

<p>Later this year Talde and team will open a restaurant, Rice &amp; Gold, and a basement lounge, The Green Lady, inside the hotel. </p>
