					<p>Nordic powerhouses Claus Meyer and Fredrick Berselius took NYC by blizzard last year&#x2014;or whatever the Scandinavian equivalent of &quot;storm&quot; might be. Meyer opened not one but <em>four</em> different entities in 2016&#x2014;a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/08/all_of_the_baked_goods_at_meyers_ba.php#photo-1">bakery</a>, a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/25/agern_grand_central_terminal.php#photo-1">restaurant</a>, a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/10/danish_dogs_grand_central_terminal.php">hot dog stand</a> and a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/27/great_northern_food_hall.php#photo-1">food hall</a>&#x2014;and Berselius <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/30/fredrik_berselius_aska.php#photo-1">revamped Aska</a>, immediately earning accolades and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/16/michelin_guide_nyc_2017.php">two Michelin stars</a>. The duo collaborated on their next Nordic venture, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://www.restaurantnorman.com/">Norman</a>, an all-day restaurant that opened Monday inside <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/https://a-d-o.com/">A/D/O</a>, a design space and work studio in Greenpoint. Disappointingly, it does not seem very <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://gothamist.com/2017/02/09/hygge_guide.php">hygge</a>. </p>

<p>Executive Chef Andrew Whitcomb will be running the open kitchen, along which runs a long counter where diners can sit and watch Scandinavian-inspired dishes come together. These are of the &quot;healthy living&quot; variety, meaning lots of fancy vegetable toasts, ancient grain bowls, salads and soups. Pastries and bread dough will be shipped in from Meyer&apos;s bakery in Williamsburg then baked on-site. The bread will be part of the restaurant&apos;s daily lunch special ($14), which will include an entree and vegetable side dish that changes each day.</p>

<p>The health kick continues with kombucha on tap, but they&apos;ll have stronger stuff available, too. Natural, organic and biodynamic wines can get you drunk with slightly less guilt and they have a collaboration beer with Brooklyn Brewery as well as Scandinavian-esque cocktails.</p>

<p><em>29 Norman Avenue, Greenpoint, (347) 966-2092; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404210721/http://www.restaurantnorman.com/">restaurantnorman.com</a></em></p>
