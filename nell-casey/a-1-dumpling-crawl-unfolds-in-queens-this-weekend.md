					<p>Bring an appetite and some singles to Queens this Sunday, when the 5th annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115045/https://www.facebook.com/events/1422018207814209/">Momo Crawl</a> unfolds once again in Jackson Heights. The annual dumpling event, which showcases the many types of momo, or Himalayan dumpling, offers a walk-around tour of the neighborhood with stops for $1 dumplings at food carts and restaurants along the way. At the end: one momo maker will take home the Momo Trophy, as voted on by attendees.</p>

<p>Last year, food truck Amdo Kitchen took home first place for their top-knotted beef momo, and the year prior, it was Phayul, who also offer top-knotted momo with meat and vegetarian fillings. Momo are either served plainly steamed or sauteed in a special sauce, depending on who&apos;s doing the making.</p>

<p>The crawl begins at 2 p.m. at Diversity Plaza (37th Road / 73rd Street), where you&apos;ll purchase a $5 Momo Map that outlines all of the momo makers. Participants have until 5 p.m. to eat as many of the dollar creations as they can before it&apos;s time to return to the start to vote. In the interim, there&apos;ll be free tattoos and &quot;momo hairdos,&quot; plus $1 momo henna and live peroformances.</p>
