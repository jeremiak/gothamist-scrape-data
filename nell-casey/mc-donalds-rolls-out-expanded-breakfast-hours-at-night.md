<p>The world has long-waited for McDonald&apos;s to expand their breakfast hours so we can cure our raging hangovers without arising before 10 a.m. Instead of extending breakfast, McDonald&apos;s has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140320185955/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/06/06/mcdonalds-after-midnight-_n_3396709.html">launched &quot;After Midnight,&quot;</a> a new late-night menu that includes some of the chain&apos;s more popular breakfast items. </p>

<p>From midnight until 4 a.m. at select 24-hour locations, guests can order Egg McMuffins, hash browns and other breakfast staples, in addition to Big Macs and french fries. The chain has been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140320185955/http://www.nydailynews.com/news/national/mcdonald-start-offering-breakfast-midnight-article-1.1131813">testing a similar deal</a> since last year and will now expand to a larger number of stores. </p>

<p>It&apos;s good news for tipsy revelers who want a cheap, filling, breakfast-y snack before passing out to reruns of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140320185955/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KnED-lQedjo"><em>Taxicab Confessions</em></a>. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140320185955/http://www.usatoday.com/story/money/business/2013/06/05/mcdonalds-after-midnight-menu/2393501/">USA Today</a> speculates that the menu will &quot;appeal more to Millennials, who typically like to eat what they want, when they want.&quot; If that&apos;s true, can some pushy Millennials please demand that McDonald&apos;s extend breakfast hours past 10:30 a.m.?
