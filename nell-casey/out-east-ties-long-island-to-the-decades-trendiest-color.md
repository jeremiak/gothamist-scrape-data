					<p>What common thread do <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616033030/http://nymag.com/thecut/2017/03/why-millennial-pink-refuses-to-go-away.html">Millennial Pink</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616033030/https://ny.eater.com/2016/12/20/14026838/out-east-long-island-restaurant-nyc">Long Island</a> share? They&apos;re both key features in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616033030/https://www.outeastnyc.com/">Out East</a>, a new East Village restaurant from Chef Tim Meyers (Charlie Bird) and Peter Kane and Anthony Serignese (The Stanton Social, Beauty &amp; Essex). </p>

<p>Strong Island&apos;s proximity to the ocean means a bounty of seafood on the menu, chiefly in crudo (raw) form including a Black Bass Carpaccio ($15) and Montauk Pearl Oysters ($3.50 each). More land-based items show up on the entrees menu, like a vegetarian Chilled Couscous ($16) with feta, radish and pistachio, and Pan Roasted Chicken ($27) with lentils and dates.</p>

<p>The bi-level eatery features a glass atrium room, under which sit the dusky rose banquettes. There&apos;s also a large bar off the front entrance serving summery-sounding cocktails like the East End Orange with orange blossom water, Flor de Cana 4 Year, Cointreau and Dolin Blanc.</p>

<p><em>509 East 6th Street, (212) 509-5096; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616033030/https://www.outeastnyc.com/">outeastnyc.com</a></em></p>
