</div></div>					<p>Dining and nightlife empresarios EMM Group have added another restaurant to their growing empire on the Bowery. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150118043048/http://emmgroupinc.com/restaurants/the-general/">The General</a>, which opened at the beginning of the month, joins nightclub Finale and lounge Bow in an enormous multi-concept space on the Bowery and Spring. Executive Chef Hung Huynh, who in addition to winning season three of <em>Top Chef</em>, also worked on EMM&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150118043048/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/06/meatpacking_district_restaurant_cat.php#photo-1">seafood-centric Catch</a>, produced a menu of modern Asian shared plates for the enormous 300-seat restaurant.</p>

<p>Chef Huynh darts around various types of Asian cuisine, including takes on Maki like the &quot;Mercy&quot; roll ($12) with spicy tuna, jalapeno salt and salsa verde and a Pad Thai ($12) with smoked tofu. Other dishes riff on Western compositions, like the Philly Peppersteak Stickybuns ($9) with braised short rib, caramelized onion and aged provolone and Chinese Chicken Tacos ($11). Large format dishes include Peking Duck ($59) with accompanying moo shu pancakes and a highbrow Beef &amp; Broccoli ($31) composed of braised, bone-in short rib, udon and Chinese broccoli.</p>

<p>Equally as impressive as the menu is the dramatic dining space, designed by ICRAVE studio, which combines seemingly disparate elements&#x2014;vintage fans, marble surfaces and exposed wooden beams&#x2014;into a cohesive and engaging meal setting. Bright red leather chairs and beautiful patterned wallpaper contrast the black lacquered tabletops and geometric floor design. Garage door-style windows, which make up most the 80-foot facade of the restaurant, can be opened to the street when the weather turns warmer.</p>

<p><em>199 Bowery // 212-271-7101</em></p>
