</div></div>					<p>Nearly 4,000 people clad all in white <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150801143909/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/18/diner_en_blanc_warning.php">descended on Bryant Park</a> last night for the third NYC installation of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150801143909/http://gothamist.com/tags/dinerenblanc">Diner En Blanc</a>. Like an elegantly dressed band of refugees, diners carted in tables, chairs and baskets of food to set up a giant communal dinner party on the lawn of the park, filling the entire space. </p>

<p>Though picnicking en mass in Bryant Park <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150801143909/http://gothamist.com/2011/06/29/what_its_like_to_be_in_that_bryant.php">isn&apos;t a novel concept</a>, co-organizer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150801143909/http://laist.com/2006/07/14/laist_interview_gilles_amsallem.php">Gilles Amsallem</a> insists the iconic park provided the perfect locale for this year&apos;s event. &quot;I think Bryant Park was grandiose. I mean it&apos;s one of the symbols, all the biggest events, all the most beautiful happenings here,&quot; Amsallem explained. &quot;The logistics, because it&apos;s all surrounded by buildings, is the most difficult part.&quot;</p>

<p>Guests seemed thrilled with the event and it was clear that many had put significant time and money into making the experience one to remember. Diana Chen and Elein Tse&#x2014;who&apos;d been accepted off the wait list just a week before the event&#x2014;had to scramble to buy all the necessary white clothing, tables, chairs and other accoutrement but both said it was &quot;totally worth it.&quot; &quot;When we walked in we almost wanted to cry,&quot; Tse admitted. &quot;It&apos;s overwhelmingly beautiful.&quot;</p>

<p>For the first few hours, the mood was mellow, with diners eating, drinking and snapping dozens of photos of themselves. Around 8:30, a DJ started cranking out a mix of contemporary hits&#x2014;can &quot;Blurred Lines&quot; season be over please?&#x2014;and some older pop songs that got the crowd on its feet. Attendees abandoned their dinners to form a giant dance party that took over the patio space behind the New York Public Library.</p>

<p>Looking ahead to next year, Amsallem envisions expanding the event to include more people. &quot;We have such a demand&#x2014;we had 14,000 people on the waiting list!&#x2014;so yes, we are building to 5,000 or 6,000,&quot; he revealed. &quot;We&apos;re trying not to grow too fast either, finding a venue is not easy. But we&apos;ll see what&apos;s going on for next year.&quot; Space might be limited in Manhattan, but we hear some cool things are going on in that place the kids call Brooklyn.<strong></strong></p>
