<p>Wake up this weekend to breakfast in Spain with an <strong>Andalusian-inspired brunch</strong> at Iron Chef Jose Garces&apos; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/26/jose_garces_amada.php#photo-1">newish Brookfield Place restaurant</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/http://newyork.amadarestaurant.com/">Amada</a>. The midday feasting includes dishes like Huevos Flamencos ($13), a tomato pepper stew with eggs, migas, and serrano ham and a brunch special ($29) that includes a bread basket, coffee or tea and a choice of dishes including serrano ham, smoked salmon opened faced sandwiches or brandade with green olive and potato. </p>

<p>Meanwhile in East Williamsburg, new <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/20/win_son_east_williamsburg.php#photo-1">Taiwanese-American soul food restaurant</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/http://winsonbrooklyn.com/#/?_k=bbeyva"><strong>Win Won</strong></a> also has a global brunch on the weekends. Snack on garlic and cilantro-marinated cucumbers ($5) and dou jiang you tiao ($7), a fried dough with salty soy milk. Larger entrees include a Taiwanese take on chicken and waffles ($15) and tsung you bing jia dan ($11), a scallion pancake with an herb omelette.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="102116soup.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/102116soup.jpg" width="640" height="480"> <br> <i> French Onion Soup from Bar Tabac (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/https://www.yelp.com/biz_photos/bar-tabac-brooklyn?select=QYamUp1v6dUdRY4LZ7W7UA">Yelp</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>On Saturday, the <strong>Smith Street Soup Festival</strong> returns with a lineup of local restaurants offering their takes on the liquid dish. From 1 - 4 p.m., stop by the corners of Bergen, Butler and President to purchase tastes ($5 for 5 tastings and $10 for 12), then walk around to sample soups from Fawkner, Bar Tabac, Avlee, Stinky Brooklyn, Vinny&apos;s, Sunken Hundred and a dozen more. Proceeds benefit the culinary arts program at the nearby High School For International Studies.</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/http://kingscountydistillery.com/">Kings County Distillery</a> hosts New York Times editor and author Clay Risen on Sunday as part of <strong>The Spirited Pen: Writers Talk About Whiskey</strong>. Risen&apos;s book, <em>American Whiskey, Bourbon, and Rye: A Guide to the Nation&apos;s Favorite Spirit</em>, makes him an ideal candidate to discuss the whiskies coming out of the Brooklyn Navy Yard-based distillery. Attendees will get a one hour guided tour of the facility, including the barrel aging room, plus a taste of four whiskies from Kings County Distillery. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/http://kingscountydistillery.com/events/">Tickets $14</a>. </p>

<p>Get another glimpse of NYC&apos;s robust alcohol production scene with the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/http://bushwickbrewingtour.com/">Bushwick Brewing Tour</a></strong> on Saturday afternoon. Tour guide Chris Heuberger leads the group around northern Brooklyn for some insight into the area&apos;s brewing history as well as general history of the city. The tour ends with a few pints at craft beer bar <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/http://www.thewellbrooklyn.com/">The Well</a>. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161109191308/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/landmark-bushwick-breuckelen-beer-oct-22-tickets-27271488757">Tickets $30</a>. </p>
