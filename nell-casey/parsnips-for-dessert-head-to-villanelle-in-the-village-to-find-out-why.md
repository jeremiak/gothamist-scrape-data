					<p>Wouldn&apos;t it be great if, when you left a job, someone reached out to offer you another? That&apos;s what happened to Nick Licata, a chef who left a restaurant job and immediately landed another when fans of his career at Degustation and Jack&#x2019;s Luxury Oyster Bar&#x2014;first time restaurant owners Catherine Manning and Andy Amer&#x2014;asked if he&apos;d head up their new spot <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122916/http://www.villanellenyc.com/">Villanelle</a>, which opens tonight on East 12th Street. </p>

<p>In the time-honored tradition of restaurants located near the Union Square Greenmarket, Licata will pillage the supplies there for things like veggies and fish. &quot;I&apos;m familiar with the purveyors,&quot; Licata <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122916/https://www.nytimes.com/2017/02/28/dining/villanelle-restaurant-opening.html?src=mv">confided to the Times</a>. (Just once I hope to see two chefs fighting each other in the streets for the last morsel of radiccio. I&apos;m looking at you, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122916/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/11/union_square_cafe.php">Carmen Quagliata</a>.)</p>

<p>Check out the opening menu below, which offers dishes like Portobello Mushroom Duxelles, a buttery mushroom and shallot mashup; Icelandic Char with dill, sorrel and caramelized cream; and sliced Beef served with polenta and mustard greens. There are even Butter-Braised Parsnips with white chocolate and sea salt for dessert. What have you wrought, Licata?</p>

<p>Drink lists haven&apos;t dropped yet, but the general manager and beverage guy is James Shields from Betony (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122916/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/28/rip_bars_restaurants_2016.php">RIP</a>).</p>

<p><em>15 East 12th Street, 212-989-2474; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307122916/http://www.villanellenyc.com/">villanellenyc.com</a></em></p>
