<p>The City Council had a busy night last night, passing bills requiring <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150305055024/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/27/nyc_now_largest_city_in_america_req.php">paid sick leave</a> and a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150305055024/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/27/council_votes_to_check_nypds_power.php">watchdog for the NYPD</a>. But more importantly, they were also busy guaranteeing your God given right to eat Eggs Benedict outside before noon on a Sunday. <em>Finally</em>!</p>

<p>After a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150305055024/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/19/williamsburg_community_board_launch.php">tense year</a> of clandestine al fresco brunching, the City Council voted to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150305055024/http://www.nydailynews.com/blogs/dailypolitics/2013/06/city-council-overrides-paid-sick-days-veto-passes-city-budgetand-ends-the-war-">abolish an outdated law</a> that barred restaurants from serving meals outdoors before noon on Sundays. We&apos;re now all free to eat pancakes outdoors starting at 10 a.m., just the way <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150305055024/http://gothamist.com/2011/11/03/williamsburg_hipster_church_way_mor.php">Jesus wanted it</a>.</p>
