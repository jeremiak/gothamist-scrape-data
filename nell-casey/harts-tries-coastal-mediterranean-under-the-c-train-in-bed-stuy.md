					<p>The tiny space beneath the clanking of the C train on Franklin Avenue that once housed a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171024082739/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/26/willow_bed-stuy.php#photo-1">contemporary seasonal restaurant</a> is now home to Mediterranean seasonal restaurant called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171024082739/http://www.hartsbrooklyn.com/">Hart&apos;s</a>. Chef/owner Nick Perkins (Diner, Marlow &amp; Sons) has put forward a streamlined menu of lighter dishes cemented in coastal Mediterranean philosophy, which celebrates simple flavors accented predominantly by ingredients like olive oil and lemon juice.</p>

<p>Vegetables dominate much of the smaller dishes, bulked up by bready accompaniments like the clam toast with pancetta ($15) and bowls of creamy ricotta cheese with chunks of fluffy focaccia for dipping. Though the menu should change frequently, a grass-fed lamb burger ($15) on a potato bun that can be topped with anchovies ($2), if desired, will anchor it indefinitely. </p>

<p>With not a lot of square footage to work with, diners will experience a similar setup to the space&apos;s previous restaurant life. Sturdy wooden benches have been replaced with padded seating, however, and a communal butcher block table with views into the kitchen has been added.</p>

<p><em>506 Franklin Street, 718-636-6228; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171024082739/http://www.hartsbrooklyn.com/">hartsbrooklyn.com</a></em></p>
