<p>A Miami-based restaurant will be throwing a one-weekend-only <strong>hot chicken pop-up</strong> on Saturday&apos;s and Sunday&apos;s Smorgasburgs in Williamsburg and Prospect Park. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616101047/https://theconfidantemiamibeach.hyatt.com/en/hotel/dining/bird-and-bone.html">Bird &amp; Bone</a> chef Richard Hales will be serving up the fiery bird&#x2014;made with Florida Honey, House Mustard, Cucumber Pickle and Zak the Baker Country Bread&#x2014;alongside Watermelon with Wife Hales&apos; Homemade Pepper Jelly, and Yellow Mustard &amp; Black Pepper Potato Salad at the weekend Smorgs. </p>

<p>Sweet tooths take note of Sunday&apos;s <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616101047/https://www.92y.org/event/chocolate-fest-2017">Chocolate Fest</a></strong> at the 92nd Street Y, where they&apos;re gathering tons of chocolate and chocolate personalities together for an epic one-day feast. Expect bonbons, ice cream, origin chocolate, cheese and chocolate pairings, plus the opportunity to be the first in NYC to sample Bonnat&apos;s Selva Maya chocolate. There&apos;ll be a &quot;personal chocolate shopper&quot; on hand, as well as chocolate book authors like Megan Giller, an expert in craft chocolate. </p>

<p>For a coffee shop, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616101047/http://www.eastonecoffee.com/">East One</a> in Carroll Gardens has an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616101047/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/11/east_one_brooklyn.php#photo-1">impressive food lineup</a>. Try it on for size during their new <strong>happy hour</strong>, which runs a schedule-friendly Monday through Sunday from 5 p.m. to 8 p.m. During those times they&apos;ll be offering $1 oysters and $6 pints of draft beer, after which you can stick around for their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616101047/http://www.eastonecoffee.com/nyc/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/dinner-menu.pdf">dinner menu</a> that includes dishes like Pozole Verde ($16) with pork and Blackened Salmon ($22) with hoppin john and spring garlic. </p>

<p>Bed-Stuy&apos;s <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616101047/https://alicesarbor.squarespace.com/">Alice Arbor</a> celebrates five years</strong> on Saturday by throwing a big anniversary party starting at 8 p.m. Stop by for free mango-ginger mojitos and hors d&apos;oeuvres, plus live music until the DJ takes over at 10 p.m. They&apos;re promising a raffle with some prizes that hopefully include free versions of their customizable Mac &amp; Cheese.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="050517paella.JPG" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616101047im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/050517paella.JPG" width="640" height="480"> <br> <i> (La Sirena)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>It&apos;s riches of rice on Sundays starting this weekend at Chelsea&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616101047/http://lasirena-nyc.com/">La Sirena</a>, one of the latest in the Mario Batali/Joe Bastianich empire. <strong>Paella Sundays</strong> means unlimited paella on the restaurant&apos;s patio, including  &quot;Gran Paella &quot;(chicken, lamb, jam&#xF3;n, razor clams, oysters, charred lemon) from 3 p.m. to 8 p.m. for $20. There are options to add on a selection of five snacks ($14 per person) and glasses of sangria, beer and the restaurant&apos;s seasonal gin and tonics are just $7. <br>
