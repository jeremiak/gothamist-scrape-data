<p>Chef Michael Psilakis&apos;s Williamsburg venue <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141204/http://thehallbrooklyn.com/">The Hall at MP</a> continues its series of pairing music with relevant cuisine. This Saturday, it&apos;s the conclusion of the <strong>New Orleans Crawfish Boil Series</strong> with the Sunny Side Social Club and four other acts, with doors opening at 6 p.m. Along with the soul-blues and funk, suck on a crawfish boil ($25) with potatoes and corn, or other NOLA specialties like Bayou Fried Shrimp ($14) or Creole Fried Rice Jambalaya ($16). <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141204/http://www.ticketweb.com/t3/sale/SaleEventDetail?eventId=6678245&amp;pl=cegpresents&amp;dispatch=loadSelectionData&amp;REFID=mp">Tickets</a> that include a 1.5 pound serving of crawfish are $35, with general admission tickets going for $15. </p>

<p>Gowanus&apos;s new <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141204/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/12/freeks_mill_gowanus.php#photo-1">seasonal small plates</a> restaurant <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141204/http://freeksmill.com/">Freek&apos;s Mill</a> begins brunch this weekend for those of us sticking around the city. Stop by for dishes that defy any genre, like Shakshuka made with duck eggs in a spicy tomato sauce, Biscuits and Red Eye Gravy with oysters and Benton&apos;s country ham, and a Dutch Pancake with blueberries and clotted cream. They&apos;re doing cocktails, too, including a Michelada with house sangrita and a Mezcal Breakfast Cocktail with cucumber, lime and sriracha. </p>

<p>Three Texas titans are teaming up for some weekend breakfast fun. Both locations of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141204/http://hillcountry.com/">Hill Country</a> will be opening early Friday, Saturdays and Sundays in July to serve kolaches, the sweet and savory pastries popular in the Lone Star State. In Brooklyn, kolaches will come from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141204/http://www.brooklynkolacheco.com/">Brooklyn Kolache Co.</a> and in Manhattan, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141204/http://www.kingskolacheny.com/#!our-story/c10tw">Kings Kolache</a>. The Texas Czech treats will be available from 8:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. in fruit, cheese and sausage flavors. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141204/http://humboldtandjackson.com/">Humboldt &amp; Jackson</a> celebrates two years of food and drink in Williamsburg the way we all celebrate a birthday: getting drunk and singing karaoke. On Sunday, they&apos;re trowing a <strong>&quot;KARAo-que&quot; Party</strong>, serving July 4th-inspired eats like smoked ribs, Maine style lobster rolls, a Pork roll hot dog, BBQ sliders, Elote corn salad and lots of other festive dishes. Then at 8 p.m., the free karaoke begins, with a prize for the evening&apos;s best <em>chanteur</em>. </p>
