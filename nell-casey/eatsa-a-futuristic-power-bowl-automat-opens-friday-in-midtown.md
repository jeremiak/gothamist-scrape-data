					<p>Employing technology to further advancements in dining continues an upward trajectory, from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223062855/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/06/behold_the_citys_first_grid-powered.php">solar panels on food carts</a> to the further reliance <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223062855/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/07/whole_foods_to_launch_cheaper_store.php">on tablet systems</a>. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223062855/https://www.eatsa.com/">Eatsa</a>, the California transplant <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223062855/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/18/eatsa_quinoa_robots.php">bringing more quinoa bowls to Midtown</a>, looks both behind and ahead for its food delivery system, borrowing elements from the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223062855/http://gothamist.com/2012/05/24/nypl_lunch_hour_nyc.php">automat era</a> as well as the iPad/phone-obsession most of us just can&apos;t quit.</p>

<p>Here&apos;s how it goes:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223062855if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/7d7dhJQBaf0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The chainlet, which debuts on Madison Avenue on Friday, has been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223062855/http://laist.com/2015/11/12/eatsa.php">popular</a> in health-forward spots like Los Angeles, and now it&apos;s betting New Yorkers will want to go wild for things like Hummus &amp; Falafel Bowls, No Worry Curry Bowls, and Berry Chia Parfaits. At least the price is a totally normal amount to spend on lunch: everything&apos;s $6.95. </p>
