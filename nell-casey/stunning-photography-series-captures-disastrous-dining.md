</div></div>					<p>A picnic disrupted by a swarm of bees, a mobster shot dead over a plate of spaghetti and meatballs. These are a few of the subjects of a new photography project called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150206202422/http://www.davideluciano.com/meals-interrupted">&quot;Meals Interrupted,&quot;</a> by photographer Davide Luciano and his wife <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150206202422/http://www.claudiaficca.com/">Claudia Ficca</a>. The colorful photos capture dining scenes being disrupted in some way, either but a catastrophic event like an explosion, or the scene in a cafeteria following a food fight.</p>

<p>They shot some of the scenes in New York City and others in their native Montreal. Ficca handled the food styling, arranging each item post-disaster. &#x201C;The idea of working directly with bees was very daunting at first,&quot; Ficca explains. &quot;But once we were in the apiary and the bees were buzzing all around us, it turned out to be very soothing.&#x201D; </p>

<p>The couple just completed the series and Luciano tells us they don&apos;t yet have a show scheduled but will keep us updated if they plan one in the city. In the meantime, check out the series <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150206202422/http://www.davideluciano.com/meals-interrupted">on their website</a> and also take a peek at their other whimsical photography collaboration on a subject near and dear to New Yorker&apos;s hearts: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150206202422/http://www.davideluciano.com/potholes">Potholes</a>.</p>
