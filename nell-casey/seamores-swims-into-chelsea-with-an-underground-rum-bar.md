					<p>Two years after tossing aside the meatballs for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170606015225/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/26/seamores_nolita.php#photo-1">fishier fare</a>, The Meatball Shop&apos;s Michael Chernow expands his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170606015225/https://www.seamores.com/">Seamore&apos;s</a> brand into Chelsea&#x2014;with an underground rum bar in tow. </p>

<p>The bar, called Fishbones, doesn&apos;t open for another two weeks or so, but they&apos;re promising bespoke rum cocktails made from smaller rum producers. GTFO, Malibu. With <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170606015225/http://gothamist.com/tags/mezcal">mezcal</a> seemingly the spirit of the moment, this is a nice departure&#x2014;and just in time for summer. Their opening signature cocktail is the &quot;Rum &amp; Coco,&quot; which can be served out of a coconut. </p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170606015225/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/07/seamores_fish_review.php#photo-1">fresh catch of the day</a> anchors the new Chelsea location, too, along with new dishes including a Blackened Fish Burger, a Nicoise Salad and a Poke Taco, perhaps modeled after the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170606015225/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/18/seamores_poke_window.php">poke takeout window</a> that popped up at the Nolita original last summer. Non-seafood options include a &quot;Big Cheese&quot; Burger with bacon, Shrimp + Short Rib Tacos, and Crispy Broccoli with fried onions, grapes and roasted garlic aioli. </p>

<p><em>161 8th Avenue, 212-597-9222; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170606015225/https://www.seamores.com/">seamores.com</a></em></p>
