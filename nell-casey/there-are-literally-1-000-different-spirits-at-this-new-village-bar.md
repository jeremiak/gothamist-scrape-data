					<p>Contemporary cocktail spots often adopt the &quot;book&quot; method for their menus, giving patrons a wide overview of their cocktail, spirit and other drinking options. The book at newly-opened <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616071416/http://www.libraryofdistilledspirits.com/">Library of Distilled Spirits</a> is a <em>tome</em> literally called the &quot;Encyclopedia.&quot; Settle in for some not-so-light reading.</p>

<p>The bar&apos;s a team up between APICII and Employee&apos;s Only Dushan Zaric with Kyle Tran (from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616071416/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/06/alinea_group_the_aviary_nyc.php">Chicago&apos;s The Aviary</a>) coming on as beverage director. In addition to studying the encyclopedia, guests can peep the collection of alcohols in a &quot;16-by-20-foot floor-to-ceiling library wall&quot; where the bottles are displayed and illuminated. Cocktails&#x2014;which number in the hundreds&#x2014;are painstakingly described down to the last detail and other books dedicated to cocktails and alcohol are on-hand for further education. </p>

<p>The adjacent restaurant, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616071416/http://www.boweryroad.com/">Bowery Road</a>, is also open and includes cocktails designed by Tran. Locanda Verde vet Ron Rosselli created some familiar and comfort food classic-type dishes including a riff on Outback Steakhouse with a &quot;Bloomin&apos; Mushrom,&quot; Brisket &quot;Pastrami,&quot; a Jerk Chicken Sandwich, Amish Beer Can Chicken and a loaded Crispy Baked Potato with french onion cream, bacon and chives. </p>

<p><em>132 4th Avenue, 212-432-1324; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616071416/http://www.boweryroad.com/">boweryroad.com</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616071416/http://www.libraryofdistilledspirits.com/">libraryofdistilledspirits.com</a></em></p>
