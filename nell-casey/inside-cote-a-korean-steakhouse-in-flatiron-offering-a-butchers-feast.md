					<p>A new restaurant opening Thursday evening combines the classic American steakhouse with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607220341/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/02/korean_bbq_how_to.php">classic Korean barbecue</a>.  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607220341/http://cotenyc.com/">Cote</a>, run by Simon Kim, an owner of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607220341/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/16/michelin_guide_nyc_2017.php">Michelin-starred Piora</a> in the West Village, will be a &quot;Korean steakhouse,&quot; as the team has dubbed it, offering both the experience of Korean barbecue with prime cuts of beef and some steakhouse staples on the menu.</p>

<p>&quot;It&apos;s more appealing to both non-Koreans and Koreans with a finer experience, good service and wines,&quot; Kim <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607220341/https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/06/dining/cote-korean-barbecue-restaurant-openings.html">described to the Times</a>. </p>

<p>The kitchen will be run by David Shim, an alum of M. Wells Steakhouse, and though customers will be responsible for cooking their own sliced dry-aged sirloins and marinated short rib&#x2014;on $4,000 in-laid grill tables, I might add&#x2014;Shim will be preparing steakhouse dishes like steak tartare and wedge salads, alongside traditional Korean dol-sot bibimbop and casseroles. </p>

<p>The primary menu option will be the &quot;Butcher&apos;s Feast,&quot; a $45 per person extravaganza featuring four prime cuts of beef alongside traditional Korean banchan, lettuce to wrap the meat, stews, egg souffl&#xE9;, salad and soft serve to close. </p>

<p>Victoria James, who was also at Piora, designed a beverage menu that consists of classic cocktails like martinis; soju, the drink of choice among Korean barbecue eaters; and wines, glasses of which will be &quot;poured out of magnums,&quot; which sounds very fancy indeed.</p>

<p><em>16 West 22nd Street, 212-401-7986; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170607220341/http://cotenyc.com/">cotenyc.com</a></em></p>
