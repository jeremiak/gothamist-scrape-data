</div></div>					<p>After <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515080114/http://www.crainsnewyork.com/article/20121114/ECONOMY/121119960">enduring Buildings Department inspection delays</a> following <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515080114/http://gothamist.com/tags/hurricanesandy">Hurricane Sandy</a>, Benjamin Prelvukaj and Ben Sinanaj&#x2014;the &quot;Bens&quot; behind popular midtown meat house Benjamin Steakhouse&#x2014;have finally opened their new restaurant, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515080114/http://theseafiregrill.com/">The Sea Fire Grill</a>, on East 48th Street. Executive Chef Richard Pims, a veteran of Stanton Social and Blue Fin, among others, has been brought aboard to helm the seafood-centric kitchen, which will also serve the Benjamin Group&apos;s signature dry-aged steaks. To guarantee the freshest catch, Chef Pims will personally select each item on a daily trip to the fish market, making sure to use sustainably sourced product whenever possible. </p>

<p>A citrus-specked Montauk Fluke Crudo ($14) stands out amidst other traditional raw bar items, including a daily selection of east and west coast oysters and Long Island&apos;s Littleneck Clams. For the appetizers, an island of airy cappuccino foam floats in a crimson bowl of Lobster Bisque ($15), and Smoked Brandade Fritters ($15) come with a rich dipping aioli flavored with truffles. Seafood entrees include whole lobsters and Alaskan King Crab Legs plus finessed dishes like the Herb Basted Atlantic Halibut ($36) in a lemon and white wine sauce and American Red Snapper ($36), served with peppery broccoli rabe and spicy chimichurri. For a taste from land and sea, the SFG Surf &amp; Turf ($74), includes a bone-in filet mignon and 1 1/2 pounds of steamed lobster. </p>

<p>Muted cream and brown tones dominate the dining areas with pops of color from recessed blue bulbs throughout the space. A shimmering blue accent wall gives the impression of being underwater and a large fireplace crackles at the far end of the dining room. The large bar area gives diners a front row seat to watch bartenders build the specialty cocktails and champagne spritzers, like the Angel&apos;s Sip ($12), which combines Old Overholt Rye and Antica Formula Sweet Vermouth with a 10-year-old balsamic vinegar. An SFG Burger ($18), Fish &amp; Chips ($20) and Oyster &quot;Po Boy&quot; Sliders ($14) can be ordered off the bar menu and are also available during lunch hours.</p>

<p><em>158 East 48th Street // 212-935-3785 // Open for lunch Monday - Friday 11:30 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.; Open daily for dinner from 4:30 p.m. to 10:45 p.m.</em></p>
