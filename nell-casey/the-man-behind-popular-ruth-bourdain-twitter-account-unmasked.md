                            <p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="071213ruthbourdain1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/071213ruthbourdain1.jpg" width="250" height="251" class="image-left"> </span>After years of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/07/did_the_internet_just_reveal_twitte.php">intense speculation</a> from the foodie media, the mind behind the hilarious Twitter mashup of Ruth Reichl and Anthony Bourdain, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/https://twitter.com/RuthBourdain">@RuthBourdain</a>, has been revealed. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/http://www.nytimes.com/2013/07/13/dining/whos-ruthbourdain-now-we-know.html?pagewanted=1&amp;_r=1&amp;smid=tw-share">The <em>Times</em></a> outs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/http://www.joshfriedland.com/">Josh Friedland</a>, a freelance food writer from New Jersey, as the composer of over three year&apos;s worth of naughty breakfast Tweets. </p>

<p>&#x201C;I never thought the joke would go on so long,&#x201D; Friedland told the <em>Times</em>. &#x201C;But the food world has become so ripe for satire in the time since I started it.&#x201D; Ripe for satire? We have <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/28/too_cheap_to_spend_650_for_dinner_a.php#photo-1">no,</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/26/foodies_who_hate_being_called_foodi.php">idea</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/10/mayonnaise_store_opens_in_brooklyn.php">what</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/08/heres_a_plate_of_pasta_thats_sellin.php">he</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820195124/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/25/photo_people_sleeping_on_street_to.php">means</a>. </p>

<p>So will Ruth Bourdain live on now as the voice of reason in this insane, foodiot town?</p>
