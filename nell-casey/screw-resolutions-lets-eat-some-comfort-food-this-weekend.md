<p>Delightful underground bar Jimmy&apos;s No. 43 hosts a Sunday full of savory stew with their <strong>annual celebration of cassoulet</strong>. This southern French dish contains hearty ingredients like meats, sausages and beans, which are slow-cooked in a a flavorful broth. From noon to 3 p.m., swing by for at least 10 &quot;creative and traditional&quot; versions of the dish, washed down with one of the bar&apos;s American beers, wines or ciders; your first pour is included in the ticket price. Tickets are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160601001116/http://eighthannualcassouletcookoff.brownpapertickets.com/">$30</a> with a portion benefiting Grow NYC Greenmarket programming. </p>

<p>Williamsburg&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160601001116/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/07/fondue_chalet_williamburg.php"><strong>pop up fondue chalet</strong></a> has extended its run until March. The cheese-drenched operation now runs Tuesdays through Saturdays from 7 p.m. until 11 p.m. at 125 Berry Street, offering crocs of fondue with bread, veggies and meats, plus a raclette service, veggies, salads and other dishes.</p>

<p><strike>Finally, if you&apos;re feeling the post-holiday pinch, soothe your bank account and line your stomach with some <strong>free nachos</strong> at the Brooklyn Brewery on Saturday night. The Brooklyn Brew HaHa! Comedy Show is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160601001116/http://www.villagevoice.com/restaurants/best-weekend-food-events-cassoulet-cook-off-free-nachos-and-a-persian-feast-8106073">offering free nachos</a> to the first 40 patrons to their show, which begins at 7 p.m. The grub will be provided by Nacho Ordinary Gals; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160601001116/http://www.eventbrite.com/e/bk-brew-haha-comedy-show-tickets-19794080628?aff=ebrowse">tickets $10</a>. </strike></p>
