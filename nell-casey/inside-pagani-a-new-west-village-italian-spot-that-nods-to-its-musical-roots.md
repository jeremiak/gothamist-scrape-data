</div></div>					<p>Restaurateur Massimo Lusardi, who opened popular UES Italian wine bar Uva in 2005, debuted his second Italian restaurant <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511170429/http://www.paganinyc.com/">Pagani</a> last week in the West Village. Commanding the bustling corner of Bleecker Street at 7th Avenue, the restaurant offers 64 seats for diners in both the sunlight-filled bar area and smaller sectioned dining sections throughout the space. At once rustic and refined, the restaurant contains elements harkening back to the space&apos;s historic roots as the O. Pagani &amp; Bros. music store. Taavo Somer&#x2014;the James Beard Award winner behind Freemans and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511170429/http://gothamist.com/2011/09/09/isa_latest_from_freemans_owner_open.php?gallery0Pic=9#photo-9">Isa</a>&#x2014;designed the space, bringing in vintage touches like reclaimed wood and tile floorings with contemporary elements that bring the space into this century.  </p>

<p>Executive Chef Mark Barrett&#x2014;whose previous gigs include Danny Meyer&apos;s fated Indian spot Tabla and Mario Batali&apos;s flagship Babbo&#x2014;steps out on his own here with a menu of multi-regional Italian fare. Starters of a Farm Poached Egg ($9) with oyster mushroom, baby spinach and crispy pancetta vinaigrette and Braised Beets ($9) with quinoa, baby arugula, citrus hazelnuts, bleu cheese and chives stand out. </p>

<p>The hearty pasta menu includes a traditional Pappardelle Bolognese ($16), whole wheat Garganelli ($19) with spicy sausage, red onion and tomato sauce and a carrot/chive Tagliatelle ($19) with zucchini, string beans and tomato. For proteins, Chef Barrett offers a &quot;Folded Chicken&quot; ($19) with arugula, Parmigiano, garden tomatoes, spinach and dried figs as well as a slow roasted Veal Shoulder ($25) with potato puree, broccoli rabe and wild mushrooms. </p>

<p>Italian cured meats and cheese platters, as well as a selection of crostini and arancini pair nicely with a glass of Italian wine or an herbacious and fizzy Aperol Spritz ($10). As befitting the current cocktail obsession, celebrated mixologist Sean McClure created a line of cocktails that also nod to the space&apos;s former musical history. The &quot;Nipper&quot; ($10)&#x2014;named for the iconic <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511170429/http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bd/OriginalNipper.jpg">RCA Dog</a>&#x2014;mixes Prosecco, Esprit de June, chamomile and lemon while the &quot;B-Side Lyric&quot; ($12) rocks Excellia Blanco Tequila, pineapple, jalapeno, Sauternes and lime.</p>

<p><em>289 Bleecker Street, (212) 488-5800; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511170429/http://www.paganinyc.com/">paganinyc.com</a>. Open daily 5 p.m. to 1 a.m.</em></p>
