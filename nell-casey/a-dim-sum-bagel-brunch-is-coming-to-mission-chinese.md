<p>Chinese food is quintessential Christmas food for New Yorkers who aren&apos;t saddled with familial obligations that involve a dry turkey or spiral ham. Danny Bowien of Mission Chinese will be adding another venue for mapo tofu-seeking folks this Christmas with the launch of a dim sum and bagel brunch that&apos;s debuting on Christmas Day. The chef <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160913225142/http://www.metro.us/new-york/dim-sum-and-bagels-mingle-at-mission-chinese-s-new-brunch-service/zsJolw---oXIadjRmdlozI/">revealed some details to Metro</a>, including the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160913225142/http://gothamist.com/2014/04/23/black_seed_bagels.php">use of Black Seed Bagels</a> for the carb component of the meal.</p>

<p>Beginning Christmas day from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.&#x2014;and continuing 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. on weekends after that&#x2014;the restaurant will have two types of carts making the rounds in the dining room, one laden with bagels and accouterments, and the other with Bowien&apos;s vision of dim sum. &quot;We&apos;re gonna have dim sum carts going around, but not with traditional dim sum,&quot; he explained. &quot;It&apos;s gonna be Mission Chinese Food: little plates of mapo tofu, and scrambled egg and tapioca dumplings.&quot; As for the bagel component, expect a whitefish salad of sorts and some chopped liver.</p>
