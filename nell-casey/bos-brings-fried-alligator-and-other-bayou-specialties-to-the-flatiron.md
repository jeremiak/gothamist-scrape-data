</div></div>					<p>This town is swamped with southern-based barbecue joints and biscuit-and-gravy comfort food eateries but with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507175155/http://gothamist.com/2011/02/06/still_got_it_great_jones_cafe.php">some exceptions</a>, New Yorkers have <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507175155/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/16/cuisine_map.php">very few places</a> to chow down on cajun and other New Orleans&apos; flavors. Enter <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507175155/http://bosrestaurant.com/">Bo&apos;s Kitchen &amp; Bar Room</a>, a new Flatiron restaurant that melds bayou cuisine with contemporary bistro fare; think fried alligator on the same menu with pork cheek ragu and red snapper crudo. </p>

<p>Chef/Partner Todd Mitgang, who also runs the kitchen at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507175155/http://gothamist.com/2012/07/19/inside_crave_fishbar.php#photo-1">Crave Fishbar</a>, created a fluid menu featuring classic New Orleans ingredients and flavors, which itself draws inspiration from France to Africa to Spain. For starters, try the Crispy Alligator ($13), served with a creamy chili aioli or Roasted Oysters ($15) from Montauk with creamed dinosaur kale and grana padano. Low Country Ham ($14), served with a biscuit and marmalade, channels southern comfort food, as do the Spicy Wild Gulf Shrimp Fra Diavolo ($28), served over cheese grits with a chili relish, and the Buttermilk Braised Fried Chicken ($23) with chicken gravy. </p>

<p>For an authentic bite of the Big Easy, opt for the Split Pea Fried Catfish ($27), which comes with crawfish, potato and cranberry beans in a shrimp Am&#xE9;ricaine sauce or the Spiced Louisiana Redfish ($29), with parsnip, sharon fruit and okra in a &quot;crabby&quot; sauce. You can also find Louisiana on the beverage menu, which offers three beers from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507175155/http://bayoutechebrewing.com/contact-us/">Bayou Teche Brewing</a> in addition to Abita Turbodog ($7). You won&apos;t find any Hurricanes, but they are shaking up other booze-heavy cocktails ($13) like the Antebellum (bourbon, blood orange tea, lemon, mint) and the Pierre Lafitte (Kappa Pisco, fresh grapefruit, Luxardo, egg white). There&apos;s also a build-your-own Pickle Martini option, where you can choose between nine vodkas and gins to mix with onion, okra, cranberry and other pickled products. </p>

<p><em>6 West 24th Street, (212) 234-2373; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150507175155/http://bosrestaurant.com/">bosrestaurant.com</a></em></p>
