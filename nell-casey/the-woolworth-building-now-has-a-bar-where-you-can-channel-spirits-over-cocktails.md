					<p>You&apos;re never going to get into the penthouse of the Woolworth Building (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015134756/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/21/110m_woolworth_penthouse_will_have.php">ever</a>), so the next best thing will be to drink inside its storied neo-Gothic interior...on the ground floor. A new bar, The Wooly Public, opens tonight inside the historic downtown building, with Death &amp; Co. and Maison Premiere vets running the drinks and food, respectively.</p>

<p>The Woolworth building&apos;s had a fancy, private bar/event space&#x2014;further cementing its exclusive status&#x2014; called The Wooly since 2009, but now the public can get into the building beyond <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015134756/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/06/woolworth_building_1.php">the occasional lobby tour</a>. Once there, it&apos;s a game of spot the wooly mammoth artwork (not going to be difficult) while trying out Eryn Reece&apos;s cocktails, divided into classic cocktails (&quot;Old Souls&quot;) and innovative (&quot;New Editions&quot;). From the latter, the Woolynesia ($15) combines Greenhook Ginsmiths Old Tom Gin, Ancho Verde, Aperol, Passion Fruit, Lime Cinnamon, Ginger and Giffard Peche de Vigne and arrives in cute little mammoth-shaped mugs.</p>

<p>The prices for Jeff Srole&apos;s food are pretty egalitarian as far as destination dining inside a famous building can be. A $12 Public Burger comes with two patties, cheese and fries on the side, and even the pricier Private Burger with dry-aged beef, applewood smoked cheddar and pickles is only $19, hardly the spendiest of the fancy burgers. </p>

<p>Channel the spirits with a round of Ouija&#x2014;board games are built into tables with pieces available to borrow&#x2014;and snack on some pimento cheese-stuffed fried olives ($9), Peking Duck Tots with house queso ($15), or Frogs Legs with tartar sauce ($12), a throwback to the Woolworth&apos;s original 1913 restaurant.</p>

<p><em>The Wooly Public is located inside the Woolworth Building at 233 Broadway, 212-571-2930; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015134756/http://thewooly.com/">thewooly.com</a></em></p>
