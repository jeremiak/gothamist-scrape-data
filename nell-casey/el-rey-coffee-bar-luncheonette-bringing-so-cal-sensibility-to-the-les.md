</div></div>					<p>Coffee shops grow like weeds, a perfect infestation for caffeine-addicted city dwellers. Lower East Siders will have another one to pluck once <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429101313/http://www.elreynyc.com/">El Rey Coffee Bar &amp; Luncheonette</a> opens its doors this Thursday. The casual service lunch spot comes from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429101313/http://gothamist.com/2010/12/07/goat_town_photos_menu.php#photo-1">Goat Town</a>&apos;s Nicholas Morgenstern and head chef Gerardo Gonzalez, who&apos;ve put together a SoCal vibe and menu of healthful, flavorful eats to fuel the day. </p>

<p>First and foremost, they&apos;re doing espresso, drip and pour over brews from Counter Culture Coffee, which you can order with sweet baked goods like Sour Plum &amp; Ginger Danish, Banana Sesame Bread or a bowl of granola. The lunch menu, which runs from 11 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., features bowls of Cashew Grits topped with either braised pork or mustard greens; seasonally rotating flavors of falafel; and &quot;Lost Bread&quot; egg frittatas, whipped up behind the marble-topped luncheonette counter. </p>

<p>Raw salads like Kale with shaved almonds and almond vinaigrette, hand pies in flavors like Duck Confit &amp; Cherries and little bites including cheese sticks and beet pickled eggs are available anytime after 11 a.m. So too are draft beers&#x2014;Fullers, Westbrook and Evil Twin are a few breweries on hand&#x2014;wines, and house-made aguas frescas like the &quot;gin-less fizz&quot; made with cucumber limeade and juniper seltzer. </p>

<p><em>100 Stanton Street (between Ludlow and Orchard), (212) 260-3950; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429101313/http://www.elreynyc.com/">elreynyc.com</a></em></p>
