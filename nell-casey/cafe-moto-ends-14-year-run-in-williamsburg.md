<p>Darling restaurant and casual live music venue <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113153326/http://www.cafe-moto.com/">Cafe Moto</a> is no more. The Williamsburg spot&apos;s last night was July 30th, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113153326/https://www.facebook.com/CafeMotoBrooklyn/photos/a.466698680026403.119765.143421949020746/1333066670056262/?type=3&amp;theater">according to Facebook</a>, when they welcomed some jazz musicians from their early days.</p>

<p>The charming restaurant, which occupied the triangle of Broadway and Hooper Street for the past 14 years, was owned by John McCormick, a restaurateur and interior designer who also owns St. Mazie in Williamsburg and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113153326/http://gothamist.com/2014/09/26/witlof_williamsburg_belgian_beer.php">designed the interior</a> of Flemish brasserie Witlof, also in Williamsburg. </p>

<p>Though this current incarnation of Moto is gone, the restaurant&apos;s website promises a &quot;new venture&quot; coming sometime this fall. It&apos;s unclear whether this will be a new restaurant within the Moto space or somewhere else in the neighborhood. Ownership for Moto did not immediately return a request for comment.</p>
