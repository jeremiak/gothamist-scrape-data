					<p>It takes a lot of chutzpah to describe your sandwich as the &quot;Best Grilled Cheese Ever,&quot; but chef Eli Kulp&#x2014;who has won many accolades for his Philadelphia restaurants&#x2014;may well have earned this self-proclaimed title. Today, Kulp and business partner Ellen Yin open <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160630054910/http://www.highstreetonhudson.com/">High Street On Hudson</a>, sister restaurant to Philly&apos;s High Street On Market, featuring an on-site bread-making program that services the restaurant&apos;s decadent breakfast and lunch sandwich program. </p>

<p>Kulp, who was paralyzed in the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160630054910/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/13/5_dead_over_50_injured_as_nyc-bound.php#photo-1">deadly Amtrak derailment</a> earlier this year, has tapped a top-notch team to help execute the restaurant&apos;s vision, including Sullivan Street Bakery alum and James Beard finalist Alex Bois, who&apos;ll be turning out the breads and pastries. This includes the roasted potato bread for his &quot;Best Grilled Cheese Ever&quot; ($13) with cultured butter and Noble Cheddar, and the sage-black pepper biscuit that serves as a base for The Bodega ($12), a gourmet breakfast sandwich with malted breakfast sausage and aged cheddar. </p>

<p>It&apos;s all about breakfast and lunch for the time being, with dinner service launching in January. Pastries, breads, espresso drinks and a handful of other breakfast sandwiches and dishes are available beginning at 7 a.m. weekdays and 8 a.m. weekends. Lunch sandwiches plus hearty vegetable salads and sides can be procured until 3:30 daily.</p>
