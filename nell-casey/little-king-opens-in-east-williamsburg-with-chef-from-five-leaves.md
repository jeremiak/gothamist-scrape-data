					<p>The waits at Five Leaves in Greenpoint remain interminable, so instead, head over to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161030050421/https://www.facebook.com/littlekingbk/">Little King</a>, where the former Five Leaves executive sous chef is now turning out upgraded bar food over in East Williamsburg. The bar&#x2014;and owners Sam Esterman, John Moskowitz and Christina Salway are clear that, first and foremost, it&apos;s about getting &quot;rowdy&quot; and having fun&#x2014;opened last night on Metropolitan Avenue just off the Graham Avenue L stop. And though it looks fancier than the average watering hole, you order everything at the bar and they&apos;ll sell you a can of Victoria Lager for $4.</p>

<p>There aren&apos;t many (or any?) spots offering a side of roasted bone marrow ($6) to accompany a Brioche Grilled Cheese ($10) with cheddar, pickled heirloom tomato, and sprouts, nor do many bars offering Scallop Crudo ($13) with charred lemon ponzu, pickled chilies and sesame. Philip Vernier&apos;s menu aims to encapsulate &quot;high-end bar food inspired by our American and Southeast Asian favorites&quot; along with &quot;what is coming out of Paris.&quot; Have the French adopted Le Burger? At least here they do.</p>

<p>Cocktails run a reasonable-for-these-times $12 and most of the booze, even top shelf, even less than that. </p>

<p><em>749 Metropolitan Avenue; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161030050421/https://www.facebook.com/littlekingbk/">Facebook</a></em></p>
