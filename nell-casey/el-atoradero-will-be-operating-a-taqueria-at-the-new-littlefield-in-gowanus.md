title: El Atoradero Will Be Operating A Taqueria At The New Littlefield In Gowanus
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="062017taqueria.jpeg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/062017taqueria.jpeg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/http://claywilliams.photoshelter.com/">Clay Williams</a>/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Eight year old <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/http://gothamist.com/2009/05/14/littlefield.php#photo-1">Gowanus venue</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/http://www.littlefieldnyc.com/">Littlefield</a> recently uprooted from Degraw Street to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/http://bklyner.com/littlefield-reopens-on-sackett-street-will-host-party-for-sunnys/">new and larger digs</a> a block away on Sackett Street. Joining them in this new venture: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/http://www.elatoraderobrooklyn.com/">El Atoradero</a>, the Prospect Heights darling serving some of the city&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/03/el_atorradero_nachos_ftw.php#photo-4">best Mexican fare</a>.</p>

<p>Owner Noah Arenstein and Chef Denisse Lina will run <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/https://www.instagram.com/taqueriaelatoradero/">Taqueria El Atoradero</a> inside the venue&apos;s &quot;Parklife,&quot; an adjacent bar and 4,000-square-foot outdoor area next to the main venue space. &quot;We&apos;ve been wanting to do a taqueria version of El Atoradero for a little while and the timing kind of worked out really well,&quot; Arenstein told Gothamist. &quot;We wanted to use this as a test to see if we could start doing taquerias like that.&quot;</p>

<p>The Taqueria will be a walk-up window in a shipping container in the outdoor space. Guests can place an order there and, eventually, there will be a kiosk on the Littlefield side where event goers can place an order to be delivered to that side of the venue. </p>

<p>The superb homemade tortillas and taco fillings that garnered Lina <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/23/el_atoradero_brooklyn.php#photo-1">success at her first restaurant in the Bronx</a> will now be on offer in Gowanus, including her famed carnitas. &quot;We&apos;re doing campechano...which is a little more free-wheeling,&quot; Arenstein explains. &quot;Campechano usually means &apos;more than one topping,&apos;&quot; which at the taqueria could mean tacos topped with chorizo, cheese and french fries; or chorizo and steak. </p>

<p>El Atoradero&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/03/el_atorradero_nachos_ftw.php#photo-4">superlative nachos</a> will be served at Littlefield, too. Their classic, with a base of house-made blue corn chips, can be upended with a french fries substitution, a riff the restaurant experimented with as an off-menu item last summer. Crisp, fast food-style fries are topped with all the fixings, including beans, cheese and pickled jalapenos. </p>

<p>&quot;I&apos;ve been to Mexico City a couple times for research and there&apos;s this great taqueria in La Merced that calls themselves McTio, with big golden arches, and one of their taco options is french fries,&quot; Arenstein says. &quot;We were playing around with hamburger tacos last year with friends and french fries and tacos taste really good, french fries and burritos is kind of a thing already, so we wanted to offer [french fry nachos].&quot;</p>

<p>Finally, they&apos;ll be creating a salsa bar that takes a page from a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/https://www.yelp.com/biz_photos/los-tacos-no-1-new-york?select=aPKyBu43ZAGOrzUtXnVLCQ">similar setup at Los Tacos No.1</a> in Chelsea Market. There will be four salsas on the menu, including cucumber verde (pepino verde) and a mango habanero, both new, joining molcajete and roja. </p>

<p>The Taqueria is slated to open on Friday.</p>

<p><em>Taqueria El Atoradero will be located inside Littlefield at 635 Sackett Street in Gowanus; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620202622/https://www.instagram.com/taqueriaelatoradero/">Instagram</a>. The Taqueria will be open Monday- Thursday 5 p.m. to midnight, Friday 5 p.m. to 2 a.m., Saturday noon to 2 a.m., and Sunday noon to midnight.</em></p>
