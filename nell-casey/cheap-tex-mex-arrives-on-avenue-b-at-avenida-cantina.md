					<p>A new spot to score queso has popped up in the East Village this week with the opening of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031153334/http://avenidacantina.com/">Avenida Cantina</a>, a collaboration between a few Tex-pats looking to drench the city in even more melted cheese&#x2014;not that there&apos;s anything wrong with that! Dallas-born Christen Hagan, who runs Double Wide Bar &amp; Southern Kitchen on nearby East 12th Street with partner Josh Gent, along with Allison and Dustin Baxley, from Austin and Corpus Christi, respectively, have created a space they hope will service ex-Texans with house-made tortillas and lots of Texas football on the teevee. </p>

<p>&quot;We believe one of the main aspects of Tex Mex that homesick southerners miss is &apos;casual,&apos;&quot; Hagan explained. &quot;While the city is flooded with fancy Mexican food, Texans and even local Tex Mex lovers in NY crave an authentic cuisine that&#x2019;s priced like Tex Mex.&quot; To that end, there&apos;s nothing on the menu priced over $12, and that amount will get you a heaping platter of three enchiladas with salad, rice and beans or a beef and chorizo burger with a side of curly fries.</p>

<p>Chips and salsa are on the house, naturally, but the restaurant&apos;s white or yellow queso ($7) is a must-order, along with a variety of tacos (ground beef or shredded chicken, $3.50; brisket, fried chicken or catfish $4), plus all-day breakfast tacos ($2.50) stuffed with eggs, cheese and a choice of meat or vegetable filling. Tortillas are made in-house and can even be ordered as a side dish ($5/$7) with warmed avocado butter for dipping and slathering. Taco salad ($10), fried stuffed avocados ($7) and single-chip nachos ($7), a Tex-Mex hallmark, are also on offer. </p>

<p>There&apos;s a full bar upstairs pouring Shiner beers and fresh fruit margaritas, but the owners are employing a basement space and transforming it into The Mockingbird Bar, which they&apos;re billing as a &quot;tequila speakeasy.&quot; In the dimly-lit spot they&apos;ll be shaking up Mexican Martinis and Mezcal Mules and hosting monthly events including a gay country night called &quot;Whose Boots?&quot; that&apos;ll be hosted every third Thursday. </p>

<p><em>25 Avenue B, 646-882-0593; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171031153334/http://avenidacantina.com/">avenidacantina.com</a></em></p>
