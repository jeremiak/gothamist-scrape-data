					<p>The 12th annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://www.vendyawards.streetvendor.org/newyorkcity/">Vendy Awards</a> unfolded on Governor&apos;s Island once again on a spectacularly beautiful Saturday afternoon this past weekend. The fete, which crowns winners in a variety of categories related to mobile food vending, is both a competition and a fundraiser for the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://streetvendor.org/">Street Vendor Project</a>, which supports all types of vendors operating on city streets and sidewalks. As has become routine, the bees also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/https://twitter.com/nellcasey/status/777195170240409600">enjoyed the day&apos;s bounty</a>.</p>

<p>Winners were crowned in seven different categories this year, including a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/28/vendy_awards_2016.php">brand new</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/06/vendys_vegan_contenders.php#photo-1">vegan category</a>, awards for the People&apos;s Choice and the coveted Vendy Cup.</p>

<p>This year, the trophy went to Tacos el Rancho, a Mexican street cart serving home-style Oaxacan food out of Sunset Park. Felix Soriano served tacos and an excellent vegetarian cheese quesadilla, which won over the day&apos;s judges. People&apos;s Choice went to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/https://www.yelp.com/biz/cachapas-on-wheels-new-york">Cachapas On Wheels</a> for their Venezuela fare, particularly the yoyo sandwiches that use mashed plantains in lieu of bread.</p>

<p>There were two winners in the vegan category. The People&apos;s Choice went to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://www.monksmeats.com/">Monk&apos;s Meats</a>, purveyors of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/09/vegan_bbq_nyc.php">vegan barbecue</a> who scored big with their trio of sliders. The judges, however, were moved by the vegan Indian food out of Yuvaraaj Thakkar&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/https://www.facebook.com/MysttikMasaala/">Mysttik Masaala</a> truck. It was indeed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/https://twitter.com/nellcasey/status/777216462637428736">quite awesome</a>. Even though they weren&apos;t required to do so, many of the competitors in the non-vegan category were also offering their own meat-free options to award visitors.</p>

<p>Rookie of the Year went to pop-up operation <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://www.mr-bing.com/">Mr. Bing</a>, serving savory Chinese crepes in both meat and vegetarian varieties. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://www.bigmozz.com/">Big Mozz&apos;s</a> mozzarella sticks were good enough to earn them the Best Market Vendor distinction, while the ice cream sandwiches from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161209125159/http://thegoodbatch.com/">The Good Batch</a> won in the Best Dessert Vendor category.</p>
