<p>Puerto Ricans celebrate the holiday season with a chilled drink called Coquito, a rich cocktail made with egg yolk, coconut milk and sweetened condensed milk. This evening, get to know the drink a little better at the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032727/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/international-coquito-masters-nyc-finals-tickets-19909245088">International Coquito Masters NYC Finals</a></strong>, where one NYC area Coquito Master will be selected to represent the region at the Northeast Championship Competition. From 5:30 p.m. to 8 p.m. tonight, join Brooklyn Beep Eric Adams in selecting the winner from this year&apos;s crop at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032727/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/international-coquito-masters-nyc-finals-tickets-19909245088">free event</a> at Brooklyn Borough Hall. </p>

<p>Williamsburg&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032727/http://www.nydistilling.com/">NY Distilling Co</a> is making a push for the Three W&apos;s: <strong>whiskey, waffles and workshops</strong> with a hands-on drinking and DIY coaster class this Saturday from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m. Roll up for sips of their bronze booze, a nosh of some syrupy waffles and learn how to make two different types of coasters, one from cork and another from rope. As we&apos;re getting down to the wire for thoughtless Christmas gifts, they&apos;ll have a wrapping station on-hand to make it even easier to gift your creations to Great Aunt Sally. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032727/https://whiskeywafflesandworkshops.splashthat.com/">Tickets</a> are $27 and include one cocktail, waffles pus toppings and the two fruits of your coaster-making labor.</p>

<p>Nothing says the weekend like <strong>Large Format Cinnamon Bun</strong>, so trek to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032727/http://www.qualityeats.com/">Quality Eats</a> on Greenwich Avenue for the artisanal Cinnabon experience plus other new brunch dishes the restaurant is debuting this week. Also on the sweet side, French Toast ($16) with spiced fruit and Gjetost, a sweet Norwegian cheese. Savory options span poach, fried, hard boiled and other egg options plus a Patty Club Melt ($17) and a Michelada for Two featuring housemade spice mix and an extra large bottle of Corona.</p>
