					<p>The slice joint is a time-honored New York City tradition that&apos;s often been swept aside by the rush of Neapolitan pies, which may soon be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170227205504/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/20/emmy_squared_detroit_pizza_heaven.php">ending their reign</a> as the city&apos;s preeminent pizza style. Unless they&apos;re doing something <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170227205504/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/29/russian_nesting_doll_pizza.php">truly outrageous</a>, neighborhood pizzerias get most of their foot traffic from locals or passersby looking for a quick carb fix. But <a href="www.bakerspizzanyc.com">Baker&apos;s Pizza</a>, which opened on Avenue A a few months back, is betting on the power of the New York-style pie to become a destination stop on any pizza journeyman&apos;s map.</p>

<p>The bakers here are twofold. There&apos;s the eponymous owner Jordan Baker, who&apos;s joined by pizza chef Jamie Cacace to turn out the hearty pies, focaccia-based sandwiches and other pizzeria staples. Your average slice joint doesn&apos;t offer toppings like brussels sprouts, bacon and white truffle oil (the B&amp;B;) or speck, broccoli rabe, roasted red peppers and parmesan (the Speck-tacular), ingredients that set Baker&apos;s apart from Muzzarella Pizza down the block, a perfectly respectable and delicious slice joint in its own right.</p>

<p>Unlike their dainty Neapolitan cousins, the pies at Baker&apos;s are enormous and generously weighted down with their toppings. Their 18&quot; inch speciality creations are $20 per pie and can easily feed four people with big appetites. Like any classic &quot;corner&quot; pizzeria&#x2014;Baker&apos;s is between 12th and 13th&#x2014;they&apos;ll have pies ready to reheat for slices, with speciality slices going for $4 each and plain cheese slices for $2.75. </p>

<p>They&apos;re also making their own homemade focaccia to use on their two sandwich offerings, a Meatball Parm with fresh mozzarella, mint and lemon zest and a Speck &amp; Broccoli Rabe with herbed ricotta spread, both $8. Pesto knots, their twist on the garlic knot, run $2.50 for four and their zeppolis, drenched in sugar and drizzles of Nutella, are $3 for four.</p>

<p>Since the space is large enough to accommodate some hightops and other tables, Baker installed a white, upright piano, which he encourages guests to play&#x2014;regardless of their skill level. For what&apos;s traditionally a grab-and-go kind of model, Baker&apos;s encourages lingering.</p>
