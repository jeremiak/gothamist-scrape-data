					<p>Restaurateur Roni Mazumdar&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615185424/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/27/tapestry_indian_west_village.php#photo-1">short-lived</a> eatery Tapestry has been reborn as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615185424/https://www.rahinyc.com/">Rahi</a>, an artisanal Indian restaurant with a brand-new chef and menu. Chef Chintan Pandya flipped the script on the now-common &quot;snacks, apps, mains&quot; menu designations, instead interpreting each dish&apos;s category by the length of time it takes to eat it. Thus we have &quot;New York Minute,&quot; &quot;At Ease,&quot; and &quot;Leisurely.&quot; </p>

<p>Those pressed for time can opt for Chilli Cheese Toast or Bhuna Bhutta, a grilled corn on the cob dish topped with turmeric, chaat masala and lime. Not-a-Saag-Roti&#x2014;a cornbread finger sandwich stuffed with mustard greens&#x2014;Chicken Kofta and Kasundi Cod are for those with a little bit more time. And finally, larger dishes like Butter Pepper Lobster and a Dalhouise Shepherds Pie with ground lamb for longer meals.</p>

<p>Booze beverages include a rum-infused Karrif Mango Lassi and a Sandalwood Old Fashioned, which is apparently served on fire. </p>

<p><em>60 Greenwich Avenue, 212-373-8900; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170615185424/https://www.rahinyc.com/">rahinyc.com</a></em></p>
