<p>Tex-Pats and Longhorn Lovers (and BBQ lovers) can kick up their cowboy boots Saturday at the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://texasexesnewyork.org/catalog/3rd-annual-holiday-hoedown">Texas Holiday Hoedown</a></strong>, a party and fundraiser for scholarships at the University of Texas. Thrown by the alumni group <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://texasexesnewyork.org/">Texas Exes</a>, the Hoedown gets down at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://www.brotherjimmys.com/_unionsquare">Brother Jimmy&apos;s Union Square</a> with a three hour open bar of liquor, wine and beer; passed barbecue hors d&apos;oeuvres; and live music that encourages some two steppin&apos;. Tickets are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://texasexesnewyork.org/catalog/3rd-annual-holiday-hoedown">$60 for members, $65 for non</a>. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="113016jasper.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/113016jasper.jpg" width="640" height="455"> <br> <i> Harbison from Jasper Hill Farm (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/https://www.facebook.com/CellarsAtJasperHill/photos/a.85906307662.81964.80962387662/10154371573927663/?type=3&amp;theater">Facebook</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Wheel upon wheel of delicious cheese roll into Flushing Saturday for the 2nd annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/https://www.facebook.com/events/167426303698772/"><strong>Great Northeast Cheese Fest</strong></a>, an evening of dairy-induced food coma courtesy of local chefs and some dope fromage talent from NY and the Northeast. Area chefs&apos;ll be cooking up their best cheese-centric dishes and cheese makers and creameries are bringing samples of their goodies (over 75 different cheese will be on offer). Samples of cheese and chef&apos;s creations are unlimited with a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/the-great-northeast-cheese-dairy-fest-tickets-27644816390?aff=erelexpmlt">$60 ticket</a>, as are beer, wine and ciders for pairing.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="120916speedrack.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/120916speedrack.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://claywilliams.photoshelter.com/">Clay Williams</a>/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Strong drinks, stiff competition, and badass women&#x2014;is there anything not to like about <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://www.speed-rack.com/">Speed Rack</a>? It&apos;s the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/15/back_the_rack.php#photo-1">fifth season</a> of the bartender mix off slash fundraiser for breast cancer charities and it&apos;s going down this Sunday at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://www.diamondhorseshoe.com/">Diamond Horseshoe</a> on West 46th Street. Contestants compete in timed heats, shaking or stirring up classic cocktails for judgement on speed and accuracy. You can watch the women in action with a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/speed-rack-season-6-new-york-regional-tickets-29622121563">$25 ticket</a>, which gets you a view of the competition in addition to punches made by liquor sponsors and something to eat as well.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://www.insabrooklyn.com/">Insa</a>, the Korean BBQ and karaoke joint that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161214093629/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/09/insa_korean_bbq_karaoke.php">opened in Gowanus</a> last December, is now offering a Korean take on brunch from noon to 4 p.m. on Saturdays and Sundays. Nosh on dishes including a restaurant take on &quot;Shin&quot; Ramen (a popular type of instant noodles), a Breakfast Hash with soy braised beef and a fried egg, and a Mochi Waffle (pictured above) with pear compote and maple syrup. They&apos;re also making a Bloody Mary with kimchi brine, which should aid in digesting all that food.</p>
