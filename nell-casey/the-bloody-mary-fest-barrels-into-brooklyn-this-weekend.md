<p>Beginning this weekend: two new brunches! First up, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://www.pigbleeckernyc.com/">Pig Bleecker</a> in the Village is dropping dishes like Buttermilk Waffles with Smoked &amp; Fried Chicken Thigh with honey butter, Linguini Cacio e Pepe with a slow-cooked egg, and Pig Bleecker Hash with fried eggs. Over in Park Slope, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://www.gristmillbk.com/">Gristmill&apos;s</a> got it going on with The Loxness Knotster, an everything garlic knot with smoked salmon, cream cheese, ricotta and dill; and wood-fired French Toast made with warthog bread, maple, bourbon and blueberry agrodolce. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="040517blacktap.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/040517blacktap.jpg" width="640" height="575"> <br> <i> Black Tap (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/https://www.facebook.com/blacktapcraftburgers/photos/a.978269038857702.1073741832.822547591096515/1381993208485281/?type=3&amp;theater">Facebook</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/https://blacktapnyc.com/">Black Tap</a> and its ludicrous milkshakes are staging a <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://humboldtandjackson.com/hj-event/kitchen-takeover-by-mystery-guest/">kitchen takeover</a></strong> at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://humboldtandjackson.com/">Humbolt &amp; Jackson</a> in Williamsburg on Saturday. From 2 p.m. until 11 p.m. (or until the blender gives out), head to Humbolt Street for three burgers (including one vegan option) and three of their signature milkshakes ($15) in Cookie Shake, Brooklyn Blackout Shake and Cookies n&#x2019; Cream Supreme flavors. Just...<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/27/black_tap_milkshake_insanity.php">be careful</a>!</p>

<p>Uncover Brooklyn&apos;s sudsy roots at the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://www.green-wood.com/event/brooklyns-brewing-history-trolley-tour/">Brooklyn&#x2019;s Brewing History Trolley Tour</a></strong> beginning at 2 p.m. at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://www.green-wood.com/">Green-Wood</a> (yes, the cemetery). On the trolley tour, beer historian Chris Heuberger and Green-Wood historian Jeff Richman will point out &quot;beer-related points of interest,&quot; which includes stops with beer connected industries including baseball and immigration. Say hi to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://gothamist.com/tags/feltmans">Charles Feltman</a> while you&apos;re there. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://www.green-wood.com/event/brooklyns-brewing-history-trolley-tour/">Tickets $30</a>. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="040517bloody.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/040517bloody.jpg" width="640" height="426"> <br> <i> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://1gsphotos.com/">Francine Dominguez</a> / Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Pre-plan your Sunday hangover, then heal yourself at the 4th annual <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://www.thebloodymaryfest.com/">Bloody Mary Fest</a></strong> happening at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/https://grandprospect.com/">Grand Prospect Hall</a>. If you <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/http://gothamist.com/2015/04/13/brooklyn_bloody_mary_festival.php#photo-22">have the stomach for it</a>, you&apos;ll be able to sample bloodies from 11 different restaurants and brands include Korean spot Insa and Congress Bar. There&apos;ll be some food snacks on hand but it&apos;s a safe bet that you should eat something before committing to three hours of brunchy booze. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170415162742/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/the-bloody-mary-fest-brooklyn-2017-tickets-31220601660">Tickets $50 - $84</a>.</p>
