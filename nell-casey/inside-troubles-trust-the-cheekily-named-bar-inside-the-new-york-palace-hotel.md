</div></div>					<p>When hotelier and notorious &quot;Queen of Mean&quot; Leona Helmsley <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150708192048/http://gothamist.com/2007/08/20/hotelier_real_e.php">passed away in 2007</a> she left her pampered pooch Trouble a hefty trust to the tune of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150708192048/http://gothamist.com/2007/08/29/leona_helmsley.php">$12 million</a>. Trouble <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150708192048/http://gothamist.com/2011/06/09/trouble_helmsley_millionaire_heires.php">went to the gilded dog house in the sky</a> a few years ago but her legacy as the richest bitch in New York City lives on with a new bar at Helmsley&apos;s former hotel The New York Palace. </p>

<p>Trouble&apos;s Trust opened underneath the hotel lobby&apos;s grand staircase last Friday offering a swanky spot to sip an NYC-themed cocktail or two. Catch the mixology action at the liquid metal bar, where bartenders craft libations like The Pampered Pooch ($19), made with Owney&apos;s rum, B&amp;B;, Cocchi Bianco, lime juice, simple syrup and creole bitters or the &quot;Queen of Mean&quot; ($19) with Breuckelen NY Wheat Whiskey, Ginger Bee NY, Red Jacket NY Apple Juice, Averell &quot;Damson Gin&quot; NY, elderflower syrup, fresh lemon and an apple chip garnish. The bar even commissions its own signature vermouth, &quot;Uncouth Vermouth,&quot; which it employs in the Spanish Harlem Rose cocktail ($19) with tequila, lime and ginger.</p>

<p>The hotel recently completed a $140 million renovation, some of which went into the creation of the luxe bar space. The striking floor-to-ceiling wine display glows seductively and the low-ceiling and muted color palate add to the intimacy of the space. On our visit, dignitaries from the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150708192048/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/23/midtown_traffic_headaches_ahead_wit.php">visiting members</a> of the United Nations General Assembly packed out the green olive-hued leather banquettes for a few bottles of Veuve Clicquot...at 11:30 a.m.</p>
