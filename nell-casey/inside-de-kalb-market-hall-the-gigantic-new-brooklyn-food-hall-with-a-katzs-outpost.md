					<p>The Dekalb Market has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616203753/http://gothamist.com/2012/06/21/dekalb_market_has_to_move.php">come a long way</a> from its origins as a ragtag collection of food vendors located inside shipping containers. On Friday, the market was reborn (if in name only) as The Dekalb Market Hall, a gigantic new food hall inside the equally gigantic City Point development in Downtown Brooklyn. The new market joins such luminaries as Alamo Drafthouse, Century 21, and Target.</p>

<p>Prior to opening, some big name vendors had already been announced, including the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616203753/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/13/katzs_deli_is_coming_to_brooklyn.php">first ever expansion of Katz&apos;s Deli</a>, which has made a new Brooklyn home for itself at the Hall. Hard Times Sundaes&#x2014;which recently jumped on the food hall bandwagon at Urbanspace Vanderbilt&#x2014;will operate two eateries here: a burger joint similar to what Andrew Zurica&apos;s been doing in his truck and a new bagel operation with bread rings from Mill Basin Bagel Cafe. Arepa Lady will be there, too.</p>

<p>Check out the full vendor list below, which includes over 40 different food vendors including ready-to-eat foodstuffs alongside a butcher, cheese shop, bakery and fishmonger. </p>

<p><em>DeKalb Market is located inside City Point at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616203753/http://dekalbmarkethall.com/#location">445 Albee Square West</a> in Downtown Brooklyn; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616203753/http://dekalbmarkethall.com/">dekalbmarkethall</a></em></p>
