					<p>Eggs, eggs and the provenance of eggs (i.e.: chicken) arrive in Williamsburg courtesy of the second location of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170609215846/https://www.eggshopnyc.com/">Egg Shop</a>, Sarah Schneider and Demetri Makoulis&apos;s love letter to all things huevos. Typically a breakfast food&#x2014;or breakfast for dinner food&#x2014;the duo, along with chef Nick Korbee, have found a way to weave eggs into just about everything, from burgers to burrata to dessert and even french fries. And now: tacos and burritos.</p>

<p>For their new location, the team has debuted a &quot;Going Back To Cali&quot; section of their brunch menu that includes egg filled Sooo-Cal burritos ($12) with carne asada, scrambled eggs, cheddar, avocado french fries and salsa; Charro burritos ($10) with egg whites, avocado, charred broccoli, roasted bell peppers, feta cheese and harissa; and tacos stuffed with things like crispy potatoes, scrambled eggs, jalapeno aioli and cotija cheese (Papas Bravas, $10) and roasted mushrooms, scrambled eggs, cream ranchera and pickled onions (Shroomin&apos;, $10).</p>

<p>Besides the new brunch additions, menus at both locations will be the same for brunch, lunch and dinner. Look for staples like their Eggshop B.E.C. ($10), Fried Chicken ($7), and Eggs Benedict ($12). </p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170609215846/http://gothamist.com/2014/07/30/cherry_izakaya.php#photo-1">former Cherry Izakaya space</a> has been transformed and feels like it might if you were inside of an egg: lots of white. Look for the adorable table shaped like an egg, complete with a little yolk in the center. </p>

<p><em>138 North 8th Street, (646) 787-7502; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170609215846/https://www.eggshopnyc.com/">eggshopnyc.com</a></em></p>
