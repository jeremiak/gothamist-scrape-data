<p>Theatergoers in search of a pre-curtain bite at this summer&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172419/http://www.shakespeareinthepark.org/">Shakespeare in the Park</a> won&apos;t have to travel far. A low-key snack bar, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172419/http://unionsquareevents.com/eateries/public_fare">Public Fare</a>, opened up once again inside the Delacorte Theater, serving picnic favorites like hot dogs, sandwiches and frozen treats. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="061313publicfare4.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172419im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/061313publicfare4.jpg" width="250" height="317" class="image-right"> </span>The cafe opens daily at 8 a.m., serving Central Park visitors a selection of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172419/http://www.shakespeareinthepark.org/#!foodanddrink/coav">casual fare</a> from Danny Meyer&apos;s Union Square Events&#x2019; culinary team. Of note are the Picnic Dog ($5.50) with crushed barbecue potato chips, scallions and mustard sauce; a Ridgecrest Smoked Turkey Sandwich ($9) with gouda, roasted tomato and spicy bbq mayo on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172419/http://gothamist.com/2012/07/23/spanish_harlem_non-profit_bakery_op.php#photo-1">Hot Bread Kitchen&apos;s</a> ciabatta served with chips; and Roasted Cauliflower ($4.75, $6.75 with field greens) with tumeric, golden raisins, capers and toasted almonds. </p>

<p>Smaller snacks and desserts include a selection of Ciao Bella gelatos and sorbets ($4), plus a new addition: Ice Push-Up Pops ($2) in Lemon Lime, Yogurt N&apos; Berries and Watermelon N&apos; Cracked Pepper flavors. Wine ($8-9), beer ($6-7) and non-alcoholic beverages are also available and a 4 p.m. to 6 p.m. Happy Hour on Fridays means $3 draft beers and $5 wines by the glass. </p>

<p>Also new this year are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172419/http://unionsquareevents.com/images/uploads/Pre-Order_One_Sheet_5.16.13.pdf">pre-order picnic packages</a> like the Picnic in the Park (sandwich, cookie, snack and a beer/wine for $21.50) and Sweet Treat Chilled (gelato, sorbet or push pop and wine/beer for $10). </p>

<p>The stall is open Wednesdays through Sundays until 5 p.m. with an extra hour until 6 p.m. on Fridays. On performance nights, the cafe stays open until 8:45 p.m., so you can throw back a few bottles of Brooklyn Lager before taking in some high art. The Public Theater&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172419/http://www.shakespeareinthepark.org/">Shakespeare in the Park</a> season began at the end of May with <a href="&lt;br /&gt;
http://gothamist.com/2013/05/31/gothamist_summer_guide_20_fun_thing.php#photo-2"><em>The Comedy of Errors</em></a>, which runs through June 30th, followed by a musical adaptation of the Bard&apos;s <em>Love&apos;s Labour&apos;s Lost</em>, running July 23rd through August 18th.  </p>
