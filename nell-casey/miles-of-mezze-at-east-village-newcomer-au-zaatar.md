</div></div>					<p>A new &quot;Arabian French&quot; bistro called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429101324/http://www.auzaatar.com/">Au Za&apos;atar</a> opened on Avenue A in the East Village last night. The restaurant gets its name from the seasoning mix known as za&apos;atar, essentially the everything bagel topping of the Middle East. It&apos;s made from spices like thyme and oregano and seasonings like sesame seeds and salt; it&apos;s spread liberally on fluffy pieces of pita or as a seasoning for meats and veggies. You&apos;ll find plenty of it on the menu here, in addition to classic French flavors and dishes at the new fusion eatery.</p>

<p>Lebanon-born chef Salwa Fallous brings her family recipes into play in the kitchen, creating a melting pot of flavors from both North Africa, the Eastern Mediterranean and parts of southern Europe. Tunisian couscous ($12 - 33) shares table space with moules frites ($16), as escargot with garlic ($12) does with grilled kafta kebabs ($12). In addition to robust grilled meat and fish dishes, the restaurant offers a wide variety of <em>mezze</em>, or small plates meant for sharing. Vegetarians are especially well-cared for in this department, with almost 20 different dishes to choose from.</p>

<p>Standards like hummus ($6), baba ghanoush ($7), falafel ($6) and vegetarian moussaka ($9) can all be procured, along with fig crostini with creme fraiche ($7), Phoenician fries topped with sumac, parsley and a garlic thyme aioli ($5) and oven roasted beets with goat cheese ($7). Those open to meat and fish should try the basturma ($9), a type of spiced, sun-dried beef; makanek ($8), a kind of Lebanese sausage; and shrimp ($12) sauteed in garlic, olive oil and white wine. </p>

<p>The handsome, mostly-wood interior comes from recycled pallets that were transformed into rustic wine and beer shelves and dining room dividers. The romantically dimmed dining room has cozy nooks for small parties, scattered high tops for twosomes and a small five-seat bar. The wine list features 16 natural, organic wines from the Mediterranean plus a reasonably varied beer list and sangria. </p>

<p><em>188 Avenue A, 212-254-5660; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429101324/http://www.auzaatar.com/">auzaatar.com</a></em></p>
