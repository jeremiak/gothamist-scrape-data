<p>Philadelphians get their fried dough fix at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://www.federaldonuts.com/">Federal Donuts</a> and now New Yorkers can get in on the action without hopping a sketchy bus. The donut crew will be at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://laboiteny.com/">La Boite</a> this afternoon for <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/https://twitter.com/FederalDonuts/status/434051442685775873">Donuts, Spices, Hot Chocolate and Champagne</a></strong>, an extra sweet fundraising event for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://www.candlerockland.org/commonthreads">Common Threads</a>. From 3 p.m. to 7 p.m. today, swing by the shop for Federal&apos;s Strawberry-Covered Chocolate donuts make with chocolate cake batter. </p>

<p>If you <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/07/the_stragglers_guide_to_valentines.php">don&apos;t yet have plans</a> for tonight&apos;s forced love fest, head over to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://momaps1.org/">MoMA PS1</a> in Queens where you&apos;ll be treated to dinner and a movie courtesy of the fun-loving food folks of M. Wells. The <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://momaps1.org/calendar/view/490/">Valentine&apos;s Dome Double Feature</a></strong> evening begins at 6 p.m. with a screening of the hysterical <em>Some Like It Hot</em> with Tony Curtis, Jack Lemon and Marylin Monroe. At 8 p.m., it&apos;s Michel Gondry&apos;s epic <em>Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind</em>. Before or after, grab creative Montreal fare like Coquilles Saint-Jacques, Venison Wellington and Cassoulet at M. Wells, where you&apos;ll get dinner and a maple whiskey for $45. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-right"> <img alt="021314scotchegg.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/021314scotchegg.jpg" width="250" height="167"> <br> <i style=" width:250px; ;display:block"> <span class="photo_caption">Breslin (via <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://www.yelp.com/biz_photos/the-breslin-bar-and-dining-room-new-york?select=7u3jgCo8DMKuOiV03qJbbA#bnkHeZb4tQItciCWsnllZA">Yelp</a>)</span></i></div> On Saturday and Sunday, The Breslin Bar plays host to a big fundraiser for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/https://www.glwd.org/">God&apos;s Love We Deliver</a>, a meal delivery service for sick people who are unable to cook for themselves. The <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://www.crowdrise.com/biglovewknd">Big Love Weekend</a></strong> includes a day of spirituality on Saturday, like yoga, inspirational talks, a Puja ceremony and snacks from The Breslin. On Sunday, it&apos;s the organization&apos;s birthday bash, which means food and drink from The Breslin chef April Bloomfield, live music and then a big dance party from 10:30 p.m. to 3 a.m. There are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/https://www.crowdrise.com/registration/event_options/3454">three ways to participate</a>, including a two day ticket for $216 or a dance party only option for $50.<p></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410024718/http://www.sonsofessexnyc.com/">Sons of Essex</a> on the Lower East Side has decided to extend Sunday Funday into the evening hours with their new <strong>Sunday Suppers</strong>. It&apos;s a good deal for folks on a budget: four courses for $29 per person. Start with cheesy garlic bread and Caesar salad, then choose one of their pastas like papardelle with short rib bolognese or cavatelli with mushrooms and parmesan. Each two people receive a gratis bottle of wine, too, plus some Italian cookies for a bit of sweet post meal. </p>
