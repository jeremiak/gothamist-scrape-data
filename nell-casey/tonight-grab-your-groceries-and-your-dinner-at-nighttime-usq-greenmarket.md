<p>Finally, a chance for desk jockeys to experience the greenmarket without waking when the rooster crows! Tonight <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130821203010/http://www.grownyc.org/nightmarkets">A Midsummer&apos;s Night at Greenmarket</a> takes over Union Square, bringing farmers, restaurants and some entertainment to the park from 4 p.m. until 8 p.m. This first-ever nighttime greenmarket in the space marks the 37th year of operation for the USQ Greenmarket.</p>

<p>Stock up on fresh, local produce, meats, cheeses and baked goods from some of the market&apos;s regular providers. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130821203010/http://www.grownyc.org/nightmarkets">Area restaurants</a> will also be on hand selling individual dishes, desserts and beverages, including <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130821203010/http://backfortynyc.com/">Back Forty/Back Forty West</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130821203010/http://rougetomatenyc.com/">Rouge Tomate</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130821203010/http://www.telepan-ny.com/welcome-to-telepan">Telepan</a>. A special Brooklyn Brewery pavilion pours the local <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130821203010/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/19/taste_new_brooklyn_brewery_beer_mad.php">Greenmarket Wheat</a> while a few local bands supply the soundtrack. Hopefully the temperature will <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130821203010/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/15/hottest_week_of_the_year_1.php">dip below 90</a> by quitting time. </p>
