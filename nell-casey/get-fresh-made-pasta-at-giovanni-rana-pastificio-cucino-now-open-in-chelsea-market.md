</div></div>					<p>Last week, venerable Italian pasta maker <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151113180622/http://www.giovannirana.com/">Giovanni Rana</a> opened its first full-service United States restaurant, cranking out a variety of noodle creations in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151113180622/http://chelseamarket.com/visitors/">Chelsea Market</a>. Depending on which entrance you use, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151113180622/http://www.rananyc.com/home.html">Giovanni Rana Pastificio &amp; Cucino</a> is a retail shop/pasta market or a full-service restaurant offering dishes to enjoy in-house or on the road. There&apos;s also an open kitchen and pasta-making station where visitors can watch as their ravioli are stuffed to bursting with meats, cheeses and other savory fillings.</p>

<p>If you&apos;re in the Market browsing for cooking supplies, Rana sells their fresh-made pastas to take home, including some unusual flavors like lentil spaghetti and curry pappardelle. There&apos;s also a small shop&#x2014;<em>A Casa Mia</em>&#x2014;where you can procure gourmet Italian staples like cheeses, olive oils, chocolate and more. If you want some ready-made grub on the go, choose from nine different pasta options, from a classic Gnocchetti alla Bolognese to a Beet Tagliatelle with Winter Vegetables. There&apos;s also a small selection of soups and salads, like the intriguing Chilled Pasta Carbonara. </p>

<p>Enter from Ninth Avenue for the restaurant section, which is designed not unlike a large, Italian country kitchen. Copper pans and kitchen accessories on steel racks dangle over marble-topped tables decorated with fresh flowers and rustic table settings. The menu leans heavily on Rana&apos;s vast selection of fresh pastas in combinations like Ravioli al Brasato, Chesnuts, and Savoy Cabbage ($19) and a Chestnut Tagliatelle tossed with Guinea Fowl Ragu ($18). A selective menu of <em>Secondi</em>, meat and fish dishes, are on offer as well, including a Bistecca dalla Costata alla Fiorentina ($76), a 32 ounce Creekstone Dry-Aged Prime Beef that serves two. </p>

<p><em>75 Ninth Avenue // 212-370-0975</em></p>
