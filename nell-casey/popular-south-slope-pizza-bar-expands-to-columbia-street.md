					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171017145343/https://www.facebook.com/TobysPublicHouse/">Toby&apos;s Public House</a>, a popular sports bar and pizza spot in South Slope, has expanded its footprint into the Columbia Street Waterfront District, opening up a second location in May for serving its wood-fired pies.</p>

<p>Toby&apos;s opened its first location during the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171017145343/http://nymag.com/restaurants/reviews/underground/47541/">Brooklyn pizza boom</a> of 2008, joining luminaries <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171017145343/http://gothamist.com/2008/02/25/camera_in_the_k_97.php">like Roberta&apos;s</a> in defining a new genre of pizza. While Roberta&apos;s has gone on to be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171017145343/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/22/photos_bill_hillary_clinton_went_to.php#photo-1">something of a phenom</a>, places like Toby&apos;s cater to neighborhood regulars and pizza-philes who travel to sample their famed Bufalina pizza and others with unique toppings.</p>

<p>The Bufalina ($16) made it to the waterfront, of course, its thin crust topped with buffalo mozzarella DOP, basil, and a final douse of olive oil post-oven. Other inventive pies coming out of the dual, wood burning ovens include a Grilled Eggplant ($17) with smoked mozz, beef steak tomato, basil and olive oil, and the intriguing Grilled Peaches ($17) with honey ricotta, mozz, prosciutto di parma and arugula.</p>

<p>Pastas, salads and small plates like meatballs and mussels round out the menu. The bar offers a solid menu of craft (and formerly craft) beers including Blue Point, Coney Island, and Dog Fish Head, plus the cheekily-named &quot;Toby&apos;s Cheap,&quot; a $4 pint of &quot;American Lager.&quot;</p>

<p><em>215 Columbia Street, Columbia Street Waterfront District, 347-689-4209; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171017145343/https://www.facebook.com/TobysPublicHouse/">website</a></em></p>
