					<p>No doubt this week&apos;s unseasonably warm weather is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102003214/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/06/bryant_park_cold_plunge.php">wreaking havoc once again</a> on Bryant Park&apos;s seasonal skating rink, but if you&apos;re disappointed with a soupy skating experience, at least there&apos;s plenty of food to serve as a distraction with the reopening of the locale&apos;s pop-up market. The (deep breath) Holiday Shops at Bank of America Winter Village at Bryant Park reopened yesterday, with a dedicated food market called &quot;Feast on 40th&quot; on the park&apos;s south side. </p>

<p>Curated by market and street fair vets <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102003214/http://www.urbanspacenyc.com/">Urbanspace</a>, the market holds over 30 different food purveyors selling comfort food and replenishment. On night one, photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102003214/http://claywilliams.photoshelter.com/">Clay Williams</a> reports that Ramen Bar by Chingu was a popular draw, selling steaming bowls of noodle soups in Spicy Pork and Bulgogi flavors. But Williams was particularly enamored of Baked Cheese Haus, offering a raclette service where they&apos;ll melt the gooey cheese over things like cured ham or simply on a slice of ciabatta. </p>

<p>Other enticing options are Cretzels (croissant-pretzel hybrids) from Breads Bakery, chicken in a cone from Chick&apos;n Cone, plump pierogi and bowls of borscht from Veselka&apos;s Borscht Bar, inventive ringed pastry from Doughnut Project, plus Perros y Vainas, a Venezuelan hot dog stand that tops their dogs with things like corn and potato sticks, and Coney Shack, who are straight up lighting their dogs on fire. </p>

<p>Also new to the scene: a restaurant by Danny Meyer. The restaurateur introduced the Public Fare cafe on the north side of the bar, offering coffee during the day and alcohol in the evening. </p>
