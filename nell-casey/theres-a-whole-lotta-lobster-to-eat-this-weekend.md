<p>Tom Yum Lobster Congee and Spicy Fried Chicken Salad are on the menu this weekend at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/http://pintogarden.com/">Pinto Garden</a>, which is launching their new Thai brunch in the West Village. Even more important: $20 for 1.5 hours of unlimited Lychee Bellinis or Classic Mimosas. In Williamsburg, it&apos;s a vegan brunch at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/http://www.modernlovebrooklyn.com/">Modern Love</a>, with dishes including Seitan &amp; Waffles, Tofu Rancheros and Challah French Toast. </p>

<p>Learn about whiskey, then drink some whiskey at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/http://kingscountydistillery.com/">Kings County Distillery&apos;</a>s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/https://www.facebook.com/events/742053632618604/">The Spirited Pen</a> event, part of a series where writers talk spirits while touring the facility. This Sunday, New York Times editor Clay Risen is on the docket, talking about his article &quot;How America Learned to Love Whiskey&quot; beginning at 5 p.m. The tour also includes tastings of four whiskies. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/http://kingscountydistillery.com/events/">Tickets $14</a>.</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/nyc-food-truck-fest-tickets-32012574472">NYC Food Truck Fest</a> rolls into <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/https://www.grandbazaarnyc.org/">Grand Bazaar</a> on the Upper West Side on Sunday, bringing a bevy of mobile food vendors into one belly-busting space. On the docket: Luke&apos;s Lobster,  Souvlaki GR, Sweet Chili, Carl&#x2019;s Steaks and eight other local food trucks. All of the proceeds from the day will be donated to four local public schools.</p>

<p>Eataly&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/11/eataly_tuscan_takeover.php">fine dining restaurant</a> Manzo is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/https://www.eataly.com/us_en/stores/nyc-flatiron/nyc-manzo/">reimagining itself</a> as a &quot;butcher&apos;s restaurant,&quot; meaning they&apos;ll be closed &apos;til the end of the month. In the interim, Eataly is hosting a Sunday pop-up from noon til 7 p.m. with highlights from the previous Manzo menu and some peeks at some of the dishes from the forthcoming revamp. Make a reservation <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/https://www.opentable.com/manzo-reservations-new-york?rid=51274&amp;restref=51274">here</a> and check out the menu <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170315032031/https://www.eataly.com/wp/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/Manzo-Pop-Up-Menu_03a.pdf">here</a>.</p>
