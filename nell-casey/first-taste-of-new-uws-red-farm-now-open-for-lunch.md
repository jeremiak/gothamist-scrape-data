                            <p>It&apos;s a boon to dumpling lovers everywhere that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131011235238/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/09/chinese_food_expert_ed_schoenfeld_o.php#photo-1">Ed Schoenfeld</a> and Joe Ng have expanded on their wildly popular <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131011235238/http://redfarmnyc.com/">RedFarm</a> with a brand new outpost on the Upper West Side. The restaurant opened for lunch over the weekend, to the delight of neighborhood foodies who <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131011235238/https://twitter.com/jenchung/status/386888825861337089">came out in droves</a> to sample Ng&apos;s famous dim sum and other Chinese-inspired dishes. </p>

<p>Gothamist Executive Editor <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131011235238/https://twitter.com/jenchung">Jen Chung</a> enjoyed a meal at the new eatery on Sunday, snapping the above photos of some of the dishes. As you can see, Ng&apos;s playful and popular &quot;Pac Man&quot; Shrimp Dumplings ($12.50) made the trek uptown and the chef also adorns his adorable Crispy Duck &amp; Crab Dumplings ($14) with google-eyed characters. The chef&apos;s interpretation of Bacon &amp; Egg Fried Rice ($16)&#x2014;made with Nueske&apos;s Applewood Smoked meat&#x2014;and the RedFarm Chicken Salad ($16)&#x2014;served in a bowl fashioned out of crispy noodles&#x2014;are also both available on the new menu.</p>

<p>For now, RedFarm UWS serves lunch daily starting at 11 a.m. until 5 p.m. Schoenfeld Tweeted earlier this weekend, revealing that dinner would begin later this month:</p>
