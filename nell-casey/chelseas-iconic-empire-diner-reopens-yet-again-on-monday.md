					<p>Chelsea&apos;s iconic <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170424212533/http://empire-diner.com/">Empire Diner</a> gets another crack at life under new ownership, the fourth in the last decade alone. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170424212533/http://gothamist.com/2008/04/08/john_delucie_ch.php">John DeLucie</a>, along with the owners of Chelsea&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170424212533/http://cafeteriagroup.com/">Cafeteria</a>, reopen the diner Monday evening serving &quot;upscale renditions of classic diner favorites.&quot; Are there diners that serve Arctic Char Crudo and Celery Root Soup?</p>

<p>The Empire Double Patty Burger ($18) has more of a diner feel and comes with two patties, American cheese, house dill pickles, aioli and a side of herb fries. Other dishes include Beef Carpaccio with horseradish cream, shaved shallots and capers; Sweet Pea and Onion Toast with goat cheese and roasted garlic; and Braised Beef Short Rib with horseradish gremolata.</p>

<p>The actual Empire Diner <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170424212533/http://gothamist.com/2010/04/25/chelseas_empire_diner_set_to_close.php">closed in 2010</a>, sitting vacant until 2012, when a new restaurant called The Highliner <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170424212533/http://ny.eater.com/2011/5/10/6681743/after-delays-the-highliner-opens-in-empire-diner-space">opened in the space</a>. After that failed, Food Network personality and chef Amanda Freitag <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170424212533/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/07/chelseas_empire_diner_reopens_tonig.php">opened Empire 2.0</a> in 2014. Freitag left the business before that version <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170424212533/http://ny.eater.com/2015/12/14/10070660/nyc-empire-diner-closed">closed in 2015</a> after reportedly falling behind on the rent. </p>

<p><em>210 10th Avenue, (212) 335-2277; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170424212533/http://empire-diner.com/">empire-diner.com</a></em></p>
