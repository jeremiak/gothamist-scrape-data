<p>2016 continues to be the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324164543/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/28/poke_explainer_guide_nyc.php">year of poke</a>&#x2014;and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324164543/http://gothamist.com/tags/pokemongo">Pok&#xE9;mon</a>&#x2014;in NYC, with another fast casual eatery dedicated to the Hawaiian dish opening this Friday just south of Union Square. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324164543/http://www.pokespot.com/#thepokespot">Pok&#xE9;Spot</a> follows the same DIY-type of operation that similar spots have offered. Pick a base&#x2014;from traditional rice to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324164543/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/15/sushi_burrito_fine_whatever.php">trendy burrito</a> to &quot;zoodles&quot;&#x2014;and proteins including raw tuna and salmon and cooked snow crab and chicken. From there, flavor as you see fit with all kinds of mix-ins, toppings and sauces, which include a Crab Guacamole and Sriracha Aioli, among others.</p>

<p>They&apos;ll also offer some &quot;signature&quot; pok&#xE9; bowls and &quot;Avocado Boats,&quot; where the fruit acts like an edible bowl filled with a protein, two toppings and a sauce. Float them in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324164543/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/04/uhoh_spaghettios.php">your favorite bone both</a>!</p>

<p><em>120 4th Avenue, 917-348-0182; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324164543/http://www.pokespot.com/">pokespot.com</a></em> </p>
