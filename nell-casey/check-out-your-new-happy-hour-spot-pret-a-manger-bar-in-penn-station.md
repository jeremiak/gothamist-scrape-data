					<p>Are the crowds <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616044222/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/19/ode_to_applebees.php">too dense at Applebee&apos;s</a>? Not a fan of the &quot;martinis&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616044222/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/17/guys_american_kitchen_revisit.php#photo-1">found in Flavortown</a>? Relax. We&apos;ve got your hottest new happy hour spot right here: Pret A Manger.</p>

<p>It&apos;s true, New York City is home to the lunch staple&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616044222/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/18/alcohol_pret_a_manger.php">first location to serve alcohol</a>, with Pret Bar opening Friday in Penn Station. And this isn&apos;t some grab-and-go operation like most Prets. The third Penn Station Pret (lol) will have a full bar with seating for 12 in its 950-square-feet. The entire operation&#x2014;which includes all of the expected sandwiches and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616044222/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/30/woman_finds_frog_in_pret_a_manger_s.php">frog salads</a>&#x2014;is nearly double the usual size of USA Prets, clocking in at 5,968-square-feet. </p>

<p>The bar will offer seven beers on tap and another six in bottles. Prices range from $7 Bud and Bud Lights to $8 for pints of Blue Point Toasted Lager and bottles of Red Hook Longhammer IPA. So, more or less par for the course for Midtown.</p>

<p>The bar will also try on a bit of Brooklyn, offering Nitro Cold Brew ($5) and friggin Kombucha ($5) on tap. I like imagining commuters grabbing a quick kombu and a Falafel &amp; Pickles Hot Brioche (another Penn Station special) before jumping on the LIRR. &quot;Gotta get my probiotics, brah.&quot; </p>

<p>The location will also have a beer cart towards the front for those who can&apos;t ride the trains without a little <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170616044222/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/12/take_shot_when_lirr_loses_power.php">liquid assistance</a>. </p>

<p><em>Pret Bar is located near the 34th Street and 7th Avenue escalators near the LIRR Corridor and 1,2, 3 subway entrance</em></p>
