					<p>To open his first New York City restaurant, &quot;Next Iron Chef&quot; Jose Garces <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171011112852/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/12/iron_chef_garces_to_open_first_nyc.php">chose Brookfield Place</a>, bringing the second location of his Philadelphia success story <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171011112852/http://newyork.amadarestaurant.com/">Amada</a> to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171011112852/http://gothamist.com/2014/05/30/hudson_eats.php">new food</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171011112852/http://gothamist.com/2015/04/06/le_district_food_market.php#photo-1">destination</a>. Last night, the Andalusian (for the region in Spain), tapas-focused restaurant made its grand debut with a mix of tried-and-true dishes from the original and a handful of options singular to NYC. </p>

<p>Of the former, Amada&apos;s Empanada&#x2014;Amada means grandmother, a nod to Garces&apos;s influencer and teacher&#x2014;with manchego cheese artichoke escabeche and a very classic Tortilla Espa&#xF1;ola, an omelet-like dish made with potatoes and served with saffron aioli. For the latter, Picado, a lamb tartare with romesco verde and cheese and a Spanish flatbread with sausage, piquillo pepper confit and spinch.</p>

<p>The sprawling menu includes dozens of traditional tapas, cheeses, charcuterie and grilled fishes, vegetables and meats. There&apos;s an option for the chef (Garces and Chef de Cuisine Justin Bogle) to curate the selection for you, beginning at $70 with additional wine pairings beginning at $35. Beyond nibbles, the Especialidades de la Casa include a decadent Lobster Paella ($78) with fava bean salad and pimenton aioli and a Roasted Sucking Pig that has to be ordered in advance. </p>

<p>By day, the adjoining Amadita serves as a coffee shop and breakfast/lunch spot, but by night it transforms into a wine bar, serving only wines from Spain. Cocktails are also a nod to Spain with names taken from Pedro Almod&#xF3;var films. They&apos;re also offering a unique DIY G&amp;T; option, where the imbiber chooses the gin, the tonic and a garnish of their choice with selections like grapefruit/licorice and kumquat/cinnamon.</p>

<p><em>250 Vesey Street, 212-542-8947; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171011112852/http://newyork.amadarestaurant.com/">amadarestaurant.com</a></em></p>
