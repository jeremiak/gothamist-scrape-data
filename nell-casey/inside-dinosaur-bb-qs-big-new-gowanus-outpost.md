</div></div>					<p>If feels like we&apos;ve been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150601001913/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/20/all_sorts_of_new_bbq_happening_in_p.php">waiting forever</a> but <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150601001913/http://www.dinosaurbarbque.com/locations/brooklyn/Default.aspx?pnl=1">Dinosaur BBQ&apos;s</a> new Gowanus outpost finally opens to the public tomorrow at 5 p.m. The giant space retains some of its warehouse charms, with tons of exposed brick, weathered wood and high, raftered ceilings. It&apos;s a familiar aesthetic if you&apos;ve visited the chainlet&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150601001913/http://gothamist.com/2012/05/09/dino_bbq.php#photo-1">other locations</a> but with unique touches like a charming Calder-esque ceiling mobile made from old glass bottles.</p>

<p>Most of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150601001913/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/05/the_best_bbq_spots_in_new_york_city.php">well-loved menu items</a> appear on the Brooklyn menu, like huge, juicy chicken wings, moist brisket sandwiches, tender pork ribs and gut-busting sides. Brooklyn specific dishes include Breakfast All Day ($13.95), a nod to the nabe&apos;s brunching culture, which tops smoked brisket and poblano hash with a sunny side up egg. </p>

<p>Two special sandwiches are also on offer: Ode to Domolise ($10.95 with one side) with brisket, beef gravy, Swiss cheese, lettuce, tomato, pickled onion, jalape&#xF1;o relish, and BBQ mayo and the F.G.T.V ($10.95 with one side) with fried green tomatoes, pimento cheese, shredded cabbage, and chow-chow. Or sample the FGT solo ($7.95), topped with housemade pimento cheese and sweet &amp; sour green tomato chow-chow.</p>

<p>Beverage options are the same as other locations, with beers from Kelso, Sixpoint and Brooklyn Brewery plus other American brews and European imports.</p>
