<p>If you <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/31/eggslut_debut_nyc.php#photo-1">miss out on Eggslut</a>, maybe you&apos;ll have better luck at Wowfulls, which is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/28/wowfulls_lower_east_side.php">celebrating its new Houston Street cafe</a> with deals on their Hong Kong egg waffles all weekend. The first 50 customers on Friday receive $1 waffles, with a special $5 deal on waffles lasting the rest of the weekend. </p>

<p>It&apos;s also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/30/spaghetti_donuts_smorgasburg.php#photo-1">weekend one</a> for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/https://www.smorgasburg.com/">Smorgasburg</a> this weekend in Williamsburg and Prospect Park. There are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/30/spaghetti_donuts_smorgasburg.php#photo-1">26 new vendors</a> debuting this year, including the now infamous Spaghetti Donuts <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/http://www.poppasta.com/">from Pop Pasta</a> and tons of other ways to stuff your face if that line&apos;s too long.</p>

<p>A week-long celebration of Cabane a Sucre (Sugar Shack) concludes this Sunday at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/http://www.mileenddeli.com/brooklyn/">Mile End Hoyt Street</a> in Boerum Hill, where former chefs from Montreal&apos;s Au Pied de Cochon have created dishes specifically celebrating the harvest of sap to make delicious maple syrup. There are two prix fixe menus where the team promises to &quot;pummel&quot; guests with food. In the morning ($20), that includes dishes like Maple Butter Scramble, Duck Fat Pancakes, and Baked Beans; in the evening ($45), Maple Ham Grilled Cheese, Sweet Onion and Raclette Pomme Boulangere Potatoes, and Chomeur Babkah. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="033117hotchix.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/033117hotchix.jpg" width="640" height="388" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>Due to popular demand, #BlazingFeathers returns to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/http://vicsnewyork.com/">Vic&apos;s</a> on Great Jones Street with Hot Chicken (choice of heat level) with sides including pimento mac and cheese, black eyed peas, slaw and a dessert of banana-nilla wafer. Grab the fiery bird for $15 a plate, with sides and sweets an additional $5. Looking ahead, subsequent Sundays will see pop-ups at Vic&apos;s including &quot;old school New York delis, Parisian falafel stands, Jersey bakeries and more.&quot; First come, first served.</p>

<p>Bed-Stuy&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402235528/http://www.barlunatico.com/#welcome">Bar Lun&#xC0;tico</a> begins a <strong>Gospel Brunch</strong> on Sunday, which they&apos;ll continue through June. For the first iteration, gospel singer Gary Samuels and organist Greg Lewis are on the docket, performing two sets beginning at 2 p.m. There&apos;s a $10 suggested donation that goes to each week&apos;s artists. Their brunch menu offers a little something for everyone, from Vegan Ranchero ($15) with potato cakes to a Breakfast Sandwich ($10) on a croissant to a spicy Tuscan Kale Salad ($8) with roasted beets. </p>
