					<p>November saw the arrival of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170316174246/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/05/sugarfish_sushi_nyc.php#photo-5">cult LA sushi chain</a> Sugarfish in the Flatiron, which opened to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170316174246/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/beloved-la-sugarfish-makes-a-huge-splash-nyc-opens-four-hour-wait-947445">outrageously long lines</a> of diners hoping to sample chef Kazunori Nozawa&apos;s fastidious, uncompromising approach to the genre. Now the Sugarfish team has opened <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170316174246/http://www.kazunorisushi.com/">KazuNori</a>, another LA transplant, in NoMad, offering a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170316174246/http://www.kazunorisushi.com/home/menu2016/">straightforward menu</a> of hand rolls (temaki), sashimi and drinks.</p>

<p>Just like its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170316174246/http://laist.com/2016/04/27/kazunori_santa_monica.php">LA siblings</a>, the NYC restaurant features counter-only seating so chefs can hand creations off immediately to customers. Speciality nori and warmed rice are signatures of the restaurant, along with the &quot;hand-selected&quot; salmon, shrimp, blue crab and other fishy (and non) fillings.</p>

<p>Keeping in line with Nozawa&apos;s &quot;Trust Me&quot; omakase experiences, this new spot will offer four set menus of handrolls that vary the amount of food you&apos;re being served. A selection of beer, wine and tea rounds out the options.</p>

<p><em>15 West 28th Street; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170316174246/http://www.kazunorisushi.com/">kazunorisushi.com</a></em></p>
