<p>When it comes to easy access to delicious sushi and Japanese food, give me <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035902/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/24/sushi_roxx_midtown.php#photo-1">all the gimmicks</a>. That includes conveyor belt sushi, or kaiten, which is getting another trip around the city courtesy of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035902/http://www.yosushiusa.com/">YO! Sushi</a>, an international chain opening its 100th restaurant in NYC this November.</p>

<p>Fresh sushi, tempura, salads, drinks, desserts and other dishes totaling 90 different options weave their way through the seating areas, where diners pluck whatever they want to eat as it passes in front of them. Dishes are priced according to the color of the plate and, like an old school dim sum spot, a server tallies your bill by counting the plates at your table. </p>

The concept&apos;s been given a turn before in NYC at places like the now-defunct <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035902/http://gothamist.com/2008/02/08/savoring_singap.php">Sakae Sushi</a> and East Japanese Restaurant, which is <strike>still doing its thing in Kips Bay</strike> <strong>[Update below]</strong>. Even Whole Foods <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035902/http://gothamist.com/2007/03/28/whole_foods_bow.php">gave it a go</a>.
<br><br>
This year&apos;s seeing an influx of international food brands hawking Japanese cuisine making their way into the NYC market. Caviar udon specialists TsuruTonTan <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035902/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/25/tsurutontan_udon_usq.php#photo-1">debuted in Union Square</a> recently, and London-based Wagamama is set to touch down with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035902/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/25/wagamama_nyc_ramen.php">two</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035902/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/14/wagamama_round_two.php">restaurants</a>, one of which is expected to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170509035902/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/25/fall_restaurant_preview_2016.php">debut this fall</a>. 
<br><br>
<em>YO! Sushi opens this November on 23rd Street between 5th and 6th Avenues</em>

<p><strong>UPDATE</strong> A reader sends word that East Japanese Restaurant will be closing its doors on September 4th. </p>
