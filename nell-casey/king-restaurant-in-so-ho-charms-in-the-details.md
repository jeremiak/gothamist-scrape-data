					<p>In the modern restaurant, very few things come for free. There&apos;s almost never a complimentary, appetite-ruining (but still welcome) bread basket to start the meal. We <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170407073044/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-MWdSnxcots">know the guac is extra</a>. Sometimes, even the reservations <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170407073044/http://nypost.com/2015/01/17/5-apps-to-help-you-score-a-coveted-restaurant-reservation/">cost money</a>. That&apos;s why it was such a lovely change of pace to be offered a little something to nibble on without asking when dining at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170407073044/http://kingrestaurant.nyc/">King</a>, a new restaurant that opened in September in SoHo. </p>

<p>Meals begin with carta di musica, a yeast-free bread with a texture like a thinner matzo cracker that&apos;s baked to resemble a translucent piece of sheet music. The crisp bread is doused in olive oil and herbs, a quick and welcome glimpse into the savory courses ahead, and an exquisite and interesting centerpiece off the bat. </p>

<p>Co-chefs Clare de Boer and Jess Shadbolt&#x2014;along with GM Annie Shi&#x2014;met working in London restaurants, before decamping to NYC to open King. Dishes are generous without being onerous; enough to share with a group if you&apos;re ordering more than one. Seasonal&apos;s the rallying cry, so don&apos;t get overly attached to one dish&#x2014;though it&apos;s hard to not be charmed by pillowy malfatti, rustic dumplings currently bathed in a butter sauce topped with crispy fried sage and cheese. </p>

<p>Amari are a popular liqueur at the moment and make for a fitting nightcap after a meal. So does the chocolate tart, if it&apos;s on the menu. Light as a feather, a wisp of pastry and fluffy chocolate mousse, bordered on one side by a pool of rich, tangy creme fraiche, there are worse ways to indulge in a bit of sweet.</p>

<p><em>18 King Street, (917) 825-1618; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170407073044/http://kingrestaurant.nyc/">kingrestaurant.nyc</a></em></p>
