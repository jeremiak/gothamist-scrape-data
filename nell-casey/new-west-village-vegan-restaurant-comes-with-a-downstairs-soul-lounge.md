					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172816/http://blossomdujour.com/">Blossom du Jour</a>, the city&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172816/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/11/blossom_du_jour_expansion_1.php">largest vegan chain</a>, adds a new restaurant to their lineup of cruelty-free dining on Wednesday with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172816/http://www.urbanvegankitchen.com/">Urban Vegan Kitchen</a>, part restaurant, part lounge, all animal-friendly (well, leave your pets at home). Like the other locations, UVK seeks to make traditionally meat-centric dishes <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172816/http://gothamist.com/2014/09/25/blossom_du_jour.php#photo-1">into vegan-friendly fare</a>. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172816/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/21/ladybird_nyc.php#photo-1">Dainty vegan tapas</a> this is not.</p>

<p>Devotees of co-founder Pamela Elizabeth&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172816/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/23/best_veggie_burgers_nyc.php">hearty &quot;cheeseburger&quot;</a> can find solace with the UVK Quarter Pounder ($18), comprised of a mushroom/barley patty topped with fried shallots, tomatoes, pickles, lettuce and sauce with the choice to add on avocado or shiitake bacon. The Seitan &amp; Gravy ($22), Lasagna with sunflower tofu ricotta ($23) and Chick-un &amp; Waffles made with fried seitan are other insta-food coma options. They&apos;ll have lighter, veggie-forward fare too.</p>

<p>Downstairs from the sun drenched cafe upstairs is Chris&apos; Cathouse and Soul Lounge, the subterranean restaurant and bar that double as an event space. Ownership is promising music nights and education sessions but also a more low-key space to listen to soul music, drink cocktails and snack on their vegan delicacies. </p>

<p><em>43 Carmine Street, 646-438-9939; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021172816/http://www.urbanvegankitchen.com/">urbanvegankitchen.com</a></em></p>
