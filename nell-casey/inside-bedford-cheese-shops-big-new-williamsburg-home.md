					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017162325/http://bedfordcheeseshop.com/">Bedford Cheese Shop</a> has returned to the Avenue of its origin in a brand new (and much larger) space further south down Bedford Avenue in Williamsburg. The shop, which <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017162325/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/29/bedford_cheese_shop_moving.php">closed at the end of August</a> to move to the new location, now looks more like its Manhattan sibling, leaving behind its cramped home of 10 years <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017162325/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/25/photos_whole_foods_williamsburg.php#photo-1">across from the new Whole Foods</a>.</p>

<p>There&apos;s an expanded selection of specialty food products on offer, including <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017162325/https://www.itsiticecream.com/">It&apos;s It</a> ice cream sandwiches from San Franciso; frozen ravioli from Brooklyn-based <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017162325/http://www.pastosa.com/">Pastosa</a>; and lots of cheese adjacent products like bacon, eggs, chips, crackers, charcuterie, candy and pickled things. And the Australian feta now comes in buckets. Lord save us all.</p>

<p>The new location (which was formerly occupied by the overpriced Khim&apos;s Millenium Market) will also be offering tastings and classes, an expanded menu of prepared foods like sandwiches and salads, and the opportunity to collaborate with chefs to throw dinner parties.</p>

<p>And don&apos;t fret breeders: there&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017162325/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/29/bedford_cheese_shop_moving.php"><em>plenty</em> of room for strollers</a>.<br>
<em><br>
265 Bedford Avenue at North 1st Street, Brooklyn</em></p>
