					<p>&quot;There&apos;s a little bit of maple syrup everywhere,&quot; says Chef Aidan O&apos;Neal, whose new restaurant Chez Ma Tante <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170429014729/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/13/jimmys_calyer_aidan_oneal.php">opened in the former Jimmy&apos;s space</a> on Greenpoint&apos;s Calyer Street this week. </p>

<p>That a Vancouver native who spent five years in Montreal prior to moving to NYC would employ Canada&apos;s sweet liquid gold wherever possible is not surprising, but O&apos;Neal&apos;s employing a nuanced, measured hand. He and chef de cuisine Jake Leiber&#x2014;who met working at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170429014729/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/22/best_new_restaurants_2016.php">Cafe Altro Paradiso</a>&#x2014;lacquer salmon gravlax with gin and maple syrup while it&apos;s drying to build up its texture, combine it with chardonnay vinegar to finish a falafel dish, and there&apos;s a little bit of it on the rhubarb custard tart, too. </p>

<p>They&apos;re also mixing maple syrup with pureed prunes, anchovies, onion and Worcestershire sauce for their homemade ketchup, a concept they came up with to utilize onion scraps during the creation of other dishes. It&apos;s served with a wagyu short rib steak with salad and &quot;chips,&quot; thick-cut wedge fries as a nod to England.</p>

<p>O&apos;Neal says London&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170429014729/https://www.stjohngroup.uk.com/">St. John</a> restaurant, his favorite in the world, was a starting point while thinking about the restaurant&apos;s aesthetic, which features no art, whitewashed brick walls and &quot;just tables chairs and that&apos;s it.&quot; &quot;If you don&apos;t notice it, then [designer Patrick] did his job perfectly.&quot; St. John also serves always serves sorbet and Polish vodka, so it&apos;s on the menu here for that in addition to the neighborhood&apos;s community. &quot;It&apos;s in Greenpoint, of course we&apos;re going to serve Polish vodka and sorbet.&quot; </p>

<p>There&apos;ll be some seasonal shifting in the menu, like different accoutrements with the falafel and the veal tongue&#x2014;a highlight of a recent press preview, along with just about everything else&#x2014;will be replaced by blood pudding. &quot;The caesar will always be there and things like the gravlax and tartare will always be there because they&apos;re not seasonal, but I&apos;d like the menu to be seasonal,&quot; O&apos;Neal promises. </p>

<p>Another staple: the bread, a sourdough miche from Bien Cuit the kitchen dispatches with relish. &quot;We just think bread&apos;s really comforting and it goes well with everything,&quot; O&apos;Neal explains. &quot;The three of us all love eating grilled bread. We pretty much just got the grill to grill bread.&quot; </p>
