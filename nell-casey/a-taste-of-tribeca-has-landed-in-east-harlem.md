					<p>There are 20% more Edison Bulbs in East Harlem now courtesy of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141518/http://www.maxwellscentralpark.com/">Maxwell&apos;s Central Park</a>, the uptown of expansion of Tribeca restaurant Maxwell&apos;s. The new restaurant, which opened last week, takes over a former Ottomanelli Bros location on 5th Avenue between 111th and 112th Streets, just a few blocks from Central Park. </p>

<p>It&apos;s an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141518/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/12/a_food_drink_tour_of_spanish_harlem.php#photo-1">interesting addition to the culinary scene</a> in that part of Harlem&#x2014;central Harlem&apos;s food renaissance notwithstanding&#x2014;and a sign that the speed of gentrification in that section of the neighborhood may be hastening. &quot;We&apos;ve been interested in Harlem for a while,&quot; said owner Alex Tortolani. &quot;We saw an opportunity, kind of an underserved area of East Harlem where there aren&apos;t a lot of bars and restaurants.&quot;</p>

<p>Like its downtown companion, the uptown Maxwell&apos;s serves gourmet comfort food like Fish &amp; Chips ($18), Mac &amp; Cheese ($15) and a selection of burgers. The menu&apos;s slightly different to accommodate the kitchen size and Tortolani says they were mindful of keeping the prices &quot;accessible&quot; for the neighborhood versus prices in Tribeca. The priciest entree is a $20 Walnut Encrusted Salmon with wilted spinach and mashed potatoes, and the bar menu has several items under $10 for the drinking set.</p>

<p>The bar section of the restaurant is large and they&apos;ll be playing sports daily as events come up. Imbibers can get away with a $4 bottle of Tecate or spring for a $12 Chimay. Classic cocktails like Negronis, Dark &amp; Stormys and Old-Fashioneds are below market rate at $11 with glasses of wine priced between $8 and $10. </p>

<p><em>1325 5th Avenue, 646-657-0993; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016141518/http://www.maxwellscentralpark.com/">website</a></em></p>
