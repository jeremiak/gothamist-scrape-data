					<p>A deli to New Yorkers can mean an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708072034/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/21/best_jewish_delis_nyc.php">excellent place for pastrami</a>, or it can mean the corner store where you make 3 a.m. beer runs. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708072034/http://twinsunsdeli.com/">Twin Suns Deli</a>, which opened last Thursday on Himrod Street in Bushwick, is a melange of the two: you can stock on up mustard, mayo and potato chips and also score a first-rate sandwich in the process. This is next-wave deli, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708072034/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/08/pastrami_perfection_at_harry_and_id.php">like Harry &amp; Ida&apos;s</a>: part artisanal sandwich shop, part cutesy general store.</p>

<p>Owners Danielle Masback, Stacy Hall, Rich Porpiglia and Montana Masback have imparted a little New Orleans flair to their shop, offering both their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708072034/http://gothamist.com/2011/11/08/muffalettas.php">take on the muffuletta</a> as well as Cafe Du Monde beignet mix and Duke&apos;s mayonnaise, a Southern pantry staple. Their Sloppy Roast Bee Po Boy ($12) employs Leidenheimer Pistolet-style rolls to contain slow-roasted beef, gravy, caramelized onions, horseradish cream and Zapps chips, and the duo say they consulted with local bakeries to give the special Big Muffaletta ($12 quarter; $40 whole) authenticity in the bread department. </p>

<p>Red sauce Italian gets a vegetarian spin here in the Parm sandwich ($11), which swaps out chicken in favor of burnt broccoli topped with tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella and parmesan cheese on garlic bread. Those wishing to make their own sauce can snag a can of San Marzano tomatoes, in addition to tomato pasta, dried pastas and fresh cheeses. Or pick up ready-made ramen kits by Sun Noodle or a box of Annie&apos;s macaroni and cheese. </p>

<p>Every deli worth its salt does some type of breakfast sandwich; here, they&apos;re offering an Egg and Cheese ($6) made with soft scrambled eggs and pimento cheese on brioche (add Taylor ham or bacon for an extra $2), which they&apos;ll serve with a free cup of coffee anytime before 9 a.m. In fact, they&apos;re doing all-day breakfast, so if the craving strikes for a Breakfast Burrito ($7)&#x2014;made here with chorizo, eggs, house-made Sonoran salsa and potato on a flour tortilla&#x2014;at 7 p.m. on a Sunday night, they&apos;ll hook you up.</p>

<p><em>244 Himrod Street, 718-484-9291; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708072034/http://twinsunsdeli.com/">twinsunsdeli</a></em></p>
