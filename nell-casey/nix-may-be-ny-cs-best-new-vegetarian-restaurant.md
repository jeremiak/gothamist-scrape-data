					<p>You may think of meat when you see carpaccio and stir fry on a menu, but at Nix there&apos;s not a single animal on the ingredient list. Chef John Fraser&#x2014;who also helms <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170721233910/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/03/nacissa_standard_hotel.php#photo-1">Narcissa in the Standard East</a> and Dovetail&#x2014;debuted the latest of the city&apos;s vegetarian eateries this week, part of a trend towards embracing vegetable-forward and vegan dining. Other than being vegetarian, the restaurant is pleasingly idiosyncratic, drawing from global inspirations to produce an eclectic menu of veggie friendly dishes. Though he&apos;s not skimping on the butter and cheese where applicable, a substantial portion of the menu is, in fact, vegan.</p>

<p>Tandoor bread, puffed, golden and herb-scattered, is a utensil for dips like creamy hummus sprinkled with zaatar and house-made cottage cheese topped with jewel-toned broccoli leaf pesto. That aforementioned Carpaccio ($14) is made with Russet potatoes spiced with habanero cream and topped with chopped eggs and crispy potato threads. Pull back the endive and dandelion leaf curtain on the Spinach Pie ($15) to reveal a beautiful olive branch of spinach pastry studded with salty feta and nuts.</p>

<p>You&apos;ll be hearing a lot about the Yukon Potato Fry Bread ($14), to be sure; the deep fried but airy creation is like gourmet county fair food for vegetarians. It&apos;s topped pizza-style with cheddar cheese, sour cream and some veggies for crunch. It&apos;s just as decadent as it sounds, same with the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170721233910/https://www.instagram.com/p/BCeBU4WkoHa/?taken-by=nellcasey">Shiitake &quot;Cacio e Pepe&quot;</a> ($18), tender mushrooms in soupy polenta with salsify. Everyone waddled out stuffed and satisfied after a recent press preview, that&apos;s for certain.</p>

<p>It&apos;s early, to be sure, but Nix seems poised to be one of the best openings of 2016 so far. In fact, on our way out the door, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170721233910/https://twitter.com/johndelsignore">my dining companion</a> made a reservation to come back for dinner a few days later. Do with that information what you will. </p>

<p><em>72 University Place, (212) 498-9393</em></p>
