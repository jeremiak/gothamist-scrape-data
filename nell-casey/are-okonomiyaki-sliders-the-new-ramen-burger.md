					<p>After the Ramen Burger forever altered the fundamental nature of the Japanese dish back in 2013, everyone was looking for a way to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160320/http://gothamist.com/2014/06/19/have_we_taken_ramen_too_far.php">cash in on the ramen zeitgeist</a> with their own noodle food mashup, to varying degrees of success. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160320/http://www.osakagrub.com/">Osaka Grub</a>, a new stand opening up in the Essex Street Market next Tuesday, looks to transform another Japanese culinary staple into a familiar American template, making okonomiyaki sliders using the Japanese pancakes as the buns.</p>

<p>David Senn, a London ex-pat, and Diana Tam, a native New Yorker, met in Tokyo and became enamored with the savory dish, which translates to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160320/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/03/league_of_kitchens_okonomiyaki.php">&quot;what you like.&quot;</a> &quot;We take a very traditional approach on our regular okonomiyaki,&quot; Senn told us, a version ($8) of which they&apos;ll be griddling up at their new quasi-brick-and-mortar. The same batter is then poured into molds to create the slider &quot;buns,&quot; which will hold a beef patty, pickled ginger, bonito flakes, and aonori, a type of green seaweed ($6). </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="062416oko_stick.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160320im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/062416oko_stick.jpg" width="640" height="426"> <br> <i> (Scott Lynch / Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The duo have been popping up at mobile markets like Hester Street Fair and LIC Flea &amp; Food, where they began their business in 2015. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160320/http://gothamist.com/tags/quickbites">Quick Bites</a> columnist Scott Lynch <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160320/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/10/photos_lic_flea_returns_with_lots_o.php">tried the burgers</a> at Hester earlier this year and determined that the burgers had &quot;potential,&quot; which will presumably be maximized with a more formal kitchen to work in.</p>

<p>They&apos;ll also be making Hashimaki ($6), a riff on okonomiyaki that&apos;s rolled onto chopsticks for portable eating, then topped with either bacon and bonito flakes or a vegetarian mushroom and miso. Karaage&#x2014;deep fried chicken&#x2014;will also be on offer, either plain ($6) or as a Karaage Slider ($4) on a potato roll.</p>

<p>Osaka Grub opens inside the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160320/http://www.essexstreetmarket.com/#welcome">Essex Street Market</a> on Tuesday, operating six days a week (excluding Mondays).</p>
