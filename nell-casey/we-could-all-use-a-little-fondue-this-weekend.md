title: We Could All Use A Little Fondue This Weekend
---

	
										<p><em>Thinking about normal things like eating and drinking and carrying on with your life seem tough right now. Trust me, I know. But endure we must, and sustenance is necessary for all the important work to be done. And also, it&apos;s fun and we could all use a little bit of that.</em></p>

<p><strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220254/http://www.mamannyc.com/shop-1/fondue-like-the-french-do-january-20th-at-maman-soho-1">Fondue Like The French Do</a></strong> this evening at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220254/http://www.mamannyc.com/">Maman</a> in SoHo. From 7 - 9 p.m. the restaurant is hosting a fondue dinner for National Cheese Lover&apos;s Day today (a wonderful antidote to Inauguration Day), offering a prix fixe dinner centered around a three cheese fondue with roasted vegetables and croutons for dipping. The meal also includes canapes, salads, desserts and two glasses of much-needed wine <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220254/http://www.mamannyc.com/shop-1/fondue-like-the-french-do-january-20th-at-maman-soho-1">for $65</a>. </p>

<p>On Saturday, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220254/http://www.craftrestaurant.com/">Craftbar</a> hosts a <strong>Wild Game Dinner</strong> of three meaty dinner courses plus a dessert. The meal begins with rabbit mortadella, followed by wild boar tortelloni, a smoked venison leg and concluding with a maple custard with a phyllo nest and wild huckleberries. The meal runs $95 per person; email astone@craftedhospitality.com or call 212-461-4300 for reservations.</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220254/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/28/poke_explainer_guide_nyc.php">era of poke</a> celebrates one year this Saturday at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220254/http://www.pokeworks.com/">Pokeworks</a>, where they&apos;re celebrating their first birthday with <strong>$1 poke</strong> for the first 100 customers of the day. Beginning at noon, snap up the dollar fish dish if you get there early enough, or at the very least get a coupon or two for new locations opening next month near Grand Central and Union Square. </p>

<p>And finally, if you have breakfast on the brain&#x2014;or need to fuel before <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220254/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/19/womens_march_nyc_q_a.php">this weekend&apos;s march</a>&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220254/http://acmenyc.com/">ACME</a> in NoHo <strong>begins brunch</strong> this weekend. Swing by for a Hot Grains Bowl with barley, quinoa, roasted squash, ricotta cheese and poached eggs ($16), Rigatoni with scallions, bacon, gutenberg cheese and an egg ($20) or get buzzed on cocktails like the Smokey Mary with mezcal, tequila, pickles and bacon ($14).</p>
