					<p>Philadelphia&apos;s all-star hummus restaurant <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214019/http://www.dizengoffhummus.com/">Dizengoff</a> opens its NYC branch this week inside Chelsea Market, whipping up chickpea dips, salads and house-made pitas beginning Wednesday. Partners Michael Solomonov and Steven Cook, along with chef-partner Emily Seaman, are recreating hummusiyas, hummus stalls found all over Israel, with 18 seats and standing tables, plus the option for takeout. </p>

<p>Hummus-seekers will be able to watch the pita puff up in the oven while awaiting their orders. The oven&apos;s also put to good use in the mornings, when the stall will offer a daily shakshuka&#x2014;the baked egg dish with tomatoes, onions and chilies&#x2014;from 10 a.m. to 11:30 a.m.</p>

Hummus remains the big draw here, with a daily rotating selection of seasonal toppings of both the carnivorous and vegetarian varieties. Chef Seaman&apos;s specialty: the lamb neck, black lime and rhubarb (seen above), is exclusive to the NYC shop. Also unique to this location, a vegan roasted romanesco, pine nuts and golden raisins toppings combination. Salatim, Israeli vegetable salads, include Moroccan carrots cooked with saffron and topped with pistachio and raw kohlrabi marinated in schug, a Yemenite chile sauce.
<br><br>
The stall serves booze as well, including Israeli wines by the glass, tap craft beer from local breweries and the option to add bourbon to Israeli mint lemonade.
