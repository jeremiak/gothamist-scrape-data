<p>On Saturday and Sunday, a massive <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/nyc-african-food-festival-tickets-23872202400">African Food Festival</a></strong> comes to the Brooklyn Navy Yard, bringing dozens of restaurants and chefs to the growing foodie hub. Dishes spanning many regions will be on offer, with a focus on healthy and nutritious food including vegetarian and vegan options. Participate in cooking demos or just dine around the continent feasting on things like &quot;puff-puffs (deep-fried Nigerian donuts), jollof (a popular West African one-pot rice dish cooked in a tomato sauce), hibiscus and ginger juices, suya (spicy kebabs)&quot; and other delectable foodstuffs, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/http://brooklynbased.com/blog/2016/08/09/new-york-african-food-festival-debut/">according to Brooklyn Based</a>. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/nyc-african-food-festival-tickets-23872202400">Tickets $10 - $500</a>.</p>

<p>Speaking of vegan, the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/https://veganshopup.wordpress.com/">Vegan Shop-Up</a></strong> returns to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/http://www.pineboxrockshop.com/">Pine Box Rock Shop</a> on Saturday from noon to 6 p.m. Pick up animal-free cheese snacks from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/https://www.instagram.com/thatscheezy/">That&apos;s Cheezy</a>, artisan peanut butter chocolates from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/https://www.facebook.com/eatchicchocolates/">Eat Chic Chocolates</a>, and scorch your mouth with salsas from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/http://www.sillychillyhotsauce.com/">Silly Chilly Hot Sauce</a>, among other cruelty-free options. Excitingly: the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/http://cinnamonsnail.com/">Cinnamon Snail</a> is back to its mobile ways slinging <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/23/cinnamon_snail_adam_sobel.php">doughnuts</a> and vegan sandwiches out front. </p>

<p>If you aren&apos;t getting the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/10/hot_steamy_sweaty_sticky.php">hell out of dodge</a> on weekends during the month of August, consider a game of <strong>ros&#xE9; pong</strong> instead. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/https://delfriscosgrille.com/new-york">Del Frisco&apos;s Grill</a> is hosting patio parties on Saturdays all month, where you can throw ping pong balls into glasses of blush wine, if that&apos;s something that appeals to you. They&apos;re also doing kegerators with different outlets each weekend, plus cornhole, life-size Jenga and giant scrabble to enjoy between bites of burgers. </p>

<p>Billed as NYC&apos;s &quot;first and only farm-to-bar cocktail festival,&quot; the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/http://secretsummernyc.com/">Secret Summer</a></strong> event on Sunday is a can&apos;t-miss for mixology devotees who don&apos;t mind a heavy head on Monday morning. Unfolding from 4 p.m. to 10 p.m. at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/http://www.thefoundry.info/">The Foundy</a> in LIC, there&apos;ll be free-flowing cocktails (and water), plus some food trucks&apos;ll be loitering&#x2014;but you&apos;ll have to pay out of pocket. Only the final ticket tier isn&apos;t sold out, so be prepared to shell out <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016142703/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/secret-summer-tickets-24232673579">$150 per person</a>, but you&apos;ll get some free ice cream from VanLeeuwen. </p>
