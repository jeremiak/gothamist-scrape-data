<p>Beginning today, Grand Central Terminal&apos;s Vanderbilt Hall will be transformed for <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://japanweek.us/">Japan Week</a></strong>, specifically the Japan of 100 years ago. There are all kinds of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://japanweek.us/theaterschedule/">demos and performances</a> but foodies shouldn&apos;t miss the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://japanweek.us/exhibitor/">sake, whiskey and bento</a> setups, offering a variety of styles to suit different tastes. Sake from 15 different regions of Japan will be poured, in addition to six different &quot;100-years old flavor&quot; bento boxes like Kanazawa Curry, Kanimeshi (crab) and Gyumeshi (beef). Don&apos;t miss the salads and sushi cakes from Azuma Foods and the Japanese confections from Minamoto Kitchoan. For even more Japanese flavors, check out <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/05/japanese_restaurant_week.php">Japanese Restaurant Week</a> as well. </p>

<p>It&apos;s the most wonderful tiiiime of the year...aka <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://www.tourdefrancenyc.com/assets/StinkyCheeseWeek2014.pdf">Stinky Cheese Week</a></strong> at Manhattan&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://www.tourdefrancenyc.com/">Tour de France</a> restaurants. Starting tomorrow March 7th and continuing through March 14th, the group&apos;s eight restaurants are offering special cheese dishes prominently featuring the most fragrant of fromage. L&apos;Express on Park Avenue offers dishes like Sausage du Jour ($14.95) with camembert sauce; Maison rocks a Welsh Rarebit ($23) made with taleggio; and Nice Matin is doing a Risotto flavored with epoisses, herbs, peas and crunchy bacon. Check out <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://www.tourdefrancenyc.com/assets/StinkyCheeseWeek2014.pdf">all the offerings here</a>.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-right"> <img alt="030614lukes.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/030614lukes.jpg" width="300" height="208"> <br> <i style=" width:300px; ;display:block"> <span class="photo_caption">(<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://www.flickr.com/photos/bettytsang/4051160296/in/photolist-7aZfPj-eRLXav-eRLXfi-9ZGp9L-9ZGpid-9ZDxhV-9Hdxg7/lightbox/">bettytsang</a>/Flickr)</span></i></div> East 7th Street has a wonderful collection of top notch eateries and to celebrate the flavorful options the street will host mini-food fest called <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://lukeslobster.com/shop/tasteofseventh">Taste of Seventh</a></strong>. A <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://lukeslobster.com/shop/tasteofseventh">$25 ticket</a> gets you bites from seven eateries, including a half lobster roll from Luke&apos;s Lobster, a vegetarian arepa from Caracas and an Apple Gobbler soft serve cone from Big Gay Ice Cream. Your ticket also gets you $1 off deals at Jimmy&apos;s No. 43, Lunasa, Burp Castle and Standings plus free transportation to the fest (up to $25) from Uber. Stop by anytime on Saturday or Sunday to redeem your bites.<p></p>

<p>Celiac sufferers or folks eschewing gluten are blessed with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/04/gluten-free_pastas_nyc.php">lots of options</a> for safe eating these days and a monthly series dedicated to their dietary needs is just one of them. This Sunday, head over to Freddy&apos;s Bar in South Slope for the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://glutenfreepopupnyc.wordpress.com/">Gluten-Free EatUP!</a></strong>, offering up a bevy of gluten-free products for on-site eating and also taking home for later. Levine&apos;s General Store and Kimcheelicious will be providing Grilled KimCheese and Classic grilled cheese sandwiches, which can be washed down with GF beers and ciders from the bar. Italian pastas and sauces, focaccia, baked goods and all sorts of other treats will also be available when the mini-market operates between 2 p.m. and 6 p.m.</p>

<p>On the LES, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140309063807/http://hotelchantelle.com/">Hotel Chantelle</a> tries to bring back the traditional weekend meal with the <strong>Improper English Roast</strong> each Sunday at 6 p.m., which includes a three-course meal plus beer pairing. The big dish is the 16 ounce thick cut English roast, served up with Yorkshire pudding, gravy, carrots and Brussels sprouts or a Fish &amp; Chips option for pescatarians. That plus a Chantelle Salad and Bourbon Bead Pudding make for a complete meal; each course also gets a specially-paired beer from the in-house sommelier. The food and booze dinner runs for $35.95 or you can get the roast a la carte for $19.92. </p>
