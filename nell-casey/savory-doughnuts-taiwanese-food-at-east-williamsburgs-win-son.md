					<p>Don&apos;t be fooled by the Nutritious Sandwich on the menu at Win Son, a new Taiwanese-American soul food restaurant that just opened on Graham Avenue in East Williamsburg. Chef Trigg Brown&apos;s irreverently-named sandwich is actually a savory doughnut stuffed with ham, pickled pineapple and cilantro. Enjoy entering that into your calorie counter.</p>

<p>Win Son is the baby of Trigg and friend/business partner Josh Ku, a first generation Taiwanese-American who introduced Trigg to his heritage cuisine. The team <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021122617/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/22/weekend_food_roundup_64.php">did a pop-up collaboration dinner</a> last month, where they introduced dishes like the Pan Griddled Pork Buns ($9) with scallions and chili vinaigrette and the Big Chicken Bun ($13), a fried chicken leg sandwich sauced with fermented tofu sauce and sliced herbs. </p>

<p>Now they&apos;ve got their full menu in swing, with a beverage program designed by Brett Helms. Cocktails, which are meant to be paired with certain dishes, are unique creations, like one with gin, pickled ramps and dry vermouth (Mr. Mitchell&apos;s Gibson, $11) and another with peanut milk, El Dorado 8 Year, rum cream and toasted coconut (Breakfast of Champions, $12). </p>

<p><em>159	Graham Avenue, 347-457-6010. Sunday - Thursday 5:30 p.m. - 12 a.m., Friday and Saturday 5:30 p.m - 2 a.m. Kitchen closes at 11 p.m.</em></p>
