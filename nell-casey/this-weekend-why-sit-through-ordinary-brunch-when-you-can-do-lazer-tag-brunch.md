<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/http://www.bonchovie.com/">Bon Chovie&apos;s</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/25/bon_chovie_bay_ridge.php">swimming up from Bay Ridge</a> for a <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/https://www.facebook.com/events/363879107282088/">Haunted Pirate Ship</a></strong> pop-up in Clinton Hill this weekend. On Saturday and Sunday, Renae Holland&apos;ll be frying up her <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/http://gothamist.com/2011/09/19/neill_and_renae_holland_bon_chovie.php">signature anchovies</a> ($8) along with calamari, fresh oysters, burgers and steamed lobster dinners on Saturday and Sunday from 2 p.m. to 10 p.m. at 884 Fulton Street. Tall boys of Narragansett are just $5 and Owney&apos;s Rum will be slinging some cocktails; they&apos;re also promising a prize for the best costume of the weekend.</p>

<p>Looking for a way to amp up your Saturday brunch? How making it about both eating and shooting your friends with lasers? This weekend, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/http://www.socialdrinkandfood.com/">Social Drink &amp; Food</a> in Hell&apos;s Kitchen invites <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/https://lazerbrunch.com/"><strong>Lazer Brunch</strong></a> onto their rooftop terrace to transform the space into a 2,000-square-foot arena of competition. Stop by from 1 p.m. to 6 p.m. to participate in the action; there&apos;ll be a DJ on hand and participants are encouraged to dress in their finest Halloween attire. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="040416AmpleHillsCreamery.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_scottheins/040416AmpleHillsCreamery.jpg" width="640" height="426"> <br> <i> via <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/https://www.facebook.com/amplehills/?fref=ts">facebook</a></i></div> <p></p>

<p>On Saturday in the Meatpacking District it&apos;s time for <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/https://harvestfest.splashthat.com/">Harvest Fest</a></strong>, an artisanal market and fair in celebration of the season&apos;s bounty alongside local, inedible products. Ample Hills Creamery, Dana&apos;s Bakery, Foragers Market, Fort Gansevoort BBQ, High Street on Hudson, Sweetgreen and Untitled are a few of the participating food vendors who&apos;ll be populating <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/https://www.hudsonriverpark.org/explore-the-park/locations/14th-street-park">Hudson River Park&apos;s 14th Street Park</a> between 11 a.m. and 4 p.m. There&apos;s also free yoga and massage chairs on the schedule for truly relaxing into the weekend.</p>

<p>Learn about a lesser-told story about hot history subject Alexander Hamilton this Sunday at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/http://kingscountydistillery.com/">Kings County Distillery</a>. Author William Hogeland will be on hand to discuss his book <em>The Whiskey Rebellion: George Washington, Alexander Hamilton, and the Frontier Rebels Who Challenged America&apos;s New Found Sovereignty</em>, a fitting topic while being shown around the distillery&apos;s floor and barrel aging room. </p>

<p>Hogeland&apos;s book recounts the pushback regarding the tax on whiskey in the 18th century, the first time an American product had ever been subject to a federal tax. Hear the &quot;gripping and sensational tale of violence, alcohol, and taxes&quot; while sipping four the distilleries whiskies. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031022526/http://kingscountydistillery.com/events/">Tickets $14</a>.</p>
