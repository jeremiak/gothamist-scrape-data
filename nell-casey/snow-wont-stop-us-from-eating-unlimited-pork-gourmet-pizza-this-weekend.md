<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058/http://www.schallerstube.com/">Schaller&apos;s Stube</a>, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058/http://gothamist.com/2015/08/31/schallers_stube_ues.php">darling little sausage window</a> serving up wurst on 2nd Avenue on the UES, begins a beer service today to accompany their delicious sausages. They&apos;re doing a fully imported German line to begin, including $5 pints of Reissdorf K&#xF6;lsch and Krombacher Pilsner and Dark. They&apos;re also doing a traditional Gl&#xFC;hwein, a mulled, spiced wine served hot and toasty for this impending weather.</p>

<p>If you manage to travel during the storm, head over to Clinton Hill&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058/http://pizzalovesemily.com/">Emily</a>, who are throwing a very special fundraiser for a member of their team while simultaneously celebrating their second birthday. On Saturday lunch, they&apos;re doing an &quot;extra shift,&quot; where all proceeds from the meal will <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058/https://www.facebook.com/pizzalovesemily/photos/a.1473416806219222.1073741830.1446266052267631/1803866306507602/?type=3&amp;theater">benefit their co-worker Carl</a>, who was injured in a bad bike accident over the holidays.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="012216fourhorse.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/012216fourhorse.jpg" width="640" height="480"> <br> <i> Poached Eggs, Polenta, Braised Spinach, Charmoula (courtesy Four Horsemen)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Two new brunches arrive this weekend to help fortify the body for the winter chill. First up, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058/http://www.fourhorsemenbk.com/#four-horsemen-home">Four Horsemen</a> tempts with Poached Eggs ($16) over polenta, braised spinach and charmoula and Steak and Eggs ($25) with eggs sunnyside up, tomatillo and peas plus &quot;Breakfast Wines.&quot; Over in SoHo, 40-year-old <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058/http://raouls.com/">Raoul&apos;s</a> debuts their first ever brunch with Souffle Apple Pancakes ($18) topped with creme Chantilly and salted caramel and a Raoul&#x2019;s Omelette ($22) with smoked salmon, salmon roe and creme fraiche.</p>

<p>Avert your gaze, vegetarians, because the city&apos;s biggest celebration of all things pork comes to Brooklyn on Sunday and it wants to get us drunk&#x2014;on bacon and bourbon. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058/http://cochon555.com/">Cochon 555</a> is bringing piles of gourmet pork dishes, plus bacon, oysters, cheese, charcuterie and lots and lots of booze to Williamsburg&apos;s stately Weylin B. Seymour&apos;s, formerly known as the Williamsburgh Savings Bank. Tickets are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605051058/http://events.cochon555.com/new_york_city_2016">$125 GA</a> for an all-you-can-eat-and-drink free-for-all from 5 p.m. to 8 p.m., meaning you&apos;ll be home in time to drunk watch the new <em>X-Files</em>.</p>
