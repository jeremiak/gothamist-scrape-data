<p>Ted Cruz might not be on the ballot (Sad!), but you can stuff your face with some Tex-Mex at <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://eloriginaltxmx.com/">El Original</a></strong> in Hell&apos;s Kitchen, which is offering some discounted food and beverages to sate you while the returns come in. Stop by for $8 nachos all evening, plus an $8 beer and tequila special that&apos;ll ramp up your buzz if the returns start going sidewise. Happy hour goes &apos;til 8 p.m., when live band karaoke takes over. <em>735 10th Avenue, (917) 382-5512; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://eloriginaltxmx.com/">eloriginaltxmx.com</a></em></p>

<p>In the Financial District, show your support for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/https://www.instagram.com/p/BMhsbSngElj/">either candidate</a> with $4 Budweisers (red) or Bud Lights (blue) at <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://ambrosebeerandlobster.com/">Ambrose Beer &amp; Lobster</a></strong> in the Seaport. They&apos;re also promising live music and feasting on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/27/yes_lobsters_used_to_be_signs_of_po.php">the people&apos;s crustacean</a>: lobster. <em>18 Fulton Street, (212) 480-0301; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://ambrosebeerandlobster.com/">ambrosebeerandlobster.com</a></em></p>

<p><strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://campeonnyc.com/">Campeon</a></strong> off Union Square is also riffing on the blue/red theme with $3 cans of Budweiser and PBR beginning at 9 p.m. The bar&apos;s also giving away free &quot;We&apos;re Screwed&quot; T-shirts to the first 200 people who order a drink. <em>9 East 16th Street, (212) 675-4700; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://campeonnyc.com/">campeonnyc.com</a></em></p>

<p>A popular spot for watching the debates due to their giant projector screens, <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://www.blackforestbrooklyn.com/">Black Forest Brooklyn</a></strong> in Fort Greene will once again use them to document our democracy. Beers and wines will be on special, including half off certain bottles of vino. <em>733 Fulton Street, (718) 935-0300; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://www.blackforestbrooklyn.com/">blackforestbrooklyn.com</a></em></p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="110816winery.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/110816winery.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> Brooklyn Winery (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/https://www.facebook.com/brooklynwinery/photos/a.10151406655379737.1073741825.360894839736/10153890845294737/?type=3&amp;theater">Facebook</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p><strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://bkwinery.com/2016-election-night-screening-party/">Brooklyn Winery</a></strong> in Williamsburg also aims to drown potential sorrows with lots of wine. They&apos;re converting their Atrium space into a watch party arena, complete with food from an Election Night menu (cheese and charcuterie plates, turkey meatballs and fried chicken, among other selections), their proprietary wines, craft beers and a specialty fall cocktail made with Laird&apos;s Applejack, Maple Syrup, Peychaud&apos;s Bitters, and Black Walnut Bitters. Doors open at 6 p.m. <em>213 North 8th Street, (347) 763-1506; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://bkwinery.com/">bkwinery.com</a></em></p>

<p>Also in Williamsburg, <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://www.nitehawkcinema.com/">Nitehawk Cinema&apos;s</a></strong> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://www.nitehawkcinema.com/lo-res/">Lo-Res Bar</a> on the ground floor will be showing the returns on the projector screen plus offering a happy hour-priced drink ($4 draft beer and well drinks) for anyone bearing an &quot;I Voted&quot; sticker. <em>136 Metropolitan Avenue, (718) 782-8370; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://www.nitehawkcinema.com/">nitehawkcinema.com</a></em></p>

<p>Inwood&apos;s <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://www.indianroadcafe.com/">Indian Road Cafe</a></strong> promises a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/https://www.facebook.com/IndianRoadCafe/photos/a.284208210669.155227.152560980669/10153789975110670/?type=3&amp;theater">bi-partisan viewing</a> of tonight&apos;s returns, along with food and drink specials &apos;til it&apos;s all said and done. The owner&apos;s also offering a drink menu of former Presidents&apos; favorite drinks, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20161020/midtown/presidential-election-returns-hillary-clinton-donald-trump-watch-party-nyc">telling DNAInfo</a> he &quot;researched them.&quot; <em>600 West 218th Street, (212) 942-7451; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://www.indianroadcafe.com/">indianroadcafe.com</a></em></p>

<p>Pet some consenting pussy at the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://catcafebk.com/">Brooklyn Cat Cafe</a></strong>, which is betting that some time spent cuddling some cute kitties should help keep anxiety at bay. Their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/https://squareup.com/store/BrooklynCatCafe/item/election-night">Election Night Party</a> ($30-35) begins at 8 p.m. with adoptable kitties, food, Brooklyn Brewery beer, and the returns screened with breaks for cute cat videos. The evening concludes with Stephen Colbert&apos;s election night special on Showtime. <em>149 Atlantic Avenue, (347) 946-2286; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://catcafebk.com/">catcafebk.com</a></em></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/http://werepair.org/category/brooklyn/">Repair The World</a> in Crown Heights is hosting the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/kegs-with-a-conscience-election-watch-party-tickets-28528892686">Kegs with a Conscience: Election Watch Party</a></strong> this evening beginning at 7 p.m. The free event (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161111081446/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/kegs-with-a-conscience-election-watch-party-tickets-28528892686">registration required</a>) is a non-partisan, all are welcome environment with food and a keg plus &quot;distractions&quot; including games and letter writing. <em>808 Nostrand Avenue</em></p>
