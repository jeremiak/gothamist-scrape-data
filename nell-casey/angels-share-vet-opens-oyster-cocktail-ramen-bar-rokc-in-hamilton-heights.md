					<p>Vets of three well-respected NYC restaurants have come together to open a new cocktail, oyster and ramen bar in Hamilton Heights. Owner Shigefumi Kabashima, a longtime bartender at Angel&apos;s Share (the cocktail bar that has been one of the city&apos;s most popular &quot;secret&quot; bars since opening over 20 years ago), Jeff Srole of Maison Premiere and Isao Yoneda of Totto Ramen/Hide-chan Ramen opened ROKC on Broadway between 140th and 141st Streets last week. </p>

<p>The name&#x2014;Ramen + Oyster  + Kitchen + Cocktails&#x2014;spells out the concept thoroughly. It&apos;s an all-in-one tour of a night on out Japan, which might begin with some raw seafood and snacks, then continue to include a few drinks, and conclude with a hearty bowl of ramen. </p>

<p>Srole selects fresh clams and oysters, which are included a special daily $1.50 happy hour Monday - Friday from 4 to 7 p.m. and Saturday and Sunday from 2 - 7 p.m. Next, a quartet of Japanese tapas including a Spicy Shrimp Bun ($4.50) and a crudite platter of crunchy veggies served with a warm fondue for dipping ($10).</p>

<p>Kabashima&apos;s crafted some intricate but playful cocktails, like the Flower ($12), a shochu-based offering flavored with elderflower, lavender and cranberry and served <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027173452/http://www.yelp.com/biz_photos/rokc-new-york?select=a6VxRjAvV9pwdPGIZDoASQ">inside a lightbulb</a>. As the exclusive bartender for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027173452/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FiQnH450hPM">Japanese whiskey Suntory</a>, Kabashima employs the dark stuff in in a Matcha latte ($15) with house-made matcha.</p>

<p>After imbibing your fill, move on to the final stage of the evening: the ramen. Yoneda has three offerings including a Tokyo ramen ($13) with a soy and fish broth topped with pork belly and garlic oil and a Sapporo ramen ($13) based in chicken topped with chicken chashu and two different types of pepper. </p>

<p><em>3452 Broadway</em></p>
