</div></div>					<p>Summer may be the season of the outdoor food market but that doesn&apos;t stop <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508161142/http://urbanspacenyc.com/mad-sq-eats/">Madison Square Eats</a> from giving us a reason to bundle up and dine al fresco when the mercury dips below 70. It&apos;s the 5th year the pop up food court has taken over the space across from the park and they&apos;ve got a pretty stellar lineup of food market regulars mixed with some of the trendy dining spots showing up on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508161142/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/18/the_best_pizza_in_nyc.php">foodie lists</a> and the like.</p>

<p>Roberta&apos;s, Calexico and momofuku milk bar all have stalls at this season&apos;s setup. Dan Delaney&#x2014;he of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508161142/http://gothamist.com/2012/07/31/brisket_town.php">rapturous brisket</a>&#x2014;debuts his foray into Mexican BBQ with TacoTown, offering four different tacos including one (shockingly!) vegetarian option. Each taco sells for $5 (!) and you can choose between Chopped Brisket (with cheese, chili sauce, and red onion relish), Pulled Pork (with chicharones and sala verde) or Fried Chicken (with cheeze sauce [<em>sic</em>], iceberg, and jalapeno). Look for a Brussels Spouts option in the next few days.</p>

<p>Other vendors include Slide, which specializes in &quot;gourmet&quot; sliders, ice cream sandwiches from Melt Bakery, creperie Bar Suzette, La Sonrisa empanadas and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508161142/http://urbanspacenyc.com/mad-sq-eats/">lots of other sweet and savory options</a>. MSE operates daily from 11 a.m. to 9 p.m. now through October 27th.</p>
