					<p>Creating al pastor tacos involves a gigantic spit&#x2014;the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170119052053/http://gothamist.com/2014/12/31/al_pastor_tacos_stupak.php#photo-15">trompo</a>&#x2014;piled high with succulent pork and pineapple, slowly spinning next to an open flame until the meat&apos;s ready to be sliced off and tucked into a tortilla. At <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170119052053/http://www.guadalupeinnbk.com/">Guadalupe Inn</a>, the third installment of Mexico City-inspired restaurants from the team behind Mesa Coyoacan and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170119052053/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/16/williamsburg_mexican_restaurant_zon.php">Zona Rosa</a>, a miniature trompito comes tableside for a DIY taco experience. </p>

<p>Owner and chef Ivan Garcia&apos;s solidified his place as NYC&apos;s ambassador to his native Mexico City with his now trio of eateries dedicated to highlight the neighborhoods&#x2014;all of his restaurants are named after sections of the city&#x2014;and flavors of his hometown. Plump corn gorditas are home to chicharron and carnitas or used as a vessel for a chicken liver pate. Larger dishes include the al pastor experience, Mexican veal meatballs and Costillas de Cordero, baby lamb chops served with beet salad.</p>

<p>The lush digs, including a chandelier and a big oak bar, are meant to evoke a &apos;40s-era supper club, whatever that means, but it <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170119052053/http://www.grubstreet.com/2016/10/guadalupe-inn-opens-in-new-york-with-ambitious-mexican-food.html">looks pretty swanky</a>. They&apos;re also doing live music from traditional mariachi bands to &quot;Brooklyn&#x2019;s most talented singers and bands.&quot; </p>

<p><em>1 Knickerbocker Avenue at Johnson Street, (718) 366-0500; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170119052053/http://www.guadalupeinnbk.com/">guadalupeinnbk.com</a>. Open 5 p.m. to 2 a.m.</em></p>
