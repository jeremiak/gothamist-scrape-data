<p>Those of us observing <strong>National Pigs In A Blanket Day</strong> on Monday can find an inventive version being served at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/http://www.nyysteak.com/">New York Yankees Steakhouse</a> in Midtown. Available throughout the season, too, the &quot;Dugout Dog&quot; is a wagyu hot dog wrapped in puff pastry with caramelized onions and American cheese. There&apos;s spicy brown mustard, naturally, but the everything bagel seasoning&apos;s an inventive touch. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="042417qdc.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/042417qdc.jpg" width="640" height="430"> <br> <i> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/https://www.facebook.com/queensdinnerclub/photos/gm.764304243752585/397801397269885/?type=3&amp;theater">Facebook</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>To celebrate one year of globe-trotting supper series <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/https://www.facebook.com/queensdinnerclub/">Queens Dinner Club</a></strong>, organizers are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/12/queens_dinner_club_kicks_off_with_i.php">taking it back to the roots</a> with an Indian-Chinese feast where it all began at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/http://www.tangrafusionnyc.com/">Tangra Asian Fusion</a> in Sunnyside. On Tuesday, enjoy a family-style feast with dishes including Tangra Fish Fingers, Chili String Beans, Tangra Masala Tiger Prawns, Vegetable Noodles and Ras Malai, a cheesecake-like dessert from India. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/https://www.eventbrite.com/e/queens-dinner-club-presents-an-indian-chinese-birthday-banquet-tickets-33640709267">Tickets $45</a>.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="042417sandwich.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/042417sandwich.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/https://www.facebook.com/sandwicherieny/photos/a.1600199343584636.1073741828.1423042601300312/1738343806436855/?type=3&amp;theater">Facebook</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>A second location of fast casual eatery <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/http://sandwicherie.com/">Sandwicherie</a></strong> opens Wednesday near Union Square with some cheap lunch specials in celebration. Between 11 a.m. and 3 p.m. Wednesday through Friday, head to 127 4th Avenue for $5 bowls and sandwiches, which come with a complimentary beverage. Look for specials including the &quot;Union Square&quot; with steak, tomato relish, avocado and citrus-cilantro aioli and the &quot;Times Square&quot; with pork shoulder, tomato relish, roasted shallots, smoked ham, Swiss, and dill pickles.</p>

<p>Welsh <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/06/sunken_hundred_welsh_gastropub.php#photo-1">newcomer to Cobble Hill</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015115133/http://www.sunkenhundred.nyc/"><strong>Sunken Hundred</strong></a> gets into the lunch game for those with free midday hours. On Thursdays and Fridays from noon to 4 p.m., head to Smith Street for a Welsh Corned Beef Sandwich ($12) with seaweed-kraut and spicy mustard or Rarebit ($9, pictured above), a Welsh staple of melted cheese over crusty bread. Lunch specials come with chips or a side salad. </p>
