</div></div>					<p>We&apos;ve almost <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430043301/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/03/snow_what.php">beaten winter into submission</a> and now it&apos;s time to start looking ahead to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430043301/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/24/the_10_best_rooftop_bars_in_nyc.php">carefree rooftop imbibing</a> we deserve. The latest in mile high dining comes courtesy of the Hyatt Times Square, which opened its new rooftop lounge Bar 54 a few weeks ago. Located on the 54th floor, naturally, the bar claims to be the city&apos;s highest rooftop lounge&#x2014;with the vista to prove it.</p>

<p>The furnishings suffer a bit from Hotel Bar syndrome, but who cares when all eyes are going to be focused on these stellar views? The bar offers stunning southern exposure&#x2014;you can peep 1 WTC if you squint&#x2014;plus the Chrysler Building to the east. Come sunset, you can just see the Hudson River peeking between the skyscrapers with a decent view of the skyline to boot. </p>

<p>Sweeping skyline views don&apos;t come cheap, though. House cocktails set you back a cool $23; glasses of wine are a &quot;bargain&quot; at $13. Oh, and that&apos;ll be $11 for that Amstel Light, pal. Group boozing for parties of three or four mean special punches ($115) like the Green Subarmine with Pimm&apos;s, Tanqueray and Green Chartreuse flavored with ginger, lemon, cucumber and soda; or Rose&apos;s Turn with Aylesbury Duck Vodka, Lillet Rose and Creme de Cassis with maraschino, lemon, grapefruit and champagne. </p>

<p>Drinks are priced on the high end, to be sure, but that&apos;s par for the course when it comes to hotel drinking. Plus, the drinks are crafted by Clover Club owner Julie Reiner and served by a hirsute hipster&#x2014;no <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430043301/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/28/no_l_train_more_mta-induced_misery.php">dicey subway trip</a> required! Certainly worth it to splurge on one drink, share a Charcuterie Plate ($24) and soak in the evening rays sparkling off the buildings.</p>

<p><em>Bar 54 is located inside the Hyatt Times Square at 135 West 45th on the 54th Floor, (646) 364-1234; open daily from 5 p.m. until 1 a.m. (Sunday through Thursday) and 2 a.m. (Friday and Saturday)</em></p>
