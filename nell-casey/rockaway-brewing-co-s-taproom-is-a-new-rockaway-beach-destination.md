					<p>It&apos;s a bit confusing that, until now, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170510173617/http://rockawaybrewco.com/">Rockaway Brewing Co.</a> has been operating in Long Island City, but the group has now returned to its Rockaway roots this summer, opening a new tap room and hang space in the Arverne section of the peninsula. This past weekend, the team flung open the doors at Beach 72nd Street on an industrial-type space where people can hang, sip some pints and chill out to some reggae music before, during, or after a day spent soaking up the rays on the beach.</p>

<p>There were four beers on offer opening weekend, including Da Beach, a hoppy session type with a 4.8% ABV for longevity and an English Style Ale at 6.4% ABV for winding down the day. No <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170510173617/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/07/best_local_beer_in_nyc.php">Nitro Black Gold</a> in sight, but milk stouts are a tough sell on a hot day.</p>

<p>Nearby <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170510173617/https://www.facebook.com/Goodys-BBQ-Chicken-Ribs-100880453289872/">Goody&apos;s BBQ Chicken &amp; Ribs</a> was on hand serving their namesake chicken with jerk sauce in addition to some grilled burgers. Additionally, Nae&apos;s Italian cycled through for some lemon, coconut, mango and other flavors of ices.</p>

<p>As it&apos;s closer to the bay side, be prepared for about a half mile walk to the boardwalk and about a mile to Rippers. As far as sipping Rockaway brews in a beach environment, this&apos;ll be the big operation, as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170510173617/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/19/an_urban_beach_beer_bar_is_coming_t.php">plans for an urban beach</a> in Ridgewood are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170510173617/http://queenscrap.blogspot.com/2016/06/ridgewood-beach-gets-benched.html">currently on hold</a> until all the proper permits and such can be acquired. </p>

<p><em>415 Beach 72nd Street, Arverne. Open Monday - Thursday 3 p.m. to 10 p.m. and Friday - Sunday noon to 10 p.m.</em></p>
