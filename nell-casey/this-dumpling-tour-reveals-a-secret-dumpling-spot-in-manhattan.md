<p>Brooklyn By Bike&apos;s <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105348/http://www.brooklynbybike.com/rides/?mc_cid=3b1dbd64cf&amp;mc_eid=4ee40ecd22">Ride in Search of Dumplings</a></strong> takes cyclists on a seven mile dumpling tour beginning Saturday at 11 a.m. On the ride you&apos;ll stop at Tasty Dumpling, a secret dumpling spot, a hand-pulled noodle spot and a pierogi place &quot;for good measure.&quot; They suggest a $10 donation for the mini tour, with a portion going to charity. </p>

<p>This weekend is the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105348/http://harlemeatup.com/">Harlem EatUp!</a></strong>, celebrating the neighborhood&apos;s diverse dining scene with a series of <a href="jsimon10@fordham.edu">dinners and events</a>. Stalwart eateries like Sylvia&apos;s and Melba&apos;s are on the docket, joined by newcomers like Blujeen and Minton&apos;s. On Saturday and Sunday, the Strolls, free events featuring live music and cooking demonstrations by well-known chefs including Emeril Lagasse and Carla Hall. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="052016crawfish.jpeg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105348im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/052016crawfish.jpeg" width="640" height="480"> <br> <i> (via <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105348/https://foursquare.com/v/sycamore-flower-shop--bar/49c4489df964a520b9561fe3/photos?openPhotoId=51858e9e498e2d5925329088">Foursquare</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105348/http://www.lightspace.tv/">LightSpace Studios</a> hosts <strong>Plant-Based Bushwick</strong> this Saturday, a free event &quot;celebrating compassion and well-being for all living beings.&quot; The event features cruelty-free vendors sampling and selling food, beauty products and clothing, in addition to free fitness classes and talks about living on a plant-based diet. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105348/http://www.sycamorebrooklyn.com/">Sycamore&apos;s</a> <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105348/http://sycamorecrawfishboil.brownpapertickets.com/">7th Annual Crawfish Boil</a></strong> claws its way into our bellies on Sunday, with three different seatings to feast on the little mudbugs. Choose either 1 p.m., 3 p.m. or 5 p.m. and buy a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110105348/http://sycamorecrawfishboil.brownpapertickets.com/">$30 ticket</a>, which buys you the crawfish cooked by Gaddy Lane, one side and a pint of beer from Threes Brewing.</p>
