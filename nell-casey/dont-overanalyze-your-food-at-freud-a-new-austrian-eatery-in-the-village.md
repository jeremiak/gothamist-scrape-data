					<p>Restaurateur and chef Eduard Frauneder has made it his mission to bring the flavors of his native Austria to New Yorkers&apos; mouths with his charming East Village spot Edi &amp; the Wolf, and now the same is true of his new restaurant, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170240/http://www.freudnyc.com/">Freud</a>, which opened yesterday on La Guardia Place. Where Edi delights with its rustic wood furnishings, Frauneder takes a more streamlined approach with Freud, a decluttered aesthetic to match the refined platings coming out of the kitchen. Freud is the cosmopolitan Viennese kindred spirit to Edi&apos;s skiing-in-the-Alps vibe.</p>

<p>Familiar dishes from the region include the Wiener Schnitzel ($23) served with traditional accompaniments of cucumber and potato salads; Frauneder also turns up the flavors on Spatzle ($11)&#x2014;chewy egg noodles&#x2014;employing a dark rye, shaved cheddar cheese and browned cauliflower. The Onion Tarte ($16) also looks delightful, boasting caramelized cups of sweet cipollini onions affixed with Gruyere cheese and an onion gravy.</p>

<p>The wine list skews Central European in the organic and biodynamic method; beers are from the same region. As Frauneder also runs cocktail bar The Third Man, there&apos;s some of that reflected on the beverage menu here. Sip on the theme-appropriate Libido ($14), an inhibitions-freeing mix of Cabeza Tequila, turmeric, Thai basil, honey and jalapeno syrup and lime.</p>
