					<p>Californians love to bray about how their Mexican food surpasses NYC&apos;s, no question. Agree to disagree. But now we have some <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104757/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/30/ottos_tacos_gives_new_yorkers_tacos.php#photo-1">West Coast upstarts</a> who&apos;ve <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104757/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/25/smorgasburg_preview_2015.php#photo-1">brought the Cali-style</a> to the East Coast, and one of them, Lucha Lucha, has just expanded to Bushwick. </p>

<p>Two years ago, the restaurant opened up its first location in Bed-Stuy, doing San Diego-style Mexican in a small space on Nostrand Avenue. Like Tex-Mex, SoCal Mexican isn&apos;t what you&apos;ll find on a trip to Oaxaca or one of Mexico&apos;s other states. Instead, it&apos;s an amalgam of Mexican and Californian cuisines, which in this case, means lots of french fries.</p>

<p>San Diegan Jay Welch&#x2014;who heeded the call of co-owners Blair Papagni and Josh Cohen to come run the restaurants&#x2014;says that rolled tacos (similar to taquitos), carne asada fries and bean-less, french fry-stuffed burritos are the hallmarks of San Diego-style Mexican. But for Welch, it&apos;s the sauces that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104757/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ETx6h3_9wzk&amp;feature=youtu.be">really matter</a>.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="100616sauces.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104757im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/100616sauces.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> (Scott Heins/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The restaurant turns out three different sauces, like a Salsa Verde that automatically comes inside the California Burrito ($10) along with those french fries, a smoky Chipotle, and an electric orange Habanero Carrot that&apos;s not as fiery as its hue would suggest. In California, it&apos;s common to alternate bites of burrito with different sauces, Welch says.</p>

<p>The &quot;$15 pound&quot; California burritos have been the big seller since day one, but the more classic burrito application of rice and beans in the Tex-Mex ($9) one has also been a hit. Instead of pintos, the Nachos ($10) employ black beans and epazote in their refried form accompanied by a mix of chorizo and steak and the Carne Asada Fries ($13) are essentially the same but with fries instead of tortilla chips.</p>

<p>There&apos;s no liquor license just yet, but when it arrives, so will margaritas on draft and frozen drinks. For now, it&apos;s just beers and wines, which are just $4 during happy hour from 3 p.m. to 7 p.m. While the weather holds, take your grub and head out to the back patio to perch on one of the colorful picnic benches. </p>

<p><em>173 Irving Avenue, (718) 366-6069; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104757/http://luchalucha.nyc/">luchalucha.nyc</a></em></p>
