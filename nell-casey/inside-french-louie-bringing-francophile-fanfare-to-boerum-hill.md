</div></div>					<p>Carroll Gardens&apos; fried chicken-friendly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426031319/http://gothamist.com/2008/11/24/buttermilk_channel_set_to_open_tomo.php#photo-1">Buttermilk Channel</a> gains a new sibling when <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426031319/http://frenchlouienyc.com/">French Louie</a> makes its grand debut this evening in Boerum Hill. The restaurant name-checks hunter/trapper and hermit Louis Seymour, who became a kind of Canadian folk hero. The name also drops hints about chef Ryan Angulo&apos;s cuisine, which includes a French-American base with flair from two of North America&apos;s other French-leaning cities: Montreal and New Orleans. </p>

<p>Francophile fare like Steak Tartare ($14), Radishes with butter and black olive salt ($4) and Steak Frites ($20 - $36) of several varieties bolster the menu&apos;s classical roots. Snails &quot;Marchand de Vin&quot; ($14) bring in some southern sensibilities with house-cured bacon and grits; as does the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426031319/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/07/menu_trend_skate_for_your_supper.php">on trend</a> Pan-Fried Skate ($23), served with crab &quot;dirty rice&quot; and a creamy crab bisque. The fun Mussels en Papillote ($12) deserves an order, as does the simple but compelling Salt-Baked Chicken ($22) with bread salad in a spicy sauce. </p>

<p>Co-owner Doug Crowell and team echoed Buttermilk Channel&apos;s simple, clean decor in the new venture. Dark woods and banquettes add depth to the milky white walls and simple fixtures. The cute log cabin wall graphics&#x2014;another nod to our hermetic friend&#x2014;add a touch of whimsey to the otherwise unadorned dining area. Bey and J <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426031319/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/01/no_baby_only_buttermilk_channel_for.php">would approve</a>. </p>

<p><em>320 Atlantic Ave between Hoyt and Smith Streets, 718-935-1200</em></p>
