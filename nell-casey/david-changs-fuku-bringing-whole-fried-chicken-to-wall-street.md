<p>The new <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/08/fuku_fidi.php">Financial District outpost</a> of David Chang&apos;s dedicated fried chicken outlet <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/https://eatfuku.com/">fuku</a> began its soft opening Tuesday evening, serving dinner Monday through Friday beginning at 5 p.m. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/16/fuku_chicken_binge.php#photo-1">superb fried chicken sandwich</a> that debuted at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/10/david_chang_fried_chicken_1.php">East Village original</a> makes the menu downtown, too, but it&apos;s the Chimek fried chicken dinner that&apos;ll be the big draw at the new locale.</p>

<p>Similar to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/https://noodlebar-ny.momofuku.com/noodle-bar/menu/fried-chicken/">fried chicken dinners</a> Chang offers at his momofuku noodle bar, the Chimek entree ($42) includes a whole fried chicken, half of which (thighs and wings) is fried and doused in a sweet and spicy sauce, the other half (breast and legs) coated in buttermilk and habanero and then fried. The chicken is accompanied by m&apos;smen&#x2014;a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/http://www.designsponge.com/2015/10/in-the-kitchen-with-hot-bread-kitchens-moroccan-flatbread.html">Moroccan flatbread</a> by Hot Bread Kitchen&#x2014;pickle chips, pickled daikon, kimchi and french fries. Add a six pack of High Life ponies for $12.</p>

<p>A summer menu also includes a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/https://www.instagram.com/p/BVk9deiBBc9/?taken-at=1473076572730606">Ginger Chicken Bowl</a>&#x2014;to which you can add a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/https://www.instagram.com/p/BVit3ullPFd/?taken-at=1473076572730606">crack-your-own egg</a> for $1&#x2014;a Crispy Kale Salad ($9) and Sugar Snap Peas ($4). </p>

<p>Lunch will debut soon but for now it&apos;s just dinner. The prolific restaurateur is also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/23/david_chang_seaport.php">opening a restaurant</a> in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621201913/http://gothamist.com/tags/pier17">Pier 17</a> at the South Street Seaport at some point in the future.</p>
