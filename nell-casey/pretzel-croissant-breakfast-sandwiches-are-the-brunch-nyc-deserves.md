<p>Bushwick&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111246/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/14/syndicated_dine_in_movies_bushwick.php#photo-1">new dine-in cinema</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111246/http://syndicatedbk.com/"><strong>Syndicated</strong></a><strong> launches the requisite brunch program</strong> this weekend, with bloodies, breakfast sandwiches and burgers to pair with their lineup of classic films. On Saturdays and Sundays from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m., the restaurant section of the operation will offer items including a sausage, egg and cheddar Breakfast Sandwich ($12) served on a pretzel croissant; French Toast Sticks ($9) with a blood orange gastrique; and Loaded Breakfast Tots ($12) topped with scrambled eggs, pulled pork, jalapenos, cheddar cheese and sour cream. </p>

<p>Speaking of brunch, if rubbing elbows with celebrity chefs while downing unlimited mimosas is your style, head to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111246/http://www.thewayfarernyc.com/">The Wayfarer</a> on Saturday, where Executive Chef Chris Shea is teaming up with Chef David Burke for a <strong>guest chef brunch</strong> at the Midtown eatery. The duo will be serving up passed hors d&apos;oeuvres like waffle bites and scrambled eggs with caviar, followed by a three course meal of dishes like Sea Scallops &amp; Corned Beef Cheek Hash with quail eggs and mustard oil. There&apos;s dessert to finish it off, plus unlimited access to the build-your-own &quot;bloody and bubbles bar.&quot; Reservations are $70 per person; 212.691.0030 or email Curtis@thewayfarernyc.com.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111246/https://www.facebook.com/RangoonNoodleLab/?fref=ts"><strong>Rangoon Noodle Lab</strong></a> will be popping up at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111246/http://humboldtandjackson.com/">Humboldt &amp; Jackson</a> Saturday night, bringing Burmese noodle salads, dishes and soups with it. On the menu, Beef Shan Noodle (Rice noodles, beef tomato sauce, pickled vegetables, egg, peanut, scallion), Garlic Pork Noodle (egg noodles, braised pork, fried garlic, bok choy, mushroom, pickled mustard) and Lemongrass Fish Noodle Soup (rice noodle, aromatic fish broth, fish cake, egg) and a few others, including a Vegan Rice Noodle Salad. The dinner starts at 5 p.m.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021111246/http://www.primechefclub.com/">Prime Chef Club Room</a>, a kosher space that&apos;ll be offering an occasional tasting menu plus cooking classes and other events, debuts this week. Their first <strong>Sunday supper</strong>&#x2014;dinners will be Thursdays and Sundays&#x2014;will be by Prime Hospitality&#x2019;s executive chef Chef David Kolotkin, who&apos;s serving an intimate dinner for 20 with dishes including corn velvet soup, miso-glazed Chilean sea bass and porcini rubbed sliced reserve steak. Dinners are $75 per person and begin at 6:30 p.m.</p>
