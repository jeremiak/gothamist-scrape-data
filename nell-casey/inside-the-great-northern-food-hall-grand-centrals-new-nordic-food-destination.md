					<p>Those commuting through Grand Central Terminal have even more tasty food and beverage options now that the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917201933/http://gothamist.com/2014/06/21/grand_central_terminal_food_hall.php">new food hall</a> curated by Claus Meyer&#x2014;godfather of the New Nordic Cuisine movement and co-owner of internationally acclaimed Noma in Copenhagen&#x2014;has officially begun serving sm&#xF8;rrebr&#xF8;d and pastry in Vanderbilt Hall. Though it&apos;s been in a kind of soft open mode over the past week, the market&apos;s officially debuting today, offering five unique &quot;pavilions&quot; for different food and beverage options, plus a bar for beer, wine and spirits. </p>

<p>Breads and pastries will be made on-site and used for sandwiches including the sm&#xF8;rrebr&#xF8;d, which are traditional open-faced Rugbr&#xF8;d (rye bread) sandwiches topped with things like smoked fish or shrimp and hard-boiled egg. The pastry program, which Meyer had been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917201933/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/08/all_of_the_baked_goods_at_meyers_ba.php#photo-1">finessing in Williamsburg</a>, includes items like Chocolate and Vanilla Wienerbr&#xF8;d, Tebirkes (Poppy Seed Danish), and Kanelsnurre (Cinnamon Swirl). </p>

<p>This opening completes a trifecta of new food offerings from Meyers, who opened his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917201933/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/25/agern_grand_central_terminal.php#photo-1">fine dining restaurant Agern</a> and a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917201933/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/10/danish_dogs_grand_central_terminal.php">fancy hot dog stand</a> in the Terminal earlier this year.</p>
