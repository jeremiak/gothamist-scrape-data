					<p>Eataly&apos;s rooftop bar and restaurant La Birreria has been transformed from an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113150903/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/11/best_roof_bars_winter_nyc.php">Italian Alps theme</a> to the beachy Sabbia, with a coastal Italian theme. Buoyed ropes, parasols, and life preservers dangle from the ceiling; while alluring vintage-style posters on the walls depict splashy spots like Taormina in Sicily and urge the viewer to &quot;Travel Italia.&quot; Signs throughout the space point to seaside destinations like Sorrento and Cinque Terre, or advertise beaches where bathing suits are optional. Cheeky Mario!</p>

<p>The menu&apos;s been revamped to highlight seafood and other coastal fare, including a charming oyster bar constructed to look like a little boat. There&apos;s an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113150903/https://www.instagram.com/p/BEyZw8XkoOK/?taken-by=nellcasey">impressively large Fritto Misto ($24)</a>, too, plus Fusilli with squid, shrimp and cockles ($21) and Grilled Swordfish stuffed with breadcrumbs ($27). There are beef, chicken and vegetarian options as well.</p>

<p>Depending on the day, there may be a spritz cart weaving its way between tables where you can design your own cocktail. Otherwise, they have their own versions by the carafe ($20) including one made with Flor Prosecco, mint, cucumber and Sorrento lemon juice. Fresh fruits and vegetables are highlighted as well, like the Hoppy Hour ($15) made with Absolut Citron Vodka, fresh carrot juice, lemon and a ginger-honey syrup. </p>

<p>Sabbia opens tonight, Friday April 29th, at 5 p.m. with tabletop corn hole in a &quot;cabana&quot; with beach chairs and a faux fire pit. After today, it opens daily at 11:30 a.m. until midnight (Thursday - Saturday) or 11 p.m. (Sunday - Wednesday). </p>
