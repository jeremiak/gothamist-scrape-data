					<p>I can&apos;t move forward writing about this new restaurant called Lady&apos;s without first typing these three words: Honey. Butter. Pizza. It&apos;s one of the pies on offer when the restaurant opens in Fort Greene on Friday and, frankly, that&apos;s probably all you need to know. But also: Calabrian chilies and sea salt (two of the pizza&apos;s toppings) and $15 (the price). Now let your imagination run wild (because the pizza ovens weren&apos;t fired up in time for photography). </p>

<p>The pasta and pizza spot comes courtesy of the restaurant group behind No. 7 and cocktail bars Weather Up, among others. Chef Aaron Harsha, who worked previously at Michael White&apos;s high end seafood spot Marea, takes us on a Mediterranean tour with non flour-based dishes like Roasted Meatballs ($12), Oysters roasted with Parmesan and garlic ($14), and roasted Bronzino with salsa verde and lemon ($65).</p>

<p>Handmade pastas, also something Harsha would have extensive experience making, include ricotta-stuffed Cappelletti ($22) with a ragu made from aged meat and confit tomatoes and purses of Casoncele stuffed with short ribs with roasted sunchokes in a wine and bone marrow sauce. </p>
