					<p>Filming recently began in NYC for the adaptation of Stephen King&apos;s opus <em>The Dark Tower</em>, starring Idris Elba as Roland Deschain, a.k.a. The Gunslinger, and Matthew McConaughey as his foe the Man In Black. Early photos show a leather-clad Elba uniting (reuniting?!) with Jake Chambers (Tom Taylor) on Lexington Avenue and 29th Street&#x2014;in the <em>Rose Hill</em> section of Manhattan&#x2014;and McConaughey <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030070550/https://twitter.com/LimitedPaper/status/748619811807105025">strutting around</a> like a slimmer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030070550/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smash_Mouth">Steve Harwell</a> in a trench coat.</p>

<p>Details about the plot of the film have so far indicated this <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030070550/http://www.ew.com/article/2016/05/17/stephen-king-dark-tower-movie-ew-podcast">won&apos;t be a straightforward adaptation</a>. King says this new organization of events &quot;may upset some of the fans a little bit,&quot; as the first film will begin in what&apos;s perceived to be the &quot;middle&quot; of Roland&apos;s quest to reach the titular Dark Tower. The action of the film(s)  could be viewed as a reinterpretation of Roland&apos;s story, which gives filmmakers license to streamline some of King&apos;s more outlandish plot details and doesn&apos;t require them to precisely follow the events of the books.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="070716darktower2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030070550im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/070716darktower2.jpg" width="640" height="440"> <br> <i> (Pacific Coast News)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Early set photos support that idea, as book characters and locations intersect in ways not set forth in the source material. Today, filming was in Chinatown, in what appears to be a scene that occurred in the second of the novel series, <em>The Drawing of the Three</em>. Roland can be seen emerging from Big Johnny Gun Shop after purchasing ammunition; Jake was also on the Chinatown set today in what appears to be a separate scene.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="070716darktower3.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030070550im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/070716darktower3.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> The exterior of the Dixie Pig (Scott Heins/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>This coming weekend, filming moves to DUMBO&#x2014;at a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030070550/http://gothamist.com/2013/05/10/photos_dumbo_getting_transformed_in.php">super</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030070550/http://gothamist.com/2014/09/11/photos_spielberg_coens_dumbo.php#photo-1">popular</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030070550/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/15/keanu_reaves_fishburne_wick.php#photo-1">intersection</a> for such things. Without actors on set, it&apos;s difficult to predict which characters these scenes will feature, but there are a few Easter Eggs/references that will be exciting for fans of King&apos;s novels. Crews were busy prepping the exterior of the Dixie Pig, a restaurant for vampires and other unsavory creatures from the novels; it&apos;s being set up as some kind of music venue, from the looks of it.</p>

<p>There are also set pieces for two antiques shops, including one named for Kurt Barlow and Richard Straker, two characters from another Stephen King novel, <em>Salem&apos;s Lot</em>. Barlow is also mentioned in <em>The Dark Tower</em> series in flashback. A painted sign on brick further down the block reads Chambers Paper Fibres, a nod to Jake&apos;s character. </p>

<p>Also on the Dumbo set: giant pieces of styrofoam crafted to resemble stone masonry that&apos;s crumbled or been blown up. Showdown at the Dixie Pig? We&apos;ll have to wait &apos;til February to find out.</p>
