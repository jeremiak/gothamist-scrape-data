					<p>Not content to produce just one intoxicant, a new East Williamsburg taproom will be brewing beer <em>and</em> distilling the hard stuff. The delightful overachievers at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092638/http://interboro.nyc/home">Interboro Spirits &amp; Ales</a> debut their new operation today on Grand Street, offering nine tap beers and their proprietary gin, Goodwin Hill, which they&apos;ll pour on tap in gin and tonic form. </p>

<p>&quot;There was an Interboro brewing company pre-prohibition,&quot; co-founder and brewmaster Jesse Ferguson explains. &quot;We discovered the old smoke stack and thought it was a good name to speak to and about the city we love.&quot; </p>

<p>Six of the tap beers are Interboro originals, including the Bushburg, a pilsner named for the brewery&apos;s placement in the borough. &quot;Five years ago I definitely would have called our neighborhood Bushwick, but I think it&apos;s really East Williamsburg.&quot; The other three beers are collaborations with three other local breweries: Transmitter, Threes, and Other Half. &#x201C;We hope to get to a point where there&#x2019;s a new beer on every week,&quot; Ferguson says.</p>

<p>Ferguson says they&apos;ll be introducing new beer and spirits &quot;constantly,&quot; starting with a whiskey and a bourbon in the coming year. &quot;We&#x2019;re not constrained by regional or territorial history. I don&#x2019;t have distilling in my family. It&#x2019;s something I&#x2019;m doing just to play with flavor and make cool stuff.&quot; They&apos;ll also be crafting spirits like amaros and liqueurs, and this fall they&apos;ll distill an Apple Brandy made with cider from Indian Ladder Farmstead.</p>

<p>Small plates and snacks like vegetarian and meat pasties will be available as well; for tonight&apos;s opening party, it&apos;s cheese plates and charcuterie, too. </p>

<p>And like every good brewery, they have their own brewery cat, Bingo. &quot;[Co-founder and CEO] Laura [Dierks] adopted him from a shelter on the North Fork of LI, he lived in and out of her house, hunting birds from what I&apos;ve heard,&quot; Ferguson says. &quot;Now he lounges around the Brewhouse and the Still sleepily giving tours and finding dangerous situations to narrowly avoid when we&apos;re operating the forklift.&quot;</p>

<p>Meet Bingo and the rest of the team at tonight&apos;s opening party from 6 to 9 p.m., Saturday noon to 10 p.m. and Sunday noon to 6 p.m. </p>
