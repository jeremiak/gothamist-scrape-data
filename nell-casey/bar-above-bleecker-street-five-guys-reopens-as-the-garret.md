</div></div>					<p>Earlier this year the &quot;secret&quot; dive bar called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429104842/http://bleeckerheights.com/">Bleecker Heights Tavern</a> closed up shop in its location above <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429104842/http://www.fiveguys.com/">Five Guys</a> burgers in the West Village. The second floor space has now reopened as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429104842/http://www.garretnyc.com/">The Garret</a> a decidedly more upscale cocktail den that channels more art house loft than dorm room disaster. </p>

<p>The eclectic decor includes multicolored skull paintings by street artist Bradley Theodore, as well as a &quot;hand-painted printed taxidermy rhinoceros head&quot; by artist Nick Bakita. If that all sounds a little macabre don&apos;t worry; two vaulted sky lights, glass chandeliers and plenty of views out onto the intersection won&apos;t let you be afraid of the dark.</p>

<p>The bar features 14 tap beers plus the de rigueur speciality cocktails if that&apos;s your thing. The herbaceous Rosarito Stick-Up ($14) whips up El Tesoro tequila and Grand Marnier with rosemary, thyme, sage syrup and egg white; on the flip side, their signature drink dubbed the Two Lime Crew ($10) is just a vodka soda with two limes. If you didn&apos;t fortify with a burger on your way up you&apos;ll be able to order special items that Five Guys created for the bar. As long as that includes their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429104842/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/19/best_french_fries_nyc.php">superlative french fries</a> we&apos;re on board. </p>

<p><em>296 Bleecker Street, (212) 675-6157; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429104842/http://www.garretnyc.com/">garretnyc.com</a>. Open daily at 3:30 p.m.</em></p>
