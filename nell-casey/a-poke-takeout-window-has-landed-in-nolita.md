<p>Established sit-down restaurants <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/18/snag_horchata_slushies_spanish_hot.php">opening</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/03/many_food_purveyors_throughout_the.php#photo-1">takeout</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/2014/10/31/east_village_broth_window.php">windows</a> are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/10/weekend_food_roundup_71.php">so hot</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/20/roll-n-roaster_window.php">right now</a>. The latest to jump on not one but <em>two</em> trends is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://www.seamores.com/">Seamore&apos;s</a>, which just opened a new to-go window serving...<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/tags/poke">poke</a>!</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/26/seamores_nolita.php#photo-1">Meatball Shop founder&apos;s</a> seafood spinoff will be handing over bowls of tune poke ($16) with peanuts, ponzu and avocado on brown rice, a dish that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/tags/quickbites">Quick Bites</a> columnist Scott Lynch <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/07/seamores_fish_review.php#photo-1">found satisfying</a> while other poke was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/02/poke_computer.php#photo-1">falling short</a>.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="081816window2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/081816window2.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> (courtesy Seamore&apos;s)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The only other thing they&apos;ll be serving (for now) are cones of vegan Coconut Lemongrass soft serve ice cream topped with shredded coconut, an offering from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170621215946/http://www.oddfellowsnyc.com/">OddFellows Ice Cream Co.</a></p>

<p>The window will be open daily between 11 a.m. and 7 p.m. </p>
