</div></div><p>A new lunchtime option has arrived in the Financial District in the form of Greek &quot;fast food,&quot; an alternative option for anyone wary of the street meat gyro. GRK keeps the menu simple with a selection of <em>Yeero</em> meats, tzatziki spreads and salads, most priced under $10. For the yeeros, select a protein of either chicken, pork or lamb &amp; beef&#x2014;all sourced from local farms then marinated and roasted&#x2014;then decide between a warm pita sandwich or a platter with an additional side. Both options are also served with a dose of tzatziki and hot sauce, upon request. </p>

<p>Vegetarians can choose between three salad options, including one with lentils, veggies and herbs and the three tzatziki spreads&#x2014;scallion/mint, roasted pepper and basil/garlic&#x2014;might make a nice afternoon snack. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130721082143/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/23/greek_yogurt_good_for_you_and_good.php">Right on trend</a>, the restaurant offers greek yogurt, either frozen or fresh, with a variety of sweet toppings&#x2014;granola, nuts, fruit and the like&#x2014;available to bulk it up. But we&apos;re most curious about the savory option with fresh figs, olives, pepper, cucumber, fresh basil and extra virgin olive oil plus pita chips for dipping. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130721082143/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TZddRRUEUck">John Stamos will be so proud</a>. </p>

<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130721082143/http://grkfresh.com/">GRK Fresh Greek</a> // 111 Fulton Street, Manhattan // 212-385-2010 // 11 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily</em></p>
