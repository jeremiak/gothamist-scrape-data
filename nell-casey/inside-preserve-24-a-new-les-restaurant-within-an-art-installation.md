</div></div>					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180429/http://www.preserve24.com/">Preserve 24</a>, a new dining destination on the Lower East Side, wants you to know that it&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180429/http://www.preserve24.com/about.html">not just another restaurant</a>. The sprawling space, which takes over three 111-year old tenement buildings, is also a multidisciplinary art installation, a social club, and a sitting room. All this is to say that artist Brian Goggin&apos;s design isn&apos;t just background noise in the newest locavore restaurant to hit the dining scene; in many cases, it&apos;s the star of the show. </p>

<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180429/http://www.todseelie.com/">Tod Seelie</a> captured some of Goggin&apos;s more whimsical design elements, like a bar built out of grand pianos with piano pedal tap handles to match; ceiling fans constructed out of oars, wooden propellers and baking handles;  and walls, booths and bars created from old doors and window shutters. </p>

<p>The distressed look is purposeful, channeling a bygone era of explorers and expeditions and charting unknown territory. One design element evokes both our past and future in the form of a giant iceberg chunk from the Greenland ice sheet, which will be suspended in a custom case kept chilly by solar power. The expedition to excavate the icy giant won&apos;t happen until next March, but the glacial relic will serve as a physical reminder of our ever-warming planet. </p>

<p>Chef John Parlatore&apos;s food follows a similar thesis, with hearty, classic New York fare composed with locally sourced ingredients. &quot;Supper&quot; items include a variety of roasted dishes from the enormous wood burning oven, like a Smoked Pork Chop ($28) with molasses butter beans and the Whole Roasted porgy ($22) with muddled tomato, oregano and garlic. Lunch means simple yet flavorful fare like the Fried Chicken ($15) with butter biscuits and honey wasabi and a selection of sandwiches like a grilled cheese ($12) with spicy pickles and a fried oyster sandwich ($15) with smoked bacon. During the day, part of the restaurant will serve Weaver&apos;s Coffee &amp; Tea&#x2014;with hand roasted beans by coffee master John Weaver&#x2014;plus homemade baked goods and other carryout items. </p>
