					<p>I&apos;m very much on board with any and all restaurant trends that involve unique seating <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402033553/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/10/larina_pastificio_vino_fort_greene.php#photo-1">like swings</a>. The moving benches at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402033553/http://www.camachosnyc.com/">Camacho&apos;s</a>&#x2014;a new Mexican spot opening today&#x2014;are more your garden variety rocking chairs made to <em>look</em> like swings with those deceptive ropes. But until we&apos;re sippin&apos; margaritas on someone&apos;s actual porch this summer, this is the next best thing.</p>

<p>Owner Michael Sinensky retooled his raucous sports bar Little Town NYC into Camacho&apos;s, whose kitchen is being helmed by Puebla native Fernando Camacho. The chef turned out tasty Mexican street food at a recent press preview, including some excellent, meat-stuffed flautas; solid mini quesadillas; and a beguiling shrimp salad served in an edible tortilla bowl. Overall, the shrimp was one of the best things on the menu.</p>

<p>The FB Hospitality Group hasn&apos;t strayed too far from their debauched roots. A giant neon sign proclaims &quot;Tequila makes my clothes come off,&quot; a nod to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402033553/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nj2700em-JQ">cringe-worthy Joe Nichols song</a> I heard <em>way</em> too many times while working for Brother Jimmy&apos;s many years back. If you, too, disrobe at the prospect of agave spirits, order up a margarita and/or tequila flight and let it all hang out.</p>

<p><em>118A East 15th Street; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170402033553/https://www.camachosnyc.com/">camachosnyc.com</a></em></p>
