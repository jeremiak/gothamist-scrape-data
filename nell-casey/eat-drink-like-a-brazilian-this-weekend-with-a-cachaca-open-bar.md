<p>Brazilian restaurant <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161107085906/http://www.esperantony.com/">Esperanto</a> celebrates 17 years in the East Village tonight and they&apos;re looking for people to help them mark this special occasion. This evening, they&apos;re throwing a <strong>celebration party</strong> that includes a two hour open bar featuring cacha&#xE7;a punch and sangria and live music courtesy of DJ Mirlet. They&apos;ll also be awarding one lucky attendee with a $500 gift certificate to redeem on Lombo de Porco, Tostones, or something else of the winner&apos;s choosing at a later date.</p>

<p>Also this evening, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161107085906/http://humboldtandjackson.com/">Humboldt &amp; Jackson</a> host Waldorf Astoria chef Tano Holmes, who helms the kitchens until the historic hotel closes to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161107085906/http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-06-26/nyc-s-waldorf-astoria-said-to-close-in-2017-to-convert-to-condos">convert much of its space</a> into luxury condos. He&apos;s brought along Chef Nate Kuester of Aquavit and Chris Davino to help in the kitchen, where they&apos;ll turn out <strong>fall-centric dishes</strong> like Carrot Soup, with ginger, coconut and granny smith apple ($9); Pork Roulade with butternut squash, Brussels sprouts and maple jus ($14); and Pumpkin Mousse with candied cranberries &amp; walnut sable ($8). The meal will be accompanied by music by Lauren Henderson, Jesse Barnes and Bird Courage. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="110416meyers.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161107085906im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/110416meyers.jpg" width="640" height="426"> <br> <i> (Scott Lynch/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>New Nordic continues its reign as NYC&apos;s hottest culinary import, and nobody&apos;s doing more to rep the cuisine than Claus Meyer, the Noma founder who&apos;s been opening eateries left and right all year. Baked goods are a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161107085906/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/08/all_of_the_baked_goods_at_meyers_ba.php#photo-1">big part</a> of his newest ventures, and this Saturday we can all learn to bake some of his team&apos;s most delicious bready creations at <strong>baking classes</strong> launching this weekend. Led by Executive Pastry Chef Noah Carroll, the classes include &quot;step-by-step instructions through the milling, mixing, proofing and kneading processes&quot; and participants will even take home their own yeast starter for authentic recreation off-site. Attendees will be plied with coffee and carbs throughout. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161107085906/http://www.brownpapertickets.com/event/2620914">Tickets $95</a>.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161107085906/http://cravefishbar.com/">Crave Fishbar&apos;s</a> Upper West Side location recently debuted a <strong>new brunch program</strong>, available both Saturday and Sunday beginning at 11 a.m. On the menu: Crab and Gruyere Cheese Omelet with Roasted Onion and Fresh Radish, and a Belgian Waffle dish with Kimchi Butter, Fried Fish, and Homemade Sweet Chili Sauce. Or you can opt for a Green Smoothie, if you&apos;re a monster.</p>
