<p>Every year, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170326003856/http://luckyrice.com/">LUCKYRICE</a> throws a huge feast celebrating &quot;the best in Asian cuisine from Hong Kong to Hanoi.&quot; This year, the New York Grand Feast moves to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170326003856/http://industrycity.com/">Industry City</a>, with dozens of local restaurants offering samplings from their menus, from fine dining or street food-style.</p>

<p>Highlights from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170326003856/http://luckyrice.com/event/new-york-feast-4/">this year&apos;s Feast</a> include the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170326003856/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/01/chomp_chomp_singaporean_hawker_food.php">incredible Shrimp Paste Chicken Wings</a> from Chomp Chomp, Pork Belly Smiling Bao from East Wind Snack Shop, Pig&apos;s Ear Tonkatsu and Vegetarian Kimbaps from Insa, a selection of raw fish bowls from Pokeworks, and Bacon and Shrimp Dumplings from Talde. On the beverage side, Asahi Beer, a Silk Journey cocktail made with Bombay Sapphire East Gin and a bunch of other cocktail offerings from spots like Extra Fancy and Saxon and Parole.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170326003856/http://luckyrice.ticketfly.com/">Tickets</a> to this year&apos;s June 2nd event have been reduced from previous years, with a GA ticket just $88 for two hours of open feasting and booze. VIP tickets ($150) buy you an extra hour at the top of the show.</p>
