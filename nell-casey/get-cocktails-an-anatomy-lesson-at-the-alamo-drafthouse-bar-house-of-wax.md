					<p>When it <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170227235915/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/18/brooklyn_alamo_drafthouse.php">opens on Friday</a>, Brooklyn&apos;s shiny new Alamo Drafthouse will make a big splash in the NYC cinema scene&#x2014;and that&apos;s not even counting the theater&apos;s bar, which is bound to cause a sensation of its own. Modeled after panoptica attractions from the late 1800s, the House of Wax Museum &amp; Cocktails features detailed&#x2014;and often graphic&#x2014;recreations of death masks, diseased body parts, graphic birth models and other depictions. Be forewarned: the images are highly realistic and some are very graphic; click with caution!</p>

<p>&quot;These collections were of immense legitimate value to the burgeoning fields of both anthropological study and medical research. They were also instrumental in disease education and prevention,&quot; according to Alamo Drafthouse founder Tim League. &quot;Even though they were, for this reason, afforded a kind of middlebrow respectability, the reality was that deep down, [creators] the Castan Brothers were some of the world&apos;s first great showmen.&quot; </p>

<p>Put another way: &quot;I have never encountered so many vintage wax vulvas before,&quot; Gothamist&apos;s Scott Heins opined.</p>

<p>The more unsettling of the figures will be in the &quot;adult&quot; section behind a velvet curtain. That&apos;s where you&apos;ll encounter the genital abnormalities, birth abnormalities, STDs, if you&apos;re daring enough to pull back the curtain.</p>

<p>If you&apos;re not unsettled eating and drinking surrounded by viscera, the bar features a lengthy spirit and cocktail list, but their beer selection&apos;s truly impressive in its own right. The draft list is exclusively sourced from New York State, with over 48 types from the five boroughs, Long Island and points north. </p>

<p>Food&apos;s of a more basic variety that seems geared towards snacking rather than a proper meal. Go all-in on the cinema setting with Truffle Parmesan Popcorn ($9) or traditional bar snacks like the Chili Lime Cerveza Nuts ($6). Heartier options include a Brooklyn Dog ($13) with sauerkraut and a Margarita Flatbread ($15). </p>
