title: A Bounty Of Bivalves Await At Oyster Week, Which Kicks Off Tonight
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="082913oysters.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/082913oysters.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> <span class="photo_caption">(<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://www.flickr.com/photos/wallyg/5903725516/">wallyg</a>/Flickr)</span></i></div> <p></p>

<p>Our city&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/30/forget_sandbags_oysters_could_have.php">special relationship</a> with oysters gets a rightful weeklong celebration starting today. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://oysterweek.com/">New York Oyster Week</a> kicks off this evening, bringing a bounty of bivalve events that even <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://www.jabberwocky.com/carroll/walrus.html">the Walrus</a> would approve of. There are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://oysterweek.com/events/">quite a few events</a> available depending on your slurping capabilities and the size of your wallet; here are a few we&apos;re looking forward to.</p>

<p>Tonight&apos;s <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://www.londonlennies.com/shes-baaaack-pearl-the-oystergirl-oysterfest-2014-sept-16-27/">Oyster Fest</a></strong> opening event goes down at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://www.londonlennies.com/">London Lennie&apos;s</a>, where guests can compete in a Slurp Off for a chance to win $500 plus Buck-A-Shuck oyster specials where you&apos;ll get the slimy creatures for, of course, just $1. The fun continues through September 27th with live music, drink specials and an oyster-packed menu of Po&apos; Boys, stew, fried oysters and lots more.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-right"> <img alt="092013oysterfest.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/092013oysterfest.jpg" width="320" height="213"> <br> <i style=" width:320px; ;display:block"> <span class="photo_caption">(<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://www.flickr.com/photos/wallyg/5091629782/">wallyg</a>/Flickr)</span></i></div> The week&apos;s biggest event goes down tomorrow at the <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://oysterweek.com/stone-street-oyster-festival">Stone Street Oyster Festival</a></strong>, the largest outdoor oyster festival in Manhattan that&apos;s now celebrating its 10th year. Organizers are expecting 10,000 bivalve fanatics to descend on the Financial District street eager to slurp up the nearly 35,000 oysters being shucked by restaurants in the area. They&apos;ll also be attempting to beat the Guinness Book of World Record for the Most Oyster Shell Recovered from a Single Day Oyster Event so...come hungry!<p></p>

<p>Hudson Terrace hosts <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://oysterweek.com/empire-oyster/">Empire Oyster</a></strong> on Wednesday, September 25th, an evening dedicated to NY Oysters, Spirits, Beers, Wines and other foods native to the state. Blue Point Brewing plans to unveil their new Oyster Stout, which contains actual Blue Island Oysters plus Great South Bay Salt. Also on hand, 4-8 varieties of NY oysters paired with a similar amount of carefully selected booze. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-left"> <img alt="092013oysters2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/nyc_nellcasey/092013oysters2.jpg" width="320" height="240"> <br> <i style=" width:320px; ;display:block"> <span class="photo_caption">(<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://www.flickr.com/photos/scottlynchnyc/8990270489/">Scoboco</a>/Flickr)</span></i></div> Brooklyn has been left out of previous oyster week festivities, but organizers are remedying that this year with <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://oysterweek.com/brooklyn-on-the-half-shell">Brooklyn on the Half Shell</a></strong>, which takes over the rooftop at 172 Sterling Place for an all-you-can-eat raw oyster extravaganza on Thursday, September 26th. Queens Brewery provides suds for sipping, Ripe Life Wines brings bottles of their Clambake Chardonnay and you can also munch on oyster po&apos;boys to offset all the alcohol. A <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://oysterweek.com/brooklyn-on-the-half-shell">$50 donation</a> is suggested with 100% of proceeds going to The New York Harbor School, a small public high school on Governors Island.<p></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511122939/http://oysterweek.com/restaurants/">Area restaurants</a> are participating as well, including the Grand Central Oyster Bar, The John Dory Oyster Bar, Blue Water Grill and other seafood-centric spots. It&apos;s here you&apos;ll find dishes like Champagne Poached Oysters (Aureole), Oysters &quot;Escargot&quot; (City Crab and Seafood Company) and Fried Oyster Po&apos; Boy with Cajun Aioli (Stone Street Tavern) plus plenty of oysters on the half shell and other oyster-focused options. </p>
