					<p>Since <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171029212629/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/02/inside_the_dead_rabbit_now_serving.php#photo-1">opening over three years ago</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171029212629/http://www.deadrabbitnyc.com/">The Dead Rabbit</a> has won just about every prestigious award revolving around cocktails, including being named the World&#x2019;s Best Bar by Tales of the Cocktail in 2015. But &quot;international barman&quot; Sean Muldoon and Jack McGarry&#x2014;who both hail from The Merchant Hotel in Belfast, itself the winner of World&#x2019;s Best Cocktail Bar at Tales of the Cocktail in 2010&#x2014;aren&apos;t ones to rest on their laurels, opening up their new cocktail haven <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171029212629/http://www.blacktailnyc.com/">BlackTail</a> this week in Battery Park. </p>

<p>In reality, they haven&apos;t traversed much geographically from their first NYC home on Water Street, setting up in a second floor space inside Pier A, the beautiful landmark that has been a Harbor Police port, a VIP entry for Ellis Island and an FDNY station since being erected in 1886. Spiritually, however, we&apos;re meant to be transported hundreds of miles away to balmy Cuba, like the many New Yorkers who actually escaped there via plane during Prohibition. Think of it as a baby step before you can <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171029212629/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/15/cuba_travel_guide_2015.php#photo-1">make it to Havana yourself</a>.</p>

<p>Sink into a soft leather couch to peruse the drink list&#x2014;well, more like <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171029212629/https://www.instagram.com/p/BHVDwPHBY9d/?taken-by=blacktailnyc">book</a>&#x2014;which spans five categories, each boasting eight different interpretations for the glass. Get an education in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171029212629/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/21/best_punch_nyc.php">punches</a> or sours or Old Fashioneds, or all of the above. With 40 different options, it&apos;ll just take you a bit to work your way through the entire catalogue.</p>

<p>Luckily, there&apos;s food to fortify you along the way. The era-appropriate food offerings lean heavily on seafood and other ingredients that would&apos;ve been common in Cuba during the &apos;20s. There&apos;s an interesting high/low balance at play here, where a Sloppy Joe ($18) shares an equal place of honor near a Traditional Caviar Service ($75). </p>

<p><em>Pier A Harbor House, 22 Battery Place, (212) 785-0153; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171029212629/http://www.blacktailnyc.com/">blacktailnyc.com</a></em></p>
