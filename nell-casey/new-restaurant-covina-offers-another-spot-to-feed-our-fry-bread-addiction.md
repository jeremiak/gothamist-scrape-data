					<p>We&apos;re fully on board with a world in which fry bread <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160801190548/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/04/nix_vegetarian_greenwich_village.php#photo-1">becomes a trend</a>, so it&apos;s delightful to have another entry with which to support our growing addiction. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160801190548/http://www.covinanyc.com/">Covina</a>, which opened inside the Park South Hotel, is serving a version of the decadent dish topped with smoked salmon and a ranch sauce made with kefir, the fermented milk that&apos;ll lend a pleasing tanginess, pushed further by sliced red onion, capers, citrus zest and fresh herbs. The fresh-and-fried pairing is key here.</p>

<p>The restaurant, run by Tim and Nancy Cushman, who are also behind lauded Japanese restaurant O Ya, presents itself as American with Mediterranean influences. Shared plates of Fresh Sardines ($12) with pickled and roasted red peppers and fennel and Salmon Tartare ($15) with black olive oil speak to coastal palates. There&apos;s a heavy Italian bent to many dishes, too, most clearly identified in the house-made pastas and pizzas chef Tim will be nightly, as well as another on-trend item: Spring Asparagus &quot;Cacio E Pepe ($12), the black pepper and cheese style from Rome.</p>

<p>For now, it&apos;s a dinner-only operation, with a full bar of wines, beers and &quot;Before Dinner&quot; cocktails by Beverage Director Ted Kilpatrick. Soon, they&apos;ll also open Covina Cafe, an all-day takeout spot serving sandwiches, coffees and pastries by their pastry chef, Deanie Hickox. At night, it&apos;ll be integrated into the dining room, which also includes a large and stately bar.</p>

<p><em>127 East 27th Street, (212) 204-0225; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160801190548/http://www.covinanyc.com/">covinanyc.com</a></em></p>
