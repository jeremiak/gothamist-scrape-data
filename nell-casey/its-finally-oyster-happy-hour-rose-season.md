<p>Greenwich village newcomer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501204623/http://www.villanellenyc.com/">Villanelle</a> offers two new reasons to stop by: lunch and an oyster happy hour. During the day the restaurant will serve dishes including Burrata with celery, unripe strawberry and husk tomato ($16) and a Deep Fried Egg with eggplant dashi, black trumpet and chive ($16). Then from 5:30 to 7 p.m., score six Glidden Point oysters and a drink for $15 Monday through Friday.</p>

<p>Elsewhere in happy hour land, Hell&apos;s Kitchen&apos;s new Greek spot <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501204623/http://www.ousianyc.com/">Ousia</a> is doing a &quot;5 to 7 on 57th&quot; menu between those hours that includes specials on drinks and bar snacks. Glasses of Greek wine are $7, as are cocktails including the Ousia Mule with cardamom-infused vodka, Aperol, lime and ginger beer. Draft beers will run you just $5 and bar snacks including citrus marinated Greek olives and a classic spanikopita spinach and feta pie are $7.  </p>

<p>Some wine experts might argue there&apos;s no &quot;right&quot; or &quot;wrong&quot; season to drink a particular type of vino, but there&apos;s no doubting that ros&#xE9; hits its peak during the warmer months. To mark the arrival of short sleeves season, all four locations of wine bar <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501204623/http://vsvwinebars.com/">Vin Sur Vingt</a> will be cutting the price of ros&#xE9; bottles in half during the week until 7 p.m. The bar stocks wines from all over France, including vintages from Provence and Sancerre. </p>

<p>On Thursday, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501204623/http://www.mofad.org/">Museum of Food and Drink</a> explores the relationship between insects and Mexican cuisine by throwing a <strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501204623/http://www.mofad.org/events/2017/5/4/mezcal-and-the-taste-of-oaxaca">Mezcal and the Taste of Oaxaca</a></strong> event. Beginning at 6:30 p.m., the Museum, along with the Mexican Cultural Institute, will serve a variety of Oaxacan food and beverages while discussing the production of mezcal and the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501204623/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mezcal_worm">role of insects</a> in crafting the spirit. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501204623/https://mofad.secure.force.com/ticket/?_ga=1.97162845.37778253.1493647998#sections_a0F50000008AmwfEAC">Tickets $60</a>.</p>
