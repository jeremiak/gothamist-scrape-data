					<p>Quality crust is the foundation of any good pizza, which is why many pizza-making enterprises <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016155244/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/31/bruno_pizza_east_village.php">put a lot of effort</a> into special flour blends and techniques. And while choosing your own toppings isn&apos;t unusual, a two month old Flatiron eatery called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016155244/http://www.pnpizzanyc.com/">PN Wood Fired Pizza</a> is flipping the model, letting guests choose the dough and style to be topped with one of eight toppings combinations. </p>

<p>The eight different flour varieties come from Molino Grassi USA, the flour company helmed by managing partner Giacomo Baldi. Some are blends of a few kinds of grains and others are singular, but each has a distinctive flavor profile that&apos;s outlined on the menu. Regardless of the variety, each are aged for a minimum of 96 hours, which &quot;eases digestion,&quot; according to the restaurant.</p>

<p>Flour types include Kronos, made from organic durham wheat semolina; Montana, a blend of organic Canadian wheat flour with rye and spelt; and Miracolo, an ancient heritage grain from Parma, Italy. Classic margherita and marinara toppings are available, as well as Porcini with pine nuts and pork jowl and a Tartufo with artichokes, black truffle, mozzarella, pancetta and raw Tuscan sheep milk cheese. </p>

<p><em>2 West 28th Street, (844) 874-5864; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016155244/http://www.pnpizzanyc.com/">pnpizzanyc.com</a></em></p>
