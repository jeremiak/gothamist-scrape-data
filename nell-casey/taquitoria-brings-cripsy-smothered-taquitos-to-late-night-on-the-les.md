                            <p>New Yorkers no longer have to make <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130926063944/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/21/i_give_up_east_villager_resigns_him.php">clandestine trips to 7-Eleven</a> to get their late-night taquito fix. A trio of Marc Forgione vets have teamed up to bring San Diego&apos;s beloved street food to the Lower East Side, opening <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130926063944/http://www.taquitoria.com/taquitoria.html">Taquitoria</a> on Ludlow just south of Houston. For those who haven&apos;t been ensnared in taquito rapture, imagine rolled-up tacos that get a quick trip to the deep fryer before being slathered in salsas, cheese and anything else the heart could want. </p>

<p>The gang fill their versions with organic meats like Bell &amp; Even&apos;s Chicken, Creekstone Farm&apos;s Beef or black beans, then lets customers decide if they want it Classic or Cheesy. Classic calls for a guacamole sauce, shredded lettuce and a sprinkling of salty cotija cheese; Cheesy means the fried rolls of delight are smothered in nacho cheese, sour cream and a piquant pickled jalapeno relish. Each taquito order also comes with &quot;Pancho&apos;s&quot; red sauce, as tradition dictates. Feeling daring? Ask for your taquito &quot;Chronic Style&quot; to get every single topping on the menu for an extra $3.</p>

<p>These fried puppies might not be health food but they&apos;re definitely healthy for your wallet: three taquitos run you just $5 and five of them just $8. </p>

<p><em>168 Ludlow Street, (212) 780-0121; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130926063944/http://www.taquitoria.com/">taquitoria.com</a>. Tuesday through Sunday 4:00 p.m. - 2:00 a.m.</em></p>
