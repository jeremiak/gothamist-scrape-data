					<p>Technically, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025133358/http://www.fairweatherbushwick.com/">Fair Weather Bushwick</a> has been slinging coffee on Wyckoff Avenue in Bushwick for about two years. But now owner Ebru Brun is shifting gears in a big way, transforming a daytime caffeine destination into an elegant nighttime eatery with a tasting menu executed by chef and partner John Creger. For a section of the neighborhood dominated by chain restaurants and bodegas, this is a big change of identity.</p>

<p>Tuesdays through Thursdays (the restaurant is closed on Mondays), Creger will serve a 10-course tasting menu with one nightly seating for 22 guests at 8 p.m. There&apos;s no set menu, as dishes will change weekly, but the opening menu included &quot;deconstructed&quot; Cold Spring River Clam Chowder&#x2014;a grilled clam served in its shell with potato puree, bacon, leeks and trout roe&#x2014;and Satur Farms beet ravioli with pesto ricotta and spiced pumpkin seeds. White Castle Crave Cases these are not.</p>

<p>Creger&apos;s got experience behind the line at some pretty gourmet establishments, including stints at Le Cirque, Employees Only and Gallow Green; not bad for someone who&apos;s self-taught. The tasting menu runs $65 per person, certainly on the more affordable spectrum of similar offerings; for context, Roberta&apos;s-run Blanca <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025133358/http://www.blancanyc.com/tastings/index.html">charges $195 for their</a>, which, in fairness, is about twice the length. There&apos;s also the option for a $30 beer pairing.</p>

<p>Fridays and Saturdays are geared to a more casual affair, with an a la carte menu in place of the formal tasting menu. Small plates include Quinoa Fritters ($12) with saffron aioli, anchovies and pickled onion and Grilled Oysters ($8) with blood orange, trout roe and nori. Entrees are rib-sticking like the Potato Gnocchi ($16) with mushrooms, black garlic, meyer lemon and gremolata and a Scallops dish ($19) with parsnips two ways (pureed and made into chips), white balsamic and coconut.</p>

<p><em>274 Wyckoff Avenue, 347-305-3736; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025133358/http://www.fairweatherbushwick.com/">weather</a></em></p>
