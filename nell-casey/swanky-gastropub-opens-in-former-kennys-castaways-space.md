</div></div>					<p>Grungy Bleecker Street music venue Kenny&apos;s Castaways, which <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504204115/http://ny.eater.com/archives/2012/10/legendary_kennys_castaways_will_close_tonight_forever.php">closed in 2012</a>, has been replaced by a swanky new wine bar and gastropub by the owners of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504204115/http://gothamist.com/2011/03/03/new_restaurant_and_bar_radar_40.php#photo-4">Osteria Cotta</a> on the Upper West Side. The venue, called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504204115/http://www.carrollplacenyc.com/">Carroll Place</a>, offers American and Italian standards like meat and cheese boards and fancy pizzas plus $12 cocktails, a far cry from the PBR cans and rail whiskey shots ingested by the space&apos;s former clientele. </p>

<p>The new owners didn&apos;t gut the place completely, opting to reuse as much of the bar&apos;s old beams, tables and floorings as they could. That said, Kenny&apos;s regulars won&apos;t recognize the space at all. The 20-seat bar downstairs overlooks a custom wood stove where they&apos;ll fire up the pizzas and some other menu items; a communal woodblock table and a few high tops offer additional seating for eating or drinking. </p>

<p>Upstairs you&apos;ll find the &quot;Living Room,&quot; which has been decked out with upholstered chairs, couches and banquettes, area rugs and even a chandelier. Mercifully, one no longer has to traverse the stage area to use the restroom. Steven Tyler <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504204115/http://www.nytimes.com/2012/10/01/nyregion/kennys-castaways-greenwich-village-music-club-is-closing.html">wouldn&apos;t know what hit him</a>.</p>

<p>Pizzas include the Tartufo ($12.50) with speck, truffle cream, button mushrooms, fresh mozzarella and truffle oil and the Salame ($13.50) with spicy salame, black olives, tomato sauce and fresh mozzarella. They&apos;re doing things like the Burrata Burger ($16) with grilled tomato and prosciutto and a Jamon Serrano focaccia ($13.50) plus larger format entrees including Housemade Ravioli ($12.50) with tomato, eggplant, ricotta and parmigiano and Grilled Salmon ($18.50) with sauteed spinach and eggplant caponata. Twenty wines are offered by the glass ($8 - 13), half carafe or full carafe, plus beers &quot;by barrel&quot; ($7)&#x2014;including Italian options Peroni and Moretti&#x2014;bottle and can. </p>

<p><em>157 Bleecker Street, (212) 260-1700; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504204115/http://www.carrollplacenyc.com/">carrollplacenyc.com</a></em></p>
