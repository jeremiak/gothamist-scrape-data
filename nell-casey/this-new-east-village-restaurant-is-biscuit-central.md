<p>With <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203150514/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/31/24-hour_biscuit_emporium_services_y.php">Empire Biscuit</a> seemingly gone forever, a new restaurant called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203150514/http://www.post-nyc.com/">Post</a> has taken up the biscuit-making mantle in the East Village, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161203150514/http://evgrieve.com/2016/11/post-new-cafe-opens-on-avenue-b.html">opening recently</a> for breakfast (and breakfast for lunch) on Avenue B. The railroad-style restaurant&apos;s a collaboration between brotheres Michael Stackleather and Bobby Stackleather along with Franchesca Green, with the former Stackleather telling us they&apos;re aiming for American Southern influences with Asian twists &quot;here and there.&quot; For now, though, it&apos;s all about biscuits.</p>

<p>Beginning at 7 a.m. and running til 4 p.m., the tiny eatery bakes up biscuits and biscuit sandwiches from their &quot;Aunt Merle&apos;s recipe.&quot; The square offerings can be stuffed with ham and Emmental cheese with mustard and pickles or a classic sausage, egg and cheese offering (both $10). For vegetarians, there&apos;s an Avocado Biscuit with egg, avocado, tomato and parmesan, plus a non-biscuit homemade granola with greek yogurt.</p>

Stackleather says proper lunch and dinner are coming soon, with salads and burgers at the midday meal and a small menu of entrees for after dark. &quot;There will be lots of steamed veggies and gluten free/vegan options. Meat as well,&quot; he says. &quot;A banging meatloaf. I hope! Still testing.&quot; 
<br><br>
Regardless of what&apos;s on the menu, it&apos;s organic and humaley-raised whenever possible, with much of their ingredients sourced from smaller, local farms.
