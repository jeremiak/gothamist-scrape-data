					<p>Tonight, Prospect Heights gains <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170810152646/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/03/el_atorradero_nachos_ftw.php#photo-1">another</a> fancy-ish Mexican spot with the opening of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170810152646/https://www.altacalidadbk.com/">Alta Calidad</a>, Chef Akhtar Nawab&apos;s cheery new Vanderbilt Avenue restaurant. The 39-seat eatery is decked out in black, white and blue motifs, with a black-and-white mural by Marysia Schultz.</p>

<p>Those expecting tacos will be well-served, with fillings including charred steak ($12), braised pork cheeks ($18) and fried shrimp ($12). Fried tortillas and fried plantains service guacamole and two types of queso fundido, respectively, including one molten cheese dish topped with artichokes, chorizo and poblano peppers (&quot;complicado,&quot; $12).</p>

<p>Smaller botanas look like good pairings for when the restaurant eventually gets its liquor license (mocktails only for now). The tamale ($9) apparently shares DNA with both Mexican tamales and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170810152646/https://www.google.com/search?q=pommes+aligot&amp;espv=2&amp;biw=1685&amp;bih=957&amp;tbm=isch&amp;source=lnms&amp;sa=X&amp;ved=0ahUKEwiN8OXs4bXSAhWI7oMKHS1PCDwQ_AUIBigB&amp;dpr=1#imgrc=fsRxdTVyEtEpEM:">pommes alligot</a>&#x2014;the decadent cheesy potato dish from France&#x2014;and is served open-faced with broccoli and bacon. </p>

<p><em>552 Vanderbilt Avenue, 718-622-1111; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170810152646/https://www.altacalidadbk.com/">altacalidadbk.com</a></em></p>
