<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_06_ricklazio.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130511140510im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/NYC_Billy/2009_06_ricklazio.jpg" width="130" height="129" class="image-right"> </span>Rick Lazio, best known for losing against Hillary Clinton in 2000 when she ran for Senate, has officially thrown his hat into the 2010 New York gubernatorial ring. After a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130511140510/http://gothamist.com/2009/06/25/rick_lazio_intending_to_run_for_gov.php">few months of speculation</a>, he <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130511140510/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/lazio_to_announce_run_for_governor_XjIm6j2g27ipbTwyFCWpCP">sent a letter to supporters</a> this weekend, &quot;The present government in Albany is broken. It is a national embarrassment. It can&#x2019;t be fixed by tweaking it here and there. It is dysfunctional and we need to start all over from the beginning.&quot; Of course, the 800-pound gorilla in the Republican sphere is Rudy Giuliani, but there&apos;s some rumor he might be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130511140510/http://gothamist.com/2009/09/14/will_ny_gop_go_for_senator_giuliani.php">better suited running against Kirsten Gillibrand</a>.  When <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130511140510/http://www.politickerny.com/5297/paterson-welcomes-lazio-will-support-democrat">PolitickerNY asked</a> Governor Paterson what he thought of Lazio&apos;s announcement, he said, &quot;<strong>Since I&apos;m not seeking the Republican nomination, and this is a democracy, I would say that&apos;s a good idea.</strong>&quot;</p>
