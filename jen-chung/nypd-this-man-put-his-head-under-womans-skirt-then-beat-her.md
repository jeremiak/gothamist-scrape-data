										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013750/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_09_84fts1.jpg"> <img alt="2014_09_84fts1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013750im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2014/09/2014_09_84fts1-thumb-300x649-859118.jpg" width="180" height="389" class="image-right"> </a> </span>The police are trying to find a man who is accused of forcibly touching a woman at a Brooklyn subway station last month.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, on August 8, at 10 p.m., a 32-year-old woman was walking up the stairs at the Dekalb Avenue D and M subway station &quot;when the suspect put his head under her skirt.&quot; </p>

<p>The victim tried to push him away, but police say that he &quot;threw her down the stairs and began punching and kicking her&quot; before fleeing. The victim was not hospitalized, but she did suffer bruises. </p>

<p>Police released a photo of the suspect and described him as being about 60 years old, about 5&apos;8&quot; and 160 pounds with brown eyes. </p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call Crime stoppers at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477). The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013750/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.<br>

All calls are strictly confidential.</p>
