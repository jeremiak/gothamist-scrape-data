<p>The police are looking for a gunman who walked up to a man standing outside a Brooklyn store and then shot him in the face. </p>

<p>According to the NYPD, at 8:16 p.m. on Sunday, July 27th, the 41-year-old victim was standing outside 653 Stanley Avenue in East New York, at the corner Quick Mart. His dog <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510015318/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Brooklyn-East-New-York-Store-Face-Shooting-Dog-Leash-269013601.html">was next to him</a>. The police say the suspect approached him, &quot;displayed a firearm and shot him one time in the face, before fleeing the scene.&quot;</p>

<p>The victim is in critical condition. Police released video of the shooting and the suspect.</p>

The suspect is described as being about 6&apos;3&quot; tall, 230 pounds, with brown eyes and close cut black hair. He was last seen wearing a white tee shirt and blue jeans.<br>
