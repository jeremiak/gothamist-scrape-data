</div></div><p>Jonah Hill&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120312064213/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/24/the_2012_oscar_nominations_are_in.php">Oscar-nominated actor</a>&#x2014;hosted Saturday Night Live last night for the second time, and he addressed his good fortune during his monologue. And actual Oscar winner and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120312064213/http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0000158/filmoseries#tt0072562">eight-time SNL host</a> Tom Hanks stopped by to explain how tough life was after winning. Twice.</p>

<p>The cold open featured Taram Killan as Rush Limbaugh, who bragged about new advertisers like the Syrian Tourism Board, and other highlights included Bill Hader&apos;s Stefon giving spring tips (and Hader broke down, cracking up), Kristen Wiig as Paula Deen, Hill reprising the bit about the 6-year-old going to Benihana&apos;s with his dad, and a weirdly fun sketch about Coolio, which is not online.  Other bits included Andy Samberg&apos;s attempt to play Sarah Palin, a scientist who had sex with a monkey to get it to talk and Liza Minnelli turning on a lamp.  The Shins were the musical guest.</p>

<p>The next new episode will be on April 7, with host Sofia Vergara and musical guest British-Irish boy band One Direction (this is their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120312064213/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QJO3ROT-A4E">hit</a>).  </p>
