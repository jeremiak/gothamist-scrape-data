					<p>The United Kingdom&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024630/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/24/uks_brexit_vote_to_leave_european_u.php">national vote to leave the European Union</a> battered financial markets today. The British pound had fallen to a low of $1.3229&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024630/http://www.bbc.com/news/business-36611512">it was last at this level in 1985</a>&#x2014;and the Dow Jones plummeted by 611 points. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024630/http://www.cnbc.com/2016/06/24/us-markets.html">CNBC reports</a> it was the Dow&apos;s &quot;eighth-largest point loss ever &#x2014; with Goldman Sachs contributing the most to declines. On a percentage basis the Dow and S&amp;P; had their worst day since August 2015, while the Nasdaq composite&apos;s 4.1 percent decline was the index&apos;s worst since August 2011.&quot;</p>

<p>Chris Semenuk, manager of the TIAA-CREF International Equity Fund, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024630/http://www.wsj.com/articles/pound-plunges-on-u-k-vote-to-leave-eu-1466753711">told the Wall Street Journal</a>, &quot;We haven&#x2019;t had what I would say is a crash, but we&#x2019;ve given back gains we&#x2019;d taken months to make.&quot; </p>

<p>British and European <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024630/http://www.cnbc.com/2016/06/24/barclays-nosedives-with-european-banks-in-the-wake-of-a-brexit.html">banks lost value</a>&#x2014;Barclays closed down 17%, while Royal Bank of Scotland fell 27%. A British fund manager <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024630/http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2016-06-23/pound-surge-builds-as-polls-show-u-k-to-remain-in-eu-yen-slips">told Bloomberg News</a>, &quot;The central banks are saying all the right things and they&#x2019;re flooding the market with liquidity so you have a more orderly move. You may be seeing some tactical rotation, the U.S. looks more solid now at least until the U.S. election.&quot;</p>

<p>Yeah, about that election: One economist <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024630/http://www.nytimes.com/2016/06/25/business/international/brexit-financial-economic-impact-leave.html?action=click&amp;pgtype=Homepage&amp;clickSource=story-heading&amp;module=span-abc-region&amp;region=span-abc-region&amp;WT.nav=span-abc-region">spoke to the Times</a>, explaining that the results from Brexit are &quot;essentially against economic rationality and driven by identity concerns and unease about globalization and trade. If such a vote can win in the U.K., that fosters among investors a sense of the likelihood that Trump can also win, and that will have an adverse effect on capital markets.&quot;</p>
