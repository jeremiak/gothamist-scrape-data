					<p>The Loews&apos; Jersey Theater is a glorious throwback to the movie palaces of yore, and Jersey City&apos;s mayor hopes that a restoration of the 85-year-old space will help revitalize the surrounding area. Today, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324052309/http://www.nj.com/hudson/index.ssf/2014/05/worlds_biggest_concert_promoters_seek_to_restore_classic_loews_in_jersey_city.html#incart_river_default">Jersey Journal reports</a> that there are four bidders competing to restore and manage the theater&#x2014;including the team behind the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324052309/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/24/more_loews_kings_theater.php#photo-1">restoration of the Loews&apos; Kings Theater in Brooklyn</a>.</p>

<p>According to the Jersey Journal, &quot;Live Nation and A.E.G. Live, the world&#x2019;s biggest concert promoters&quot; as well as &quot;Ace Theatrical Group, which is restoring the Loew&apos;s Kings Theater in Brooklyn, and Catch Holdings&quot; are in the hunt. Additionally: &quot;Each bidder plans to partner with smaller groups - such as Newark&apos;s New Jersey Performance Arts Center, Mana Fine Arts in Jersey City and New Jersey City University - to create a community programming component.&quot;</p>

<p>Jersey City&apos;s RFP had these requirements:</p><blockquote>1) At least thirty (30) community/local performances/events (sponsored and/or <br>
produced by local community groups or other City residents), which include but are <br>
not limited to school/youth programs, programs for senior citizens, performances <br>
from local artists, lectures or other academic programs, or cultural celebrations <br>
 <br>
2) At least twenty (20) musical performances by nationally recognized artists <br>
 <br>
3) At least ten (10) theatrical or other stage performances <br>
 <br>
4) At least ten (10) comedy performances <br>
 <br>
5) At least twenty (20) other performances/events (may include event types listed <br>
above) <br>
 <br>
6) Allowance for twenty (20) events to be sponsored and produced privately by the <br>
Friends of the Loew&#x2019;s Inc. community group <br>
 <br>
7) Allowance for private event booking only during times in which the facility is not <br>
reserved for any of the types of events listed above</blockquote>Jersey City Mayor Steve Fulop said, &quot;The Loew&#x2019;s Theatre will be the centerpiece of the revitalization of Journal Square. A world class performance venue will not only bring life and culture back to Journal Square, but will be a magnet for additional development and tourism.&quot;<p></p>

<p>Back in 1974, the theater&#x2014;subdivided into many theaters&#x2014;had fallen into disrepair and, by 1986, it was scheduled for demolition. The non-profit group <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324052309/http://friendsofloews.com/q-a/">Friends of Loew&apos;s rallied to save it</a> and has been putting on local shows; the group is suing Jersey City, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324052309/http://www.nj.com/hudson/index.ssf/2014/03/landmark_loews_group_sues_jersey_city_claiming_breach_of_lease.html">claiming its lease has been breached</a>.</p>
