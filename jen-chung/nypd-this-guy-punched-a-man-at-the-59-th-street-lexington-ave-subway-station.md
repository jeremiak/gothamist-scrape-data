---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2017_02_trainaslt.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307101445im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2017_02_trainaslt.jpg" width="300" height="473" class="image-right"> </span>Police are hoping the public can help them identify a man accused of socking a subway rider at a Manhattan station last year.</p>

<p>The assault occurred on December 15th, 2016, around 3:10 p.m. According to the NYPD, a 29-year-old man on the northbound 4/5 platform got into a verbal dispute with the suspect, which culminated with the victim getting punched in the face. The assailant then fled the platform on a northbound 4 train. </p>

<p>The victim was treated for a laceration to his mouth at NY Presbyterian Hospital.</p>

<p>Investigators released an image of the man they&apos;re looking for, describing him as being about 35-40 years old, 6&apos; tall and 200 pounds. He was last seen wearing a brown jacket. <br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477) or for Spanish, 1-888-57-PISTA (74782).  </p>

<p>The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307101445/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
