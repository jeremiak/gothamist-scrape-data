<p>The NYPD Queens Special Victims Squad is investigating a reported rape that occurred yesterday morning in south of Kissena Park.</p>

<p>The rape took place at 6:22 a.m., according to the NYPD, who say that police officers responded to a 911 call about the incident at 10:45 a.m. Police say the victim told them she was walking along the Horace Harding Expressway near 168th Street hours earlier &quot;when an unidentified male grabbed her from behind, threw her to the ground and sexually assaulted her before fleeing northbound on Horace Harding Expressway.&quot; </p>

<p>She was reportedly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021138/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Woman-Rape-Walking-Work-Queens-Police-Investigation-Attack-Horace-Harding-Expressway-397286871.html">on her way to work</a>.</p>

<p>The authorities described the suspect as a black male in his 40s, about 6&apos; and 180 pounds, with a slim build, clean shaven face and short hair, last seen wearing a black long sleeve shirt and blue jeans.</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477) or for Spanish, 1-888-57-PISTA (74782). All calls are strictly confidential.<br>
