<p>Disgraced Twitter user<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://gothamist.com/tags/anthonyweiner"> Anthony Weiner</a> is getting some nice news: Even though he&apos;s only been running for mayor of <strike><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://gothamist.com/2013/05/23/anthony_weiners_campaign_website_fe.php">Pittsburgh</a></strike> New York City <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://capitalnewyork.com/article/politics/2013/05/8530305/scenes-anthony-weiner-show?top-featured-image">for a week</a>, he&apos;s now placing second in a Democratic poll, and appears to be gaining on frontrunner Christine Quinn.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/look_over_your_shoulder_quinn_UBgBxyd1j5sq8vzO254efO?utm_medium=rss&amp;utm_content=Local">According to a Marist College/WNBC poll</a>,  City Council Speaker Quinn has 24%, while Weiner has 19%. After that, Public Advocate Bill de Blasio has 12%, Bill Thompson has 11% and Comptroller John Liu is at 11% (Sal Albanese and Erik Salgado have 1% each; 23% of voters are undecided). Perhaps more interesting is that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/weiner-wins-male-voters-poll-article-1.1357005?localLinksEnabled=false">Democratic men prefer Weiner to Quinn</a>.</p>

<p>Other Weiner news: He says <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://www.nydailynews.com/news/election/weiner-ll-fight-cuomo-elected-mayor-article-1.1357117?localLinks">he&apos;ll fight Governor Cuomo</a>, he&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/take_cover_WqS45SX3mHD03Qp3U8f1mN?utm_medium=rss&amp;utm_content=Local">looking for unpaid interns</a> and he&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://politicker.com/2013/05/anthony-weiners-wise-cracking-continues-in-first-mayoral-debate/">loving being back in the spotlight</a>! At an education forum that Quinn opted out of, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415082737/http://capitalnewyork.com/article/politics/2013/05/8530450/anthony-weiner-stands-quinnless-debate?--bucket-headline">Capital New York reports</a>, &quot;He stood up to answer questions while his opponents remained seated, wore a blue shirt with rolled-up sleeves while they all wore dark suit jackets and ties, and said he wouldn&apos;t provide special funding for arts instruction in public schools, while all his rivals said they would.&quot;</p>
