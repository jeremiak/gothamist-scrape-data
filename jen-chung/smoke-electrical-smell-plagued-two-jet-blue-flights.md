title: Smoke, Electrical Smell Plagued Two JetBlue Flights
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_09_jetbl.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509165331im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_09_jetbl.jpg" width="140" height="94" class="image-right"> </span>According to the Post, two JetBlue flights had <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509165331/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/regional/jetblue_smoking_signs_on_oW7wmk3AyUkush4MvMwBoI">issues after take-off from JFK Airport</a> last week, requiring both to return to the airport. On Monday, &quot;smoke seeped into the cabin of JetBlue Flight 654, which was bound for Rochester, NY, and carrying 40 passengers, just after takeoff.&quot; The pilot told air-traffic control, &quot;We need to return immediately. We have some smoke in the cabin,&quot; before &quot;briefly&quot; losing contact but ultimately made it back safe. The FAA found no cause for the smoke.  Then on Friday, &quot;an electrical smell filled JetBlue Flight 63, bound for Tampa and carrying 63 passengers, just after it took off&quot;; the plane managed to land safely. And a JetBlue flight landed in Bahamas on Thursday just as a fire was spotted on the left wing. The Post says all planes were the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509165331/http://www.jetblue.com/about/whyyoulllike/about_whyembraer.html">Embraer 1910 model</a> and have been taken out of service as JetBlue investigates.</p>
