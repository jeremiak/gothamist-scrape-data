title: Richard Meier Gloomy on Real Estate
---

<p><img alt="2008_10_perryst.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629170447im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_10_perryst.jpg" width="120" height="111" class="right">Architect Richard Meier <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629170447/http://nymag.com/news/intelligencer/51560/">told NY magazine</a>, when asked about the real estate market in the next year or so, &quot;I think there will be a lot of empty apartments.&quot; Meier&apos;s NYC designs include the Perry Street towers and a big building at Brooklyn&apos;s Grand Army Plaza, which he says is 60% sold, but &quot;the next 40% will go slowly.&quot;  Luckily, he pointed out, &quot;I&apos;m just the architect.&quot; Meier previously <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629170447/http://www.observer.com/2008/o2/richard-meier-real-estate-market-fortunately-im-just-architect">told the Observer</a>, back in September (the day Lehman Brothers declared bankruptcy), &quot;I don&apos;t know how to deal with it or what it means.  Certainly, it&apos;s going to have a serious effect on my work.&quot;</p>
