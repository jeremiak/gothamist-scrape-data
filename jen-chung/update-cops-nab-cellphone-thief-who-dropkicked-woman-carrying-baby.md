<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-right"> <img alt="2014_11_susp1040.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510011354im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_11_susp1040.jpg" width="200" height="250"> <br> <i style=" width:200px; ;display:block"> Alonzo Brown (DCPI)</i></div> Police are looking for an 18-year-old who was caught on video assaulting a woman&#x2014;who was carrying a three-month-old baby&#x2014;in order to steal her phone. <b>Update below</b>: Police have made an arrest.<p></p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the robbery took place on Friday, November 28, 2014 at 12:45 p.m., in front of 1155 East Tremont Avenue. The suspect, who police identify as Alonzo Brown, apparently targeted the 24-year-old woman, who had the baby strapped her to chest. </p>

<p>Police say he then approached her and &quot;kicked the woman causing her and the child to fall to the ground. Brown took the woman&apos;s cell phone and fled the location on foot.&quot;</p>

<p>The woman and baby were taken to Jacobi Hospital for treatment and were released. </p>

<p>Anyone with information is asked to call Crime Stoppers at (800) 577-TIPS (8477). The public can also submit their tips by logging onto Crime Stoppers&apos; website at www.nypdcrimestoppers.com or by texting their tips to CRIMES (274637), then enter TIP577.</p>

<p><b>UPDATE</b>: Police have arrested Brown and charged him with first-degree and second-degree robbery; third-degree attempted robbery; assault (cause injury to person under 17); act in a manner injurious to a child under 17; assault with intent to cause physical injury; assault (recklessly cause physical injury); and criminal possession of stolen property in the fifth degree.</p>

He was snuggled into a sling when the kick came out of nowhere.&quot;</p>
