title: NYPD: This Guy Punched 15-Year-Old Subway Rider For His iPhone
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2014_03_subicrime.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427150454im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_03_subicrime.jpg" width="300" height="192" class="image-right"> </span>The police are looking for a person suspected of punching a teenager on a 7 train last month.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, at 3:30 p.m. on Thursday, February 27th, a 15-year-old was on a southbound 7 train at the Main Street station when the suspect approached him, &quot;snatched his iPhone from his hand, punched him in the face and fled.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call Crime Stoppers at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477). The public can also submit tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427150454/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>

<p>NY Attorney General Eric Schneiderman has been urging <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427150454/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/05/attorney_general_schneiderman_wants.php">cellphone makers to offer &quot;kill switches&quot;</a> to make stolen cellphones inoperable.</p>
