<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="svususp.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170506194720im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/svususp.jpg" width="225" height="436" class="image-right"> </span>Police are looking for a person accused of slapping a woman at a Brooklyn subway station in March. And the suspect was allegedly peeing on the platform before the assault.</p>

<p>The incident took place around 10:30 a.m. on March 25. A 27-year-old was walking on the northbound platform of the Metropolitan Avenue and Lorimer Street G train station when, police said, &quot;she observed the individual urinating behind a garbage can.&quot;</p>

<p>As she walked away, &quot;the individual ran up behind her and slapped her on the buttocks,&quot; police said.</p>

<p>The suspect, who fled towards the G train, is described as being about 18 years old and was last seen wearing a black hoodie, blue jeans and white Adidas sneakers. A photograph of the suspect was released.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS or for Spanish 1-888-57-PISTA (74782).</p>
