<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2011_10_brassknuckles.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018072329im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2011_10_brassknuckles.jpg" width="269" height="187" class="image-left"> </span>Sure, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018072329/http://www.tsa.gov/travelers/airtravel/prohibited/permitted-prohibited-items.shtm">TSA&apos;s list of prohibited items</a> for carry-on and checked luggage might say that brass knuckles, stun guns, daggers and swords are allowed in checked bags, but when TSA agents find &quot;f<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018072329/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/queens/weapons_bust_at_jfk_TMcyLGHDESvBNYl4f4hprM?CMP=OTC-rss&amp;FEEDNAME=">our sets of brass knuckles, four stun- gun batons, a dagger and a sword</a>&quot; in your bags at JFK Airport, they are probably going to arrest you.  Because items like brass knuckles and stun guns are <em>illegal</em> in New York.</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018072329/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/queens/weapons_bust_at_jfk_TMcyLGHDESvBNYl4f4hprM?CMP=OTC-rss&amp;FEEDNAME=">Post reports</a> that Ahmed Aboukhabar, 31, of Alexandria, Egypt was arrested earlier this week before his Air France flight to Paris: &quot;He allegedly told Port Authority cops he needed the items &apos;for personal protection&apos; due to civil unrest in his homeland.&quot;</p>

<p>The Queens DA&apos;s office will drop charges against Aboukhabar if he stays out of trouble until January 27.</p>
