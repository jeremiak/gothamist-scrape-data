<p>Ever since the viral video of a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015110904/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/21/pizza_rat.php">rat dragging a slice of pizza</a> down subway stairs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015110904/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/07/journalism.php">turned out to be an elaborate bit</a> of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015110904/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/30/everythings_a_hoax_i_am_zardulu.php#photo-1">rodent performance art from Zardulu</a>, other videos and photos have come and gone. From <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015110904/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/30/see_it_vermin.php">Donut Rat</a> to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015110904/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/12/video_subway_rats_waging_epic_war_o.php">Churro Rats</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015110904/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/16/video_pita_rat.php">Pita Rats</a> to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171015110904/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/05/move_over_taco_dave.php">Taco Squirrel</a>, every new post has tried to sate your hunger for footage of animals eating human food our hunger for shareable #content. But the latest entry is actually worth watching from start to finish over and over again&#x2014;meet &quot;Two Rats, Half a Bagel&quot;:</p>

<p>Anthony Dorsey filmed the encounter at the Lexington Avenue-59th Street station on Friday at around 3:30 p.m. &quot;Out of the corner of my eye I saw movement on the tracks. I wasn&apos;t surprised to see a rat. [But] it wasn&apos;t until I saw that one rat had a slice of NY&apos;s favorite carb in its slimy grip that I was compelled to start filming,&quot; he admitted.</p>

<p>He&apos;s also familiar with the subway rat-and-food oeuvre, having watched &quot;most recently the rat battle for a Churro,&quot; calling it &quot;very relatable... Who wouldn&apos;t risk their lives for free piece of cinnamon laden grease-sugar underground?&quot;</p>

<p>As for the A+ ending, Dorsey said &quot;If we could all just split a bagel as diplomatically as rodents do, then the world would be a better place.&quot;</p>
