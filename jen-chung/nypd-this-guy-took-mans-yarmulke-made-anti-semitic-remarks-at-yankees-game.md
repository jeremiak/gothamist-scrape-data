<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-right"> <img alt="2017_02_aggharass.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170228104840im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2017_02_aggharass.jpg" width="200" height="356"> <br> <i style=" width:200px; ;display:block"> Photo of the suspect</i></div> The police are hoping the public can help them find a man accused of hateful, anti-Semitic actions during a Yankees game last fall. <p></p>

<p>And all the NYPD has is a very blurry image of the suspect.</p>

<p>The incident took place during a Dodgers-Yankees game at Yankees Stadium on September 12, 2016. The NYPD says that around 9:45 p.m., &quot;the victim, a 45-year-old male was watching [the game]... when the individual sitting behind him removed his yarmulke from his head and made anti-Jewish statements in regards. The individual returned the victims&apos;s yarmulke and left the stadium without any further incident.&quot;</p>

<p>The suspect, who is wanted for aggravated harassment, is described as being about 28 years old, 6&apos;2&quot; and 190 pounds, last seen wearing a white shirt and blue jeans. <br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS or for Spanish 1-888-57-PISTA (74782)<br>
The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170228104840/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
