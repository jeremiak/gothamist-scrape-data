</div></div><p>Maya Rudolph returned to <em>Saturday Night Live</em> as host last night, and tackled a larger-than-life female music superstar.  No, she didn&apos;t <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120219180922/http://www.hulu.com/watch/1406/saturday-night-live-whitney-houston">play Whitney Houston</a>, but she did spoof Beyonce in a sketch about the Most Important Baby In The Whole World, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120219180922/http://gothamist.com/tags/blueivy">Blue Ivy Carter</a>. With Rudolph as Bey and Jay Pharoah at Jay-Z, the happy parents greeted a bevy of celebrities, from Prince (Fred Armisen) to Nicki Minaj (Nasim Pedrad), from Taylor Swift (Kristen Wiig) to Bon Iver (Justin Timberlake).</p>

<p>Former SNL cast member Amy Poehler also showed up to give Rudolph support in a Bronx Beat sketch&#x2014;Poehler also did &quot;Really?!?!? With Seth and Amy&quot; during Weekend Update.  The show was pretty good, with a great cold opening <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120219180922/http://gothamist.com/2012/02/19/video_snl_goes_linsane_mocks_espn_r.php">about Linsanity/Chinksanity</a>, &quot;What&apos;s Up With That&quot; (Bill O&apos;Reilly! Kate Upton! Lindsay Buckingham!) and a funny commercial about a Maya Angelou prank show, &quot;I Know Why The Caged Bird Laughs.&quot;  </p>

<p>On March 3, SNL&apos;s host will be Lindsay Lohan.</p>
