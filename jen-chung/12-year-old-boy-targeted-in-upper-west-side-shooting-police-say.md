<p>Police believe a gunman who fired into a Manhattan barbershop on Tuesday afternoon was aiming for a 12-year-old boy. The boy was not injured.</p>

<p>The incident took place around 2:15 p.m. on April 11th, outside 927 Columbus Avenue between 105th and 106th Streets. The NYPD says the gunman fired one shot at the boy &quot;following an exchange of words.&quot; </p>

<p>The suspect, who fled north on Columbus Avenue, was described as being 5&apos;9&quot; and was last seen wearing a black shirt and blue shorts. Cops released a surveillance video showing the man walking on the sidewalk, stopping and then taking a gun out of his shorts to fire:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170412203750if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/MDyo8rKV--I" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Nobody was hit, and the gunman ran away. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170412203750/http://abc7ny.com/news/target-in-uws-barbershop-shooting-a-12-year-old-boy-police-say/1861528/">The barbershop&apos;s owner told WABC 7</a>, &quot;There were two young men in the store, one was getting a haircut another young man entered and fired two shots. I don&apos;t know what happened, after when I saw the gun, I threw myself on the floor.&quot;<br>
