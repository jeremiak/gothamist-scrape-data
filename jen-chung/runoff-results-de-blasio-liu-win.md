										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150521210226/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_09_runoff2.jpg"> <img alt="2009_09_runoff2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150521210226im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2009/09/2009_09_runoff2-thumb-140x158-444335.jpg" width="110" height="124" class="image-right"> </a> </span>Today&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150521210226/http://gothamist.com/2009/09/29/runoff_elections_today_for_public_a.php">runoff elections</a> for Public Advocate and City Comptroller were greeted by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150521210226/http://gothamist.com/2009/09/29/tumbleweeds_outnumber_voters_in_tod.php">lower voter turnout</a>.  According to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150521210226/http://ny1.com/content/top_stories/106555/polls-close-in-democratic-primary-runoff/Default.aspx">NY1&apos;s results</a>, Bill de Blasio has won the Public Advocate race with 62.50% of the vote to Mark Green&apos;s 37.50% while John Liu won the Comptroller race, with 55.68% of the vote while David Yassky earned 44.32%.  This means the Working Families Party had a big night&#x2014; both its candidates won.</p>
