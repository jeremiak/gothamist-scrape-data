<p>The police are hoping the public can help them find a three-year-old boy who went missing this afternoon while riding the subway. <b>Update, 10:35 p.m.</b>: Now the NYPD says the boy has been found.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, Kah-yon Reynolds, 3, was last seen on a northbound F train near Smith and 9th Street, in Brooklyn. The boy was with his grandfather, who fell asleep on the train. When the grandfather woke up, he was in Queens and Kah-yon was missing. </p>

<p>The police say that Kah-yon is a black male about 31&quot; tall, 40-50 pounds and wearing a yellow t-shirt, green camouflage shorts and red sneakers. The police do not have a photograph yet but will share it once it&apos;s available.</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this missing is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150424141521/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.<br>
 <br>
<b>Update</b>: The police just released a statement, about 20 minutes after their initial request for help, &quot;The child has been found in Newark and is in good health. The investigation is ongoing.&quot; Kah-yon lives in Newark.</p>
