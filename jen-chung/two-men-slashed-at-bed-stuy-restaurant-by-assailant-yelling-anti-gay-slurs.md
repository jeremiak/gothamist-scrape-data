<p>Police are looking for a man who is accused of assaulting two men while using anti-gay slurs at a Bed-Stuy restaurant.</p>

<p>The incident occurred on Sunday, March 5th, around 5 a.m.. According to the NYPD, the suspect approached a 34-year-old man inside the Crown Fried Chicken at 1147 Fulton Street and &quot;proceeded to display a cutting instrument, which he used to slash&quot; him on the &quot;left side of his face while uttering anti-gay slurs.&quot;</p>

<p>Then, police say, the man stabbed a 24-year-old man &quot;in his right torso and slashed him in his right shoulder while in front of the location while also uttering anti-gay slurs.&quot; The first victim was treated for his facial laceration at Kings County Hospital while the second victim was taken to Methodist Hospital for two stab wounds to his torso and a laceration to his shoulder. </p>

<p>Authorities released surveillance footage of the suspect, who is described as 20 to 26 years old and approximately 150 pounds.<br>
