										<p>In honor of Prince, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160923053650/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/21/prince.php">who has left us way too early</a>, the next <em>The New Yorker</em>&apos;s cover is all about The Purple One.</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160923053650/http://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/cover-story-2016-05-02?mbid=social_twitter">cover is by Bob Staake</a>; here is Sasha Frere-Jones&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160923053650/http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2007/04/09/dorian-purple">2007 piece about seeing Prince in Las Vegas</a> and Claire Hoffman&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160923053650/http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2008/11/24/soup-with-prince">2008 visit to Prince&apos;s home in Los Angeles</a>: &quot;In the living room, he&#x2019;d installed purple thrones on either side of a fireplace, and, nearby, along a hallway, he had hung photographs of himself, in a Moroccan villa, in various states of undress.&quot;</p>
