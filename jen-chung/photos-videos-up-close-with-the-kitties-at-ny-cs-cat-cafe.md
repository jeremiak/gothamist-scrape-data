					<p>It&apos;s the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170330205348/http://gothamist.com/2014/04/21/cat_cafe_popping_up_in_nyc_this_wee.php">hottest cafe to open in New York City</a> since <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170330205348/http://gothamist.com/2011/01/26/last_meow_for_pussycat_lounge.php">the Pussycat Lounge</a>, and it&apos;s only around for four days! <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170330205348/http://www.purinaone.com/cats/28-day-challenge/nyc-cat-cafe">Purina One&apos;s pop-up Cat Caf&#xE9;</a> at 168 Bowery features cat&apos;achinos, pastries and, yes, adorable and adoptable cats from the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170330205348/http://www.animalleague.org/">North Shore Animal League</a>!</p>

<p>The food part of the operation is very much separate from the bigger area where the cats are. The caf&#xE9; offers espresso and coffee drinks&#x2014;if you get the cat&apos;achino or latte, you&apos;ll get kitty cat latte/foam art&#x2014;and the pastries are pre-packaged off the premises. That, coupled with the fact that the <strong>food and drink are free</strong>, is probably why the cafe was able to swing the Health Department Sanitary Inspection Grade of &quot;A&quot; that is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170330205348/http://instagram.com/p/nIu6B6Nl-j/">prominently displayed in a window.</a> (The Health Department told us, &quot;For any food service establishment regulated by the Health Department, live animals, other than fish in a tank or service animals, are prohibited.&quot;)</p>

<p>You can bring your cat&apos;achino and snack into the main space&#x2014;which has tables, couches and lots of caturniture&#x2014;and nosh amid some of the most friendly and social cats we&apos;ve ever encountered. (Disclosure: My cats are neurotic, probably because <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170330205348/https://www.flickr.com/photos/jenchung/5361124129">we do stuff like this</a>.) Sixteen cats will be roaming the caf&#xE9; (eight were at the press preview today) exploring, climbing or playing. And if you can&apos;t make it, don&apos;t worry&#x2014;it&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170330205348/http://new.livestream.com/PurinaONE/CatCafe">going to be on Livestream</a>!</p>

<p><iframe src="//web.archive.org/web/20170330205348if_/http://player.vimeo.com/video/92761967?title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0" width="640" height="360" frameborder="0" webkitallowfullscreen="" mozallowfullscreen="" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Matthew Carroll, offsite coordinator for the North Shore Animal League (which is the world&apos;s largest no-kill shelter), said the cats were carefully selected to make sure they&apos;d be comfortable with people. Reporters, photographers and camera operators oohed and awwwed over the kitties who are looking for their forever (furever) homes. </p>

<p><iframe src="//web.archive.org/web/20170330205348if_/http://player.vimeo.com/video/92751361?title=0&amp;byline=0&amp;portrait=0" width="640" height="360" frameborder="0" webkitallowfullscreen="" mozallowfullscreen="" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Duchess, a lovely white cat, strutted over to wherever there seemed to be people and demanded our attention while Zander, a grey and white cat, was very relaxed and chilled out by a window sill. My personal favorite was Ciarra, who hung back near a trash can but happily submitted to chin scratches and belly rubs. Here&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170330205348/http://www.animalleague.org/events-news/events/purina-cat-cafe.html">information about all the cats</a>.</p>

<p>Purina&apos;s Niky Roberts says the cat food company was inspired to open the Cat Caf&#xE9; to start a dialogue about feline health issues&#x2014;and, obviously, to promote Purina One&apos;s benefits. (Mission accomplished!) Purina has no plans to launch another cat cafe at this point, but they are &quot;open to it.&quot;</p>

<p><em>The Purina One Cat Cafe is at 168 Bowery. Its opurrrating hours are Thursday, April 24, to Sunday, April 27, from 10 a.m. to 7 p.m. Also, capacity for the cat cafe is 65 people, so we can&apos;t wait to see the long lines of people, anxiously waiting for the chance to see the kitties.</em></p>
