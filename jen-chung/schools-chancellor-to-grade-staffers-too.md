title: Schools Chancellor to Grade Staffers, Too
---

<p><img alt="2008_09_reportcard.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122114im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_09_reportcard.jpg" width="120" height="131" class="right">Talk about accountability:  The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122114/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/16/education/16grades.html?ref=nyregion">NY Times reports</a> that Schools Chancellor Joel Klein, who ushered in report cards for public schools, may also grade the staffers who organized the news conference announcing this year&apos;s school grades!  It turns out that Klein already grades his press aides.  Example: &quot;Preparation: Please grade, using an A-F scale, how well you were prepared for the appearance by the press office staff.&quot; (but the Times semi-snarks the press grading form is &quot;far more straightforward than the formula used to evaluate schools&quot;).   The chancellor&apos;s press secretary declined to comment, sending this e-mail to the Times: &quot;<strong>Value of this story: F.</strong>&quot; </p>
