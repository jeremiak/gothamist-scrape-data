<p>Police say that a pair of men&#x2014;one of them dressed as a UPS delivery man&#x2014;robbed an apartment in the 26th Precinct. And that apartment had $40,000 in it!</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130322172306if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/ECmovdDuQ24" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130322172306/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/manhattan/man_dressed_home_ups_worker_grabs_omH0XqgqLYqmBt5igOg6PK?utm_medium=rss&amp;utm_content=%20%20%20%20%20%20%20%20%20%20Manhattan">According to the NYPD</a>, on Monday, March 11, at 10:30 a.m., a man dressed as a UPS worker knocked on the victim&apos;s door.  When the apartment was opened, the fake UPS worker and his accomplice pushed through, tying up three people.  Police sources tell the Post they took &quot;$28,000 from their safe... They also took $12,000 from a dresser and $500 from one victim&apos;s pants pocket.&quot; <br>
 <br>
The pair got into a silver Nissan Quest and then drove westbound on West 133rd Street. The first suspect is described as a Hispanic male, age 20-30, around 5&apos;10&quot;-6&apos; and 180-220 pounds, wearing a &quot;UPS&quot; uniform with brown vest, brown long sleeved button down shirt, brown knit hat, and blue jeans. The second suspect is also a Hispanic male, approximately 5&apos;10&quot;-6&apos;0&quot;,  and 180-220 pounds, wearing gray sweat pants, gray hooded sweatshirt, baseball hat with a gray brim, white sneakers and carrying a black backpack.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information is asked to call Crime Stoppers at (800) 577-TIPS (8477). The public can also submit their tips by logging onto Crime Stoppers&apos; website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130322172306/http://www.nypdcrimestoppers.com/">www.nypdcrimestoppers.com</a> or by texting their tips to CRIMES (274637), then enter TIP577.</p>
