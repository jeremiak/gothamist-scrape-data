<p>Firefighters rushed to a Bronx apartment building late Saturday night where a basement fire had been reported. And then they found a marijuana farm.</p>

<p>The FDNY responded to a 911 call about a fire at 2131 Chatterton Avenue in the Bronx at 11:30 p.m. They extinguished the fire and called the NYPD because numerous marijuana plants were found.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170328184938/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/bronx-basement-blaze-leads-firefighters-marijuana-operation-article-1.3009491">According to the Daily News</a>, &quot;The smell of smoldering cannabis wafted down the Castle Hill street for hours after the flames were put out.&quot; Neighbors were evacuated as, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170328184938/http://nypost.com/2017/03/26/bronx-pot-farm-goes-up-in-smoke-cops/">Post reports</a>, &quot;A team of cops wearing masks &#x2014; seemingly to prevent getting a contact high &#x2014; filled several huge garbage bags with weed and loaded it into a police van as evidence.&quot;</p>

<p>An NYPD spokesman said that Alberto Martinez, 65, who is connected to the residence, was arrested and charged with criminal possession of marijuana and criminal sale of marijuana. </p>
