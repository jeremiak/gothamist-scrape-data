<p>A 53-year-old dog walker was found dead in the stairwell of a Brooklyn apartment building, and it&apos;s believed he died because the dogs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602193316/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/brooklyn/dog-walker-dies-brooklyn-pooches-force-stairs-article-1.2981021">&quot;overpowered&quot;</a> him in the stairwell. </p>

<p>Police say that officers responded to a call about an unconscious male inside 15 Carroll Street, in Red Hook, around 5:22 p.m. yesterday. EMS pronounced the man dead at the scene.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602193316/http://nypost.com/2017/02/24/police-believe-dog-walkers-deadly-fall-was-caused-by-dogs/">The Post reports</a>, &quot;The 53-year-old victim was leaving the building where the two dogs live on Carroll street near Van Brunt, just after 5 p.m. when cops believe the canines pulled or tripped the man, causing him to fall down the stairs and fatally hit his head.&quot;</p>

<p>The dogs are a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602193316/http://abc7ny.com/news/professional-dog-walker-overpowered-by-dogs-killed-in-brooklyn/1770515/">pit bull and a mixed breed</a>.</p>

<p>&quot;A couple who answered the door at the Red Hook apartment declined to comment Thursday night, as a dog inside barked once in the background,&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602193316/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/brooklyn/dog-walker-dies-brooklyn-pooches-force-stairs-article-1.2981021">according to the Daily News</a>.</p>
