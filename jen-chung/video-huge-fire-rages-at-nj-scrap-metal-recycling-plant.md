<p>Late last night, a fire broke out at a scrap metal recycling facility in Jersey City. Firefighters have been on the scene at Sims Metal since after 11 p.m. and they are still trying to contain it. Smoke <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131128061938/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Recycling-Plant-Fire-Jersey-City-Blaze-Smoke-220324371.html">has been drifting towards</a> the harbor and NYC.</p>

<p>No injuries have been reported so far. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131128061938/http://abclocal.go.com/wabc/story?section=news/local/new_jersey&amp;id=9212127">WABC 7 reports</a>, &quot;Firefighters were battling the flames from land and from a marine vessel, and crews in claw cranes have been moving debris out of the way in an effort to aid the fight. Firefighters contained the flames to a mound of metal about 30 feet high and 100 feet wide.&quot; Here&apos;s video:</p>

<p>Jersey City officials are trying to figure out <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131128061938/http://www.nj.com/hudson/index.ssf/2013/08/large_scrap_metal_pile_creates_large_blaze_at_jersey_city_recycling_plant.html#incart_m-rpt-2">what the cause of the fire is</a>. According to NBC, Sims Metal Management is the world&apos;s largest recycler of metals and electronics.</p>
