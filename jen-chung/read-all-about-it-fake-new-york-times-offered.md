<p><strong><em>Earlier coverage and updates below</em>:</strong><br>
This morning, New Yorkers are being offered free copies of the New York Times--which happen to be fake.  One reader tells us, &quot;After taking it I realized that the date was 7/4/09, and the headline read &apos;Iraq War Over&apos;. It&apos;s full of fake articles, fake ads (<strong>even a Dr Zizmor &apos;Clean up the mess in the Iraq&apos; ad</strong>), and a clever twist on the Times&apos; slogan reading &apos;All the news we hope to print.&apos;&quot;  </p>

<p>You can see the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160525115125/http://www.nytimes-se.com/">fake New York Times website</a>, which has articles like &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160525115125/http://www.nytimes-se.com/2009/07/04/new-york-bike-path-system-expanded-dramatically/">New York Bike Path System Expanded Dramatically</a>&quot; and &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160525115125/http://www.nytimes-se.com/2009/07/04/public-relations-industry-forecasts-a-series-of-massive-layoffs/">Public Relations Industry Forecasts a Series of Massive Layoffs</a>&quot;--and &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160525115125/http://www.nytimes-se.com/2009/07/04/national-health-insurance-act-passes/">National Health Insurance Act Passes</a>.&quot;</p>
