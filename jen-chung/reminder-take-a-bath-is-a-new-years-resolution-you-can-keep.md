<p>A last minute, New Year&apos;s Eve reminder to those of you who insist on making resolutions for the New Year: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120104105740/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/28/five_new_years_resolutions_you_can.php">Make them achievable</a>.  We offered <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120104105740/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/28/five_new_years_resolutions_you_can.php">some suggestions on Wednesday</a>, taking some inspiration from Woody Guthrie&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120104105740/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/20/woody_guthries_new_years_resolution.php">list of resolutions for 1943</a> (as seen above), which include &quot;Stay Glad&quot; and &quot;Take A Bath.&quot;</p>

<p>A behavioral health doctor <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120104105740/http://abcnews.go.com/blogs/health/2011/12/20/5-reasons-why-your-new-years-resolution-will-fail/">gave ABC News some reasons</a> why resolutions fail, including people making too many&#x2014;focusing one thing helps&#x2014;and people expecting a magical change (&quot;Most people try to quit smoking and fail six or seven times before they quit. Looking at your life magically on January 1st doesn&#x2019;t make it all go away.  Find things that you&#x2019;ll be able to do in the context of your life&quot;).</p>
