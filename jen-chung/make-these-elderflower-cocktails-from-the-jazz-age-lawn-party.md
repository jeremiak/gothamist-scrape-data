                            <p>Today is the fourth and final <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140210111041/http://www.jazzagelawnparty.com/">Jazz Age Lawn Party</a> at Governors Island, but you can capture some of that same Roaring &apos;20s spirit at home by making <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140210111041/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/17/photos_jazz_age_lawn_party_takes_go.php#photo-28">some of the cocktails served at the event</a>.</p>

<p>St. Germain, the elderflower liqueur <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140210111041/http://www.stgermain.fr/story.php">handcrafted by French stereotypes</a> and favored by your aunt who spent that summer in Nice, was a sponsor of the event and they&apos;re kind enough to share two recipes: one is an effervescent cocktail with champagne or sparkling wine, and the other is a version of a white sangria.</p>

<p><strong>The St. Germain Cocktail</strong><br>
2 parts Brut Champagne or Dry Sparkling Wine<br>
1&#xBD; parts St-Germain<br>
2 parts Club Soda</p>

<p>Method: Stir ingredients in a tall ice-filled Collins glass, mixing completely. Think of Paris circa 1947. Garnish with a lemon twist.<br></p>

<p><strong>Sangria Flora</strong><br>
1 bottle  Sauvignon Blanc or Dry White Wine<br>
1 cup  St-Germain<br>
2 Fresh Peaches<br>
5-6 Fresh Strawberries<br>
6 Fresh Raspberries<br>
1 bunch Fresh Grapes</p>

<p>Method: Stir ingredients in a pitcher or carafe. Soak for approximately 15 minutes (or longer if desired). Serve in an ice-filled glass, then telephone your physician and regale him with stories of your exemplary fruit consumption.</p>
