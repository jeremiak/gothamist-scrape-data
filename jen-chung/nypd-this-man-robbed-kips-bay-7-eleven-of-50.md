										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2015_10_7eleven.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025134036im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_10_7eleven.jpg" width="640" height="387" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>A Manhattan 7-Eleven was robbed earlier this week and police have released images of the suspect looking around the convenience stores in hopes someone might be able to recognize him.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the commercial robbery occurred at the 7-Eleven at 478 Third Avenue around 10 p.m. on Wednesday, October 14th: &quot;the suspect entered the location, produced a knife, demanded money, received $50, and fled the scene on foot.&quot;<br>
 <br>
The suspect is described as being in his 30s, about 5&apos;10&quot; and 185 pounds with a beard. </p>

<p>Anyone with information is asked to call Crime Stoppers at (800) 577-TIPS (8477). The public can also submit their tips by logging onto <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025134036/http://www.nypdcrimestoppers.com/">Crime Stoppers&apos; website</a> or by texting their tips to CRIMES (274637), then entering TIP577. All calls are strictly confidential.</p>
