										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020935/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_10_pl90.jpg"> <img alt="2016_10_pl90.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020935im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2016/10/2016_10_pl90-thumb-275x455-971505.jpg" width="220" height="364" class="image-left"> </a> </span>A man wearing a yellow hardhat followed a woman into a Brooklyn apartment building on Monday and exposed himself, and now cops are hoping the public can help track him down.<br>
 <br>
The incident occurred at 10:30 a.m. on October 24th. According to the NYPD, the victim, 24, entered a residential building near Humboldt Street and Seigel Street when &quot;she was followed by an unidentified male onto the elevator. The male pulled down his pants and exposed himself in front of the victim.&quot;</p>

<p>The victim was not physically injured. The suspect, who fled in an unknown direction, is described as being 5&apos;9&quot; tall and was last seen wearing a yellow hat, white sneakers, gray sweatshirt and gray sweatpants. A surveillance image also shows him wearing a construction-type vest. He is wanted for public lewdness.</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477) or for Spanish, 1-888-57-PISTA (74782). The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020935/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
