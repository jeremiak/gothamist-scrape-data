</div></div>					<p>Former British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher, who <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429120539/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/08/defining_british_prime_minister_mar.php">passed away earlier today</a>, was in power for 11 and a half years, the &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429120539/http://www.nytimes.com/2013/04/09/world/europe/former-prime-minister-margaret-thatcher-of-britain-has-died.html?hp&amp;_r=0">longer than any other British politician in the 20th century.</a>&quot; However, her reign of power ended months after introducing a deeply unpopular&#x2014;and, as critics stated, unfair&#x2014;poll tax that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429120539/http://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/the-battle-of-trafalgar-square-the-poll-tax-riots-revisited-1926873.html">plunged Great Britain</a> into protests and riots.</p>

<p>The poll tax, known as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429120539/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_Charge">Community Charge</a>, had changed the old tax system by implementing a tax on each person (before, a tax was based on properties people owned or rented). However, there <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429120539/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_Charge#Implementation">were problems</a>&#x2014;some renters could dodge payment and many of the wealthy ended up saving money, while the poor got hit hard. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429120539/http://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/the-battle-of-trafalgar-square-the-poll-tax-riots-revisited-1926873.html">The Independent</a>: &quot;In practice, critics pointed out, that meant a millionaire living alone in a mansion would pay less than the average family.&quot;</p>

<p>Protests broke out all over the country, most notably in London on March 31st, 1990 in what&apos;s known as the &quot;Battle of Trafalgar,&quot; with anti-tax protesters <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429120539/http://www.nytimes.com/1990/04/01/world/tax-protesters-battle-police-in-london.html">throwing rocks, bottles and stones at police officers</a>. </p>
