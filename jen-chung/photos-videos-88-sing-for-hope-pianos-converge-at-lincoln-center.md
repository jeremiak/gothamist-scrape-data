</div></div>					<p>Have you spotted one of the 88 colorfully-painted pianos that have been in public spaces across the city over the past few weeks? The non-profit <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160209095318/http://singforhope.org/">Sing for Hope</a>, started by a pair of Juillard grads and known as an &quot;artists&#x2019; peace corps,&quot; will be donating the pianos to needy schools, healthcare facilities, and community organizations (and Sing for Hope artists will visit and perform). Yesterday was the climax of the public art installation, with all pianos reuniting at Lincoln Center for a concert and beautiful explosion of music.</p>

<p>The finale was a group performance on all 88 pianos of Bach&#x2019;s prelude in C. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160209095318/http://pix11.com/2013/06/16/dozens-of-pianists-serenade-new-yorkers-during-sing-for-hope-concert/#axzz2WU6Qu5O3">One woman told PIX11</a>, &quot;I decided to learn the piece. I hadn&#x2019;t  played in years... I lived it. I finally got to play the piano at Lincoln Center.&quot; Here are some more videos:</p>
