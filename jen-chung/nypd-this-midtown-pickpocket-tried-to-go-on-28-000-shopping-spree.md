title: NYPD: This Midtown Pickpocket Tried To Go On $28,000 Shopping Spree
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160406052610/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_11_midtownpick.jpg"> <img alt="2015_11_midtownpick.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160406052610im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2015/11/2015_11_midtownpick-thumb-288x449-922204.jpg" width="220" height="342" class="image-right"> </a> </span>Police are looking for a man who stole a Manhattan bar patron&apos;s wallet and then tried to go crazy with the victim&apos;s credit cards.<br>
 <br>
According to the NYPD, the wallet theft took place around 7 p.m., on November 4th, at East 41st Street bar Stout NYC: &quot;An unknown male suspect removed a 43-year-old male victim&apos;s wallet from the victim&apos;s sports jacket, which was hanging on the back of a chair at the bar. The wallet contained credit cards from Bank of America, American Express and Citibank as well as a driver license and a MTA/LIRR pass.&quot;</p>

<p>Police say the suspect then tried to rack up $28,000 in charges at Macy&apos;s and Saks Fifth Avenue. </p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160406052610/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577. All calls are kept strictly confidential.<br>
