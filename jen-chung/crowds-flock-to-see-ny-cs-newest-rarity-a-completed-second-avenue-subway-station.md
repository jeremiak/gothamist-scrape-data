					<p>The 96th Street Second Avenue subway station, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021830/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/23/second_ave_subway_96.php#photo-1">which was shown off</a> in all of its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021830/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/21/2nd_avenue_subway_history.php">decades-in-the-making glory</a> on Thursday, was open to the public for two open houses yesterday, one in the morning and one in the evening, ahead of its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021830/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/19/second_avenue_subway_coming_soon.php">official opening on January 1, 2017</a>. At the second one, Governor Andrew Cuomo proudly greeted the public, signing autographs, taking selfies and (groan) answering questions from the media.</p>
