                            <p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_05_bmadoff.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20131206010758im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_05_bmadoff.jpg" width="120" height="138" class="image-right"> </span>Bernard Madoff, everyone&apos;s favorite multi-billion-dollar Ponzi schemer, was supposed to be sentenced for his fraud on June 16, but it was postponed till June 29.  While U.S. District Judge Denny Chin didn&apos;t explain why the sentencing rescheduled, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131206010758/http://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=20601170&amp;sid=azahvTQk_qbQ">Bloomberg News reports</a> that he &quot;[outline] a procedure for victims who wish to submit letters to him or to speak at the sentencing,&quot; with the order reading, &quot;Due to the number of victims in the case, the court cannot guarantee that every victim who wishes to speak at sentencing will have the opportunity to do so.&quot;  (His victims do <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131206010758/http://gothamist.com/2009/03/21/fraud_victims_rip_into_madoffs_in_p.php">have a lot to say</a>.) Madoff, who <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131206010758/http://gothamist.com/2009/03/13/wah-wah_madoff_doesnt_want_to_be_in.php">faces 150 years in prison</a>, is being <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131206010758/http://gothamist.com/2009/03/14/madoff_who_wants_outta_jail_worth_8.php">held that Metropolitan Correctional Center</a>.  In other news, the trustee charged with liquidating Madoff&apos;s assets believes he&apos;ll be able to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131206010758/http://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=20601087&amp;sid=a6ktRa_cxvn8&amp;refer=home">approve $100 million in investor claims</a> this month and win clawback settlements later on.</p>
