<p>Police are looking for a person accused of shoving a senior citizen at a Manhattan subway station before robbing him this past Friday.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2016_01_bsusp.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160613234159im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_01_bsusp.jpg" width="170" height="267" class="image-right"> </span>According to the NYPD, the incident occurred at 3:05 p.m. on January 29: A 70-year-old man had entered the Bowery Street J/Z subway station, &quot;when he was  approached from behind&quot; by a male suspect &quot;who pushed the victim causing him to fall to the ground. The individual removed an undetermined amount of money and a senior citizen&apos;s metrocard and fled eastbound on  Bowery Street.&quot;</p>

<p>The victim, who suffered swelling and a laceration to his forehead, was taken to a hospital. </p>

<p>Police released a surveillance image of the suspect (right). <br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS.The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160613234159/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>

<p>This comes as the NYPD is reportedly examining <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160613234159/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/30/nypd_reportedly_wants_to_ban_career.php">how to drastically decrease subway crime</a>.</p>
