<p>Police are looking for a suspect who stabbed another J train passenger in Brooklyn yesterday afternoon. The pair apparently got into a verbal fight that turned physical, police say, and some of it was caught on video.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the victim was a 19-year-old male who was on a northbound J train when, around 3 p.m., he got into a verbal dispute with another passenger that resulted in them physically fighting: The suspect &quot;stabbed the victim with a sharp object in the lower torso. When the train pulled into the Chauncey Street subway station, the victim exited and the individual remained on the train, continuing northbound.&quot;</p>

<p>The victim was taken to Brookdale Hospital <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170125193518/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Subway-Stabbing-Brooklyn-J-Train-NYPD-411425025.html">in stable condition</a>. </p>

<p>Authorities released video of the struggle and described the suspect as approximately 25-35 years of age, 5&apos;8&quot; tall and 250 pounds with brown eyes, black hair. He was last seen wearing yellow work boots, a gray hooded jacket, and blue jeans.</p>
