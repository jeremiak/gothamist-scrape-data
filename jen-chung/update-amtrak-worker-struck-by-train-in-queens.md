<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-left"> <img alt="2012_05_transworker.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20120519051723im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2012_05_transworker.jpg" width="300" height="225"> <br> <i style=" width:300px; ;display:block"> Via WCBS 2</i></div> Just before 4 a.m., a <strike>LIRR</strike> Amtrak worker was hit by a train near the Hunterspoint Avenue station.  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120519051723/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2012/05/18/transit-worker-struck-by-lirr-train-near-hunterspoint-avenue-station/">According to WCBS 2</a>, the worker was taken to Elmhurst Hospital with serious but non-life-threatening injuries.<p></p>

<p>The worker had been in a tunnel, working on the East Side Access project, which will bring LIRR service to Grand Central Terminal. The incident <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120519051723/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/lirr_track_worker_struck_by_train_Vdj6l73YaKCeqcV9wkgt8K">suspended service</a> between Woodside and Jamaica stations, but was restored by 6 a.m. </p>

<p>WCBS 2 adds, &quot;Investigators are trying to determine if alarms that warn workers of oncoming trains sounded.&quot; Last year, a sandhog working on the East Side Access project was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120519051723/http://gothamist.com/2011/11/18/worker_killed_by_falling_concrete_i.php">killed when a piece of falling concrete hit him</a>.  </p>
