<p>Are you looking for another <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014237/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/18/free_outdoor_movies_this_wk.php">free, outdoor movie screening option</a> for tonight? <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014237/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/03/gothamists_best_nyc_movie_scenes_1.php">One of the most wonderful NYC movies ever made</a>, <em>Moonstruck</em>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014237/http://www.filmlinc.com/blog/entry/moonstruck-free-screening-lincoln-center-ausgust">will be shown tonight</a> at on the Josie Robertson Plaza at Lincoln Center&#x2014;the very setting of a pivotal scene!</p>

<p>In the 1987 film directed by Norman Jewison, Cher plays Loretta Castorini, a dowdy Brooklyn widow who works as a bookkeeper, finds herself engaged to one man, Johnny Cammareri (Danny Aiello), but then attracted to his estranged brother with a wooden hand, Ronny Cammareri (Nicolas Cage). At one point, Ronny demands, &quot;Meet me at the Met!&quot; and Loretta says, &quot;Where&apos;s the Met?&quot;:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="//web.archive.org/web/20150510014237if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/DTGeYXydspI?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Also, Loretta lives with her parents (Olympia Dukakis and Vincent Gardenia) in a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014237/http://gothamist.com/2008/08/18/moonstruck_house_sells_on_cranberry.php">gorgeous Brooklyn Heights home</a>. Cher won the Oscar for Best Actress, and Dukakis won for Best Supporting Actress; John Patrick Shanley won for Best Screenplay.</p>

<p>The screening is at 8 p.m. and is first-come, first serve outside the Metropolitan Opera House. It&apos;s a special kick-off to the Met Opera&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014237/http://www.metopera.org/metopera/liveinhd/summer-hd-festival">Summer HD Festival</a>, which will feature &quot;10 of the Met&#x2019;s most popular Live in HD productions from past seasons for free,&quot; like Tchaikovsky&#x2019;s <em>Eugene Onegin</em>, Verdi&#x2019;s <em>Falstaff</em>, and, yes, Puccini&#x2019;s <em>La Boh&#xE8;me</em>&#x2014;the opera that Loretta and Ronny see&#x2014;which will be shown tomorrow, August 23. </p>
