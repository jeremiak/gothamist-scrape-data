					<p>Yesterday, Bronx graffiti artist Cope2 began working on the Bowery Mural on East Houston Street. Legendary street art documentarian Martha Cooper, who was on the scene photographing Cope2, pointed out, &quot;It&apos;ll have wildstyle, bubble letters, tags and pieces, which is unusual for the Bowery wall.&quot;</p>

<p>Cope2 (born Fernando Carlo) has been tagging since the late 1970s but he&apos;s especially happy to be working on the Bowery Mural. He contacted <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223151840/http://www.goldmanproperties.com/Art-and-Culture/Houston-Street-Wall.asp">Goldman Properties</a> four years ago about painting the wall, which first gained fame when Keith Haring painted it in 1982. More recently, How &amp; Nosm, Twist and Revok and POSE has taken their turn with the wall. Cope2 told us, &quot;<strong>It&apos;s the best wall in New York City.</strong> Most walls are ruined now.&quot;</p>

<p>The South Bronx native added, &quot;It&apos;s good exposure for any artist,&quot; and he does <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223151840/http://www.12ozprophet.com/cope2/entry/cope2-under-the-influence-may-15-azart-gallery-nyc/">have a show at Azart Gallery</a>, opening Thursday, May 15. But he thought he&apos;d be further along with the wall by now.</p>

<p>Cope2 pointed to a scissor lift that was being serviced by a mechanic. The machine &quot;never started&quot; so he hadn&apos;t been able to work on the top of the mural. With rain in the forecast for tomorrow, Cope2 said, &quot;I&apos;m just trying to finish the bottom and hopefully I can get to the top later.&quot; (A new lift was being sent to the mural.) If it rains tomorrow, he&apos;ll take a break and come back to it. </p>

<p>Many people stopped to observe Cope2 work on the colorful wall, with admirers shaking his hand or giving him a fist bump. However, there are some detractors: Vandalog editor-in-chief RJ Rushmore posted Tweets and Instagrams showcasing Cope2&apos;s homophobic rants:</p>

<p>When asked about them, Cope2 emphatically told us, &quot;I&apos;m not homophobic,&quot; adding, &quot;<strong>I have many gay friends.</strong>&quot; He did admit, however, that &quot;it&apos;s wrong to use those words.&quot;</p>

<p>But why use them? Cope2 said it&apos;s because he&apos;s getting attacked, adding, &quot;I have to retaliate...to piss off&quot; the haters. Cope2 claimed his gay friends are okay with him using homophobic language: &quot;They say, &apos;Do what you gotta do.&apos; They know I&apos;m pissing people off.&quot; </p>

<p>He told us again, &quot;I shouldn&apos;t be using that...[but] people say things out of anger,&quot; and he&apos;s been responding to a lot of &quot;bull crap&quot; that&apos;s been said about him. Specifically referring to Rushmore, Cope2 said, &quot;He has a personal problem with me. He&apos;s been disrespecting me for a while... I never did anything to that kid.&quot; </p>

<p>If Rushmore came to the mural while he&apos;s working or at the gallery show this Thursday, Cope2 promised, &quot;If I see him, I&apos;m gonna smack him... Some people really need to get their ass kicked.&quot; Cope2 opined, &quot;Blogs attack you to get publicity to their sites.&quot; Rushmore told us that any dispute between the two of them &quot;does not excuse his language.&quot; (Apparently the beef stems from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170223151840/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/rjrcope.jpg">Rushmore&apos;s disinterest in Cope2&apos;s work</a>.) </p>
