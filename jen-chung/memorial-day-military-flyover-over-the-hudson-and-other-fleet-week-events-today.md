<p>It&apos;s Memorial Day, and Fleet Week New York organizers have a number of activities planned across the city. Including two flyovers and a search-and-rescue mission.</p>

<p>At noon, the U.S. Navy is conducting a flyover with four FA-18&apos;s east up the Hudson and past the Intrepid, for the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016130725/http://www.intrepidmuseum.org/fleetweek.aspx">Intrepid&apos;s Memorial Day Ceremony</a>. This is what it looks like:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016130725if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/PRf2tySlsow" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>At 12:55 p.m., the U.S. Navy Leap Frogs will be doing a parachute jump into Citi Field, before the Mets&apos; game against the White Sox. Here&apos;s video of the Leap Frogs jumping into University of Tennessee&apos;s stadium:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016130725if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/uHsol_j0J-Q" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>At 1 p.m., the FA-18&apos;s will be flying over Citi Field, too.</p>

<p>And at 1:30 PM, the U.S Coast Guard is be demonstrating its search and rescue operations near Pier 86 by the Intrepid Air &amp; Space Museum. The demo, which is weather-permitting, will consist of one swimmer exiting a U.S. Coast Guard helicopter to perform a rescue demonstration. </p>

<p>Here&apos;s a list of NYC activities today; you can look at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016130725/http://www.fleetweeknewyork.com/">Fleet Week website</a> for more details:<br>

Lines may be capped at 3 p.m. to allow guests to finish their tours. Tours are free and open to the public. (*Tours will begin at 10 a.m.)<p></p>

This year&apos;s event will commemorate the 40th Anniversary of the death of Admiral Chester W. Nimitz. Event is free and open to the public.<br>

A rescue swimmer will then jump into the water to secure the mannequin. Both the rescue swimmer and the mannequin are then lifted securely back into the helicopter.
