<p>The police are searching for a bodega employee who allegedly slashed a customer in Brooklyn.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2015_10_deliattack.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151222053112im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_10_deliattack.jpg" width="300" height="254" class="image-right"> </span>According to the police, the assault occurred on August 6th, at 3:40 a.m.: An employee of the deli at 48 North 6th Street (near Kent Avenue) &quot;engage[d] in a verbal argument with the 23 year-old male victim. The argument became physical and the suspect did stab the victim with a knife causing lacerations to the victim&apos;s stomach.&quot;</p>

<p>The victim <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151222053112/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/nyc-crime/brooklyn-deli-worker-slashed-customer-store-fight-cops-article-1.2402465">was treated at a hospital</a>. The suspect fled, and police released images of him, describing him as having brown eyes and  black hair, being about 5&apos;8&quot; and 140 pounds, and wearing a white shirt and tan slacks.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call Crime stoppers at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477). The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151222053112/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
