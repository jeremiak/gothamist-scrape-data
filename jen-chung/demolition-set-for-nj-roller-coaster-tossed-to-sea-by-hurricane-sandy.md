</div></div>					<p>One of the most iconic images of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509174327/http://gothamist.com/tags/hurricanesandy">Hurricane Sandy</a>&apos;s destruction was the Seaside Heights roller coaster thrown into the Atlantic Ocean. Now, over six months later, the roller coaster is set to be demolished.</p>

<p>The demolition will start tomorrow and it expected to last a few days. But don&apos;t worry&#x2014;it&apos;ll still be there for Prince Harry&apos;s photo op when he visits the hurricane-ravaged shoreline with NJ Governor Chris Christie. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509174327/http://www.nj.com/ocean/index.ssf/2013/05/demo_set_for_jet_star_rollercoaster_for_same_day_as_prince_harry_visit_to_seaside.html#incart_river_default">The British royal will be in Seaside Heights</a> at noon and demo is starting at 2 p.m.</p>

<p>Various officials have wanted the roller coaster out before Memorial Day, and Casino Pier spokeswoman <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509174327/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2013/05/13/seaside-heights-roller-coaster-to-be-removed-from-atlantic-ocean/">Toby Wolf explained</a>, &quot;[It&#x2019;s] pretty close to shore and that&#x2019;s a safety hazard for swimmers, surf.&quot;</p>
