					<p>Paul McCartney made 55,000 concertgoers feel like the luckiest people in the world Sunday night as he brought his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016131409/https://www.paulmccartney.com/live">&quot;One on One&quot; tour</a> to MetLife Stadium, performing over three dozen songs in a rousing and rocking show. Grown men were screaming &quot;I love you!&quot; and people were even hugging the security staff when the show was over. </p>

<p>Superlatives seem clich&#xE9; when talking about a Legend, but the 74-year-old Macca deserves them. He and his exceptional band&#x2014; Rusty Anderson (guitar), Abe Laboriel, Jr. (drums), Paul Wickens (keyboards) and Brian Ray (guitar and bass)&#x2014;didn&apos;t just play songs, they delivered a master class in arena concerts, with impeccable playing and incredible energy. If you&apos;ve ever played a Beatles or Wings album, then that&apos;s pretty much how it sounded in East Rutherford&#x2014;a flawless living jukebox.</p>

<p>After playing an intimate version of &quot;Blackbird&quot;&#x2014;well, as intimate as you can get when <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016131409/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u5Uar3H7XUo&amp;feature=youtu.be&amp;a">one is on a hydraulically elevated stage</a> surrounded by video screens&#x2014;he mentioned how all over the world, people tell him how they learned to play the song. &quot;How many of you learned to play &apos;Blackbird&apos;?&quot; he asked the crowd. Thousands of hands raised. McCartney paused and said, &quot;And you all got it wrong.&quot; My husband nodded&#x2014;he raised his hand and thought he knew how to play it... until he saw the monitors showing close-ups of McCartney playing it.</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016131409/http://www.setlist.fm/setlist/paul-mccartney/2016/metlife-stadium-east-rutherford-nj-2bfc9c2a.html">setlist included</a> many Beatles classics and deep cuts, plus a song from his Quarrymen days, as well Wings favorites (&quot;Live and Let Die&quot; turned the concert into a KISS spectacle for a few minutes, with fire and fireworks) and newer songs, like an ode to his wife, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016131409/http://gothamist.com/2011/05/06/macca_to_marry.php">former MTA board member</a> Nancy Shevell, &quot;My Valentine&quot;; &quot;Queenie Eye&quot;; and his collaboration with Rihanna and Kanye West, &quot;Four Five Seconds.&quot;</p>

<p>He did remark, &quot;When we play an old Beatles song, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016131409/https://www.instagram.com/p/BI1M1S8hib_/?taken-by=chungjen">your phones light up like a galaxy</a>... When we play a new one, it&apos;s like a black hole.&quot; Then he said, &quot;Well, here&apos;s another black hole,&quot; before playing another more recent song.</p>

<p>But the phones lit up when there was a singalong for &quot;Hey, Jude.&quot;</p>

<p>McCartney&apos;s banter was engaging throughout the night: At one point he admitted that he tries not to read the signs in the crowd while playing, lest he forget lyrics or a chord. Then he proceeded to read the signs: &quot;&apos;Sign my butt&apos;?!&quot; he said, in a wounded voice, before asserting, &quot;Let&apos;s have a look at it.&quot; </p>

<p>By the way, getting to MetLife Stadium <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016131409/http://www.njtransit.com/sf/sf_servlet.srv?hdnPageAction=MeadowlandsTo">by way of NJ Transit</a> is fine once you accept that you&apos;ll be swept into a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016131409/https://twitter.com/jenchung/status/762405020167835648">slightly stinky mass of humanity at Penn Station</a>: You take a train to Seacaucus, where you transfer for a shuttle to MetLife Stadium, where you are treated to a temporary view of the NYC skyline <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016131409/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/29/supertall_towers_nyc.php">with that skinny middle finger</a>. There is walking involved, as you go up and down stairs to train platforms or walk to the stadium&apos;s entrances, but it&apos;s efficient and way easier than driving and definitely cheaper than getting an Uber.</p>
