<p>Last night at around 10:18 p.m., two people were fatally struck by an oncoming Long Island Rail Road train in Queens. The pair apparently <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130823211226/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/double_death_leap_on_lirr_6dOstg3bRamE0OEeSrCHdM?utm_medium=rss&amp;utm_content=">&quot;leaped in front&quot;</a> of the Manhattan-bound train at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130823211226/http://lirr42.mta.info/stationInfo.php?id=30">Hollis station</a>, which is at 193rd Street and Woodhull Avenue. </p>

<p>It took two hours for the bodies to be removed from the track. The victims&apos; ages and identities <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130823211226/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2013/07/29/2-people-struck-by-lirr-train-in-queens/">haven&apos;t been released</a>, but the Post reports, &quot;Witnesses said that they saw the pair jump from the platform as the train was approaching the station.&quot; The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130823211226/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/double_death_leap_on_lirr_6dOstg3bRamE0OEeSrCHdM?utm_medium=rss&amp;utm_content=">Daily News says</a> police are looking into whether the pair had a suicide pact.</p>

<p><em>If someone you know <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130823211226/https://www.afsp.org/understanding-suicide/risk-factors-and-warning-signs">exhibits warning signs of suicide</a>: do not leave the person alone, remove any firearms, alcohol, drugs or sharp objects that could be used in a suicide attempt, and call the U.S. National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 800-273-TALK (8255) or take the person to an emergency room or seek help from a medical or mental health professional.</em></p>
