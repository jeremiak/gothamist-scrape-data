										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160904020649/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_04_shooterbrownsville.jpg"> <img alt="2016_04_shooterbrownsville.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160904020649im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2016/04/2016_04_shooterbrownsville-thumb-400x356-943801.jpg" width="250" height="222" class="image-right"> </a> </span>Police are searching for a shooter who fired off multiple rounds on a Brooklyn street last week. </p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incident took place on Friday, April 15th around 8:30 p.m. in Brownsville: &quot;An unidentified male individual was walking southbound on Mother Gaston Boulevard when he approached Sutter Avenue and fired approximately five rounds. The individual then fled northbound on Mother Gaston Boulevard.&quot;</p>

<p>No one was injured, but police are looking for the suspect, who is wanted for reckless endangerment. </p>

<p>He&apos;s described as being 18-22 years old and was last seen wearing a blacked hooded sweatshirt and a baseball cap. Police shared surveillance video from in front of 448 Sutter Avenue; in the video, he appears to be waving his hand to tell someone to move and later, during some shots, he puts his left arm over his chest.</p>
