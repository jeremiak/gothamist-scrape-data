Mohamed Hamoud Alessa, 20, and Carlos Almonte, 24, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408141703/http://gothamist.com/2010/06/06/two_men_with_alleged_terror_ties_ar.php">were arrested over the weekend</a> at JFK Airport, about to head to Cairo, after a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408141703/http://gothamist.com/2010/06/08/nj_terror_suspects_mom_says_hes_cra.php">years-long investigation</a> into their alleged plans for jihad.  However, reports suggest they had troubled youths.  <p></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408141703/http://www.northjersey.com/recap/060710_North_Jersey_terrorism_suspects_products_of_different_backgrounds.html">According to the Bergen Record</a>, Alessa left North Bergen High School in 2004 to attended a private Islamic school because of, a school spokesman said, &quot;concerns of safety and well-being of other students and school personnel.&quot; And while attending KAS Prep in 2005, he &quot;talked about mutilating homosexuals, subordinating women in the name of Islam and bringing a gang of Muslims to blow up the school.&quot; </p>

<p>A KAS Prep school official said, &quot;Everyone tried to help this kid, he&#x2019;s just an angry young man.&quot; Authorities started to monitor Alessa after a tip in 2006; in 2007, he returned to North Bergen High &quot;but took his lessons in the township&#x2019;s public library with a security guard present.&quot;  </p>
