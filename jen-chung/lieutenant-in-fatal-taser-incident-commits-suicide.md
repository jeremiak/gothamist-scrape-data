										<p><img alt="2008_10_ltmp.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20140606142445im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_10_ltmp.jpg" width="120" height="121" class="right">Lieutenant Michael Pigott, who ordered the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140606142445/http://gothamist.com/2008/09/25/after_cop_tasers_him_naked_man_fall.php">fatal Tasering</a> of an emotionally disturbed person, killed himself this morning.  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140606142445/http://abclocal.go.com/wabc/story?section=news/local&amp;id=6427185">WABC 7 reports</a> &quot;Pigott reported to Floyd Bennett Field in Brooklyn, where he took his life shortly after 6 a.m.&quot; and another officer &quot;sustained a non-life threatening injury during the incident.&quot; Pigott had been stripped of his gun and badge after the Tasering, where Iman Morales fell to his death, and yesterday, the 21-year police force veteran <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140606142445/http://gothamist.com/2008/10/01/tasered_mans_family_will_sue.php">had said</a> he was &quot;truly sorry for what happened.&quot;  The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140606142445/http://www.nydailynews.com/news/ny_crime/2008/10/02/2008-10-02_nypd_lt_who_ordered_fatal_tasering_of_ps.html">Daily News suggests</a> that Pigott, who turned 46 today, went to Floyd Bennett Field--where the Emergency Services Unit in headquartered--because there are weapons there.</p>
