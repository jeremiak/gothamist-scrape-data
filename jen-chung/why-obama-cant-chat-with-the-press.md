Yesterday, he prematurely Tweeted, &quot;Pres. Obama just called Kanye West a &apos;jackass&apos; for his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629201807/http://gothamist.com/2009/09/14/after_kanye_west_steals_taylor_swif.php">outburst at VMAs when Taylor Swift won</a>. Now THAT&apos;S presidential.&quot;  Prematurely because it turns out the President&apos;s remarks were off-the-record and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629201807/http://www.politico.com/blogs/michaelcalderone/0909/ABCs_Moran_tweeted_OTR_Obama_swipe_at_Kanye.html?showall">Moran deleted the Tweet</a> (but it&apos;s still <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629201807/http://search.twitter.com/search?q=&amp;ands=obama&amp;phrase=&amp;ors=&amp;nots=&amp;tag=&amp;lang=all&amp;from=terrymoran&amp;to=&amp;ref=&amp;near=&amp;within=15&amp;units=mi&amp;since=&amp;until=&amp;rpp=15">here</a>).  ABC News <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629201807/http://www.politico.com/click/stories/0909/did_obama_call_kanye_a_jackass.html">then told Politico</a>: &quot;In the process of reporting on remarks by President Obama that were made during a CNBC interview, ABC News employees prematurely tweeted a portion of those remarks that turned out to be from an off-the-record portion of the interview. This was done before our editorial process had been completed. That was wrong. We apologize to the White House and CNBC and are taking steps to ensure that it will not happen again.&quot; On the upside, Kanye probably thinks <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629201807/http://gothamist.com/2009/09/15/video_kanye_west_apologetic_on_jay.php">he was a jackass</a>, too.</p>
