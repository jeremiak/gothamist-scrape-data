										<p><img alt="2008_07_coyote.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428152316im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_07_coyote.jpg" width="90" height="76" class="right">Yesterday, we mentioned that a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428152316/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/09/early_addition_47.php">coyote was spotted</a> near the Horace Mann School in the Bronx.  It turns out the coyote was a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428152316/http://www.nypost.com/seven/07102008/news/regionalnews/bx__coyotes_walk_on_the_wild_side_119260.htm">pup of about 6 months</a>.  Animal Care and Control took the little fella to its Manhattan facility and nicknamed the 9-pound coyote &quot;Van Cort,&quot; since he was near Van Cortlandt Park.  A specialist told the Post, &quot;My guess would be that he was part of a litter. Maybe it was hunting and it got lost.&quot;  Van Cort will probably be headed to an animal sanctuary upstate; more <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428152316/http://www.wnbc.com/news/16841608/detail.html?dl=mainclick">pictures here</a>.</p>
