<p>A man was injured after being shoved into a tree on Monday afternoon, and now the police are looking for the culprit who did it.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incident occurred at 3:53 p.m. in the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160406052110/http://www.nyc.gov/html/nypd/html/precincts/precinct_061.shtml">61st Precinct</a>, in front of 1753 East 16 Street: &quot;An unknown suspect pushed a 60-year-old male on the sidewalk, causing the victim to come in contact with a tree. The victim, who suffered pain and redness to his left ear, refused medical attention at the scene.&quot;</p>

<p>The suspect then fled southbound on East 16th Street. Police released video of a person of interest, describing him as wearing a grey sweatsuit. </p>

All calls are kept strictly confidential.</p>
