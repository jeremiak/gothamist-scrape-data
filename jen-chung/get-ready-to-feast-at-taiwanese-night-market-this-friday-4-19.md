</div></div>					<p>Are you down to try some Taiwanese tapas? This Friday, April 19, the second annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406141024/http://tap-ny.org/tap-night-market/">Taiwanese Night Market</a> will be held at in Williamsburg&#x2014;and some of the goodies are stinky tofu and Taiwanese fried chicken!</p>

<p>Night markets, where food vendors sell their dishes and drinks, are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406141024/http://www.taiwan.gov.tw/mp.asp?mp=1002">popular in Taiwan</a>, and the event&apos;s organizer, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406141024/http://tap-ny.org/">Taiwanese American Professionals New York</a>, wants to give New Yorkers a chance to experience a little bit of Taiwan. Here are the food vendors:</p><blockquote>Taicken Chicken - back by popular demand, authentic and 100% delicious Taiwanese fried chicken.<br>
A-Pou Dumplings - a rarely seen dumpling truck with ingredients straight from Taiwan.<br>
Thirstea Cafe - artisanal tea and of course...bubble tea.<br>
Sweet Pleasure Confections - an all new handmade gourmet dessert line, debuting Taiwanese flavored macarons.<br>
Mamak - will be debuting its delectable Malaysian cuisine at TAP&apos;s night market.<br>
Stinky Tofu from Mama&apos;s Stank - handmade from a mother&apos;s recipe, it doesn&apos;t get any more authentic than this.<br>
Filled with Sweets - baked goods with high quality ingredients and a Taiwanese flair.<br>
Passport 2 Taiwan - one of the largest and oldest Taiwanese festivals in America is partnering with TAP to bring Taiwan to NYC once again.<br>
Best Summer - fresh summer rolls filled with premium meats and herbs.<br>
REMY Martin - a new line of clear pineapple flavored spirits, shots for all.<br>
Imperial Woodpecker Snowballs - Manhattan&apos;s premier shaved ice shop.<br>
Umame Nuts - Lightly roasted peanuts with a fresh seaweed core.<br>
Macaron Parlour - delectable macarons and desserts<br>
Tiger Beer - award winning and adored across Asia, Tiger Beer will only add to the fun of TAP&apos;s night market.<br>
Taiwan Beer - need we explain?</blockquote>The event is from 6:30 p.m. until 10 p.m. Tickets are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406141024/http://tapnightmkt.eventbrite.com/">$40, plus $2.99 handling</a> for <strong><em>unlimited</em></strong> food (there&apos;s also Taiwan Beer and Tiger Beer) and games, plus entry to an afterparty. More <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150406141024/http://tap-ny.org/tap-night-market/">details here</a>.<p></p>
