					<p>While some chefs might be happy with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102071217/http://www.nytimes.com/2010/09/29/dining/29rest.html">four stars from the NY Times</a>, Del Posto&apos;s Mark Ladner has been working on another venture: A gluten-free pasta bowls to be served in &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102071217/https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/pastaflyer/pasta-flyer-italian-cuisine-japanese-style-andglut">family-friendly, ready-to-eat ramen shop setting</a>.&quot; After a successful Kickstarter and a food truck tour of colleges, he&apos;s opened a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102071217/http://pastaflyer.com/">Pasta Flyer pop-up restaurant</a> in the West Village today. And it&apos;s really delicious!</p>

<p>There are three pastas to choose from&#x2014;elbows, screws and tubes&#x2014;and three sauces&#x2014;marinara, pesto and porcini cream&#x2014;along with toppings like meatballs, egg and bacon. Plus, there&apos;s a brown rice lasagna:</p>

<p>We tried the pesto spirals, tubes with marinara and meatballs and lasagna. The pasta did not seem gummy, like some gluten-free pastas tend to be&#x2014;tubes were hearty and lasagna was rich. The pesto spirals were a particular revelation, firm and flavorful. (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102071217/http://www.epicurious.com/articlesguides/blogs/editor/2014/06/mark-ladner-is-here-to-change-the-way-we-eat-gluten-free-pasta.html">Ladner has said</a> he once served Jean-Georges Vongerichten gluten-free pasta at Del Posto without telling him, and Vongerichten couldn&apos;t tell the difference.)</p>

<p>The pop-up, located at 7 Cornelia Street, is open for lunch today, Friday and Saturday (11:30 a.m.-3:30 p.m.), plus dinner on Saturday (5 p.m.-8 p.m.). They are investigating whether they can open on Sunday. Tomorrow, you can also add grated black Urbani truffles to your dish for an extra $10, and on Saturday, there will be grated white Urbani truffles as a $20 add-on. </p>
