										<p><img alt="2008_11_norov.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522150146im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_11_norov.jpg" width="120" height="116" class="right">The Health Department is urging New Yorkers to wash their hands more after noting 19% more <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522150146/http://cityroom.blogs.nytimes.com/2008/11/07/another-year-another-bout-of-stomach-flu/">cases of the norovirus stomach virus</a> at city hospitals.  Also called the &quot;stomach flu&quot; (but nothing like influenza that strikes during the cold months), the norovirus causes vomiting and diarrhea, as well as include nausea, stomach cramping, fever, chills, aches and tiredness.  A <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522150146/http://abclocal.go.com/wabc/story?section=news/health&amp;id=6494229">Health Department epidemiologist said</a>, &quot;Norovirus infection is a miserable experience, and can be serious if you become dehydrated. Thorough hand-washing with soap and warm water - especially before eating and after using the bathroom - is the best way to avoid it.&quot; You can get the norovirus in a number of ways, like &quot;Eating food or drinking liquids that have been contaminated by a person with norovirus,&quot; and you can help prevent it by washing your hands for at least 20 seconds with warm water (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522150146/http://www.nyc.gov/html/doh/html/cd/cdnor.shtml">more tips here</a>).</p>
