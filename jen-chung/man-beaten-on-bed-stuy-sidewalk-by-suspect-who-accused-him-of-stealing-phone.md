<p>A man was hospitalized after being beaten on a Brooklyn street, and police believe that his attacker pretended the victim had stolen his phone in order to confront and rob him.</p>

<p>The incident occurred on Saturday, October 15th, at 5:40 a.m. According to the NYPD, the victim, a 56-year-old man, was followed from the Kingston Avenue and Throop Avenue train station to front of 1442 Fulton Street. There police say the suspect &quot;began assaulting the victim, forcibly removed his bag and went through his pockets.&quot;</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031223610/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/NYC-Robber-Says-Sleeping-Man-Stole-His-Phone-Then-Attacks-398998301.html">NBC New York reports</a> that the suspect had woken him on the train, &quot;The suspect said his phone was missing and accused the man of stealing it.&quot; </p>

<p>The mugger punched and shoved the victim, causing him to land on a fire hydrant that left him with a &quot;deep laceration to his face,&quot; police say. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2016_10_attackbe.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031223610im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_10_attackbe.jpg" width="640" height="338"> <br> <i> The suspect (NYPD)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Investigators also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161031223610/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/NYC-Robber-Says-Sleeping-Man-Stole-His-Phone-Then-Attacks-398998301.html">found the assailant&apos;s phone</a> at the scene. The suspect, who is wanted for attempted robbery, is described (photos above) as being 20-25 years old, 5&apos;7&quot; to 5&apos;9&quot; tall and 160 pounds; he was last seen wearing a red shirt, a black jacket, a black jeans, black sneakers, and black baseball cap.</p>
