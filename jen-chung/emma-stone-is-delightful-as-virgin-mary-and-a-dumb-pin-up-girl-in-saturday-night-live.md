</div></div>					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020226/http://gothamist.com/2010/10/24/vido_the_rent_is_too_damn_high_on_s.php#photo-1">first time</a> Emma Stone hosted <em>Saturday Night Live</em>, the politician everyone was talking about was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020226/http://gothamist.com/2010/10/19/ny_governors_debate_send_in_the_clo.php#photo-1">Jimmy &quot;The Rent Is Too Damn High&quot; McMillan</a>. The second time, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020226/http://gothamist.com/2011/11/13/videos_emma_stone_hosts_snl_calls_m.php#photo-2">it was Rick Perry</a>. And <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020226/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/04/again_snl_mocks_donald_trumps_tweet.php">now</a> we&apos;ve got someone who combines McMillan&apos;s showmanship instincts with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020226/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EwG67GpJ63E">Perry&apos;s razor sharp intellect</a>. Anyway, the <em>La La Land</em> star did a terrific job hosting the show for a third time, committing to every sketch with a twinkle in her eye. </p>

<p>In the ten-to-one sketch, Stone was the perfect Virgin Mary, who gets increasingly aggravated by the visitors (some of whom you might also recognize from the Bible) barging in, uninvited, just after she&apos;s given birth in a barn. She also played a mother in a commercial for a Fisher-Price toy for, well, for boys who are more sensitive than the usual ones. <br>
 <br>
During the monologue, Stone likened SNL to &quot;high school,&quot; revisiting interactions with the cast (Vanessa Bayer is a great mean girl). In the recurring, &quot;Theatre Showcase&quot; segment, Stone, Aidy Bryant (Stone&apos;s actual high school classmate) and the rest of the well-meaning but clueless drama club kids take on today&apos;s issues, promising that all proceeds from the show will go towards Standing Rock to help get &quot;Native Americans the pipeline that they want.&quot; Pete Davidson continued his stretch of playing high school students, this time by playing a kid whose posters&#x2014;including Stone as a ditzy pinup with a love of hot dogs&#x2014;come to life to explain how important math and staying in high school are. </p>

<p>Michael Che sounded off on the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016020226/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/01/public_housing_smoke_ban.php">federal ban on smoking in public housing</a>&#x2014;Che grew up in projects on the Lower East Side&#x2014;in Weeknd Update while Leslie Jones gave her thoughts on relationships and Bayer reprised her &quot;Rachel&quot; from <em>Friends</em>, with the real Jennifer Aniston making an appearance. </p>
