</div></div>					<p>This weekend, the American Kennel Club and the Cat Fanciers&apos; Association are joining forces at the Jacob Javits Center this weekend to allow the public to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170211050154/http://www.meetthebreeds.com/">Meet the Breeds</a>.  There will be 160 purebred breeds of dogs and 41 purebred breeds of cats on the scene&#x2014;&quot;each in its own designated booth, decorated to reflect the heritage of the breed, its characteristics and unique attributes as a pet.&quot;  We went to the press preview yesterday and got to meet some of the animals&#x2014;they seem pretty excited about this weekend!<br>
 <br>
Besides seeing cats and dogs, there will be experts discussing training, grooming, nutrition and first-aid, as well as &quot;five rings with simultaneous demonstrations and activities including law enforcement K9s, agility, obedience, dog and cat breed showcases and &apos;Cat Idol.&apos;&quot;  PLUS there is an Adopt-a-Cat &quot;extravaganza,&quot; with numerous rescue organizations bringing in hundreds of sweet shelter cats and kittens who are available for adoption.</p>

<p>Tickets are $12 for adults and $8 for kids under 12, but there&apos;s a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170211050154/https://v2.interactiveticketing.com/tickets/t248_2009_meet_the_breeds_090520c157/form_purchase.php?step=intro">discount available</a> with online pre-event ticketing.  And here are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170211050154/http://www.akc.org/meet_the_breeds/the-details.html#directions">directions to the Javits Center</a>.  </p>
