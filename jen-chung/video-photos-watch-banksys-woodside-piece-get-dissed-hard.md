                            <p>Just after 8 a.m., we received the scoop that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205204451/http://gothamist.com/tags/banksy">Banksy</a>&apos;s 14th NYC piece this month <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205204451/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/14/photo_latest_banksy_seems_to_be_in_1.php#photo-1">was in Woodside, Queens</a>. Now, 13 hours later, the stencil has been basically destroyed. Man, where are those <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205204451/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/10/video_things_are_getting_interestin.php#photo-1">savvy East New Yorkers when you need them</a>?</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205204451/http://kevinmazeski.com/">Kevin Mazeski</a> sent us this video of the piece being tagged by someone who called himself &quot;Problem Child.&quot; Mazeski said, &quot;He kept saying about how this was his area. And the guy was pissed.&quot;</p>

<p><iframe src="//web.archive.org/web/20140205204451if_/http://player.vimeo.com/video/76920502" width="640" height="1134" frameborder="0" webkitallowfullscreen="" mozallowfullscreen="" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Now <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205204451/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/14/banksy_2.php">where are the babies going to pose</a>?! Anyway, rival graffiti artists marking/dissing/capping/slashing/going over each other&apos;s pieces is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140205204451/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/08/the_guy_who_keeps_tagging_the_banks.php">nothing new</a>. </p>
