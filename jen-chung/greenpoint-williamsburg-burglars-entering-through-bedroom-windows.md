<p>The police released details about two different burglaries that occurred last month in Brooklyn&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014134/http://www.nyc.gov/html/nypd/html/precincts/precinct_094.shtml">94th Precinct</a>&#x2014;and in both cases, prowlers entered by apartment windows.</p>

<p>The first burglary took place on Williamsburg&apos;s northside, near Herbert Street and Kingsland Avenue on July 19th, at 12:30 p.m. Police say that the suspect &quot;entered a first floor apartment by pushing in the air conditioner that was in a window and then climbed in the apartment. Once inside, the suspect removed an Apple MacBook Pro and cash and then fled through the front door.&quot; Police shared a photograph of the suspect (top right). <br>
 <br>
The second took place blocks away at 3:31 a.m. on July 24th, at a first floor apartment near Diamond and Newell Streets. Police say the suspect entered through the front bedroom window and then &quot;removed 2 laptop computers and 2 cellphones.&quot; The police released a &quot;photo of the suspect taken from inside Farmers in the Deli, located at 541 Myrtle Avenue, is attached.  The suspect used an app on the victim&apos;s cellphone to purchase food at the location.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to these incidents is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014134/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
