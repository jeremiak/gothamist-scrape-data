<p><img src="https://web.archive.org/web/20110811135925im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_08_strahan.jpg" class="right">After <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110811135925/http://gothamist.com/2008/08/25/michael_strahan_8_million_man.php">two days</a> of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110811135925/http://gothamist.com/2008/08/26/giants_reportedly_talking_to_straha.php">speculation</a>, given the season-ending injury of defensive end Osi Umenyiora, retired Giant Michael Strahan <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110811135925/http://www.nydailynews.com/sports/football/giants/2008/08/26/2008-08-26_michael_strahan_is_staying_retired-1.html">will stay retired</a>.  He told Fox Sports, &quot;I wish I could&apos;ve had different news for Giants fans, but I&apos;ve known for awhile I&apos;m done...<strong>After long deliberation and throwing around a million scenarios in my head for the past day, I think it&apos;s just best if I stay retired</strong>,&quot; and said he couldn&apos;t &quot;commit 100% physically, mentally, emotionally.&quot;  Currently, the plan is to move linebacker Mathias Kiwanuka to defensive end. In other Strahan news, a three-judge panel says his<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110811135925/http://ap.google.com/article/ALeqM5gYP3LHxp81DtAd6vVS51xvXOZE1QD92Q8ERO0"> child support payments should be recalculated</a>, because things like diamond jewelry to the children&apos;s grandmother and a 10-day trip to the Bahamas for the children&apos;s nanny and her family were found to be questionable.</p>
