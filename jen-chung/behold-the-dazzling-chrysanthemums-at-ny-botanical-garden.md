</div></div>					<p>If it&apos;s October, it means it&apos;s time for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013130/http://gothamist.com/2014/10/01/pumpkin_trader_joes.php">pumpkin everything</a> AND the New York Botanical Garden&apos;s incredible <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013130/http://www.nybg.org/exhibitions/2014/kiku/index.php">display of chrysanthemums, Kiku</a>. </p>

<p>Kiku is the Japanese word for the flower, and the NYBG explains, &quot;These stunning plants are trained for many months into the dazzling shapes on display.  Yes, that large dome-shaped &apos;ozikuri&apos; is one plant, and so are the ones that look like bridges, cascades, and towers.  Visitors have been awe-struck to discover that those mums are singular plants growing from one small stem. Taught by &apos;Kiku&apos; masters in Japan, the Botanical Garden&apos;s horticulture team works for many months to grow these amazing works of living art.&quot; </p>

<p>Seriously&#x2014;they are from ONE STEM, and we have videos to prove it:</p>

<p>It&apos;s hard to overstate how beautiful and incredible these formations are. I went last year and was bowled over by the combination of the flowers&apos; delicacy and rigor. Plus, it&apos;s always wonderful to visit the NYBG&apos;s beautiful grounds outside of the Enid A. Haupt Conservatory.</p>
