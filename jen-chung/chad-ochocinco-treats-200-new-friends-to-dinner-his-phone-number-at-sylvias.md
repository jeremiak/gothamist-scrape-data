</div></div><p>As promised, Chad &quot;Ochocinco&quot; Johnson treated 200 people to dinner at Sylvia&apos;s last night, all of them <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120323031955/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/12/ochocinco_wants_to_buy_you_dinner_a.php">invited by way of a Tweet</a>.  During the meal, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120323031955/http://twitter.com/#!/ochocinco/status/179366341675982848">he Tweeted</a>, &quot;Met 200 hundred people 2night. Now I&apos;ve 200 new friends, even better 200 new friends have my phone number. Now that shit is #Epic or #Crazy&quot;&#x2014;is it epic or crazy that everyone loves a New England Patriots player?!</p>

<p>Within minutes of Ocho&apos;s Tweet announcing his plans to buy dinner to the first 200 people who showed up at the famed Harlem restaurant, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120323031955/http://www.dnainfo.com/20120313/harlem/new-england-patriot-chad-ochocinco-buys-dinner-at-sylvias-for-200-fans">DNAinfo reports</a> that diners began to appear at Sylvia&apos;s, which is located at Lenox Avenue between 126th and 127th streets. Tren&apos;ness Woods-Black, granddaughter of founder Sylvia Woods and the restaurant&apos;s head of public relations declared, &quot;This was totally unplanned. That&apos;s the cool thing about Chad.&quot;</p>

<p>Besides ribs, macaroni and cheese, fried chicken and collard greens, there were also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120323031955/https://twitter.com/#!/justinebronson/status/179379889567305729/photo/1">cupcakes from Cakeman Raven</a>!  It looks like it was a fun night&#x2014;check out some photos, plus see this video of Ocho leading a prayer before the meal:</p>

Please excuse the increase in traffic to my device. Sincerely: &quot;The Black Guy&quot;&quot;</p>
