					<p>By the end of the year, circus elephants will no longer be part of Ringling Bros. and Barnum &amp; Bailey acts. Feld Entertainment, Ringling&apos;s parents company, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021213703/http://nypost.com/2016/01/11/ringling-ending-elephant-acts-over-a-year-early/">told the Associated Press</a> &quot;all of the iconic elephants will be permanently retired to the company&#x2019;s 200-acre Center for Elephant Conservation in central Florida&quot; by May. </p>

<p>Currently, there are 11 elephants touring, but the company had been working on phasing out the pachyderms <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021213703/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/16/video_alec_baldwin_joins_peta_to_st.php">after years of criticism and animal cruelty allegations</a> (Feld always insisted their animals were treated well; apparently these bull hooks are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021213703/http://gothamist.com/2013/03/20/peta_protesters_stand_in_shadows_of.php">part of &quot;being treated well&quot;</a>), announcing last year that it would <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021213703/http://gothamist.com/2015/03/05/ringling_bros_elephants.php">end all elephant acts by 2018</a>. </p>

<p>At the time, PETA said &quot;many of the elephants are painfully arthritic, and many have tuberculosis, so their retirement day needs to come now. Three years is too long for a mother elephant separated from her calf, too long for a baby elephant beaten with the sharp fireplace-poker like weapon called bullhook that Ringling handers use routinely, too long for an animal who roams up to 30 miles a day in the wild to be kept in shackles.&quot;</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2015_02_eles.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021213703im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_02_eles.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> Elephants in Brooklyn, in 2013 (Jake Dobkin / Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The AP reports:</p><blockquote>It costs about $65,000 yearly to care for each elephant, [Feld VP Alana Feld] said, and the company had to build new structures to house the retiring elephants at the center, located between Orlando and Tampa.<p></p>

<p>Last year, Feld Entertainment announced that the elephants would be phased out and eventually retired by 2018. Once the company began planning, it realized it could retire the elephants a lot sooner, Feld said...</p></blockquote><p></p>
