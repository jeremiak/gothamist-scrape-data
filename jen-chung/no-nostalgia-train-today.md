										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214752/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/%202009_12_nostaltrain2.jpg"> <img alt=" 2009_12_nostaltrain2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214752im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2009/12/ 2009_12_nostaltrain2-thumb-432x367-467631.jpg" width="92" height="78" class="image-right"> </a> </span>NYC Transit has cancelled today&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214752/http://gothamist.com/tags/nostalgiatrain">Nostalgia Train</a>, because it&apos;s trying to deal with making sure the subways and buses work on this snowy day.  According to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214752/http://mta.info/alert/alertnyct.htm">NYCT Service Alerts</a>, &quot;Due to snow removal efforts, please expect delays on all New York City bus and subway service at this time.&quot; And LIRR service is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214752/http://mta.info/alert/alertlirr.htm">described as &quot;extremely limited&quot;</a> (the Nassau County Office of Emergency Management urges people to stay at home) &#x2014;150 people were stranded for hours on a Ronkonkoma-bound LIRR train from Penn Station, because of &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214752/http://www.newsday.com/long-island/lirr-rescues-150-train-passengers-1.1662774">drifting snow, frozen switches and equipment failure</a>.&quot;  </p>
