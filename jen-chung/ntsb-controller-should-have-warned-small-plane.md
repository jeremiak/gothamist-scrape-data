title: NTSB: Controller Should Have Warned Small Plane
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_08_hud51.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150902153445im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_08_hud51.jpg" width="140" height="117" class="image-right"> </span>The National Transportation Safety Board says that the air traffic controller on duty at Teterboro Airport during the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150902153445/http://gothamist.com/2009/08/08/helicopter_and_plane_plunge_into_hu.php">crash between a small plane and sightseeing helicopter</a> had a &quot;light&quot; workload and<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150902153445/http://www.nypost.com/seven/08282009/news/regionalnews/no_excuse_for_hudson_controller_186853.htm"> &quot;nothing should have prevented him&quot;</a> from alerting the small plane&apos;s pilot about aircraft in his route.  The crash, which killed nine people, occurred as the controller were on the telephone, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150902153445/http://gothamist.com/2009/08/20/teterboro_controller_joked_about_ba.php">joking about a dead cat</a>.  Additionally, the NTSB has made <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150902153445/http://www.nytimes.com/2009/08/28/nyregion/28crash.html">suggestions for air safety over the Hudson River</a>, &quot;including having helicopters and planes fly at separate altitudes&quot; and that &quot;pilots who are to fly in the Hudson River air corridor and around the Statue of Liberty complete a special training course.&quot;</p>
