</div></div>					<p>A driver and his passenger were killed after their car crashed into another vehicle on Flatbush Avenue at Avenue U in Marine Park, Brooklyn last night around 6:40 p.m. Multiple sources say the driver was going 100 mph or faster.</p>

<p>According to the official police statement, the driver of a Nissan Maxima was traveling south on Flatbush Avenue when he struck a BMW, which was turning onto Avenue U from northbound Flatbush Avenue. The Nissan&apos;s driver, 20-year-old Philbert Martin Williams, was pronounced dead at Coney Island Hospital. His passenger, Christina Wipper, 18, was pronounced dead at the scene. The driver and passenger of the BMW suffered minor injuries and were taken to Kings County Hospital. </p>

<p>A security camera captured the crash, and the man who installed the camera said he <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231070055/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2014/05/19/1-dead-3-hospitalized-in-marine-park-brooklyn-crash/">thought Williams was speeding.</a> A source <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231070055/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/brooklyn/brooklyn-car-wreck-claims-lives-article-1.1798579">tells The Daily News</a> he was going 100 mph.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231070055/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Death-Brooklyn-Car-Crash-Marine-Park-259868361.html">One witness told NBC New York</a>, &quot;Forget it, it was crazy, he was going over 100 miles an hour. You could just tell by the sound of the breaking and everything in the crash, it was definitely over a hundred.&quot; </p>

<p>A <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231070055/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/brooklyn/brooklyn-car-wreck-claims-lives-article-1.1798579">police source said</a> the Nissan was &quot;was completely shredded. It was wrapped around the pole,&quot; while another neighborhood worker told the News the firefighters seemed stunned as they cut out someone from the car: &quot;They looked like they were in shock, like they had seen a ghost.&#x201D;</p>
