					<p>Donald Trump was stumping upstate yesterday, and to prove he&apos;s fit to be the greatest President this world has ever seen, he made the obligatory mention of the September 11, 2001 attacks. But he was actually thinking of a convenience store chain.</p>

<p>He told the crowd in Buffalo, &quot;It&#x2019;s very close to my heart because I was down there, and I watched our police and our firemen down at 7/11, down at the World Trade Center right after it came down, and I saw the greatest people I&#x2019;ve ever seen in action.&quot;</p>

<p>Besides Trump, former NY Jets coach and pedicure expert Rex Ryan spoke, as did developer Carl Paladino, whose memorable 2010 gubernatorial campaign involved him <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171020153617/http://gothamist.com/2010/09/16/wit_and_wisdom_of_republican_candid.php">essentially becoming an insult comic</a>. No stranger to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171020153617/http://gothamist.com/2010/09/16/democrats_worry_about_paladinos_vio.php">violence-tinged rhetoric</a>, Paladino said, &quot;What we&#x2019;ve watched in Washington for the past seven years is disgusting, and we&#x2019;re not going to take it anymore. Are we mad? Turn around and tell that to the press. We allow them to think they can continue to control our thinking and our acting - and we&#x2019;re done with them.&quot;</p>

<p>About 20 protesters were ejected from the First Niagara Center (Trump said, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171020153617/http://politicsnow.buffalonews.com/2016/04/18/on-primary-eve-trump-stresses-jobs-to-the-faithful/">Get &#x2019;em outta here!</a>&quot;) and six people were arrested outside.  </p>
