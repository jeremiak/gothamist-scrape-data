					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117210156/https://www.studiomuseum.org/">Studio Museum in Harlem</a> is usually closed on Mondays, but this year, it&apos;s opening its doors for Martin Luther King Jr. Day&#x2014;and admission will be free.</p>

<p>There will be full access to the museum&apos;s galleries and exhibits from 12 p.m. until 6 p.m. Exhibits include <em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117210156/https://www.studiomuseum.org/exhibition/circa-1970">Circa 1970</a></em>, which are paintings, photographs and more from 1970-1979 from the museum&apos;s collection; <em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117210156/https://www.studiomuseum.org/exhibition/the-window-and-the-breaking-the-window">The Window and the Breaking of the Window</a></em>, which looks at artworks inspired by protest (Rudy Shepherd&apos;s watercolors of the 2015 Charleston church shooting victims are particularly devastating); and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117210156/https://www.studiomuseum.org/exhibition/black-cowboy"><em>Black Cowboy</em></a>, showing the African-American presence in the history of training horses. There are also video pieces <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170117210156/https://www.studiomuseum.org/exhibition/videostudio-meeting-points">about identity and culture</a> on view. </p>

<p><em>The Studio Museum in Harlem is is located at 144 West 125th Street in Manhattan.</em></p>
