					<p>Yesterday was International Topless Day, and women in New York City gathered for a march in midtown Manhattan to celebrate their right to lose their shirts and ditch their bras.</p>

<p>GoTopless spokesperson Rachel Jessee <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161128112926/http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2733560/Bare-breasted-women-parade-New-York-seventh-annual-Go-Topless-Day.html">said</a>, &quot;New York is the ideal location&quot; for the topless parade, &quot;Since 1992, it&apos;s been one of the few cities in the United States or the world where women&apos;s topless rights are already recognized by law.&quot; Indeed&#x2014;as we all know, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161128112926/http://gothamist.com/2014/07/27/nsfw_photos_nude_modelsin_body_pain.php#photo-1">topless models are allowed to be painted</a> in the middle of Times Square.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161128112926/http://gotopless.org/faqs/list/all">GoTopless&apos;s FAQ explains</a>, &quot;In our society, men and women are supposed to have equal rights. But women are commonly arrested, fined and humiliated for daring to go topless in public, a freedom men have had for decades... On August 26, 1920, following a 72-year struggle, the U.S. Constitution was amended to grant women the right to vote. And in 1970, as an ongoing reminder of women&apos;s equality, Congress declared August 26 Women&apos;s Equality Day. But even in the 21st century, women need to stand up and demand that equality in fact&#x2014;not just in words.&quot; </p>

<p>One participant, Brooklyn resident Maggie Sawyer Bawrick, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161128112926/http://nypost.com/2014/08/25/go-topless-day-the-best-sunday-ever-in-nyc/">told the Post</a>, &quot;I developed my breasts in second grade, believe it or not, and I was really ashamed for a long time. It feels wonderful to have them out and free and feel comfortable and supported.&quot; The Post also found some men who were eager to show their support:</p><blockquote>&quot;love this!&#x201D; Tom Parks, 27, told The Post. &#x201C;This is great!&#x201D;<p></p>

<p>&#x201C;This is the best Sunday ever!&#x201D; said Jose Ruiz, 34.</p></blockquote><p></p>

They would just be surrounded by 50 dudes taking pictures of their boobs. And that&apos;s why although it&apos;s legal to be topless, it&apos;s not equal.&quot;</p>
