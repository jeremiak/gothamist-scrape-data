					<p>The holiday shopping rush is over, but decorations are still up, which makes this the perfect time to catch the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016014258/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/01/holiday_windows_2016.php#photo-1">extravagant window displays</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016014258/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/30/holiday_window_displays.php#photo-1">at Manhattan&apos;s big department stores</a>. And there&apos;s something for everyone, especially those in need of a little escape. </p>

<p>In the mood for adorable woodland animals? Head to Lord &amp; Taylor, where raccoons, bears, mice and geese are frolicking in an &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016014258/http://wwd.com/fashion-news/fashion-features/lord-taylor-holiday-2016-windows-daya-10703469/">Enchanted Forest</a>.&quot; Craving more of a classic Christmas scene? Santa and the gang are in the windows at Macy&apos;s. Bergdorf Goodman and Saks Fifth Avenue are all about fantasy land&#x2014;the former has an intricate jungle forest tableau, and the latter will bring you the Nutcracker, the Sugar Plum Fairy, and elaborate candy lights. Meanwhile, Bloomingdales has a dazzling light installation, as well as chandeliers that are being auctioned off for charity.</p>

<p>And over at Barneys, they&apos;re all about the Love Peace Joy Project. Their windows feature creations from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016014258/http://thewindow.barneys.com/holiday-2016-nick-cave/">Nick Cave</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016014258/http://thewindow.barneys.com/holiday-2016-trey-parker-matt-stone/">South Park creators Matt Stone and Trey Parker</a>, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016014258/http://thewindow.barneys.com/holiday-2016-studio-job/">Studio Job</a>.</p>

<p>See them now before they disappear early in the new year.</p>
