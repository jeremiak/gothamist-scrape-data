title: Port Authority, NYPD Agree on New WTC Security Plan
---


                            
                            
                            
                            <p><img alt="2008_07_wtcpa.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20131128231938im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_07_wtcpa.jpg" width="115" height="105" class="right">The NYPD and Port Authority have finally worked out a plan on who will be in charge of the World Trade Center when it&apos;s developed (whenever that is), and it <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131128231938/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/07/25/nyregion/25security.html?scp=4&amp;sq=port+authority&amp;st=nyt">will be the NYPD</a>.  Mayor Bloomberg noted how the WTC is a big part of downtown security and Police Commissioner Ray Kelly said, &quot;Considering the history of this site and the ongoing threat of terrorism, it only makes sense that the N.Y.P.D. devote significant resources to protect the World Trade Center.&quot;  The NYPD will develop a plan, with help from the Port Authority, and use a new WTC unit of 600 some cops to secure the area.  Still, the Port Authority police say they <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131128231938/http://www.nydailynews.com/ny_local/2008/07/25/2008-07-25_world_trade_center_security_deal_for_por-2.html">will not walk away from the site</a>; Port Authority Police Benevolent Association president Gus Danese pointed out that 37 PAPD officers died on 9/11, &quot;I lost the most police officers in the history of law enforcement in one event.  The families of the PA officers killed want the Port Authority police to maintain a presence, and we have.&quot; <br>
</p>
