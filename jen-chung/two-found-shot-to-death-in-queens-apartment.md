<p>Last night around 6:30 p.m., the police responded to a 911 call about shots fired in a Ridgewood, Queens building. Inside an apartment, they found the bodies of a man and woman.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the shooting occurred at 1815 Summerfield Street. Natalie Mejia-Tavares, 21, was found shot in the torso and pronounced dead at the scene. A man was found shot in the head; he was also pronounced dead at the scene. </p>

<p>There were &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140330184542/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/queens/2-found-shot-dead-ridgewood-queens-apartment-article-1.1737531">no signs of forced entry</a>&quot; and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140330184542/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/28/nyregion/man-and-woman-shot-to-death-in-queens.html?partner=rss&amp;emc=rss">no gun was found at the scene</a>. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140330184542/http://nypost.com/2014/03/28/man-and-woman-found-shot-dead-in-queens/">Post reports</a>, &quot;Cops said they&#x2019;re investigating it as a probable double homicide, but have not ruled out the possibility of a murder-suicide.&quot; </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140330184542/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2014/03/27/man-woman-found-shot-dead-in-ridgewood-queens-home/">1010 WINS says</a>, &quot;A neighbor said the two were a couple living together, and they had just recently moved in.&quot;</p>
