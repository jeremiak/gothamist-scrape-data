					<p>Times Square was a little more unusual yesterday, with dozens of naked models, clad in only body paint, frolicking amongst the tourists and the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161215171510/http://gothamist.com/2014/07/27/video_times_square_spider-man_attac.php">violent comic book characters.</a> The occasion: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161215171510/http://www.andygolub.com/index.php/blog/new-york-city-bodypainting-day">NYC Bodypainting Day</a>, an event created by artist and body painter extraordinaire <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161215171510/http://andygolub.com/">Andy Golub</a>.</p>

<p>Golub and a model <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161215171510/http://gothamist.com/2011/08/31/naked_flesh_still_dangerous_crimina.php">were briefly arrested</a> three years ago for public lewdness when he was painting on her&#x2014;she was topless (which is legal!) and had a G-string on. Lately, though, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161215171510/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/10/naked_models_take_over_times_square.php#photo-1">no one has been batting an eye</a> and, for NYC Bodypainting Day, the Parks Department gave him a permit for the procession, which started at Columbus Circle, made its way through Central Park and then ended up in Times Square.</p>

<p>Our photographer Sai Mohktari noted:</p><blockquote> Was a funny contrast to see people&apos;s reactions around Columbus Circle and Central Park vs. Times Square.  We all walked past picnicking families at the park and the reaction for the most part was &quot;Oh, art!&quot;/cool.  <p></p>

<p>Once we got to Times Square, people started ogling and reacting more. One girl covered her sister&apos;s eyes. Overheard lots of &quot;ew!&quot;s, etc... Reactions certainly weren&apos;t all negative, but there was definitely more shock in the air around Times Square.  </p></blockquote><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161215171510/http://pix11.com/2014/07/26/artists-to-paint-40-nude-models-in-columbus-circle/">The artist told PIX 11</a>, &quot;We&#x2019;re not using nudity as a form of exploitation. It&#x2019;s all about body acceptance,  accepting each other as we are.&quot; Indeed: The male and female bodies were of various ages, shapes and colors (ultimately ending up shades of blue).<p></p>

<p>Golub hopes NYC Bodypainting Day 2015 will have a 1,000 naked models.</p>
