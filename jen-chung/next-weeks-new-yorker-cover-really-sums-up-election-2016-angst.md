title: Next Week's New Yorker Cover Really Sums Up Election 2016 Angst
---

	
										<p>Yes, The New Yorker is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022155/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/22/the_new_yorker_will_endorse_hillary.php">totally in the bag</a> for Hillary Clinton; the magazine <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022155/http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2016/10/31/the-new-yorker-endorses-hillary-clinton">gave her a long, ringing endorsement</a> in last week&apos;s issue. Next week&apos;s cover, by illustrator Barry Blitt, taps into the enormous anxiety and dread many New Yorker subscribers are struggling with at we near Tuesday&apos;s election:</p>

<p>Blitt <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022155/http://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/cover-story-2016-11-14?mbid=social_twitter">told New Yorker art editor Fran&#xE7;oise Mouly</a> that the sentiment in his cover, titled &quot;Anything But That,&quot; will &quot;apply next week, whether it&#x2019;s one or the other.&quot; </p>

<p>&quot;And it&#x2019;s nice to take a break and not have to draw Trump&#x2014;or Hillary, for that matter,&quot; he added.</p>

<p>Blitt <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022155/http://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/cover-story-2016-11-14?mbid=social_twitter">also reflected on his previous political New Yorker covers</a> for this insane election cycle, starting with the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022155/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/17/the_new_yorkers_new_cover_will_feat.php">July 2015 cover that first featured Trump</a>, bellyflopping into the GOP; Blitt had said at the time, &quot;I&#x2019;ll certainly be watching the first televised debate, just around the corner, on August 6th. Trump never fails to provide hours of slack-jawed amazement.&quot;</p>

<p>He also said, &quot;I hope Donald Trump doesn&#x2019;t actually become President.&quot;</p>
