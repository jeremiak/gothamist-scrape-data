<p><strong>[UPDATE BELOW] </strong>West 57th Street between 6th and 7th Avenues is closed because the crane at 157 West 57th Street&#x2014;yes, at the massive <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150312/http://gothamist.com/2012/07/02/the_100_million_nyc_apartment_barri.php">157 West 57th luxury condo</a> skyscraper under construction&#x2014;got <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150312/http://abclocal.go.com/wabc/story?section=news/local/new_york&amp;id=9277781&amp;rss=rss-wabc-article-9277781&amp;utm_source=twitterfeed&amp;utm_medium=twitter">stuck at the about 20th floor</a>. With <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150312/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Crane-57th-Street-Closed--226756081.html">13,000 pounds of concrete</a>.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2013_09_157crane.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150312im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2013_09_157crane.jpg" width="640" height="969"> <br> <i> Photograph of the crane from a Gothamist reader</i></div> <p></p>

<p>You&apos;ll recall that this is the very same location where a crane partly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150312/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/29/crane_collapse_on_west_57th_crane_d.php#photo-1">snapped during Hurricane Sandy</a> and dangled over West 57th Street.</p>

<p><strong>Update 4:41 p.m.:</strong> The load was slowly lowered down to the street over the course of six hours, finally returning to terra firma at about 3:30 p.m., and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150312/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Crane-57th-Street-Closed--226756081.html?_osource=SocialFlowTwt_NYBrand">NBC New York reports</a> that the street has reopened. </p>
