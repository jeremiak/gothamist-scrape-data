<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171025132342/http://www.levainbakery.com/">Levain Bakery</a> is finally going big on the Upper West Side: The bakery&apos;s super tiny West 74th Street location will be joined by a much bigger location two blocks away. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171025132342/http://nypost.com/2016/05/30/levain-bakery-set-to-move-into-larger-location/">Real estate broker Rafe Evans told the Post</a>, &quot;They got so sick of being cramped.&quot;</p>

<p>While the bakery&apos;s humongous, decadent cookies&#x2014;they are six ounces each!&#x2014;have always attracted crowds, the lines have gotten longer earlier and earlier over the past year or so. Blame the Instagram age of sharing your food pr0n:</p>

<p><br> The new location is at 351 Amsterdam Avenue (between West 76th and 77th Streets), where a retail location vacated by an optometrist has been empty for years. There&apos;s 2,000 square feet on the first floor and another 1,000 in the basement&#x2014;a huge addition on top of their existing 400-square foot &quot;basement&quot; space on West 74th.</p>

<p>The asking monthly rent for the new space is $27,500 (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171025132342/http://www.walkermalloy.com/pimages/P10331351_Amsterdam_Flyer.pdf">PDF</a>). Which works out to be 6,875 cookies a month.</p>

<p><br>If you go to Levain, we recommend you try the chocolate brioche (think chocolate chips in between chunks of bread) and the blueberry muffins (so light and blueberry-y!). The flatbread pizzas are also quite tasty.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171025132342/http://www.levainbakery.com/locations/">Levain also has a location</a> in Harlem (2167 Frederick Douglass Blvd.) and a seasonal shop in the Hamptons. </p>
