<p>Amtrak is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503201525/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/02/amtrak_says_penn_station_commuters.php">planning to close multiple tracks at Penn Station this summer</a> for much-needed maintenance, which will cause &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503201525/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2017/05/02/amtrak-penn-station-repair-plan-2/">significant service impacts</a>&quot; for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503201525/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/06/lirr_njtransit_commuting_yay.php">beleaguered</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503201525/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/24/amtrak_train_derails_collides_with.php">commuters</a>. Maybe they can take this opportunity to fix the pipes while they&apos;re at it, because it looks like some of them broke at Penn Station this morning, sending water&#x2014;and possibly other materials&#x2014;through the ceiling onto a concourse.</p>

<p><b>Update</b>: Daniel Goodwin, who filmed the footage in the first video, told us, &quot;It just smelled like poop or rather sewage water. It was brownish but that could&#x2019;ve been from rust or debris. As I was walking by a custodian said out loud that it smelled like sewage water&#x2014;I imagine he has a better nose for these things than I&#x2014;and was telling other passers-by. You could smell it by the 1/2/3 subway entrance.&quot;</p>

<p>Goodwin has been commuting to Penn Station via NJ Transit for 10 years. He added, &quot;Before I could start recording a part of the ceiling tile fell near the guys on the platform.&quot;</p>
