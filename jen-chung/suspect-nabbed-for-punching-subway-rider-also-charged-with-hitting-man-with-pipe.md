<p>Police arrested a man accused of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170508195259/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/07/cops_search_for_man_who_randomly_pu.php">randomly punching a woman</a> at a midtown Manhattan subway station last week&#x2014;and also charged him with attacking another person in April with a metal pipe.</p>

<p>The NYPD says that around 7:30 a.m. on May 4th, Daniel Evans, 45, allegedly approached a 30-year-old woman walking on the southbound F train platform at 34th Street-6th Avenue station in Herald Square and punched her in the head. </p>

<p>The suspect fled and the victim, who suffered bruising to the head, refused medical attention at the scene. </p>

<p>Police announced Evans&apos;s arrest yesterday, adding that he was also accused of another assault at the same subway station. On April 22nd, at around 10:45 a.m., Evans allegedly approached a 23-year-old man and got into an argument with him. &quot;The argument escalated to the individual producing a pipe and striking the victim in the left forearm causing pain and swelling,&quot; police said.</p>

<p>Evans was reportedly found at the Herald Square subway station in a &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170508195259/http://nypost.com/2017/05/07/man-arrested-for-randomly-punching-woman-in-subway-station/">restricted area</a>.&quot; He was charged with two counts of assault, plus criminal trespass.</p>
