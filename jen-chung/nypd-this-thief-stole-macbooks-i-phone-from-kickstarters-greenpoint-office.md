										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2014_09_kickstarter100.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013220im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_09_kickstarter100.jpg" width="640" height="287" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>The NYPD released images and video of a person suspected of breaking into the Brooklyn offices of Kickstarter&#x2014;and then making off with various Apple products.</p>

<p>According to the police, the burglary took place on Wednesday, September 24, at 8:20 p.m.: &quot;The suspect entered 58 Kent Street, the Kickstarter Company, by grabbing the door and not letting close after a food delivery.&quot; This is also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013220/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piggybacking_(security)">known as tailgating</a>. </p>

<p>The NYPD added, &quot;The suspect waited for the employees to leave the front door area and proceeded to enter the location. Once inside, the suspect removed 2 Macbook Pros, a Macbook Air and an Apple iPhone 5S. The suspect then fled the location.&quot;</p>
