<p>A woman was sexually abused and almost raped while walking down a Manhattan street yesterday morning, police say.</p>

<p>The incident occurred around 4 a.m. on July 30, according to the NYPD, who describe the attempted rape this way: &quot;A 20 year old female was walking south bound on 5th Avenue towards west 8th street when an unknown male suspect approached her and engaged her in a conversation. The suspect then grabbed the victim from behind, and sexually abused her by forcible touching in an apparent rape attempt.&quot;</p>

<p>The victim was able to fight off her attacker, who fled. Police describe the suspect as a being 5&apos;6&quot; with a medium build and wearing a dark colored baseball cap and backpack.</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477) or for Spanish, 1-888-57-PISTA (74782).  The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108042207/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
