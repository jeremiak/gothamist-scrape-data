										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2015_12_subqp.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402034930im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_12_subqp.jpg" width="220" height="245" class="image-right"> </span>A woman riding the Q train was groped by another straphanger in Brooklyn last month, and she took a photo of the alleged perpetrator.</p>

<p>The NYPD says the incident happened during the morning rush hour, at 8:49 a.m. on Monday, November 9th. A 28-year-old woman was boarding a northbound Q train at the Gravesend Neck Road and East 16 Street station when &quot;a male suspect approached [her]...and grabbed her buttocks. The victim then took a picture with her cellphone while the suspect was on the train.&quot;</p>

<p>The victim was not injured. Police released her photo, describing the suspect as being approximately 40-45-years-old, 5&apos;4&quot; tall, with straight black short hair. He was last seen carrying a large black canvas bag.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577. </p>

<p>You can also report any inappropriate subway behavior to the MTA: Here is their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402034930/http://mta-nyc.custhelp.com/app/ask_sh">website where you can detail the incident</a>, as well upload a photo.</p>
