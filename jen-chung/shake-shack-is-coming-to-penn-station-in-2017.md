<p>Penn Station commuters, you&apos;re getting another <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170245/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/22/the_pennsy_yes_pennsy.php">tasty food option</a>&#x2014;you&apos;ll just need to be patient. Shake Shack confirmed that it is taking space in an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170245/http://nypost.com/2016/04/05/experts-warn-affordable-housing-rules-will-hurt-development/">underground concourse at the maligned transit hub</a>.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170245/http://nypost.com/2016/04/05/experts-warn-affordable-housing-rules-will-hurt-development/">The Post first reported</a>, &quot;The 10-year lease had an asking rent of $500 per square foot for the 2,489-square-foot store.&quot; A spokesperson for the popular burger chain <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170245/https://commercialobserver.com/2016/04/shake-shack-inks-deal-for-penn-station-digs/">told the Commercial Observer</a> that they&apos;ll open next year. </p>

<p>In 2014, Shake Shack announced it <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170245/https://www.shakeshack.com/2014/09/17/give-our-regards-to-broadway-nyc-shack-to-open-in-herald-square-area/">would open a location in Herald Square</a>, on Broadway and 36th Street. Both locations better be ready in time for the pre-Thanksgiving crowds, or there&apos;s going to be riots: </p>
