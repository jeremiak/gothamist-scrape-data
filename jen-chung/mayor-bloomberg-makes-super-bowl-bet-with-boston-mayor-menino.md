<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405015513/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/09/last_nights_action_heading_to_green.php">After an</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405015513/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/16/last_nights_action_on_to_san_fran.php">amazing</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405015513/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/23/last_nights_action_a_tale_of_two_tu.php">playoff run</a>, the Giants are facing off against the Patriots in the 46th Super Bowl.  And now that Mayor Bloomberg has won <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405015513/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/13/bloomberg_makes_sacrificial_giants.php">cheese curds from Green Bay</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405015513/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/18/bloomberg_wagers_street_naming_righ.php">sourdough bread from San Francisco</a>, he&apos;s giving his stomach a break and will give a New York City family the chance to savor seafood from Boston&#x2014;plus much more&#x2014;in a bet with Boston Mayor Thomas Menino. </p>

<p>Here are the details from the Mayor&apos;s office:</p><blockquote>If the Giants win, one New York City family will win a Super Tour of Boston. They will receive:<br>
&#xB7;         Four roundtrip tickets on JetBlue<br>
&#xB7;         A two-night stay at the Ritz Carlton<br>
&#xB7;         Dinner for four at Legal Seafoods Harborside<br>
&#xB7;         Dinner for four at the Top of the Hub restaurant<br>
&#xB7;         Four tickets to a performance at the Citi Performing Arts Center<br>
&#xB7;         Four box seats at a Bruins or Celtics game at the TD Garden<br>
&#xB7;         A behind-the-scenes-tour of the newly expanded Isabella Stewart Gardner museum<br>
&#xB7;         A Duck Tour ride<br>
&#xB7;         A VIP tour of the personal diaries of John Adams, which inspired the HBO series, at the Boston Public Library, the first publically-support free city library in the world<br>
&#xB7;         A VIP tour of the Samuel Adams Brewery in Jamaica Plain<br>
&#xB7;         A photo with Mayor Menino<br>
 <br>
If the Patriots win, one Boston family will win a Super Tour of New York City. Thanks to New York City&#x2019;s outstanding partners in the tourism industry, they will receive:<br>
&#xB7;         Four roundtrip tickets on the Delta Shuttle<br>
&#xB7;         A two-night stay at the Grand Hyatt<br>
&#xB7;         Dinner for four at the newly reopened Russian Tea Room<br>
&#xB7;         Lunch or dinner for four at B. Smith&#x2019;s restaurant<br>
&#xB7;         Four club seats at a Rangers or Knicks game at Madison Square Garden<br>
&#xB7;         Four tickets to the Phantom of the Opera, celebrating its 10,000th performance right after the Super Bowl<br>
&#xB7;         A ride with the captain on Statue Cruises to Liberty and Ellis Islands with a VIP tour of the Statue of Liberty crown and museum, including lunch<br>
&#xB7;         A VIP tour of the new American wing at the Metropolitan Museum of Art<br>
&#xB7;         A VIP tour of the Brooklyn Brewery<br>
&#xB7;         A photo with Mayor Bloomberg</blockquote>Fun fact: In 2008, when Bloomberg and Menino <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405015513/http://gothamist.com/2008/01/30/mayors_of_nyc_a.php">made a bet for the 42nd Super Bowl, it was ALL food, like</a>, &quot;100 cups of New England Clam Chowder from Legal Sea Foods&quot; and &quot;12 dozen Boston Cream Pies and 12 Dozen Parker House Rolls (in honor of Tom Brady&apos;s #12) from Boston&apos;s Omni Parker House Hotel.&quot;  Obviously Bloomberg <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405015513/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/10/nanny_nyc_wonders_if_you_really_wan.php">changed his tune about portion sizes</a>.<p></p>
