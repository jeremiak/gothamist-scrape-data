					<p>Midtown workers now have more, much-needed lunch (and post-work dinner) options, as Whole Foods opened a new 43,000 square foot location across from Bryant Park last week. The store not only sells groceries and prepared food items, but there&apos;s also a number of new food options, including Frankies Sputino, Japanese omakase from Genji Sushi, a raw bar from Chef Daniel Boulud, and a place that offers &quot;artisanal&quot; avocado toast.</p>

<p>This Whole Foods also looks lovely, with big windows and warm wood finishes. Besides the new culinary offerings, there&apos;s a coffee and juice bar, called &quot;News Agency&quot; (a node to classic newsstands) which opens at 6:30 a.m., a half hour before the rest of the store opens at 7 a.m. </p>

<p>There&apos;s also a &quot;Produce Butcher.&quot; That&apos;s right, it&apos;s someone who will, the store explains, &quot;cut, slice, dice, julienne, chop, and grate any produce item at a shopper&#x2019;s request.&quot; The Produce Butcher&apos;s service cost, for items by the pound (like apples) is $1 per pound (5 pound maximum), while items sold individually (like a pineapple) cost $1 on top of the price of the fruit.</p>

Kano by Genji Sushi: Shoppers can indulge in a traditional Japanese omakase, available at various price levels, run by Genji Sushi, longstanding partners of Whole Foods Market.<br><br><br>
Harbor Bar: Featuring a signature menu item created exclusively in partnership with renowned Chef Daniel Boulud, this raw bar will offer oyster towers, lobster rolls and seafood-centric small plates. The bar will also offer 24 beers on tap, draft wine from Gotham Project and a full cocktail menu.<br><br><br>
Simit + Smith: The store will feature a cart dedicated to serving authentic Turkish-style artisanal breads from this celebrated local purveyor, including Simit, a circular, sesame-covered bread that&#x2019;s soft and light on the inside and crunchy on the outside. Simit is traditionally served with feta cheese and sliced olives.<br><br><br>
Seed + Mill: This is Seed + Mill&#x2019;s first offering with Whole Foods Market in New York City. The cart will feature a tahini mill, churning out jars of fresh, warm tahini made from premium Ethiopian seeds, and 8 to 10 flavors of handmade halva, including some vegan varieties. Seed + Mill at Bryant Park will also feature the brand&#x2019;s famous tahini goat milk ice cream, topped with halva crumbles and fresh tahini.<br><br><br>
Quick service kiosks - The store will offer an array of freshly prepared items with a focus on quick service. Food orders can be placed through an easy-to-use digital ordering kiosk. Pizza: The Bryant Park location will be the first Whole Foods Market in New York City to offer Detroit-style square pies with a variety of seasonal toppings.<br><br><br>
