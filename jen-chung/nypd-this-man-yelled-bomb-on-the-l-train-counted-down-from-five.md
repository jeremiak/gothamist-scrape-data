<span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161028111200/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_09_lbrt.jpg"> <img alt="2016_09_lbrt.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161028111200im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2016/09/2016_09_lbrt-thumb-240x512-966683.jpg" width="180" height="384" class="image-right"> </a> </span>Hours before an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161028111200/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/17/chelsea_explosion.php">explosion rocked Chelsea</a> on Saturday, a subway passenger yelled &quot;BOMB!&quot; and then counted down from five, according to police. Now the NYPD intends to charge him with falsely reporting a bomb threat&#x2014;if they can find him.<p></p>

<p>The incident occurred on Saturday, September 17th, at 5:45 p.m.: According to the NYPD, the man was riding a &quot;northbound&quot; L train leaving the Bedford Avenue station in Williamsburg when he yelled &quot;BOMB!&quot; and counted down from 5. Then&#x2014;wait for it&#x2014;he yelled &quot;BOOM!&quot; Hilarious, right?</p>

<p>A passenger took a photograph of the man before she left the train at First Avenue. </p>

<p>The suspect (pictured, right) is described as being in his 20s and 5&apos;10&quot; and was last seen wearing a white t-shirt and blue pants.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477) or for Spanish, 1-888-57-PISTA (74782). </p>

All calls are strictly confidential.</p>
