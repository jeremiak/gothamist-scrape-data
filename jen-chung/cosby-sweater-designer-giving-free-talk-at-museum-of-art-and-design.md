</div></div>					<p>There&apos;s a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140521022513/http://www.thecosbysweaterproject.com/">Cosby Sweater Tumblr</a> and there&apos;s even been a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140521022513/http://billcosby.com/polls">Cosby Sweater Poll</a> on Bill Cosby&apos;s site, but what does the designer behind many of those bold geometric sweaters think? This Thursday, you can find out&#x2014;fashion designer Koos Van Den Akker is giving a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140521022513/http://madmuseum.org/events/koos-van-den-akker">free talk at the Museum of Art of Design</a> this Thursday. </p>

<p>Dutch-born Van Den Akker, who trained in France, is known for his &quot;couture collage&quot;&#x2014;his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140521022513/http://voguepatterns.mccall.com/koos-van-den-akker-pages-305.php">intricately patterned designs</a>&#x2014;and his bold designs caught the eye of Cosby&apos;s wife, who admired a friend who wore Van Den Akker. That friend then <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140521022513/http://fashionista.com/2013/07/bill-cosby-is-holding-a-sweater-tournament-so-we-talked-to-the-guy-who-designed-them#awesm=~oEFUo7uWEg6MaR">got Cosby a sweater as a gift</a>. Cosby recently explained why the sweaters became a calling card for Dr. Cliff Huxtable, his groundbreaking take on the sitcom father:</p><blockquote>Instead of the jacket and the tie and the professional look of a highly-educated person who knows more than any of us, put a sweater on him. It&apos;s comfort. He&apos;s comfortable. He makes you feel comfortable.<p></p>

<p>When we got into geometric cuts, then that became art. And the fellow&apos;s name is Koos Van Den Akker and that art, those blocks of leather, different shades of cotton, silk, interwoven. <br>
They&apos;re really pieces of art. </p></blockquote>Cosby admitted, &quot;I don&apos;t wear them anymore. In those days, it was all right to have shoulder pads. And it was very smooth and cool looking and I had a flat stomach.&quot; <p></p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20140521022513if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/-SCfYf0q8dM" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Even though the term &quot;Cosby sweater&quot; has been used pejoratively, Von Den Akker doesn&apos;t mind: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140521022513/http://www.collectorsweekly.com/articles/bill-cosby-schools-us-about-those-crazy-sweaters/">he told Collectors Weekly</a>, &quot;There have been jokes about the sweaters in the &#x2018;New Yorker&#x2019; and places like that, and it&#x2019;s always negative. A Bill Cosby sweater stands for &#x2018;crazy,&#x2019; and I love it.&quot; </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20140521022513if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/a-34LnSpZ_A" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The talk will be at The Theater at MAD, starting at 7 p.m.</p>
