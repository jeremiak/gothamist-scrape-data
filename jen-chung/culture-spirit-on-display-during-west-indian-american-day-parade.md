</div></div><p>Elaborate, colorful, and sometimes skimpy costumed celebrants strutted their stuff during the 42nd Annual West Indian American Carnival yesterday.  The parade, which celebrates Caribbean culture, is the city&apos;s biggest parade, attracting millions to Eastern Parkway.  One paradegoer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110811080237/http://ny1.com/content/top_stories/105350/caribbean-pride-fills-the-streets-of-brooklyn/Default.aspx">told NY1</a>, &quot;It&apos;s very lively and it&apos;s very colorful. Multi-cultural, what can I say, it&apos;s people all over the world. Everybody gets together and just embraces each other,&quot; while one dancer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110811080237/http://www.nydailynews.com/ny_local/2009/09/08/2009-09-08_revelers_enjoy_picture_perfect_day_at_annual_brooklyn_parade.html">explained to the Daily News</a>, &quot;We wine and we gyrate to the pulsating music. You&apos;re getting loose, you&apos;re feeling no hangups, nothing, no inhibitions. It&apos;s just about having a good time.&quot;</p>

<p>We liked the parade strategy of someone NY1 spoke to, &quot;<strong>We come mostly for the food. We love the food. We march up and down the parkway and gain weight. March back and lose the weight.</strong>&quot;</p>
