					<p>Tomorrow, a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925220644/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/27/friends_central_perk_nyc.php">re-creation of the Central Perk cafe</a> from <em>Friends</em> will open to the public, full of props from the hit sitcom and free coffee. In other words, could this <em>be</em> anymore nostalgic? And could I feel any older?</p>

<p>While it&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925220644/http://gothamist.com/2014/04/23/nyc_cat_cafe_photos_video.php">no cat cafe</a>, the pop-up does offer the actual orange couch from the show, Ross&apos;s list of celebrities he&apos;s allowed to sleep with, the cover of &quot;Buffay the Vampire Slayer&quot; (the porno that Phoebe&apos;s twin sister Ursula starred in) and Pat the dog statue that Joey Tribbiani bought with his big soap opera earnings. </p>

<p>The pop-up is to celebrate the 20th anniversary of <em>Friends</em>&apos; premiere. James Michael Tyler, who played Gunther, the manager of Central Perk, will be on-hand for select appearances. He told us that he bleached his hair for the first time in 10 years&#x2014;the show ended in 2004&#x2014;to &quot;reprise&quot; the character for the event. </p>

<p>When Warners Bros. approached him, Tyler said the anniversary caught him by surprise, &quot;My whole concept of time has been that it&apos;s 10 years since it ended, and, wow, it&apos;s been 20 years since it began!&quot; He said, &quot;I fully embrace it&#x2026; I was a minor character on a major show.&quot; Then he looked down at his lime green shirt, &quot;Now I&apos;m wearing loud shirts again.&quot; But only Gunther was brave enough to confront Phoebe&apos;s oversharing boyfriend:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="//web.archive.org/web/20170925220644if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/rYJVo9v6MNI" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Tyler, who still acts and is also working on an animated feature screenplay and TV pilot (&quot;a dark comedy&quot;), isn&apos;t sure if the exact concept of <em>Friends</em> would work with today&apos;s audiences, since it&apos;s &quot;a different world&quot; now, driven by smartphones and social media and not necessarily meeting up IRL. However, he said, &quot;The characters are universal.&quot; Also, his favorite episode is The One With The Prom Video:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20170925220644if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/_qqQIxifU-w?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>If only Ross could see into the future, the future where  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925220644/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/11/david_schwimmer.php">his East Village neighbors would hate him</a>.</p>

<p>As you take in the paraphernalia, you can drink <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925220644/http://www.eightoclock.com/friends/">coffee courtesy of Eight O&apos;Clock</a>, but in paper cups&#x2014;the huge, oversized mugs that could fit on an infant&apos;s head are available for sale for $20. There are also bracelets ($29-32) with charms like the orange couch, a smelly cat and a speech bubble with the words &quot;How you doin&apos;?&quot; According to a press release, the bracelets are &quot;all designed to bring back the happy times fans shared while watching the series.&quot; So the real question is: What kind of <em>Friends</em> super fan are you if you&apos;re not buying a $13 iPhone case? Also, did any of the <em>Friends</em> have cellphones? And were they aware of Brooklyn? And why was the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925220644/http://gothamist.com/2011/05/10/nyc_as_seen_on_tv_top_5_worst.php#photo-3">apartment so freaking huge</a>?</p>

<p>Fine, the &quot;You are my lobster&quot; bracelet is kinda cute.</p>
