title: Paterson Blames Failed (Preliminary) Budget Talks on Skelos
---

<p><img src="https://web.archive.org/web/20110521075915im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_11_pater11.jpg" class="right">Yesterday, Governor Paterson <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110521075915/http://wcbstv.com/local/secret.budget.meeting.2.866093.html">had a &quot;secret&quot; meeting</a> with Assembly Speaker Sheldon Silver and Senate Majority Leader Dean Skelos to discuss the state&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110521075915/http://gothamist.com/2008/10/29/governor_paterson_in_dc_bail_ny_out.php">severe budget crisis.</a> And apparently it didn&apos;t go very well: The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110521075915/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/11/17/nyregion/17budget.html?ref=nyregion">NY Times reports</a> that they &quot;failed to reach a resolution...throwing into doubt the chances that an agreement could be reached&quot; before Tuesday&apos;s emergency legislative session.  Paterson, whose proposed budget cuts would <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110521075915/http://gothamist.com/2008/11/13/patersons_budget_cuts_2_billion_by.php">hit Medicaid and schools the hardest</a>, blamed Skelos, stating, &quot;<strong>It was clear from the meeting that Majority Leader Skelos has not changed his position</strong>,&quot; but the Senate GOP said, &quot;<strong>We are being called to Albany by the governor to act on specific bills, which he has yet to send us</strong>.&quot;  And even some Democrats may be uneasy, since the plan hits hospitals and schools.</p>
