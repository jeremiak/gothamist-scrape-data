<p>A boy died from injuries he suffered from a Sunday car crash, and his stepfather will face upgraded charges. </p>

<p>Anthony Pollydore <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140328075815/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/24/allegedly_drunk_dad_drive_car_into.php">had been initially charged</a> with aggravated vehicular assault, driving while intoxicated, and vehicular assault, but now that Dylan Perry has died, Pollydore <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140328075815/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/nyc-crime/brooklyn-boy-drunk-driving-crash-dies-article-1.1732099">will likely face tougher charges</a>.</p>

<p>Pollydore had been driving on Eastern Parkway at 8 a.m. when, after <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140328075815/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/25/nyregion/crash-kills-boy-11-stepfather-charged.html?ref=nyregion">hitting a median and fishtailing</a>, he barreled into a pole at Dean Street. Stepsons Dylan and Alex Perry, 12, were injured, as was his son Kyle, 5. None of the children were wearing seatbelts; the n, &quot;The youngest child was thrown into the front of the car but &apos;didn&apos;t have any injuries, which is a miracle,&apos; a source said. The 12-year-old had minor injuries to his leg.&quot;</p>

<p>Pollydore, who has other previous DWI convictions, blew a 0.094 for his blood alcohol, over the limit of 0.08.</p>
