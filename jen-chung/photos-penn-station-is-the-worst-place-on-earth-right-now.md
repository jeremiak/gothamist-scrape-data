					<p>You may be happy you didn&apos;t <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043023/http://gothamist.com/2014/11/26/thanksgiving_weather_travel.php">plan on flying out of town for Thanksgiving</a> but if you&apos;re traveling by train, it&apos;s possible you are in NYC&apos;s center of soul-crushing activity: Penn Station. </p>

<p>Photographer Tod Seelie braved the crowds trying to get to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170102043023/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/11/23/fashion/crisis-negotiators-give-thanksgiving-tips.html?_r=0">their chill family gatherings</a> by Long Island Rail Road, NJ Transit and Amtrak today. He found that things started to get packed at 3 p.m., with folks putting in a half day at work then rushing for their train&#x2014;with luggage and pies&#x2014;gazing at the departures board in desperation.</p>
