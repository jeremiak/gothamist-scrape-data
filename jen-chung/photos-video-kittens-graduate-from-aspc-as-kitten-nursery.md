					<p>Earlier this year, the ASPCA <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160505225342/https://www.aspca.org/about-us/press-releases/aspca-open-new-facility-care-nycs-most-vulnerable-animals">opened up a new nursery</a> to offer specialized, &quot;life-saving care for kittens too young to survive on their own.&quot; Yesterday, the last group of adorable felines &quot;graduated,&quot; and the ASPCA is says they will be up for adoption at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160505225342/https://www.aspca.org/nyc/adoption-center">ASPCA Adoption Center</a>.</p>

<p>Gail Buchwald, senior vice president of the ASPCA Adoption Center, noted that the facility is NYC&apos;s first high-volume, neonate kitten nursery and nearly 300 kittens have passed through the doors. Ooh and ahh right now, then go adopt one or two:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20160505225342if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/K9kFmFh1qKM?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The ASPCA says, &quot;The nursery was designed to contain over 200 adjustable cages, each with the ability to accommodate a combination of orphaned kittens or a litter and nursing mother. Kittens received around-the-clock, life-saving care from more than 60 specially-trained ASPCA staff and volunteers until they were old enough to be micro-chipped, vaccinated and spayed/neutered. At eight weeks of age, they are ready to be made available for adoption at the ASPCA Adoption Center.&quot;</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160505225342/https://www.aspca.org/blog/were-expecting-new-nursery-will-help-curb-kitten-season">One technician explained</a> as he fed kitten milk replacement through a syringe to a five-day-old kitten, &quot;We&#x2019;re doing the mama&#x2019;s job.&quot; You can <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160505225342/https://www.aspca.org/secure/201406ea1-web-p1?ms=wb_blo_wereexpectingnewnurserywillhelpcurbkittenseason&amp;initialms=wb_blo_wereexpectingnewnurserywillhelpcurbkittenseason&amp;pcode=WEB1BLOG&amp;lpcode=WEB2BLOG">also donate to the ASPCA</a>.</p>
