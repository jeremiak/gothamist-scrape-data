National Weather Service officials will also look at the storm data and see whether it was in fact a tornado.  </p>

<p>The NY Times <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120605125132/http://www.nytimes.com/2010/09/18/nyregion/18weather.html?hp">looks at the storm&apos;s path</a>: </p><blockquote> The worst of the storm started about 5 p.m., as a warm front from the south approached New York City. A line of thunderstorms moved through, intensifying as they reached the shore, causing winds to rotate within a small area, a characteristic that prompts a tornado warning, according to John Murray, a National Weather Service meteorologist. The storm tore through Staten Island, then Brooklyn, hitting Park Slope and Bedford-Stuyvesant hard. It then moved into Queens, striking strongly at Middle Village, Forest Hills and Bayside.</blockquote><p></p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120605125132/http://www.nydailynews.com/ny_local/2010/09/16/2010-09-16_fastmoving_storm_packing_tornado_conditions_tears_across_new_york_city_with_wind.html">Daily News has details on the fatality</a>:  &quot;Aline Levakis, 30, of Mechanicsburg, Pa., was killed when a tree crushed a car she was driving on the Grand Central Parkway near Jewel Ave. in Queens, police said. Her husband, Billy Levakis, 60, was treated for minor injuries at New York Hospital Queens and released, a family friend said.&quot;  Apparently the couple had just switched seats. </p>
