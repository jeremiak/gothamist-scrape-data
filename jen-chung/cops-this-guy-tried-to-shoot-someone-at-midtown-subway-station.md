title: Cops: This Guy Tried To Shoot Someone At Midtown Subway Station
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2014_07_midtownsubwaygun.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510015234im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_07_midtownsubwaygun.jpg" width="250" height="337" class="image-right"> </span>The NYPD is asking the public for help in finding someone who apparently tried and failed to fire his gun at a Manhattan subway station earlier this month.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incident occurred at 3:05 a.m. on July 13th, when a 50-year-old man got into a &quot;verbal dispute&quot; with the suspect on a northbound 4 train: &quot;As both parties exited the train at the 51 Street and Lexington Avenue subway station, the male suspect attempted to discharge a firearm at the victim and subsequently fled after the gun failed to fire.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call Crime stoppers at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477).  </p>

<p>The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510015234/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.<br>
