</div></div>					<p>We may be bidding <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160426124628/http://gothamist.com/2008/09/22/yankees_pregame.php">farewell to Yankee Stadium</a>, but there is a new (old) Yankee Stadium emerging:  LEGO artist <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160426124628/http://seankenney.com/">Sean Kenney</a>, along with help from a grade schooler, has been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160426124628/http://seankenney.com/portfolio/yankee_stadium_preview/">working on a replica of the stadium</a> for the past few years.  He writes, &quot;It will be complete in the next few months; these preview photos give you an in-progress look at the sculpture. <strong>The sculpture is 5 feet wide and 5 feet long, built to an approximate scale of 1:150.</strong>&quot;</p>

<p>Kenney designed the awesome <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160426124628/http://www.brickapple.com/">Brick Apple</a>--and check out <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160426124628/http://www.seankenney.com/portfolio/greenwich_village/">his LEGO version of Greenwich Village</a> and his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160426124628/http://seankenney.com/portfolio/empire_state_building/">Empire State Building</a> is on display at the real Empire State Building.</p>
