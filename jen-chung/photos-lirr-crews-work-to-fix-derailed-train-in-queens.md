	<p>Yesterday&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130604104715/http://gothamist.com/2013/03/18/winter_weather_not_giving_up_despit.php">wintry mix</a> hit the region and many are facing a messy commute in. Long Island Rail Road riders, on certain lines, are also dealing with cancelled and crowded trains after non-passengert train derailed in Rego Park, Queens.</p>

<p>The MTA says, &quot;Out of the 144 normal AM rush hour trains, 11 trains will be canceled, 5 will be diverted to Brooklyn&#x2019;s Atlantic Terminal and 4 trains will be terminated at Jamaica, where commuters will be accommodated by other trains.&quot; You can see which trains <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130604104715/http://mta.info/news/stories/?story=998">were affected here</a>. </p>

<p>The MTA also shared photos of crews working through the night into the morning to clear the derailment and fix the track: &quot;One of the LIRR&#x2019;s four main tracks between Penn Station and Jamaica is out of service. Repairs are continuing to broken rails and to remove four cars of the train that derailed last night.&quot;</p>
