title: State Senate Dems Agree On Tentative MTA Bailout Plan
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_04_metrdca.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151123021759im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_04_metrdca.jpg" width="140" height="108" class="image-right"> </span>According to reports, the State Senate Democrats have <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151123021759/http://www.nypost.com/seven/05042009/news/regionalnews/senate_reaches_tentative_deal_in_mta_bai_167622.htm">reached a tentative agreement on a plan</a> to bailout the MTA.  Originally, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151123021759/http://gothamist.com/2009/04/21/senate_dems_mta_bailout_plan_has_ta.php">plan floated</a> by Senate Majority Malcolm Smith included a taxi dropoff surcharge, an additional motor vehicle registration fee and a payroll tax (the payroll tax would fund the bulk of the plan), all while raising fares by a more moderate 8%; it appears that the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151123021759/http://www.ny1.com/content/top_stories/98443/senate-strikes-tentative-deal-on-mta-bailout/Default.aspx">tentative plan has</a> a 50-cent (not a $1) taxi surcharge and the already-reported <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151123021759/http://gothamist.com/2009/05/01/patersons_plan_to_save_mta_means_sh.php">payroll tax reimbursement to school districts</a>. Next up, getting the plan approved by the rest of the State Senate, and if it passes, then the Assembly and Governor.  Governor Paterson doesn&apos;t think it&apos;s a great plan, but signaled he <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151123021759/http://gothamist.com/2009/05/03/paterson_wants_mta_plan_vote_this_w.php">was open to any plan</a> over no plan-and-a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151123021759/http://gothamist.com/2009/03/25/doomsday_mta_board_votes_on_fare_hi.php">doomsday fare hike</a>.  </p>
