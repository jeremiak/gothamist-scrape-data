</div></div>					<p>Finally, Tim Burton&apos;s gothic imagination has made it to Macy&apos;s Thanksgiving Day Parade&#x2014;his Boy B. balloon appeared this morning much to the delight of parade watchers on the ground and at home. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150320051748/http://twitter.com/#!/MirandaLea13/status/139744982495010816">One Tweeted</a>, &quot;TIM BURTON&apos;S BALLOON! That brief moment totally made my day =]&quot; while <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150320051748/http://twitter.com/#!/AjCuzzacrea/status/139752075566387200">another admitted</a>, &quot;My hero Tim Burton got his own float!? I actually teared up. &#xDC;ber dork alert:) #holidays.&quot; </p>

<p>Macy&apos;s parade organizers had <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150320051748/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/21/tim_burton_will_have_creepy_balloon.php">been hoping Burton would agree to design a balloon</a> for them for years, and Burton&apos;s dark mind offered up this backstory for his offering: &quot;B. was created, Frankenstein&#x2019;s monster-style, from the leftover balloons used in children&#x2019;s parties at the Great Ormond Street Hospital in London. Forbidden from playing with other children because of his jagged teeth and crazy-quilt stitching, B. retreated to a basement lair, where he obsesses over Albert Lamorisse&#x2019;s film The Red Balloon and dreams that he, too, will be able to fly someday.&quot;  </p>
