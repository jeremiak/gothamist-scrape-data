										<p><img alt="2008_09_newcrane.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150759im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_09_newcrane.jpg" width="120" height="131" class="right">The Daily News <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150759/http://www.nydailynews.com/news/2008/09/29/2008-09-29_new_cranes_up_at_disaster_site.html">checked out the new crane</a> at the 1st Avenue and East 91 Street site where a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150759/http://gothamist.com/2008/05/30/breaking_manhat.php">crane collapsed, killing two men, in May</a>. Neighbors were wary--one said, &quot;The first thing I thought was, &apos;Oh, here we go again,&apos;&quot; while another said, &quot;<strong>I tend to think they won&apos;t screw up twice, but I don&apos;t want to be the one who is told, &apos;I told you so.&apos;</strong>&quot; The Buildings Department said the crane is &quot;fresh out of the box,&quot; referring to how the previous crane was 25 years old--and was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150759/http://gothamist.com/2008/06/08/questions_about_4.php">struck by lightning in 2007</a>.  The DOB <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150426150759/http://gothamist.com/2008/09/20/industry_unhappy_with_citys_new_cra.php">recently introduced new rule</a>s requiring crane contractors to have the cranes&apos; manuals on hand for inspectors, which the industry isn&apos;t very happy with because many cranes are so old, the manuals are lost.</p>
