<p>Two police officers were shot while on duty at a public housing building on East 156th Street in the Bronx. </p>

<p>According to initial reports, one officer was grazed in the head while the other was shot in the leg around 8 p.m. The NYPD says that the officers are in stable condition:</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160511061824/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/NYPD-Officer-Shot-Bronx-367754811.html">NBC New York reports</a>, &quot;The officers, who are assigned to Police Service Area 7 -- the unit that patrols public housing within the NYPD&apos;s 40th and 42nd precincts  -- were inside a NYCHA complex on East 156th Street when they were shot in a hallway, sources said.&quot;</p>

<p>At least one suspect is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160511061824/http://abc7ny.com/news/2-police-officers-shot-at-melrose-houses-in-the-bronx;-officers-expected-to-survive/1187590/">apparently in custody</a>:</p>

<p>A press conference will be held at Lincoln Hospital at 9:40 p.m. Mayor Bill de Blasio will be there; he was already in the Bronx, giving the State of the City address. </p>

<p><b>Update</b>: The NYPD said, &quot;One suspect was found dead with an apparent self inflicted gunshot wound, several suspects in custody &amp; 2 guns recovered.&quot;</p>
