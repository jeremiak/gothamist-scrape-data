	<p>Boston, the surrounding suburbs and the rest of the country breathed a sigh of relief when Boston Marathon bombing suspect <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524022040/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/19/massachusetts_authorities_give_upda.php">Dzhokhar Tsarnaev was captured</a> after a massive manhunt. Tsarnaev was found in a backyard&#x2014;hiding in a boat&#x2014;in Watertown, hours after the lockdown was lifted. Here&apos;s what the neighborhood looked like before he was apprehended and after.</p>

<p>Here&apos;s footage of the loud shots at one point during the confrontation between cops and the suspect: </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524022040if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/YhHGj9iHHnc" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524022040/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/17/all_three_fatalities_in_boston_bomb.php#photo-1">Three people were killed</a> at the marathon, 8-year-old Martin Richard, 29-year-old Krystle Campbell and 23-year-old Lu Lingzi, and a MIT security officer, 26-year-old Sean Collier, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130524022040/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/19/one_boston_bomb_suspect_is_dead_as.php">was &quot;assassinated&quot;</a> by Tsarnaev and his older brother Tamerlan last night. Nearly 200 people were injured, many grievously, during Monday&apos;s bombing at the marathon.</p>
