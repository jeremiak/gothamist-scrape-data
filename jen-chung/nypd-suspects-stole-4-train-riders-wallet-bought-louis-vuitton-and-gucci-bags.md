<p>A 4 train rider&apos;s stolen wallet ended up with suspects who like luxury goods.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2015_12_shoplift.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160507200535im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_12_shoplift.jpg" width="300" height="184" class="image-right"> </span>According to the NYPD, a 29-year-old woman boarded a southbound 4 train at Grand Central Terminal on October 21st at 5:30 p.m.:  &quot;[The victim&apos;s] bag [was] jostled while en route to Bowling Green, where upon disembarking she discovered that her bag was open and her wallet containing credit cards and personal identification documents had been removed.&quot;</p>

<p>At 8:09 p.m. that day, police say that &quot;two unknown female suspects used the victim&apos;s credit card to make unauthorized men&apos;s luxury purchases totaling $6,080.67 at the Louis Vuitton New York store at Macy&apos;s Herald Square, 151 West 34 Street, in the confines of the Midtown South Precinct.&quot; Then, a half hour later, &quot;the suspects used the victim&apos;s credit card to make an unauthorized purchase of a handbag totaling $2,395.25 at Gucci at Macy&apos;s Herald Square,&quot; cops added.<br>
 <br>
Police released surveillance images of the suspects.</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160507200535/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">the Crime Stoppers Website</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then entering TIP577. All calls are kept strictly confidential.</p>
