<p>When Beyonce took the stage, she paid tribute to her grandmother, uncle and especially her mother for their creativity and work in outfitting her during her Destiny&apos;s Child years, because &quot;high-end labels didn&apos;t really want to dress four black, country, curvy girls.&quot; But thanks to her mother and uncle&apos;s efforts, &quot;When I wore these clothes I felt like Khaleesi.&quot; </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021130304/http://www.eonline.com/news/770710/beyonce-recalls-a-time-when-high-end-labels-wouldn-t-dress-the-four-black-country-curvy-girls-of-destiny-s-child">Here&apos;s a transcription of her speech from E!</a>: </p><blockquote>I feel so much love and I feel so proud. As long as I can remember, fashion has been part of my life. Its effect on me actually started before I was born. Many of you guys don&apos;t know this, but my grandmother was a seamstress. My grandparents did not have enough money; they could not afford my mother&apos;s Catholic school tuition. So my grandmother sewed clothes for the priests and the nuns and made uniforms for the students in exchange for my mother&apos;s education. She then passed this gift onto my mother and taught her how to sew.&quot;<p></p>

<p>Starting out in Destiny&apos;s Child, high-end labels didn&apos;t really want to dress four black, country, curvy girls, and we couldn&apos;t afford designer dresses and couture. My mother was rejected from every showroom in New York. But like my grandmother, she used her talent and her creativity to give her children their dreams. My mother and my uncle, God rest his soul, made all of our first costumes, individually sewing hundreds of crystals and pearls, putting so much passion and love into every small detail. When I wore these clothes I felt like Khaleesi. I had an extra suit of armor. It was so much deeper than any brand name.</p></blockquote><p></p>

<blockquote>My mother is fabulous and beautiful and she&apos;s here tonight. My mother, my grandmother, and my uncle are always with me so I cannot fail. And this to me is the true power and potential of fashion. It&apos;s a tool for finding your own identity. It transcends style, and it&apos;s a time capsule of all of our greatest milestones,. So to my mother, my grandmother, my uncle, thank y&apos;all. Thank you for showing me that having presence is about far more than the clothes you wear and your physical beauty. Thank you for showing me how to take risks, work hard, and live life on my own terms.

<p>I want to say thank you to every designer who works tirelessly to make people think they can write their own story. Y&apos;all are fairy godmothers, magicians, sculptors, and sometimes even our therapists. I encourage you to not forget this power you have or to take it lightly. We have the opportunity to contribute to a society where any girl can look at a billboard or magazine cover and see her own reflection. Soul has no color, no shape, no form. Just like all of your work, it goes far beyond what the eye can see. You have the power to change perception, to inspire and empower, and to show people how to embrace their complications, and see the flaws, and the true beauty and strength that&apos;s inside all of us.&quot;Thank you so much for this incredible award. I&apos;ll never forget this night. God bless you all.
