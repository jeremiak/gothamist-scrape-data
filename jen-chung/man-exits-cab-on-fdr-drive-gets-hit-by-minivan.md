<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-left" style=" width:250px; "> <img alt="2012_01_fdrdriver.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20120116220253im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2012_01_fdrdriver.jpg" width="250" height="193"> <br> <i>Via WABC 7</i></div> A taxi cab passenger was struck by another vehicle on the FDR Drive last night around 96th Street.  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120116220253/http://abclocal.go.com/wabc/story?section=news/local/new_york&amp;id=8502353">According to WABC 7</a>, the cab was stuck in traffic at 11:30 p.m., and the man &quot;apparently didn&apos;t want to wait for the car to move again, so he jumped out and tried to run across the highway. He was then struck by the driver of a minivan headed south.&quot;<p></p>

<p>A police scanner <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120116220253/https://twitter.com/#!/Breaking911/status/157338060990849024">suggested the victim was killed</a>, but <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120116220253/http://www.dnainfo.com/20120112/harlem/pedestrian-serioulsy-hurt-on-fdr-drive-east-harlem">DNAinfo reports</a>, &quot;The victim was taken to Metropolitan Hospital Center, fire officials said. He was in critical condition, police added.&quot;  The cab driver and driver of the minivan stayed at the scene and are not likely to be charged.</p>
