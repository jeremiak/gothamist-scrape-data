title: NYPD: This Guy Made Anti-Gay Comment And Then Kicked A Man In Midtown
---


                            
                            
                            
                            <p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2013_07_hatecrimesusp.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820062807im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2013_07_hatecrimesusp.jpg" width="250" height="274" class="image-right"> </span>The police are asking for the public&apos;s help in identifying and locating a man suspected of assaulting a person in midtown Manhattan yesterday. </p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incident happened at 3:10 a.m. at Speedy Cafe, at 1271 Broadway when the suspected approached two men and made an anti-gay comment at them. Then he kicked one of the victims in the left shoulder</p>

<p>The suspect fled west on West 32nd Street, but police released this surveillance photograph of him on a subway platform. He&apos;s described as being in his 40s with black hair and wearing a black tank top, black shorts and black sneakers while carrying a dark-colored backpack.</p>

<p>Anyone with information regarding this incident is asked to call Crime Stoppers at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477).  The public can also submit tips by logging onto <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130820062807/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">the Crime Stoppers website</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.  All calls are strictly confidential.</p>
