title: Police Release Sketch Of Suspect Wanted For Three Sex Attacks Near Flushing Park
---

	
										<p>Earlier this month, three women were attacked in separate incidents in or near Kissena Park in Flushing. Now police have a sketch of the suspect and hope the public can help them identify him. <br>
 <br>
<span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092328/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_06_kparksusp.jpg"> <img alt="2016_06_kparksusp.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092328im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2016/06/2016_06_kparksusp-thumb-450x552-954171.jpg" width="250" height="306" class="image-right"> </a> </span>On June 10, at 6:30 p.m., a 50-year-old woman was biking by Kissena Park on Oak Avenue when, police say, &quot;a male grabbed her and pulled her towards the park. The male attempted to talk to the victim as he touched himself over his clothing.&quot; The victim was able to break free and flee. </p>

<p>At 10:15 a.m. on June 12, at the intersection of Colden Street and Mulberry Avenue, a 24-year-old woman was &quot;approached by a male who engaged her in conversation&quot;&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092328/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/queens/cops-seek-tattooed-man-groped-woman-queens-park-article-1.2677924">apparently asking her for a hug</a>. Police say &quot;the male hugged her, kissed her on the cheek, and grabbed her buttocks. The male then touched himself over his clothing.&quot; The victim was able to flee.<br>
 <br>
The last incident was on June 15, at 6:30 p.m., near 56 Avenue and 148 Street, when authorities say a 41-year-old woman was &quot;approached by a male who grabbed both her arms and tried to pull her into a backyard. The victim screamed and the male fled in a red vehicle.&quot;<br>
 <br>
Queens Special Victims Squad Sgt. Douglas Biscardi <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092328/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/queens/cops-seek-tattooed-man-groped-woman-queens-park-article-1.2677924">told the Daily News</a>, &quot;These have happened very quickly. There&apos;s definitely some escalation with him. In the first two, he tries to grab them &#x2014; and in the last he tries to pull her into the yard.&quot;</p>

<p>Police describe the suspect as around 40 years old, 200 pounds and 6&apos;, with a slim and muscular build and black hair with some grey. He also a tattoo of a red and green star on the back of his arm above the elbow.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to these incidents is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477) or for Spanish, 1-888-57-PISTA (74782).  The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092328/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.<br>

I grew up in the neighborhood. I would never think that thing anything like that would happen here,&quot; but now she&apos;s worried about bringing her kids to the park.</p>
