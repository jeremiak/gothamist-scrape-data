					<p>If you&apos;ve ever traveled on NJ Transit or Amtrak from Penn Station, you&apos;ve no doubt gazed longingly (or glared angrily) up at the big board of departures, eagerly awaiting your train&apos;s track number to appear before throwing yourself <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/25/more_like_no_thanks_giving.php">into the mob of commuters</a>. Now things have changed: Crews put an end to the board by turning it off on Monday and covered it up as the train station moves to new, smaller LCD displays.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/http://bigstory.ap.org/article/82e50d65cdd74803a1bac609518148fe">The AP reports</a>, &quot;On Tuesday, it was a dark hulk, replaced with 40 eye-level digital panels scattered around the station. An Amtrak team will dismantle the sign within a week.&quot; There&apos;s currently <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/http://nymag.com/daily/intelligencer/2017/01/the-penn-station-amtrak-departures-board-goes-dark.html?mid=full-rss-di">a &quot;mourning&quot; tarp on it</a>:</p>

<center><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr"><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/https://twitter.com/hashtag/EndOfAnEra?src=hash">#EndOfAnEra</a>! Big departure/arrival board at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/https://twitter.com/hashtag/PennStation?src=hash">#PennStation</a> is no longer! <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/https://t.co/3uaNRVjpx3">pic.twitter.com/3uaNRVjpx3</a></p>&#x2014; William Daroff (@Daroff) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/https://twitter.com/Daroff/status/823987329333362688">January 24, 2017</a></blockquote>

<p>The now-departed board was a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/https://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/29/nyregion/penn-station-departures-board-replaced.html?_r=0">combination of digital and analog</a> which, in 2002, replaced an old-fashioned <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split-flap_display">Solari board</a>. </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/7KcYcaqQU2U" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The 30th Street Station in Philadelphia had a Solari board, beloved for its oddly gratifying clacking sounds, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/http://www.philly.com/philly/news/20160828_Sign_of_the_times__At_30th_Street_Station__display_board_will_soon_be_no_more.html">until last year</a>:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/44_M2BtMUKQ" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Last October, Amtrak&apos;s Mike DeCataldo <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170501194224/http://www.northjersey.com/story/travel/2017/01/24/new-amtrak-display-board-coming-ny-penn-station/96978880/">told the Bergen Record</a> that the old &quot;terminal complex presents a unique set of logistical and physical challenges &#x2014; some of which we believe this new system will help to address.&quot; The old board will be fully dismantled and removed this week, mostly overnight.</p>
