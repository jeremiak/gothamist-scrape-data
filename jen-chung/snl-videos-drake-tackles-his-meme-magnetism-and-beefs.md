</div></div>					<p>Drake <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110090710/http://gothamist.com/2014/01/19/videos_drake_impresses_doing_double.php#photo-1">joins the two-timers&apos; club</a>&#x2014;or is it the four-timers&apos; club, because he did double duty as host and musical performer again&#x2014;on Saturday Night Live, bringing his Canadian charm to Studio 8H. And let&apos;s say this: He&apos;s very good at playing a hapless Canadian &quot;Black Jeopardy&quot; contestant. </p>

<p>Since the presidential election is truly the best entertainment out there right now, the cold open <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110090710/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/15/video_chris_christie_suggests_bruce.php">was Donald Trump and Chris Christie</a>. Drake&apos;s monologue gave him an opportunity to cover off those memes everyone loves to make of him (he discussed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110090710/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pjMLvHQJU40">this with Jimmy Fallon as well</a>) and his alleged romance with RiRi. He also channeled his singing into a good digital sketch about his beefs with the staff: In a slam directed at Pete Davidson, &quot;You skinny as hell!... You ain&apos;t Josh Gad!&quot;</p>

<p>This episode was a good showcase for Jay Pharoah, who appeared in the cold open as Ben Carson and in &quot;Black Jeopardy,&quot; but his real capstone was his impressions of rappers for Weekend Update&#x2014;and then being confronted by Drake about his Drake impression. Anyway, we have a suspicion the cast won&apos;t mind having him back:</p>
