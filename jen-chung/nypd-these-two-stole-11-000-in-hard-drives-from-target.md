title: NYPD: These Two Stole $11,000 In Hard Drives From Target
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2014_06_siharddrive.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510015716im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_06_siharddrive.jpg" width="350" height="263" class="image-right"> </span>Police are hoping the public can help them identify two people wanted for stealing many, many pricey hard drives from a Staten Island Target last month.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incident occurred at 4:45 p.m. on Tuesday, June 24. A man and a woman entered the Target store on 2873 Richmond Avenue and, police say, &quot;removed 12 exterior digital hard drives from a display shelf valued at approximately $11,000 dollars.&quot; The pair then left on foot towards Yukon Avenue. </p>

<p>The male suspect is described as being 20-25 years old, 5&apos;5&quot; tall, 150 pounds, wearing a black t-shirt, white hat and tan shorts. The female suspect is described as wearing a grey hat, blue shorts and a black tank top. <br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510015716/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
