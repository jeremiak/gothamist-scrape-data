</div></div>					<p>This weekend, a 250-foot barge featuring some beloved hallmarks of NYC skating will be open to skateboarders in Brooklyn Bridge Park! It&apos;s the Nike SB Skate Safari Barge, which debuted last night in Manhattan, right by the Intrepid, with professional skaters like Paul Rodriguez and Stefan Janoski. Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508183839/http://colormekatie.com/">Katie Sokoler</a> got these amazing shots from the twilight skate session.</p>

<p>Before the Nike SB Skate SafarI Barge headed to Pier 88, it had to be built&#x2014;and some skaters tested it out while it was traveling up the Hudson:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508183839if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/ekpNG5mBLIk" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The barge has elements of classic NYC spots, like the Brooklyn Banks, OG Seaport benches, and Pyramid Ledges. You can skate today and tomorrow (9 a.m.-7 p.m.) at Brooklyn Bridge (Pier 5) by registering at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150508183839/http://www.skatesafarinyc.com/index/register">SkateSafariNYC.com</a>. After this weekend, Nike tells us parts of the skate barge will be donated to a skate park in the Rockaways.</p>
