title: Woman Sues NJ Hospital For Losing Dead Baby's Body
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_01_chosp.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502192452im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_01_chosp.jpg" width="145" height="126" class="image-right"> </span>A NJ woman filed a lawsuit against Jersey City&apos;s Christ Hospital for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502192452/http://gothamist.com/2009/01/06/stillborn_baby_thrown_out_in_hospit.php">losing the body of her stillborn baby</a>.  Kalynn Moore had given birth in December;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502192452/http://www.nj.com/news/jjournal/jerseycity/index.ssf?/base/news-8/1241504759238490.xml&amp;coll=3"> according to the Jersey Journal,</a> &quot;Arrangements were made to pick up the remains for a burial on Jan. 2, but when representatives from the funeral parlor arrived at the hospital, the remains couldn&apos;t be located in the morgue.&quot; Hospital officials admitted that the baby&apos;s corpse was probably thrown out in the trash; police <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502192452/http://www.nj.com/news/index.ssf/2009/01/hudson_authorities_search_for.html">searched garbage dumps</a> in NJ and Pennsylvania to no avail, leading them to suspect the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502192452/http://www.nj.com/news/index.ssf/2009/01/missing_jersey_city_babys_body.html">body was incinerated</a>.  The lawsuit notes that the incident has left Moore unable to get closure on her son&apos;s death.  The state has already fined the hospital over $20,000 for &quot;failing in its morgue operations for bodies and remains.&quot;</p>
