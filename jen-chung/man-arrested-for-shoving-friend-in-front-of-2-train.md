<p>A 25-year-old man allegedly shoved his friend onto the subway tracks and into the path of an oncoming train during a drunken argument in Manhattan on Sunday morning. </p>

<p>According to police, officers and first responders found 54-year-old Mike Allison under a subway car at 7:30 a.m. at the West 18th Street 1 train subway station&#x2014;the northbound 2 train (weekend service) hit him after he was thrown into its path. Allison suffered injuries to his hand and foot and was taken to Bellevue Hospital in stable condition. </p>

<p>Aaron Clary, a NJ resident, was charged with attempted manslaughter and assault after turning himself in to police. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161117023203/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/manhattan/n-man-nabbed-pushing-subway-tracks-nyc-article-1.2872278">Daily News reports</a> that Clary, Allison and a third friend had been &quot;partying at Peter McManus Cafe a block away along Seventh Ave. near W. 19th St. prior to the push.&quot;</p>

<p>When the bar closed at 5 a.m., the three friends then went to Chelsea Deli where, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161117023203/http://abc7ny.com/news/police-intoxicated-man-pushed-friend-onto-subway-tracks-in-chelsea/1605790/">according to WABC 7</a>, &quot;they drank for two more hours.&quot;</p>

<p>Clary reportedly surrendered after &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161117023203/http://abc7ny.com/news/police-intoxicated-man-pushed-friend-onto-subway-tracks-in-chelsea/1605790/">he woke up from his stupor hours later</a>.&quot;</p>
