</div></div>					<p>There&apos;s a reason why <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925161024/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/20/record_50_million_tourists_will_hav.php">50 million tourists visited New York City</a> last year, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925161024/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/24/new_yorkers_arent_friendly_enough_s.php">in spite of New Yorkers being rude jerks</a>: We have amazing sights.  Travel &amp; Leisure&apos;s readers, who deemed the Big Apple the rudest city in an annual reader survey, has unveiled its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925161024/http://www.travelandleisure.com/articles/worlds-most-popular-landmarks/">first reader survey of landmarks</a>, and NYC have five landmarks in the top 20&#x2014;including the number one position.</p>

<p>Number 1 is the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925161024/http://gothamist.com/tags/statueofliberty">Statue of Liberty</a>, followed by the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925161024/http://gothamist.com/tags/empirestatebuilding">Empire State Building</a>.  Number 10 is the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925161024/http://gothamist.com/tags/highline">High Line</a>, while 14 is the Frank Gehry-designed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925161024/http://gothamist.com/2011/02/18/gehrys_first_new_york_skyscraper_ge.php">8 Spruce Street</a> and 18 is the newly-opened <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925161024/http://gothamist.com/2011/09/12/opening_day_at_the_national_911_mem.php">9/11 Memorial</a>.  What&apos;s shocking is that tourists overlooked  Brooklyn Bridge, Chrysler Building, and Central Park among many other places! </p>
