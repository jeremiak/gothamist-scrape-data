<p>Pour one out (or light one up) for a Weed World Candies truck that caught fire on the Delaware Memorial Bridge last night, causing delays. </p>

<p>The driver of the vehicle, which was on the north side of the bridge, was en route from D.C. to Philadelphia. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027003813/http://www.nbcphiladelphia.com/news/local/Weed-World-Candies-Delaware-Memorial-Bridge-Fire-398124981.html">According to NBC Philadelphia</a>, &quot;&apos;Dr. Dro,&apos; the owner of &apos;Weed World Candies,&apos; told NBC10 more than $50,000 in merchandise was lost in the fire. The company is currently visiting colleges on the east coast.&quot; </p>

<p>Weed World Candies has been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027003813/http://gothamist.com/2012/07/23/subtle_weed_van_selling_cannabis-fl.php">peddling mildly pot-flavored lollipops</a>, as well as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027003813/http://www.weedworldcandies.global/store-1.html">Rice Krispie Treats and brownies</a>, and promoting the legalization of marijuana for years. But the candy sold from the vans is made from hemp oil and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027003813/http://observer.com/2012/09/fake-drugs-that-look-like-real-drugs-are-the-new-real-drugs-video/">will not get anyone stoned</a>. </p>

<p>No injuries were reported, and the fire was cashed by 9 p.m.  </p>
