										<p><img src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307210035im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_07_rangelrent21.jpg" class="right">Representative Charles Rangel discussed his decision to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307210035/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/15/rangel_will_give_up_one_rentstabili.php">give up one</a> of his 3-4 rent-stabilized apartments by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307210035/http://www.nydailynews.com/ny_local/2008/07/15/2008-07-15_we_didnt_want_that_apartment_anyway_rep_.html">saying</a> his re-election campaign-- which used a one-bedroom apartment with a monthly rent of $630 at Lenox Terrace in Harlem--&quot;had decided they were going to move anyway&quot; since the lease is up in October.  Last week, the NY Times revealed that Rangel <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307210035/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/11/congressman_charles_rangel_and_his.php">rented four apartments</a> at rent-stabilized rates (one apartment is made up of two units) raising questions about whether Rangel was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307210035/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/12/rumblings_over_representative_range.php">implicitly receiving a gift</a> from the landlord, a large real estate developer.</p>
