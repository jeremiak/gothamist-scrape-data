					<p>The high hopes for a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421112637/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/01/hillary_clintons_already_booked_fir.php">joyful Election night</a> at the Javits Center for Hillary Clinton and her supporters were dashed when Donald Trump stunned pundits and pollsters by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421112637/http://dcist.com/2016/11/election_2016_results.php">coming close to winning the 2016 election</a>, with Pennsylvania, Michigan and Wisconsin&#x2014;all needed for a Clinton victory&#x2014; in the balance as of 1:30 a.m. in the morning. [<b>UPDATE</b>: At 2 a.m., campaign chair John Podesta appeared, saying that since states were still close to call, they&apos;d have more to save in the more. <b>UPDATE 2:45 a.m.</b>: Clinton called to concede, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421112637/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/09/trump_president.php#photo-1">making him the next president</a>.]</p>

<p>As various states were called for Trump, and many states that Clinton was supposed to win falling in the &quot;too close to call&quot; column, the upbeat mood curdled. Supporters started to cry and Clinton campaign staffers became harder to find.</p>
