</div></div>					<p>Yesterday, one of Manhattan&apos;s most awwwww-some events took place: The annual Blessing of the Animals <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150821052221/http://www.stjohndivine.org/visit/calendar/events/liturgy-worship/12115/feast-of-saint-francis-and-blessing-of-the-animals">at Cathedral of Saint John the Divine</a>. </p>

<p>The cathedral was packed with humans and many, many animals, a tribute to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150821052221/http://www.catholic.org/saints/saint.php?saint_id=50">St. Francis of Assisi</a> and his love and compassion for animals. Attendees brought their pets to enjoy a program of dance and music and sermons. According to photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150821052221/http://saimokhtari.com/">Sai Mokhtari</a>, Dean James Kowalski&apos;s sermon reflected on &quot;the need to cultivate a renewed focus on global warming,&quot; discussing <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150821052221/http://gothamist.com/2014/09/21/photos_thousands_gather_for_peoples.php">the recent climate march</a> and comparing the environmental movement to the civil rights movement: &quot;He talked about endorsing a carbon emission tax and holding polluters financially accountable, about responsibility and conservation.&quot;</p>

<p>Still, the show-stopper, as always, was the procession of animals&#x2014;llama! predator birds! goat! snakes! camel! turtle on a wagon! The events inside the cathedral were followed by a fair on the church grounds, with face-painting, animal adoptions, and animals rights information.</p>
