</div></div>					<p>The ALS Ice Bucket Challenge may be raking in millions (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014149/http://www.alsa.org/news/media/press-releases/ice-bucket-challenge-082514.html">$79.7 million at last count</a>) for a worthy cause, but there have been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014149/http://www.courier-journal.com/story/news/local/2014/08/21/firefighters-ice-bucket-challenge/14396311/">some injuries</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014149/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WQlvW0aFnKo">embarrassments</a> along the way. It turns out one of those embarrassments happened last night in the East River.</p>

<p>According to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014149/http://nypost.com/2014/08/26/man-jumps-into-river-for-ice-bucket-challenge-but-cant-swim/">The Post</a>, &quot;Two dimwitted daredevils were taunted by friends to jump into the East River as part of the ALS ice bucket challenge Monday night&#x2014;even though one couldn&#x2019;t swim.&quot; The men, in their 20s, jumped in from Long Island City at around 8 p.m. and the paper reports that one of them &quot;swam to safety and the other needed to be saved by a good Samaritan who dove into the water and held onto the desperate diver until NYPD Emergency Service Unit cops arrived.&quot; Luckily, they did not suffer any serious injuries.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014149/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/22/patrick_stewart_highly_dignified_al.php">Just do it the Patrick Stewart way</a>, folks!</p>
