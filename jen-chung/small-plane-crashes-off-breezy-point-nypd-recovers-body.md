<p>A small plane crashed into the waters off Breezy Point last night, and police have recovered debris from the aircraft, as well as a body. </p>

<p>Officials believe the plane was headed from Philadelphia to Portsmouth, NH. The Fire Department said a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160305152806/http://www.nytimes.com/2015/11/05/nyregion/debris-of-small-plane-is-found-off-queens-officials-say.html?ref=nyregion">a witness called 911</a> about the plane falling into the water around 8 p.m. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160305152806/http://nypost.com/2015/11/04/police-search-for-passengers-in-small-plane-crash/">One witness told the NY Post</a>, &quot;I saw that crazy angle of lights in the sky. Half a second later to a second later, I heard the boom and I was like, &#x2018;Oh, my God, it was a plane crash!&#x2019;&quot;</p>

<p>A male body was found, and it&apos;s believed he was the pilot and only one aboard the aircraft. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160305152806/https://twitter.com/NYCAviation/status/662091980327923713">According to NYC Aviation</a>, the plane appears to be a 2-seat Flight Design CTLS. The plane is registered to James McGee of Rye, NH.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160305152806/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/small-plane-crashes-rockaway-debris-found-water-article-1.2424077">Another witness told the Daily News</a>, &quot;It was like he was joyriding. His flight path was just way too low. It didn&#x2019;t make any sense.&quot;</p>
