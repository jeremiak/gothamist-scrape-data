<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130701210122/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_05_somapit.jpg"> <img alt="2009_05_somapit.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130701210122im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2009/05/2009_05_somapit-thumb-136x153-238722.jpg" width="112" height="126" class="image-right"> </a> </span>The surviving Somali pirate involved in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130701210122/http://gothamist.com/2009/04/12/container_ship_ashore_negotiations.php">taking a U.S. container ship captain hostage</a> a year ago may plead guilty. The NY Times reports the U.S. Attorney&apos;s office has asked a judge to schedule a plea proceeding for Abduwali Abdukhadir Muse, who <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130701210122/http://gothamist.com/2009/05/21/somali_pirate_pleads_not_guilty.php">pleaded not guilty</a> last May to charges including piracy under the law of nations and kidnapping: &quot;If convicted of piracy, Mr. Muse would face a mandatory life sentence. One possible strategy by Mr. Muse&#x2019;s lawyers would be to seek a deal in which he could plead guilty to a lesser charge that would reduce his potential sentence; or to have any cooperation be factored into a request for leniency.&quot; It&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130701210122/http://gothamist.com/2009/05/20/somali_pirate_indicted_on_ten_count.php">unclear how old Muse is</a>: Prosecutors said he was 19 last year, while his defense said he was 15.</p>
