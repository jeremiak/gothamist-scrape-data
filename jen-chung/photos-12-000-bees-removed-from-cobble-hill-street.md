</div></div>					<p>This is a <em>beeg</em> week for bees! On Tuesday, a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427170925/http://gothamist.com/2014/05/08/30k_bee_swarm_scares_astoria_but_lo.php">swarm of 30,000 bees escaped a hive in Astoria</a> and, yesterday, another large swarm emerged on a tree last night&#x2014;right across from a school in Brooklyn.</p>

<p>The NYPD&apos;s beekeeper Anthony Planakis was on the scene at a tree in front of 176 Kane Street in Cobble Hill. P.S. 29 is located across the street. </p>

<p>Apparently neighbors had complained about the busy insects, and a<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427170925/http://nypost.com/2014/05/09/massive-bees-nest-removed-from-schoolyard/"> police source told the NYPD</a>, &quot;It&#x2019;s a good thing this didn&#x2019;t happen during school hours. These bees can be very aggressive this time of year.&quot;<br>
