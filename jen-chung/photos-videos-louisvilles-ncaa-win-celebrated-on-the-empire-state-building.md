</div></div>					<p>Last night, the University of Louisville&apos;s men&apos;s basketball team defeated the University of Michigan, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503145517/http://www.ncaa.com/game/basketball-men/d1/2013/04/08/michigan-louisville">82-76</a>,in the NCAA Championship game, in what many are calling one of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503145517/http://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/ncaab/2013/04/09/bracket-briefing-best-ncaa-tournament-title-game-ever-a-perfect-ending-to-the-madness/2066263/">most thrilling games ever</a>. The Empire State Building celebrated Louisville&apos;s win with a light show, which you could watch while listening to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503145517/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=peu_PJ7NuA8">Louisville fight song</a>:</p>
