					<p>As we mentioned two weeks ago, pets are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016014414/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/10/pet_adoption_healing_power.php#photo-1">wonderful stress relievers</a> in this uncertain time&#x2014;and the ASPCA is waiving its cat and kitten adoption fees for its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016014414/http://www.aspca.org/news/better-black-friday-dont-miss-cat-friday-aspca-adoption-center-2016">annual Cat Friday event</a>.</p>

<p>&quot;The holiday season can be a great time to welcome a new pet into your home,&quot; said Gail Buchwald, senior vice president of the ASPCA Adoption Center. &quot;This year, consider skipping the long lines at department stores and spend your Black Friday expanding your family with a new furry friend.&quot;</p>

<p>The ASPCA says all kittens and cats will also go home with a free collar and personalized identification tag, and adopters of cats over one year old will receive a free hard cat carrier while supplies last. </p>

<p>Cat Friday takes place Friday, November 25th, from 11 a.m. until 7 p.m. at the <a href="www.aspca.org/adopt">ASPCA Adoption Center</a>, located at 424 E 92nd Street (between 1st and York Avenues) on the Upper East Side of Manhattan. Some more info: adopters must be 21 years of age or older to adopt; adopters are required to provide a government issued photo ID and separate proof of current address; and cat adopters who already have a resident cat or dog, please leave them at home, as the ASPCA cannot perform any cat adoptions with resident pets present.</p>

<p>Note to anyone going: be <em>very patient</em>, because there are many people who are also interested in adopting cats and kittens and the staff is working as hard as they can! Bring a book, listen to some music, or peruse the finest cat videos on your phone.</p>
