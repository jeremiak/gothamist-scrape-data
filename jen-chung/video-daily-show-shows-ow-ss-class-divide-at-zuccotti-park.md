</div></div>					<p>Yes, Occupy Wall Street has been a rich source of material for The Daily Show, from protesters pooping in&#x2014;and ruining&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503153419/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/07/daily_show_occupy_wall_street_prote.php">local businesses&apos; bathrooms</a> to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503153419/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/19/daily_shows_john_oliver_visits_occu.php">temperature checks</a>. Jon Stewart himself has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503153419/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/20/video_jon_stewart_criticizes_occupy.php">questioned the group&apos;s theatrics</a> (though &quot;I am very much interested in the message that&apos;s being articulated&quot;) and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503153419/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/18/videos_jon_stewart_scolds_protester.php">pooping on cop cars</a>, so he enjoyed correspondent Samantha Bee&apos;s visit to Zuccotti Park before the police raid.  Bee found that the class war was alive and kicking&#x2014;within the encampment&#x2014;as some referred to one part of the park as the &quot;aristocratic&quot; part where Brooklyn hipsters reign and another as &quot;the ghetto.&quot;</p>
