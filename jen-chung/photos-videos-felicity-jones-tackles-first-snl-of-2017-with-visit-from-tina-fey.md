</div></div>					<p>British actress Felicity Jones kicked off the new year by hosting <em>Saturday Night Live</em>. The <em>Rogue One</em> star didn&apos;t reference her <em>Star Wars</em> film as much as Adam Driver <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015804/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/17/videos_adam_driver_snl.php#photo-1">did when he hosted last year</a>, but there was a hologram of Tina Fey during the monologue. And, no, despite what you&apos;re thinking, Fey wasn&apos;t dressed as Princess Leia&#x2014;it was just something she got at Eileen Fisher.</p>

<p>Overall, the episode was alright&#x2014;the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015804/http://gothamist.com/2017/01/15/watch_alec_baldwins_donald_trump_br.php#photo-1">cold open dealt with golden showers</a>; the &quot;Theater Donor&quot; sketch was an opportunity for a gross-out and seeing if the cast would break; &quot;Beard Hunk&quot; amusingly covered how horrible &quot;The Bachelor&quot; and its contestants are. The sharpest sketches were &quot;Shonda and Malik,&quot; a digital short about two rival criminals; &quot;Susan B. Anthony,&quot; about a group of feminists who conjure up Susan B. Anthony; and &quot;The Princess and the Curse,&quot; another digital short about true love (with Kate McKinnon as a Maleficent-style witch). Michael Che was also great during a Weekend Update riff on black-on-black crime.</p>

<p>Perhaps SNL writers are just gearing up for the post-Inauguration episode on Saturday, January 21, which will feature Aziz Ansari and Big Sean.<br>
