title: Volatile Economy Could Mean Lower Rents, More Concessions

<p><img alt="2008_09_bldgs.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117114041im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_09_bldgs.jpg" width="130" height="99" class="right">Wall Street rebounded at the end of the week, but the commercial and residential real estate markets are getting ready for financial sector fallout.   The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117114041/http://www.nysun.com/new-york/streets-consolidation-could-flood-city-office/85920/">NY Sun reported</a> that Bank of America&apos;s acquisition of Merrill Lynch may mean another 4.2 million square feet of office space on the block. Still, brokers and the city are relieved that Barclays is taking <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117114041/http://gothamist.com/2008/09/18/barclays_new_nyc_home.php">Lehman&apos;s 7th Avenue office</a> and the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117114041/http://gothamist.com/2008/09/16/aig_may_get_75_billion_emergency_fi.php">feds saved AIG</a>, as they make up 6.2 million square feet.  On the residential side, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117114041/http://ny.therealdeal.com/articles/landlords-offer-more-concessions-fear-lease-breaks">the Real Deal suggests</a> landlords may offer more concessions, like out-of-state guarantors on top of free month&apos;s rent and owner-paid commissions.  One rental firm said a <strong>landlord &quot;called and asked me how many of our tenants work for Lehman Brothers.&quot;</strong></p>
