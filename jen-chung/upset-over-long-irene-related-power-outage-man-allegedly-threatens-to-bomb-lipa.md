										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2011_08_bombthreat.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427140646im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2011_08_bombthreat.jpg" width="250" height="212" class="image-left"> </span>It&apos;s<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427140646/http://www.newsday.com/news/breaking/lipa-vows-99-to-have-power-back-sunday-1.3142454"> has taken nearly a week</a> to bring electricity back to 99% of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427140646/http://www.27east.com/news/article.cfm/East-End/396673/LIPA-Scrambles-To-Repair-Massive-Power-Outages">hundreds of thousands</a> of Long Island Power Authority customers who lost power when Hurricane Irene knocked it out, but some Long Islanders are really upset.  Like Rajiv Pandey, a Central Islip 33-year-old, who was arrested for allegedly calling News 12 and saying he would plant a bomb at a LIPA facility on Friday.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427140646/http://www.newsday.com/news/breaking/man-charged-with-making-lipa-bomb-threat-1.3142537">According to Newsday</a>, &quot;The threat prompted police to dispatch officers to more than 30 facilities to check for an explosive. An investigation revealed that bomb threat call was placed from Pandey&apos;s cellphone.&quot;  Pandey was arrested yesterday and is charged with falsely reporting an incident.  Last week, a Hicksville man was arrested for aggravated threat when he allegedly called Nassau County emergency management and made threats against LIPA.  </p>
