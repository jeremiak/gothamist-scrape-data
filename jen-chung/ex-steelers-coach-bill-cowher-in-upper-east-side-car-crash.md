<p>Bill Cowher, the Super Bowl-winning former coach of the Pittsburgh Steelers, was involved in a car accident yesterday on the Upper East Side that involved some pesky Park Avenue scaffolding. Luckily, no one was hurt.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130729120439/http://www.nydailynews.com/news/national/bill-cowher-new-york-car-crash-article-1.1380602">According to the Daily News</a>, &quot;Cowher, 56, was heading north [on Park] in a black Mercedes-Benz when a woman driving a Ford Fusion going in the same direction cut him off attempting to turn onto E. 92nd St. about 1:10 p.m., according to police and witnesses.&quot; When his Benz hit the Fusion, the Fusion went into building scaffolding on the corner. The FDNY had to shore up the scaffolding. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130729120439/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/manhattan/nfl_cowher_ny_ram_WH1nFoKrnWDr5hczTREhIN?utm_medium=rss&amp;utm_content=Manhattan&amp;utm_source=feedly">An EMT told the Post,</a> &quot;With how banged up that car is, it&#x2019;s amazing no one was really hurt.&quot; The Post&apos;s sources add that no one was arrested or issued summonses, because bad drivers can get away with anything.</p>
