<p>Four residents in upper Manhattan are reeling after a cat burglar struck in three different buildings over five hours to break into their apartments. Police have released surveillance footage of suspect, seen climbing down from a fire escape:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130516071919if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/yAaMhPUUdGA" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incidents all occurred between 8:15 p.m. on March 15 and 12:36 a.m. on March 16, with the suspect accessing the locations from the same rear alley for all the buildings&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130516071919/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/manhattan/quick_cat_robs_apts_in_hrs_ym5k6apehwdLsnU15rmnQI?utm_medium=rss&amp;utm_content=Manhattan">according to the Post</a>, he took jewelry, cash and electronics from three of the apartments (the ones he hit at 8:15 p.m., 11:15 p.m. and 12:36 a.m.) but left one apartment that he entered at 11:30 p.m.  without taking anything. </p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to these incidents is asked to call Crime Stoppers at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477).  The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130516071919/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.<br>
