title: Film Society Of Lincoln Center's Summer Talks Line-Up Includes Sofia Coppola

<p>Starting tomorrow, May 16, the Film Society of Lincoln Center is launching its new&#x2014;and free&#x2014; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://www.filmlinc.com/films/series/summer-talks">Summer Talks program</a>, where the public can hear filmmakers discuss their upcoming movies. The first film is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://www.sonyclassics.com/beforemidnight/"><i>Before Midnight</i></a>, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://gothamist.com/2013/03/28/video_before_midnight_trailer.php">eagerly anticipated</a> sequel to <em>Before Sunrise</em> and <em>Before Sunset</em>, starring Ethan Hawke and Julie Delpy. The actors collaborated on the script with director Richard Linklater and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://www.filmlinc.com/films/on-sale/summer-talk-before-midnight">all three will be there</a>!  </p>

<p>The next one is on June 10&#x2014;and it&apos;s Sofia Coppola discussing <em>The Bling Ring</em>, based on the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://www.vanityfair.com/culture/features/2010/03/billionaire-girls-201003">Vanity Fair article chronicling</a> the real-life escapades of Hollywood teens who robbed celebrities homes (Paris Hilton, Orlando Bloom and Lindsay Lohan were among the victims). Emma Watson gets to play Valley Girl thief:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/BST3CCnP6uE" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Tickets are free and will be given out an hour before the event. You can <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://www.filmlinc.com/films/series/summer-talks">see the whole list here</a> (there will be more additions later)&#x2014;definitely <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://www.filmlinc.com/films/on-sale/summer-talk-fruitvale-station">consider the July 11 talk</a> with Ryan Coogler, who directed <em>Fruitvale Station</em> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://sfist.com/2013/05/13/fruitvale_station_trailer_released.php">trailer</a>), based on the moments leading up to the fatal shooting of Oscar Grant by a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130608134316/http://sfist.com/2010/07/08/johannes_mehserle_guily_of_involunt.php">BART transit cop</a>. </p>
