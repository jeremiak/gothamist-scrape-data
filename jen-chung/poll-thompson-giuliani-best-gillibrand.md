										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160303084120/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_05_gillyk.jpg"> <img alt="2009_05_gillyk.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160303084120im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2009/05/2009_05_gillyk-thumb-125x142-239162.jpg" width="90" height="102" class="image-right"> </a> </span>Some new polls show that junior Senator Kirsten Gillibrand may be vulnerable during her first election next year.  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160303084120/http://www.observer.com/2009/politics/polls-gillibrand-loses-thompson-giuliani">According to the Observer</a>, a Siena poll found that &quot;I don&apos;t know&quot; (35%) won out over Gillibrand (32%), who was followed by City Comptroller Bill Thompson (23%), while a Quinnipiac poll found that Thompson would beat Gillibrand 41-28 in a hypothetical primary. However, Quinnipiac&apos;s Maurice Carroll says, &quot;Giuliani, who is pondering a Senate race, tops both of them&quot; and Siena says Giuliani would beat Gillibrand 50-40.</p>
