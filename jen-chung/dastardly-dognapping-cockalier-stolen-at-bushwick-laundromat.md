										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2016_08_cockaliertheft.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041224im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_08_cockaliertheft.jpg" width="640" height="400" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2016_08_puppeh.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108041224im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_08_puppeh.jpg" width="194" height="285" class="image-right"> </span>A tiny Cockalier was stolen from a Brooklyn laundromat this week, and police are hoping the public can help them find the dognapper.</p>

<p>The incident occurred on Monday, at 3:33 p.m., at the Laundry Station located at 334 Knickerbocker Avenue in Bushwick. </p>

<p>The NYPD says &quot;an unknown individual entered... and took the 36-year-old male victim&apos;s Cockalier dog. The individual then fled the location on foot southbound on Knickerbocker Avenue.&quot; </p>

<p>Cockaliers are a cross between a Cocker Spaniel and Cavalier King Charles Spaniel. </p>

<p>The suspect is described as being in his 30s, 6&apos; tall and 170 pounds, with a light complexion, black hair and brown eyes.</p>

<p>He was last seen wearing gray sneakers, blue jeans and blue shirt. He also has a tattoo on his arm. Police released surveillance video from the laundromat:</p>
