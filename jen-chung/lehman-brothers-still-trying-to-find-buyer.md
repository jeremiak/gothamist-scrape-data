title: Lehman Brothers Still Trying to Find Buyer
---

<p><img src="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122455im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_09_lehman2.jpg" class="right">The fate of Lehman Brothers is still to be determined, as the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122455/http://online.wsj.com/article/SB122134089502132567.html?mod=hpp_us_whats_news">Wall Street Journal reports</a> that it has become &quot;increasingly clear that a clean sale of the entire firm to a big bank would be too difficult to execute.&quot;  More &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122455/http://www.ny1.com/content/top_stories/85807/lehman-brothers-may-be-sold-in-pieces/Default.aspx">high-level talks were held Saturday</a>&quot; --including participants like Treasury Secretary Henry Paulson, who doesn&apos;t want the government to bail out the bank a la <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122455/http://gothamist.com/2008/04/03/fed_jp_morgan_b.php">Bear Stearns</a>.  One possibility: Lehman <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122455/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/14/business/14lehman.html">might be sold in pieces</a>.  One employee described the company&apos;s Midtown headquarters as a &quot;morgue&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122455/http://www.nydailynews.com/money/2008/09/12/2008-09-12_fate_of_beleaguered_lehman_brothers_coul.html">to the Daily News</a>, who also spoke to local restaurants who say their business would go down 25-50% if Lehman folds. <strong>Update</strong>: The NY Times reports this afternoon that Barclays, considered the leading contender to buy all or part of Lehman, says that it <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117122455/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/15/business/15lehman.html?hp">could not reach a deal</a> without financial support from the federal government or other banks. Thus it looks likely that Lehman will have to liquidate.</p>
