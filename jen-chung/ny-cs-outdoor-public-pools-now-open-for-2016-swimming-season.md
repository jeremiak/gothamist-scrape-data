					<p>Just in time for today&apos;s (mostly) blazing sun, the NYC Parks and Recreation Department opened all 55 public pools. Parks Commissioner Mitchell Silver made the ceremonial jump into Staten Island&apos;s Faber Pool, where he also announced, &quot;For the first time ever, we&apos;re extending the outdoor pool season by one week so that New Yorkers have even more access to a cool pool when the temperatures are high.&quot;</p>

<p>The outdoor pools&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007053541/https://www.nycgovparks.org/facilities/outdoor-pools">here&apos;s a list</a>&#x2014;are open daily 11:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. and 4:00 p.m. to 7:00 p.m., seven days a week (that 3 p.m.-4 p.m. break is for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007053541/http://gothamist.com/2012/08/05/mccarren_park_pool_horror_diarrhea.php">pool cleaning</a>). And, as Silver mentioned, the NYC&apos;s 36 intermediate- and Olympic-sized pools are open through Sunday, September 11; the other 19 pools are open through September 4. </p>

<p>The Parks Department also wants pool visitors remain &quot;cautious and alert in and around the water&quot; and offers these water safety tips:</p><blockquote>1.         Only swim where lifeguards are present and follow their directions<br>
2.         Learn to swim<br>
3.         Never leave children unattended<br>
4.         Obey all posted signs and flags<br>
5.         Watch for dangerous waves and rip currents<br>
6.         Stay alert and aware of your surroundings<br>
7.         Wear a life jacket when boating<br>
8.         Don&#x2019;t take unnecessary risks<br>
9.         Be safe in the sun<br>
10.       Know before you go</blockquote>Here are some <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007053541/http://gothamist.com/2012/06/28/5_tips_for_enjoying_nycs_fabulous_p.php">other tips to enjoying the public pools</a> (remember, no newspapers!). <p></p>
