</div></div>					<p>Yesterday was one of the cutest of all annual traditions: The Blessing of the Animals at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522062308/http://www.stjohndivine.org/">St. John the Divine</a>. What better way to celebrate the life of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522062308/http://www.catholic.org/saints/saint.php?saint_id=50">St. Francis of Assisi</a> and his love of animals than communing with a kangaroo, a camel, a miniature pony, some alpacas, bunnies, cats, dogs and many other animals?</p>

<p>The event involves a ceremony inside the cathedral with a procession of animals and their handlers (like Edie Falco, holding a baby Alpaca) and then a fair outside where people can have their own pets blessed. And let&apos;s face it&#x2014;if you have a double stroller, you might as well use it to take your two pugs to be blessed.</p>
