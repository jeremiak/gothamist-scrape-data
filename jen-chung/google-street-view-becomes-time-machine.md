</div></div>					<p>Google Street View is quite useful when you need to get a sense of place, but now you&apos;ll be able to get a sense of place and <em>time: </em>Starting today, Google Street View is formally offering users the chance to travel back in time to see how a location has changed, thanks to images dating all they way back to... 2007. For you millennials in the audience, that was the year of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428125823/http://gothamist.com/2007/07/18/explosion_in_vi.php">Great Midtown Steam Explosion</a> (you probably won&apos;t find it on Google Street View).</p>

<p>If you spot a clock icon under an image in Street View, you&apos;ll be able to toggle back in time to the happy, free-love days of 2007 (and you won&apos;t have to dig back into the cache, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428125823/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/19/see_nycs_gentrification_through_goo.php">the way some very enterprising folks have</a>). Some NYC time travel locations include One World Trade Center, the ever-changing Bowery mural&apos;s graffiti and 5 Pointz&#x2014;it&apos;ll be depressing to see the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428125823/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/19/photos_graffiti_mecca_5_pointz_is_b.php">5 Pointz update</a>!</p>
