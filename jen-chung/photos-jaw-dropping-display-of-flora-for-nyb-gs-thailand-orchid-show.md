					<p>An explosion of colors and shapes awaits you at the New York Botanical Garden, which will open its 15th annual Orchid Show tomorrow. This year&apos;s theme is <em>Thailand</em>, and you&apos;ll see that in its topiary elephants, covered in orchids, as well as a stunning orchid-decked fac&#x327;ade of a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170222043731/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sala_(architecture)">sala</a>, the traditional open pavilion,</p>

<p>The NYBG explains, &quot;Thailand is home to more than 1,200 native orchid species and over the last century it has become the leading exporter of cultivated tropical orchids in the world.&quot;</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170222043731if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/9YYPdmPZq0g" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>And, yes, one of the programs accompanying The Orchid Show is<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170222043731/http://www.nybg.org/exhibitions/2017/orchid-show/programs.php"> a screening of <em>Adaptation</em></a>, the whimsical Charlie Kaufman/Spike Jonze film based on Susan Orlean&apos;s wonderful book about the plant and obsession around them, <em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170222043731/https://www.amazon.com/Orchid-Thief-Obsession-Ballantine-Readers/dp/044900371X/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1487345946&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=orchid+thief">The Orchid Thief</a></em>. </p>

<p>Our photographer, Scott Heins, reports back that <em>Thailand</em> &quot;continues the NYBG Orchid Show tradition of being serenely beautiful. The fine details of Thai altars, hanging lanterns, and of course, those elephants, mean that even if you&apos;ve been to past Orchid shows, you&apos;ll find plenty of new things to enjoy. The Garden&apos;s staff has done a great job with information boards explaining why Thailand&apos;s climate is paradise for orchid plants&#x2014;and in the middle of February, the Enid A. Haupt Conservatory&apos;s tropical climate feels like paradise for New Yorkers.&quot;</p>

<p><em>&quot;The Orchid Show: Thailand&quot; opens Saturday, February 18, and runs through April 9, 2017. There are a number of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170222043731/http://www.nybg.org/exhibitions/2017/orchid-show/evenings.php">&quot;Orchid Evenings&quot; in March and April</a>, if you want a private look at the flora, plus open bar.</em></p>
