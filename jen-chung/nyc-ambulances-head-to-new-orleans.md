title: NYC Ambulances Head to New Orleans
---

<p><img alt="2008_08_ambulance.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20110624103746im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_08_ambulance.jpg" width="130" height="98" class="right">A number of NYC ambulance companies are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110624103746/http://wcbstv.com/local/nyc.bronx.gulf.2.805902.html">sending trucks and crews to New Orleans</a> as Tropical Storm Gustav continues to move towards the Gulf Coast.  Citywide Mobile Response Corp. spokesman Isaac Newman says FEMA contacted them at 10 p.m. last night to deploy ambulances; Citywide sent five ambulances and 15 crew members on the road this morning.  Right now, Gustav is expected to be a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110624103746/http://www.nola.com/hurricane/index.ssf/2008/08/gustav_forecast_still_aimed_at.html">major Category 3 hurricane</a> when it reaches Louisiana on Tuesday at 1 a.m., with winds at 115mph.  The GOP <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110624103746/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2008/08/28/AR2008082803165.html?hpid=topnews">may consider delaying their convention next week</a>--or making it more low-key--because of Gustav. The White House is also debating whether President Bush should cancel his appearance at the convention, given previous criticism of his handling of Hurricane Katrina.</p>
