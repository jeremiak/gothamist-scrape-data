<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2015_11_glq.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402175438im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_11_glq.jpg" width="220" height="278" class="image-right"> </span>Another day, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402175438/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/14/smartphone_subway_theft.php">another smartphone snatching</a> on the subway. But this time the suspect also helpfully took a selfie of himself.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the grand larceny took place at 5:45 a.m. on October 24, 2015: A 36 year-old man &quot;was texting on his Galaxy S3 while aboard a south bound Q train.  As the train entered the Beverley Road station the suspect snatched the phone from his hand and fled the train when the doors opened.&quot;<br>
 <br>
The victim later noticed this photograph of the suspect on his Google account. <br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402175438/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
