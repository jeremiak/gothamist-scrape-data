                            <p><img alt="2008_10_parktix.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130810105122im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_10_parktix.jpg" width="130" height="115" class="right">A House Transportation Committee <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130810105122/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/10/24/nyregion/24scofflaw.html?ref=nyregion">report stated</a>, &quot;<strong>Essentially, all of Lower Manhattan is a free parking lot for government vehicles</strong>,&quot; confirming what we all know: Government vehicles park illegally! But here&apos;s the icing: The biggest parking ticket scofflaws are the government.  The NYPD ran up $193,000 in unpaid tickets, the FBI had $34,908, and State Department had $28,33 in unpaid violations. An FBI spokeswoman said, &quot;<strong>Parking in New York City is a huge challenge.</strong> Parking facilities do not exist for the majority of F.B.I. vehicles assigned to the New York division.&#x201D; Still, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130810105122/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2008/10/23/AR2008102303248.html?nav=rss_metro/dc&amp;sid=ST2008102303410&amp;s_pos=">report blames</a> &quot;lax fleet management practices&quot; for allowing government employes to take advantage of the system and create unsafe conditions for others.
