					<p>Earlier this week, police officers in the Bronx helped rescued nearly two dozen dogs from a filthy, hot SUV. And now <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108050454/https://www.facebook.com/adoreabullie/posts/783044971838236">these cuties need homes</a>!</p>

<p>The police had received calls about a Mercedes-Benz SUV with dogs inside at Edison Avenue and Middletown Road. When officers got there, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108050454/http://nypdnews.com/2016/08/bronx-cops-rescue-22-dogs-living-in-suv/">the NYPD says</a>, &quot;they witnessed 22 dogs also inside the small truck with a 54-year-old woman. After speaking with the woman, it was determined that she was emotionally disturbed and she was taken to a local hospital for evaluation.&quot;</p>

<p>The puppies were &quot;living in terrible filth and temperatures in the car were dangerously high,&quot; the authorities explained, and officers worked with rescued groups <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108050454/http://www.adore-a-bullie.org/">Adore-a-Bullie</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108050454/https://www.facebook.com/BobbiandtheStrays/posts/10150769939464956">Bobbi and the Strays</a> to shelter the dogs.</p>

<p>It&apos;s believed the dogs lived their <em>entire lives</em> in the car, and many of them were in poor health. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108050454/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/NYC-Rescue-Puppies-German-Shepherd-Living-in-Veterans-SUV-Bronx-389826111.html">One rescuer said</a>, &quot;She had the German shepherd kind of crated really to the back and the rest of them were just loose all over the place.&quot;</p>

<p>Now, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108050454/http://pix11.com/2016/08/11/german-shepherd-21-puppies-rescued-from-living-in-bronx-veterans-suv/">PIX11 reports</a>, rescue workers say &quot;the pups are growing stronger and are becoming accustomed to being outside of the car. They are also being watched over by the German shepherd named Bruno.&quot;</p>

<p>You can help by donating and offering to adopt or foster a dog&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108050454/http://www.adore-a-bullie.org/">Adore-a-Bullie</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108050454/http://www.bobbiandthestrays.org/donate1">Bobbi and the Strays</a>.</p>
