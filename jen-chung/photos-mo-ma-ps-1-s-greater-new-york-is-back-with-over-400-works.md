</div></div>					<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160306201130/http://momaps1.org/exhibitions/view/403">Greater New York</a></em>, MoMA PS1&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160306201130/http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/quinquennial">quinquennial</a> exhibit showcasing work from artists in and around New York City, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160306201130/http://momaps1.org/exhibitions/view/403">opens tomorrow</a>, giving visitors a chance to savor hundreds of pieces from 157 artists as the art world continues to shift.</p>

<p>The 2015 collection of works reflects how real estate is affecting the NYC art scene. From the press release:</p>

<blockquote>Greater New York arrives in a city and art community that has changed significantly since the first version of the survey. With the rise of a robust commercial art market and the proliferation of art fairs, opportunities for younger artists in the city have grown alongside a burgeoning interest in artists who may have been overlooked in the art histories of their time. Concurrently, the city itself is being reshaped by a voracious real estate market that poses particular challenges to local artists. The speed of this change in recent years has stoked a nostalgia for earlier periods in New York&#x2014;notably the 1970s and 1980s, and the experimental practices and attitudes that flourished in the city during those decades. Against this backdrop, Greater New York departs from the show&#x2019;s traditional focus on youth, instead examining points of connection and tension between our desire for the new and nostalgia for that which it displaces.</blockquote>

<p>Peter Eleey, who led the curatorial team, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160306201130/http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2015-10-07/greater-new-york-at-moma-ps1">told Bloomberg</a>, &quot;A huge amount of work is made in New York, and while a lot leaves it, a lot stays. So while other museums would have to truck things from around the country, we have an enormous wealth of art and artworks to draw from right here.&quot;</p>

<p>This go-around, there are works from young artists as well as established ones. The show will be free for a year to all New York City residents, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160306201130/http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2015/10/07/free-admission-for-city-residents-at-moma-ps1/">thanks to a grant</a> from the Anna-Maria and Stephen Kellen Foundation. </p>

<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160306201130/http://momaps1.org/exhibitions/view/403">&quot;Greater New York&quot;</a> will be shown from October 11 through March 7, 2016 at MoMA PS1, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160306201130/http://momaps1.org/visit/">located at 22-25 Jackson Ave.</a> at the intersection of 46th Ave., in Long Island City.</em></p>
