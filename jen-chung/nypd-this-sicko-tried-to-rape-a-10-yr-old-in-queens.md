                            <p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2013_09_attrape.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130922035149im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2013_09_attrape.jpg" width="200" height="247" class="image-right"> </span>The police have released a sketch of a man suspected in the attempted rape of a girl in the Jamaica section of Queens earlier this week.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, at around 7:15 a.m. on Monday, September 16, a man approached a 10-year-old girl near 168th Street and Douglas Avenue, pushed her to the ground and tried to rape her. The suspect fled when a car drove past. Fortunately, the victim was not injured. </p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130922035149/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
