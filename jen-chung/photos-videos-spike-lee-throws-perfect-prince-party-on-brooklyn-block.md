					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421005038/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/21/prince.php">unexpected death of performer, innovator and icon Prince</a> shocked the world yesterday, leaving a sudden void in his magnificent wake. So Spike Lee, a fan, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421005038/https://www.instagram.com/p/BEeG0NhSqnD/">friend</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421005038/https://www.instagram.com/p/BEeLE3Yyqgj/">collaborator</a>, decided to throw a block party in Fort Greene. His invite, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421005038/https://www.instagram.com/p/BEeSxEOSqhQ/?taken-by=officialspikelee">posted on Instagram</a>, read, &quot;We will dance, sing and shout to his music. Wear something purple.&quot; And they did, with gusto.</p>

<p>The street, S. Elliott Place between DeKalb and Lafayette Avenues in Fort Greene, was closed to traffic and transformed into a party, with a diverse mix of people pouring in to show their love and appreciation of the Purple One. Lee danced with the crowd and everybody sang. </p>

<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421005038/http://todseelie.com/">Tod Seelie</a>, who estimated there were 500-800 attendees, added that even the cops who were on duty were also singing along, &quot;It was a really good vibe all around.&quot;</p>
