					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015410/http://gothamist.com/2017/06/10/nsfw_photos_naked_people_times_square.php">Times Square wasn&apos;t the only place</a> where body positivity was celebrated over the weekend; a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015410/https://www.facebook.com/events/1259933680721956/">Body Pride Parade</a> brought dozens to the East Village, too. Those in attendance stripped down and marched while holding handwritten signs stating &quot;Love Your Body&quot; and &quot;Accept Yourself,&quot; with some passers-by joining in along the way.</p>

<p>The starting point was Tompkins Square Park, where people danced to a portable sound system while painting their bodies and making signs, according to photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015410/https://www.gretchenrobinette.com/">Gretchen Robinette</a>. They ended the march in Union Square. </p>

<p>Robinette said, &quot;The event&apos;s main purpose was providing a collective space to celebrate each other&apos;s unique body, creating a welcoming and accepting group to bare all. A large percentage of the over 50 or so participants took off their shirts, with some choosing to remain clothed, and others who stripped completely naked.&quot;</p>

<p>They also passed out stickers to everyone along the route that said to &quot;Love Thy Self&quot; and &quot;Create Positive Energy.&quot;</p>

<p>The parade was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015410/https://www.facebook.com/events/1259933680721956/">organized by Lightsource</a>, a self-described &quot;nonprofit nondenominational spiritual organization... dedicated to fostering art and creativity&quot; that encouraged participants to &quot;Be extravagant, be courageous, be proud!&quot;</p>
