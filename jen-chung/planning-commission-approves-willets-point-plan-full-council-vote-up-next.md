										<p><img alt="2008_09_willets2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150123020332im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_09_willets2.jpg" width="130" height="111" class="right">The Bloomberg administration&apos;s plan to transform the industrial Willets Point section of Queens was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150123020332/http://www.nysun.com/new-york/willets-point-plan-gets-planning-approval/86578/">approved by the City Council&apos;s planning committee.</a>  But, the Sun points out, &quot;the council vote will be its biggest hurdle.&quot;  The $3 billion to make way for a hotel, convention center, offices, and stores, is supposed to generate 5,300 jobs and billions in economic activity, but about 200 small business owners, who would be forced out, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150123020332/http://gothamist.com/2008/08/14/bloombergs_willets_point_plan_draws.php">have been bitterly opposing the project</a>, unhappy the city calls the area &quot;neglected,&quot; when it&apos;s the city <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150123020332/http://gothamist.com/2008/04/10/willets_point_l.php">that neglected Willets Point</a>.  Those critics <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150123020332/http://www.nypost.com/seven/09252008/news/regionalnews/willet_ever_be_finished__130661.htm">believe the plan will be quashed</a> by the Council.</p>
