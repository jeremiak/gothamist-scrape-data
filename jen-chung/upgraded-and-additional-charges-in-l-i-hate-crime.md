title: Upgraded and Additional Charges in L.I. Hate Crime

                            <p><img src="https://web.archive.org/web/20131129184932im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_11_liteens.jpg" class="right"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131129184932/http://www.newsday.com/news/local/suffolk/ny-lihate1121,0,5394708.story">Newsday reports</a> Jeffrey Conroy, the teen who allegedly stabbed immigrant <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131129184932/http://gothamist.com/2008/11/11/teens_charged_with_hate_crime_in_lo.php">Marcelo Lucero to death</a>, will now face the charge of murder as a hate crimes charge, upgraded from manslaughter as a hate crime.  Conroy and six other teens involved in the attack on Lucero (prosecutors say they were specifically looking to &quot;f--- up Mexicans&quot;) were also charged with conspiracy in Lucero&apos;s death and second-degree attempted assault <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131129184932/http://wcbstv.com/topstories/hector.sierra.hate.2.864853.html">related to another attack</a>.  Newsday also went to a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131129184932/http://www.newsday.com/news/local/suffolk/ny-lipatc205934527nov20,0,380680.story">parents&apos; meeting at Patchogue-Medford High School</a>, which the teens attend. While many spoke of healing the community, one parent yelled, &quot;<strong>What are we doing to make sure the media doesn&apos;t grab our kids to be interviewed?</strong>&quot; and then many parents screamed and confronted the Newsday reporter--who was escorted out even though she was given permission to attend.</p>
