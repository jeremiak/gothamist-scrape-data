<p>Bernie Sanders capped off a long day of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024607/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/09/bernie_brooklyn_rallies.php">stump speeches in Brooklyn</a> on Friday by taking his wife to the smash hit Broadway musical <em>Hamilton</em>. A Sanders campaign spokesperson told <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024607/https://twitter.com/Yamiche/status/718585151505186816">New York Times reporter Yamiche Alcindor</a> that &quot;they paid for the tickets themselves.&quot; Did they pay for someone to get <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024607/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/06/ham4ham_lottery_blaz.php">$10 Ham4Ham tickets</a>, or did they <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024607/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/17/pro_tip_dont_buy_fake_hamilton_tick.php">shell out a fortune for non-counterfeit ones</a>? </p>

<p>Actually, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108024607/http://www.nytimes.com/politics/first-draft/2016/04/09/bernie-sanderss-big-win-tickets-to-hamilton/">they are just special $167 tickets</a>: &quot;While Mr. Sanders didn&#x2019;t acquire the tickets like the 99 percent of Hamilton fans scouring the internet for seats or buying them months in advance, Michael Briggs, a spokesman for the campaign, said Mr. and Ms. Sanders did pay $167 each for two house seats, the small number of seats that are generally reserved for people like dignitaries and relatives of cast members.&quot;</p>
