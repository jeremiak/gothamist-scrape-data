<p>Big news:  A judge has dismissed the<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427162957/http://gothamist.com/tags/ppw"> lawsuit trying to block the Prospect Park West bike lane</a>.  Mayoral spokesman <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427162957/http://twitter.com/#!/marclavorgna/status/103618636496908291">Marc La Vorgna Tweeted</a>, &quot;In a decision issued late today, the City prevails in Prospect Park West Bike Lane lawsuit. Judge dismisses plaintiffs claims. #bikenyc&quot; The Department of Transportation&apos;s Twitter followed with, &quot;City prevails in Prospect Park West bike path case! Details and links to come Wednesday morning. #bikenyc&quot;</p>

<p>We&apos;ll cover the details tomorrow morning, but keep in mind that while this is a victory for the city and bicyclists and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427162957/http://gothamist.com/2011/04/11/video_big_crowd_rides_controversial.php">families</a> and community boards, this is also a defeat for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427162957/http://gothamist.com/2011/02/04/markowitz_rides_tricycle_into_state.php">bike lane-hating</a> Brooklyn Borough President Marty Markowitz (who <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427162957/http://gothamist.com/2011/07/19/was_ppw_bike_lane_a_trial_in_realit.php">insisted the bike lane was just a trial</a>), big shots like <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427162957/http://gothamist.com/2011/03/07/park_slope_big_shots_sue_dot_over_p.php">former DOT Commissioner Iris Weinshall</a> (and her <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427162957/http://gothamist.com/2011/03/28/schumer_wont_comment_on_bike_lanes.php">husband Senator Chuck Schumer</a>) and former Sanitation Commissioner Norman Steisel and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150427162957/http://gothamist.com/2011/03/08/6_out_of_6_local_pedestrians_agree.php">everyone else who hates the bike lane</a>. </p>
