title: 5-Alarm Fire Rips Through Under-Construction Chelsea Luxury Condo Building
---

	
										<p>A fire that started in a Manhattan apartment building under construction turned into a five-alarm blaze this morning. No injuries have been reported yet, and now the Fire Department is reportedly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402042016/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2015/10/20/chelsea-5-alarm-fire/">concerned the structure will collapse</a>.</p>

<p>The fire was reported around 3 a.m. at 221 West 17th Street in Chelsea. It quickly grew to five alarms, as it spread to the 4th, 5th, 6th floors and roof.</p>

<p>The building is vacant, and the Fire Department evacuated neighboring buildings. One resident told CBS New York, &quot;We woke up and smelled smoke. Always upsetting when you see firemen in your hallway. It&#x2019;s never a good sign,&quot; while another said, &quot;They want everyone out of the building. In case it comes down, you&#x2019;d be sort of trapped inside.&quot;</p>

<p>The fire was under control by 6 a.m.</p>

<p>The site is to be the location of The Dorian, a 14-unit residential condo; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402042016/http://therealdeal.com/blog/2015/05/21/revealed-pricing-renderings-for-delshahs-new-chelsea-project/">the Real Deal reported in May</a> that it would include &quot;full-floor penthouse units on the seventh through 10th floors and two units on each of the second through sixth floors... with lower-floor residences starting at around $3 million and the building&#x2019;s top-floor 3,000-square-foot penthouse, which features three bedrooms and private roof access, asking between $9 million and $10 million.&quot;</p>
