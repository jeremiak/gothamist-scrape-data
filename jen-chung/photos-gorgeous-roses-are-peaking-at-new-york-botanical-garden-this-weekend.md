					<p>This weekend, the New York Botanic Garden is having a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160801211434/http://www.nybg.org/exhibitions/2014/festivals/rose-garden-festival/rose-garden-festival.php">celebration in the Peggy Rockefeller Rose Garden</a>, just in time for the roses&apos; peaking. The garden&apos;s curator, Stephen Scanniello, tells us, &quot;This weekend will be the cusp of peak bloom. The peak will continue for the next two weeks. This weekend is a rare opportunity to see the most beautiful of the oldest and the most beautiful of the newest rose varieties. Usually one precedes the other, but right now, they&#x2019;re blooming together.&#x201D;</p>

<p>Scanniello gave some examples of those old and new varieties: &quot;Apothecary&#x2019;s Rose&quot; which &quot;goes back to the 16th century,&quot; was &quot;grown in colonial NYC, petals were used as a medicinal plant - red with visible yellow center,&quot; and the pale pink &quot;Kazanlik Rose,&quot; or &quot;ancient rose...also grown in colonial New York and used as a source of fragrance&quot; is &quot;still used to make rosewater or rose oil.&quot; Two of the newest roses are &quot;Poseidon,&quot; which is &quot;lavender, very fragrant,&quot; and &quot;Old Baylor&apos;s,&quot; a &quot;pure white and extremely fragrant flower.&quot;</p>

<p>There are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160801211434/http://www.nybg.org/exhibitions/2014/festivals/rose-garden-festival/rose-garden-festival.php">also many festivities on Saturday and Sunday</a>&#x2014;a phonograph (yes, <em>phonograph</em>) DJ, dance and jazz performances and sessions on planting roses and watercolor painting. </p>

<p>Tickets are $30 for adults and $15 for children 2-12 (chi;dren under 2 are free). It&apos;s free for members. </p>
