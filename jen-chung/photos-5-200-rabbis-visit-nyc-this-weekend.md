</div></div>					<p>In what&apos;s considered the largest Jewish gathering in North America, thousands of rabbis from 86 countries are in New York City for International Conference of Chabad-Lubavitch Emissaries. And this morning, they visited the gravesite of the Rebbe, Rabbi Menachem M. Schneerson.</p>

<p>Schneerson died on June 12, 1994 and he <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160325074013/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/02/nyregion/thousands-descend-on-queens-to-mourn-rabbi-menachem-mendel-schneerson.html?partner=rss&amp;emc=rss&amp;_r=1">&quot;is considered one of the most influential Jewish leaders of the 20th century&quot;</a>, as head of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160325074013/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chabad">Chabad-Lubavitch</a> movement. </p>

<p>Chabad says this is a year of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160325074013/http://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/744089/jewish/What-Is-Hakhel.htm">Hakhel</a>, a time to promote Jewish unity (here&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160325074013/http://www.jewishpress.com/judaism/parsha/the-beauty-of-hakhel/2015/11/06/">another explanation</a>). At this weekend&apos;s conference, rabbis will discuss efforts to expand at college campuses and how to have more gatherings, to observe and study the Torah, through synagogues and organizations across the world. </p>

<p>If you happen to be near Chabad&apos;s headquarters (Eastern Parkway in Crown Heights) on Sunday morning, at 8:30 a.m., you&apos;ll see the 5,200  rabbis gathering for their &quot;class picture.&quot; Later that night, they&apos;re having dinner and &quot;spirited Hasidic dancing&quot; in Sunset Park.</p>
