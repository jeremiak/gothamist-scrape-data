</div></div>					<p>Waking up early and watching pre-dawn coverage of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504151822/http://gothamist.com/2011/04/29/royal_wedding_prince_william_and_ka.php#photo-1">Prince William and Kate Middleton&apos;s wedding</a> might be impressive&#x2014;but waking up and dressing up in wedding finery and hats at a Wills and Kate party takes real commitment!  There were a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504151822/http://gothamist.com/2011/04/26/where_to_watch_the_royal_wedding_in.php">number of places</a> where New Yorkers could watch the glittering event, and those who showed up had no regrets: One woman <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504151822/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/international/royalwedding/the_big_apple_awakens_for_royal_IiTEPY7bj8YD9L36RfWWbJ">told the Post</a>, &quot;I wasn&apos;t going to get up so early and do this, but then decided, what the hell? And I&apos;m glad I did! It had been 30 years since I saw the last one.&quot;</p>

<p>At the DUMBO Archway under the Manhattan Bridge, the DUMBO Business Improvement District held a viewing party and they tells us, &quot;We had over over 300 people from all five boroughs at 5:30AM this morning.&#xA0; The first guests arrived at 4:15AM.&quot;  In Times Square, TLC had a viewing party&#x2014;and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504151822/http://www.reuters.com/article/2011/04/29/uk-britain-wedding-newyork-idUSTRE73S3C620110429">some couples were even married</a>!  Over in the West Village (aka &quot;Little Britain&quot;), watchers seemed to be happy for the couple and one ventured that the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504151822/http://www.nydailynews.com/lifestyle/royalwedding/2011/04/29/2011-04-29_royal_wedding_fever_hits_new_york_as_excited_british_expats_gather_to_watch_cere.html">Duchess of Cambridge&apos;s sister Pippa Middleton was sexier</a>.</p>
