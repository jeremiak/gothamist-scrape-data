---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_05_megmccain.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160528235801im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_05_megmccain.jpg" width="140" height="144" class="image-right"> </span>Meghan McCain, daughter of the Senator and presidential candidate John McCain, has an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160528235801/http://www.nydailynews.com/opinions/2009/05/22/2009-05-22_why_the_new_york_gop_should_embrace_gay_marriage.html">opinion piece in the Daily News today</a>. It&apos;s actually an open letter to NY State&apos;s Republicans, asking them to support the state&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160528235801/http://gothamist.com/2009/04/16/paterson_proposes_to_legalize_gay_m.php">gay marriage legislation</a>, &quot;No matter how politically charged the discussions about marriage equality may get, the question is really a simple one: Do the rights and privileges we offer citizens include everyone in our country, or only some of us? I believe that allowing gays and lesbians the freedom to marry is an idea whose time has come... Equality under the law and personal freedoms are what make America the greatest country in the world, and they are core values that I hold as a Republican.&quot;   McCain also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160528235801/http://twitter.com/McCainBlogette/status/1883196114">Tweets</a>, &quot;<strong>I sincerely hope new york lawmakers will vote YES on the marriage equality bill!</strong>&quot;  If more than a few Republicans were to vote yes, the bill could possibly pass (the Senate&apos;s Democrats <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160528235801/http://gothamist.com/2009/05/18/dueling_views_on_gay_marriage_at_mi.php">are split</a>).</p>
