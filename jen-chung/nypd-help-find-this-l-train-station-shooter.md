<p>A 35-year-old man was shot in the leg at a Brooklyn train station last week, and now the police are hoping the public can help them find the assailant.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2015_12_livonia.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160428152449im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_12_livonia.jpg" width="376" height="243" class="image-right"> </span>According to the NYPD, the assault occurred on Thursday, December 17, at around 12:50 a.m.: Apparently the victim had gotten into an argument with another man at the Livonia L station which resulted in the other man shooting him in the right lower leg. </p>

<p>The suspect fled and the victim went to Brookdale Hospital on his own.</p>

<p>Police released photographs of the suspect, who is described as being about 20-30 years old and about 5&apos;6&quot;-5&apos;8&quot; tall and was last wearing a red hooded sweatshirt, white t-shirt and jeans.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160428152449/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
