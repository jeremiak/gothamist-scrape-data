					<p>Befitting an historic election with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161126154113/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/08/photos_nyc_election_day_2016.php#photo-1">impressive turnout</a> (and the usual round of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161126154113/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/08/democracy_held_together_by_tape.php">frustrating, democracy-suppressing problems</a>), hundreds of people are waiting to enter the Javits Center for Hillary Clinton&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161126154113/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/01/hillary_clintons_already_booked_fir.php">Election night big bash</a>.</p>

<p>The Javits Center, on Manhattan&apos;s West Side on 11th Avenue between 34th and 38th Streets, is the perfect all-glass setting for Clinton. Members of the public, who submitted requests to attend via a website, had been waiting for hours, with lines stretching to 12th Avenue and 40th Street. And there&apos;s a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161126154113/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/07/nypd_election_nyc_terrorism.php">serious police presence</a>, of course.</p>
