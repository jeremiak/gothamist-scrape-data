										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-left" style=" width:260px; "> <img alt="2010_10_dhlcscare.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407153242im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2010_10_dhlcscare.jpg" width="260" height="193"> <br> <i>At JFK Airport last night (WABC 7)</i></div> Last night, the DHL cargo facility at JFK Airport was evacuated due to a suspicious package.  The package, which had a cellphone and paperwork, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407153242/http://abclocal.go.com/wabc/story?section=news/local&amp;id=7763750">was ultimately deemed safe</a>, but the episode signaled how worried authorities are in the wake of last week&apos;s mail bombs, which were sent from Yemen and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407153242/http://gothamist.com/2010/10/30/explosives_bound_for_us_bear_hallma.php">intercepted in the U.K. and Dubai</a>.  Now, France&apos;s interior minister claims one of the bombs was defused <em>17 minutes before it was set to explode</em>.<p></p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407153242/http://www.nydailynews.com/news/world%20/2010/11/04/2010-11-04_mail_bomb_from_yemen_bound_for_us_was_17_minutes_away_from_exploding_french_offi.html">Daily News reports</a>, &quot;Brice Hortefeux declined to reveal where he got the information from, which bomb it was, or where it could have potentially exploded, during an interview with the country&apos;s state-run channel, France-2 Televison.&quot; The packages, which contained printer parts as well as the explosive PETN, are believed to be the work of Al Qaeda operatives, specifically <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407153242/http://gothamist.com/2010/11/01/yemeni_mail_bomb_suspect_blew_up_hi.php">Ibrahim Hassan al-Asiri</a>, in Yemen. </p>

<p>The packages, shipped by UPS and FedEx, were addressed to synagogues in Chicago, but experts say they were designed to explode mid-air.  One of the bombs also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407153242/http://gothamist.com/2010/10/31/mail_bomb_found_in_dubai_flew_on_tw.php">traveled on two passenger flights</a>.</p>
