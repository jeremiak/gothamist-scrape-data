<span class="photo_caption">Photos from Saturday&apos;s robbery at the East Village Chase</span></p>

<p>Over the weekend, the police asked for the public&apos;s help in finding a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510012904/http://gothamist.com/2014/10/05/cops_say_possible_albino_suspect_ro.php">suspect who had robbed an East Village Chase bank</a> on Saturday, describing him as a &quot;white male (possibly albino).&quot; Now it turns out that the same suspect is accused of robbing two other Manhattan banks <em>on Monday/em&gt;.</em></p><em>

<p>According to police, at about 2:41 p.m., the suspect &quot;entered the Citibank Branch located at 1107 Broadway [at 24th Street]. The suspect then handed a demand note to the teller. The teller complied and the suspect fled the location with $600.00.&quot;</p>

<p>Then, at 3:22 p.m., he &quot;entered the Chase Bank Branch located at 835 Broadway. The suspect then handed a demand note to the teller. The teller complied and the suspect fled the location with $4,740.00.&quot;</p>

<p>Police released another photograph of the suspect on a subway platform, along with a video:</p>

<p>After the first incident on Saturday, police described the suspect as  &quot;white male (possibly albino), 5&apos;10&quot;, mid to late 20s, light colored hair with a medium build. He was last seen wearing a black and grey jacket, dark rimmed glasses, black sneakers with a red bandana around his neck.&quot; The additional photograph and video show he&apos;s wearing a similar, if not the same, outfit.</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510012904/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
