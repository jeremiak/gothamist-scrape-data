					<p>The intense <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161029193958/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/12/video_firefighters_battle_jersey_sh.php">10-alarm fire that devastated</a> the Jersey Shore on Thursday and Friday briefly rekindled this morning. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161029193958/http://www.nj.com/ocean/index.ssf/2013/09/seaside_boardwalk_fire_rekindles_briefly_as_investigators_continue_to_probe_cause.html">According to the Star-Ledger</a>, &quot;The latest hot spot, which flared up around 4 a.m., is one of two or three that continue to challenge firefighters, who have been at the scene round-the-clock since Thursday, [Ocean County Prosecutor&apos;s Office spokesman Al] Della Fave said.&quot; </p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161029193958/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/13/photos_video_unthinkable_6-alarm_fi.php#photo-1">fire consumed the boardwalk and businesses</a> in Seaside Park and Seaside Heights, areas that were badly hit during Hurricane Sandy. While there had been some reports that the fire was suspicious, Della Fave said, &quot;There&apos;s still no determination on cause. No one at the Ocean County Prosecutor&apos;s Office has labeled the fire suspicious. Our only comment is that the investigation continues. There will be no more additional comments or releases, until the investigation is complete.&quot;</p>

<p>The fire, which started at 2:30 p.m., was spread in part because of the 30 mph winds and the tar roofs of buildings. It started near Kohr&apos;s ice cream, on the boardwalk, and was out of control from nearly the beginning. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161029193958/http://www.nj.com/news/index.ssf/2013/09/last_stand_at_lincoln_avenue_becomes_decisive_moment_in_boardwalk_blaze.html#incart_m-rpt-1">From the Star-Ledger</a>: </p><blockquote>It was just after 4 p.m. Thursday and the Last Stand at Lincoln Avenue was about to begin.<p></p>

<p>The decision was made to use an excavator to tear up part of the boardwalk and dig a trench to stem the advance of the fire that had already ravaged four blocks of shops, stores and arcades.</p>

<p>&#x201C;It was the decisive moment,&#x201D; Gov. Chris Christie said. &#x201C;That&#x2019;s where we decided to put all our resources and make our stand. If it had gotten by us on Lincoln Avenue, there&#x2019;s no telling where it would have stopped.&#x201D;</p>

<p>About 11 p.m. Thursday, firefighters brought the boardwalk blaze under control at Lincoln, preventing the fire from advancing deep into Seaside Heights.</p></blockquote>NJ Governor Chris Christie has promised state aid to help with the rebuilding, and rebuilding the boardwalk <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161029193958/http://www.app.com/article/20130914/NJNEWS/309140019/Seaside-boardwalk-repairs?nclick_check=1">might cost $1.75 million</a>. He said, &quot;We will rebuild. We&#x2019;ll make new memories for our families, because that&#x2019;s what we do.&quot; And Seaside Park&apos;s administrator Bob Martucci was weary, after Hurricanes Irene and Sandy, &quot;I don&#x2019;t know.
