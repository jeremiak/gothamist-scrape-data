					<p>If you had the choice of seeing a movie about some burned guy in Italy reminiscing about Tunisia or a movie about a family who are in a brown paper bag, what would you choose? And if you picked the brown paper bag movie, then you&apos;re in the fine company of Elaine Benes&#x2014;and you better hope this Upper East Side movie theater opens back up, so it can show <em>Sack Lunch</em>.</p>

<p>Reader Pasquale Iacono tipped us off about the posters and indeed they are outside a shuttered movie theater on Second Avenue and 64th Street. The fictional movie was mentioned in the Seinfeld episode during the eighth season. Everyone except Elaine wants to see <em>The English Patient</em> (&quot;It&apos;s up for all those Oscars&quot;):</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="//web.archive.org/web/20161108004539if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/8u91zAolT3A?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Elaine wonders, &quot;So d&apos;you think they got shrunk down, or is it just a giant sack?&quot; and throughout the episode (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108004539/http://www.seinfeldscripts.com/TheEnglishPatient.html">script</a>), she fights against The English Patient Conspiracy.</p>

<p>The poster was made by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108004539/http://jayshells.com/">Jayshells</a>, the street artist behind <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108004539/http://gothamist.com/tags/jayshells">some other fun signs</a>. In fact, last December, he put up <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108004539/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/05/posters_for_seinfeld_movie_rochelle.php"><em>Rochelle, Rochelle</em> signs at the same theater</a>!</p>

<p>In the movie credit copy section of the poster, Jayshells explains the movie&apos;s role in the episode, including how it&apos;s an in-flight movie for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108004539/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B57bOy2Dzjg">Elaine&apos;s plane ride</a>.</p>
