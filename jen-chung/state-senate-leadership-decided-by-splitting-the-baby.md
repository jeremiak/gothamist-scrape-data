title: State Senate Leadership Decided By Splitting the Baby
---

	
										<p><img alt="2008_12_senst.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160229204604im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_12_senst.jpg" width="145" height="119" class="right">After weeks of hold outs preventing the fate of the State Senate leadership to be announced, the parties have <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160229204604/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/12/05/nyregion/05senate.html?ref=nyregion">agreed to a Solomonic deal</a>: The NY Times explains that Malcolm Smith, the former Senate minority leader, &quot;would be elected president pro tempore, a constitutional office that makes him the chamber&#x2019;s leader,&quot; while Senator-elect Ruben Espada Jr. &quot;would be elected to what would be the subordinate post of majority leader and appointed vice chairman of the Rules Committee, which must approve all legislation that goes through the Senate.&quot;  This is based on a handshake deal from Smith, Espada, Senator Ruben Diaz, and Senator Carl Kruger (the latter two also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160229204604/http://www.nydailynews.com/blogs/dailypolitics/2008/12/details-of-a-leadership-deal.html">get big roles</a>)--and it still needs to be approved.  And rumor has it that the deal was reached partly because Smith agreed he <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160229204604/http://weblogs.newsday.com/news/local/longisland/politics/blog/2008/12/president_malcolm_a_few_detail.html">wouldn&apos;t bring up same-sex marriage</a> during the next session.</p>
