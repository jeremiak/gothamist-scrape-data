<p>Two royal fans outside the hotel told WCBS 2, &quot;We just want to see them, even if it&#x2019;s just a glimpse&quot; and &quot;Their lifestyle is like a fairytale. It&#x2019;s so intriguing.&quot; Another, a 19-year-old woman, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510011218/http://pagesix.com/2014/12/07/royal-couple-lands-in-nyc/?_ga=1.105546819.1965030224.1401028033">said to the Post</a>, &quot;I&#x2019;m obsessed with Kate Middleton. She&#x2019;s a great role model and a great person to look up to. They are a great power couple.&quot; (A great power couple who has NEVER BEEN TO NYC BEFORE. And people call them worldly!) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510011218/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/12/08/nyregion/prince-william-and-catherine-arrive-in-new-york-city.html?_r=1&amp;emc=edit_ur_20141208&amp;nl=nyregion&amp;nlid=63420699">From the NY TImes</a>:</p><blockquote>Nearby, a woman had lugged life-size cutouts of the duke and duchess to the edge of a police barricade. A group sang &#x201C;My Favorite Things,&#x201D; swaying in the cold. Others, like Jayda Green, 13, from Idaho Falls, Idaho, held flowers.<p></p>

<p>&#x201C;Our celebrities and politicians, they&#x2019;re shooting stars. They come and go,&#x201D; said Ms. Green&#x2019;s mother, Colleen, who brought her daughter to New York for the occasion. &#x201C;Royalty is forever.&#x201D;</p></blockquote>The British taxpayers, plus some other groups, are footing the bill for the trip, but we&apos;re pretty sure NYC taxpayers are paying for the police escorts.<p></p>

<p>Today, Prince William will be heading to D.C. for the day while the Duchess will be in NYC, meeting with First Lady Chirlane McCray. Tonight, the couple will be at the Barclays Center for the Brooklyn Nets&apos; game against the Cleveland Cavaliers. It&apos;s unclear if the British royals will meet America&apos;s royals, Beyonce and Jay-Z, but a royal spokesman told the Times, &quot;They&#x2019;re not due to meet. But who knows what will happen?&quot; Sounds like someone wants to Break The Internet, but protesters <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510011218/http://nypost.com/2014/12/08/protesters-want-to-wreck-will-and-kates-nba-game/">are hoping to shut down</a> the game, which is more interesting.</p>

<p>The Duke and Duchess will have to deal with our gauche, New World ways. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510011218/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/12/08/nyregion/prince-william-and-catherine-arrive-in-new-york-city.html?_r=1&amp;emc=edit_ur_20141208&amp;nl=nyregion&amp;nlid=63420699">Times spoke to an etiquette person</a>:</p><blockquote>It is unclear if other residents might be up to the task of impressing the royals, though few will get the chance. Patricia Napier-Fitzpatrick, the founder of the Etiquette School of New York, said that certain traits associated with many New Yorkers &#x2014; talking with one&#x2019;s hands, say, or asking personal questions without a second thought &#x2014; should be curbed.<p></p>

<p>&#x201C;Be yourself, but best self,&#x201D; Ms. Napier-Fitzpatrick suggested, before reconsidering.</p>

<p>&#x201C;Better self,&#x201D; she amended.</p></blockquote>In other words, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510011218/http://gothamist.com/2014/11/24/nyc_subway_worst_ranking.php">they should not take the subway</a>.<p></p>
