                            <p>Last night, many of us took a moment out to observe the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130915060206/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/28/tribute_in_light_coming_back_for_91.php">Tribute in Light</a>, the stunning light installation that marks the 9/11 attacks in a simple yet incredibly moving way way. </p>

<p>The project, designed by Julian Laverdiere and Paul Myoda, was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130915060206/http://creativetime.org/projects/tribute-in-light/">initially commissioned by Creative Time</a> six months after the attacks. Creative Time aptly describes the memorial as &quot;an ethereal surrogate for the absent towers, the lights&#x2019; white luminescence not only replaces the void in New York&#x2019;s skyline with a sense of memory and the possibility of hope and rebirth, but its ghostly presence is also a moving commemoration of the thousands of men and women who died on September 11, 2001.&quot;</p>

<p>Ever since then, the Tribute in Light has returned for at least one night every year.</p>
