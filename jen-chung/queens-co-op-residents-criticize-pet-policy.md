<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120602120013/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2010_05_cats.jpg"> <img alt="2010_05_cats.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20120602120013im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2010/05/2010_05_cats-thumb-249x208-504361.jpg" width="110" height="91" class="image-right"> </a> </span>Tenants at a co-op on 47th Avenue in Woodside are upset about a &quot;restrictive&quot; pet policy (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120602120013/http://gothamist.com/2010/04/29/pet-friendly_apartments_have_re-sel.php">nothing new there</a>)&#x2014;and some face eviction!  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120602120013/http://www.nydailynews.com/ny_local/queens/2010/05/05/2010-05-05_big_six_towers_coop_tenants_face_eviction_over_pets_in_woodside.html">According to the Daily News</a>, the co-op board at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120602120013/http://www.facebook.com/group.php?gid=48985131099">Big Six Towers</a>, originally built as &quot;an affordable haven for members of the New York Typographical Union-Local 6,&quot; prohibits dogs over 40 pounds, limits their presence in hallways, and allows the board to &quot;kick out any pooch that causes &apos;discomfort, annoyance or nuisance ... inconvenience or causes complaints&apos; to other residents.&quot;  Plus there&apos;s a $100/year dog fee and a $15 monthly fee.  A lawyer for a number of the tenants says, &quot;This policy interferes with people&apos;s rights under the law. They are charging fees that are unlawful and burdensome.&quot;</p>
