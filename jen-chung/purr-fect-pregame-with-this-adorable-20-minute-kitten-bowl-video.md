</div></div>					<p>While this year&apos;s Super Bowl LI can be viewed as an unsubtle <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170208044053/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/bill-maher-super-bowl-donald-trump-patriots_us_5895853ee4b0406131372618">metaphor for the state of politics today</a>, there is one thing everyone can agree on: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170208044053/http://www.hallmarkchannel.com/kitten-bowl">The Kitten Bowl</a> rules. This year, the fourth edition of the feline matchup features players like Tony Slo-Mo and Pawdell Beckham Jr.</p>

<p>Here&apos;s a 20-minute clip, which features North Shore Animal League America&apos;s Volunteer, Foster mom and National Spokesperson, Beth Stern; reporter Mary Carillo; Yankees announcer John Sterling; and former quarterback Boomer Esiason, who happens to be the Feline Football League Commissioner. </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170208044053if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/gPfPGpRY5oU" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>The program, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170208044053/http://www.hallmarkchannel.com/kitten-bowl">which started airing on 12 p.m.</a> on the Hallmark Channel and continues until 9 p.m. (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170208044053/http://www.oregonlive.com/tv/2017/02/when_are_the_2017_puppy_bowl_a.html">a three-hour block</a> featuring a pre-game show, playoffs and then a championship game repeats three times), highlights the importance of animal adoptions. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170208044053/http://animalleague.org/?referrer=https://www.google.com/">North Shore Animal League</a> and more than 100 other shelters are showing Kitten Bowl IV at their locations, as well as offering adoption specials. </p>
