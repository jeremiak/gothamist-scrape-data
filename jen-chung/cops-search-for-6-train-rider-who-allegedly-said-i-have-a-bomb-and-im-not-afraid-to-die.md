title: Cops Search For 6 Train Rider Who Allegedly Said, 'I Have A Bomb And I'm Not Afraid To Die'
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170312115514/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2017_01_threats1.jpg"> <img alt="2017_01_threats1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170312115514im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2017/02/2017_01_threats1-thumb-400x497-984601.jpg" width="270" height="335" class="image-right"> </a> </span>Last month, police say a man told Manhattan subway riders that he had a &quot;bomb and I&apos;m not afraid to use it.&quot; Now investigators have released a sketch of the person, who is &quot;wanted for questioning in connection to making terroristic threats,&quot; and hope the public can help identify him.</p>

<p>The incident occurred on January 5th, around 6 p.m. on a 6 train. According to the NYPD, as the train was heading north from Lexington Avenue and 86th Street, the man yelled, &quot;I have a bomb and am not afraid to die,&quot; panicking and alarming fellow riders. </p>

<p>The suspect fled and no injuries were reported.</p>

<p>Police describe the suspect as being about  25-35 years old and 5&apos;5&quot;- 6&apos;0&quot;, and was last seen wearing a black coat, blue jeans and a knit hat.</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477) or for Spanish, 1-888-57-PISTA (74782). The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170312115514/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
