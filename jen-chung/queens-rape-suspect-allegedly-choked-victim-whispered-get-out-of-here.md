---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022004/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_10_rsuspect1.jpg"> <img alt="2016_10_rsuspect1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022004im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2016/10/2016_10_rsuspect1-thumb-300x367-970284.jpg" width="250" height="305" class="image-left"> </a> </span>Police have released a sketch of the suspect wanted for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022004/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/17/woman_raped_on_queens_sidewalk_whil.php">raping a woman on a Queens sidewalk</a> Sunday morning. The victim, a recent immigrant from China, was on her way to work; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022004/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/nyc-crime/new-details-sickening-rape-51-year-old-queens-woman-article-1.2833797">the Daily News reports</a>, she &quot;was walking the 45 blocks from her home to work because she hasn&#x2019;t yet learned the transit system.&quot;</p>

<p>The 51-year-old woman had been walking along the Horace Harding Expressway when was attacked around 6:22 a.m. and sexually assaulted behind 60-07 168 Street. According to the NYPD, she was approached &quot;from behind&quot; and then &quot;placed&apos; in a chokehold as the suspect &quot;rendered her semi-conscious threw her down to the ground and sexually assaulted her.&quot; </p>

<p>Authorities say the suspect fled in a dark, four-door sedan westbound on the Horace Harding Expressway. </p>

<p>A source tells the Daily News that the victim had been on the phone with her sister in China during the time of the attack. After the assault, the suspect &quot;tried to wipe both of them clean with a striped T-shirt and gave the victim some liquid to wash herself with, sources said... He spoke to the woman in a Chinese dialect before fleeing, sources said, uttering something to the effect of &apos;get out of here.&apos;&quot;</p>

<p>She managed to walk to her work, the IOG Supermarket on 69th Avenue, still 1.4 miles away, and called her son when she got there. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022004/http://nypost.com/2016/10/17/woman-attacked-raped-during-early-morning-walk-to-work/">The Post reports</a>, &quot;The son later went to the grocery store and the two reported the crime to police at around 10:45 a.m.&quot;</p>

A couple interviewed near the location told the Post they were frightened; the husband said, &quot;I&#x2019;m a man, but I&#x2019;m scared to walk by myself. If someone wants to do something bad, they are gonna study the area. They come here, they say, &#x2018;Oh, its dark. It&#x2019;s lonely...&#x2002;I can do something bad here.&apos;&quot;<p></p>

<p>Police say the suspect is between 35 to 45 years old, 5&apos;10&quot;, 180 lbs; and was last seen wearing dark colored hooded sweater and dark colored jeans.</p>

All calls are strictly confidential.</p>
