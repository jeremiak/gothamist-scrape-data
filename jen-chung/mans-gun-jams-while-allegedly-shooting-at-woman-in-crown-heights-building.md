										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2016_02_crownheightsstairs.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160712161306im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_02_crownheightsstairs.jpg" width="640" height="395" class="image-none"> </span><br>
 <br>
Yesterday afternoon, a woman walking up the stairs of her Brooklyn apartment building&#x2014;with her daughter&#x2014;was nearly shot by a man behind her.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incident occurred around 4:08 p.m. on March 5th. The victim was going upstairs in a building in near Bedford Avenue and Union Street &quot;when an unknown male approached her from behind and attempted to shoot her. The gun jammed and the victim fled into the apartment and locked the door behind her.&quot;</p>

<p>While she was inside the apartment, the suspect allegedly &quot;fired several additional rounds into the door before fleeing the location.&quot; The woman was not injured. </p>

<p>Police released surveillance video of the suspect, describing his as 35-40 years old and 6&apos;-6&apos;2&quot; and last seen wearing a black waist length jack, black cap, black pants and black shoes.</p>
