					<p>It&apos;s a beautiful day (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170420181951/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/14/forecast_summer_temperatures_for_su.php">so far</a>), and if you&apos;re looking for some weekend plans, you could do worse than heading to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170420181951/https://www.bbg.org/">Brooklyn Botanic Garden</a> for a lovely prelude to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170420181951/https://www.bbg.org/visit/event/sakura_matsuri_2017">Sakura Matsuri festival</a>. These new photographs of beautiful <em>Prunus persica</em> and <em>Prunus subhirtella</em>, not to mention <em>Prunus pendula</em> and <em>Prunus serrulata</em>, bode well for the celebration, which takes place April 29-30.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170420181951/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/01/warm_winter_flowers.php#photo-1">Worrying about the blooms</a> after rollercoaster weather is an annual tradition, and Melanie Sifton, the vice president for horticulture at the BBG, confirmed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170420181951/https://www.nytimes.com/2017/04/12/nyregion/cherry-blossoms-brooklyn-botanical-garden.html?_r=0">to the Times</a>, &quot;We obsess over them all year long.&quot;</p><blockquote>Though most New Yorkers don&#x2019;t pay any attention to the cherry trees until spring, maintaining the two dozen varieties is a yearlong project. Arborists test for microbes in the soil and build wooden buttresses for the lower branches of some of the oldest trees in the collection. Each year, a couple of dying trees are replaced with young trees...<p></p>

<p>Generally, the cherry blossom season lasts for only about six weeks &#x2014; what&#x2019;s called the hanami (literally &#x201C;flower viewing&#x201D;) &#x2014; from bloom to fallen petals. (Each tree blooms for just about a week and a half.) The Prunus Kanzan trees on the Esplanade, always the last to bloom, provide the finale for the festival.</p></blockquote>Here&apos;s the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170420181951/https://www.bbg.org/collections/cherries">BBG&apos;s cherry tree tracker</a>.<p></p>
