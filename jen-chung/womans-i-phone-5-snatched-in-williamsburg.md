<p>Smartphone snatchers aren&apos;t <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151223092114/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/14/smartphone_subway_theft.php">just on the subway</a>! </p>

<p>Police say that this past Thursday, just after 2 p.m., a 32-year-old woman was walking near 93 North 9th Street in Williamsburg&#x2014;where the Social Security office is&#x2014;when someone suddenly &quot;approached her and attempted to take her iPhone 5 from her hand.&quot; </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2015_10_iph.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151223092114im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_10_iph.jpg" width="380" height="180" class="image-right"> </span>According to the NYPD, &quot;The victim struggled with the suspect who was able to overpower her and remove the phone.&quot; The male suspect then fled the scene; police released images of him.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151223092114/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">the Crime Stoppers website</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then entering TIP577.</p>
