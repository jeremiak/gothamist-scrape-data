title: Will Rangel's Income be Above Rent Stabilized Ceiling?
---

<p><img alt="2008_09_rangelpc2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629085415im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_09_rangelpc2.jpg" width="130" height="118" class="right">The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629085415/http://www.nypost.com/seven/09202008/news/regionalnews/rangel_now_faces_losing_low_rent_apts__129974.htm">Post suggests</a> that Representative Charles Rangel&apos;s income may now be above $200,000, which is more than the $175,000-ceiling to qualify for rent-stabilized apartments.  Rangel up until very recently <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629085415/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/11/congressman_charles_rangel_and_his.php">occupied four rent-stabilized units</a> at Lenox Terrace, three for living and one as an office (he <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629085415/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/15/rangel_will_give_up_one_rentstabili.php">gave up the office</a> after the disclosure).  Previously, Rangel claimed his income was around $175,000, but there are recent revelations about <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629085415/http://gothamist.com/2008/09/05/rangels_75000.php">unreported income from a vacation villa</a>, unreported <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629085415/http://gothamist.com/2008/09/15/rangels_taxes_keep_turning_up_mangl.php">property sales</a>, and a free <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629085415/http://gothamist.com/2008/09/19/charles_rangel_may_get_house_ethics.php">parking space for an old car</a>.  Of course, the Post points out his landlord doen&apos;t have to deregulate his apartments.  But the building <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629085415/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/17/rangels_landlord_tried_to_evict_oth.php">has tried to kick out</a> rent-stabilized tenants for things like their children sending in rent checks for them (because the tenant was going blind).</p>
