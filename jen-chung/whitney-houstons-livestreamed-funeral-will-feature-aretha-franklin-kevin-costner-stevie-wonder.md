<p>The funeral for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/http://gothamist.com/2012/02/11/whitney_houston_has_died_at_age_48.php">Whitney Houston</a> is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/http://gothamist.com/2012/02/14/now_whitney_houstons_funeral_will_b.php">open only to invited guests</a>&#x2014;who include Aretha Franklin, Kevin Costner, Jay-Z, Beyonce, Oprah Winfrey, and Stevie Wonder so far&#x2014;but the public will get a chance to observe the services through a livestream.  The AP will broadcast the funeral at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/http://livestream.com/aplive.">http://livestream.com/aplive</a>: &quot;It will provide a much-needed connection for fans who have lacked a formal place to eulogize Houston, one of the world&apos;s best-selling artists who died in Beverly Hills, Calif., on Saturday at the age of 48.&quot;</p>

<p>Details about the funeral, scheduled for Saturday, have been leaking out: Kevin Costner, her bodyguard co-star, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/http://www.people.com/people/package/article/0,,20569493_20570875,00.html">will speak</a> as will her mentor Clive Davis.  Aretha Franklin and Stevie Wonder <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/http://www.nj.com/entertainment/celebrities/index.ssf/2012/02/report_kevin_costner_to_speak.html">will perform</a>, according to reports. Others guests expected are Elton John, Bill Cosby, David Bowie, and Chaka Khan.  </p>

<p>Houston&apos;s cause of death <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/http://laist.com/2012/02/13/beverly_hills_police_will_not_comme.php">has not been released yet</a>; it&apos;s believed that she was taking prescription drugs before she was found <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/http://laist.com/2012/02/12/report_whitney_houstons_body_was_fo.php">unconscious in her bathtub</a> at a Beverly Hilton suite on Saturday.  NJ Governor Chris Christie has been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/http://www.nytimes.com/2012/02/16/nyregion/christie-spars-on-twitter-over-honor-for-whitney-houston.html?ref=nyregion">getting flak for asking</a> that NJ state flags be flown at half-mast in honor of Houston, noting that she &quot;was an important part of the fabric, the cultural fabric of this state.&quot;  But annoyed people Tweeted at him, &quot;Whitney is no hero, she was a crack head who killed herself. Our flag is not to be lowered for a junkie.&quot;  Christie replied, &quot;Flag being lowered for her cultural contributions as an artist &amp; New Jerseyan. Her struggles w/substance abuse r a diff topic.&quot;</p>

<p>The other drama around Houston&apos;s funeral involves ex-husband Bobby Brown.  At first, it was rumored he was not invited because her family didn&apos;t want him; now it seems like he&apos;s going but he&apos;ll <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/there.http://www.nypost.com/p/news/national/family_thinks_fortune_brown_report_Rki12fA1wSKkVqdToeFKVM">still perform with New Edition after the services</a>.  And the family is also reportedly worried Brown will try to get his hands on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120218031708/there.http://www.nypost.com/p/news/national/family_thinks_fortune_brown_report_Rki12fA1wSKkVqdToeFKVM">Houston&apos;s money via their child Bobbi Kristina</a>.</p>
