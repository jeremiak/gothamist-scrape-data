title: 2 Dead After Chinatown Tour Bus Crashes In Delaware

<p>A tour bus from Chinatown crashed and overturned in Delaware yesterday afternoon, killing two people and injuring 48. A driver who pulled over before first responders arrived <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013527/http://www.delawareonline.com/story/news/local/2014/09/21/bus-overturns-del-red-lion-road-exit/16015463/">told the News Journal</a>, &quot;Everyone was in pain and crying out for help... There was not one person without blood on them.&quot;</p>

<p>The crash occurred south of of Wilmington on Delaware Route 1. The bus was exiting onto the off-ramp to US-13 in New Castle, and Sgt. Paul G. Shavack said, &quot;As the bus attempted to negotiate a moderate curve on the ramp, it exited the roadway and overturned onto its roof. The bus continued to slide on its roof traveling down a slight grass embankment, rotating onto its left side where it came to rest.&quot;</p>

<p>Passenger Hau&#x2019;y Chen, 54, of New York City, was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013527/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/New-York-City-Delaware-Tour-Bus-Crash-Kills-2-Chinatown-New-Castle-275972811.html">ejected and pinned under the bus</a>; she died at the scene. Idil Bashi, 30, of Istanbul, Turkey, was critically injured and died at a hospital.</p>

<p>The bus was operated by Am USA, a tour bus operator in Chinatown; the bus company has had no recorded crashes in 24 months. The bus driver, Jinli Zhao of Flushing, Queens, is being interviewed. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013527/http://7online.com/news/2nd-person-dies-in-chinatown-tour-bus-crash-in-delaware/318493/">According to the AP</a>, &quot;The passengers were taking a 3-day sightseeing tour to Washington that began Friday in New York, authorities said. The crash happened as the bus was heading back to New York.&quot; </p>
