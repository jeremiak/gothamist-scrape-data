										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2014_08_bruckern1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014305im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_08_bruckern1.jpg" width="640" height="446" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>Police are searching for a driver who left the scene of an accident, apparently after deliberately striking a pedestrian in the Mott Haven section of the Bronx yesterday.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014305/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Woman-Critically-Injured-Hit-Run-Bruckner-Expressway-Bronx-271765261.html">According to NBC New York</a>, &quot;Three women and a man were walking underneath Bruckner Expressway at East 138th Street in Port Morris at about 1 p.m., when the man exchanged words with a passenger in a passing vehicle, according to police.&quot;</p>

<p>The car, which might be a Chevy Impala, then made a U-turn and headed south&#x2014;and into one of the female pedestrians, a 50-year-old woman, who was crossing Bruckner Boulevard westbound. The victim, Christine Aybar, is in critical condition at Lincoln Hospital. </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="//web.archive.org/web/20150510014305if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/VO7ElIMcF_4?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510014305/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2014/08/19/police-search-for-driver-in-bronx-hit-and-run-that-left-woman-critically-hurt/">A man told WCBS 2</a>, &quot;The guy deliberately, deliberately turned the car around and threw the car towards me to hit me, but she jumped out of my way and she took the full impact.&quot; And a witness said, &quot;When she pushed him out the way, she tried to run the other way, but the car caught her and the front and back tires ran over her.&quot;</p>

<p>The police say that the car is a white sedan with a dark-colored front passenger quarter panel and bumper. Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
