<p>Will the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120314230606/http://gothamist.com/2008/08/27/hillary_clinton_says_barack.php#photo-1">Sisterhood of the Traveling Pantsuits</a> come back in four years?  Game Change authors John Heilemann and Mark &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120314230606/http://gothamist.com/2011/06/30/video_time_editor_calls_obama_a_dic.php">Obama is a dick</a>&quot; Halperin were on Morning Joe this morning predicting that Secretary of State is interested in running for President again.  </p>

<p>Heilemann, who covers politics for New York magazine <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120314230606/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/national/game_change_authors_say_hillary_6mC4RZva4SEGaktOmV764M">said the chances were &quot;99.4.</a> I think very high,&quot; while Halperin, an editor at Time, said, &quot;A little lower. I think that if Joe Biden wins re-election and runs, she&apos;s much less likely to run.&quot;</p>

<p>Clinton has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120314230606/http://abcnews.go.com/blogs/politics/2010/01/clinton-suggests-shes-a-oneterm-secretary-of-state/">previously suggested she&apos;s a one-term Secretary</a> and stated she thinks America is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120314230606/http://gothamist.com/2010/11/05/hillary_clinton_america_should_be_r.php">ready for a female President</a>.  Well, she can always <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120314230606/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/27/ice-t_endorses_hillary_clinton_for.php">be Ice T&apos;s homegirl</a>.  </p>

<p>Also, the Post thinks the current Democrat 2016 contenders are Maryland Gov. Martin O&apos;Malley, New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo and Colorado Gov. John Hickenlooper. </p>
