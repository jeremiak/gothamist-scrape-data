										<p><img alt="2008_08_starbur.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605050822im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_08_starbur.jpg" width="110" height="131" class="right">A judge approved for Steve &amp; Barry&apos;s,  the cheap-n-chic retail chain which <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605050822/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/09/did_898_dresses_do_steve_barrys_in.php">faced bankruptcy earlier</a> this summer, to be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605050822/http://www.newsday.com/business/ny-bzsteve0823,0,7987177.story">acquired by two investments firms for $163 million</a>.  Newsday reports the  new group, BHY S&amp;B; Holdings LLC, made up of Bay Harbor Management and York Capital (with Steve Shore and Barry Prevor as investors), will &quot;Steve &amp; Barry&apos;s merchandise inventories, store leases and all of Steve &amp; Barry&apos;s intellectual property rights, including its celebrity and brand licenses.&quot; Though Sarah Jessica Parker is still on board, Stephon Marbury claims Steve &amp; Barry&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605050822/http://www.newsday.com/news/local/nassau/ny-bzstep225811338aug22,0,1154436.story">still owes him $2 million</a> in licensing fees--plus more in roylaties--for the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160605050822/http://www.starbury.com/">Starbury brand</a>.  But Newsday points out, &quot;Under the bankruptcy proceedings, the new owners would owe Starbury nothing.&quot;</p>
