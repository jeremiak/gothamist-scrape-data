title: Property Rebate Checks Sent Out Without Ceremony

										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_01_rebcheck.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160304065001im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_01_rebcheck.jpg" width="140" height="111" class="image-right"> </span><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160304065001/http://www.politickerny.com/1319/rebate-checks-much-shorter-note">Politicker NY finds</a> that the finally-released $400 property rebate checks have a much shorter note than last year&apos;s checks.  This year, it&apos;s five words&#x2014; &quot;Enclosed is your rebate check&quot;&#x2014;while last year, it was, &quot;The Mayor and the City Council have approved another property tax rebate. Your enclosed rebate check is the City&#x2019;s way of recognizing the sacrifice you made to keep New York City fiscally sound.&#x201D; The Mayor was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160304065001/http://gothamist.com/2008/12/18/city_council_gets_400_property_reba.php">against sending out the checks</a> up until late last year but before the 2005 mayoral election, Bloomberg made sure homeowners knew who was helping them. The mayor&apos;s &quot;name [was mentioned] no less than four times: once on the envelope, twice in an accompanying letter and once on the check itself&quot; in 2004, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160304065001/http://www.nytimes.com/2005/03/06/nyregion/06rebate.html">according to the NY Times</a>.</p>
