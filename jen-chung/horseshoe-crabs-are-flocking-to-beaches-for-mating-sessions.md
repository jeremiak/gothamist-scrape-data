<p>Beachgoers up and down the East Coast have been coming across horseshoe crabs. And that&apos;s because it&apos;s time for the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170603220217/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/02/brooklyn_crabs_ihtm.php#photo-1">marine arthropods to mate</a>. </p>

<p>Last weekend, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170603220217/https://www.instagram.com/p/BUqESlXhiIb/?taken-by=fireislandnps">National Parks Service on Fire Island posted a photo on Instagram</a>, noting, &quot;Have you seen horseshoe crabs on Fire Island? Keep an eye out: Horseshoe crabs come ashore around the new and full moons of May and June to mate and lay eggs.&quot;</p>

<p>&quot;During the spawn, the larger female crabs, with one or more male crabs in tow, dig into the sand at high tide and lay 80,000 to 100,000 eggs in a clutch, where the male will fertilize them,&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170603220217/http://www.pressofatlanticcity.com/news/breaking/horseshoe-crabs-which-pre-date-dinosaurs-spawn-in-droves-on/article_5eaec24c-9976-5c83-b502-506505aaa9a1.html">according to the Press of Atlantic City</a>. And this is what that looks like:</p>

<p>The NY State Department of Environmental Conservation has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170603220217/http://www.dec.ny.gov/outdoor/107977.html">closed areas to hand harvests of horseshoe crabs</a>, but there is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170603220217/http://www.nyharbornature.com/blog/its-not-easy-being-a-horseshoe-crab-around-new-york-harbor">no total moratorium on harvesting</a>. Horseshoe crabs are sought after because of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170603220217/http://www.cnn.com/2014/09/04/health/this-crabs-blood-could-save-your-life/">their blood&apos;s clotting properties</a>. </p>

<p>It&apos;s also considered to be an aphrodisiac in some cultures; in April, a woman was issued a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170603220217/https://www.nps.gov/gate/learn/news/illegal-taking-of-horseshoe-crabs.htm">summons for harvesting the crabs</a> in Plumb Beach  for her business.</p>

<p>Last year, Gothamist&apos;s Emma Whitford went to Calvert Vaux Park to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170603220217/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/02/brooklyn_crabs_ihtm.php#photo-1">watch the spawning and wrote:</a></p><blockquote>The biggest threat to New York City horseshoe crabs is the proliferation of bulkheads&#x2014;especially since Hurricane Sandy. Before the Parks department restored Calvert Vaux in 2012, horseshoe crabs weren&apos;t able to spawn there. &quot;They would just hit the wall and swim around and that was it,&quot; [NYC Parks Department projects manager Ellen] Hartig recalled.<p></p>

<p>City-wide, Calvert Vaux is the only sandy slip that&apos;s been restored specifically as a spawning ground for horseshoe crabs. &quot;The things people do to protect their property are affecting the intertidal zone,&quot; [Cornell Cooperative Extension marine educator Matt] Sclafani said. &quot;If the crabs can&apos;t lay on the beach, they can&apos;t survive.&quot;</p>

<p>But horseshoe crabs are notoriously resilient. Coney Island Creek is connected to a sewer overflow pipe that releases road water and, when it rains, sewage. The night we visited, one horseshoe crab swam along with a piece of toilet paper trailing from his shell.</p></blockquote><p></p>
