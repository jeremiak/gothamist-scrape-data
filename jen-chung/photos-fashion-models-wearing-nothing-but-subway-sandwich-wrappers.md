                            <p>Fashion Week is all about seeing what&apos;s in store for spring/summer 2014, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131203065115/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/09/fashion_week_3.php">edgy street style</a> and big brands capitalizing on to the buzz. Brands like Subway, the sandwich chain, which resurrected its &quot;Project Subway&quot; fashion challenge, which involved designers making dresses out of Subway sandwich wrappers and dishes.</p>

<p>The event was held at Pier 59 yesterday, with designers like Danilo Gabrielli, Jennifer Henry, Mariana Valentina, and Ainslee Bowers demonstrating that they could make a stylish gowns. And the winner would get their dress displayed in a midtown Subway&#x2014;and a year of free Subway Footlongs (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131203065115/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/17/the_5_best_parts_of_the_ny_posts_in.php">which may not be a foot-long</a>). </p>

<p>In the end, Gabrielli won. Subway marketing guy Tony Pace said, &quot;While folks might not readily equate FOOTLONGS&#x2122; and fashion, there are some similarities between the two. Both allow for individualized customization and creativity. We&apos;re glad to provide designers a new venue to express their unique style on the runway.&quot;</p>
