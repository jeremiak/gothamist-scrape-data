<p>Police believe the man arrested for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131003191326/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/01/subway_rider_beaten_with_pipe_cops.php">beating a man in a Brooklyn subway station</a> with a pipe is the same man who <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131003191326/http://gothamist.com/2013/09/25/man_bashed_in_the_head_with_brick_n.php?no_redirect=true">attacked a man in Manhattan with a brick</a>.</p>

<p>The incident in Manhattan occurred on September 21st at 11 p.m., when Andrew Billington, 37, and his girlfriend were walking down West 58th Street after seeing a concert at Terminal 5. A suspect attacked Billington from behind, smashed him on the head with a cloth-covered brick and then beat him in the face. The suspect rifled through Billington&apos;s pockets but didn&apos;t take anything. </p>

<p>The Brooklyn subway assault occurred a few hours later, at 1:20 a.m. on September 22nd, when a man sitting on a bench at the Ralph Avenue A/C subway station was beaten by a man with a pipe. Leon Sims, 25, was arrested for felony assault for the pipe beating; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131003191326/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2013/10/01/man-arrested-in-brooklyn-subway-pipe-attack/">according to WCBS 2</a>, &quot;Police spotted Sims while searching for the suspect in the Flatbush Avenue attack. When police approached him, they say Sims ran and was hit by a car.&quot;</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20131003191326/http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20131001/hells-kitchen-clinton/man-arrested-brick-attack-on-marathoner-police-say">Police sources tell DNAinfo</a> that Sims, who is still in the hospital, is being charged for the brick bashing, the pipe beating, and assaulting his grandmother on September 20th.</p>
