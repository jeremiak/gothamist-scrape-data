										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2016_11_smashgrab.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022259im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_11_smashgrab.jpg" width="640" height="466" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>Police released dramatic footage of an Upper East Side jewelry store heist that shows a suspect breaking into a display case with claw hammer.</p>

<p>The robbery was reported at the Jaeger-Lecoultre store, at 701 Madison Avenue, yesterday at 10:41 a.m. According to the NYPD, two suspects entered the location, with one &quot;display[ing] a firearm and order[ing] three employees to lay down on the floor&quot; and the other &quot;proceed[ing] to smash two display boxes with a claw hammer and removed five watches valued at more than $300,000.&quot;</p>

<p>Both suspects fled southbound on Madison Avenue and then east on East 62nd Street. No one was injured. </p>

<p>The video shows the suspects; the gunman is a 6&apos;4&quot; and 240 pound male &quot;with a light complexion&quot; who was wearing a black winter coat, black dress pants, baseball cap and sunglasses while the other is described as a 6&apos;2&quot; and 190 pound male, also with a &quot;light complexion,&quot; who was wearing a gray winter coat, multi-colored ski cap, black pants, black Nike boots, and black sunglasses and carrying a claw hammer.<br>
