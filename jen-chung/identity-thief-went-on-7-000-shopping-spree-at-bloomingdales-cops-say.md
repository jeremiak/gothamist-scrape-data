title: Identity Thief Went On $7,000 Shopping Spree At Bloomingdale's, Cops Say
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2017_02_grandlarcesu.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170307030500im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2017_02_grandlarcesu.jpg" width="640" height="446" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>Police are searching for a woman who apparently used a Queens man&apos;s identity to open up a credit card account to facilitate a shopping spree at Bloomingdale&apos;s.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the 46-year-old man, who lives near Dalian Court and Bay Park Drive, realized on January 30th, 2017 that &quot;his personal information [was used] to open a credit card account with American Express.&quot; American Express records then showed that the card was used on January 16th at Bloomingdale&apos;s on the East Side, where the suspect spent over $7,000.<br>
 <br>
Video from Bloomingdale&apos;s was released, and police describe the suspect as a female with a black purse, last seeing wearing a dark colored coat and a white shirt.</p>
