Police are looking for a man accused of groping three subway passengers along the 1 line, twice last month and once a few years ago.<p></p>

<p>Here are the incidents, which took place by Lincoln Center, Times Square and Columbia University, as the NYPD describes them: </p><blockquote>Incident #1 (20 pct.): It was reported to police that on Monday September 19, 2016, at approximately 1740 hours, inside of a moving &quot;1&quot; train at the 66 Street subway station, the individual sat next to the 24 year-old female victim and rubbed the outside of her thigh.<br>
 <br>
Incident #2 (Midtown North pct.): It was reported to police that on Wednesday September 9, 2016, at approximately 1845 hours, inside of a moving &quot;1&quot; train at the 42 Street subway station, the individual sat next to the 18 year-old female victim and rubbed the inside of her thigh.<br>
 <br>
Incident #3 (34 pct.): It was reported to police that sometime between September 29, 2011 to June 30, 2012, inside of a moving &quot;1&quot; train at the 110 Street subway station, the individual sat next to the 13 year-old female victim and rubbed the inside of her thigh.</blockquote><b>Update</b>: Well, this is awful&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013754/http://nypost.com/2016/10/10/groper-attacks-victims-twin-sister-years-later/">the NY Post reports</a> that after the victim of the Times Square incident told the police about the incident, &quot;The victim&#x2019;s 18-year-old twin sister told cops the same man touched her at some point between 2011 and 2012 when she was 13.&quot;<br>
 <br>
