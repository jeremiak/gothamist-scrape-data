										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2016_11_ecigexplo.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015523im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_11_ecigexplo.jpg" width="640" height="384" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>An employee at a Grand Central Terminal wine shop was injured when an e-cigarette exploded in his pants pocket yesterday. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015523/http://abc7ny.com/news/video-e-cig-explodes-inside-mans-pocket-in-grand-central-store/1622511/">His colleague told ABC</a>, &quot;Out of nowhere, a huge explosion came from one of my co-worker&apos;s pockets. It just shot at us.&quot;</p>

<p>Video shows the injured worker, Otis Gooding, standing behind the counter of Central Cellars around 9:50 a.m. Wednesday when the e-cigarette started to smoke:</p>

<p>The footage shows him trying to put out the fire. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015523/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Man-burns-hand-leg-ecigarette-explodes-pants-NYC-grand-central-station--402686205.html">Assistant manager John Lee said</a>, &quot;He went to pull the cigarette out of his pocket and burned his hand, his nail. There were some some projectiles during the explosion&#x2014;I ducked out and we all evacuated...I guess these e-cigarettes are a dangerous thing.&quot;</p>

<p>Gooding was the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015523/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/e-cigarette-catches-fire-man-pants-pocket-grand-central-article-1.2884904">only one injured</a>. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015523/http://abc7ny.com/news/video-e-cig-explodes-inside-mans-pocket-in-grand-central-store/1622511/">WABC 7 reports</a>, &quot;He was taken by private ambulance to a local hospital, where he&apos;s in stable condition. His attorney, Sanford Rubenstein, said he was admitted to the burn unit for treatment and will require surgery.&quot; </p>

<p>There was no disruption to train service at Grand Central.</p>

<p>Dr. Anne Wagner of the University of Colorado Hospital Burn Center <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015523/http://www.nbcnews.com/business/consumer/what-s-causing-some-e-cigarette-batteries-explode-n533516">described e-cigarette explosions </a>earlier this year, &quot;It&apos;s literally an explosion, a super-hot explosion... We&apos;re seeing deep third-degree burns and almost all of them require skin grafts and these grafts leave a significant scar.&quot;</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015523/http://www.nbcnews.com/business/consumer/what-s-causing-some-e-cigarette-batteries-explode-n533516">NBC News reports</a> that a 2014 FDA study found the &quot;shape and construction of e-cigarettes can make them more likely than other products with lithium-ion batteries to behave like &apos;flaming rockets&apos; when a battery fails.&quot;</p>
