										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013145/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_09_gpunch1.jpg"> <img alt="2014_09_gpunch1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013145im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2014/10/2014_09_gpunch1-thumb-200x341-861998.jpg" width="160" height="272" class="image-right"> </a> </span>The police are hoping the public can help them identify a man who allegedly punched a subway rider on the G at a Brooklyn station last month. </p>

<p>According to the NYPD, on Friday, September 5, 2014 at approximately 7:25 a.m., &quot;the suspect punched the 35-year-old male victim in the face as he exited a northbound &apos;G&apos; train at the Hoyt Street and Schermerhorn Street subway station. The suspect then fled the subway station to parts unknown. The victim did not go to the hospital.&quot;</p>

<p>Police released a photograph of the suspect, who is described as being about 5&apos;11&quot; and 185 pounds and who was wearing a dark polo shirt, dark blue jeans, and eyeglasses while carrying a messenger bag.</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this assault is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510013145/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
