 <br>
Police are looking for two suspects who were caught on video violently attacking and then robbing a man at a Manhattan subway station on Sunday morning. <p></p>

<p>The incident occurred around 5 a.m. at the Columbus Circle stop, on the platform for the southbound A/C and B/D trains. According to the NYPD, &quot;the two suspects approached the 25 year-old male victim and knocked him to the ground. Both of the suspects punched and kicked the victim while he was on the floor; one of the suspects wore brass knuckles during the assault. They then removed his property and $350 in cash.&quot;</p>

<p>The two suspects fled on a southbound A train, and the victim was taken to a hospital for bruising and lacerations to his face. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021433/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2016/09/26/columbus-circle-subway-attack-suspects/">1010 WINS&apos; John Montone visited the station</a>, and spoke to a Metro newspaper worker, &quot;I work Monday through Friday, I work the metro papers here, and so far I&#x2019;ve been seeing a lot of crazy people here but they haven&#x2019;t messed with me so far.&quot; Another commuter said, &quot;Things are getting crazy. People don&#x2019;t have any respect for anybody else and it&#x2019;s really the worst part about it all.&#x201D;</p>

<p>The authorities released video of the suspects, describing the first male, who is seen wearing a gray hooded sweater and light colored pants, as being in his 30s, 5&apos;8&quot; to 5&apos;10&quot;; and the second male as being in his 30s, 5&apos;8&quot; to 5&apos;10&quot; and last seen wearing a gray t-shirt, black long sleeved undershirt and black pants. </p>
