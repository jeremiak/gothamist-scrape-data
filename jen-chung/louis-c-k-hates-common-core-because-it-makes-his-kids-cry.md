</div></div>					<p>NY State kids <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160129150333/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/07/nyc_test_scores_highlight_extreme_r.php">did terribly on the Common Core tests</a>, the new national standardized tests, and parents, teachers and, obviously,<em> the children</em>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160129150333/http://www.nytimes.com/2013/11/18/nyregion/at-forums-state-education-commissioner-faces-a-barrage-of-complaints.html">are very upset</a>, with many blasting Governor Cuomo and his state education officials for how they handled rollout. By far the most vocal critic yesterday was Louis C.K., who vented through a series of tweets <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160129150333/https://twitter.com/louisck/status/460765469746929664">that began</a>, &quot;My kids used to love math.  Now it makes them cry.  Thanks standardized testing and common core!&quot;</p>

<p>Louis C.K., Upper West Side resident, has two <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160129150333/http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-2418286/Comedian-Louis-C-K-shares-giggle-adorable-real-life-daughter-Mary.html">elementary-school aged kids</a>. While the tests are only administered for third- to eighth-graders, many school curriculums have been changing to accommodate kids to be ready for Common Core&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160129150333/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/27/public_school_kindergarteners_cryin.php">algebraic thinking for kindergarteners</a>, essays <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160129150333/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/12/wtf_albany_high_essay_asks_students.php">asking if Jews are evil</a>, canceling <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160129150333/http://gothamist.com/2014/04/28/long_island_school_cancels_kinderga.php">five-year-olds&apos; end-of-year recitals</a>&#x2014;which will, ideally, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160129150333/http://www.nytimes.com/2010/03/11/education/11educ.html">help raise education levels nationally</a> (versus various states picking and choosing). Still, the comedian fumed:</p>
