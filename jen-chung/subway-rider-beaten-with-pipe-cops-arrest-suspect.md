title: Subway Rider Beaten With Pipe, Cops Arrest Suspect
---


                            
                            
                            
                            <p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2013_09_pipeattacker.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20131128051423im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2013_09_pipeattacker.jpg" width="315" height="252" class="image-right"> </span>The police have arrested a man who allegedly beat a man at a Brooklyn subway station with a pipe last week.</p>

<p>Leon Sims, 25, was charged with two counts of felony assault for the September 22 incident. According to the NYPD, at 1:20 a.m., a 35-year-old man was sitting on a bench inside the Ralph Avenue subway station when Sims struck the victim on the head repeatedly with a pipe. </p>

<p>The victim fell into the subway tracks and Sims allegedly fled along the trackbed.</p>

<p>The victim was removed from the tracks and taken to Interfaith Medical Center, where he was treated for cuts to his head and a chipped tooth.</p>
