					<p>If you&apos;re going to be rushing through Grand Central Terminal at some point this holiday season, we recommend you carve Take some time out to slow down and visit the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013703/http://www.nytransitmuseum.org/">NYC Transit Museum</a>&apos;s annex at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013703/http://www.grandcentralterminal.com/store/new-york-transit-museum-gallery-annex-and-store/2137026154/2138781576">Grand Central</a>, because their 15th Annual Holiday Train Show is up and running!</p>

<p>Lionel trains, including the Grand Central Express, Metro-North, Pennsylvania Flyer, Polar Express, and vintage subway train sets run along a 34&apos; long, two-level O-gauge model train layout in the center of the space. The trains depart, of course, from a model of Grand Central Terminal on their way to the North Pole, all against a backdrop by artist Julia Rothman (it was designed for the Museum&apos;s 40th anniversary).</p>

<p>The show is free and is open Monday-Friday, 8 a.m.-8 p.m., and Saturday and Sunday, 10 a.m.-6 p.m. and runs through February 26, 2017. The Transit Museum&apos;s Grand Central Gallery Annex &amp; Store&#x2014;yes, you can also knock off some holiday shopping at the same time&#x2014;is located off the Main Concourse in the Shuttle Passage.</p>
