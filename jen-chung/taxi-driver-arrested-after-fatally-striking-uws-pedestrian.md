title: Taxi Driver Arrested After Fatally Striking UWS Pedestrian
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2015_11_taxiu1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160322025104im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_11_taxiu1.jpg" width="640" height="425"> <br> <i> (David Torres)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>An 88-year-old woman was killed earlier today when a taxi cab slammed into her in Manhattan. Police have arrested and charged the taxi driver in the fatal incident. </p>

<p>According to the NYPD, officers responded to a 911 call around 12:40 a.m. about a pedestrian who was struck by a vehicle at West 109th Street and Columbus Avenue. When they arrived, they found the victim &quot;unconscious and unresponsive with trauma laying on the pavement.&quot; She was taken to St. Luke&apos;s Hospital, where she was pronounced dead.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2015_11_taxiu2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160322025104im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_11_taxiu2.jpg" width="640" height="425"> <br> <i> (David Torres)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The police say their investigation found that the woman, Luisa Rosario, who lived on West 110th Street, &quot;was attempting to cross West 109th St. from North to South, in the marked crosswalk with a green signal, and was struck by Vehicle #1, a 2014 Toyota, who was turning right onto West 109 St from Columbus Avenue, also with the green signal.&quot; </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2015_11_taxiu3.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160322025104im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_11_taxiu3.jpg" width="640" height="425"> <br> <i> David Torres</i></div> <p></p>

<p>The driver, Salifu Abubkar, 73, was charged with failure to yield to a pedestrian. On Friday, during a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160322025104/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/06/de_blasio_vision_zero.php">press conference about Vision Zero</a>, Mayor Bill de Blasio noted that failure to yield tickets were at 30,000 for the year so far, three times higher than last year.</p>
