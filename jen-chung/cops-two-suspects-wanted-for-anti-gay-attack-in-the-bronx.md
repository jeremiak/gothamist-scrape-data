										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2014_08_bxassault.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509194059im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_08_bxassault.jpg" width="640" height="240" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>The police are hoping for the public&apos;s help in identifying two people who allegedly attacked a woman and made anti-gay remarks to her.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incident occurred on July 23 at 2:30 a.m., when the two suspects followed a woman into 395 East 149th Street. Inside, the police say the pair &quot;made anti -gay comments and punched her in the face and body causing lacerations to her lip and ear.&quot;</p>

<p>Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS.  The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509194059/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
