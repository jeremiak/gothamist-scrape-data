					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170403235306/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/31/march_out_like_a_wet_lamb.php">The rain</a> seems like it&apos;s stopped, which is great because you can check out the first of the blooming cherry blossom trees at the Brooklyn Botanic Garden.</p>

<p>The flowers <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170403235306/https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20170329/prospect-heights/cherry-blossoms-blooming-brooklyn-botanic-garden-spring">first started to appear on Wednesday</a>, and the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170403235306/https://www.bbg.org/collections/cherries">latest map</a> shows seven trees in &quot;First Bloom&quot; and one tree, a <em>Prunus sargentii </em>, in &quot;Peak Bloom&quot;!</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2017_03_cherryblossomflower.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170403235306im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2017_03_cherryblossomflower.jpg" width="640" height="437"> <br> <i> Prunus &#x2018;Accolade&#x2019; beginning to bloom in BBG&apos;s Cherry Cultivars Area. (Blanca Begert / Brooklyn Botanic Garden)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>BBG experts think the cherry blossoms <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170403235306/https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20170320/prospect-heights/cherry-blossoms-spring-brooklyn-botanic-garden">will be fine</a> after our winter and have previously said, &quot;A good stretch of warm weather will trigger the cherries and other spring-blooming plants to flower.&quot;</p><blockquote>&#x201C;When it&#x2019;s above 60 degrees or so for several days in a row, you&#x2019;ll see the buds of the early-blooming cherries like Prunus &#x2018;Okame&#x2019; start to swell. Around this same time, you&#x2019;ll also see the bud scales from the magnolias start to drop,&#x201D; says Brian Funk, curator of the Japanese Hill-and-Pond Garden.</blockquote>The dozens of cherry trees are getting ready for the big cherry blossom festival, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170403235306/https://www.bbg.org/visit/event/sakura_matsuri_2017">Sakura Matsuri</a> on April 29 and 30. Clearly the only way to celebrate is for some enterprising pizzeria to make this <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170403235306/http://www.eater.com/2017/3/28/15087390/cherry-blossom-cotton-candy-pizza">crazy cherry blossom pie</a>.<p></p>
