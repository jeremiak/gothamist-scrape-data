title: Man Keeps Cutting Sleeping L Train Riders' Pockets To Steal Their Wallets & Cellphones

										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2017_04_ltrain1.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022312im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2017_04_ltrain1.jpg" width="640" height="479"> <br> <i> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016022312/https://www.flickr.com/photos/stephen_rees/5126019106">Stephen Rees / Flickr</a>)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Police believe the same man has robbed six sleeping subway riders and tried to rob another on the L train in February and March. His M.O.: Using an &quot;unknown sharp object&quot; to cut the victims&apos; pockets.</p>

<p>All the incidents occurred between midnight and 5 a.m., on the L line in Brooklyn. Here are the NYPD&apos;s descriptions of the grand larcenies:</p><blockquote>Incident 1:  (83 Pct) It was reported to the police that on Saturday, February 18, 2017 at 0205 hours, while aboard the southbound &quot;L&quot;, the individual used an unknown sharp object to cut the rear pants pocket of the 22-year old male victim while he was asleep and removed the victim&apos;s cellphone and bank card. The victim made the discovery when the train entered the Bushwick Avenue and Aberdeen Street subway station.<br>
 <br>
Incident 2: (69 Pct) It was reported to the police that on  Tuesday, February 28, 2017 at 0045 hours, while aboard the southbound &quot;L&quot;, the individual used an unknown sharp object to cut the rear pants pocket of the 23-year old male victim while he was asleep and removed the victim&apos;s wallet. The victim made the discovery when the train entered the Glenwood Road and Rockaway Parkway subway station.<br>
 <br>
Incident 3: (83 Pct) It was reported to the police that on Saturday, March 11, 2017 at 0230 hours, while aboard a southbound &quot;L&quot;train the individual used an unknown sharp object to cut the front and rear pants pocket of the 21-year old male victim while he was asleep and removed the victim&apos;s wallet and cellphone. The victim made the discovery when the train entered the Wyckoff Avenue and Dekalb Avenue subway station.<br>
 <br>
Incident 4: (73 Pct) It was reported to the police that on Tuesday, March 28, 2017 at 0415 hours while aboard the southbound &quot;L,&quot; the individual used an unknown sharp object to cut the front pocket of the 28-year old male victim and attempted to remove the victim&apos;s property. The individual was unsuccessful and the victim made the discovery when the train entered the Havens Place and Atlantic Avenue subway station.<br>
 <br>
Incident 5: (73 Pct) It was reported to the police that on April 2, 2017 at 0300 hours while aboard the southbound &quot;L&quot; train between the Broadway Subway Station and the Sutter Avenue subway station , the individual used an unknown sharp object to cut the rear pants pocket of the 27-year male victim and removed his wallet. The individual exits the train as it entered the Sutter Avenue subway and flees to parts unknown.<br>
 <br>
Incident 6: (69 Pct ) It was reported to the police that on Monday , April 2, 2017 at 0500 hours while aboard the southbound &quot;L,&quot; the individual used an unknown sharp object to cut the front pocket of the 39-year old male and removed his wallet. The victim made the discovery when the train entered the Glenwood Road and Rockaway Parkway subway station.<br>
 <br>
Incident 7: (69 Pct) It was reported to the police that on Monday , April 2, 2017 at 0500 hours while aboard the southbound &quot;L,&quot; the individual used an unknown sharp object to cut the front pocket of the 24-year old male and removed his wallet. The victim made the discovery when the train entered the Farragut Road and East 105 Street.</blockquote>Cops say the man is approximately 30-40 years old, 5-8&quot;-6&quot; and 180-190 pounds. He was last seen wearing a light blue button up shirt, black NY hat, blue jeans and black sneakers. Surveillance footage was also released:<p></p>
