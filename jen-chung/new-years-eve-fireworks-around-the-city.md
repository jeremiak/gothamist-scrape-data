<p>If you&apos;re not going to Times Square for New Year&apos;s Eve (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103113706/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/30/what_to_expecting_when_youre_expect.php">if you are, heed this</a>) there are other places to see fireworks and get a bit of good cheer:</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong>Central Park</strong>: There are fireworks for the New York Road Runners&apos; Club <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103113706/http://www.nyrr.org/races/2011/r1231x00.asp">Emerald Nuts Midnight Run</a>, near the Hecksher Ballfields.  </li>
	<li><strong>New York Harbor</strong>: There are fireworks near Liberty Island, which should be seen from Brooklyn, lower Manhattan and NJ. And the water, if you happen to be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103113706/http://spiritofnewyork.com/New-York/new-years-eve-cruises">on a dinner cruise</a>.</li>
	<li><strong>Prospect Park</strong>: The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103113706/http://www.prospectpark.org/calendar/event/1471647">Prospect Park Alliance says</a>, &quot;Start the celebrations off around 11 p.m. with entertainment and hot refreshments at Grand Army Plaza.  We can&#x2019;t help you keep your resolutions, but we can get you off to a dazzling start!  Best locations for viewing the fireworks include: anywhere in Grand Army Plaza, inside the Park on the West Drive, and along Prospect Park West between grand Army Plaza and 9th Street.&quot;</li>
</ul>
