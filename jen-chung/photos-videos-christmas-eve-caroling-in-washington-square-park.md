</div></div>					<p>Last night, people gathered in Washington Square Park to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160301161415/http://www.washingtonsquarenyc.org/events/2013/12/24/christmas-eve-caroling-under-the-arch">sing Christmas carols</a> by the Arch. </p>

<p>The history of carols dates <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160301161415/http://www.whychristmas.com/customs/carols_history.shtml">back to the pagan singing</a> during solstices, but the Greenwich Village tradition is about 100+ years old. It was probably one of the warmest, most <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160301161415/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/24/im_dreaming_of_a_warm_christmas.php">balmy Christmas Eves</a> in recent memory, as some carolers were just wearing t-shirts. </p>

<p>Merry Christmas, to those of you who celebrate!</p>
