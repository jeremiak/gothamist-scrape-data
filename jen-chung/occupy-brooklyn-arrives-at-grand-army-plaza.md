</div></div><p>Today&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018075312/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/12/occupy_wall_street_is_moving_to_bro.php">Occupy Brooklyn protest</a> is <em>happening right now</em>.  Folks are at Grand Army Plaza, and the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018075312/http://www.occupybrooklyn.org/posts/occupy-brooklyns-1st-rally-this-sat-oct-15th/">group explains</a>, &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018075312/http://gothamist.com/tags/occupywallstreet">Occupy Wall Street</a> has grown too big for Manhattan, so we&#x2019;re rising up in Brooklyn. Come stand with the 99% and make your voice heard this Saturday &#x2014; and fight for economic justice everywhere. Let&#x2019;s Occupy Brooklyn, too! Enough is enough! Note: This is a rally, not an occupation. Not yet anyway.&quot;  </p>

<p>The rally is scheduled to go until 2 p.m. One person <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018075312/http://twitter.com/#!/sarahkbklyn/status/125225002483191808">Tweeted</a>, &quot;Headed to #occupybrooklyn aka the greenmarket. Who says the 99% can&apos;t enjoy some locally-grown produce?&quot;</p>
