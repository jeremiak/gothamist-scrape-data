										<p>Last week, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013512/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/04/new_yorker_cover_fear.php">The New Yorker&apos;s cover</a> tapped into our collective anxieties about the looming election, featuring a man on the subway reading a post-election newspaper with headlines like, &quot;OH, SWEET JESUS PLEASE GOD, NO&quot; and &quot;ANYTHING BUT THAT.&quot; Now <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013512/http://dcist.com/2016/11/election_2016_results.php">that nightmare is somehow our reality</a>, and the magazine&apos;s cover next week, illustrated by Bob Staake, shows how deeply troubling this new reality is:</p>

<center><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">An early look at next week&apos;s cover, &#x201C;The Wall,&#x201D; by Bob Staake: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013512/https://t.co/f9qJvRniou">https://t.co/f9qJvRniou</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013512/https://t.co/u6RQRfQsI1">pic.twitter.com/u6RQRfQsI1</a></p>&#x2014; The New Yorker (@NewYorker) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013512/https://twitter.com/NewYorker/status/797065122585919489">November 11, 2016</a></blockquote> <script async src="//web.archive.org/web/20171016013512js_/http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></center>

<p>The New Yorker&apos;s art editor Fran&#xE7;oise Mouly writes:</p><blockquote>When we first received the results of the election, we felt as though we had hit a brick wall, full force. <p></p>

<p>&#x201C;The election of Donald Trump to the Presidency is nothing less than a tragedy for the American republic, a tragedy for the Constitution, and a triumph for the forces, at home and abroad, of nativism, authoritarianism, misogyny, and racism,&#x201D; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013512/http://www.newyorker.com/news/news-desk/an-american-tragedy-donald-trump">David Remnick wrote in a piece</a> posted shortly after the announcement. </p>

<p>&#x201C;The pathos&#x2014;the disappointment at waking up on Wednesday morning and not finding a woman President-elect&#x2014;is undeniable, and heartbreaking,&#x201D; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016013512/http://www.newyorker.com/news/amy-davidson/trumps-stunning-win">added Amy Davidson</a></p>

<p>&#x201C;To combat authoritarianism, to call out lies, to struggle honorably and fiercely in the name of American ideals&#x2014;that is what is left to do. That is all there is to do,&#x201D; Remnick concluded. And so we must go on&#x2014;with words and images such as this cover by Bob Staake for next week&#x2019;s issue.</p></blockquote>The New Yorker&apos;s cartoonists have been contributing some on their Instagram:<p></p>
