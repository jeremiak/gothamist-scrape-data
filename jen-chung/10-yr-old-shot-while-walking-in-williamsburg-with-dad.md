<p>Just before 11 p.m. last night, a 10-year-old boy was hit by a stray bullet in Williamsburg.  The child and his father were apparently <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120405050247/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2012/04/03/police-looking-for-suspects-after-10-year-old-hit-by-stray-bullet-in-brooklyn/">walking down from the Hewes Street J/M station&apos;s platform</a> when a gun fight broke out.</p>

<p>A nearby resident told WCBS 2, &quot;I was in my room studying, I heard two shots right after each other in succession. I was curious just to open my window to let the noises come in... Then I heard a woman screaming and I heard a couple of guys arguing back and forth for another minute and then sirens showed up about five minutes later.&quot;  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120405050247/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/brooklyn/boy_shot_in_burg_CTLxVJnFglCEcoOKE2qqJI">According to the Post</a>, two groups of teens had been fighting; another person said, &quot;Ten or 15 people [were] fighting. I called 911 when I heard the gunshots, two or three shots.&quot; </p>

<p>The boy&apos;s wound&#x2014;the bullet went in and out of his body&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120405050247/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/boy-struck-stray-bullet-walking-father-brooklyn-cops-article-1.1054943?localLinksEnabled=false">were not deemed life-threatening</a>.  No arrests have been made yet.  </p>
