</div></div>					<p>Pro wrestler John Cena has been crossing over to bigger Hollywood roles, like an against-the-type turn as Amy Schumer&apos;s buff yet sensitive boyfriend in <em>Trainwreck</em> and as a drug dealer in <em>Sisters</em>, so it was just a matter of time before he would host <em>Saturday Night Live</em>. The show played off his physique (those guns!) more than a few times, but Cena showed that in the ring or on a soundstage, he loves playing off a crowd. </p>

<p>The monologue featured various SNL cast members trying out different wrestling characters to take on Cena&#x2014;Bobby Moynihan was &quot;The Waddler&quot; and Leslie Jones was...&quot;Leslie Jones&quot;&#x2014;while he also played athletes in a <em>Karate Kid</em> send-up, a University of Alabama science presentation and a game show, &quot;Where&apos;d Your Money Go?&quot; (he was a bro-tastic Rob Gronkowski). </p>

<p>While the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171021214011/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/11/snl_walter_white_trump_dea.php">cold open was Trump-less</a>, Cena played Donald Trump in a <em>Being John Malkovich</em>-ish digital short later in the program that showed the world &quot;Through Donald Trump&apos;s Eyes.&quot; </p>
