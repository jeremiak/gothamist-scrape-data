<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025113318/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_05_gtrainsa.jpg"> <img alt="2016_05_gtrainsa.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025113318im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2016/05/2016_05_gtrainsa-thumb-350x646-948959.jpg" width="200" height="369" class="image-right"> </a> </span>Police are hoping the public can help them identify a man accused of stalking a subway rider.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, they received a report of an incident that occurred on April 15 at around 8 a.m: &quot;The victim, a 16-year-old female boarded the north bound &apos;G&apos; train at 4th Avenue and 9th Street subway station, when a male individual was following her towards the Fulton Street subway station. The female exited the train at Fulton Street subway station and the male individual remained on board the &apos;G&apos; train.&quot;</p>

<p>The police say that the victim &quot;has been followed by this male individual on four previous separate occasions at multiple subway stations within the month of February, April and May of 2016.&quot; The victim did not report any injuries.</p>

<p>The authorities released an image of the suspect that the victim took with her cellphone. The suspect is described as being in his late 40&apos;s, 5&apos;3&quot; and 170 pounds, with black hair. He was last seen wearing blue jeans, a blue overcoat and wearing glasses.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025113318/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
