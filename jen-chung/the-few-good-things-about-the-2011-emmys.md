<p>We <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428150044/http://gothamist.com/2011/09/18/reminder_emmys_are_on_tonight_and_c.php">knew the Emmys would be terrible</a> (even <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428150044/http://www.patspapers.com/story_stack/item/emmy_review_ho-hum_at_best/">Pat Kiernan says</a>, &quot;They weren&#x2019;t funny&#x2014;Jane Lynch wasn&#x2019;t funny. And the voiceover guy was really not funny.&quot;) yet we still watched.  Obviously, we suffering from some sort of Academy of Television Arts &amp; Sciences Stockholm Syndrome, but at least there were these moments:</p>

<p>Some problems with the Emmys: <em>Modern Family </em>is hilarious, but we get that the Academy likes it&#x2014;how about sharing the love to <em>Parks &amp; Recreation</em>.  Also, <em>Mad Men</em> again as Best Drama?  There were some great episodes, but overall it was a pretty uneven season.  The Emmys need some sort of Larroquette Rule&#x2014;John Larroquette won four Best Supporting Actor in a Comedy Emmys in a row for  <em>Night Court</em>. Sure, he will forever be horndog ADA Dan Fielding in many of our collective memories, but FOUR EMMYS IN A ROW?  Candice Bergen withdrew herself from the Best Actress Emmys-running after winning five awards for <em>Murphy Brown</em>.  And let&apos;s not get into the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428150044/http://insidetv.ew.com/2011/09/19/alec-baldwin-emmys-murdoch-bennett/">whole Alec Baldwin thing</a>.</p>
