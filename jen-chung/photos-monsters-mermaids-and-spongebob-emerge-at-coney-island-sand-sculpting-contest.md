					<p>Nothing says summer like a good, old-fashioned sand sculpture contest! Yesterday, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021114537/http://www.wearebcs.org/bcs/news_events/sand-sculpting2016/">the 26th Annual Coney Island Sand Sculpting Contest</a> was held, bringing out a mix of pro sand specialists as well as novices and families to show their grit.</p>

<p>Judges, who included NYC Parks Commissioner Mitchell J. Silver, Assemblymember Pamela Harris, Pamela Harris, and Council Member Mark Treyger, surveyed the works and selected three winners. An elaborate &quot;Creature From the Black Lagoon&quot; sculpture by Larry Watkins and Tom Oldewurtel <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021114537/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/brooklyn/artists-put-sand-castles-shame-coney-island-contest-article-1.2759359">won first place</a> for adult group sculptures, while Jose Aguirre&apos;s &quot;Two Mermaids&quot; won for solo adult sculpture. The family winner was the Andrades for the timely &quot;Pokemon Cellphone.&quot; </p>

<p>One of the sculptures was an interactive &quot;Winter Wonderland&quot; sculpture and children pretended it was snowy. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021114537/http://www.ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/news/2016/08/20/visionaries-let-their-imaginations-take-form-at-coney-island-sand-sculpting-contest.html">One participant summed up the day to NY1</a>, &quot;It definitely is an opportunity for everyone to get together and have a great time, you know. And you always hear bad news in the news, and this is something that actually gets the community together and do something good.&quot;</p>
