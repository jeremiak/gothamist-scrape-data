</div></div>					<p>While he&apos;s pretty much been a regular <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917053308/http://gothamist.com/tags/alecbaldwin">playing President Trump since last fall</a>, Alec Baldwin returned to <em>Saturday Night Live</em> this week for his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917053308/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Saturday_Night_Live_guests#Five-Timers_Club">17th time as host</a>. For his monologue, they sent Pete Davidson out to comment on how much Baldwin has changed from his first stint as host in 1990.</p>

<br><br>&quot;Wow, that&apos;s unbelievable, dude,&quot; Davidson exclaimed. &quot;I can&apos;t believe that&apos;s you! You were so handsome! Did everybody look like that then? Like a young NBA coach?&quot; Davidson continued that Baldwin should have done movies and later confirmed that he was totally high.

<p>The show kicked off with a cold open featuring Melissa McCarthy <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917053308/http://gothamist.com/2017/02/12/this_is_a_new_spicey_watch_melissa.php">reprising her take-no-prisoners portrayal</a> of White House press secretary Sean Spicer. <p>The Trump-free sketches of the evening included a surprise appearance from Tracy Morgan as one of Beyonce&apos;s babies in utero (Kenan Thompson was the other twin); an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917053308/http://gothamist.com/2017/02/07/brooklyn_coffee_luxury.php">$18 coffee cup shout-out</a>; a commercial for Russell Stover&apos;s &quot;Black History Month chocolates&quot; (Bill Clinton&apos;s in there!); Baldwin as a proud father of a cadet in basic training; the cutthroat tactics of getting a Super Bowl ad concept by the agency; and a gross-out high school gym class sit-up record attempt, plus Ed Sheeran as musical guest.</p>
