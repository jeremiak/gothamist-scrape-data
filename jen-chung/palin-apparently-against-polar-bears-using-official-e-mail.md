<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117120140/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/14/us/politics/14palin.html?hp">NY Times article about the Republican vice presidential candidate&apos;s governing style</a> begins, &quot;Gov. Sarah Palin lives by the maxim that all politics is local, not to mention personal.&quot;  It details how she hired many friends for state positions, turns against friends or supporters if they criticize her, and created an atmosphere of intense secrecy as governor.  </p>

<p>Here are two excerpts from the lengthy Times article.  First, a little something about Palin and polar bears:</p><blockquote>Rick Steiner, a University of Alaska professor, sought the e-mail messages of state scientists who had examined the effect of global warming on polar bears. (Ms. Palin said the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111117120140/http://www.nytimes.com/2008/01/05/opinion/05palin.html">scientists had found no ill effects</a>, and she has sued the federal government to block the listing of the bears as endangered.) An administration official told Mr. Steiner that his request would cost $468,784 to process.<p></p>

<p>When Mr. Steiner finally obtained the e-mail messages &#x2014; through a federal records request &#x2014; he discovered that state scientists had in fact agreed that the bears were in danger, records show.</p>
