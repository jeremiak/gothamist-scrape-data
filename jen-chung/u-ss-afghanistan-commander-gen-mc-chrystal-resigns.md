										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407144509/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2010_06_obmc.jpg"> <img alt="2010_06_obmc.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407144509im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2010/06/2010_06_obmc-thumb-250x211-521056.jpg" width="120" height="101" class="image-right"> </a> </span>After an incredibly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407144509/http://www.rollingstone.com/politics/news/17390/119236">candid interview with Rolling Stone</a>, General McChrystal, who headed the U.S.&apos;s war efforts in Afghanistan, offered his resignation today (as <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407144509/http://www.newsweek.com/2010/06/22/why-military-code-demands-mcchrystal-s-resignation.html">predicted</a>) and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407144509/http://abcnews.go.com/Politics/obama-accepts-stanley-mcchrystal-resignation-rolling-stone-article/story?id=10992188">President Obama accepted it</a>.  McChrystal was critical of the administration&apos;s actions in the interview (Rolling Stone&apos;s journalist spent a lot of time with the general, partly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407144509/http://www.nytimes.com/2010/06/23/world/23rollingstone.html?ref=media">thanks to the ash-spewing Icelandic volcano</a>) and he admitted to White House officials that he &quot;compromised the mission.&quot; Now General David Petraeus, the head of Central Command, will take over.  Last December, Obama said that he wanted to start <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150407144509/http://gothamist.com/2009/12/02/obamas_afghanistan_plans_30000_more.php">withdrawing by July 2011</a>.</p>
