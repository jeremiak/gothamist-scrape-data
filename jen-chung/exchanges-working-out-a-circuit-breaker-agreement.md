title: Exchanges Working Out A Circuit Breaker Agreement
---

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-left" style=" width:250px; "> <img alt="2010_05_circbreaker.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119093432im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2010_05_circbreaker.jpg" width="250" height="189"> <br> <i>Trader at the NYSE (AP)</i></div> After last Thursday&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119093432/http://gothamist.com/2010/05/06/dow_falls_900_points_amid_europe_wo.php">stock market plunge</a>, six major stock exchanges will, in the words of the SEC, agree upon &quot;a structural framework, to be refined over the next day, for strengthening circuit breakers.&quot;  And the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119093432/http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748704879704575236752132638846.html?mod=WSJ_hps_LEFTWhatsNews">Wall Street Journal reports</a> that they will &quot;introduce temporary trading limits on individual stock moves.&quot;  <p></p>

<p>The CEOs of NYSE Euronext, Nasdaq OMX Group Inc., Bats Global Markets Inc., Direct Edge Holdings LLC, International Securities Exchange Holdings Inc. and CBOE Holdings Inc. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119093432/http://www.nytimes.com/2010/05/11/business/11agencies.html?ref=business">met with the SEC yesterday</a> and apparently &quot;Nearly all of the exchange officials agreed that the differing rules among the various stock markets about temporarily halting or slowing trading in individual stocks worsened the decline on Thursday.&quot; Columbia University law professor John Coffee explained, &quot;You have to agree in advance on the point at which a short-term circuit breaker would be put in. We may want the circuit breaker to kick in on a 5 percent decline.&quot;</p>

<p>As the investigation into what caused the nearly 1,000-point decline continues, it appears that a trade in Chicago could have pushed things to the brink.  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120119093432/http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748704879704575236771699461084.html?mod=WSJ_hps_LEFTWhatsNews">According to the WSJ</a>, &quot;On any other day, this $7.5 million trade for 50,000 options contracts might have briefly hurt stock prices, though not caused much of a ripple. But coming on a day when all varieties of financial markets were deeply unsettled, the trade may have played a key role in the stock-market collapse just 20 minutes later.&quot; </p>
