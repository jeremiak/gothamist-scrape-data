<p>Earlier this morning, the NYPD was investigating a &quot;suspicious device&quot; left in the Flatiron District. Now, the police have released video of the person who left it there, in hopes of getting more information from the public.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, at around 3:06 a.m., a man approached a telephone booth at the corner of Fifth Avenue and West 25th Street and &quot;placed inside a device consisting of a black pipe attached to two pieces of wood with white cord. A flashing red LED light was attached to the device.&quot; He then left, walking south on Broadway.<br>

<p>During the investigation this morning, the police held office workers from entering some of the surrounding buildings, and according to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151222154348/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/nypd-investigating-bomb-found-jay-z-40-40-club-article-1.1981611">the Daily News</a>, &quot;broke up the device with a controlled blast of air.&quot;</p>
