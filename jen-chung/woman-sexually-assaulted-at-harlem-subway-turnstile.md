<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2012_02_135susp.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20120311013842im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2012_02_135susp.jpg" width="300" height="225" class="image-left"> </span>The NYPD released images of a man who assaulted a woman entering the a Harlem subway station last week.  The police say that on March 8, around 5:20 a.m., a 25-year-old woman entered the West 135th Street station (which has the B and C0 at West 137th and St. Nicholas Avenue.  &quot;As she went through the turnstile the suspect ran up to the victim, grabbed her and sexually assaulted her.  The suspect then fled the location on foot.  The victim later went to an area hospital where she was treated for her injuries.&quot;<br>
 <br>
The NYPD describe the suspect as &quot;a light skinned male black, approximately 20 to 25 years old.  He is approximately 5&apos;9, with a thin build. He was last seen wearing a red hooded sweatshirt with white lettering in the front.&quot;  Anyone with information is encouraged to contact Crime Stoppers, either by calling 1-800-577-TIPS (8477) or going to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120311013842/http://www.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.com/">www.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.com</a>.</p>
