<p>Ah, it&apos;s that time of the year when kids are back in school, days become darker earlier, and traffic is a complete mess on the East Side of Manhattan&#x2014;yes, it&apos;s United Nations General Assembly time.  With leaders from around the world in town to talk about global goals (or just <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150505163248/http://gothamist.com/2010/09/19/hes_back_ahmadinejad_arrives_in_new.php">arouse controversy</a>), there&apos;s a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150505163248/http://www.ny1.com/content/top_stories/125703/united-nations-general-assembly-brings-traffic-headaches">heightened police presence</a> and many street closures (see below).  Also, if you&apos;re anywhere near Dag Hammarskjold Plaza&#x2014;or a hotel where a world leader is staying...or most <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150505163248/http://newyork.cbslocal.com/2010/09/19/group-protests-iranian-presidents-visit-to-nyc/">anywhere else</a>&#x2014;expect to see many protests.</p>

<p>President Obama will be in town on Wednesday and Thursday&#x2014;he&apos;s the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150505163248/http://gothamist.com/2010/09/14/biden_visit_accidents_mean_new_nypd.php">only world leader who gets a NYPD motorcade</a> these days, after VP Biden ruined everything for everyone else.</p>
