</div></div>					<p>This morning, hundreds of people vied for coveted applications for the apprenticeship program at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405032554/http://nycdistrictcouncil.com/for-immediate-release-carpenters-recruit-apprentices.aspx">United Brotherhood of Carpenters</a>. Only 750 applications were being handed out this morning, prompting many to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405032554/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/18/hundreds_line_up_overnight_for_unio.php">sleep outside the Hudson Street building for days</a>. </p>

<p>Applications were handed out starting at 9 a.m. and by 11 a.m., all 750 were in hopefuls&apos; hands. What many are hoping for is a chance to learn a trade (like &quot;linoleum, resilient, tile and carpet layer,&quot; &quot;pile driver/dockbuilder&quot; or &quot;carpenter&quot;) and earn $50/hour, even $99/hour. Union spokesman <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150405032554/http://blogs.wsj.com/metropolis/2013/08/19/line-for-carpenters-union-jobs-spans-two-manhattan-blocks/?mod=WSJBlog">Kwame Patterson told the Wall Street Journal</a>, &quot;these are the types of jobs that are shrinking as the non-union construction sector continues to grow and undercut workers.&quot;</p>

<p>The union says, &quot;Applicants will be notified by mail if their apprenticeship application lottery card is selected. These individuals will be required to attend a two-hour orientation where they will be provided with an apprenticeship application form and be scheduled for an interview.&quot;</p>
