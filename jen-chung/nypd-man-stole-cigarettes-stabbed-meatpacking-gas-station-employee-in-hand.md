title: NYPD: Man Stole Cigarettes, Stabbed Meatpacking Gas Station Employee In Hand
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408151207/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2014_03_gasstation.jpg"> <img alt="2014_03_gasstation.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408151207im_/http://gothamist.com/assets_c/2014/04/2014_03_gasstation-thumb-350x335-838155.jpg" width="250" height="239" class="image-right"> </a> </span>The police are hoping the public can help them find a man who allegedly stole cigarettes from a downtown gas station, and then attacked the employee who chased him.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, at 1:50 a.m. on Monday, April 7, a man entered the Mobil Gas Station at 63 8th Avenue, and removed a pack of cigarettes before fleeing. An 18-year-old male store employee chased the suspect before the suspect turned around and stabbed the employee&apos;s left hand. The employee was taken to Bellevue Hospital and received several stitches for his injured hand.</p>

<p>The suspect is described as being approximately 35 years old, 5&apos;10&quot; and 200 pounds. Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call the NYPD&apos;s Crime Stoppers Hotline at 800-577-TIPS. The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime Stoppers Website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150408151207/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or texting their tips to 274637(CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
