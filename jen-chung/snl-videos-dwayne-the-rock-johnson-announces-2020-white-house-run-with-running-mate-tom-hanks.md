</div></div>					<p>Cementing his status as a superstar Renaissance man, Dwayne &quot;The Rock&quot; Johnson joined <em>Saturday Night Live</em>&apos;s Five-Timers Club AND officially announced his plans to run for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170717005940/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/14/melissa_mccarthy_snl_5_timers.php">president in 2020</a> with fellow Five-Timers Club member Tom Hanks as his vice president during the monologue. Hanks predicted, &quot;Together we would get 100% of the vote. I would get the senior vote because I fought in World War II in like ten different movies.&quot; </p>

<p>&quot;And I would get the minority vote because everyone just assumes I&apos;m whatever they are,&quot; Johnson declared. </p>

<p>This episode was also the 42nd season&apos;s finale, a season in which Alec Baldwin&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170717005940/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/02/video_alec_baldwin_makes_his_debut.php">Donald Trump impression</a> become a cultural phenomenon (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170717005940/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/21/snl_trump_finale_cold_open_alec_baldwin.php#photo-1">he was in the cold open, as Trump</a>, as well as the one who gave Johnson his Five-Timers&apos; robe). It also marked the final shows for longtime cast members <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170717005940/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/18/drunk_uncle_bobby_moynihan_leaving.php">Bobby Moynihan</a> and Vanessa Bayer. </p>

<p>Overall, the show was another solid if not spectacular outing. The return of David S. Pumpkins helped <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170717005940/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/21/david_s_pumpkins_snl_return.php">put the &quot;Rap Song&quot; parody</a> over the top, while other filmed segments&#x2014;the Cartier Fidget Spinner and erectile dysfunction medication that is suspiciously like meth&#x2014;were amusing. Of the live segments, the WWE promo with Johnson and Moynihan; the superhero Scorpio interlude; and the Wingman sketch were highlights. Cecily Strong&apos;s Gemma was back, and Johnson was as fearless a host as ever, totally willing to make fun of himself:</p>
