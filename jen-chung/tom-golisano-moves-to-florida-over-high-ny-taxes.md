										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_05_golisan.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160207125017im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_05_golisan.jpg" width="120" height="117" class="image-right"> </span>Western NY billionaire&#x2014;and three time NY gubernatorial candidate&#x2014;Tom Golisano has <em>had it</em> with New York&apos;s taxes so he&apos;s changing his legal address to Florida.  Hey, if only the little people had residences in Florida too!  Notably, it&apos;s the<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160207125017/http://gothamist.com/2009/03/30/ny_states_132_billion_budget_is_10.php"> increased taxes to wealthier individuals</a> that has Golisano riled up.  The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160207125017/http://www.buffalonews.com/cityregion/story/672153.html?imw=Y">Buffalo News reports</a>, &quot;Golisano told a gathering of Rochester business executives that he will remain as owner of the Buffalo hockey team, but<strong> he is fleeing the Empire State to avoid paying $13,000 a day in state income taxes</strong>. While Golisano said his move will not end his role as outspoken critic of New York state government, it remains uncertain how seriously his cause will be taken at the State Capitol as a resident of the Town of Naples, Fla.&quot;  Golisano, who is working with another billionaire, Mayor Michael Bloomberg, on possibly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160207125017/http://gothamist.com/2009/01/17/billionaires_bloomberg_golisano_bon.php">revamping the Independence Party</a>, says he&apos;ll use the money he&apos;s saving (over one year, that would be about $4.75 million) to finance charities and maybe his political action committee.</p>
