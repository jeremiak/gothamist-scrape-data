</div></div><p>Tomorrow is the start of the city&apos;s third annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110623145145/http://www.nyc.gov/html/dot/summerstreets/html/home/home.shtml">Summer Streets program</a> where the city closes down a 6.9 mile stretch of roads&#x2014;along Park Avenue and other connecting streets from the Brooklyn Bridge to Central Park&#x2014;to vehicular traffic.  And this year, there&apos;s a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110623145145/http://gothamist.com/2010/07/16/summer_streets_come_back_to_park_av.php">new twist: Dumpster pools</a>!  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110623145145/http://gothamist.com/2010/08/02/photos_first_dip_in_summer_streets.php">Three pools, which have filtration systems</a>, will be set up near the Grand Central viaduct&#x2014;and yay, it&apos;s going to be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110623145145/http://gothamist.com/2010/08/06/very_pleasant_weather_arriving_for.php">beautiful tomorrow</a>!</p>

<p>Summer Streets hours are 7:00 am to 1:00 pm tomorrow, August 14 and August 21.  You can share your photos of the fun by sending them to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110623145145/mailto:photos@gothamist.com">photos@gothamist.com</a> or tagging them &quot;gothamist&quot; on Flickr.  </p>
