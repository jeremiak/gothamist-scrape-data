					<p>New York City&apos;s (and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016132812/http://gothamist.com/2014/10/14/midtown_skyscraper_now_the_tallest.php">the Western Hemisphere&apos;s</a>) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016132812/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/28/432_park_ave_babylon.php">tallest residential building</a> is dramatically altering the Manhattan skyline. Though many pitiful mortals wonder what the world must look like from atop its lofty heights, the only <strike>lizard</strike> people who&apos;ll ever enjoy those stunning vistas are the gazillionaires who can afford the apartments (which start at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016132812/http://gothamist.com/2014/10/14/midtown_skyscraper_now_the_tallest.php/">$16.95 million and go up to $95 million for the penthouse</a>)&#x2014;and the urban explorers who keep sneaking in.</p>

<p>Back in September, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161016132812/http://gothamist.com/2014/09/25/photos_432_park.php">dreamy photos of the skyline</a>, apparently taken from 432 Park, were shared on Instagram, and now, another set of photos from the top have been posted. The photographer told us, &quot;There were about 8 of us on that trip... The view is incredible, especially because there are only a handful of people who will ever see it in person. When you&apos;re up there, you&apos;re the highest person in the city and you can see the horizon on all sides (it&apos;s pretty trippy to look down on the Empire State Building)... I&apos;m not entirely sure, but I think we walked through the $95 million penthouse.&quot;</p>

<p>The photographer, who requested anonymity because of the illegality involved, took a picture of what looks like a pool under construction, as well as feet dangling off the side, adding, &quot;We left things as we found them; we&apos;re not vandals or burglars.&quot;</p>
