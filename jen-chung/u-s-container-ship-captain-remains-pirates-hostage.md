title: U.S. Container Ship Captain Remains Pirates' Hostage

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2009_04_navalbo.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629173222im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2009_04_navalbo.jpg" width="140" height="105" class="image-right"> </span>U.S. Naval warships are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629173222/http://www.cnn.com/2009/WORLD/africa/04/11/somalia.u.s.ship/index.html">gathering around a fiberglass lifeboat</a> carrying four Somali pirates and their hostage, Maersk Alabama Captain Richard Phillips, whose container ship they tried to hijack.  A Somali journalist told CNN that a German ship taken by other pirates attempted to reach the lifeboat (to aid their fellow pirates) in the Indian Ocean but turned back due to the U.S. presence.  Phillips, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629173222/http://www.nytimes.com/2009/04/11/world/africa/11pirates.html?_r=1&amp;hp">described as the &quot;consummate regular guy&quot;</a> by friends and family, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629173222/http://gothamist.com/2009/04/10/us_navy_pirates_face_off_in_indian.php">attempted to escape yesterday</a> but was recaptured; a childhood friend told the NY Times, &quot;When he went for that swim today, it didn&#x2019;t surprise me at all. He&#x2019;s got good intuition, and he&#x2019;s a very determined guy.&quot; The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629173222/http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/africa/7994566.stm">FBI is involved with the hostage negotiations</a> (Defense Secretary Gates said Phillips&apos; return was a &quot;top priority&quot;), which may be a long process&#x2014;he pirates have demanded $2 million and safe passage.  Additionally, French commandos <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110629173222/http://www.nydailynews.com/news/us_world/2009/04/11/2009-04-11_fatal_french_commando_rescue_of_pirate_hostages_underscores_risk_of_sneak_attack.html">rescued hostages from pirates</a> in the Gulf of Aden, leaving one hostage and two pirates dead in the process.</p>
