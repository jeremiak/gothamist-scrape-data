	<p>Yesterday was the first day of the two-day <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130727022822/http://bigapplebbq.org/">Big Apple Barbecue Block Party</a>, which is sponsored by Southern Living. Scott Lynch visited and took photographs of the mouthwatering fare&#x2014;and the huge crowds.</p>

<p>The event has come a long way from its <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130727022822/http://gothamist.com/2003/06/02/1st_annual_big_apple_barbecue_block_party.php">humble start on one small block</a> (now it wraps around Madison Square Park) and features a wonderful sampling of pitmasters from all over the country. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130727022822/https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10152843959135184&amp;set=a.206346065183.277637.101319660183&amp;type=1&amp;theater">Check out the operation</a> that the 17th Street Bar &amp; Grill, headed by legendary champion pitmaster Mike MIlls, brought to town!</p>

<p>There are lots of people vying for the delicious fare, so be patient. One standout, according to Lynch, was the Smoked Sausage with Pimento Cheese from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130727022822/https://www.facebook.com/JimNNicks">Jim &apos;n Nick&apos;s</a>.</p>

<p>The gluttony starts up again at 11 a.m. at Madison Square Park. Plates are $9. <br>
