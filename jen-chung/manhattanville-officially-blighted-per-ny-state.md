---

<span class="photo_caption">Photograph of Manhattanville by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120125111232/http://flickr.com/photos/mmj171188/2045070726/">Mira (on the wall) on Flickr</a></span> </p>

<p>Unsurprisingly, the state agency the oversees development <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120125111232/http://cityroom.blogs.nytimes.com/2008/07/17/state-declares-columbia-expansion-site-blighted/">declared a 17-acre area eyed by Columbia University was &quot;blighted&quot;</a>, opening the door for the state to use eminent domain to seize properties for Columbia&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120125111232/http://gothamist.com/2006/11/30/university_may.php">grand Manhattanville expansion plan</a>.</p>

<p>The school owns most of the 17 acres, but there are still a few holdouts who are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120125111232/http://www.nypost.com/seven/07172008/news/regionalnews/columbia_deal_avoids_eminent_domain_pain_120277.htm">promising go to court</a> over the matter. A lawyer for the other owners, Norman Siegel, said, &quot;The battle lines have been drawn.&quot; And one resident and former community board member <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120125111232/http://www.columbiaspectator.com/node/55228">told the Columbia Spectator</a>, &#x201C;It&#x2019;s never been blighted. It&#x2019;s an industrial area &#x2026; Any semblance of blight is strictly on the properties owned by Columbia University.&quot; </p>

<p>Columbia&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120125111232/http://gothamist.com/2006/11/30/university_may.php">controversial</a> plan was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120125111232/http://gothamist.com/2007/11/26/manhattanville_1.php">approved by the City Planning Agency last fall</a>.  </p>
