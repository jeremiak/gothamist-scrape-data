										<p>President Trump has said he abruptly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/comey-misstated-key-clinton-email-evidence-at-hearing-say-people-close-to-investigation/2017/05/09/074c1c7e-34bd-11e7-b373-418f6849a004_story.html?utm_term=.1383ddae9873">fired FBI Director James Comey</a> this week <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/http://www.foxnews.com/politics/2017/05/10/trump-takes-aim-at-schumers-reaction-to-comey-firing.html">because</a>, &quot;Very simply, he was not doing a good job.&quot; Later, the president <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/http://www.cnn.com/2017/05/12/politics/trump-comey-russia-thing/">said</a>, &quot;When I decided to just do it, I said to myself, I said &apos;you know, this Russia thing with Trump and Russia is a made-up story.&apos;&quot; Days earlier, around the time that Comey <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/https://www.nytimes.com/2017/05/10/us/politics/comey-russia-investigation-fbi.html?_r=0">reportedly requested more funding</a> for the FBI&apos;s investigation into possible collusion between the Trump campaign and Russia&apos;s election tampering, the President tweeted:</p>

<p>There are many reports <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/http://www.cnn.com/2017/05/11/politics/trump-comey-loyalty/">analyzing</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/http://www.newyorker.com/news/news-desk/james-comeys-conspicuous-independence">why Comey was fired</a>, not to mention ample speculation about whether deputy Attorney General Rod Rosenstein <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/http://www.politico.com/story/2017/05/11/rod-rosenstein-blamed-james-comey-firing-238266">is being used by the administration</a> to give cover to Trump&apos;s decision, while the stated reasons that went into the decision <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/https://www.wsj.com/articles/donald-trump-says-he-decided-to-fire-james-comey-before-rosenstein-meeting-1494524604">continue to shift</a>. But at least we have this illustration for next week&apos;s New Yorker help put the whole thing into terms <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170512173718/http://chicagoist.com/2017/04/10/video_man_screams_as_he_is_forcibly.php">everyone can understand</a>.  </p>
