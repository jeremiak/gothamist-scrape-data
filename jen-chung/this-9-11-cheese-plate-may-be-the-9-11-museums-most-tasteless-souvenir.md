					<p>Setting aside the question of whether a reporter can ask a question at the 9/11 Museum (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102035733/http://gothamist.com/2014/05/22/911_museum_censorship.php">short answer: she can&apos;t!</a>), let us tackle another timely inquiry: Just <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102035733/http://gothamist.com/2014/05/18/families_offended_by_crass_insensit.php">how crass is the 9/11 Museum&apos;s gift shop</a>?</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102035733/http://nypost.com/2014/05/18/outrage-over-911-museum-gift-shops-crass-souvenirs/">Post previously detailed some of the items</a>, like a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102035733/https://www.911memorial.org/catalog/darkness-hoodie.html">&quot;Darkness Hoodie&quot;</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102035733/https://www.911memorial.org/catalog/survivor-tree-single-flower-wire-drop-earrings.html">&quot;Survivor Tree&quot; earrings</a>. But they forgot to mention the 9/11 cheese plate.</p>

<p>The cheese plate is in the shape of the continental U.S., with hearts where the attacks hit NYC; the Pentagon in Arlington, VA; and Shanksville, Pennsylvania. Because no cheese course is complete without a moment to &quot;Never forget&quot; (to take our Lactaid). Let&apos;s roll... that wheel of cheddar to Freedomtown?</p>

<p>The shop has more common museum gift shop items, like museum t-shirts and museum rubber bands. One visitor <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102035733/http://nypost.com/2014/05/22/gift-shop-inside-911-museum-draws-criticism-on-opening-day/">told the Post yesterday</a>, &quot;As rotten and heartless as it may seem, it&#x2019;s always about money. Educational books and T-shirts and posters that say, &#x2018;Never forget 9/11&#x2019; are OK, but the dog vests and the cheap earrings need to go.&quot;</p>

<p>Museum spokesman Michael Frazier <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171102035733/http://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-nation/wp/2014/05/19/families-infuriated-by-crass-commercialism-of-911-museum-gift-shop/">explained the Washington Post</a> that the items in the shop were &quot;carefully selected... To care for the Memorial and Museum, our organization relies on private fundraising, gracious donations and revenue from ticketing and carefully selected keepsake items for retail.&quot; He also noted that during the period where 9/11 workers, survivors and victims&apos; families visited the museum, &quot;[m]any of our guests from the 9/11 community have visited the shop and purchased a keepsake from their historic experience.&quot;</p>
