<p>While keeping resolutions <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103115237/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/31/reminder_taking_a_bath_is_a_new_yea.php">may be a losing battle</a> for us weak humans, it may be easier for you to keep them when they relate to your pet&apos;s health.  <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103115237/http://gothamist.com/2010/09/21/fat_pets_are_getting_fatter_thanks.php">Studies say </a>that about half of dogs and cats are overweight or obese, which can result diabetes, respiratory disease, kidney disease, and other ailments including hip dysplasia.  </p>

<p>Steven Budsberg, director of clinical research at the University of Georgia College of Veterinary Medicine, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103115237/http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748704476604576158372088195308.html?mod=WSJ_LifeStyle_LeadStoryNA">told the Wall Street Journal last year</a>, &quot;Obesity in pets is almost the equivalent of smoking in human medicine. There&apos;s the high cost to people, and it&apos;s self-induced. I never met a German shepherd who could open the refrigerator or food bag and pour himself another bowl.&quot;  </p>

Here are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103115237/http://www.petmd.com/cat/nutrition/evr_ct_obesity_in_cats_and_what_to_do_about_an_overweight_cat">tips on how to control your desire to give kitty treats</a> and how to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120103115237/http://www.aspca.org/pet-care/dog-care/overweight-dogs.aspx">change your behavior to get Fido healthier</a>.</p>
