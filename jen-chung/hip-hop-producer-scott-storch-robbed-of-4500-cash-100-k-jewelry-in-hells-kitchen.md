                            <p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-right"> <img alt="2013_08_scottstorch.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928023950im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2013_08_scottstorch.jpg" width="300" height="351"> <br> <i style=" width:300px; ;display:block"> Scott Storch in 2010 (Pacific Coast News)</i></div> Scott Storch, the hip-hop producer who has worked with Dr. Dre and Beyonce, was robbed at gunpoint this morning at 46th Street between 8th and 9th Avenues. <p></p>

<p>The robbers allegedly took a briefcase that had $4,500 in cash as well a $50,000 Rolex and a $50,000 diamond necklace.</p>

<p>Apparently Storch had been at Platinum Sound Recording Studios. The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928023950/http://blogs.wsj.com/metropolis/2013/08/20/hip-hop-producer-robbed-at-gunpoint-police/?mod=WSJBlog">Wall Street Journal reports</a>, &quot;The group spent the night listening to music tracks before deciding to leave Platinum about 6:45 a.m. Mr.  Storch told police, the official said. The group had entered a livery cab and was waiting for a second one for additional passengers when they were approached by two men in their mid-20s.&quot;</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928023950/http://www.tmz.com/2013/08/20/scott-storch-cops-robbed-gunpoint-jewelry-threatened/">TMZ adds</a> that Storch had been in the SUV when &quot;he was approached by two men with guns... who said, &apos;Give us all your jewelry and cash or you&apos;re dead.&apos;&quot;</p>

<p>Storch was wildly successful after producing songs like Justin Timberlake&apos;s &quot;Cry Me A River,&quot; Beyonce&apos;s &quot;Baby Boy,&quot; 50&apos;s &quot;Candy Shop&quot; and Fat Joe&apos;s &quot;Lean Back&quot; and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928023950/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PnXSSkjZhZ0">dated</a> Lil Kim, Kim Kardashian and Paris Hilton. </p>

<p>He ended up <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130928023950/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1613761/scott-storchs-rise-fall-return.jhtml">squandering $30 million in six months</a>, thanks to a 2006 cocaine addiction.</p>
