</div></div>					<p>Last night was the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150903134005/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/10/supermoon_supersoon_biggest_full_mo.php">biggest and brightest full moon of 2014</a>, and it was pretty spectacular. </p>

<p>The U.S. Naval Observatory said last night&apos;s moon would be about 12% bigger (and 30% brighter) than the January Supermoon, but Susan Rose, president of the Amateur Observers Society of New York, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150903134005/http://www.newsday.com/business/technology/supermoon-is-larger-brighter-but-difficult-to-see-1.9019963">explained to Newsday</a>, &quot;The only way you can tell the difference is ... to take a photograph&quot; and then compare it with &quot;a full moon that is at its farthest from Earth.&quot; And former Newsday photographer Ken Spencer said, &quot;To try and decide if it&apos;s 5 percent larger or smaller seems like a fool&apos;s errand. Just go out and soak it in.&quot;</p>
