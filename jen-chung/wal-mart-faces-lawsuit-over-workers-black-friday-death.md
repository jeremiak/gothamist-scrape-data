title: Wal-Mart Faces Lawsuit Over Worker's Black Friday Death
---

	
										<p><img alt="2008_12_walmstam.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171022023157im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2008_12_walmstam.jpg" width="140" height="105" class="right">The family of Jdimytai Damour, the Wal-mart worker <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171022023157/http://gothamist.com/2008/11/29/cops_on_fatal_li_walmart_stampede_t.php">killed in the Black Friday stampede</a>, has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171022023157/http://www.newsday.com/news/local/nassau/ny-limart1204,0,7774674.story">filed a wrongful-death lawsuit against the retailer</a>.  Damour, a temporary employee hired at the Valley Stream store for the holidays, was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171022023157/http://gothamist.com/2008/12/01/autopsy_walmart_worker_was_trampled.php">trampled to death by shoppers</a> who had been waiting for hours for Wal-Mart to open its doors at 5 a.m.  Newsday reports that the lawsuit  faults the store for not providing enough security and says <strong>Wal-Mart &quot;engaged in specific marketing and advertising techniques to specifically attract a large crowd and create an environment of frenzy and mayhem and was otherwise careless, reckless and negligent.&quot;</strong> (You can <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171022023157/http://www.adrants.com/2008/12/whats-that-underfoot-no-not-a.php#more">see one Wal-Mart Black Friday ad here</a>.)  Other parties named in the suit are the realty company that manages the property and the security firm for the store; Damour&apos;s family &quot;also filed notice saying it plans to sue Nassau County and the Nassau County Police Department.&quot;</p>
