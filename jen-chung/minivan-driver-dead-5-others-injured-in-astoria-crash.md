</div></div>					<p>Yesterday afternoon, a minivan crashed in Astoria&#x2014;the driver reportedly suffered a fatal heart attack while driving his family, including a baby grandchild.</p>

<p>Police say the crash occurred at 35th Avenue and 21st Street. The 2002 Kia Sedona had jumped the 35th Avenue curb then hit a hydrant before flipping onto its side. The driver, Anthony Boyd, 45, was taken to Mount Sinai Hospital where he was pronounced dead.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150510015344/http://nypost.com/2014/07/27/grandfather-suffers-fatal-heart-attack-while-driving-injuring-tot/">According to the Post</a>, Boyd had a heart attack: &quot;Shocked bystanders raced over and began breaking the car&#x2019;s windows to free the man&#x2019;s 44-year old wife, 28-year-old daughter and two grandchildren, ages 10 and 1, cops and witnesses said.&quot; </p>

<p>One witness noted that many people were trying to help, &quot;The little boy, the baby, with braids, he crawled out himself. [The man who found him] was trying rock him, calm him down. But the baby kept screaming.&#x201D;</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the minivan&apos;s five passengers were taken to Elmhurst Hospital in stable condition, while a pedestrian who was struck by debris was taken to Mount Sinai in stable condition. </p>
