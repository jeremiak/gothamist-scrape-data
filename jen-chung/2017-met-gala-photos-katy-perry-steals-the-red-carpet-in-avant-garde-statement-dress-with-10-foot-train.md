					<p>It&apos;s the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170502051022/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/30/get_ready_for_some_fashion_the_met.php">Party of the Year</a>! The cream-colored red carpet is lining the Metropolitan Museum of Art&apos;s annual Costume Institute gala, and this year&apos;s event is centered around the new exhibit about <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170502051022/http://gothamist.com/2016/10/21/rei_kawakubocomme_des_garcons_will.php">Rei Kawakubo and her groundbreaking line, Comme de Gar&#xE7;ons</a>. Kawakubo is known for her avant-garde and, at times, and some of the party&apos;s attendees tried to push the envelope. Others, not so much.</p>

<p>Katy Perry, who is a co-chair of the gala, wore a dramatic red Maison Marigela dress with a 10-foot train, a look that channeled a gothic wedding gown or mourning dress. Priyanka Chopra also made an entrance, with her Ralph Lauren trench coat gown, which also had a long train. As Rihanna proved in 2015, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170502051022/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/04/met_gala_photos_rihanna_crushes_it.php">long trains look amazing in photos</a>.</p>

<p>Helen Lasichanh, wife of gala co-chair Pharrell Williams, wore a wonderful red Commes des Garcons &quot;gown&quot; while Lily Collins went for a dramatic Kawakubo-esque haircut. Tracee Ellis Ross also went for Kawakubo-ish volume in a blue brocade dress.</p>

<p>La La Anthony killed with a perfect revenge dress that says &quot;Carmelo who?&quot; but <br>
overall many other dresses seemed more standard issue red carpet fare&#x2014;like Gisele Bundchen&apos;s silver gown&#x2014;pretty, but boring given the context of the night. </p>
