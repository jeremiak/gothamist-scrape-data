<p>&apos;Tis the season for Republicans to crap on America&apos;s favorite Obama&#x2014;yes, we&apos;re talking about Bo Obama, the First Dog.  Yes, half-term Governor <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120504163422/http://radio.foxnews.com/toddstarnes/top-stories/no-christmas-in-white-house-holiday-card.html">Sarah Palin thought it was &quot;odd&quot;</a> that the Obamas&apos; family holiday card featured the adorable pup (and his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120504163422/http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2011/12/22/article-2077573-0F41E0AC00000578-258_634x408.jpg">&quot;signature&quot;</a>) and Senator John McCain said Obama <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120504163422/http://www.politico.com/news/stories/1211/70779.html">should be leading</a>, not taking Bo shopping (<a href="youtu.be/j1nSzE6-ptM">video</a>) for Christmas presents.  But liberal MSNBC host Ed Schultz took aim at the criticisms by pointing out some classic GOP hypocrisy.</p>

<p>Schultz, accompanied by an adorable beagle named Rex, noted that former President George W. Bush made <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120504163422/http://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/barney/">numerous popular videos of his dogs Barney and Miss Beazley</a>.  Amusingly, Rex became agitated after clips of Republicans aired! </p>
