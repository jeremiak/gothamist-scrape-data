title: Watch Out For Smartphone Snatchers On The Subway
---

	
										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="2015_10_iphonerobbery.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160326151918im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2015_10_iphonerobbery.jpg" width="200" height="336" class="image-left"> </span>Earlier this month, a mugger managed to steal an iPhone 6 during a classic &quot;grab and go&quot; on a subway in Queens.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, on October 5th, at 3:45 p.m., a 25-year-old woman was sitting on a Queens bound A train that pulled in the Beach 25 Street station when the male suspect approached her: &quot;As the train was stopped in the station with the train car doors open, the suspect removed the victim&apos;s iPhone 6 from her hand and fled.&quot;</p>

<p>The police released an image of the suspect, describing him as being about 5&apos;7&quot; to 5&apos;9&quot; with brown eyes, black hair and a mustache.  He was last seen wearing a baseball hat, a long sleeve blue shirt, dark sweatpants, white sneakers, and carrying a book bag.<br>
 <br>
Anyone with information in regards to this incident is asked to call Crime stoppers at 1-800-577-TIPS (8477).  The public can also submit their tips by logging onto the Crime stoppers website at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160326151918/http://WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM/">WWW.NYPDCRIMESTOPPERS.COM</a> or by texting their tips to 274637 (CRIMES) then enter TIP577.</p>
