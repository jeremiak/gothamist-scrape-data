<p>While you may only be getting up from your Derby watching (really drinking) or <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130507184022/http://gothamist.com/2013/05/04/7_places_to_go_for_cinco_de_mayo_to.php">Cinco de Mayo</a> pre-gaming, thousands of bicyclists have been on the streets for hours for the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130507184022/http://www.bikenewyork.org/ride/five-boro-bike-tour/">5 Boro Bike Tour</a>. With 32,000 participants, the starts are staggered to manage the flow of riders. There are also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130507184022/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/26/weve_been_wondering_how_the.php">other security measures in place</a>.</p>

<p>The ride starts in Manhattan, goes to the Bronx, then it&apos;s back to Manhattan and into Queens. From Queens, bicyclists head to Brooklyn and finally Staten Island. According to NYPD, these streets will be closed Sunday from 7:45am to 6:30pm:</p>
