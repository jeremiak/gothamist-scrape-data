<p>A man was wounded after being shot by a police officer this morning in Brooklyn.</p>

<p>The incident occurred around 7:15 a.m. at Lefferts and Rogers Avenues in Prospect Lefferts Gardens. The wounded man was taken to Kings County Hospital with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015131/http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/Prospect-Lefferts-Gardens-Police-Involved-Shooting-386198881.html">non-life-threatening injuries</a>. </p>

<p>An NYPD spokesperson confirmed that the officer fired and was not injured. However, the officer is <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015131/http://pix11.com/2016/07/10/person-wounded-during-police-involved-shooting-in-brooklyn-sources/">being treated for tinnitus</a>. </p>

<p>The spokesperson added that a gun was found at the scene.</p>

<p><b>Update</b>: Police say they were alerted to the scene when their Shotspotter technology notified them of gunfire at the intersection. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015131/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/brooklyn/suspect-wounded-police-involved-shooting-brooklyn-article-1.2706203">The Daily News reports</a>, &quot;one lieutenant and two officers spotted Paul Mathurin in middle of the street &apos;menacing motorists.&apos;&quot; Police say that Mathurin waved his gun and refused to drop it, but a friend who was looking out the window says he was only holding his cellphone and headphones. </p>
