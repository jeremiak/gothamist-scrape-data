					<p>Yesterday, I was exhilarated by the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/08/photos_nyc_election_day_2016.php">voter turnout I saw</a> in my Manhattan neighborhood. I hadn&apos;t seen lines this long in 2008 or 2012, and when a woman asked how long the wait was, a few of us guessed maybe 15-20 minutes (it was actually 30 minutes) and I added, &quot;It&apos;s worth it!&quot; She looked at me and brightened, &quot;It is!&quot; </p>

<p>And it was&#x2014;I took my 6-year-old daughter to cast a vote for Hillary Clinton, the first female candidate for president. I hoped that we could make history, and hoped that I could share in, yes, a tough working mother&apos;s belief in the &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/29/clinton_accepts_nomination.php#photo-1">boundless confidence in America&#x2019;s promise</a>&quot; where women <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/23/clinton_attack_ad_trump_misogyny.php">aren&apos;t judged on their looks</a> and no one is judged by their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/01/samantha_bee_trump_racist.php">skin color or religion or accents</a>... I could go on. </p>

<p>I think a lot of other parents in New York had the same optimism, especially the ones who brought their children to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/08/cobble_hill_election_party_or_riot.php">big block party at President and Clinton Streets</a> in Cobble Hill last night. Our photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://www.gretchenrobinette.com/">Gretchen Robinette</a> was there, marveling at the packed streets (she estimated a few hundred were there at one point). </p>

<p>&quot;Everyone started out with happy faces and cheering, then turned to sadness and fear,&quot; she said, and then at 10 p.m., when the permit for the party ended, so did the gathering &quot;with absolute sadness and silence.&quot; </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2016_11_clinton123.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2016_11_clinton123.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> At the block party last night (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://www.gretchenrobinette.com/">Gretchen Robinette</a> / Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>My husband and I let our daughter Katie stay up to watch some of the returns. She even played with the maps on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://www.270towin.com/">270 To Win</a> before going to bed a little after 9 p.m. I <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://dcist.com/2016/11/election_2016_results.php">watched the returns come in</a>, going from unease and agitation to sorrow and weeping. Then I realized, shit, we&apos;re going to have to tell our daughter in a few hours... and, SHIT, what about all the other kids in her school, especially the ones who have been targeted by Trump&apos;s rhetoric? Children her age have talked about Trump making non-white kids go back to their countries, while other classmates said they were afraid their parents would have to leave, leaving them behind because they are U.S. citizens.</p>

<center><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">my 8 year old asked if president trump once shoved his hand down a ladies pants &amp; laughed about it. she heard about it at school. i froze</p>&#x2014; BRIAN MICHAEL BENDIS (@BRIANMBENDIS) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/https://twitter.com/BRIANMBENDIS/status/796346197162139648">November 9, 2016</a></blockquote> <script async src="//web.archive.org/web/20171016015744js_/http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></center>

<p>After barely two and a half hours of sleep&#x2014;and the realization that I should try not to cry when telling her&#x2014;we went to Katie&apos;s room at 6:15 a.m. She wanted to tell us how one of our cats sounds like an alien, not a feline. I pulled her into my lap and said, &quot;So, I have to tell you something: Donald Trump got more votes than Hillary Clinton, so he&apos;s our president.&quot; </p>

<p>Her face dropped. &quot;What happened?&quot; she asked, and I did my best to try to explain that a lot of people voted, and that while New York City was for Hillary, Trump won in many more places.</p>

<p>&quot;But isn&apos;t Trump bad?&quot; she asked, because she definitely heard TV news reports as well as our own discussions about Trump&apos;s consistently abhorrent behavior (we turned off the groping stuff, but everything about immigrants, how he loves bullying people and mocking the vulnerable, etc., was fair game). </p>

<p>I sighed. &quot;Well, last night, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://gothamist.com/2016/11/09/trump_president.php#photo-1">Donald Trump said</a> that he wanted to be a president for everybody, to help everybody have a better life.&quot; </p>

<p>Katie perked up, &quot;That&apos;s good.&quot;</p>

<center><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">My kids will be up any minute and I&apos;m struggling to put on a brave face. How do I tell them a man they know to be hateful is now president?</p>&#x2014; Kristin Dos Santos (@KristinDSantos) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/https://twitter.com/KristinDSantos/status/796353833387462657">November 9, 2016</a></blockquote> <script async src="//web.archive.org/web/20171016015744js_/http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></center>

<p>My husband tried to explain how this was like a sports game (if only!) and her side lost. I continued, &quot;There were so many votes, so no matter who won, there would be a lot of people who would be disappointed.&quot;</p>

<p>&quot;But why didn&apos;t she win?&quot;</p>

<p>&quot;Well, I think that while we and a lot of other people believed in her ideas, there were many people who thought Donald Trump had better ideas&quot;&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/http://www.npr.org/sections/thetwo-way/2016/11/09/501393501/shades-of-2000-clinton-surpasses-trump-in-popular-vote-tally">though not really</a>&#x2014;&quot;so he is the president now. What we can do is hope that he can help everybody.&quot;</p>

<p>&quot;This. Is. The. Worst. Day. Of. My. Life,&quot; she declared.</p>

<p>&quot;Well, I hope this <em>is</em> the worst day of your life,&quot; I said.</p>

<p>&quot;Why?&quot;</p>

<p>&quot;Because we&apos;re healthy&#x2014;and we&apos;re all together. Think about how much worse it would be if someone you knew and loved died. So I hope this is the worst day of your life.&quot;</p>

<p>We told her how Hillary Clinton worked so hard and that everyone was still proud of her and her work. &quot;What Hillary Clinton would love is for you to keep learning and being curious and kind,&quot; I said. </p>

<center><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">My wife&apos;s guidance to our despondent kids this morning: &quot;Be informed. Speak up for what is right. Be good to people.&quot;</p>&#x2014; Ed Zimmerman (@EdGrapeNutZimm) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016015744/https://twitter.com/EdGrapeNutZimm/status/796349268680462336">November 9, 2016</a></blockquote> <script async src="//web.archive.org/web/20171016015744js_/http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></center>

<p>Katie nodded and became distracted by her toys. But then she asked, &quot;Can you tell me that thing that Donald Trump said again?&quot;</p>

<p>&quot;Do you mean how he wants to be a president for everybody, to help all of us?&quot;</p>

<p>&quot;Yes.&quot; She was thoughtful and exclaimed, &quot;It&apos;ll be okay because he wouldn&apos;t lie about that!&quot;</p>

<p>My heart broke for her innocence. I hope she&apos;s right and that we&apos;ll all get through this.</p>
