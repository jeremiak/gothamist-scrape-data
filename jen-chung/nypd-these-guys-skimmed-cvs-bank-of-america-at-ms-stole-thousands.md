<p>The police are looking for two suspects who stole thousands of dollars from victims&apos; bank accounts by setting up skimming devices at Manhattan ATMs. </p>

<p>In January, police said that the pair &quot;attached a skimming device to an ATM located at 420 5th Avenue (CVS pharmacy) and to an ATM located at 21 East 96 Street (Bank of America)&quot; and then took the ATM card holder&apos;s personal information to create duplicate cards and withdrew money from the victims&apos; accounts at Queens banks. The police mentioned these six incidents:<br>
 <br>
<em>Incident #1 - Grand larceny reported at approximately 1658 hours on Friday, October 16, 2015. $1,000 taken.<br>
Incident #2 - Grand larceny reported at approximately 2100 hours on Friday, October 16, 2015. $1,180 taken.<br>
Incident #3 - Grand larceny reported at approximately 1000 hours on Thursday, October 22, 2015. $1,640 taken.<br>
Incident #4 - Grand larceny reported at approximately 0000 hours on Saturday, November 21, 2015. $1,515 taken.<br>
Incident #5 - Grand larceny reported at approximately 1430 hours on Tuesday, December 1, 2015. $986 taken.<br>
Incident #6 - Grand larceny reported at approximately 1500 hours on Wednesday, December 2, 2015. $1,483 taken.</em><br>
