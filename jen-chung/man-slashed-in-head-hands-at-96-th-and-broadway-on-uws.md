<p>A 19-year-old man was attacked on the Upper West Side early this morning by four suspects, police say.</p>

<p>According to the NYPD, the incident occurred around midnight. Police say the victim was waiting for a bus at the northeast corner of West 96th Street and Broadway when he was approached by four males who asked him where he was from. A police spokesperson said that the victim got a &quot;bad vibe&quot; from the males and fled to a center median, where a physical altercation took place, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007053134/http://abc7ny.com/news/teen-slashed-at-subway-station-on-upper-west-side/1264005/">apparently at the West 96th Street and Broadway</a> subway station. </p>

<p>The suspects tried to grab at the victim&apos;s pants for his cellphone and allegedly slashed him on the back of his head and on both hands. The victim was taken to St. Luke&apos;s Hospital and is in stable condition.</p>

<p>Police are continuing their investigation. The NYPD is currently trying to stem the tide of recent slashings <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007053134/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/22/this_isnt_1995_at_the_tunnel_okay.php">with &quot;Operation Cutting Edge.&quot;</a></p>
