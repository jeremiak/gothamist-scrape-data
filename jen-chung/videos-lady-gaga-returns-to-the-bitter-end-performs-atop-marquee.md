					<p>As we all know by now, Lady Gaga is a Native New Yorker who went to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021351/http://gothamist.com/2010/06/10/gaga_4.php">Sacred Heart</a>, dropped out of NYU, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021351/http://gothamist.com/2011/11/18/live_in_lady_gagas_old_les_apartmen.php">lived on the Lower East Side</a> and considers fashion to be performance art. To help promote her new album, <em>Joanne</em> (her &quot;best in years&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021351/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/review-lady-gagas-joanne-w446032">says Rolling Stone</a>), the artist also known as Stefani Joanne Angelina Germanotta headed to The Bitter End and performed inside, as well as on the marquee:</p>

<p>The album&apos;s name is also the name of the aunt she never knew, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016021351/http://nypost.com/2016/10/20/how-lady-gagas-tragic-family-history-inspired-her-new-album/">her father&apos;s sister Joanne who died at age 18</a>. Joanne was a sexual assault survivor, Gaga has recounted, who later contracted lupus. </p>

<p>The stop at the Bitter End as Gaga&apos;s second of three intimate shows, sponsored by Bud Light (yes, that&apos;s why she&apos;s wearing the Bud Light bodysuit). Helen Mirren and husband Taylor Hackford were in attendance, as well as Robert DeNiro.</p>
