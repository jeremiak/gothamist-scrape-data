<p>Take a look at the excitement of thousands of Occupy Wall Street protesters and supporters when it was announced that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018081146/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/14/live_from_occupy_wall_street_where.php#photo-1">Brookfield Properties was postponing the cleaning</a>.  The announcement was made around 6:30 a.m., moments after a Direct Action rep told the crowd, &quot;You may not be able to resist arrest but stay in this park in any capacity! Our time is now!&quot;</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018081146if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/c9D33hr4CW0?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe> </p>

<p>The good feelings prompted the protesters to march... and there were some violent clashes.  We&apos;re culling through reports, but we&apos;re hearing that at least a half dozen people were <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111018081146/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/14/live_from_occupy_wall_street_where.php#photo-1">taken away in plastic handcuffs</a>.</p>
