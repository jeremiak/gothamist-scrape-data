	<p>Yesterday&apos;s Puerto Rican Day Parade brought Boricua pride to Fifth Avenue.  And since it&apos;s an election year, many politicians were out front and center, including Anthony Weiner, who drew a mixed response from the crowd.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130717101021/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/weiner_banner_day_1zyHqnwBc5sIxLpjFeJmCO?utm_medium=rss&amp;utm_content=Local">According to the Post</a>, there were three types of Weiner observations: </p>

<p><em>&quot;Everybody makes mistakes. He&#x2019;s a good man, and I hope that he wins. I&#x2019;d vote for him.&quot;</em> &#x2014; 45-year-old woman who said she&apos;d vote for him.<br>
<em>&quot;He&#x2019;s disgusting, putting his body on the Web and degrading women like he did, embarrassing his wife.&quot;</em> &#x2014; Vietnam vet who booed him.<br>
<em>&quot;There&#x2019;s a lot of pretty girls with Weiner.&quot;</em> &#x2014; Anonymous paradegoer.</p>

<p>An estimated one million people turned out to watch the parade, which largely unfolded without incident. The cop suffered a broken leg, the driver was not charged.</p>
