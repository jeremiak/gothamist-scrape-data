</div></div>					<p>The big food-and-music festival, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504165811/http://googamooga.com/">The Great GoogaMooga</a>, is starting in less than an hour in Prospect Park.  The natives are eager to try the decadent dishes&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504165811/http://gothamist.com/2012/05/16/gut-busting_googa_mooga_food.php">Fried Cheesecake Bombs and Dirty Duck Dogs</a>, anyone?&#x2014;while listening to the<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504165811/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/23/hall_oates_will_headline_great_goog.php"> sweet sounds of Hall &amp; Oates, The Roots</a> and many more bands (here&apos;s the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504165811/http://lineup.googamooga.com/events/2012/05/19/">lineup for today and tomorrow</a>). But here&apos;s a look at the quiet before the storm.</p>

<p>Free general admission tickets have been sold out for a while, but the Extra Mooga tickets ($250 per day) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504165811/http://extramooga.eventbrite.com/">are still available</a>&#x2014;the &quot;Extra&quot; includes things like Marcus Samuelsson&apos;s Harlem Renaissance Brunch, all-inclusive food, &quot;from tastes to heaping plates of your favorites,&quot; wine, beer, cocktails, and a DJ set from James Murphy. </p>
