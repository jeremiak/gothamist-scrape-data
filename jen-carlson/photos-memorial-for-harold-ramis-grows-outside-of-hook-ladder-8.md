</div></div>					<p>Yesterday, just after the news spread that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429053842/http://chicagoist.com/2014/02/24/harold_ramis_actor_writer_and_direc.php">Harold Ramis</a> had died, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429053842/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/24/photos_harold_ramis_memorial_at_gho.php">a small memorial</a> went up at Hook &amp; Ladder 8 (aka Ghostbusters HQ) in TriBeCa. The memorial has since grown, with more Twinkies than your local bodega (a nod to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429053842/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pzaQjS1JstY">this scene</a>), samples of spores and fungus, Nestle Crunch bars (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429053842/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x2uMM34zafA">for this scene</a>), <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429053842/http://jgtwo.wordpress.com/2012/10/28/honest-ghostbusters-3-concerns/">Cheez-Its</a>, flowers, and photos. </p>

<p>There is one thing missing, however... the Ghostbusters sign is still inside the firehouse. While a photo has been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429053842/https://twitter.com/dennismons/status/438278062430384128">passed around on Twitter</a>, declaring the sign has been hung as a tribute to Ramis, that photo is many years old. We reached out to the FDNY to find out if they&apos;ll display the sign outside, and we&apos;ll update when we hear back.</p>

<p><strong>UPDATE:</strong> The FDNY&apos;s Frank Dwyer tells us the sign will not be going up, as they only place things outside the firehouse when a firefighter has died.</p>
