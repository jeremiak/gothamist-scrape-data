</div></div>					<p>As <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511170710/http://gothamist.com/tags/phish">Phish</a> sets up for a four night residency at the current incarnation of Madison Square Garden, let&apos;s daydream about what it would be like to see them <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511170710/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ceqHqT9q0_E">hover over the crowd in a giant hot dog</a> inside one of the <em>older</em> MSGs. Or let&apos;s just daydream about what it would be like <em>not </em>to be inside of an architectural atrocity during concerts and other events. Click through for a look inside <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511170710/http://gothamist.com/2009/08/25/flashback_madison_square_garden.php#photo-6">the second Madison Square Garden</a> (also known as Madison Square Garden II), which existed at the northeast corner of Madison Square from 1890 to 1925 (at which point it was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150511170710/http://gothamist.com/2012/07/30/tk_photos_of_old_manhattan_being_to.php#photo-1">demolished</a>).</p>
