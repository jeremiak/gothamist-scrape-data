</div></div>					<p>The Wes Anderson Art Show made its New York City <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151118101358/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/23/wes_anderson_art_show.php">debut this week</a>, opening last night to big crowds and long lines. The good news is: the show is fantastic. The bad news is: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151118101358/http://www.eventbrite.com/e/6th-annual-wes-anderson-themed-art-exhibit-tickets-17870722813">tickets are sold out</a>. Like really sold out. </p>

<p>Organizers are currently saying that they will not release any more tickets, and they will not extend the show. Additionally, they discourage walk-ups, so you aren&apos;t getting in by just showing up. Not even for old time&apos;s sake. (UPDATE: Organizers are now telling us that &quot;folks can swing by during our daytime hours, 11-5 p.m. - Fri, Sat, Sun, and if we&apos;re not completely packed we&apos;ll be allowing ticket sales at the door.&quot;)</p>

<p>The best we can do is give you this look inside&#x2014;these are our photos from last night&apos;s preview and opening. And, even better, if you see something you like, the entire exhibit will be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151118101358/http://www.spoke-art.com/">brought online</a> on Monday, August 17th, where you&apos;ll be able to purchase pieces.</p>

<p>There are over 70 artists represented on the walls, all working in various mediums&#x2014;you&apos;ll find paintings, sculptures, prints, that Scalamandre wallpaper, and more.</p>
