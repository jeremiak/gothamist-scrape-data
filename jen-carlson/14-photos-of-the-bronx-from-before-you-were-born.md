</div></div>					<p>We&apos;ve revisited <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522014219/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/02/14_photos_of_brooklyn_before_you_we.php#photo-1">Brooklyn</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522014219/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/05/14_photos_of_manhattan_before_you_w.php#photo-1">Manhattan</a> in our &quot;before you were born&quot; photo series, and next up we visit the Bronx. Here are some photos of the borough before you were born <em>and</em> before it was burning. It&apos;s also nice to look back at this borough <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150522014219/http://gothamist.com/2010/06/16/flashback_the_bronx.php#photo-1">before Robert Moses</a> &quot;slammed his fist into the neighborhood in the 1940s and 1950s, &apos;clearing the slums,&apos; stacking the displaced in spiritless public housing towers, and sealing them behind a rampart of expressways: the Bruckner to the east and the Major Deegan to the south and west.&quot;</p>
