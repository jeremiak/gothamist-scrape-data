</div></div>					<p>Tomorrow morning <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429053752/http://momath.org/">The National Museum of Mathematics</a> (MoMath) will officially open in Manhattan, and will be the nation&apos;s only museum dedicated to math. MoMath, located at 11 East 26th Street near Madison Square Park, will provide hands-on math programming and exhibits, all highlighting the WONDERS OF MATH. The museum will be of interest to all ages, as well as &quot;those who are math-challenged, and math enthusiasts of all backgrounds and levels of understanding.&quot; (But probably not to those who just like to read and draw and use their right brain.)</p>

<p>Museum co-founder Cindy Lawrence <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429053752/http://www.newscientist.com/blogs/culturelab/2012/12/momath-manhattan-museum-of-mathematics.html">says</a>, &quot;We want to show a different side of mathematics. Our goal is to get kids excited, and show them the math they&apos;re doing in school is just one tree in a whole huge forest.&quot;</p>

<p>Meet us at the Mathenaeum, nerds! </p>
