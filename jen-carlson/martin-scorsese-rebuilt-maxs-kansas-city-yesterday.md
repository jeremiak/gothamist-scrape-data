					<p>Yesterday afternoon the iconic Max&apos;s Kansas City <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007080407/https://twitter.com/jonah_green/status/627165527572660225">was rebuilt</a>, right around the corner from the where the original stood. By sundown, the cast and crew for Martin Scorsese&apos;s upcoming HBO series brought the replica to life. The show, which has previously been listed under <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007080407/http://gothamist.com/2015/07/24/scorsese_rock_drama.php"><em>Rock n&apos; Roll</em></a>, but now appears to be called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007080407/http://www.imdb.com/title/tt3186130/"><em>Vinyl</em></a>, is set in the 1970s and focuses on music exec and fictional record label called American Century Records. The series counts Mick Jagger as a producer, and stars Bobby Cannavale, Ray Romano, Andrew Dice Clay, James Jagger (the son of Jerry Hall and Mick), and Olivia Wilde.</p>

<p><a href="www.maxskansascity.com">Max&apos;s Kansas City</a> had 3 incarnations, and 2 addresses, if you count the failed attempt at revival in the 1990s. The original was at 213 Park Avenue South, from 1965 to 1974, and 1975 to 1981 (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007080407/http://www.nytimes.com/2003/05/11/nyregion/neighborhood-report-union-square-archetypal-host.html">fun fact</a>: Ed Koch had a campaign office upstairs); the second at 240 West 52d Street (1998 to... 1998). The HBO show&apos;s set was on 19th Street and Broadway, around the corner from the original Max&apos;s.</p>

</p><p>Along with the facade, the exterior of the set featured old cars, including the &quot;official pace car Mercury Comet Cyclone GT&quot; (which was actually a pace car for the 1966 Indianapolis 500), and a newsstand with of-the-era covers from LIFE, Penthouse, Telegraph, and Ebony magazines.</p>

<p>During its peak, Max&apos;s saw acts like the Ramones, Springsteen, the Velvet Underground, and many more. There are very few details that have been made public about Scorsese&apos;s show so far, so it&apos;s unclear what year the cast and crew stepped into during last night&apos;s filming.</p>
