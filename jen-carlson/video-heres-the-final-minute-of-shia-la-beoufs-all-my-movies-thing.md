<p>Just before 9 p.m. Thursday night, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160507201630/http://gothamist.com/tags/shialabeouf">Shia LaBeouf</a> ended his 2.5 day residency at the Angelika Film Center, where he&apos;s been holding court since Tuesday, just before noon. The 29-year-old actor has now completed his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160507201630/http://gothamist.com/tags/allmymovies">#AllMyMovies art project thing</a>, having watched every flick he&apos;s starred in in reverse chronological order&#x2014;from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160507201630/http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2461520/?ref_=nm_flmg_act_2"><em>Man Down</em></a> (2015) to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160507201630/http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0157675/?ref_=nm_flmg_act_49"><em>Breakfast With Einstein</em></a> (1998). </p>

<p>The entire thing was livestreamed, with a camera focused on LaBeouf&apos;s face, allowing all to see the laughter, tears, yawns, boredom, and pizza. He ended the performance with a brisk escape from his seat, and applause from the crowd&#x2014;DID YOU MISS IT? You can watch it below (and you can rewatch <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160507201630/http://newhive.com/allmymovies">the entire stream here</a> if you wanted to for some reason? Why?)</p>
