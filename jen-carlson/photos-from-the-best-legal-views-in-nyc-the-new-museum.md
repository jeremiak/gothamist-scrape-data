					<p>The views around New York City&#x2014;whether you&apos;re on the ground or 100-floors over it, whether you&apos;re a tourist or native&#x2014;are spectacular to behold, and you don&apos;t need to be an urban explorer leaning precariously over a ledge to enjoy some of the best ones. We&apos;ve been visiting some of the highest legally-accessed views around Manhattan, previously making stops at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170451/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/11/legal_highs_1wtc.php#photo-1">1WTC</a>, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170451/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/12/top_of_the_rock_views.php#photo-1">Top of the Rock</a>, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170451/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/13/legal_highs_times_square_hyatt.php#photo-1">the tallest hotel rooftop lounge</a> in the city. Today, we&apos;re heading to the Bowery for a look at one of the New Museum&apos;s permanent installations: its view.</p>

<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170451/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/03/goodbye_tod_all_that.php">Tod Seelie</a> recently captured the golden hour up on the New Museum&apos;s 7th floor&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170451/http://www.newmuseum.org/building">the Sky Room</a>&#x2014;which offers panoramic views from a more tucked away, lower-level vantage point. Bonus: You can enjoy the views from indoors <em>or</em> outdoors, as the space has floor-to-ceiling glass walls, and a terrace running along the east and south sides of the building.</p>

<p>Click through to check out the layers you get from a rooftop-view on the Lower East Side.</p>
