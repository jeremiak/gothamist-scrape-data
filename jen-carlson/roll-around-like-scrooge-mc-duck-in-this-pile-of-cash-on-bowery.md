</div></div>					<p>There are eight more days left of the Stefan Bondell &quot;Black Box&quot; exhibit at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150506162759/http://theholenyc.com/2013/12/13/stefan-bondell-the-black-box-opening-black-friday/">the Hole</a> on Bowery, which features large-scale paintings <em>but also</em> $1 million in US currency shredded and displayed on the floors. Certainly there is symbolic meaning to all of this, but it&apos;s also a good opportunity to empty your soul and roll around in money like Scrooge McDuck. &apos;Tis the season, and all that.</p>

<p>Photographer Nic Garcia was there today, and tells us, &quot;You can walk all over it and do what you want, nobody watching, no crowds.&quot; He also says it smells like money. Not like your sad tin of dimes and nickels, either... real Fat Cat money.</p>

<p>The exhibit has, fittingly, been up since Black Friday, and remains open through December 28th. To further take away from the artist&apos;s commentary on putting the financial side of the art market underfoot, we offer you this song:</p>
