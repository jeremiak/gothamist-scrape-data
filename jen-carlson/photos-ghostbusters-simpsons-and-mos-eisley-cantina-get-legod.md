</div></div>					<p>While some Lego artists <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410034907/http://gothamist.com/2013/02/21/ghostbuster_legos.php">have independently recreated Ghostbusters HQ</a> with the bricks, now the company has unveiled their official kit, which will be on sale in June. They showed off their Ghostbusters kit ($49.99), along with other new releases, at the New York Toy Fair over the weekend. </p>

<p>The Ghostbusters kit was designed by fan Brent Waller, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410034907/http://www.pocket-lint.com/news/127284-lego-ghostbusters-price-and-release-date-revealed-cheaper-than-lego-simpsons">who said</a>: &quot;As a child I would recreate Ghostbusters out of Lego. I had a very primitive Ecto-1, Ecto-1A and the Firehouse as well as all of the main characters with &apos;Classic Space&apos; air-tanks for Proton Packs. As an adult rediscovering my love of Lego, recreating it now was a way of recapturing that unbridled creativity of my youth.&quot;</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140410034907/http://www2.toyassociation.org/AM/Template.cfm?Section=tf_Home">The Fair</a> runs through the 19th, and the Lego folk also have The Simpsons, Star Wars&apos; Mos Eisley Cantina, and (Michael Bay&apos;s version of) the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles on view at their stand. Click through for a preview.</p>
