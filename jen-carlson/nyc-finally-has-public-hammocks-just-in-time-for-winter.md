					<p>A couple of years ago we requested one simple thing: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030182056/http://gothamist.com/2014/07/22/nyc_needs_more_hammocks.php">more public hammocks in NYC</a>. At the time, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030182056/http://gothamist.com/tags/governorsisland">Governors Island</a> just built a &quot;Hammock Grove,&quot; featuring a few hammocks suspended over a section of grassy lawn. But why not have hammocks in the parks, we wondered. </p>

<p>Late last month, the Flatiron Sky-Line was unveiled, the winner of the Flatiron Public Plaza Holiday Design Competition, an annual affair that awards the best design with a temporary installation. This year&apos;s installation features &quot;ten large contiguous arches constructed of white powder-coated steel tubes with LED lights,&quot; as well as hammocks suspended from the arches. </p>

<p>HAMMOCKS! The hammocks are here. Just in time for winter.</p>

<p><iframe src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171030182056if_/https://www.facebook.com/plugins/video.php?href=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.facebook.com%2FFlatironNY%2Fvideos%2F10154664102369120%2F&amp;width=640&amp;show_text=false&amp;appId=268908126463230&amp;height=343" width="640" height="343" style="border:none;overflow:hidden" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowtransparency="true"></iframe></p>

<p>Yeah, so, as you may know, it&apos;s December. And December in NYC is not exactly hammock season, but if you think a little chill in the air is gonna stop New Yorkers from partaking in some much-needed hammock time, you are very wrong. Especially since it&apos;s gonna look so great on your Instagram.</p>
