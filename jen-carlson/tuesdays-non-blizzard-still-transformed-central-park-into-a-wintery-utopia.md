					<p>While we didn&apos;t get as much snow as the city prepared for, Central Park did top out at around seven inches, and plenty of New Yorkers took advantage of what will probably be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170721212915/http://gothamist.com/tags/marchblizzard2017">the last big snowfall of the season</a>. </p>

<p>Photographer Scott Heins trudged through the snow and ice to capture the picturesque scenes, and noted that it was &quot;a genuinely great snow day scene in Central Park. The painful ice that had been falling in the morning subsided by afternoon and both kids and grown-ups were having a blast sledding. The pathways and bridges were filled with tourists and locals snapping photos, and a handful of cross country skiers were enjoying the fresh stuff.&quot;</p>

<p>Up next: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170721212915/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/14/de_blasio_freeze_up.php">the big freeze</a>, which will encase the landscape in ice for eternity.</p>
