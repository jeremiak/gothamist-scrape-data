                            <p>Today the Museum of the City of New York announced that they&apos;ve uploaded new photos to their archives taken by Bronx-native Arthur Rothstein, who graduated from Columbia University, where he founded the University Camera Club and was the photo editor of the Columbian. While he went on to spend years documenting rural and small-town America during the Great Depression (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130926212150/http://www.weru.ksu.edu/new_weru/multimedia/dustbowl/big/cimarron_ok.jpg">here is one of his most famous photos</a> from 1936), he eventually returned to New York City. These photos were all taken for Look magazine in 1957, and you can find more at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130926212150/http://collections.mcny.org/">the MCNY&apos;s website</a>.</p>
