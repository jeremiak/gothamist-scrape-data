					<p>The landmarks chosen to represent NYC&apos;s iconic skyline <em>in LEGO form</em> have been announced, and they are (drumroll please): the Flatiron Building, Chrysler Building, Empire State Building, One World Trade Center and the Statue of Liberty. But... why? The LEGO rep couldn&apos;t tell us why those particular structures were the ones chosen, so it remains a mystery why <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161014100609/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/25/happy_belated_birthday_woolworth_bu.php">the Woolworth Building</a> was cruelly left out.</p>

<p>LEGO does say that, despite that glaring omission, this set does &quot;capture the architectural essence of New York City.&quot; And somehow does it all in an homage that&apos;s just 10&quot; high, 9&#x201D; wide and 1&#x201D; deep. </p>

<p>This is the first time LEGO has brought entire city skylines to their architecture series, and it won&apos;t hit shelves until January 1st... so they still have time to pay respect to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161014100609/http://gothamist.com/2014/04/24/happy_101st_woolworth_building.php">Sir Woolworth</a>, who deserves better <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161014100609/https://ideas.lego.com/projects/29358">than a DIY version</a>.</p>
