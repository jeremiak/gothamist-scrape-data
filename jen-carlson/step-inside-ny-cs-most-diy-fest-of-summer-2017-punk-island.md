					<p>While Randall&apos;s Island transforms into a corporate-funded festival site every summer, there is one exception this year: the DIY fest called Punk Island. Typically the event takes place <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170619151024/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/22/punk_island_photos.php">on Staten Island</a>, but relocated its 7 stages and 100 band lineup this year. </p>

<p>Photographer Gretchen Robinette was on hand, noting that &quot;Punk Island is as DIY and anti-corporation as you would expect... tents and tarps, plastic tables and duct tape.&quot; The non-profit event is hosted by &quot;a collective of artists, musicians, and educators who have a love for all things punk and punk music,&quot; Robinette says, adding it brings &quot;like-minded individuals together to keep the punk culture alive.&quot; </p>

<p>Their website states that they &quot;aim to support a philosophy of creativity, rebellion and work to feed those who are hungry for a world where passion is valued over profit.&quot;</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="punkislandembed17.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170619151024im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/punkislandembed17.jpg" width="640" height="427"> <br> <i> (Photo by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170619151024/https://www.gretchenrobinette.com/">Gretchen Robinette</a>/Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p>Part of the DIY aspect of the event means you&apos;ve got to carry your own gear, and since no vehicles were permitted on site, the bands that participated had to lug their instruments there via the 125th street Manhattan footbridge, or take a bus.&quot; </p>
