					<p>Last weekend, just off the B/Q in Brooklyn, owners of some gorgeous old homes opened their doors to strangers during the annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220653/http://gothamist.com/tags/victorianflatbush">Victorian Flatbush</a> House Tour, reminding us that we live in a city where we will pay money to see a wrap-around porch... and driveways, garages, and what more than 600-square-feet looks like together all in one space. </p>

<p>The event was hosted by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170122220653/http://www.fdconline.org/">the Flatbush Development Corporation</a>, and the area &quot;is comprised of about twelve micro-neighborhoods, many of which are landmarked, with others seeking the protected status to preserve the remarkable streetscapes.&quot; It was developed around 1900 and billed as &quot;a piece of suburban life just minutes from Manhattan.&quot; The Vanderbilts, the Carnegies, the Gillettes, and the Guggenheims all once called it home.</p>
