					<p>The main attractions at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012122830/http://gothamist.com/tags/governorsisland">Governors Island</a> include the lush green grass, sprawling views of Manhattan, and the fact that you aren&apos;t bumping into someone every time you turn a corner. Serenity now, etc. </p>

<p>There&apos;s solitude, but there&apos;s also a lot of activity, and the latter can typically be discovered near the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012122830/http://www.figmentproject.org/">Figment</a> installations, which were just unveiled last weekend. In addition to Figment&apos;s Minigolf, Pavilion, Sculpture Garden and Treehouse, there are a few new projects which will be on view through September 25th. Click through for a look at this weekend&apos;s action, and scroll down for the new pieces. Might we recommend that next year they <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012122830/http://gothamist.com/2014/07/22/abandoned_governors_island_1.php">recreate the island&apos;s old Burger King</a>?</p>

<p>As a reminder: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012122830/http://www.nps.gov/gois/planyourvisit/hours.htm">Governors Island</a> is opened 7 days a week, through September 27th, from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. (and 7 p.m. on weekends). Tickets range from free, to $2, to $20 for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012122830/http://govisland.com/info/ferry-schedule">a season pass</a>.</p>
