					<p>On July 10th, 1975, the NY Times <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170314065317/http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=9E00E6D6153DEF31A25753C1A9619C946490D6CF">printed a photo</a> showing visitors at the a minipark behind <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170314065317/http://www.pocketparksnyc.com/the-blog/waterfall-behind-mcgraw-hill-building">the McGraw-Hill Building</a>, &quot;emerging from a 17 1/2-foot-long glass tunnel that runs beneath a waterfall at site.&quot; 40 Years later and that waterfall is still there, partially housed in plexiglass at 1221 6th Avenue, between 48th and 49th Streets. While walking through the tunnel won&apos;t cool you down, you can attempt to get some backsplash if you lean in close enough at certain areas. </p>

<p><iframe frameborder="0" width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20170314065317if_/http://www.dailymotion.com/embed/video/x2zobt5" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Check out the urban oasis, which isn&apos;t too far from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170314065317/http://www.timessquarenyc.org/entertainment-attractions/392/waterfall/details.aspx#.VbkEszBVhHw">Times Square</a>... and on some days, during the lunch rush, can get almost as crowded. Just like <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170314065317/http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=9402E6D71131E632A25754C2A9609C946990D6CF">back in 1978</a>, when the Times ran a photo spread showing the &quot;charming&quot; park filling up on a hot summer day.</p>

<p>The water feature itself is about 40-feet-wide, and 20-feet-tall.</p>
