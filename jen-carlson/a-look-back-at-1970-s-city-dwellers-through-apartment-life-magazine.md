</div></div>					<p>Recently we came across some old Apartment Life magazines online&#x2014;the publication that later became <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502104816/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolitan_Home">Metropolitan Home</a>, part of Hachette Filipacchi Media, and folded in 2010. But in the 1970s and early &apos;80s (it was published from 1974 through 1981) it thrived&#x2014;according to an old article from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502104816/http://my-retrospace.blogspot.com/2010/08/vintage-scan-21-1977-apartment-life.html">RetroSpace</a>, during its first incarnation as Apartment Life it &quot;perfectly encapsulated the culture, the fashion, the look and the feel of the time.&quot; Click through for a look back at how urbanites lived decades ago... what&apos;s not to love about <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502104816/http://gothamist.com/2011/10/03/brooklyn_apartments.php#photo-1">time traveling apartment voyeurism</a>?</p>
