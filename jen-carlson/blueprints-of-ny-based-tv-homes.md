</div></div>					<p>Recently we&apos;ve been enjoying looking inside some <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430042630/http://gothamist.com/tags/movieflashback">movie</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430042630/http://gothamist.com/tags/tvflashback">television</a> character&apos;s homes; now <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430042630/http://www.boingboing.net/2010/03/01/architectural-fan-dr.html">Boing Boing</a> points to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430042630/http://www.markmooregallery.com/artists/mark-bennett/">Mark Bennett</a>&#x2014;&quot;a compulsive television watcher in his youth, he makes careful observations of the sets inhabited by popular TV shows, transforming them into fully realized architectural drawings.&quot; His blueprints, some of which are published in his book <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430042630/http://www.amazon.com/TV-Sets-Fantasy-Blueprints-Classic/dp/1579121071/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&amp;s=books&amp;qid=1267544309&amp;sr=8-2-spell"><em>TV Sets</em></a>, include the Jeffersons&apos; home, Uncle Bill&apos;s home from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430042630/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bdjcE1VA8Xs"><em>Family Affair</em></a>, Bruce Wayne&apos;s mansion in Gotham and Lucy and Ricky Ricardo&apos;s digs.</p>
