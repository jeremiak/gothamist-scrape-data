					<p>Tomorrow, the New-York Historical Society will unveil a treasure trove of historic comic book stuff&#x2014;from Adam West&apos;s Batmobile to early drawings of all your favorite superheroes. </p>

<p>Walking into &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160630053012/https://www.nyhistory.org/exhibitions/superheroes-gotham">Superheroes In Gotham</a>,&quot; you&apos;ll be greeted by the Batmobile in the museum&apos;s lobby, an authentic set piece driven by Adam West in the 1960s television show. In order to get the custom 1966 Ford Galaxy in there, several glass panels from the building&apos;s entrance had to be removed. #WorthIt.</p>

<p>Next you&apos;ll step into a room that will educate you on the origins of six superheroes: Superman, Batman, Iron Man, Wonder Woman, Captain America, and Spiderman. Given the exhibit is at the Historical Society, there is a huge emphasis put on the genesis of these characters, though it will also include more recent characters inspired by a more contemporary Gotham City. As a whole, the exhibit contemplates &quot;the importance of New York as a creative force behind this uniquely American mythology.&quot; After all, the art form and cultural phenomenon was born in New York City.</p>

<p>Click through for a preview of the expansive exhibit, where you&apos;ll also find old issues like Amazing Fantasy #15, Superman&apos;s original costume from the 1950s TV show, and a typewriter used by Jerry Siegel to create the Man of Steel&apos;s original story lines. Many of these items are normally nearly impossible to see, so you&apos;ll want to <em>POP!</em> (sorry) over there before February 21st to take it all in.</p>
