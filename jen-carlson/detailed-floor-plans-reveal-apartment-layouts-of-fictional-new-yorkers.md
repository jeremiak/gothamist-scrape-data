</div></div>					<p>This certainly <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429074248/http://gothamist.com/2010/03/02/blueprints.php#photo-1">isn&apos;t the first time</a> that floor plans have been drafted to show the layouts of some of the more famous on-screen homes, but it&apos;s always fun to see in black &amp; white (or in this case pastels) how accurate or inaccurate the entertainment industry is when it comes to New York City living spaces. Click through to see the floor plans for the apartments of Jerry Seinfeld, Monica and Rachel, Joey and Chandler, Holly Golightly, Ricky and Lucy, Will and Grace (and Jack), Ted Mosby (though not his apartment from this season), and of course Carrie Bradshaw. These were created by artist <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429074248/http://nikneuk.deviantart.com/">I&#xF1;aki Aliste Lizarralde</a>, who is also selling the prints <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429074248/http://www.etsy.com/shop/TVFLOORPLANSandMORE">on Etsy</a>&#x2014;did she get everything right?</p>
