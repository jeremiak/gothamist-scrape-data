</div></div>					<p>We first heard about The Queens High Line, or <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501193734/http://gothamist.com/tags/queensway">the Queensway</a>, in 2011 when <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501193734/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/22/queenss_high_line_the_queensway_inc.php#photo-1">it was proposed</a> that an abandoned 3.5-mile stretch of the LIRR Rockaway Beach line become a park... one that we hope one day becomes home <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501193734/http://standardculture.com/lebain">to a sick hotel rooftop bar</a>. Since then, the Trust For Public Land has been leading the way to make the project happen, but it&apos;s <a 29="" 2013="" href="" http:="" gothamist.com="" 01="" not_everyones_so_crazy_about_the_qu.php"="">been criticized</a> by those who want to make the line active again. However, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501193734/http://www.queenstribune.com/2013Weekly/Trib%20Web%20060613/Deadline_060613_QueensWayDesignToBeChosenLaterThisMonth.html">later this month</a> the non-profit will narrow down the contenders who submitted designs for the park proposal.</p>

<p>Before that happens, get a look at the outdoor space as is&#x2014;recently <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501193734/http://gothamist.com/tags/jeffseal">Jeff Seal</a> walked the entire length of the QueensWay, from Rego Park to Ozone Park, and filmed his adventure through a territory that hasn&apos;t been fully explored much lately. (When <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501193734/http://abandonednyc.com/2013/05/05/a-baseball-graveyard-in-queens/">Abandoned NY</a> went through, an old baseball field was discovered.) Here&apos;s what he saw:</p>
