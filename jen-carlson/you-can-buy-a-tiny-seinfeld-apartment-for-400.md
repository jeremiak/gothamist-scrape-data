					<p>The <em>Seinfeld</em> apartment is one of the more realistic <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170409215214/http://gothamist.com/2011/05/10/nyc_as_seen_on_tv_top_5_worst.php#photo-1">New York City television apartments</a>&#x2014;a pretty standard, no-frills one-bedroom, featuring a basic couch, basic decorations, and one not-very-basic neighbor. Classic NYC living. And now you can own a tiny version of it to put inside your own realistic NYC apartment. BYO-doll...</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170409215214if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/8gbM2Kr3RdI" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>According <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170409215214/http://laughingsquid.com/a-tiny-detailed-replica-of-jerry-seinfelds-apartment/">to Laughing Squid</a>, &quot;artist Alan Wolfson worked with the original production designer of <em>Seinfeld</em>, Thomas Azzari, to create a tiny and highly detailed replica of Jerry Seinfeld&#x2018;s iconic apartment.&quot; (People love recreating this apartment&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170409215214/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/24/seinfeld_apartment_reality_tour.php#photo-1">in 2015</a>, Hulu built a life-size version.) It&apos;ll cost you about as much as that couch from IKEA, though&#x2014;he&apos;s now accepting pre-orders, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170409215214/https://seinfeldsetreplica.com/products/seinfeld-set-replica">the price tag is $399.99</a>. They will ship in June of this year, about 27 years after the show premiered.</p>

<p>It doesn&apos;t feature <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170409215214/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/01/seinfeld_wall.php">that wall you never saw</a>, but it does have: &quot;100 unique miniature elements&#x2014;including the furniture, bicycle on the wall, lighting fixtures and even a working version of Jerry&#x2019;s front door.&quot; Here&apos;s a walk-through: </p>
