</div></div>					<p>While it wasn&apos;t the epic <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150506012722/http://gothamist.com/tags/amnestyinternational">Amnesty International</a> concert of yesteryear, last night&apos;s resurrection of the big events (which took place in the &apos;80s and &apos;90s) did bring two formerly jailed members of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150506012722/http://gothamist.com/tags/pussyriot">Pussy Riot</a> to Brooklyn, alongside their champion Madonna. The latter was not there to perform, but to introduce the members of the band, who spoke through a translator before leaving Barclays Center (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150506012722/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/02/07/arts/music/members-of-russian-punk-band-get-warm-greeting-in-brooklyn.html?_r=0">reportedly</a> the trio went to have dinner together). Their message: &#x201C;Freedom is not a given. It&#x2019;s something we have to fight for.&#x201D;</p>

<p><iframe src="//web.archive.org/web/20150506012722if_/http://player.vimeo.com/video/86030501?color=d1d1d1" width="640" height="360" frameborder="0" webkitallowfullscreen="" mozallowfullscreen="" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>But the other members of Pussy Riot are lambasting Nadezhda Tolokonnikova and Maria Alyokhina for taking part in the concert, writing on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150506012722/http://pussy-riot.livejournal.com/34528.html">their website</a> that selling tickets to a show &quot;is highly contradictory to the principles of Pussy Riot. We&apos;re a female separatist collective. We never accept money for our performances... we only stage illegal performances in unexpected public places.&quot; They added that &quot;Masha and Nadia are out of the group.&quot;</p>

<p>As for the rest of the show, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150506012722/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/pussy-riot-inspire-brooklyn-at-amnesty-international-concert-20140206">Rolling Stone</a> reports that it lacked in &quot;revolutionary energy,&quot; but it wasn&apos;t without some fleeting highlights from the likes of Debbie Harry, Flaming Lips, Yoko Ono, and of course the supportive crowd, who chanted: &quot;Russia will be free,&quot; over and over.</p>
