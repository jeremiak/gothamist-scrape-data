</div></div>					<p>In the past, our very own <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141011003420/http://gothamist.com/2011/05/27/flashback_high_line_2002.php">Jake Dobkin has explored</a> the undeveloped parts of the High Line, a little bit... illegally. But over the weekend, the final phase (Phase III) was opened up to Open House NY visitors. They don&apos;t want their thunder stolen by the new <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141011003420/http://gothamist.com/2011/09/19/forget_the_high_line_say_hello_to_t.php"><em>Low</em> Line</a>, after all. </p>

<p>This section of the line runs from 30th to 34th Streets, and is the last section to be developed. Click through for a peak courtesy of OHNY attendee <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141011003420/http://twitter.com/#!/RolandoPujol">Rolando Pujol</a>, for a look at its neglected state.</p>
