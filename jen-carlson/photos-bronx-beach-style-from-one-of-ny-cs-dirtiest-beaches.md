</div></div>					<p>Yesterday the Natural Resources Defense Council <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429214252/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/26/beaches_1.php">released a report card</a> for New York City beaches, handing out zero gold stars, and declaring the dirtiest beaches of all to be in the Bronx, with 15% of water samples violating health standards. Words like sewage, stomach flu, and hepatitis were tossed around, and yet people still flock there on hot summer days. So in that spirit, we&apos;re going to LA LA LA ignore all of that, and take a look at a crowded Orchard Beach, which used to be referred to as the Bronx Riviera, and has more recently been dubbed &#x201C;Horse Shit Beach&#x201D; by locals.</p>

<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429214252/http://charleslebrigand.com/portfolio/">Stephane Missier</a> captured some spectacular photos from one of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429214252/http://cleanoceanaction.org/fileadmin/editor_group2/General/Public_Relations/Press_Releases_2013/TTW_2013_-_Final_NY-NJ_Press_Release.pdf">our lowest graded</a> beaches last year, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429214252/http://charleslebrigand.com/2012/05/10/the-bronx-riviera/">reporting back</a> that &quot;Bronx inhabitants choose Orchard Beach due to its relatively convenient location. It&#x2019;s the quickest way for them to access the sea, sand and sun.&quot;</p>

<p>Click through for a look at some Bronx Beach Style, set against a backdrop of one of our most polluted beaches.</p>
