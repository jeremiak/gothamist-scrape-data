<p>Remember when it snowed so much you could build entire forts larger than your studio apartment? Times <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503044757/http://gothamist.com/tags/climatechange">have changed</a>, and if you want to see it in fast-motion, look no further than <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503044757/http://controlgeek.net/">John Huntington</a>, who has been documenting NYC&apos;s snowfalls for as long as we can remember. During that time the feet of snow have diminished to inches, and now centimeters. Just <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503044757/https://vimeo.com/19263336">look at this timelapse</a> he made during the January of 2011 snowstorm... and now see the below one, captured on Friday:</p>
