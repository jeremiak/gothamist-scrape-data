</div></div>					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429184909/http://gothamist.com/tags/brooklynbridgepark">Brooklyn Bridge Park</a> didn&apos;t begin construction until just a few years ago, in 2008. Since then, it&apos;s transformed into a gorgeous oasis with expansive views of Lower Manhattan, with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429184909/http://gothamist.com/2013/03/21/this_brooklyn_pedestrian_bridge_wil.php#photo-1">a pedestrian bridge</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429184909/http://gothamist.com/2012/12/13/photos_pier_5_opens_in_brooklyn_bri.php#photo-1">soccer fields</a>, and perfect picnic spots. But what did it look like in the mid-1970s, before residents of DUMBO even started to call the neighborhood by that acronym? (That didn&apos;t happen until around 1978.) Click through for a look of the Brooklyn Bridge Park area of Fulton Ferry in 1975, courtesy of the Brooklyn Historic Society and their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429184909/http://gothamist.com/2013/02/28/brooklyn_historic_photo_archive.php">amazing new archives</a> at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429184909/http://www.brooklynvisualheritage.org/">Brooklyn Visual Heritage</a>.</p>
