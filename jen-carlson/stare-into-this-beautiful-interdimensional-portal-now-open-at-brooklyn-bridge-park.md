					<p>Anish Kapoor&apos;s &quot;most viscerally arresting installation&quot;&#x2014;titled &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170504185531/http://gothamist.com/2017/02/20/portal_coming_soon.php">Descension</a>&quot;&#x2014;is now installed in Brooklyn Bridge Park after weeks and weeks of digging. How deep did they dig? To Hades? To the C train? Please don&apos;t try to find out. Just let the continuously swirling water hypnotize you from behind the barricades, because maybe it&apos;s not an interdimensional portal, maybe it&apos;s just your typical Brooklyn hellmouth.</p>

<div style="position:relative;height:0;padding-bottom:56.25%"><iframe src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170504185531if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/dYVWtrBFuPQ?ecver=2" width="640" height="360" frameborder="0" style="position:absolute;width:100%;height:100%;left:0" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>

<p>This gurgling beast is 26 feet in diameter, and the official unveiling of the installation, presented by the Public Art Fund, took place yesterday. You&apos;ll find it in the park&apos;s Pier 1 (right near the ferry landing) through September 10th.</p>

<p>This is the first time &quot;Descension&quot; has been installed in North America, but you may have seen Kapoor&apos;s work here before&#x2014;in 2006 he brought <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170504185531/https://www.publicartfund.org/view/exhibitions/5775_sky_mirror">his Sky Mirror to Midtown</a>.</p>
