</div></div>					<p>Not too long ago, we <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429112511/http://gothamist.com/2011/04/19/is_this_the_most_vertigo-inducing_p.php">visited five vertigo-inducing photos</a> taken in NYC over the decades. Soon after, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429112511/http://gothamist.com/2011/08/12/rooftopping.php">rooftopping</a> was declared the latest <em>thing</em> by The Internet, and we got visually dizzy once again. And now, we revisit it all with a series of photos by Navid Baraty, who has been working on a project called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429112511/http://www.navidbaraty.com/gallery.php?gallery_id=13">Intersections</a>. His photos are taken from many, many stories above street level, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429112511/http://www.mymodernmet.com/profiles/blogs/navid-baraty-nyc-intersections">Baraty says</a> he took them by &quot;leaning over the edge of buildings, by extending my arms out if necessary.&quot;</p>
