</div></div>					<p>In 2009 we looked back at the Miss Subways of yesteryear, as the ol&apos; gals gathered for a bit <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164534/http://gothamist.com/2009/05/10/meet_miss_subways.php#photo-1">of a reunion</a> when photographer Fiona Gardner and journalist Amy Zimmer got them together to gather their stories, along with some new portraits. Now they&apos;ve funded the resulting book <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164534/http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/27932455/meet-miss-subways-new-yorks-beauty-queens-1941-197">via Kickstarter</a>, and the Transit Museum is hosting a new exhibition called &quot;Meet Miss Subways: New York&#x2019;s Beauty Queens 1941-76,&quot; which will open on October 23rd.</p>

<blockquote>&quot;For over thirty years, photos and biographies of New York women were displayed in subway cars as part of the &#x201C;Meet Miss Subways&#x201D; advertising campaign and beauty pageant. Young women of mostly middle and working class backgrounds were selected, not just for their looks, but for their hopes and aspirations. &apos;Meet Miss Subways: New York&#x2019;s Beauty Queens 1941-76&apos; will look beyond the pretty faces and pageant sashes to expose a fascinating and invaluable record of the changes and challenges which have shaped New York women. Originally conceived as a way to draw attention to nearby ads for chewing gum, tobacco and other products, the contest became a compelling platform for civil rights debates in the city.&quot;</blockquote>

<p>While the program ended in 1976, it was brought back in 2004 to honor the 100th anniversary of the subways. Click through for a little sneak peek at the exhibit, which will also include <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164534/http://www.mta.info/mta/museum/programs.htm">a few events</a>.</p>
