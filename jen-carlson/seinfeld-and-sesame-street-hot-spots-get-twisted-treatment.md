</div></div><p>Ever find Sesame Street to be a little <em>too</em> happy? Well artist Tim Doyle has recreated it, and other television show landscapes, with a dark twist. Click through for some of his &quot;Unreal Estate&quot; pieces, where you&apos;ll see Hooper&apos;s Store, <em>Seinfeld</em>, and even <em>All in the Family</em> spots like you&apos;ve never seen them before. More of his work can be seen <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120203082001/http://www.flickr.com/photos/75435172@N00/sets/72157628830333881/">right here</a>, and hopefully he&apos;ll bring his show (currently in San Francisco) to New York, along with some more <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120203082001/http://gothamist.com/tags/nycasseenontv">NYC-based pieces</a>. [via <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120203082001/http://www.buzzfeed.com/whitneyjefferson/unreal-estate-art-gives-television-landmarks-a-d">Buzzfeed</a>]</p>
