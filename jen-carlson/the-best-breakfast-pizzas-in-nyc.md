										<p>If I were to open a restaurant, it would be a slice joint with one option: put an egg on it. Why should the eggs be reserved for $22 brick-oven pies? When you add an egg to any dish, you get more flavor, more texture, and more protein. Plus it looks pretty, a bonus only if you&apos;re into Instagramming your food. While the gold standard pizza egg is sunny-side up, you can also go with poached, over easy, hard or soft boiled, or scrambled. Of course, there are other ways to bring pizza to the breakfast table: smoked salmon, maple syrup, and even cream cheese. Below, some of our favorite brunch pies in NYC.</p>

<p><strong>TRUFFLE EGG PIZZA @ PIZZA BEACH, $18</strong></p>

<p>Pizza Beach is killing the breakfast pizza game&#x2014;they&apos;ve got a Bacon, Egg and Cheese Pizza; an Avocado Pizza; a Lox &amp; Cream Cheese Pizza; and a Truffled Egg Pizza. Tip: for the egg pizzas, order extra eggs, as they each only come with one. You&apos;ll also get a &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://gothamist.com/2015/02/11/pizza_beach_okay.php#photo-1">&apos;90s retro beachside cafe</a>&quot; vibe if you consume this on site.</p>

<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://pizzabeachclub.com/">Pizza Beach</a> is located at 167 Orchard Street on the LES, and 1426 3rd Avenue on the UES</em></p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-none"> <img alt="2017_02_carbpizza2.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/jen/2017_02_carbpizza2.jpg" width="640" height="484"> <br> <i> The patate alla carbonara pizza at Marta (Jen Chung / Gothamist)</i></div> <p></p>

<p><strong>PATATE ALLA CARBONARA @ MARTA, $23</strong></p>

<p>The Union Square Hospitality Group&apos;s two-year-old restaurant in NoMad features <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://gothamist.com/2014/09/11/danny_meyer_pizza_marta.php#photo-1">Roman-style pizzas with super-thin crusts</a>. A standout is the <em>pizza biacnhe</em> selection, &quot;patata alla carbonara&quot; ($23, available at brunch, lunch and dinner). That&apos;s right&#x2014;it&apos;s all the goodies from pasta alla carbonara (plus potatoes), but on a pizza.<br>
There&apos;s guanciale, black pepper, egg and potato, with a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://ny.eater.com/2014/10/3/6900399/nick-anderer-makes-a-potato-pizza-with-a-carbonara-kick-at-marta#the-ingredients">coddled egg</a> poured over the pizza after cooking. The result is a luscious slice that will have you reaching for a second (or wondering why you offered to share the whole pie with the rest of your party). Bonus: You can add an egg to any pizza for $3.  &#x2014; <em>Jen Chung</em></p>

<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://www.martamanhattan.com/menus/#brunch">Marta</a> is located at The Redbury Hote at 29 E 29th Street in the Flatiron</em></p>

<strong>DUCK EGG PIZZA @ LEUCA, $17</strong>
<br><br>
Andrew Carmellini&apos;s hotel restaurant sets its breakfast pie apart by eschewing the traditional chicken&apos;s egg in favor of one from a duck. At brunch, their Graham Avenue pie stars a golden duck egg at its center, flanked by bacon, sprigs of fresh rosemary and slices of nutty Grana Padano cheese. The weighty toppings are anchored by tomato sauce and baked until browned and blistered. &#x2014; <em>Nell Casey</em>

<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://leuca.com/">Leuca</a> is located inside the William Vale Hotel at 111 North 12th Street in Williamsburg</em></p>

<p><strong>OFF-THE-MENU SMOKED SALMON PIZZA @ GRISTMILL, $28</strong> </p>

<p>Park Slope&apos;s Gristmill knows what&apos;s up&#x2014;they added a whole brunch pizza program to their weekend menu <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/02/weekend_food_roundup_95.php">recently</a>. Their Smoked Salmon Pizza is baked in the wood-fired oven with a blend of house made ricotta &amp; Ben&apos;s cream cheese and topped with Acme smoked salmon, fresh lemon, and dill. They&apos;ve also got a bacon and potato option. Sadly, the restaurant didn&apos;t have any photographic evidence of the pizzas, and you probably won&apos;t see them on the menu either, we&apos;re told: &quot;The Smoked Salmon Pizza is still available, just off the menu.&quot;</p>

<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://www.gristmillbk.com/">Gristmill</a> is located at 289 5th Avenue in Park Slope</em></p>

<p><strong>FUNGHIi Y UOVO @ LADY&apos;S, $18</strong> </p>

<p>This Downtown Brooklyn newcomer has become the default dining spot for people heading to a performance at nearby BAM and their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://gothamist.com/2017/02/24/weekend_food_roundup_maple_pizza.php">newly launched weekend brunch</a> provides an excellent pre-matinee meal. They&apos;re offering two egg-based pizzas on the weekends, including a vegetarian Funghi y Uovo ($18) made with piles of sauteed mushrooms seasoned with thyme and a freshly-cracked hen egg that&apos;s just barely baked. If a BEC-style brunch beckons, they&apos;ve also got a Bacon, Egg, and Cheese pie ($18) topped with Mozarella di Bufala, country ham and a hen&apos;s egg. </p>

<p>For the egg-averse, Lady&apos;s also offers a Maple Butter pie ($13) seasoned with Calabrian chilies and sea salt. &#x2014; <em>Nell Casey</em></p>

<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/https://ladysnyc.com/">Lady&apos;s</a> is located at 572 Fulton Street in Downtown Brooklyn</em></p>

<strong>PURGATORIO PIZZA @ BARANO, $20</strong> 
<br><br>
Taking <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/02/barano_italian_south_williamsburg.php#photo-1">scissors to your pizza</a> made this Williamsburg Italian eatery pop when it first opened in 2016, but gimmicks aside, their pies are a reason to rise early on a Saturday, in particular their messy Purgatorio Pizza&#x2014;though we wish it were spicy like the name suggests. Regardless, it&apos;s a pie packed with flavors including charred onions, salty Parmigiano Reggiono, and sliced prosciutto. Farm eggs make this a breakfast pie, but be forewarned: they&apos;ll run all over the place the moment your shears hit that runny yolk. &#x2014; <em>Nell Casey</em>

<strong>THE RITZ @ SPEEDY ROMEO, $20</strong>
<br><br>
Speedy Romeo isn&apos;t afraid to put anything on a pizza (have you seen <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/https://www.instagram.com/p/BQ4QA9qBwaK/">their Katz&apos;s pastrami pie</a>?), so they were quick to the breakfast pizza trend, adding The Ritz to their menu. The pie is basically a hangover sandwich, featuring Taylor ham, egg, and cheese.

<p><em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://www.speedyromeo.com/">Speedy Romeo</a> is located at 376 Classon Avenue in Clinton Hill, and 63 Clinton Street on the LES</em></p>

<p><strong>BREAKFAST PIZZA @ WOLFGANG PUCK EXPRESS, $14+</strong></p>

<p>Sure, airports are trying to upgrade their dining options for travelers with Shake Shacks and... <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/28/cbgb_newark_airport.php">CBGBs</a>, but my culinary expectations are extremely low when I&apos;m flying. That&apos;s why one of my most satisfying memories of airport dining is the classic breakfast pizza I ordered from the Wolfgang Puck Express at O&apos;Hare. It&apos;s eggs, bacon and a ton of cheese; it&apos;s probably AWFUL for you if you eat the whole thing but it comes out of the oven hot and what else do you really need? Also you don&apos;t have to worry about what snacks the plane might have because this will definitely send you into a food coma. &#x2014; <em>Jen Chung</em></p>

<p><em>Wolfgang Puck Express is at various airports, including <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170630063828/https://www.yelp.com/biz/wolfgang-puck-gourmet-express-new-york">JFK Terminal 7</a></em></p>
