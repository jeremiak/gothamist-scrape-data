                            <p>As <em>CBGB</em>&#x2014;the feature film on the legendary venue&#x2014;arrives on the big screen, the walls of the original club have been taken out of storage for the NYC premiere. Yesterday, CBGB&apos;s new owner Tim Hayes invited us by to see the nostalgia-soaked walls. Along with Hayes, 20 year CBGB manager and booker Louise Parnassa Staley are carefully preserving and guarding the legacy of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140221071348/http://gothamist.com/tags/cbgb">the club</a> and its late founder Hilly Kristal. </p>

<p>Staley helped Kristal dismantle the interior of the club in 2006 after they were evicted by their landlord. These items were <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140221071348/http://gothamist.com/2008/11/25/cbgb.php">put into storage</a> and haven&apos;t been in public since... though the original bathrooms were rebuilt and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140221071348/http://gothamist.com/2012/06/08/cbgb_biopic_will_feature_authentic.php">used in the film</a>.</p>

<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140221071348/http://www.flickr.com/photos/jimkiernan">Jim Kiernan</a> dropped by to shoot the walls for us yesterday, which tell the club&apos;s story in layers of stickers, posters, carvings, and spray paint. Click through for a look&#x2014;and we&apos;re told &quot;on October 10th the walls, the original sound system and other items from the historic venue will be reunited with two longtime friends of CBGB: Van Toffler (MTV) and Duff McKagan (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140221071348/http://gothamist.com/2012/12/25/video_celebrate_xmas_with_acoustic.php">Guns n&apos; Roses</a>) at the Landmark Sunshine Theater on Houston Street,&quot; not far off from the original venue. At 1 p.m. that day McKagan will deliver the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140221071348/http://www.cbgb.com/conference-music.php">Musicians Keynote Address</a> for the CBGB Festival, presumably surrounded by the walls.</p>
