</div></div>					<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="bbgmap00411.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140239im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/bbgmap00411.jpg" width="350" height="289" class="image-right"> </span>Just like that, Cherry Blossom Season is almost behind us. <em>Almost</em>. This weekend the Brooklyn Botanic Garden will be hosting their annual cherry blossom festival, which typically marks the end of Hanami (the Japanese cultural tradition of enjoying each moment of the cherry blossom season!). </p>

<p>While just a few weeks ago there was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140239/http://gothamist.com/2011/04/06/cherry_blossom_watch_one_tree_peaks.php">only one tree</a> that had bloomed in the area, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140239/http://www.bbg.org/discover/cherries/">their map</a> now shows that most trees are in first bloom, and a number of them are at peak bloom. But enough talking, check out the sweet blooms for yourself, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140239/http://www.bbg.org/news/cherry_esplanade_bloom1">courtesy of BBG</a>, and get over there this weekend before the branches are bare.</p>

<p>More on this weekend&apos;s Sakura Matsuri festival (taking place this Saturday and Sunday) can be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140239/http://www.bbg.org/visit/event/sakura_matsuri_2011/">found here</a>.</p>
