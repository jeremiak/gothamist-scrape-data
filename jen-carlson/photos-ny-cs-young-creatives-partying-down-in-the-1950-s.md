</div></div>					<p>In the 1950s, LOOK Magazine documented the Art Students Leagues&apos; Dream Balls, held at places like Hotel Commodore and the Waldorf-Astoria. But it looked more like a high society gala held inside Warhol&apos;s Factory. The annual costume ball included the presentation of the queen of the ball (actress Deborah Kerr was crowned in 1954), costumes and props galore, and plenty of booze. </p>

<p>Click through and try not to even <em>think of</em> the &quot;H&quot; word, these young creatives were much more than that! <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160303034624/http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1368&amp;dat=19500418&amp;id=hTExAAAAIBAJ&amp;sjid=Jw0EAAAAIBAJ&amp;pg=5180,1100405">One newspaper</a> at the time called the balls: &quot;The Greatest Event Since The Nude Descended The Stairs,&quot; adding that it was &quot;a mad, moist, marvelous, magical, magnificent, mammoth, meaty, massive, monstrous, muscular. muliebrous, Machiavellian, multimolecular masterpiece.&quot;</p>
