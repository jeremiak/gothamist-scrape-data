<p>The Bryant Park summer movie series is one of the most well-attended outdoor movie series in the city (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160154/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/29/nyc_free_summer_movies.php">and there are a lot</a>). It also has the odd tradition of what we&apos;ll call: The Lawn Rush. At exactly 5 o&apos;clock every Monday movie night, attendees (some of whom have been sitting at the perimeter of the lawn since 3 p.m.) charge onto the grass like it&apos;s the Oklahoma Land Rush of 1889. Blankets (sometimes <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160154/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/20/outdoor_movie_etiquette.php">massive frankenblankets</a>) are thrown down to claim pieces of the lawn, which is packed in seconds.</p>

<p>Last night the summer series <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017160154/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/23/how_to_watch_a_movie.php">kicked off with <em>Ghostbusters</em></a>, and you can watch the 2015 Inaugural Lawn Rush below&#x2014;the first video was taken from the 40th floor of the MetLife Building.</p>
