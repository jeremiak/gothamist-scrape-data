</div></div>					<p>Every year at at Van Cortlandt Manor in Croton-On-Hudson, somewhere around 5,000 pumpkins get transformed into Jack O&apos; Lanterns, turning the classic fall landscape into a magical twinkling tableau. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151016000214/http://www.hudsonvalley.org/events/blaze">The Great Jack O&#x2019;Lantern Blaze</a> goes on for 25 nights (there are two more weekends left), and this year&apos;s hand-carved creations include dinosaurs, witches, skeletons, snakes, spider webs, sea serpents, and a Tunnel O&#x2019; Pumpkin Love that you can walk through! Not to mention a towering pumpkin bonfire and a working doomsday grandfather clock and are those pumpkins dangling from all the trees? Yes, yes they are.</p>

<p>The whole thing is pretty spectacular, and because of that, it sells out quickly&#x2014;for the remaining weekends, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151016000214/https://www.hudsonvalley.org/store">buy tickets here</a>. And in the meantime, click through for a look through the lens of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151016000214/http://www.flickr.com/photos/joshbousel/sets/72157631868729673/with/8129761729/">Joshua Bousel</a>, who shot this year&apos;s event.
