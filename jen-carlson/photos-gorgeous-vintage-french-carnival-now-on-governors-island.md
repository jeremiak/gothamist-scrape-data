</div></div>					<p>Starting this weekend, you&apos;ll be able to travel back in time to France in the late 19th Century, just by taking a ferry ride to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140423135436/http://gothamist.com/tags/governorsisland">Governors Island</a>. Yesterday photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140423135436/http://www.colormekatie.com/">Katie Sokoler</a> had a chance to check out <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140423135436/http://www.feteparadiso.com/">F&#xEA;te Paradiso</a> for us in advance of the opening&#x2014;this is the world&apos;s first festival of vintage French carnival rides and carousels, and it&apos;s making its American debut right here in NYC. </p>

<p>We&apos;re told &quot;the rare, museum-quality collection, which includes a diverse array of attractions from the late 19th and early 20th centuries, such as carousels, flying swings, carnival games and a pipe organ, will be available for the public to ride and enjoy every weekend from July 13th through September 29th.&quot; Among the attractions&#x2014;which have all come from the collections of Francis Staub and Regis Masclet&#x2014;is a bicycle carousel from the late 19th century (there are only two in the world, the other was featured in <em>Midnight in Paris</em>).</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="//web.archive.org/web/20140423135436if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/fh-MCQsZ5eE" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>There is also an early 20th century Music-Hall Ball Guzzler, a carnival game that features life-size caricatures of Josephine Baker, Charlie Chaplin and other celebrities of the time. Apparently in France &quot;they just play games for the sake of having fun,&quot; but they&apos;re Americanizing the experience here with prizes of lollipops. </p>

<p>On top of all that, there&apos;s a bumper car pavilion from 1900 that&apos;s been transformed into a beer garden and special event space, serving &quot;food reminiscent of a French carnival&quot; (think vichyssoise, crepes and croques-monsieur) courtesy of French bistro Le Gamin.</p>
