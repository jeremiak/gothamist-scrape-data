</div></div><p>While we wait to find out if <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120504035850/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/26/jeff_koons_bringing_full-size_old_s.php">a giant locomotive</a> will be dangling over <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120504035850/http://gothamist.com/tags/highline">the High Line</a> in the future, a much smaller-scale exhibit has popped up along the elevated park. Starting today, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120504035850/http://thehighline.org/about/public-art/lilliput">the Lilliput exhibit</a>&#x2014;named for the island of little people from Jonathan Swift&apos;s <em>Gulliver&apos;s Travels</em>&#x2014;is taking over tiny parts of the park. Six artists have created the miniature sculptures, which are all &quot;installed in unusual and unexpected places at the High Line... to create an art treasure hunt for visitors.&quot; Click through for a little preview before stumbling over the pieces yourself&#x2014;they&apos;ll be there through <em>next</em> April.</p>
