</div></div><p>This evening at 5:30 p.m., Mayor Bloomberg will officially unveil the Public Art Fund&apos;s latest park curation: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130512005116/http://sollewitt.publicartfund.org/">Sol Lewitt Structures (1965-2006)</a>. The City Hall Park installation features 27 works and &quot;is the first outdoor career survey of this extraordinary and influential body of work.&quot; (That doesn&apos;t mean you haven&apos;t seen these pieces outdoors before, though&#x2014;remember the Met&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130512005116/http://gothamist.com/2007/04/09/sol_lewitt_1929.php">rooftop installation</a>?)</p>

<p>With everything being so interactive these days, there&apos;s even an app and an audio tour that will guide you through the pieces (along with this <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130512005116/http://sollewitt.publicartfund.org/maps/">online map</a>). On a more old school tip: there will be events with noted poets and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130512005116/http://sollewitt.publicartfund.org/interact/">other programming</a> surrounding the installation throughout the summer (though the sculptures won&apos;t come down until December 3rd).</p>
