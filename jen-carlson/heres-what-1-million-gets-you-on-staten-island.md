</div></div>					<p>The Staten Island waterfront isn&apos;t just going touristy, with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160310000116/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/10/video_heres_what_the_dazzling_view.php">a $25-$45 per ride</a> ferris wheel currently being built&#x2014;luxury living has arrived. Click through for a look at what $1 million can get you there.</p>

<p>According <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160310000116/http://ny.curbed.com/archives/2015/09/01/staten_island_penthouse_with_manhattan_views_nets_1_million.php">to Curbed</a>, two penthouse apartments in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160310000116/http://www.theaccoladecondos.com/residences/">The Accolade</a> have sold&#x2014;the building is located next to the ferry in St. George, and each penthouse closed for over $1 million.The biggest amenity is the view, including sprawling looks at the harbor, Manhattan, and the Verrazano-Narrows Bridge. All that said, the exterior looks like a storage unit in Scranton.</p>
