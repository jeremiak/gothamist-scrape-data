<p>The first of two <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170911200855/http://gothamist.com/tags/davidbowie">David Bowie</a> tribute shows took place last night at Carnegie Hall, following the musician&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170911200855/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/11/remembering_david_bowie.php">death in January</a>. </p>

<p>Last night&apos;s concert included Patti Smith, Pixies, Michael Stipe, Debbie Harry, The Flaming Lips, J Mascis, Perry Farrell, Cyndi Lauper, Cat Power, Laurie Anderson, Robyn Hitchcock, The Mountain Goats, Ann Wilson of Heart, Rickie Lee Jones, Jakob Dylan and more. (Tonight&apos;s lineup will perform at Radio City Music Hall and is very similar with a few additions.) Tony Visconti, who worked on 13 of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170911200855/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/08/best_david_bowie_albums.php">Bowie&apos;s albums</a> including <em>Blackstar</em>, will be leading the house band, Holy Holy, again tonight, which includes Woody Woodmansey on drums. Both are original members of The Spiders from Mars.</p>

<p>Tonight&apos;s show at Radio City Music Hall is sold out, but you can <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170911200855/http://musicofdavidbowie.com/">stream it here</a> (this will cost $5 and 100% of the revenues will go to the non-profit organizations).</p>
