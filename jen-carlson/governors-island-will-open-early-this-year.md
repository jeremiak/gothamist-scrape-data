					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012122604/http://gothamist.com/tags/governorsisland">Governors Island</a> will open one month early this year, on May 1st, giving New Yorkers the ability to explore the area during spring <em>for the first time in history</em>. In the past, the Island has opened around Memorial Day, so this extends their season from 120 days to 146 days. Thanks, Governors!</p>

<p>&#x201C;An extra month on Governor&#x2019;s Island means more arts, culture and outdoor time for New York families. We are proud of the focused progress that got us to a spring opening this year, and will note that a May Day opening stands as a symbol of changes as we create a more livable and affordable city,&#x201D; said Mayor Bill de Blasio.</p>

<p>The opening date announcement came with another one: Island Oyster, &quot;a new food and beverage concession featuring sustainably sourced seafood in a garden-like atmosphere,&quot; will also be opening on this year, providing &quot;expanded dining options and unparalleled views of the Lower Manhattan skyline and New York Harbor.&quot;</p>

<p>On top of their usual food, arts and events offerings, last year the Island delivered the most precious thing to New Yorkers: more space. Their newly landscaped area, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012122604/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/31/governors_island_hills_sneak_peek.php">The Hills</a> features slides, grassy knolls, and yes, more views.</p>

<p>Ronay Menschel, Chair of the Trust for Governors Island, said upon the early opening announcement: &quot;With the Hills and stunning harbor views, an ever-expanding rotation of arts and cultural programming, and now a longer public season, Governors Island continues to be a unique public resource for all New Yorkers.&#x201D; And we couldn&apos;t agree more. Take a look at the bucolic setting above, and count down to May 1st.</p>
