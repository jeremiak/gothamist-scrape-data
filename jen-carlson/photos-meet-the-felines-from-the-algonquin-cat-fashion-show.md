</div></div>					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150810001135/http://gothamist.com/tags/catfashionshow">Cat Fashion Show</a> hosted at the Algonquin Hotel in 2010 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150810001135/http://gothamist.com/2010/08/12/cat_fashion_show.php">was a Big Event</a>. You know? There were a ton of fantastically-costumed felines walking down a real deal runway! There was a host! There were celebrity guests! it was the goddamn Miss Universe Pageant, but for cats. But over the years it&apos;s gone kind of downhill, with just a handful of half-asleep cats begrudgingly participating. Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150810001135/http://www.colormekatie.com/">Katie Sokoler</a> attended this year&apos;s annual event (a birthday party for the hotel&apos;s cat, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150810001135/http://gothamist.com/tags/matilda">Matilda</a>), which took place last night&#x2014;she reports back:</p>

<blockquote>&quot;It seems to be getting more and more lame each year. Luckily they had different outfits this time. But there weren&apos;t that many cats and they just lay around. It takes place on the second floor of the hotel in a cramped room and you basically just walk around and look at the few cats.&quot;</blockquote>

<p>But you know what, <em>there are still cats in costumes</em>, and we&apos;ll continue to stare on in wonderment at them and their humans until the end of time. And those humans, by the way, are dressing up in cat-themed fashions for the event these days, totally fulfilling the whole Cat Person idea. Click through for a look at them, and their furry friends.</p>
