					<p>This year <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170504145305/http://www.ohny.org/site-programs/weekend/sites/twa-flight-center-0">OHNY</a> opened up the doors to the gorgeous TWA building at JFK Airport, a mid-century time capsule that sits abandoned across from Terminal 5. Over there, you&apos;re reminded of a time when <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170504145305/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/19/8_reasons_air_travel_used_to_be_coo.php">air travel had a bit of dignity and elegance</a>. You could sit down at a sleek bar and drink a dirty martini, instead of waiting on line at Starbucks for a $13 scone. Goddamn it was a beautiful time to be launched 30,000 feet in the air. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="twa2015main.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170504145305im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/twa2015main.jpg" width="640" height="427" class="image-none"> </span><br>
<span class="photo_caption">(Photo by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170504145305/http://www.todseelie.com/">Tod Seelie</a>/Gothamist)</span></p>

<p>The Eero Saarinen-designed building is on the National Register of Historic Places, and may soon, eventually, finally come back to life... but in the form of a hotel. While there, photographer Tod Seelie noted there was a &quot;strong indication that the proposed hotel development is a go,&quot; with posters of the plans displayed around the former terminal. </p>

<p>The mid-century modern gem has been slowly restored over the last six years, and MCR Development is currently &quot;advancing a privately-funded $265 million plan to rehabilitate the national landmark to its original splendor and deliver the first on-site, world-class airport hotel at JFK International Airport.&quot; <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170504145305/http://twaflightcenterhotel.com/history-design/">They promise</a> to preserve the building&apos;s history and beauty as they move forward with their plans&#x2014;&quot;The TWA Flight Center Hotel will celebrate and preserve Eero Saarinen&#x2019;s masterpiece,&quot; CEO Tyler Morse said, &quot;returning the landmark to its original glory and reopening it to the public&quot; in 2018.</p>

<p>The building is closed to the public, but you can expect more tours like the one at OHNY before it reopens as a hotel. You can also gaze upon it as you depart Terminal 5... but they need to fire whoever put that light pole there.</p>
