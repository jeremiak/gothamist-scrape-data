					<p>The New York City <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134822/http://gothamist.com/2013/05/21/houseboats.php#photo-1">houseboat</a> is such a wonderful, magical idea. On paper. In reality it&apos;s probably a floating money pit that will eventually be destroyed by rising tides or a hurricane. But if you&apos;re willing to take a risk and sink some money into a houseboat within the five boroughs, this one is a good one. </p>

<p>This dream home&#x2014;a 2007 Custom Flo-Lodge&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134822/http://www.zillow.com/homedetails/5914-Beach-Channel-Dr-Arverne-NY-11692/2106323312_zpid/">is a mere $59,000</a> (although that doesn&apos;t include any fees involved with owning a houseboat), and the seller notes it&apos;s equipped to handle all four seasons. It has spent the last year at Marina 59 In Far Rockaway, Queens, where it remains now.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="8houseboatrock17main.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134822im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/8houseboatrock17main.jpg" width="640" height="434" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>The floating home is located in the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134822/http://arvernebythesea.com/what-is-the-history-of-arverne-by-the-sea/">Averne</a> section of Far Rockaway, which was originally more of a resort area, featuring grand hotels, a theater, and summer bungalows, as well as a &quot;grandiose plan for a canal running through the neighborhood,&quot; which never came to be. [via <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170114134822/https://www.reddit.com/r/nyc/comments/5nfqqf/floating_house_in_a_marina_in_far_rockaway_is/">Reddit</a>]</p>
