</div></div>					<p>Tatzu Nishi&apos;s <em>sort-of</em> controversial installation, &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141221122427/http://gothamist.com/tags/discoveringcolumbus">Discovering Columbus</a>,&quot; will open tomorrow in Columbus Circle&#x2014;but today we got a sneak peek.</p>

<p>During construction, a couple of New York tabloids tracked down some Italian Americans that were offended by the installation, calling it &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141221122427/http://gothamist.com/2012/08/20/columbus_circle_art_installation_is.php#photo-1">an abomination</a>,&quot; but for the most part, people seem to be indifferent. The installation is really pretty unique, and through <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141221122427/http://www.publicartfund.org/view/exhibitions/5495_discovering_columbus/">November 18th</a> it will be one of the most coveted &quot;apartments&quot; in the city.</p>

<p>To give viewers a closer look at the statue, a living room&#x2014;complete with furniture and television and windows offering sweeping views&#x2014;was built around it. Click through for a look&#x2014;inside and out.</p>
