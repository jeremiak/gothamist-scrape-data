</div></div>					<p>While hoards of tourists flock to Times Square on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140423141520/http://gothamist.com/newyearseve">New Year&apos;s Eve</a>, some of us who actually live here use the night as an opportunity to dress up, get inordinately wasted, and eat diner food at 4 a.m. We dispatched photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140423141520/http://saimokhtari.com/">Sai Mokhtari</a> to capture locals on their way out, and journey back home this New Year&apos;s Eve, so if you stayed in (like the rest of us) you can click through for a glimpse at what you &quot;missed.&quot; Drink every time you see someone hunched over on a curb!</p>

<p>The photos were taken at various locations and times, from Atlantic Terminal (at 8 p.m.), to Grand Central, to Union Square, to the always photogenic Bedford L stop (as well as the Lorimer stop), and finally near 23rd Street near 8th Avenue. The adventure concludes at the Chelsea Square Restaurant, where the evening&apos;s revelries are being soaked up by pancakes and grilled cheese sandwiches.</p>
