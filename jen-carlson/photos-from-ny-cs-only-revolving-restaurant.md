					<p>The views around New York City&#x2014;whether you&apos;re on the ground or 100-floors over it, whether you&apos;re a tourist or native&#x2014;are spectacular to behold, and you don&apos;t need to be an urban explorer leaning precariously over a ledge to enjoy some of the best ones. We&apos;ve been visiting some of the highest legally-accessed views around Manhattan, previously making stops at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025130058/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/11/legal_highs_1wtc.php#photo-1">1WTC</a>, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025130058/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/12/top_of_the_rock_views.php#photo-1">Top of the Rock</a>, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025130058/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/13/legal_highs_times_square_hyatt.php#photo-1">tallest hotel rooftop lounge</a> in the city, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025130058/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/14/legal_highs_new_museum_views.php#photo-1">the New Museum&apos;s terrace</a>. Now, we&apos;re heading back to Midtown where, did you know?, there&apos;s a rotating restaurant 48 floors above Times Square.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025130058/http://www.theviewnyc.com/">The View</a> is New York&#x2019;s only revolving rooftop restaurant and lounge, taking you on a 360&#xB0; turn every hour, delivering the most striking changing views if you head over there just before sunset. They open at 5 p.m. (and remain open until about 1:30 a.m. on weekdays, 10 or 11 p.m. on weekends), and on Sunday they offer brunch from 10 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. You can find it on the 48th floor of the Marriott Marquis in Times Square. </p>

<p>The View was designed by John Portman and opened in 1985. At that time, Windows on the World&#x2014;located on the 106th and 107th floors of the North Tower of the World Trade Center&#x2014;had already been opened nearly 10 years. That could have been one reason The View seems to have been overlooked in the NY Times dining section, though curiously enough <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025130058/http://www.nytimes.com/1986/08/03/nyregion/dining-out-rotating-view-and-versatile-menu.html">in 1986</a> they took a trip to Stamford, CT to dine at <em>their</em> Marriott&apos;s rotating restaurant. </p>

<p>In 2005, however, Charles McGrath did mention The View <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025130058/http://www.nytimes.com/2005/05/26/garden/a-lunch-club-for-the-higherups.html">in the Paper of Record</a>, declaring: &#x201C;The only place where you can pretend to be a tycoon and sip a martini while looking down on the city is The View, the cocktail lounge at the top of the Marriott Marquis, a space so unglamorous that it makes you understand the current fashion for hanging out not at the tops of buildings but in their atriums.&#x201D; It should be noted this was before the restaurant&apos;s redesign, though he&apos;s right, it&apos;s no <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025130058/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_Club">Cloud Club</a> (but that place didn&apos;t rotate).<small></small></p>
