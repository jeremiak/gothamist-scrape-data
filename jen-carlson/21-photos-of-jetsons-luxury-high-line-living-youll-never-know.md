					<p>A new attraction is coming to the High Line! Soon you&apos;ll be able to stand on the free elevated parkland and gaze into the future, like you&apos;re on some Epcot Center ride. Look inside and see a life you could be living if you had just decided to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108042906/http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2015-05-07/here-are-the-college-majors-that-make-the-most-money">major in Engineering</a> instead of English Lit. It&apos;s an immersive experience with a <em>Sliding Doors</em> meets Ghost of Decisions Past theme.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="phpUAAmniPM.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108042906im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/phpUAAmniPM.jpg" width="640" height="360" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>If you have walked <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108042906/http://gothamist.com/tags/highline">the High Line</a> lately you&apos;ve likely seen this Jetsons-esque building taking shape in the 20s. It&apos;s Zaha Hadid&apos;s luxury baby, rising at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108042906/http://www.520w28.com/">520 West 28th Street</a> and opening up to residents in 2017. But we like to think of it as public art, not a new condo for some generic rich guy with fifteen Noguchi coffee tables.</p>

<p>Inside this attraction you&apos;ll find amenities that may seem real, but they&apos;re just commentary on conspicuous consumption. They include: <br>
</p><ul><li>The first private IMAX in NYC<br>
</li><li>99 Bottle Full-Height Gaggenau Wine Cooler<br>
</li><li>34-foot stone feature wall in the lobby<br>
</li><li>75-foot skylit saline-system pool<br>
</li><li>Private &amp; reservable spa<br>
</li><li>Automated robotic parking &amp; storage<br>
</li><li>Motorized floor-to-ceiling windows<br>
</li><li>10&#x2019; long terrace doors</li></ul>We are also told there will be &quot;a huge sculpture garden that is unlike any other.&quot; Art <em>inside of art</em>, that&apos;s deep.<p></p>
