</div></div>					<p>This weekend the Brooklyn Cyclones saluted <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151212163644/http://gothamist.com/tags/seinfeld">Seinfeld</a> at <strike>MCU Park</strike> Kramerica Industries Park, and they didn&apos;t stop at the Little Jerry bobblehead giveaway. </p>

<p>Steve Hytner, who played Kenny &quot;That&apos;s Gold!&quot; Bania was in attendance, there was soup from the Soup Nazi, puffy shirts, a Fusilli Jerry craft table, a Top of the Muffin to YOU contest, a Costanza trash eating competition, an Ovaltine Drinking Relay, an Urban Sombrero Catch, a These Pretzels Are Making Me Thirsty Challenge, and our favorite: the Elaine Dance Contest, with contestants donning their finest <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151212163644/http://www.nytimes.com/2010/08/19/fashion/19ELAINE.html?_r=0">Elaine Benes apparel</a>.</p>

<p>The team also wore these special jerseys, inspired by Kramer&apos;s Technicolor Dreamcoat. Sadly, however, Keith Hernandez did not join them.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="dreamcoatbaseball.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20151212163644im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/dreamcoatbaseball.jpg" width="640" height="427" class="image-none"> </span><br>
<span class="photo_caption">(Scott Lynch/Gothamist)</span></p>

<p>Still, the Cyclones won, 9-2 over Williamsport, thanks in part to Brandon Brosher, who &quot;had a night that was real and spectacular as the 20-year-old slugger set a franchise record with seven RBI.&quot; </p>

<p>Next up, on July 11th, the Cyclones will host <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151212163644/http://www.brooklyncyclones.com/news/?article_id=2007">a Star Wars sleepover</a>.</p>
