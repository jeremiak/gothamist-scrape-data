					<p>Back in June, the pumpkin carving process began for New York&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101101613/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/30/photos_nys_great_jack_olantern_blaz.php#photo-1">most intense pumpkin fest</a>, The Great Jack O&apos;Lantern Blaze at Van Cortlandt Manor in Croton-On-Hudson. The spectacular display features thousands (somewhere around 10,000 this year) of illuminated pumpkins, all carved by a small team from Historic Hudson Valley, which produces the event. Each pumpkin is formed into something: a sunflower, a Frankenstein, a dinosaur... and each night it takes 30 volunteers around 3 hours to light all the candles.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="dinos15pmpkin.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101101613im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/dinos15pmpkin.jpg" width="640" height="426" class="image-none"> </span><br>
<span class="photo_caption">(Photo by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101101613/http://www.todseelie.com/">Tod Seelie</a>/Gothamist)</span></p>

<p>On the historic, 18th-century riverside grounds, you&apos;ll also find a Pumpkin Planetarium, a Circus Train (with what are surely terrifying pumpkin clowns), flying pumpkin ghosts, the Headless Horseman, gourd-filled Jack-in-the-Boxes that spring and bounce around, a working doomsday grandfather clock, a giant sea serpent, and so much more. 10,000 PUMPKINS. This is all accompanied by sound effects, elaborate synchronized lighting and an all-original soundtrack by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101101613/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Christy">Richard Christy</a>.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="blazepost15.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101101613im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/blazepost15.jpg" width="640" height="427" class="image-none"> </span><br>
<span class="photo_caption">(Photo by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101101613/http://www.todseelie.com/">Tod Seelie</a>/Gothamist)</span></p>

<p>It&apos;s worth the trip out of the city (a little under an hour by train or car), just be sure to buy your tickets <em>in advance</em>. The event ends on November 15th, and the evenings sell out fast, but there are currently still a few dates with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101101613/http://www.hudsonvalley.org/events/blaze/tickets">tickets available</a>. This year photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171101101613/http://www.todseelie.com/">Tod Seelie</a> headed up there to get us some shots of the lit up jack o&apos; lanterns&#x2014;click through for a look.</p>
