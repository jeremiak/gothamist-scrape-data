										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> </span></p><div class="image-left" style=" width:200px; "> <img alt="biggshorse.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20141008203434im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/biggshorse.jpg" width="200" height="267"> <br> <i><span class="photo_caption">Photo via <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141008203434/http://www.flickr.com/photos/23912576@N05/3428496207/">laverrrue&apos;s flickr</a></span></i></div> A police horse named Mr. Biggs is at the center of a lawsuit filed against the city last week. Allegedly the member of the NYPD&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141008203434/http://gothamist.com/tags/mountedunit">Mounted Unit</a> took a bite out of a New Jersey woman last summer, and now that woman is suing.<p></p>

<p>According to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141008203434/http://www.nypost.com/p/news/local/nypd_bite_suit_from_the_horse_mouth_jjqtBkMa2E6JdJhOzMelKL">the NY Post</a>, Fair Lawn resident Dineen Rudd was &quot;menaced and attacked outside a Chelsea restaurant last summer... near the bar Porky&apos;s on West 21st Street.&quot; Court papers say the mounted police officer allowed the horse to approach her, at which point the horse allegedly dug into Rudd and left her with permanent injuries.</p>

<p>The city has not yet commented on the suit, but Rudd insists that they should have been aware of the horse&apos;s &quot;propensity to attack and bite human beings.&quot; No word yet on how much she&apos;s seeking in the &quot;horse suit.&quot; And after the jump, video of police officers from the Mounted Unit explaining their day, their training, and their horses:</p>
