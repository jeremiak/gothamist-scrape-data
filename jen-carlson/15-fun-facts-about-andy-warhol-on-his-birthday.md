</div></div>					<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://gothamist.com/tags/andywarhol">Andy Warhol</a> would have been 85 today, and while the Andy Warhol Museum is celebrating <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://www.warhol.org/figment/">by <em>livestreaming his grave</em></a>, we&apos;re going a little less morbid and looking at his stretched out 15 minutes with 15 fun facts. Did you know...</p>

<ul><li>His first commission was from Glamour Magazine&#x2014;he did the illustration accompanying an article called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://www.warhol.org/collection/aboutandy/biography/successisajob/1998-3-2453/"><em>Success Is A Job In New York</em></a>. It appeared in the September 1949 issue.
</li><li>He was a <em>Mad Men</em> man&#x2014;he started out in advertisements, winning the Art Director&#x2019;s Club Medal for a shoe advertisement in 1957. Here he is as a young man in the 1950s:</li></ul>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="andyy0713.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/andyy0713.jpg" width="638" height="569" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<ul><li>He was hanging out at Serendipity 3 on East 58th Street back in 1954, when it opened. It was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://www.serendipity3.com/history.htm">his favorite sweet shop</a>.
</li><li>He was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/16/motivational_psa_moma_sent_andy_war.php">rejected by MoMA</a> around that time.
</li><li>He once said: &#x201C;If you want to know all about me, just look at the surface of my paintings and me, and there I am. There&#x2019;s nothing behind it.&#x201D;
</li><li>He was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/10/wrestling_fan_andy_warhol_attends_w.php">really into WWF wrestling</a>.
</li><li>He had a show on MTV:</li></ul>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="//web.archive.org/web/20150429001322if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/DL41g_2GkNw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<ul><li>He wore those glasses because he had lazy eyes, they had a tiny pinhole for him to see through.
</li><li>He was the producer of the first Velvet Underground album, and he painted the cover art, too. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Velvet_Underground_%26_Nico">You know the one</a>.
</li><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://www.warholstars.org/chron/1966.html">In 1966</a>, he took out an ad in the Village Voice that read: &quot;I&#x2019;ll endorse with my name any of the following: clothing, AC-DC, cigarettes, small tapes, sound equipment, ROCK &#x2018;N&apos; ROLL RECORDS, anything, film, and film equipment, Food, Helium, Whips, MONEY; love and kisses Andy Warhol.&quot;
</li><li>He took out a lot of ads:</li></ul>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="warholad.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/warholad.jpg" width="640" height="319" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<ul><li>The East Village&apos;s Polish National Home was turned into the Dom Restaurant in the &apos;60s, and Andy Warhol sublet the ballroom upstairs, turning it into a nightclub called the Open Stage. The Velvet Underground was the house band. In 1967 this became <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://www.nyrocker.com/blog/tag/electric-circus/">the Electric Circus</a> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/22/unpacking_last_nights_mad_men.php">as seen on <em>Mad Men</em></a>).
</li><li>The IRS <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://books.google.com/books?id=OmBQv6Ib6tgC&amp;pg=PT17&amp;lpg=PT17&amp;dq=warhol+irs+audit+1972&amp;source=bl&amp;ots=-hTzPkW8Ve&amp;sig=YL46FtXjwQfOfqoBxx73xQb_IM8&amp;hl=en&amp;sa=X&amp;ei=Yl0BUoO9E-STyQGzvICwDQ&amp;ved=0CC0Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&amp;q=warhol%20irs%20audit%201972&amp;f=false">first audited</a> Warhol in 1972, and they did so every year through his death in 1987.
</li><li>He had a lot of famous guests stay at his five-home Eothen estate in Montauk, including <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429001322/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/05/photo_chef_keith_richards_cooking_i.php">Keith Richards</a>.
</li><li>He did one of those dope Braniff Airlines commercials:</li></ul>
