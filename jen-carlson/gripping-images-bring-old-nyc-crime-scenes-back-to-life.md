                            <p>While we may be fully aware of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140301084300/http://gothamist.com/2012/05/09/14_grisliest_crime_scene_photos_fro.php">horrific crimes, accidents, and other unseemly things</a> that have happened in our fair city in the past, recently photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140301084300/https://twitter.com/MHermannPhoto">Marc Hermann</a> has brought some striking crime scene photos (like those from <a href="&#x201D;http://gothamist.com/2009/12/16/flashback.php&#x201D;">that Park Slope plane crash</a>) into <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140301084300/http://marchermann.com/dnthenandnow/">the present day</a>. </p>

<p>Click through for a look at old images from crime scenes (from <a href="nydailynewspix.com">Daily News&apos; archives</a>)&#x2014;some of which are graphic&#x2014; superimposed with photos from the same location today. And then try not to think too much about all of the ghosts that are lurking around us at all times.</p>

<p>Hermann tells us, &quot;As the NY Press Photographers hits its centennial, I&apos;ll definitely be doing more.&quot; What other scenes from the city&apos;s past should be brought back to life in the modern day? And can someone do this <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140301084300/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/04/grisly_crime_scene_photos_from_1930.php#photo-1">with Weegee&apos;s archive</a>?</p>
