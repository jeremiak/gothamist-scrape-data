</div></div>					<p>Sure, we&apos;ve seen old photos of Williamsburg <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430052607/http://gothamist.com/2011/07/08/flashback_photos_of_williamsburg_in.php">in the early to mid 1900s</a>, but now that the NYC Municipal Archives has dusted off their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150430052607/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/24/nyc_municipal_archives_releases_nea.php#photo-1">nearly one million old photos</a> and made them available online, we can get an even better look (and travel further back in time).</p>

<p>Their photo archive site was down for a while after it launched last week, but is now <em>sort of</em> working again. Click through for 28 photos of the neighborhood before it became home to artisanal cocktails and luxury condos&#x2014;you&apos;ll see an old newsstand on Bedford Avenue in 1955, the construction of the Williamsburg Bridge, and more.</p>

<p>Note: The Williamsburg Bridge was called the NERB, or New East River Bridge, during the time many of these photos were taken, as you&apos;ll notice in the captions.</p>
