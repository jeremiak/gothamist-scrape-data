					<p>Bryant Park hosted its first <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620201329/http://gothamist.com/2017/05/24/bryant_park_movies_2017.php">movie night of the summer</a> on Monday with a screening of the classic 1933 film <em>King Kong</em>, and the show went on despite <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620201329/http://gothamist.com/2017/06/19/thunderstorm_video_nyc.php">the horrendous weather</a>. </p>

<p>The organizers note that &quot;since we do not have rain dates, we always try to screen the film,&quot; and they haven&apos;t cancelled one since 2007! While they will cancel if there&apos;s lightning, a little thunder, rain, and wind won&apos;t stop them ... though one would think it would stop those who were planning to attend. No, the true <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170620201329/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/21/bryant_park_lawn_movie_video.php">diehards of the Bryant Park summer movies</a> still showed up. However, they were relegated to the chairs encircling the lawn, which was closed. </p>
