</div></div>					<p>If you saw <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140811052802/http://gothamist.com/2008/07/24/philippe_petit_man_on_wire.php"><em>Man On Wire</em></a> then you know that Philippe Petit is one of the more interesting, quirky characters to walk the earth&#x2014;and that he&apos;s the <em>only</em> man to ever have <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140811052802/http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/amex/newyork/peopleevents/p_petit.html">walked a wire between the Twin Towers</a>. Now you have a rare chance to spend two days with him at his Action Maverick Master Class at STREB Lab For Action Mechanics in Williamsburg (which is run by Petit&apos;s friend, the amazing <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140811052802/http://www.streb.org/V2/vision/elizabeth.html">Elizabeth Streb</a>).</p>

<p>We dropped by the space on Friday to get a little taste of what the class will be like, and stuck around for a while as Petit fine-tuned what will be his &quot;Theater of Balance.&quot; If you want to be a part of it all, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140811052802/http://events.constantcontact.com/register/event?oeidk=a07e2y4e5rt18d2dd9c&amp;oseq=">you can register here</a>&#x2014;there are three 2-day classes in August (August 9-10, August 11-12, and August 14-15th) and costs $1200 (deadline for payment is July 19th).</p>

<p>Fun fact: as Petit ran around tightening ropes and teaching his trade, he would often make notes on his yellow pad of paper&#x2014;on top of which he also kept his glasses and a cassette tape of the <em>Amadeus</em> soundtrack.</p>
