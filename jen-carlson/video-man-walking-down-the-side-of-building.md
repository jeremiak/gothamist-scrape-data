										<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="STREBWHITNEY0910.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503150235im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/STREBWHITNEY0910.jpg" width="200" height="178" class="image-left"> </span>That building is the Whitney, and at least one of the &quot;men&quot; is (friend <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503150235/http://gothamist.com/2010/06/28/philippe_petit.php">of Philippe</a>) Elizabeth Streb. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503150235/http://twitter.com/#!/STREBSLAM/status/25904011968">Here she is</a> rehearsing for her part in Off the Wall: Part 2, which features works by the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503150235/http://www.trishabrowncompany.org/">Trisha Brown Dance Company</a>, on the occasion of the company&#x2019;s 40th anniversary. </p>

<p>Iconic works from the 1970s are all on the schedule, and you can check out a rehearsal walk in the video below (that&apos;s Stephen Petronio walking in it)&#x2014;it was first performed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503150235/http://whitney.org/Events/ManWalkingDown">at the Whitney</a> in 1971 and will happen again tonight at 5 p.m. and Saturday and Sunday at 1:30 and 5 p.m. (all performances are weather permitting). Sunday&apos;s event wil also <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503150235/http://whitney.org/Exhibitions/OffTheWallPart2/Livestream">livestream here</a>. If you catch the walk, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150503150235/mailto:photos@gothamist.com">send us</a> your photos and videos, or tag them &quot;Gothamist&quot; on Flickr.</p>
