					<p>Governors Island is now open for the season, but their new rolling hills will remain closed to the public until July 19th. We got a sneak peek of the area last week, however (thanks to an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012132059/http://www.atlasobscura.com/">Atlas Obscura</a> tour), which we hadn&apos;t visited since it was just <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012132059/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/23/governors_island_hills.php">a pile of rubble</a>. </p>

<p>The Hills&#x2014;a section in the works <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012132059/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/25/check_out_the_hills_a_new_park_comi.php">for years now</a>&#x2014;features slopes up to 70-feet high, all with different names: Slide Hill, Outlook Hill, Grassy Hill, and Discover Hill. While the main attraction is the sweeping views of the city&#x2014;including the Statue of Liberty, the Harbor, and the Lower Manhattan skyline&#x2014;there&apos;s also a lush landscape to picnic upon, a permanent installation by British artist Rachel Whiteread, and a rocky wall of slides.</p>

<p>While there are slides of various lengths and widths, Slide Hill most notably boasts the longest slide in New York City&#x2014;at least, that&apos;s what we&apos;re told&#x2014;measuring 57-feet long and 3 stories tall.</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012132059if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/vPajscO9n4Y" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>In 2013, Building 877 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171012132059/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/09/watch_the_the_tallest_building_on_g.php">was imploded</a>, and this new section was built on top of it&#x2014;50,000 cubic yards of the fill used for The Hills is made up of recycled debris from the demolition.</p>
