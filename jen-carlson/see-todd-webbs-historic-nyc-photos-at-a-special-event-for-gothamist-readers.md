					<p>Last month we previewed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503232909/http://gothamist.com/2017/04/17/todd_webb_old_nyc_photos.php">the work of photographer Todd Webb</a> prior to the opening of two exhibits highlighting his images of 1940s NYC. &quot;Webb&apos;s photographs of Post-War New York captured the city&apos;s iconic sites as well as its small, fleeting dramas,&quot; former <em>LIFE</em> magazine editor-in-chief Bill Shapiro says, and this weekend you can hear more insights from him.</p>

<p>The exhibit Shapiro curated in Chelsea, at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503232909/http://www.thecuratorgallery.com/">the Curator Gallery</a>, will welcome Gothamist readers for special tours this coming Saturday, May 6th. Shapiro will personally guide intimate tours of the gallery and talk about Webb&apos;s work, discussing, among other things, why Webb is the best NYC street photographer you&apos;ve never heard of, the role his famous friends (like Georgia O&apos;Keeffe) played in his art, and his secrets for capturing the heart of the city. </p>

<span class="photo_caption">(Courtesy of the Curator Gallery)</span></p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170503232909/http://www.thecuratorgallery.com/">The Curator Gallery</a> is located at 520 West 23rd street. Talks are ongoing between noon and 3 p.m. this Saturday May 6th. No need to RSVP, just show up and mention Gothamist.<br>
