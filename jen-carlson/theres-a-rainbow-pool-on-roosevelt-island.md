					<p>Roosevelt Island&apos;s rainbow pool is back, with a brand new paint job serving up a stark contrast to the adjacent Manhattan skyline. Looking a bit different from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161014085317/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/20/roosevelt_rainbow_pool.php">last year&apos;s</a>, which was created by artist HOT TEA, this year&apos;s design (titled &quot;Block Party&quot;) comes from Andrew Faris... and you can bask in its radiance for a price.</p>

<p>Day passes for the Manhattan Park Pool Club are $35 during the week and $50 on the weekend, though there&apos;s also a season pass option that will cost you $700 (per person). The pool is the main attraction here, so aside from the shower facilities don&apos;t expect much else for that price.</p>

<p>Barak Pliskin of Pliskin Architecture, who are involved in the project along with Roosevelt Island Associates, told us this morning that it &quot;uses a bright array of colors similar to those used last year, while reimagining the pool deck in a style that is unique to Andrew&#x2019;s work. Not counting several rain delays, the mural took 10 days to complete and over 60 gallons of paint.&quot;</p>

<p>Grab a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161014085317/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/09/rainbow_bagels_williamsburg.php">rainbow bagel</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161014085317/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/28/rainbow_latte_video_whoa.php">rainbow latte</a> and get a lot of &quot;likes&quot; on your Instagram.</p>
