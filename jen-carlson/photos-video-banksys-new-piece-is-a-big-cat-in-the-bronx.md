</div></div>					<p>It&apos;s Day 30 of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509023322/http://gothamist.com/tags/banksy">Banksy</a>&apos;s month-long takeover of NYC&apos;s streets, and for his penultimate piece he&apos;s hit Yankee Stadium with this cool stencil of a jaguar? A cheetah? His <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509023322/http://banksy.co.uk/2013/10/30/bronx-zoo">website says</a> &quot;Bronx Zoo (at Yankee Stadium),&quot; and specifically this is at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509023322/https://maps.google.com/maps?q=Macombs+Dam+Bridge+and+Jerome+Ave&amp;ll=40.829641,-73.925908&amp;spn=0.010131,0.016093&amp;hnear=Jerome+Ave+%26+Macombs+Dam+Bridge,+Bronx,+New+York+10452&amp;gl=us&amp;t=h&amp;z=16&amp;layer=c&amp;cbll=40.830649,-73.927171&amp;panoid=LwWprpim8QmfspHlkS-TtQ&amp;cbp=12,305.92,,0,-12.36">Macombs Dam Bridge and Jerome Avenue</a>. </p>

<p>Of course, the 1972-1981 Yankees were referred to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509023322/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Yankees#Steinbrenner.2C_Martin.2C_Jackson.2C_and_Munson:_the_Bronx_Zoo_.281973.E2.80.931981.29">as the Bronx Zoo</a>, because of the fighting between George Steinbrenner, Billy Martin and Reggie Jackson.</p>

<p><strong>UPDATE:</strong> The paint is fresh, Scott Lynch tells us, &quot;still a bit tacky and very aromatic.&quot;</p>

<p>Nic Garcia tells us, &quot;There&apos;s all kinds of layers and hidden messages and words and stuff in here. I can&apos;t imagine how long he worked on this. Multiple sessions?&quot; Also, there&apos;s a COPE shout out by the peace sign!</p>

<p>Yesterday, the ol&apos; Bankster re-donated a painting he had purchased at Housing Works, altering it with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150509023322/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/29/photo_new_banksy_piece_goes_up_in.php#photo-1">the addition of a Nazi soldier</a>&#x2014;it is now being sold at auction by the organization.</p>
