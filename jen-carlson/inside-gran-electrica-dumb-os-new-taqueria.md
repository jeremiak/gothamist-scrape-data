</div></div>					<p>The DUMBO neighborhood of Brooklyn is sorely lacking in decent dining options, so it&apos;s with open arms that the newest spot, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150313115858/http://www.granelectrica.com/">Gran Electrica</a>, is welcome into the small fold. Especially since it&apos;s taking over Pub One&apos;s space, which had a dismal <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150313115858/http://www.yelp.com/biz/pubone-brooklyn">2 star Yelp rating</a>, and often surly service (and a <em>very stringent</em> enforcement of their &quot;customers only&quot; bathroom policy). The latest incarnation of the space comes from the people behind <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150313115858/http://gothamist.com/2011/02/17/new_restaurants_20.php#photo-1">Colonie</a>, and has chefs Sam Richman (Jean Georges, The Fat Duck) and Brad McDonald (Noma, Per Se) doing Mexican dishes.</p>

<p>Gran Electrica will serve up tilefish, brisket, sweetbreads, pork shoulder, <em>and</em> tongue tacos ($3.50 to $4.50), amongst others. They also have gorditas, quesadillas, oysters, clams, scallops, churras and more... including $13 margaritas and $5 cans of Tecate. Which is the price you pay for imbibing under the iconic Brooklyn Bridge.</p>

<p>The restaurant is now open for business, however, it doesn&apos;t seem like they expected such a warm spring, because their expansive outdoor space isn&apos;t quite ready yet. Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150313115858/http://www.colormekatie.com/">Katie Sokoler</a> dropped by yesterday for a look at how things we&apos;re coming along&#x2014;click through for a look at the menu and the space.</p>

<p><em>Gran Electrica // 5 Front Street, Brooklyn // (718) 852-2789</em></p>
