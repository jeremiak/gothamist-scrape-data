					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108032844/http://misplaced.design/">Misplaced Series</a> has taken &quot;notable New York buildings from their surroundings&quot; and dropped them into desolate landscapes far away from Manhattan. &quot;Out of context, architectural forms become more pronounced and easily understood,&quot; the creators of the project explain. But really, it just <em>looks cool</em>. Our museums and other notable buildings, in their new landscapes, begin to look like surreal roadside attractions. </p>

<p>Click through for a look at the Guggenheim, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108032844/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/01/met_breuer_preview.php">the Met Breuer</a>, the UN Headquarters, and more buildings in their new surroundings of sand dunes, mud flats, beaches, and lunar plains. [h/t <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161108032844/http://kottke.org/16/04/misplaced-new-york">Kottke</a>]</p>
