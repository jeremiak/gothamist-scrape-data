					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170518174759/http://gothamist.com/2016/05/10/central_park_new_section.php">newly revealed &quot;secret&quot; section of Central Park</a>, which has been closed off since Robert Moses declared it a bird sanctuary in the 1930s, has now reopened to humans. The fenced-off, 4-acre peninsula&#x2014;located near The Pond&#x2014;has been largely neglected for decades, becoming overrun by weeds and left untouched by man. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170518174759/http://www.centralparknyc.org/things-to-see-and-do/attractions/hallett-nature-sanctuary.html?referrer=http://gothamist.com/2016/05/10/central_park_new_section.php">The Hallett Nature Sanctuary</a>&#x2014;renamed so in 1986 for George Harvey Hallett Jr., a birdwatcher, naturalist, and civic leader&#x2014;is now open to the public a few days a week. This follows a landscape makeover which included the addition of a new rustic fence, benches, pathways, and new plantings, as well as a ton of weeding courtesy of the Central Park Conservancy&apos;s ROOTS participants. The high school students in the program focus on &quot;the ecological restoration in Central Park&#x2019;s woodlands,&quot; we&apos;re told, which includes &quot;maintaining rustic trails, removing invasive plants, and cultivating new plantings.&quot;</p>

<p>There is now a mini-sanctuary within the sanctuary, as well, thanks to (rare gem dealer) Sima Ghadamian and her husband Morad&#x2014;it is named for her parents, Mahrokh and Iradj Sakhai. In the gallery above you&apos;ll see a photo of their plaque, as well as photos of the revitalized plot of land (and its raccoon residents!) from our visit this week.</p>

<p>To keep the area serene and preserved, the CPC tells us they will only allow around 20 people in at a time.</p>

<p>The CPC tells us the park will be open past August, as well, but will determine Fall open hours at a later date, depending on attendance and staffing.</p>
