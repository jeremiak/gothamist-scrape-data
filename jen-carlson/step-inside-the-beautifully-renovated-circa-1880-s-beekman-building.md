					<p>It&apos;s almost impossible to believe how long the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404221006/http://gothamist.com/tags/5beekman">5 Beekman</a> building <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404221006/http://gothamist.com/2011/01/03/inside_5_beekman.php">sat in abandonment</a>, given its gorgeous bones and glorious glass atrium. The building was in decay when we last stepped <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404221006/http://gothamist.com/2010/11/10/live_from_gothamist_house_small_bla.php#photo-1">inside in 2010</a>&#x2014;at the time developers were working out what was next for the space, originally known as the Temple Court Building and Annex. After some changes in plans and keys changing hands, the new Beekman is finally here, and visually it looks like a Wes Anderson set, but one created for a grand party thrown by Baz Luhrmann. The top floor, sitting under a dramatic glass roof, is made for tossing confetti and popping champagne. </p>

<p>Actually, it was made for office workers, as the building (which rose back in the 1880s) has prior to this mostly operated as an office building. Sad!</p>

The landmarked building, under the control of GFI Capital Resources Group, has now reopened as a hotel, with a sweet lobby, two restaurants and bar. (There&apos;s also a tower right next door that&apos;s attached to the project and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404221006/http://gothamist.com/2016/08/26/5_beekman_condos_inside.php#photo-1">houses condos</a>.) While the restaurants (run by Tom Colicchio and Keith McNally) and bar aren&apos;t quite open yet (they&apos;re expecting to open later in September), <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170404221006/http://thebeekman.com/">the hotel rooms</a> await those who have around $600/night to spend. Click through for a look at the building&apos;s more public spaces&#x2014;from the ground floor to the glass top.
