<p>Five years ago Steve Powers (a.k.a. ESPO) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160529162226/http://gothamist.com/2011/09/13/photos_espo.php">wrote a beautiful love letter</a> on an ugly old parking garage in Downtown Brooklyn, or more specifically, on the ugly old parking garage&apos;s cool skybridges. The words have hung above the streets since, shouting: Turn to me, Euphoria is you and me, I am made to leave I am made to return. It all makes you feel very warm inside when you&apos;re walking around the Fulton Mall area.</p>

<p>The parking garage is owned by Macy&apos;s, who brought the graffiti artist on for this makeover in 2011, but now they&apos;re getting ready to scrub it. On <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160529162226/https://www.instagram.com/p/BAcYkyorCtT/?taken-by=steveespopowers">his Instagram</a> yesterday, Powers wrote: &quot;Last days for Love Letter Brooklyn. The garage closes today,&quot; thanking all of those involved, including the department store. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160529162226/http://ny.curbed.com/archives/2016/01/13/downtown_brooklyn_is_losing_its_love_letter_to_the_borough.php">Curbed</a> notes that Macy&apos;s &quot;inked a deal with Tishman Speyer earlier this year to completely revamp its Downtown Brooklyn store; the parking spaces next door, meanwhile, could become a mixed-use development.&quot; Which will likely be a less inspirational vision.</p>
