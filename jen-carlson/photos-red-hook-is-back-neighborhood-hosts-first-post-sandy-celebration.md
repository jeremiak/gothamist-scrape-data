</div></div>					<p>Over the weekend <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180427/http://gothamist.com/tags/redhook">Red Hook</a> came to life when New Yorkers flocked to the neighborhood for a rebirth parade, kayaking, celebratory dips in the river (which were frowned upon by the NYPD), and some dance dance dancin&apos;. Photographer Tod Seelie was on hand to capture the The Red Hook Fest, where the theme this year was &quot;Rebuild, Restore, Rebirth: Resilience after Sandy.&quot;</p>

<p>But even before <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180427/http://gothamist.com/tags/hurricanesandy">Hurricane Sandy</a>, the parade had been missing in previous summers&#x2014; this was the first time it was held in 11 years&#x2014;it featured community members and organizations, the always fun Hungry March Band, and the OWS Puppetry Guild with their dancing puppets.</p>

<p>Meanwhile, on the stage was world roots band The Brown Rice Family, hip-hop legends Kool Keith and Dead Prez, and bluegrass/hip-hop hybrid Gangstagrass (they sing that cool <em>Justified</em> theme song)...</p>
