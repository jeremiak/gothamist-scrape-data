					<p>Every year, the massive <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170711205931/http://www.amnh.org/exhibitions/permanent-exhibitions/biodiversity-and-environmental-halls/milstein-hall-of-ocean-life/the-giant-blue-whale-model">blue whale</a> that hangs in the American Museum of Natural History&apos;s Milstein Hall of Ocean Life gets a bath. Or rather, gets vacuumed. And this year&apos;s cleaning will take place on Tuesday and Wednesday, May 30th and May 31st, if you want to see <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170711205931/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/06/whale_cleaning_livestream.php">the oddly satisfying event</a> live. </p>

<p>The Exhibition Department staff uses long-handled brushes and vacuums to get the past year&apos;s worth of dust and dirt of the whale, which has been a centerpiece at the museum since 1969. Some fun whale facts: </p>

<ul><li>This is the largest model of the largest creature that has ever lived on Earth
</li><li>It&apos;s made of fiberglass and polyurethane
</li><li>It weighs 21,000 pounds!
</li><li>It&apos;s 94-feet long
</li><li>The model is based on a blue whale found in 1925 near South America
</li><li>The model cost $200,000 at the time, and took two year to build
</li><li>The Whale (it&apos;s official name) replaced a 76-foot blue whale that was installed in 1908, which &quot;did not bulge properly,&quot; according to a NY Times story (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170711205931/http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=9F06E0DF1230E034BC4D52DFB7678383679EDE">PDF</a>) from 1968.</li></ul>

<p>The Times also reported that &quot;the boss of the crew, Tom O&apos;Toole, a man who walks the girders of skyscrapers without visible fear, gave in and took some tranquilizers during the day,&quot; later saying, &quot;This was my first whale and I hope it&apos;s my last one.&quot;</p>

<p>If you can&apos;t make it, the museum will have a live broadcast of the cleaning <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170711205931/http://facebook.com/naturalhistory">streaming on Facebook</a> for some of the time. Click through the above photos from a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170711205931/http://gothamist.com/2014/07/09/photos_how_to_clean_a_giant_blue_wh.php">previous year</a>&apos;s cleaning to see that before/after goodness.</p>
