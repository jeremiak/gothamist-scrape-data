</div></div><p>The new movie <em>Burlesque</em>, starring Cher and Christina Aguilera, is sadly not the second coming of <em>Showgirls</em>, in fact, New York&apos;s real burlesque performers are basically calling the entire production a prude. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110412165853/http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/movies/2010/11/29/2010-11-29_burlesque_starring_christina_aguilera_is_no_strip_tease_say_new_yorks_real_burle.html">The Daily News</a> talked to some of the city&apos;s real-life stars after seeing the movie; Stormy Leather told them it&apos;s not what you&apos;d see in New York, noting that &quot;Neo-burlesque is dominant in the New York scene, and what we saw in this film was definitely more classic burlesque. We didn&apos;t see a stocking peel or glove peel. I don&apos;t even remember seeing a feather boa.&quot;</p>

<p>These outrageous atrocities don&apos;t end at stocking peels, either&#x2014;performer Melody Sweets says &quot;it doesn&apos;t represent burlesque past or present. They could have done a little homework.&quot; With no stocking peels, it&apos;s not surprising that those looking to see a little skin are going to be disappointed, word is that no one gets naked! Which could be a blessing. Anyway, if you want to see the real thing, head to a real burlesque show (above are some performers having fun with the movie at a recent performance)&#x2014;otherwise, here&apos;s what you&apos;re in for:</p>
