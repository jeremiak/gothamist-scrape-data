					<p>Last night the three structures that make up the New York State Pavilion, built for the 1964-65 World&apos;s Fair, were illuminated following a screening of the documentary <em>Modern Ruin</em>. Last year, Queens Borough President Melinda Katz performed a lighting test, in hopes of bringing a semi permanent lighting installation to the Pavilion&#x2014;while that has yet to happen, last night&apos;s brief light show proved fairly popular. (The NYPD did show up, an officer addressing the crowd: &quot;Well, no one invited me!&quot;)</p>

<p>Will the lights become a regular feature? Director of the doc and co-founder of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161021115013/https://www.facebook.com/peopleforthepavilion/">People for the Pavilion</a>, Matthew Silva, told us today: &quot;Last night&apos;s thing was a one off kinda thing.  We are unsure of the future of any lighting schemes at this time.&quot;</p>
