</div></div>					<p>Prohibition ended <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429154938/http://life.time.com/culture/prohibition-scenes-from-the-speakeasies-of-new-york-in-1933/?iid=lb-gal-viewagn#1">80 years ago</a> today (here&apos;s where to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429154938/http://gothamist.com/2013/12/05/its_repeal_day_lets_get_our_drink_o.php">celebrate tonight</a>), and we thought we&apos;d look back at some of the more horrifying images captured during that dry time: when they flushed away the sweet, intoxicating alcohol before it could touch one&apos;s lips. Click through for a look (from all over the U.S.) at &quot;wine flushes,&quot; whiskey bottles being busted, kegs being smashed, and beer being poured down the dirty streets. </p>

<p>We included a few more hopeful images of beer shipments following the repeal, to help you stave off nightmares. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429154938/http://www.nytimes.com/learning/general/onthisday/big/1205.html">The NY Times</a> reported on the repeal 80 years ago, noting that New Yorkers celebrated with &quot;quiet restraint&quot;... yeah right.</p>
