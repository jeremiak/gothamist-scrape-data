</div></div>					<p>A giant, illuminated number &quot;16&quot; arrived in Times Square yesterday to remind us that yet another year has gone by and <em>what did we even do during all of that time?</em> </p>

<p>Every December a new numeral rolls through town, a visual ticking down of our brief yet seemingly inexorable time here on earth: <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402032605/http://gothamist.com/2007/12/14/17_days_from_8s.php">8</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402032605/http://gothamist.com/2012/12/20/photos_lucky_13_lights_arrive_in_ti.php#photo-1">13</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160402032605/http://gothamist.com/2014/12/16/nye_15_times_square.php">15</a>, pour me another martini.</p>

<p>And now &quot;16,&quot; beaming bright with hundreds of 9-watt energy efficient Philips LEDs, has arrived via flatbed truck. It stands 7-feet-high, and for now is still grounded&#x2014;we&apos;re told that 16 is &quot;resting for a few days&quot; before finishing its journey to One Times Square.</p>
