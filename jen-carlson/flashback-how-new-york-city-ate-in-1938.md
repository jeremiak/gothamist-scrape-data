</div></div>					<p>These photos were all taken by Sol Libsohn in 1938, and now reside in a collection in the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429070846/http://collections.mcny.org/">Museum of the City of New York&apos;s archives</a> in a series titled &quot;Food in New York.&quot; From massive lobsters at the Fulton Fish Market to live sheep on Pier 78 to the inside of the laboratory at the Health Department, click through for a little look back at how we ate 74 years ago. Sadly, the NYPL doesn&apos;t have any menus from 1938 in their ever-expanding collection <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429070846/http://menus.nypl.org/">of vintage menus</a>, but you can further check out what the city looked like during that time <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429070846/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/06/vintage_video_shows_the_city_of_won.php">in this video</a>.</p>
