</div></div>					<p>Previously, street artist <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429130042/http://gothamist.com/tags/jayshells">Jay Shells</a> has taken on etiquette of all kinds, from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429130042/http://gothamist.com/2011/09/08/new_etiquette_rules_posted_around_t.php">human behavior</a> to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429130042/http://gothamist.com/2010/05/27/ettiquette_artists_takes_on_dog_own.php">animal behavior</a> to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429130042/http://gothamist.com/2010/04/22/subway_signs.php">subway behavior</a>. But now he&apos;s back with something totally different, albeit still in his signature sign form: Rap Quotes. The idea is simple, and the result is sort of amazing: he has taken rap lyrics that mention specific locations around New York City, created signs with those lyrics, and has installed them at their appropriate locations. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429130042/http://animalnewyork.com/2013/jay-shells-rap-quotes/">Animal</a> has video of the installation process of the signs, which feature lyrics from Kanye, Jay-Z, GZA, Mos Def, Nas, and more.</p>

<p>Click through for a preview, and you can follow along on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429130042/https://twitter.com/TheRapQuotes">The Rap Quotes Twitter feed</a>.</p>
