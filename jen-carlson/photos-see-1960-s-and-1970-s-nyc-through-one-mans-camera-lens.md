</div></div>					<p>If you want to travel back to New York City in the late <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231085040/http://gothamist.com/tags/1960s">1960s</a> and early <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231085040/http://gothamist.com/tags/1970s">1970s</a>, look no further than <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231085040/http://www.flickr.com/photos/asterisktom/">Tom Riggle</a>&apos;s time machine&#x2014;aka his Flickr account, where he&apos;s uploaded <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231085040/http://www.flickr.com/photos/asterisktom/sets/72157625537261966/">nearly 200 photos</a> from his dad&apos;s personal collection from that time. Riggle was kind enough to share the photos with us, and you can click through to get a look at Chinatown, the Lower East Side, Columbia, and even parts of Brooklyn... decades and decades ago, when everything just looked <em>cooler</em>&#x2014;you hardly ever see poodles in convertibles anymore.</p>

<p>He tells us, &quot;We lived in the Bay Ridge area of Brooklyn. Fort Hamilton. I went to the high school of the same name. I have lots of memories of these trips we took around the city, just one being when we went up inside the Statue of Liberty, looking out of the windows from the head. You can see me and my brother in these pics, and my sister as well. I am the teenager often with a camera or binoculars. As you can see from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141231085040/http://www.flickr.com/photos/asterisktom/">this site</a>, I have been bitten by the same photo bug as my dad.&quot;</p>
