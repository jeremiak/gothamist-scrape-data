					<p>In recent years <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171013210600/http://gothamist.com/tags/foresthillsstadium">Forest Hills Stadium</a> has been coming back to life, and last summer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171013210600/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/10/photos_forest_hills_stadium_2016.php#photo-1">they were back in full swing</a> with a packed schedule for the season. Dolly Parton, Paul Simon, and Bob Dylan were just a few of the big names on the bill in 2016, and now the stadium has delivered what is hands down the most excellent summer concert lineup in the city.</p>

<p>In 2017, the stadium will host: The XX, The New York Pops (playing the music of John Williams), The Chainsmokers (okay, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171013210600/http://ohnotheydidnt.livejournal.com/103358321.html">we didn&apos;t say it was perfect</a>), Jack Johnson, Daryl Hall &amp; John Oates AND Tears For Fears, Sigur Ros, Dispatch (with Guster), John Mellencamp and Emmylou Harris, My Morning Jacket and Gary Clark, Jr., Tom Petty &amp; the Heartbreakers, and in October: The National.</p>

<p>The best part of all of this, of course, is not having to go to that <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171013210600/http://gothamist.com/2009/08/25/flashback_madison_square_garden.php">circular cement chamber</a> in Midtown to see these bands. The open-air venue is about as perfect as it comes&#x2014;planted in the Forest Hills section of Queens, it&apos;s right off the F train and surrounded by Tudor-style architecture and lush green tennis courts. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171013210600/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/06/forest_hills_stadium_history.php">Once home to legends</a> like The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, Leonard Cohen, The Supremes, Joan Baez, The Who, Jimi Hendrix, Simon &amp; Garfunkel, and Frank Sinatra, it&apos;s no surprise the booking dips into the past with performances from the likes of Petty, but they also strive to keep things <em>aesthetically</em> nostalgic.</p>

<p>&quot;It still feels like it could be 1923 when you&apos;re in there,&quot; promoter Mike Luba told us last year. &quot;We worked pretty hard to keep that alive.&quot; Click through the photos to see the stadium and grounds from a tour we took last year.</p>
