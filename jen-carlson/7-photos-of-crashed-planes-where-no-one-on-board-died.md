	<p>Remember that show we used to watch where all of those people got stuck on an island after surviving a plane crash? This is like that <em>but IRL.</em> Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130527165923/http://www.dietmareckell.com/">Dietmar Eckell</a> has documented a number of plane crash sites where they planes still lay abandoned&#x2014;the tie between them all is that no one on board died. He hopes to publish his stunning photos from these shoots in a book, called <em>Happy Ending</em>, and is looking to raise funds via Indiegogo&#x2014;click through for a preview, and click play below to learn more about the project:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130527165923if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/8ohJknVwmrE" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>All in all, Eckell documented &quot;15 airplanes that had forced landings [and] remain abandoned&quot; for decades... which is why <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130527165923/http://gothamist.com/2009/01/15/plane_crash_in_hudson.php#photo-1">Sully&apos;s crash landing on the Hudson</a> wasn&apos;t included. [via <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130527165923/http://laughingsquid.com/happy-end-photos-of-miracle-aircraft-crash-sites-where-no-passengers-died">Laughing Squid</a>]</p>
