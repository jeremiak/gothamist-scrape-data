					<p>Holi Phagwa is the springtime Hindu <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025135953/http://gothamist.com/tags/holi">festival of colors</a>, a ritual dating back thousands of years in honor of Sri Krishna. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025135953/http://www.sanskritimagazine.com/events-festivals/the-story-behind-the-festival-of-holi/">From a fascinating history of the tradition</a>: &quot;Inner illumination is the real Holi. The spring season is the manifestation of the Lord... Holi is said there to be His heart.&quot; The celebration is visually recognizable because it features colorful powders which end up all over attendees to varying degrees, not unlike the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025135953/http://gothamist.com/2014/06/02/photos_the_color_run_just_hit_queen.php">Color Run</a>.</p>

<p>There are several festivals around the city every year celebrating Holi, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025135953/http://brokelyn.com/two-ways-looking-holi-festival-bushwick-past-weekend/">Brokelyn recently questioned</a> if they have gone from harmless celebration to cultural appropriation. &#x201C;I saw it on <em>Eat Pray Love</em> and I was like &#x2018;I gotta go to this,&apos;&#x201D; one attendee at a recent Holi celebration <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025135953/http://www.festivalofcolors.org/">in Bushwick</a> told them. </p>

<p>Photographer Gretchen Robinette was at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161025135953/http://nycholi.com/">another Holi celebration</a> over the weekend&#x2014;click through for a look. Indeed, you could play a game of &quot;Holi or Coachella?&quot; with these. But regardless of all the selfies, maybe we can agree that the celebration has remained true to its spirit and customs, which include &quot;loosening strictness of social structures&quot; and being happy.</p>
