</div></div>					<p>What if the subways were a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164539/http://gothamist.com/2011/03/18/video_subway_spaghetti_spat_sparks.php">spaghetti-throwing</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164539/http://gothamist.com/2012/11/30/two_young_ladies_filmed_giving_thei.php">blowjob-giving</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164539/http://gothamist.com/2012/08/23/is_this_the_most_horrifying_subway.php">fecal</a>-free zone in which we were all just blissfully transported from point A to point B without any hassles or disturbing encounters? A few times a year we get a glimpse of what that sort of underground utopia might look like, when the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164539/http://gothamist.com/tags/nostalgiatrain">Nostalgia Train</a> pulls into select stations and straphangers get decked out in threads from decades when cargo pants and flip flops didn&apos;t exist. </p>

<p>Photographer Paul Ker went on a recent journey into the tunnels with one particularly classy crew&#x2014;click through for his photos. You can hop on one yourself for the remaining Sundays of the year&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164539/http://www.mta.info/metrocard/promos/Vintagetrain/index.html">get the schedule here</a>. Just don&apos;t expect everyone to be decked out, these photos were from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164539/http://levysuniqueny.com/events/vintage-peacock-party-on-the-vintage-nyc-subway-trains/">the Levys&apos; Unique NY party</a> that they host on the train <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151021164539/http://gothamist.com/2011/12/19/photos_vintage_train_tea_party.php#photo-1">every year</a>.</p>
