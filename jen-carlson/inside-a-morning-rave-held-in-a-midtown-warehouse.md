					<p>If you&apos;ve seen the greatest TV show about NYC, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016010435/http://gothamist.com/2016/09/13/high_maintenance_review.php">High Maintenance</a>, you are already privy to morning raves. Not the brightly lit yoga den offered at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016010435/http://gothamist.com/2014/07/20/photos_heres_what_a_7_am_sober_rave.php">&quot;sober raves&quot; of 2014</a>, but the kind with disco balls.</p>

<p>Photographer Sai Mokhtari checked out <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016010435/http://www.daybreaker.com/">the Daybreaker rave</a> (also hyped as a &quot;soul disco&quot;) held in a Midtown warehouse last week, which features all of the above, all starting at 6 a.m. Their simple motto is: wake up &amp; dance, but you&apos;ll also find yoga, fitness experts, bubbles, and baristas&#x2014;there&apos;s no alcohol, but there is plenty of coffee, tea, granola, yogurt, and juices.</p>

<p>The organizers say they&apos;ve &quot;designed an experience that helps release your brain&#x2019;s natural &apos;happy chemicals&apos;: Dopamine, Oxytocin, Serotonin and Endorphins.&quot; And Mokhtari says it works:</p><blockquote>&quot;I can comfortably say this was the most positive atmosphere I&apos;ve ever encountered. It&apos;s like church. THE CULT OF POSITIVITY. I compare it to religion because it&apos;s definitely way more than a party&#x2014;there&apos;s a sense of community and purpose and a striking absence of cynicism. [It was] a very giddy atmosphere. So much goddamn positivity, openness and acceptance&#x2014;if you go to a party like this and roll your eyes, you are a monster.&quot;</blockquote>Keep in eye <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20171016010435/http://www.daybreaker.com/world/">on their calendar</a> for the next event, and in the meantime, get inspired:<p></p>
