</div></div><p>It&apos;s been a while since we last checked in on Charlie Sheen (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130317063324/http://gothamist.com/2011/03/07/charlie_sheen_gets_fired_seeks_inte.php">no it hasn&apos;t</a>), so let&apos;s see how he spent his night after getting fired from <em>Two and a Half Men</em>. Yep, he stood on a rooftop in Beverly Hills, California and waved a machete whilst drinking from a bottle of &quot;Tiger Blood&quot; (according to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130317063324/http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-31749_162-20040532-10391698.html">CBS</a>, &quot;a crowd cheered him on&quot; all the while). </p>

<p>Sheen continued with his little one-man show by quoting Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. (&quot;Free at last, free at last!&quot;), because Sheen&#x2014;as a middle-aged, privileged, white male who <em>made a choice</em> to throw his life away&#x2014;is somehow so delusional that he thinks there are parallels between him and the heroic civil rights activist? And parallels between his departure from a television sitcom and King&apos;s leadership during the struggle for equal rights? </p>

<p><iframe title="YouTube video player" width="640" height="390" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20130317063324if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/D-I4fUc-mfw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Enjoy your latest look at the train wreck, America&#x2014;and if you haven&apos;t already, now would be a good time to read Anna Holmes&apos;s piece on Sheen in the NY Times, titled <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130317063324/http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/04/opinion/04holmes.html?partner=rss&amp;emc=rss">The Disposable Woman</a>. Or watch Sheen&apos;s Korner, whatever.</p>
