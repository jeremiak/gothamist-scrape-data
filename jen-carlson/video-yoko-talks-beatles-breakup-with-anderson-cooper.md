										<p>Recently <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502151734/http://gothamist.com/tags/yokoono">Yoko Ono</a> discussed that whole <em>thing</em> about breaking up The Beatles, with Anderson Cooper <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150502151734/http://edition.cnn.com/video/data/2.0/video/bestoftv/2010/10/12/am.yoko.ono.breakup.cnn.html">on CNN</a>. She told him, &quot;I think I was used as a scapegoat, and it&apos;s a very easy scapegoat, you know, Japanese woman.&quot; When he asks if she believes it was mostly about sexism and racism, she answers: &quot;sexism, racism, but also just remember the United States and Britain were fighting with Japan in WWII, it was just after that in a way, so I could understand how they felt. [It hurt] in a way, but it was sort of like a distance thing... John and I were so close, and we were totally involved in each other.&quot;</p>
