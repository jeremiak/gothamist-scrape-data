</div></div>					<p>This summer Coney Island has been adorned by a slew of artists, all under the direction of curator Jeffrey Deitch and Thor Equities&apos; Joseph Sitt. The latter owns most of Coney Island now, and very nearly shut down <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150826054445/http://gothamist.com/tags/Ruby&apos;s">the beloved Ruby&apos;s</a> until The People fought back. (Other small businesses <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150826054445/http://gothamist.com/2010/04/29/sitt_to_replace_old_coney_island_bu.php">weren&apos;t so lucky</a>.) Now he&apos;s apparently trying to get some street cred by teaming up with art world impresario Deitch and an all-star roster of street artists.</p>

<p>But it&apos;s easy on the eyes, and Thursdays through Sundays the murals surrounded by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150826054445/http://brooklynflea.com/markets/coney-smorgasburg/">the Smorgasburg vendors</a>. </p>

<p>The outdoor museum, called <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150826054445/http://www.coneyartwalls.com/">Coney Island Walls</a>, features work from Lee Qui&#xF1;ones, Lady Pink, Crash, Daze, Futura, Kenny Scharf, Shepard Fairey and Maya Hayuk. The walls were unveiled last month at 1320 Bowery Street in Coney Island&#x2014;click through for a preview.</p>
