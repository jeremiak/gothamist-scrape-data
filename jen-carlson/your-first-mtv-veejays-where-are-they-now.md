										<p>On August 1st, 1981 MTV launched, with The Buggles &quot;Video Killed The Radio Star&quot;... it was really pretty amazing, looking back:</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="510" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160506153008if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/Cw6xesXLIAA" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>But do you remember your five original veejays? Before Jesse Camp, and Adam Curry, and even the newsier heads <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160506153008/http://gothamist.com/2008/12/30/john_norris_mtv_news.php">John Norris</a> and Kurt Loder... there was Martha Quinn, Alan Hunter, Mark Goodman, JJ Jackson and Nina Blackwood! While <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160506153008/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1485838/jj-jackson-original-mtv-vj-dies.jhtml?headlines=true">Jackson died</a> at the age of 62, the remaining veejays got together recently for an MTV 30th Anniversary Special. Click through for a little bit of then and now comparisons!</p>
