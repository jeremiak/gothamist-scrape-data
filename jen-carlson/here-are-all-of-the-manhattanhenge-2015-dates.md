<p>Manhattanhenge is making its 2015 debut this weekend, and for those who don&apos;t know about <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170913231702/http://gothamist.com/tags/manhattanhenge">the Event</a>&#x2014;which was first named and noticed by Neil deGrasse Tyson&#x2014;here&apos;s a little primer.</p>

<p>In short, Manhattanhenge is when the sunset aligns perfectly with the city&apos;s grid. This happens twice a year with a full sun, and twice a year with a half sun&#x2014;all four times the sun illuminates both the north and south sides of every cross street of the borough&apos;s grid. It is our Stonehenge, and deGrasse Tyson has declared it to be &quot;a unique urban phenomenon in the world, if not the universe.&quot;</p>

<p>What it <em>really</em> means though, is that there will be groups of people standing in the middle of the street this evening trying to capture the perfect shot to Instagram (#nofilter).</p>
