                            <p>Have you been looking for a new place to hang your hat, but can&apos;t quite find the perfect apartment that has both enough room for your cat <em>and</em> your stash of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130930090223/http://gothamist.com/2012/05/23/the_great_gatsby_trailer_is_here.php">Gatsby-sized bottles of champagne</a>? Look no further than DUMBO&apos;s 1 Main Street, where the Clocktower Penthouse is now on the market at a discounted price of $18 million (in 2009 it was going for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130930090223/http://gothamist.com/2010/07/20/brooklyns_most_expensive_apartment.php">$25 million</a>). Not only will you hover above the humble non-penthouse <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130930090223/http://gothamist.com/2012/03/15/anne_hathaway_moving_to_brooklyn.php">digs of Anne Hathaway</a>, but you&apos;ll get &quot;4 famous 14-foot glass clocks&quot; to lovingly encircle your living quarters.</p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130930090223/http://www.corcoran.com/nyc/Listings/Display/2546956">Corcoron</a> lists off the other highlights, which include a master suite (that &quot;will delight you with views of lower Manhattan&quot;), two guest bedrooms with their own bathrooms, a recreational space, sky roof cabana, and open deck. And no $18 million apartment is complete without a custom <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130930090223/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5k8nbpFKAsI">Willy Wonka-esque</a> glass-enclosed 3-story private elevator, featuring an architectural wraparound stairway. Hate-click through for a glimpse at a life you&apos;ll never have...
