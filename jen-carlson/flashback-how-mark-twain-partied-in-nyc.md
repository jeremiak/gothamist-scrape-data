                            <p>Dig deeper in to the old photo archive that the Museum of the City of New York <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140104021717/http://gothamist.com/2010/12/16/old_nyc.php">just released</a> and you&apos;ll find some treasures, like <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140104021717/http://collections.mcny.org/MCNY/C.aspx?VP3=ViewBox&amp;IT=ZoomImageTemplate01_VForm&amp;IID=2F3XC5U0AB9V&amp;CT=Image&amp;Flash=1#/ViewBox&amp;VBID=24UP1GEANJS&amp;IT=ThumbImageTemplate01_VForm&amp;CT=Search&amp;PN=5&amp;SH=1&amp;SF=1&amp;PPM=0&amp;CTID=K9YR82GPMK&amp;TM=">these photographs</a> of Mark Twain&apos;s 70th birthday dinner at Delmonico&apos;s restaurant in NYC. The event was held on December 5th, 1905, and had a huge guest list&#x2014;with names like Mrs. Woodrow Wilson, Dorothy Canfield, and Twain&apos;s nephew Samuel E. Moffett. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140104021717/http://www.delmonicosny.com/">Delmonico&apos;s</a> opened at 56 Beaver Street in 1837, and we&apos;re told that&#x2014;while there is still a Delmonico&apos;s there&#x2014;the original folded during Prohibition.</p>
