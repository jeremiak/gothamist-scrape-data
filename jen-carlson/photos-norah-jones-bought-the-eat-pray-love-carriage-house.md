					<p>Earlier this year a house that&apos;s reportedly been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032113/http://observer.com/2015/05/cobble-hill-eat-pray-love-house-sells-for-6-25m/">featured in</a> the film <em>Eat, Pray, Love</em>&#x2014;a charming carriage house in Cobble Hill&#x2014;sold for $6.25 million. Now <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032113/http://www.nydailynews.com/life-style/real-estate/nora-jones-mystery-buyer-eat-pray-love-house-article-1.2352491?utm_source=twitterfeed&amp;utm_medium=twitter">the Daily News</a> reports that the buyer was singer/songwriter/<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032113/http://gothamist.com/2010/04/01/norah_jones_1.php">Brooklyn real estate queen</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032113/http://gothamist.com/tags/norahjones">Norah Jones</a>. The paper made two &quot;Feels Like Home&quot; &quot;jokes&quot; in their reportage on this story.</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032113/http://www.corcoran.com/nyc/Listings/Display/3099028">Pacific Street house</a> was formerly a firehouse, dating back to the 1840s, and contains a wood-burning stove, wood beams, plank floors, and a greenhouse out back.</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="phpvl6tSePM.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032113im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/phpvl6tSePM.jpg" width="640" height="360" class="image-none"> </span></p>

<p>It&apos;s currently configured as a two-unit home, but Jones will be transforming it into one large palace... probably from money made via her adult contemporary pop songs, and not her <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160427032113/http://gothamist.com/2006/05/13/norah_jones_goe.php">struggling punk band</a>. Here is a short film on the property&#x2014;click play and watch it while listening to &quot;Come Away With Me&quot; backwards and, well, <em>you&apos;ll see</em>...</p>
