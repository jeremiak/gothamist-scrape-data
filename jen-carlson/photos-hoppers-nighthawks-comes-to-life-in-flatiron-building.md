</div></div>					<p>Last night The Whitney unveiled and lit up their life-size 3D installation of Edward Hopper&apos;s &quot;Nighthawks &quot;painting. As we <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172904/http://gothamist.com/2013/08/15/famous_nighthawks_painting_to_be_re.php">noted yesterday</a>, the famous work features a 1940s diner which <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172904/http://gothamist.com/2010/06/09/nighhawks_diner.php">never actually existed</a>, but the museum claims it was inspired by the Flatiron building (the two have similar shapes), where their installation is housed. </p>

<p>Click through for a look at it, but go see it yourself anytime through October 6th (which also marks the end of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172904/http://whitney.org/Exhibitions/HopperDrawing">their Hopper exhibition</a>)&#x2014;preferably at night. You won&apos;t even have to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504172904/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/24/rain_room.php">wait on line</a> for anything!</p>
