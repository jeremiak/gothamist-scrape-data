</div></div>					<p>Yesterday, during some appropriately wintery weather, Prospect Park&apos;s new Lefrak Center opened amongst 26 acres of restored parkland in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428040007/http://www.prospectpark.org/about/lakeside">the Lakeside Section</a> of the park. </p>

<p>Mayor Bloomberg, BBP Marty Markowitz, Parks Commissioner Veronica White, and the President of the Prospect Park Alliance Emily Lloyd cut the ribbon at a ceremony opening up the center, which features a cafe, event space, and <em>two</em> ice skating rinks (one open, one covered)&#x2014;though one will be used for roller skating during the warmer seasons.</p>

<p>This was all part of the final phase of a larger $74 million restoration and redesign of an underutilized section of Prospect Park, and Lloyd explained how the undertaking was funded, noting that &quot;The Prospect Park Alliance and its individual, corporate, and foundation donors played a critical role in making the project a reality, raising close to $20 million&#x2014;nearly 30% of the funds for the entire project. Because of this generous private giving, we attracted significant public investment to make our plans a reality. Plus the contribution of our naming donors, the LeFraks, means that Lakeside will remain an attraction for the next 100 years.&quot; The center is named for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150428040007/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_J._LeFrak">Samuel J. and Ethel LeFrak</a>. </p>

<p>The recreational center will open to the public this Friday, December 20th. Hours: Monday through Thursday 11 a.m. to 6 p.m.; Friday and Saturday 9 a.m. to 10 p.m.; Sundays 9 a.m. to 8 p.m. Admission: $6 (weekdays) and $8 (weekends), with skate rentals for $5.</p>
