</div></div>					<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180423/http://todseelie.com/">Tod Seelie</a>, whose work you&apos;ve seen around here, has a photography book coming out this fall, titled <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180423/http://www.mcnallyjackson.com/book/9783791348551"><em>Bright Lights: Photographs of Another New York</em></a>. The book will feature some of his most stunning shots from the city, taken over about a 15 year period&#x2014;and as the book description notes, &quot;In Seelie&apos;s city, cars burst into flames, punks knock each other off mutant bicycles, and women crowd surf in high heels.&quot; You&apos;ll get to travel high atop bridges, go way underground, and see what happens at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180423/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/24/photos_illegal_party_in_an_abandone.php#photo-1">those secret adventures</a> you never hear about until it&apos;s too late. </p>

<p>This Thursday he&apos;ll be previewing the book at Slideluck in Bushwick at 7 p.m. (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504180423/http://slideluck.com/bushwick-ii/">details + RSVP info here</a>)&#x2014;but before that, click through for a glimpse into Seelie&apos;s darker, deranged, and more vibrant side of the city.</p>
