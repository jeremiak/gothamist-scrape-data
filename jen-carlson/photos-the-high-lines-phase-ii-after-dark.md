</div></div>					<p>Did you hear the High Line opened up Phase II of their elevated park this week? <em>Of course you did</em>, but for the latecomers, we&apos;ll catch you up with some links, which will also describe our nine year long history with the latest unveiled space.</p><ul><li>We first journeyed onto the tracks <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141015010314/http://gothamist.com/2011/05/27/flashback_high_line_2002.php">in 2002</a>, which wasn&apos;t exactly allowed back then. </li><li>Nine years later (just weeks ago, in fact) we scaled buildings to get <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141015010314/http://gothamist.com/2011/05/20/exclusive_illicit_photos_of_high_li.php">aerial views</a> of the not-yet-opened Phase II section. <br>
</li><li>After all that, we were still invited to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141015010314/http://gothamist.com/2011/06/07/check_out_the_new_section_of_the_hi.php">official opening</a> on Tuesday, where we checked out the unidentifiable (to us) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141015010314/http://gothamist.com/2011/06/07/high_line_phase_ii_check_out_this_h.php">horticulture</a>, up close and in full daylight.<br>
</li><li>Then we went back <em>at night</em> to discover things get <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141015010314/http://gothamist.com/2011/06/09/after_dark_the_high_lines_phase_ii.php">very R-rated up there <em>after dark</em></a>.</li></ul><br>
And now you can click through for our latest photos: a more G-rated look through the space at night. As for the next step, Phase III... we were there already <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20141015010314/http://gothamist.com/2011/05/27/flashback_high_line_2002.php#photo-2">back in 2002</a>, and will have more to come as soon as we&apos;re totally over how gorgeous Phase II is.<p></p>
