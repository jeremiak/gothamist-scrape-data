</div></div>					<p>When we were invited to check out the new, unfinished section of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150918055734/http://gothamist.com/tags/highline">the High Line</a> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150918055734/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/20/bloomberg_officially_breaks_ground.php#photo-1">again</a>), we were warned: &quot;The terrain is uneven, the site is overgrown with self-seeded wildflowers, grasses, and shrubs, and there are rail tracks and spikes along the way, so everyone must wear closed-toe shoes. No high heels or flip flops.&quot; Old school, just like it should be. </p>

<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150918055734/http://samhorine.com/">Sam Horine</a> dropped by yesterday to brave the spiked ground, and delivered us these gorgeous shots from atop the Phase 3 tracks, which will open next year. But if you want in before then, the Friends of the High Line are still offering <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150918055734/http://www.thehighline.org/tours">tours</a> three days a week... and you&apos;ll get the same warning we did.</p>

<p>As <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150918055734/http://www.nytimes.com/2013/06/10/nyregion/high-line-offers-a-walk-on-the-wild-side.html?_r=0">the NY Times</a> recently noted, this final section will be different from the previous two&#x2014;&quot;instead of modernistic benches and showy perennials, there will be rusted tracks overgrown with Queen Anne&#x2019;s lace and goldenrod.&quot;</p>

<p>But amidst it all will be an office tower, straddling the elevated park. This will belong to Coach, who donated $5 million to Friends of the High Line. But surely the classic company will be tasteful in their design of this. Along with that, the overgrown tracks, and a sick view <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150918055734/http://instagram.com/p/afOl_YCvjW/">of the rail yards</a>, the new section will also have a &quot;postindustrial jungle gym comprising a series of exposed beams covered in a soft play surface.&quot;<br>
