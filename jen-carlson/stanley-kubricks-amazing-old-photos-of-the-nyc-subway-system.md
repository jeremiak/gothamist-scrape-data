</div></div>					<p>These photos were all taken by Stanley Kubrick during his time as photographer for Look Magazine. The series of images, from the Museum of the City of New York&apos;s expansive archives, is titled &quot;Life and Love on the New York City Subway.&quot; The images were taken in 1946 and show straphangers from that time... as well as a gum machine right there on the subway platform. Back in the day, the platforms had candy, cigarette, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504235050/http://gothamist.com/2013/04/26/photos_the_subway_in_the_1960s.php#photo-2">and even horoscope machines</a>! These existed for decades to come, and you can see some of them in this video showing the system in the 1950s&#x2014;which is pretty terrific in its own right: </p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="480" src="//web.archive.org/web/20150504235050if_/http://www.youtube.com/embed/yxbn8jDp0Gk" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>More photos of 1940s New York through Kubrick&apos;s lens can be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150504235050/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/25/stanley_kubrick.php#photo-1">seen here</a>.</p>
