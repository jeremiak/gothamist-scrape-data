</div></div>					<p>One of the greatest annual events we look forward to each summer is The Met rooftop&apos;s seasonal rooftop exhibit. Throughout the years the artists given the massive space have ranged from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515013941/http://gothamist.com/2007/04/09/sol_lewitt_1929.php">Sol LeWitt</a> to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515013941/http://gothamist.com/2009/04/24/a_silver_tree_grows_in_manhattan.php">Roxy Paine</a> to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515013941/http://gothamist.com/2008/04/22/jeff_koons_atop.php#photo-1">Jeff Koons</a> to the awesome (and interactive) <a href="Doug and Mike Starn">bamboo forest</a> in 2010. Last year we were a little bit disappointed with the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515013941/http://gothamist.com/2011/04/26/the_met_opens_roof_shows_off_sculpt.php">metal sculptures</a>, but this year&apos;s installation looks magnificent, particularly with the Manhattan backdrop. Click through for some stunning photos of Tom&#xE1;s Saraceno&apos;s Cloud City&#x2014;the 38-year-old&apos;s first major commissioned, site-specific installation.</p>

<p>The structure contains sixteen interconnected segments, measuring 54-feet long, 29-feet wide and 28-feet high. One visitor told <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515013941/http://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/travel/nys-met-museum-visitors-climb-to-cloud-nine-via-rooftop-art-by-argentinian-who-likes-bubbles/2012/05/16/gIQAms60TU_story.html">the Washington Post</a>, &#x201C;You are not necessarily sure-footed on where you are going to put your feet because of the Lucite floor and mirror floor, so you do it very, very slow. But otherwise, I thought it was really cool.&#x201D;</p>

<p>The exhibit is open <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150515013941/http://www.metmuseum.org/visit/plan-your-visit/dining-at-the-museum/roof-garden-cafe-and-martini-bar">during regular museum hours</a>, through November 4th.</p>
