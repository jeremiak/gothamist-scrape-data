</div></div>					<p>While we&apos;re all waiting in line to see <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501025736/http://gothamist.com/tags/alexandermcqueen">Alexander McQueen</a>&apos;s handmade masterpieces up close, let&apos;s take a look at what&apos;s at the end of the <strike>rainbow</strike> really long line. Click through for a little glimpse at what you&apos;ll see inside the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501025736/http://blog.metmuseum.org/alexandermcqueen/">Savage Beauty</a> exhibit. And if you simply can&apos;t fathom the idea of waiting in line for two hours, the museum would be happy to let you in on Monday... for <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501025736/http://gothamist.com/2011/06/01/met_extends_ragingly_popular_mcquee.php">50 bucks</a>.</p>

<p>If you do decide to wait your turn, here&apos;s something to ponder over the hours: Is McQueen this year&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501025736/http://gothamist.com/tags/marinaabramovi%C4%87">Abramovi&#x107;</a>? Last year the performance artist&apos;s exhibit at MoMA brought long lines... and rumors that VIPs were getting <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150501025736/http://gothamist.com/2010/05/13/the_artist_is_present_for_vips.php"><em>to cut</em></a> those long lines.</p>
