					<p>Blame <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602214637/https://www.etsy.com/shop/Hoardaculture">Etsy</a> or Brooklyn or high school biology teachers or just our own morbid curiosities, but dead animals are everywhere. They&apos;re in our museums. They&apos;re in our flea markets. They&apos;re on our restaurant walls. They&apos;re on the shelf in your girlfriend&apos;s bedroom. But how does that taxidermy sausage <em>get made</em>? You&apos;ll need a strong stomach and about five hours to <em>Weekend At Bernie&apos;s</em> a tiny mouse. </p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="himouse15.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602214637im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/himouse15.jpg" width="640" height="486" class="image-none"> </span><br>
<span class="photo_caption">(Photo by Amy Finkel/Gothamist)</span></p>

<p>Photographer (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602214637/http://www.fureverfilm.com/">and documentarian</a>) Amy Finkel recently dropped by a class hosted by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602214637/http://hoardaculture.com/about.html">Amber at Hoardaculture</a>, returning with these photos. She noted, &quot;I cut out the ones that were way way too gross&quot;... but do not look at these before lunch.</p>

<p>If you want to try it out yourself, you&apos;ll need to block out some time this Sunday, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602214637/http://www.eventbrite.com/e/mouse-taxidermy-class-tickets-17414352798?aff=erelexporg">drop about $120</a>. The class takes place in the back of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602214637/http://shop.roseredandlavender.com/">a really gorgeous flower shop</a>, so you&apos;ll also be surrounded by LIFE. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170602214637/http://gothamist.com/2013/07/19/how_to_taxidermy_a_sparrow.php">Here&apos;s our primer on attending a taxidermy class</a>, which you may want to check out first.</p>
