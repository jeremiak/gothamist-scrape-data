author: Jen Carlson

title: Polar Bears Plunge into '09
---

<p>Yesterday afternoon, as the air temperature was around the 20-degree mark, the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111116190428/http://www.polarbearclub.org/">Polar Bear Club</a> celebrated New Year&apos;s Day in their traditional style: in the freezing cold waters off Coney Island. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111116190428/http://www.time.com/time/nation/article/0,8599,1869194,00.html">TIME Magazine</a> takes a look at the tradition and talked to the club&apos;s head honcho. They note that the annual plunge has been going on since 1903, and that this year, nearly 700 were expected to take a dip while thousands watched from land. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111116190428/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=27xfY1ALju8">The AP</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111116190428/http://www.newyorkology.com/archives/2009/01/video_coney_isl.php">NewYorkology</a> have some video, and there&apos;s more footage <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111116190428/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eEHNgWRKHG8">here</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111116190428/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZyVX3SaM-9o">here</a>&#x2014;though it all has us wondering if the people who don&apos;t fully submerge themselves in the frosty water are <em>truly</em> worthy of their &quot;I Did It&quot; certificates.</p> <div class="galleryEase" id="gallery-0">
