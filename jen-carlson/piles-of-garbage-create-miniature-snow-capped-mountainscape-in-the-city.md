</div></div>					<p>The Andes, the Himalayas, Mount Everest... these have nothing on New York City&apos;s miniature snow-capped mountain ranges, which are smaller in size, but larger in number. The impressive mountain ranges can be found on every street in every borough, and were a collaboration between Man and Mother Nature. </p>

<p>The piles of garbage have been growing as trash pick up gets delayed <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160329065841/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/18/more_snow_kill_them_all.php">with every snowfall</a>, but you know what they say: one man&apos;s trash is another man&apos;s picturesque mountainscape! Until the snow melts, and the once beautiful snow caps melt into miniature rivers, carrying the soggy garbage through the city like a rubbish-filled Venice Canal.</p>

<p>Mayor de Blasio alerted us of this city-wide masterpiece last week, noting that garbage trucks would be used for snow removal, thus delaying trash removal. Anyway, New Yorkers <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160329065841/http://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20140218/williamsburg/new-yorkers-raise-stink-over-mountains-of-trash-piling-up-after-snowstorms">can&apos;t get enough</a> of it!</p>
