					<p><em>The views around New York City&#x2014;whether you&apos;re on the ground or 100-floors over it, whether you&apos;re a tourist or native&#x2014;are spectacular to behold, and you don&apos;t need to be an urban explorer leaning precariously over a ledge to enjoy some of the best ones. Over the next week, we&apos;ll be visiting some of the highest legally-accessed views around Manhattan, starting now with One World Trade Center. Click through for a look at these magnificent vistas, and below are some facts about the building.</em></p>

<p><strong>12 Facts About 1WTC</strong><br>
</p><ul><li>The building&#x2014;including the spire&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2013/11/12/america_fck_yeah_1_world_trade_cent.php#photo-1">stands at 1,776 feet tall</a>, symbolizing the year of America&#x2019;s declaration of independence.<br>
</li><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/21/1_wtc_observatory_ooh_ahh.php#photo-1">The observation deck is on the 100th floor</a>, at 1,362 feet high, with a glass parapet bringing it to 1,368 feet, which was the height of the Twin Towers.<br>
</li><li>There are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/https://oneworldobservatory.com/restaurant/">three dining options</a> on the 101st floor.<br>
</li><li>The 104-story building opened in 2014, and includes <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/https://www.wtc.com/about/buildings/1-world-trade-center">3 million rentable square feet</a> of space. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2014/11/04/conde_nast_employees_at_1_world_tra.php#photo-1">Cond&#xE9; Nast is the most high-profile company</a> that relocated to the 1WTC offices.<br>
</li><li>A proposal for the site in 2002 from Daniel Libeskind included a scheme called &quot;Vertical World Gardens,&quot; featuring plants and trees representing various areas (desert, tropical, etc). It was one of the many design features that didn&apos;t move forward.<br>
</li><li>You can see a lot of landmarks from the Observation Deck, including the Empire State Building, which is 322-feet shorter.<br>
</li><li>The transportation hub <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2016/03/03/oculus_open_wtc.php">is not only Instagram-worthy</a>, it will connect you to 11 subway lines and the PATH. The latter is linked via a 600-foot long marble-clad walkway that runs below West Street.<br>
</li><li>A group of young men base-jumped from 1WTC <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/25/video_watch_wtc_base_jumpers_dive_o.php#photo-1">in 2014</a>, filming on their way down.<br>
</li><li>A teen also snuck to the top of 1WTC <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/22/teen_sorry_not_sorry.php">in 2014</a>, aided by an easygoing elevator operator, a sleepy security guard, and the ridiculous utter lack of security cameras at the site.<br>
</li><li>The elevator to the Observatory shows a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2015/04/20/original_wtc_briefly_seen_in_new_1.php">pretty remarkable time-lapse video showing the development of lower Manhattan</a> from pre-Colonial times to present day.<br>
</li><li>During his 2013 residency, the British street artist/anarchist Banksy wrote an op-ed&#x2014;rejected by the NY Times&#x2014;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/27/banksy_says_latest_greenpoint_piece.php#photo-1">that said 1WTC should never have been built</a>.<br>
</li><li>Vendors may try to rip you off outside of 1WTC&#x2014;hot tip: never buy <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/20/ripoff_hot_dog_vendor.php">a $30 dirty water dog</a>.</li></ul><p></p>

<p>Tickets to visit 1WTC&apos;s observatory cost anywhere <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104425/https://oneworldobservatory.com/tickets/">from $32 to $92</a>, depending on when you go and what package you purchase.</p>
