</div></div>					<p>Yesterday the Museum of the City of New York <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429005331/https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151942580422690&amp;set=a.133685672689.108769.24425672689&amp;type=1">announced</a> that they&apos;ve <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429005331/http://bit.ly/19MwFRW">been digitizing</a> over 1,200 recently acquired photographs by Edmund V. Gillon, mostly taken in the 1970s (though some are from the &apos;80s). Going through the collection we noticed a lot of familiar buildings, which means a lot of these places are still standing today... even if most look a little worse for the wear, or have become storefronts or luxury apartments. Click through for a look at 14 different locations around Manhattan that look the same today as they did in the 1970s.</p>
