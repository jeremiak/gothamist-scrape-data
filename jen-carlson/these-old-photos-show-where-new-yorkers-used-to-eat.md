</div></div>					<p>From the Tavern on the Green in 1934 to the famous Cavanaugh&apos;s Restaurant on West 23rd in 1938 to an old dining shack in Sheepshead Bay in 1953&#x2014;click through for a look at where New Yorkers used to eat back in the day. The captions (along with the photographs) are all courtesy of the NYC Municipal Archives, who just released <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211520/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/24/nyc_municipal_archives_releases_nea.php">nearly one million photos</a> online. Now if only there were a million hours in a day or <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429211520/http://menus.nypl.org/">the NYPL Old Menus</a> crew could combine forces with the Municipal Archives and fully piece together these old spots for us.</p>
