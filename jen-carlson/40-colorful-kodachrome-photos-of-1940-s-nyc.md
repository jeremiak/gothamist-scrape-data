					<p>It&apos;s rare we can look back at an era of NYC like the 1940s and see it through such sharp, colorful images. Thanks to Charles W. Cushman, a tourist here during that time with a knack for photography, it&apos;s possible. We&apos;ve looked at Cushman&apos;s vast archives before&#x2014;which <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/https://www.amazon.com/Day-Its-Color-Photographic-Vanishing/dp/0199772339">span the nation</a> and overseas over a few decade&#x2014;all in all containing 14,500 Kodachrome color slides. He donated the images to his alma mater, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://webapp1.dlib.indiana.edu/cushman/projectInfo/index.jsp">Indiana University</a>, and they digitized everything after nearly tossing them in the garbage. Check out his <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2016/01/04/1960_nyc_cushman.php">1960s NYC photos here</a>, and click through for his shots of New York City in the early 1940s. </p>

<p>Here are a few things that were going on in 1940s NYC: <br>
</p><ul><li>Nazi saboteurs <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2015/10/14/grand_central_hilter_nazis.php">tried to infiltrate Grand Central&apos;s secret basement</a>.<br>
</li><li>A NYC bus driver spontaneously took a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/28/busmans_florida_holiday.php">joy ride to Florida</a> during his shift. <br>
</li><li><em>Someone</em> had a sweet party on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2014/06/17/photos_nyc_in_the_summer_of_49.php#photo-1">their terrace</a>. <br>
</li><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2012/06/05/inside_new_yorks_new_yorkiest_joint.php#photo-1">New York&apos;s New Yorkiest joint</a> was expanding. <br>
</li><li>LIFE magazine taught us <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2014/03/14/how_to_kiss_according_to_life_magaz.php#photo-1">how to kiss</a>.<br>
</li><li>A sailor must have read that article, and used his skills on a nurse <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2010/06/26/the_photographer_behind_the_kiss.php">in Times Square</a>, creating one of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/V-J_Day_in_Times_Square">iconic images</a> of the WWII era.<br>
</li><li>Our mass transit system <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/01/1940s_subway_video.php">looked archaic af</a>.<br>
</li><li>Weegee was out there taking <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2012/01/04/grisly_crime_scene_photos_from_1930.php#photo-1">grisly crime photos</a>.<br>
</li><li>A peanut Butter &amp; Bacon sandwich <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2012/10/23/airport_food_in_the_1940s.php#photo-1">went for 15 cents</a> at LaGuardia Airport.<br>
</li><li>It was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2010/11/02/flashback_jazz.php#photo-1">the Golden Age of Jazz</a>, man.<br>
</li><li>Pennsylvania Station existed. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2014/02/10/watch_the_beautiful_old_penn_statio.php">The good one</a>.<br>
</li><li>Everyone fell in love with Eames <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170925212804/http://gothamist.com/2013/06/13/moma_2.php#photo-1">at a MoMA exhibit</a>.<br>
</li><li>And there was a big push for tourism:</li></ul><p></p>
