	<p>This Sunday, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130604133034/http://gothamist.com/2012/04/09/photos_easter_parade_2012.php#photo-1">bonnets and bunnies</a> will <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130604133034/http://gothamist.com/2013/03/28/guide_to_the_fifth_avenue_easter_pa.php">take over 5th Avenue</a> for the annual Easter Parade, and according to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130604133034/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Easter_parade#The_New_York_parade">The Internet,</a> &quot;from the 1880s through the 1950s, New York&apos;s parade was one of the main cultural expressions of Easter in the United States.&quot; The parade grew out of the city&apos;s ornamented churches, eventually expanding to elaborate floral displays, and fashion... most notably: hats. In fact, in the 1880s the annual event was already &quot;a vast spectacle of fashion&quot; as much as it was a religious observance. Click through for a look at Easter in New York City, from the 1880s through the 1970s.</p>
