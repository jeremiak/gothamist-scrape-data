</div></div>					<p>On July 4th, 1976, New York City hosted a massive bicentennial celebration, which included a procession of tall ships and fireworks&#x2014;it brought <em>a lot</em> of people downtown. Recently, a remnant of the day was unearthed near 4 South Street in the Seaport area, which is currently subject to some <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20151110063844/http://gothamist.com/2014/11/20/south_street_seaport.php">controversial development</a>. Underneath a facade that was taken down, this perfectly preserved piece of the bicentennial was discovered.</p>

<p>Reader Mary Pendergast sent these photos along, noting that there&apos;s &quot;at least 30 feet of graffiti all dating from 7/4/76.&quot; The &quot;graffiti&quot; is more akin to what you&apos;d find etched onto a tree in the countryside&#x2014;hearts, declarations of love, So-And-So Was Here, that kind of thing. The markings all look like they could have been put up yesterday, but they&apos;re nearing 40 years old. Click through for a look. </p>
