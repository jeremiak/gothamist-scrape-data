title: The NYPL's Outdoor Reading Rooms Return For Summer

<span class="photo_caption">(Photo by Jonathan Blanc/The New York Public Library)</span></p>

<p>Can&apos;t pop open a Corona and stare at the turquoise waters while flipping through <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170510233147/http://gothamist.com/2014/06/05/summer_reading_recommendations.php">your beach read</a> in the sand? Well, the NYPL has a fine, free alternative: their outdoor reading rooms <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170510233147/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/05/nypl_opening_outdoor_reading_room.php">will reopen</a> tomorrow, and this year they&apos;ve got swanky umbrellas to shade the sun from your pale winter skin.</p>

<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170510233147/http://www.nypl.org/events/outdoor-reading-room">Outdoor Reading Room</a> will be located on the plaza of the Stephen A. Schwarzman Building on 5th Avenue, where there will be chairs and artificial grass to sit upon. If solitary reading isn&apos;t your thing, they&apos;ll also have author talks and other events, food trucks, and librarians who will help you find your soulmatebook. </p>

<blockquote>The reading room will include colorful lounge chairs arranged on a plush &quot;grass&quot; carpet of soy bean based turf. Its mid-century modern design was inspired by the iconic 1970 Slim Aarons photograph Poolside Gossip, which epitomized the chic poolside social scene of the times.</blockquote>

<blockquote>Featured in the photo is a period-appropriate DoubleDecker umbrella, the same style that will grace the outdoor reading room. Custom-crafted expressly for the Reading Room and donated by California-based Santa Barbara Designs, the shape of the canopy valance on the four donated umbrellas matches the design of the dental moldings on the Library&#x2019;s exterior.</blockquote>

<p>They&apos;ll be open Mondays through Saturdays, 10 a.m. to 7 p.m., until July 17th. After that, maybe we can urge them to move the patio furniture indoors?</p>
