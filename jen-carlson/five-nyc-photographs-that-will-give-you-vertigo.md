</div></div>					<p>Yesterday we revisited <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150330063506/http://gothamist.com/2011/04/18/that_time_sol_lewitt_removed_three.php">Sol LeWitt&apos;s altered aerial</a> of &quot;part of Manhattan,&quot; and while pondering the definition of art, we got distracted by other aerial images of New York City... particularly the vertigo-inducing ones. Here are five photographs that will give you that special feeling of dizziness (if you&apos;re into that sort of thing), plus one bonus image for <em>reverse</em> vertigo (if that even exists).</p>

<p>Of course, you can&apos;t talk about vertigo without recalling Alfred Hitchcock&apos;s 1958 classic film starring James Stewart and Kim Novak. Hitchcock used the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150330063506/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dolly_zoom">dolly zoom</a> to represent the sensation of vertigo&#x2014;check it out in the trailer below.</p>
