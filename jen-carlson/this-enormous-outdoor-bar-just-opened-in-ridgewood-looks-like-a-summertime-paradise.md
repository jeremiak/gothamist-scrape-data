					<p>Last night a new spot opened in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007074628/http://gothamist.com/tags/ridgewood">Ridgewood</a>, this time far off the Seneca Avenue stretch that&apos;s seen <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007074628/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/12/ridgewood_venue.php">a number of openings</a> this year. This one is right on the border of Bushwick, in a more industrial area, but you&apos;ll feel like you&apos;re in the country. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161007074628/http://nowadays.nyc/">Nowadays</a> is like a giant, suburban backyard, with plenty of picnic tables, a giant open gravel yard, and grassy hills. There&apos;s a ping pong table, checkers, and other games, too. </p>

<p>While there&apos;s little shelter aside from umbrellas and pop-up tents if it rains&#x2014;the place&#x2014;run by Justin Carter, Eamon Harkin and Botanica&apos;s Mark Connell, according to <a href="www.grubstreet.com/2015/06/nowadays-ridgewood-opens.html">Grub Street</a>&#x2014;does have just about everything else. On the menu you&apos;ll find items like burger ($9) + fries ($4), pulled pork + grilled corn, as well as vegetarian and vegan options like grilled cheese and avocado salad.</p>
