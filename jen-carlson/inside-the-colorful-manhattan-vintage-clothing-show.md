					<p>A few times a year <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708073311/http://manhattanvintage.com/">the Manhattan Vintage Clothing Sale</a> sets up shop inside of the Metropolitan Pavilion, and allows people from the Present Day to travel back to a time when clothing was, somehow, <em>better</em>. The show brings the best vintage vendors together to sell and show their wares, and we dropped by this weekend to check it out.</p>

<p>David Ornstein and his wife Maureen, of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708073311/http://www.daybreakvintage.com/">Daybreak Vintage Rentals</a>, started the whole thing over a decade ago, and still run it with their sons. The family, through their rental company, also supply era-appropriate clothing to TV shows and movies (including <em>Catch Me If You Can</em>, <em>Boardwalk Empire</em>, <em>Factory Girl</em>, <em>Mad Men</em>, and the upcoming <em>Vinyl</em>).  Every show is given a theme by David, and this year the special focus was on <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708073311/http://gothamist.com/2016/02/02/bowie_clothes_on_display.php">David Bowie</a>, and Fargo (through a special collection <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708073311/http://manhattanvintage.com/shop/current-collection/">sold online</a>).</p>

<p><span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;"> <img alt="mackiedetail16.jpg" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708073311im_/http://gothamist.com/attachments/arts_jen/mackiedetail16.jpg" width="640" height="427" class="image-none"> </span><br>
<span class="photo_caption">(Photo by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708073311/http://www.fureverfilm.com/">Amy Finkel</a>/Gothamist)</span></p>

<p>The show features one of the greatest collections of vintage clothing and antique textiles, from the 18th to 20th centuries. You&apos;ll get everything from vintage Dior bangles to Bob Mackie dresses designed for Mitzi Gaynor&apos;s variety show. While this one is all packed up, they&apos;ll be back <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708073311/http://manhattanvintage.com/upcoming-shows/">April 8th and 9th</a> (and again in October)&#x2014;until then, click through for a look at some of the more enviable items.</p>
