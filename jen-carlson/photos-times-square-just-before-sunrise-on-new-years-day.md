					<p>Times Square during its transitional hour just <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708080915/http://gothamist.com/2014/08/20/times_square_twilight.php">around 5 a.m.</a> is a strange scene, when one of the busiest places on earth takes a pause and momentarily presents as empty and quiet. This movement from a state of chaos into total stillness is even more jarring in the early morning hours of New Year&apos;s Day, when crowds of NYE revelers have departed and left a colorful mess behind. </p>

<p>Photographer <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708080915/http://www.gretchenrobinette.com/">Gretchen Robinette</a> ventured to the Crossroads of the World at 5:45 a.m. on January 1st, where she found street sweepers covered in confetti and stragglers white-knuckling on to the night. And the DSNY shoveling some of the city&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708080915/http://gothamist.com/2015/12/29/times_square_nye_confetti.php">prettiest trash</a>...</p>
