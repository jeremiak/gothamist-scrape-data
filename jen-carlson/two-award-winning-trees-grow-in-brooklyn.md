</div></div>					<p>Congratulations to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140437/http://www.bbg.org/">Brooklyn Botanic Garden</a> for housing not one, but <em>two</em> champion trees. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150429140437/http://www.1010wins.com/2-New-York-State-Champion-Trees-Grow-in-Brooklyn/5525819">1010 Wins</a> reports that &quot;The New York State Department of Environmental Conservation has named two trees at the Brooklyn Botanic Garden state champions &#x2014; meaning they are the largest of their species on record in the state.&quot; The Kansas hawthorn is 31&apos; tall, and the Carolina holly is a whopping 35&apos; tall. Together they are the first two trees in New York City to receive the honor, and they were presented their awards today.</p>
