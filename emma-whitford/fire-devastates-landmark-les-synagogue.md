					<p>A three-alarm fire ripped through an empty, landmarked synagogue on the Lower East Side Sunday night, according to the FDNY. Nearly 140 firefighters battled to bring the blaze under control for about five hours, from p.m. to shortly after midnight. </p>

<p>The cause of the fire is still under investigation, an FDNY spokesman said Monday. No civilians were rescued from the building, and no civilian injuries were reported. Two firefighters were treated for minor injuries. </p>

<p>Beth Hamedrash Hagodol synagogue, at 64 Norfolk Street at the corner of Broome Street, was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170517011917/http://www.neighborhoodpreservationcenter.org/db/bb_files/BETH-HAMEDRASH.pdf">landmarked in 1967</a> and has been shut down since 2007, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170517011917/http://www.thelodownny.com/leslog/2012/08/rabbi-seeks-partners-for-restoration-reuse-of-distressed-norfolk-street-synagogue.html">according to the Lo Down</a>. </p>

<p>The Department of Buildings website shows a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170517011917/http://a810-bisweb.nyc.gov/bisweb/ECBQueryByNumberServlet?requestid=3&amp;allbin=1004155&amp;ecbin=34915919M">class-one violation</a> for a sagging and breaking first-floor ceiling that has been open since 2011. </p>
