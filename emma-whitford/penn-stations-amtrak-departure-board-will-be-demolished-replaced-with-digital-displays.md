title: Penn Station's Amtrak Departure Board Will Be Demolished & Replaced With Digital Displays
---

	
										<p>Amtrak is in the process of replacing the central departure board at Penn Station&#x2014;which you&apos;ve probably squinted at while sitting on a duffle bag and drinking a Jamba Juice strawberry smoothie&#x2014;with digital information screens. The switch over to digital is scheduled for completion this year, and Amtrak will demolish the existing departure screen in the process. </p>

<center><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">You know that archaic <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170528/https://twitter.com/Amtrak">@Amtrak</a> departure board in NY Penn Station? It&apos;s finally being replaced with digital screens. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170528/https://t.co/xb4ozYLh9d">pic.twitter.com/xb4ozYLh9d</a></p>&#x2014; Jason Rabinowitz (@AirlineFlyer) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170528/https://twitter.com/AirlineFlyer/status/767520042158264320">August 22, 2016</a></blockquote>

<p>&quot;We have a project underway to improve the passenger information displays at New York Penn Station,&quot; said Amtrak spokesman Craig Schulz in an e-mail to Gothamist on Monday. &quot;We&apos;re making good progress on it and hope to have it online later this year.&quot; </p>

<p>&quot;The idea is to improve the passenger experience through more dynamic, easier to read and strategically located information points throughout the concourse,&quot; he added. According to Amtrak, the new boards are intended to improve the flow of passengers through the station and make departure times more accessible to passengers with disabilities. </p>

<p>Reporter <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170528/https://twitter.com/AirlineFlyer">Jason Rabinowitz</a> tweeted out the news on Sunday, clarifying that while the Penn Station departure board is old, it doesn&apos;t make a nice, vintage flippy sound. </p>

<p>But while New Yorkers view Penn Station more or less unanimously as an airless, dignity-crushing, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161027170528/http://gothamist.com/2016/06/29/penn_station_lirr_why_god.php">optimism-evaporating subterranean maze</a> that simultaneously smells like Dunkin Donuts and Subway, the departure board is, for some, far from Penn Station&#x2019;s most pressing problem.</p>
