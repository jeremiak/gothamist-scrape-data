										<p>While <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092939/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/25/empire_state_lightning.php">Monday night&apos;s storm</a> enraptured the Instagramming masses with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092939/http://gothamist.com/2016/07/25/weather_so_weathery_now.php">pink lightning</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092939/https://www.instagram.com/p/BITDxoGgBYn/">hail</a>, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110092939/https://www.instagram.com/p/BITe8crjseN/?taken-by=gothamist">rainbows</a>, the more discerning urban explorers headed underground, where subway platforms were transformed into a tropical rain forest&#x2014;humid with human sweat and cooled, in places, by cascading flood waters.    <br>
  <br>
Like the R train platform at Jay Street in Brooklyn, as captured by tipster Mark C. Plug in your headphones, close your eyes, and it&apos;s almost meditative...</p>
