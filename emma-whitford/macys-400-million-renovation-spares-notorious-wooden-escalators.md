										<p>Twenty wooden <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/http://gothamist.com/2010/07/03/child_loses_finger_in_macys_escalat.php">finger-slicing</a> and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/https://www.instagram.com/p/-T--gSk7ZO/">photogenic</a> escalators carved from oak and ash have survived a $400 million, four year renovation aimed at turning Macy&apos;s Herald Square into an &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/http://www.nytimes.com/2014/11/02/business/for-macys-a-makeover-on-34th-street.html">upscale global shopping destination</a>,&quot; to the relief of many sentimental <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/http://forgotten-ny.com/2007/01/macys-wooden-escalators/">Forgotten New York</a> junkies. </p>

Bu-da-bumbum, bu-da-bumbum, bu-da-bumbum&quot;) points out that the best spot to gawk at the very vintage and authentic artifacts is on the sixth floor of the store, where four escalators converge at one landing&#x2014;No. 55 and 65 going up, No. 54 and 64 going down. </p>

<p>Otis, the world&apos;s biggest &quot;vertical transportation system&quot; company, installed all of the surviving escalators, plus an additional 20 that have since been renovated with metal treads, at a time when Otis still had a patent on the term. (A set of original Otis elevators were installed in 1902, with flat steps.) </p>

<p>But while the remaining wooden treads are instagrammable, they&apos;re also wide-set enough to catch a baby&apos;s finger. In 2005, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/http://gothamist.com/2005/10/17/macys_escalator_defingers_child.php">a little girl&apos;s finger was sliced off</a> when she tripped on them and, in 2006, a two-year-old&apos;s thumb was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/http://gothamist.com/2006/12/25/macys_escalator.php">nearly severed</a> during the Christmas rush. And in 2010 4-year-old Maxlee Gell <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/http://gothamist.com/2010/07/03/child_loses_finger_in_macys_escalat.php">lost a finger</a> when he leaned over to pick up a water bottle he&apos;d dropped on the escalator. </p>

<p>To appease fears, Macy&apos;s has added automatic switches that will stop the escalators in response to a sudden jolt. </p>

<p>But safety concerns have done little to slow so-called &quot;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/http://forgotten-ny.com/2007/01/macys-wooden-escalators/">handrailfanning</a>,&quot; the term for fanatically obsessing over and admiring <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=macy%27s+wooden+escalator&amp;page=1">these specific wooden escalators</a>&#x2014;an activity that&apos;s perhaps more authentic than <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160809231517/http://gothamist.com/2015/11/24/photos_holiday_windows_are_here_to.php">holiday window display</a> fanning. </p>
