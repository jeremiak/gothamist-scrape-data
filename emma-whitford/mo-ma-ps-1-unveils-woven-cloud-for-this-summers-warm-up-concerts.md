					<p>MoMA PS1 announced today the winner of its annual Young Architects Program, which picks an emerging architect and design team to take over the museum&apos;s courtyard for the duration of its annual <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708082838/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/28/photos_first_ps1_warm_up_of_the_sum.php#photo-1">summer Warm Up</a> concert series. </p>

<p>The 17th-annual installation, <em><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708082838/http://press.moma.org/2016/02/young-architects-program-2016/">Weaving the Courtyard</a></em>, comes from Mexico City-based <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708082838/http://www.escobedosoliz.net/">Escobedo Sol&#xED;z Studio</a>, and strays from the sculpture trend of recent years. Rather than break up the museum&apos;s courtyard with blobby or rigid forms, this year&apos;s winner will install a colorful yarn &quot;cloud&quot; overhead, woven through existing holes in the courtyard&apos;s concrete walls. The weave will vary in density, creating patches of shade and geometric patterning across the courtyard. </p>

<p>Like the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708082838/http://gothamist.com/2013/01/17/moma_ps1_gets_party_wall_for_this_s.php#photo-1">party wall</a>, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708082838/http://gothamist.com/2014/06/27/photos_get_ready_to_party_inside_ps.php#photo-1">fungus tower</a>, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160708082838/http://gothamist.com/2015/05/22/moma_ps1_lineup.php">glowing, plastic mesh water purification system</a> that preceded it, this year&apos;s installation will use only sustainable and eco-friendly materials. Come summer&apos;s end, the yarn will be intact and reusable. </p>

<p>According to the architects, the project as a whole will aim to be &quot;neither an object nor a sculpture standing in the courtyard, but a series of simple, powerful actions that generate new and different atmospheres.&quot;</p>

<p>There will also be a fresh-water wading pool in the back of the courtyard, where you&apos;ll be able to cool your heels and presumably take a break from Instagramming your friends against the yarn-scape. </p>
