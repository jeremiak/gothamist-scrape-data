<p>A man opened fire on a Queens house late Saturday night, striking a woman who was asleep inside, according to the NYPD. Surveillance footage shows the man walking along a residential sidewalk in Jamaica with his hands in his pockets, then firing multiple rounds at the home.</p>

<p><iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324214257if_/https://www.youtube.com/embed/R02n0WfYs4E?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p>

<p>Police said the shooting took place shortly before 11:30 p.m. on Saturday, near the corner of 171st Street and 105th Avenue in Jamaica. The victim, a 27-year-old woman, was asleep in bed on the first floor, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324214257/http://nypost.com/2017/03/21/dramatic-video-shows-gunman-firing-into-home-injuring-woman/">according to the NY Post</a>. She was struck once in her right ankle, sources <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170324214257/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/queens/queens-woman-27-shot-ankle-bullet-fires-window-article-1.3003421">told the Daily News</a>, and transported to Jamaica Hospital in stable condition. </p>

<p>The tabloid reports that cops discovered three bullet holes in one window, and five shell casings on the ground. Sources said the woman had &quot;no idea&quot; why she had been shot. </p>

<p>The shooter fled the scene on foot, according to police, and was last seen wearing a grey hoodie and pants, and black sneakers. </p>
