					<p>Crowds in Midtown have been building steadily since 7:00, around massive screens in Times Square and in front of the Fox News studios on Sixth Avenue. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170421054456/http://dcist.com/2016/11/election_2016_results.php">With most swing states still too close to call</a>, a contingent of Trump supporters in Make America Great Again hats is cheering outside Fox News with every state called in Trump&apos;s favor: Texas, South Carolina. Some Trump supporters we talked to said they&apos;d accept the result if their candidate lost, but others were adamant he would win.  </p>

<p>&#x201C;He&#x2019;s gonna win,&#x201D; said Tonye-D&#x2019;mitrias Vickers, holding a sign that read &#x201C;Hillary Cares About Black Votes, Not About Black Voters.&#x201D;</p>

<p>&#x201C;You know why?&#x201D; he added. &quot;Because Americans are not stupid. Americans aren&#x2019;t going to be duped.&#x201D;</p>

<p>Alex Vasquez stood in front of the Fox monitors wearing a MAGA hat, with an American flag draped over his shoulders. &#x201C;I was previously an Obama fan, I liked Bernie a bit, but ever since the first debate Trump has had my vote. I don&#x2019;t like the establishment. I come from a family of immigrants: Dominican, Puerto Rican. My family knows what it&#x2019;s like to live under leaders who don&#x2019;t treat their country well. And in my opinion, Trump is more likely to respect the country&#x2019;s laws.&#x201D;</p>

<p>Around 8:00 p.m., the Columbia University marching band gathered across Sixth Avenue from the New York Hilton, blocked off at street level by a row of Department of Sanitation trucks, for a cover of Cee-Lo Green&#x2019;s &#x201C;Fuck You.&#x201D; Asked if the band was out to support Clinton, a percussionist said, &#x201C;We&#x2019;re mostly against Trump.&#x201D;</p>
