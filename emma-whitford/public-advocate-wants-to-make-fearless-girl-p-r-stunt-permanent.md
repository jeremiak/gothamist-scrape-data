<p>Last Tuesday, a day before International Women&apos;s Day and a coinciding <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/http://gothamist.com/2017/03/09/womens_strike_profiles.php">Women&apos;s Strike</a>, a statue of a small girl, shoulders defiantly squared, appeared head-to-head with the bronze-testicled Charging Bull of Wall Street. Multi-trillion asset manager State Street Global Advisors <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/http://www.adweek.com/creativity/why-mccann-dropped-a-statue-of-a-fearless-girl-next-to-wall-streets-charging-bull-overnight/">and NY advertising firm McCann</a> are behind artist Kristen Visbal&apos;s installation, which is  accompanied by <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/https://www.bostonglobe.com/business/2017/03/08/there-bull-message-behind-fearless-girl-statue/yb5MtonhyQwkXrGFqkfnnN/story.html">a plaque advertising State Street</a>, embellished with a platitude about women in leadership. While there are no shortage of <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/https://www.instagram.com/p/BRekrKtApgk/">empowering selfies</a> on Instagram, critics have <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/http://www.villagevoice.com/news/too-bad-that-statue-of-a-girl-staring-down-the-wall-street-bull-is-a-pr-stunt-by-wall-street-patriarchs-9749317">dismissed as toothless</a> any rebuke of Wall Street culture sponsored by Wall Street itself. </p>

<p>Love her or hate her, &quot;The Fearless Girl&quot; is currently only permitted to stay for one month, up from an initial one-week permit. But now Public Advocate Letitia James is calling on the Mayor&apos;s Office to make the statue a permanent Wall Street fixture. <br>
 <br>
&quot;Perhaps the strongest influencers to promote corporate diversity are the corporations themselves,&quot; James said in a statement to Gothamist. &quot;State Street Global not only created this symbol of diversity, but is putting its money where its mouth is. More companies should follow suit.&quot;</p>

<p>As for the company&apos;s unsavory past&#x2014;State Street has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/https://www.bostonglobe.com/business/2012/02/29/state-street-global-advisors-agrees-million-penalty-settlement-with-secretary-state-galvin/5iajTOtAW2knOOaibq1KdN/story.html">paid millions</a> in fines for its implication in a mortgage scandal that hit pension holders&#x2014;a spokeswoman for the advocate said James is more interested in the company&apos;s current efforts to increase diversity on corporate boards (its own currently has three women, out of a total 11 members). The company is now <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/http://fortune.com/2017/03/07/money-manager-gender-equality/">focusing its investment</a> on companies in its portfolio with women board members. </p>

<p>&quot;Today, we are calling on companies to take concrete steps to increase gender diversity on their boards and have issued clear guidance to help them begin to take action,&quot; said Ron O&apos;Hanley, CEO of State Street, in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/http://fortune.com/2017/03/07/money-manager-gender-equality/">a recent statement to Fortune</a>. </p>

<p>In her letter to the Mayor, James noted that about 60 percent of publicly traded firms have no women on their boards. She went on to refer to the statue as &quot;a powerful beacon, showing women... that no dream is too big and no ceiling is to high.&quot; </p>

<p>The Mayor&apos;s Office didn&apos;t immediately comment on Fearless Girl&apos;s fate, but de Blasio spokeswoman Olivia Lapeyrolerie <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170822134929/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/manhattan/exclusive-letitia-james-fearless-girl-permanent-article-1.2996620">told the Daily News</a> that the city isn&apos;t planning to extend the permit. If this position stands, there are about three weeks ticking down on the selfie clock. </p>
