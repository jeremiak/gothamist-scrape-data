title: 'Suspicious Device' That Shut Down UWS Intersection Was Just An Empty Locked Box
---

	
										<p>The NYPD Bomb Squad shut down the intersection of West 70th Street and Broadway for over an hour on Monday night following reports of a &apos;suspicious device&apos; that turned out to be an empty, locked box. </p>

<p>An ABC reporter noted the closure around 8:30 p.m., and the intersection was <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113152801/https://twitter.com/DanLinden/status/772965380289622016">still blocked off 45 minutes later</a>, diverting cars and buses. </p>

<p><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161113152801/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/manhattan/broadway-briefly-shut-suspicious-device-report-article-1.2779784">According to the Daily News</a>, the Bomb Squad responded to the intersection at 8:30 p.m. following a report of a suspicious device in the roadway&apos;s center median. Forty-five minutes later, an NYPD spokesman confirmed that it was just a locked box. The News reports that the box was attached to a bench.</p>
