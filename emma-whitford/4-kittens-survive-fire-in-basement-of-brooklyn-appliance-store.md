					<p>Four sooty kittens were saved from a flaming storefront in Bushwick on Sunday morning, after firefighters responded to a one-alarm blaze inside an appliance store near the Kosciuszko Street J station. </p>

<p>&quot;[Firefighters] broke down the front glass window,&quot; said Joanna Shkrely, who lives across the street from Yuna Appliances, where the fire broke out. &quot;It seemed to be a situation where there were stray cats in the building. They came across really small kittens in the basement.&quot; </p>

<p>&quot;They didn&apos;t find the mother&apos;s body or anything like that,&quot; she added. &quot;But they didn&apos;t reunite them with the mother either.&quot; </p>

<p>A spokesman for the FDNY said that 60 firefighters responded to 1123 Broadway near the corner of Dekalb Avenue around 9:40 a.m. on Sunday. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161017153708/http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/brooklyn/kittens-rabbit-rescued-burning-brooklyn-building-article-1.2654024">The Daily News reports</a> that the fire originated in the basement, and was under control in an hour. No humans were hospitalized, although one sustained a minor injury. The FDNY has yet to determine what caused the blaze. </p>

<p>As for the kittens, firefighters on the scene treated them with oxygen for smoke inhalation. </p>

<p>A large brown rabbit was also recovered from the blaze. </p>

<p>&quot;I really like this rabbit,&quot; the survivor&apos;s 11-year-old owner Joshua Rodriguez told the News. &quot;If he died I would feel sad. At first when I never took care of him I never used to like him, but now I started to like him&#x2026; I have a relationship with the rabbit.&quot; </p>
