					<p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170612143708/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/21/2nd_avenue_subway_history.php">long-awaited</a> first leg of the Second Avenue Subway from 72nd Street to 96th Street won&apos;t be <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170612143708/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/19/second_avenue_subway_coming_soon.php#photo-1">open to the public</a> until January 1st&#x2014;and with <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170612143708/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/20/second_avenue_subway_limited.php">reduced service</a>, at that. But Governor Andrew Cuomo is not one to miss an <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170612143708/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/23/second_ave_subway_96.php#photo-27">infrastructure-related photo opportunity</a>, hence the inaugural New Year&apos;s Eve ride planned for Saturday, December 31st. </p>

<p>The trip&#x2014;reserved for press and co-hosted by organizations including the Central Park Conservancy, &#x200E;Manhattan Chamber of Commerce, and the Metropolitan Museum of Art&#x2014;will depart from 72nd Street at 10:30 p.m. with stops at each new station, according to the Governor&apos;s Office. MTA Chairman Thomas Prendergast will join the ride, along with Governor Cuomo. Mayor Bill de Blasio, who declined an invitation to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170612143708/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/23/second_ave_subway_96.php#photo-15">press tour at 96th Street last week</a>, will also join the New Year&apos;s Eve party train, according to spokesman Eric Phillips. </p>

<p>Plebeians, meanwhile, will be stuck nursing their <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170612143708/http://gothamist.com/2016/12/25/2nd_ave_subway_sads.php">subway-related complaints</a>. </p>

<p>Here&apos;s to confronting delays to the Second Avenue Subway&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170612143708/http://gothamist.com/2016/04/21/money_in_money_out.php">Harlem extension</a> in the cold, sober light of 2017. </p>

<p><strong>[Update 3:00 p.m.]:</strong> A spokesman for Governor Cuomo confirmed Mayor de Blasio&apos;s invitation and added that community members and business owners along the line will also be in attendance. </p>
