					<p>Construction crews began pouring concrete this weekend for the foundation of Staten Island&apos;s $500 million-plus, 60-story <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/10/video_heres_what_the_dazzling_view.php">New York Wheel</a>, which project developer and CEO Rich Marin has <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20150908/st-george/new-york-wheel-developers-try-crowdfund-30m">described lovingly</a> as a &quot;populist attraction,&quot; even though one spin on the hulking tourist magnet will likely cost $35. </p>

<center><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">A <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://twitter.com/hashtag/beforeafter?src=hash">#beforeafter</a> view of 420 <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://twitter.com/hashtag/cement?src=hash">#cement</a> trucks working 15+ hours to finalize the wheel&apos;s <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://twitter.com/hashtag/foundation?src=hash">#foundation</a> work. <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://twitter.com/hashtag/onebigwheel?src=hash">#onebigwheel</a> <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://t.co/uy8TWMjHfr">pic.twitter.com/uy8TWMjHfr</a></p>&#x2014; New York Wheel (@TheNewYorkWheel) <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://twitter.com/TheNewYorkWheel/status/742410355670941697">June 13, 2016</a></blockquote>
<script async src="//web.archive.org/web/20161110104931js_/http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></center>

<p>Marin defended the inevitability of his massive wheel this weekend, which was initially <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/http://gothamist.com/2013/10/31/tallest_ferris_wheel_on_earth_appro.php">scheduled for completion this year</a> (since then, he&apos;s had to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/http://gothamist.com/2015/09/08/ferris_wheel_needs_donations.php">turn to crowdfunding</a> to make up the difference). Plans were wrinkled further last December, when <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/http://therealdeal.com/2015/12/16/wheel-of-misfortune-original-developer-of-new-york-wheel-says-hes-been-improperly-shut-out-of-project/">the Real Deal reported</a> that the chair of the project&apos;s board, developer Meir Laufer, filed suit claiming he was pushed out of the planning process and discriminated against because he is Jewish. </p>

<p>&quot;I would challenge anybody who doesn&apos;t believe to come down here and look at the $250 million we have in the ground,&quot; Marin <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20160613/st-george/first-section-of-massive-new-york-wheel-put-place">told DNAInfo over the weekend</a>. Forcefully imposing his opinion on the assembled crowd he added, &quot;We&apos;re either delusional or something if we don&apos;t think it&apos;s real at this point.&quot;</p>

<p>The wheel&apos;s foundation&#x2014;which has been <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/https://twitter.com/TheNewYorkWheel/status/741329232094203904">prepped steadily</a> for the last 13 months&#x2014;will eventually rest 150 feet long by 30 feet wide, supporting the 20 million-pound wheel, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/http://www.ny1.com/nyc/all-boroughs/news/2016/06/11/observation-wheel-on-staten-island-is-one-step-closer-to-turning.html">NY1 reports</a>. It took 420 concrete trucks about 16 hours to negotiate Saturday&apos;s massive, 11-foot-deep pour. </p>

<p>There&apos;s another concrete pour scheduled for June 25th&#x2014;the site is a few minute&apos;s walk from the Staten Island Ferry terminal&#x2014;and Marin told reporters this weekend that the wheel&apos;s legs will likely be embedded this October. As of this writing, completion is estimated for late 2017. In the meantime, developers are continuing to carry out their plans for Staten Island waterfront domination, which include a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/http://bfcnyc.com/portfolio/empire-outlets/">large outlet mall</a>, hotels, and shops and restaurants inside what Elysa Goldman of Triangle Equities has described as a &quot;historic waterfront village&quot; at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/http://www.nycedc.com/project/lighthouse-point">Lighthouse Point</a>. </p>

<p>&quot;Having a vibrant, diverse, hip waterfront and being a bedroom community are not mutually exclusive,&quot; Borough President James Oddo told residents last year at a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20161110104931/http://gothamist.com/2015/06/26/staten_islands_north_shore_might_lo.php">panel discussion with Marin and his fellow developers</a>. &quot;We can have it all.&quot; </p>
